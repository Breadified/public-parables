# Luke 20:20-25

**Title:** The Welfare of the City
**Date:** 19 October 2018 (SMAC)
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## A Clever Trap

The religious authorities set a trap for Jesus, hoping to catch him between two impossible positions. They asked: "Is it right for us to pay taxes to Caesar or not?"

The trap was ingenious. If Jesus said no to paying taxes, they could hand him over to the Roman governor as a revolutionary and lawbreaker. If he said yes, he would appear to endorse Caesar's rule and seem disloyal to God, especially given that Caesar's authority stood in opposition to God's kingdom.

It was an either/or argument with no way to win—or so they thought.

Jesus, with characteristic wisdom, saw through their duplicity and offered a response that transcended their false dilemma. He asked to see a denarius and then asked whose image and inscription were on it. When they replied "Caesar's," Jesus said simply: "Then give back to Caesar what is Caesar's, and to God what is God's."

The questioners were astonished and fell silent, unable to trap him.

## Give to Caesar What Is Caesar's

Jesus affirmed that government has a legitimate function in society. This wasn't a casual statement—it established the biblical framework for understanding authority.

Under Roman law, every subject had to pay a poll tax. A denarius was worth a day's wage. The coin bore Caesar's image and inscription, which meant it belonged to his realm. Jesus' logic was straightforward: since this coin is Caesar's, give it back to Caesar.

Government exists as an institution established by God. Paul made this clear in Romans 13:

> Let everyone be subject to the governing authorities, for there is no authority except that which God has established. The authorities that exist have been established by God. Consequently, whoever rebels against the authority is rebelling against what God has instituted.

Governments serve a specific purpose: they bear the sword—the power to enforce law and punish wrongdoers. They protect citizens from criminals, maintain order, and administer justice. This is their God-given function. Paying taxes supports this necessary work.

We live in relative peace because government provides police, courts, armies, and legal systems. These institutions restrain evil and maintain the social order that allows commerce, families, and communities to flourish. Even imperfect governments, when functioning properly, provide better outcomes than chaos.

Yet this doesn't mean government should do everything.

## Give to God What Is God's

The deeper implication of Jesus' teaching cuts the other direction: **not everything belongs to Caesar.**

What belongs to God? We can understand this by asking what bears God's image and inscription.

Genesis tells us that mankind is made in God's image:

> Then God said, "Let us make mankind in our image, in our likeness..."

Moreover, God has written his law on every human heart:

> I will put my laws in their minds and write them on their hearts. (Hebrews 8:10)

And even those outside faith recognize this written law:

> When Gentiles, who do not have the law, do by nature things required by the law, they show that the requirements of the law are written on their hearts. (Romans 2:14-15)

Every person bears God's image and bears God's inscription. Therefore, every person belongs to God.

This means we owe God our complete allegiance, loyalty, and tribute. We give God everything—our breath, our energy, our faithfulness, our service. As the heavenly chorus declares:

> You are worthy, our Lord and God, to receive glory and honor and power, for you created all things, and by your will they were created and have their being. (Revelation 4:11)

### Two Critical Implications

**First:** We personally owe Jesus our complete tribute. In these last days, we are called to deny ourselves, take up our cross, and follow him. God has given us everything; we return everything to him in worship and obedience.

**Second:** Caesar's rule is limited because Caesar himself is subject to God. Augustus may have claimed to be the "Son of God," yet the true Son of God—the real source of all authority—stands above him. Caesar is not supreme. God is supreme, and all earthly rulers answer to him.

## The Danger of State Idolatry

Here lies a troubling truth: many of us have become practical atheists regarding government. We claim to trust God, yet we trust Caesar with far more.

Consider where we turn when facing problems:

- **Housing affordability?** We turn to government. (Thirty years of policies have only worsened it.)
- **Racism?** Government will fix it. (A century of attempts; still getting worse.)
- **Mental health and suicide?** Government programs abound. (Yet suicide rates remain stubbornly high.)
- **Poverty, unemployment, health, obesity, depression, medicine costs, war?** We believe government will solve them all.

We speak of government solving problems with an almost divine confidence. We trust them with our finances, our children's education, our healthcare, our retirement. We obey their regulations without question. We've divided our loyalty: Monday through Saturday belongs to Caesar; Sunday belongs to God.

Yet if extraterrestrials observed our behavior, they might offer this modern prayer:

> Our Government in Canberra, hallowed be thy name. Thy bureaucracy comes, thy will be done. Give us today our Medicare. Forgive us our political incorrectness as we forgive those who tax us. Lead us not into enterprise, but keep us dependent on you. For thine is the kingdom of government, the power of Centrelink, and the Department of Human Services, forever and ever.

This is idolatry of the state. When we unconsciously believe the government is all-powerful and capable of solving every problem, we've replaced worship of God with worship of Caesar.

## The Limits of Government

Government cannot do what God does. It cannot:

- Create true morality or transform hearts
- Rehabilitate sin or bring genuine repentance
- Forgive or show mercy in a redemptive sense
- Love genuinely or care with authentic compassion
- Create culture or instill true virtue

God, through Christ, creates, guides, teaches, forgives, transforms, cares, and loves. These are his unique works.

Government is force—people with guns. Force can restrain evil temporarily, but it cannot regenerate the human soul. Government officials are merely ordinary people, no wiser than the general population. They lack the resources they give away (all government money is tax money taken first). They cannot balance budgets the way households must. They often cannot even accomplish simple tasks, yet we expect them to manage vast complexes of modern life.

The irony is striking: we restrict where Jesus can go in public life—in schools, hospitals, courts—while simultaneously giving Caesar unlimited authority and never telling him he's limited.

## Understanding Authority Structures

God has established different institutions with different spheres of authority:

**Family:** Parents have authority over children. The family procreates, feeds, nurtures, and teaches. Government cannot replace these functions.

**Church:** Church leaders have authority over members through persuasion and spiritual discipline—not force.

**Government:** Civil rulers have authority to maintain order, punish evil, and protect citizens through law.

**Business:** Employers oversee employees; workers serve their employers.

Each institution has distinct tools and purposes. Government's tool is force; the church's tool is the word; the family's tool is love combined with authority.

This is what's called "sphere sovereignty"—each institution derives its authority directly from God, not as a grant from the state. The state exists alongside other institutions, not above them. When government overreaches and tries to do what only families, churches, or individuals can do, tyranny results.

## The Growth of Government Authority

Historically, government has been the natural state of human affairs. Kings ruled with plenary power for millennia. Modern democratic and constitutional limits on government are relatively recent innovations.

Yet we face creeping expansion:

- Government increasingly decides what may be taught to children
- Government defines economic priorities and consumption patterns  
- Government redefines fundamental concepts like marriage, sex, and language
- Government takes approximately half our income and controls half our productive lives
- Government increasingly restricts peaceful protest and free speech

When government becomes too large, Christians must speak to it. Not from partisanship, but from principle. The church has largely abdicated this responsibility, and we're seeing the consequences.

## A Call to Engagement

This doesn't mean withdrawing from politics or refusing to participate. Rather, it means:

- Being informed about government's proper role
- Holding government accountable when it overreaches
- Participating in the democratic process with biblical wisdom
- Being willing to engage in civil disobedience when government commands what God forbids
- Recognizing that Christians can and should be involved in politics—bringing God's wisdom to bear on questions of justice, liberty, and the common good

Australia has a remarkable constitutional heritage rooted in Christian values. For two hundred years, Christians have stewarded institutions that limit power and protect freedom. That legacy is fraying because believers have been too busy, or influenced by poor theology, to remain engaged.

Nothing is inevitable. By becoming better informed and some of us engaging more sacrificially, we can help preserve freedom and sanity in our nation.

## The Ultimate Choice

Jesus' teaching presents a choice: Caesar or God? Who do you truly trust?

The consequences differ dramatically. Give everything to Caesar, and when his spirit departs, his plans come to nothing—and yours with them. Give everything to God, and you belong to the One who created all things and sustains them by his power.

> Do not put your trust in princes, in human beings, who cannot save. When their spirit departs, they return to the ground; on that very day their plans come to nothing. Blessed are those whose help is the God of Jacob, whose hope is in the Lord their God. (Psalm 146:3-5)

The choice is yours. Which will it be?
