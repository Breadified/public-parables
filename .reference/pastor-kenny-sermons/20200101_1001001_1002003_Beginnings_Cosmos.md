## The Beginning of Time

> **Genesis 1:1** In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth.

When we say "in the beginning," we're making a profound statement about time itself. Unlike cyclical worldviews that dominate ancient and modern philosophies, the Bible declares that time is linear—it has a beginning and moves forward with purpose and direction.

This is a countercultural claim. In the ancient West, Greek philosophers like Aristotle believed time was infinite and eternal, while Plato saw time as merely "a moving image of eternity"—something illusory. In the East, religions like Hinduism and Buddhism taught that time moves in endless cycles of death and rebirth, where karma determines your station across reincarnations. The Mayan calendar itself cycled through time in vast repeating patterns.

But into this worldview, Scripture introduces something revolutionary: **time has a beginning**.

### Why This Matters

This doctrine transforms how we understand reality and ourselves. If life is an endless cycle with no real progress, then meaning collapses into nihilism. There's no incentive for change, no hope for improvement, no reason to help others. Under karma, if someone is poor, the universe has determined it—why interfere? This despair became so absolute that the highest Eastern spiritual goal is *Nirvana*—literally "extinguishment," the cessation of existence itself.

By contrast, the Christian faith declares that **real change is possible**. Because time moves forward, not in circles, life can genuinely improve. Poverty can be alleviated. Diseases can be healed. The weak deserve our protection and care, not abandonment to "survival of the fittest." History has a direction and an endpoint.

### Linear Time and Human Progress

The ancient Jews counted time not in cycles but in a linear progression of years from creation. Later, the Christian world reset the calendar around the Incarnation of Christ—*Anno Domini*, "the year of our Lord." We're now approaching year 2021, and this number only grows larger because time is genuinely linear.

This linear view of time explains something profound: humans accumulate knowledge across generations in ways no other creature does. Your pet dog is no more intellectually capable than dogs of two thousand years ago. But humans build on what previous generations discovered. We accomplish things—space travel, transplant surgery, computer technology—that our ancestors never imagined. This is only possible because we understand time as a linear progression where we carry forward the gains of the past.

## God Created an Ordered Universe

> **Genesis 1:2** Now the earth was formless and empty, darkness was over the surface of the deep, and the Spirit of God was hovering over the waters.

The creation wasn't from absolute nothingness—there was water, matter waiting to be ordered. The ancients who first heard this account understood something crucial: the world before God's creative word was *chaotic and unordered*.

### The Pattern of Order

As we read through Genesis 1, we discover God's creative work follows a deliberate pattern. For the first three days, God *creates environments*: light, sky, and land. For the next three days, He *fills those environments*: with celestial bodies, with sea creatures and birds, and with animals and mankind. This isn't random; it's systematically structured.

The text itself reinforces this order:
- The repeated phrase "And God said... and it was so... and he saw that it was good" creates rhythmic, orderly prose
- The word "good" (Hebrew: *tov*) appears seven times, and the number seven represents completeness in Jewish tradition
- The first sentence contains exactly seven words in Hebrew
- God is mentioned 35 times (7 × 5)—mathematical perfection woven through the account

All this structural excellence communicates that **God is a God of order, not chaos**.

### Ancient Context: Order Against Chaos

The pagan religions surrounding ancient Israel taught that the world emerged from cosmic conflict—that chaos, represented by warring gods and monsters, was the fundamental nature of reality. A god had to die to create the world from the remnants.

Genesis directly contradicts this. God didn't fight chaos; He *ordered* it. He speaks creation into being with calm authority. The universe isn't the leftover debris of divine warfare—it's the intentional product of one sovereign intelligence.

Even today, materialistic science sees the world as fundamentally random—DNA mutations, survival of the fittest, the gradual assembly of order from chaos through blind processes. But Scripture reveals a cosmos that testifies to intelligent design and purposeful arrangement.

### Science and the God of Order

It should not surprise us that modern science itself emerged from Christian thinkers. Francis Bacon, the founder of the scientific method, was a believer who wrote: "Knowledge is the rich storehouse for the glory of the Creator and the relief of man's estate." Isaac Newton, who wrote more theology than physics, declared: "The most beautiful system of the sun, planets and comets could only proceed from the counsel and dominion of an intelligent and powerful Being." Galileo, Kepler, Faraday, Maxwell, and even Charles Darwin acknowledged design and divine wisdom.

These men pursued science because they believed in a rational, ordered universe created by a rational God. Real science is humble investigation into the laws God has written into creation.

Unfortunately, modern science has drifted from this humility. It's become politicized—"the consensus has spoken," "science has declared," "conspiracy theory." Rules change arbitrarily. During the pandemic, authorities ordered lockdowns based on shifting guidance. Scientists used to be those humbly pursuing truth through trial and error. Now they often become gatekeepers of what's permitted to be questioned. But God remains the God of order, unmoved by human politics or fear.

## Mankind: Created in God's Image

> **Genesis 1:27–28** So God created mankind in his own image, in the image of God he created them; male and female he created them. God blessed them and said to them, "Be fruitful and increase in number; fill the earth and subdue it. Rule over the fish of the sea and the birds in the sky and over every living creature that moves on the ground."

The creation of humanity marks the climax of the six days. Notice how the text shifts: God doesn't simply say "let there be man" like He does for other creatures. Instead, He says, "Let **us** make man in **our** image." Something more personal, more intentional happens here.

### Human Dignity and Divine Image

When God declares that humans are made in His image, He establishes the foundation for all human rights and dignity. We are not merely sophisticated animals following evolutionary imperatives of dominance and reproduction. We carry the very image of the eternal God.

This is why slavery is wrong, murder is wrong, and the abandonment of the weak to perish is wrong. A worldview that reduces humans to animals has no basis for condemning these things. In nature, the strong dominate, kill, and consume the weak. But humans are different because we are created in God's image.

This doctrine enabled the abolition of slavery, the establishment of universal human rights, and the Christian commitment to care for the vulnerable. It's the only stable foundation for human dignity in a world that otherwise knows only competition and survival of the fittest.

### God's Provision and Love

> **Genesis 1:29** Then God said, "I give you every seed-bearing plant on the face of the whole earth and every tree that has fruit with seed in it. They will be yours for food."

Notice the reversal of what pagan religions taught: in those systems, humans were created to *serve the gods*—to provide food and sacrifice. Here, God *serves humanity* by providing abundance. He gives us dominion and sustenance. This reveals God's love for mankind.

## Living as Bearers of God's Image

We live in a time of spiritual confusion. As culture drifts from Christian foundations, it loses the basis for hope. We see rising despair: abortion, euthanasia, suicide, and a pervasive sense that life isn't worth living unless it's Instagram-perfect.

Yet Genesis speaks a different word: **there was a beginning, and there is direction to time**.

Real change is possible. Not through endless cycles of karma, not through the blind processes of evolution, but through the intentional purpose of a God who creates, sustains, and loves His image-bearers.

### Your Personal Beginning

Think about your own life. You have a beginning—a birth date you celebrate. You've experienced key chapters: achievements, failures, moves, relationships, career transitions. Your life has shape and direction, just as creation does.

This month, consider creating a personal timeline. Reflect on where you've come from and the trajectory you're on. Better yet, gather with others and share your stories. These personal narratives reveal that our lives, like creation itself, have purpose and order.

### The Ultimate Beginning and Hope

But where is all this heading? The answer comes not at the beginning of Scripture but at the climax: in Jesus Christ.

> **John 1:1–2, 14** In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God. He was with God in the beginning... The Word became flesh and made his dwelling among us. We have seen his glory, the glory of the one and only Son, who came from the Father, full of grace and truth.

The cosmic Creator who ordered chaos now enters history as a human. This is not a cyclical return—it's a once-and-for-all, never-to-be-repeated event. It's the climax toward which all of linear time moves.

Jesus is Lord over the sun and moon, over disease and death itself. He heals the paralyzed. He controls the virus. He brings hope, not fear, to a frightened world.

And He promises that one day He will return to create *new heavens and a new earth*—a world where evil is banished, decay is reversed, war ceases, disease is eradicated, and death itself is defeated. This is the Christian's hope: not extinction like Nirvana, but resurrection and eternal fellowship with God.

## Conclusion

In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth. He brought order from chaos, time from eternity, purpose from void. He made you—deliberately, lovingly, intentionally—in His own image.

This is your foundation. This is your hope. This is your beginning.
