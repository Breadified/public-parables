# 1 Timothy 4:16

**Title:** Watch Your Life and Doctrine
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction: The Apple Watch and Our Calling

This week brought the Apple Watch release, but there's a deeper lesson in that name. The apostle Paul's exhortation rings just as true today: "Watch your life and doctrine closely."

## Watch Your Life: Living with Integrity

As you step into adulthood and especially into university life, you'll experience unprecedented freedom. There's a temptation to compartmentalize—to live one way at church and another way in your residence hall. But Paul calls us to integrate our faith with our daily living.

### The Challenge of Growing in Knowledge

Don't let your understanding of God remain at the level you learned in Sunday school or high school. As you encounter sophisticated thinking at university, your faith must grow correspondingly. If your knowledge of God doesn't mature, you'll find yourself surrounded by brilliant people who make faith seem inadequate by comparison. That's when many abandon their faith entirely.

Here's practical wisdom: if you're taking five subjects at university, make your Christian community your fifth—not as an afterthought, but as essential to your intellectual and spiritual health.

### The Perseverance That Sustains

We're not naturally good at perseverance. We avoid pain, we hate failure, we crave comfort. But the Christian life was never promised to be easy.

Externally, we face the world's hardships—persecution, illness, death. Internally, we battle guilt, mental illness, exhaustion, and forgetfulness. Yet Christ calls us to persist.

**Two things must anchor your perseverance:**

First, persevere *with Jesus*. Whatever life throws at you—achievement or failure, health or sickness, belonging or loneliness—never abandon your relationship with Him. He is the constant when everything else shifts.

Second, persevere *in living for Jesus*. Your life doesn't belong to you. It belongs to Him and to your community. This isn't bondage—it's freedom from the lie that you must live for yourself. And when people see your life and doctrine acted out together, they see the beauty of the gospel.

## The Exhortation in Every Season

You may achieve what the world calls success—the dream job, the status, the comfort. Don't abandon God in your prosperity.

You may face devastating failure—mental illness, broken relationships, financial ruin, family dysfunction. Don't abandon God in your despair.

Today, if you're feeling positive about Jesus, write yourself a note. Years from now, when life tests your faith, read those words and remember: you will persevere with Him whether life is good or whether it's hard.

## Why Perseverance Is Possible

Because Jesus loves you with a love nothing in this world can match. He died for you. He rose again. He's alive today, interceding for you. And He's promised to return and make all things right.

In the meantime, He sustains you. He's the light at the end of the tunnel. God will preserve you through His Son—not because life becomes easy, but because you're never alone in it.

**Watch your life. Watch your doctrine. And trust the One who watches over you.**
