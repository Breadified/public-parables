## Introduction: Paul's Ambition

At the end of the church fiscal year, let me share something about ambition. What drives your life's work? For many, it's climbing the corporate ladder, enjoying comfort in an air-conditioned office, or accumulating possessions. But Paul had a different aspiration entirely.

> **Romans 15:20** "I have always been ambitious to preach the gospel where Christ was not known."

Paul's ambition was radical: to pioneer gospel work in unreached territories. This teaching unfolds in three movements through Romans 15:7-33—glorifying God through the nations, leading the nations, and serving the nations.

---

## Part One: Glorifying God Through the Nations (Romans 15:1-14)

### The Gospel's Surprising Inclusivity

This wasn't an obvious career path. In Jewish tradition, Gentiles were excluded from covenant privileges. Yet Scripture reveals something revolutionary:

> **Romans 15:8-9** "Christ became a servant of the Jews on behalf of God's truth, to confirm the promises made to the patriarchs so that the Gentiles may glorify God for his mercy."

The Old Testament anticipated this mystery. Paul strings together passages showing Gentiles joining Israel's worship:

> **2 Samuel 22:50, Psalm 18:49:** "I will praise you among the nations; I will sing the praises of your name."
>
> **Deuteronomy 32:43:** "Rejoice, you nations, with his people."
>
> **Psalm 117:1:** "Praise the Lord, all you nations; extol him, all you peoples."
>
> **Isaiah 11:10:** "The Root of Jesse will spring up, one who will arise to rule over the nations; the Gentiles will hope in him."

What's remarkable isn't that Gentiles are included, but that they're included *directly*—without first becoming Jewish, without circumcision. This is the mystery Paul celebrates.

### The Mystery Revealed

For centuries, this truth was hidden. God's plan included Gentiles as full heirs, not as second-class subjects:

> **Romans 16:25-27** "The mystery hidden for long ages past...has now been revealed and made known through the prophetic writings...to all the Gentiles to bring about the obedience that comes from faith."
>
> **Ephesians 3:6** "This mystery is that through the gospel the Gentiles are heirs together with Israel, members together of one body, and sharers together in the promise in Christ Jesus."
>
> **Galatians 3:28** "There is neither Jew nor Gentile, neither slave nor free, nor is there male and female, for you are all one in Christ Jesus."

The point isn't sameness, but standing before God. Under the old order, Jews were landowners in God's household while Gentiles were servants and hired hands—all serving in one house, but with different roles. The mystery reveals that Gentiles aren't slaves but *children*, with full inheritance rights.

### Application: Racial Equality in Christ

This ancient debate about Jewish and Gentile distinction remains relevant to modern conversations about racism. The issue isn't between Jews and Gentiles anymore, but among Gentiles themselves.

When governments implement race-based policies—giving special voice to one ethnic group while excluding others—they contradict the gospel's fundamental principle. If you want to help marginalized people, help the marginalized. If you want to serve the poor, serve the poor—regardless of ethnicity. Creating special boxes for one race while ignoring the suffering of other races reverses the gospel.

The solution to racism isn't reverse racism. When we drift from Christian teaching on racial equality, we lose sight of what Christ has already accomplished. His mystery has been revealed—we shouldn't hide it again.

## Part Two: Leading the Nations (Romans 15:15-23)

### Paul's Apostolic Authority

Paul sees himself in a priestly role toward the Gentiles:

> **Romans 15:15-16** "I write you quite boldly on some points...because of the grace God gave me to be a minister of Christ Jesus to the Gentiles with the priestly duty of proclaiming the gospel of God, so that the Gentiles might become an offering acceptable to the Lord, sanctified by the Holy Spirit."

His authority came directly from Christ:

> **Acts 9:15** "This man is my chosen instrument to proclaim my name to the Gentiles and their kings and to the people of Israel."

Paul understood himself as continuing Jesus' work. Just as Jesus was the servant of the Jews, Paul became the servant of the Gentiles, fulfilling the suffering servant passages:

> **Acts 13:46-47** "It was necessary that the word of God be spoken to you first...Since you reject it and do not consider yourselves worthy of eternal life, we now turn to the Gentiles. For this is what the Lord has commanded us: 'I have made you a light for the Gentiles, that you may bring salvation to the ends of the earth.'"

This echoes Isaiah's suffering servant role, which Paul continued through his own apostolic ministry.

### Paul's Unique Missionary Strategy

> **Romans 15:17-20** "Therefore I glory in Christ Jesus in my service to God. I will not venture to speak of anything except what Christ has accomplished through me in leading the Gentiles to obey God—by what I have said and done—by the power of signs and wonders, through the power of the Spirit of God. So from Jerusalem all the way around to Illyricum, I have fully proclaimed the gospel of Christ. It has always been my ambition to preach the gospel where Christ was not known."

Paul's strategy was distinctive: he went to new territories rather than building on others' foundations. Some scholars suggest this was respect for other apostles' work. Others note Paul's ambition to be a pioneer missionary.

Yet statistics about unreached peoples can be misleading. The real question is local: Does your neighbor know Christ? Is there a Bible study for children who don't know Jesus? Paul understood evangelism as *labor-intensive*—person-to-person work through youth leaders, Sunday school teachers, Scripture teachers, conversations with colleagues and neighbors.

The important statistic isn't global demographics but local reality. In Sydney, thousands of non-believers arrive weekly. They're unreached. Evangelism requires actual human presence and effort.

### A Legal Metaphor

Paul's role mirrors a senior partner at a law firm. Jesus came for three extraordinary years—the high-level work. Paul spent the next thirty years as the junior associate, executing the vision, bringing the gospel to civilization's ends. By the time Paul reaches Rome, the project is nearly complete.

> **Romans 15:19** "From Jerusalem all the way around to Illyricum, I have fully proclaimed the gospel of Christ."

Though secondary sources suggest Paul may have reached Spain, Acts concludes in Rome—the empire's center. From Rome's roads, the gospel spread to the ends of the earth. From Rome to Britain, then to Britain's colonies, including Australia. Then to you.

The gospel's theological conquest of the world was complete.

## Part Three: Serving the Nations (Romans 15:24-33)

### The Collection as Symbolic Act

But before Spain, Paul must return to Jerusalem:

> **Romans 15:25-29** "Now, however, I am on my way to Jerusalem in the service of the Lord's people there. For Macedonia and Achaia were pleased to make a contribution for the poor among the Lord's people in Jerusalem. They were pleased to do it, and indeed they owe it to them. For if the Gentiles have shared in the Jews' spiritual blessings, they owe it to the Jews to share with them their material blessings. So after I have completed this task and made sure they have received this contribution, I will go to Spain and visit you on the way. I know that when I come to you, I will come in the full measure of the blessing of Christ."

This seems like mere travel logistics. Why would Paul risk his life delivering a gift when others could do it? Why organize a large delegation of church representatives? Why the elaborate arrangements?

The answer lies in Paul's priestly language and Old Testament prophecy. Paul understands himself as fulfilling the prophecy of Gentiles bringing gifts to Zion:

> **Isaiah 18:7** "At that time gifts will be brought to the Lord of hosts...to Mount Zion, to the place of the name of the Lord."
>
> **Isaiah 60:14** "The children of your oppressors will come bowing before you; all who despise you will bow down at your feet and will call you the City of the Lord."

In Roman military processions, the general leads captives and spoils through the gates. Paul mirrors this—not as conqueror but as priest, leading Gentile representatives bearing gifts to Jerusalem. He's fulfilling the prophetic vision of the end times, when nations stream to Zion with their offerings.

When Jews rejected the gospel, Paul wasn't discouraged. He would lead an eschatological procession of Gentiles to Jerusalem, signaling that the end times had begun. Perhaps this would provoke Jewish jealousy—"If the Gentiles have come to worship in Jerusalem, maybe we're missing something. Maybe Jesus really is the Messiah."

### The Call to Priestly Living

This eschatological act points to our own calling:

> **Romans 12:1** "Therefore, I urge you, brothers and sisters, in view of God's mercy, to offer your bodies as a living sacrifice, holy and pleasing to God—this is your true and proper worship."

We are the sacrificial offering to the world. The end times aren't distant—death surrounds us. Our choices about following God or rejecting him are permanent, irreversible.

So we proclaim the gospel. We build the infrastructure for continued gospel teaching. We raise children who will become future heralds. We direct our ambitions heavenward, not toward worldly comfort or status. We put aside earthly desires and remember that our ambition transcends this world.

> **Romans 15:30-33** "I urge you, brothers and sisters, by our Lord Jesus Christ and by the love of the Spirit, to join me in my struggle by praying to God for me. Pray that I may be kept safe from the unbelievers in Judea and that the contribution I take to Jerusalem may be favorably received by the Lord's people there, so that by God's will I may come to you with joy and together with you be refreshed. The God of peace be with you all. Amen."

## Closing: What Is Your Ambition?

The end times have arrived. Death is near. We are fighting to preserve what matters eternally. Whatever you choose regarding God will be permanent and irreversible.

Therefore:
- Proclaim the gospel
- Build infrastructure for continued gospel ministry
- Raise children as future gospel heralds
- Set aside worldly ambitions
- Long for Christ's return
- Prepare everyone for his coming

Your ambition is not abroad or in accumulating possessions. Your ambition transcends this world. It is heavenly. We long for God to come again, and everyone to be ready for his return.
