# Galatians 1:1-10

**Title:** The One Gospel  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Central Issue

Paul opens his letter to the Galatians with urgent concern: the church is abandoning the one gospel they received.

> I am astonished that you are so quickly deserting the one who called you by the grace of Christ and are turning to a different gospel. (Galatians 1:6)

This is not a gentle rebuke. Paul's astonishment reflects the gravity of spiritual drift.

## The Gospel's Origin and Content

### It Comes From God

Paul establishes the gospel's authority immediately:

> Paul, an apostle—sent not from men nor by man, but by Jesus Christ and God the Father, who raised him from the dead. (Galatians 1:1)

This is not Paul's invention or human opinion. His apostolic authority flows directly from the risen Christ and God the Father.

### It Centers on Jesus

The gospel's substance is crystalline:

> Grace and peace to you from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ, who gave himself for our sins to rescue us from the present evil age, according to the will of our God and Father, to whom be glory for ever and ever. Amen. (Galatians 1:3-5)

The gospel is not a philosophy or self-improvement program. It is the good news that Jesus gave himself—his life, his body, his blood—as the once-for-all sacrifice for our sins. He rescued us from the present evil age. This is the heart of all Christian faith.

## The Danger of Perversions

### What Others Offer is No Gospel at All

Some in Galatia were promoting a different message:

> This is really no gospel at all. Evidently some people are throwing you into confusion and are trying to pervert the gospel of Christ. (Galatians 1:7)

Paul's response is uncompromising:

> But even if we or an angel from heaven should preach a gospel other than the one we preached to you, let him be eternally condemned! As we have already said, so now I say again: If anybody is preaching to you a gospel other than what you accepted, let him be eternally condemned! (Galatians 1:8-9)

This is not tolerance toward different opinions. This is the sternest warning Scripture permits. Anything other than the true gospel deserves eternal condemnation.

### The Real Motive Behind False Gospels

What drives these perversions?

> Am I now trying to win the approval of men, or of God? Or am I trying to please men? If I were still trying to please men, I would not be a servant of Christ. (Galatians 1:10)

False gospels exist to please people. They soften the message. They add human merit and works. They make everyone comfortable. But the true gospel cannot be softened—it is absolute.

## The Contrast

The real issue is stark:

**The One Gospel:** Jesus gave himself for our sins to rescue us from the present evil age.

**All Others:** No gospel at all—worthy of eternal condemnation.

There is no middle ground, no compromise, no blending of messages.

## The Practical Reality

We live in an age of religious pluralism where people assume all faith traditions teach essentially the same thing. We imagine we can place every belief in a basket, shake it up, and out comes something that makes everyone happy.

But Jesus refuses to fit into that basket of irrelevance.

If the message is that He gave Himself for our sins to rescue us from the present evil age, then that is the message. Nothing can be added to it. Nothing can be fixed or improved about it. You cannot add human works to complete it. You cannot add your personal merit. You cannot supplement it with anything else.

The gospel stands alone, complete, and sufficient. To pervert it is to destroy it.
