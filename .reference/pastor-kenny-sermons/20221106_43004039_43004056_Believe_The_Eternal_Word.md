## The Power of Words

Elon Musk recently acquired Twitter for $44 billion, a company that loses $4 million per day and faces intense criticism. Why bother? Because he believes words matter. He fought to preserve free speech, recognizing that words carry weight and shape our world—determining discourse, influencing elections, even threatening global conflict.

If Musk understands the power of a mere 28-character tweet, how much more powerful is the Word of God?

Today's passage shows us that the most transformative faith comes not from witnessing miracles, but from believing God's Word itself.

## The Word of Eternity (John 4:46-54)

### The Official's Desperation

Jesus returns to Cana in Galilee, the place where He first turned water into wine. A royal official from Capernaum—a town twenty-five miles away at sea level—comes desperately seeking Jesus. His son is dying, close to death, and the man's plea is urgent and simple: "Sir, come down before my child dies."

His desperation is palpable. He offers everything: travel provisions, food, payment—just come. He wants Jesus to physically travel to his home to perform a miracle and heal his son.

### Jesus' Unexpected Rebuke

But Jesus responds with what sounds like criticism: "Unless you people see signs and wonders, you will never believe."

This rebuke cuts at the heart of what Jesus observes. The crowds want Him for His wonders, not for who He is. They saw His signs in Jerusalem during the Passover Festival and welcomed Him—but Jesus knew such belief was shallow. As John 2:23-24 reveals, many believed in His name because of the signs, yet Jesus would not entrust Himself to them, knowing their hearts.

Jesus saw that people offer belief conditionally: "Show me the money, then I'll follow. Show me the results, then I'll follow." But true faith doesn't work that way.

### True Faith Believes the Word First

Yet something remarkable happens next. After His lecture against seeking signs, Jesus performs a miracle—but not in the way the official expected.

> "Go," Jesus replied, "your son will live."

The official doesn't see any sign. He doesn't witness any wonder. Instead, something far more significant occurs: **he takes Jesus at His word** (John 4:50). The Greek is explicit—the man believed *the word that Jesus said*. The word is *logos*.

The official then departs. He leaves without any guarantee, without seeing his son alive, without any visible confirmation. This is genuine faith. He trusts a promise about the future based solely on Jesus' spoken word. When he arrives home, he discovers his son was healed at precisely the seventh hour—the exact moment Jesus spoke His promise.

### What Faith Really Means

This reveals a profound truth about Christian faith. Hebrews 11:1 defines it: "Faith is confidence in what we hope for and assurance about what we do not see."

"Do not see" doesn't mean blind faith or faith without reason. Rather, it means you haven't yet seen the *result*. Faith operates in the present regarding what will happen in the future. We believe today; we see the fulfillment tomorrow.

In our culture, we want immediate gratification. We demand to see results before we commit. We want to enjoy the fruits of faith *before* we actually have faith. But Jesus teaches a different rhythm: follow first, and the blessings follow.

The Bible is full of future-oriented promises: "Follow me and you *will* be blessed." Not "be blessed first, then follow." Jesus asks us to believe His Word now, to trust His promises, to step out in faith without seeing the destination.

This is especially true for the greatest promise of Scripture—eternal life. That blessing comes *after* death. There's waiting involved. It requires faith that extends beyond what we can see or verify in this lifetime.

### The Credibility of God's Promise

But this faith isn't blind. It rests on solid ground. Jesus has already descended from heaven. He came down to earth for us. And most profoundly, just as the official's son was dying and needed healing, Jesus was about to die to secure our salvation. As John 12:31-32 states, He was lifted up from the earth to draw all people to Himself.

This is why we can trust God's Word, even when we don't see immediate results. The promise of eternal life is secured by the blood of Jesus. His sacrifice guarantees every word He speaks.

So when life brings trials, ups and downs, exams and sufferings, the difficulties of motherhood, the discouragement of work—we have solid ground beneath us. We'll be okay. If He is for us, who can be against us? We can depart in faith, trusting His Word, knowing that He keeps His promises.

## The Eternal Word Continues Working (John 4:39-42)

### Words Transform Through Testimony

Earlier in this passage, Jesus encounters the Samaritan woman at the well. After their conversation, she returns to her town with nothing but testimony: "He told me everything I ever did."

Her words are remarkably simple, yet remarkably powerful. John 4:39 says: "Many of the Samaritans from that town believed in him because of the woman's *testimony*"—literally, because of her *logos*, her words.

But the word keeps working. When the Samaritans come to Jesus and He stays with them two days, something happens: "And because of *his words* many more became believers" (4:41). Direct exposure to Jesus' teaching deepens and solidifies their faith.

Eventually they tell the woman: "We no longer believe just because of what you said; now we have heard for ourselves" (4:42). They didn't believe because of miracles or signs. They believed because of testimony—because of words—and those words led them directly to Jesus and His teaching.

### The Power of Truth

The power of Scripture doesn't come from special ink or magic. It comes from being *pure truth*. Truth transforms people. Words that are true do things. They are performative—they create reality and change hearts.

Perhaps our world's fascination with miracles reflects a loss of confidence in God's Word. We think it's easier to convince others through spectacular signs than through mere testimony. We feel more confident presenting something tangible than something rooted in the past, future, or invisible spiritual reality.

But the Samaritan woman's testimony was enough. Her few words pointed others to Jesus. And that's our calling.

## A Charge to Evangelism

If you have been given this powerful weapon—God's Word—wield it for the kingdom. Be alert to opportunities this week. When you share your testimony with others, you connect them directly to Jesus' Words. You invite them to encounter the truth that transforms hearts.

Because words are powerful. God's words especially so.

The eternal Word continues its work through your witness today.
