# Philippians 2:13-16

**Title:** Waiting with Purpose: Shining as Lights in a Dark World
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Our Season of Waiting

We all wait for something. Some of us wait for exam results or graduation. Others wait for a wedding day, a proposal, or a baby's arrival. Some wait to see our children grow up and make us proud. As Christians, we share a deeper wait—for the day of Christ's return.

This anticipation shapes everything Paul writes to the Philippians. He reminds us that waiting is not passive resignation but active, purposeful preparation for the greatest day of all.

## The Day We Long For

> For it is God who works in you to will and to act in order to fulfill his good purpose. Do everything without grumbling or arguing, so that you may become blameless and pure, "children of God without fault in a warped and crooked generation." Then you will shine among them like stars in the sky as you hold firmly to the word of life. And then I will be able to boast on the day of Christ that I did not run or labor in vain. (Philippians 2:13-16)

The "day of Christ" is the day of His return—judgment day, the day when all wrongs are made right. It's a day of glory and celebration, a great banquet where good triumphs over evil. We will receive our rewards, exams and the treadmill of life will finally end, and the church will be united with her bridegroom. It's when labor pains will cease and a new creation will be born. When we will be made whole.

In our darkest moments and deepest despair, this day shines like a distant light. We are getting closer. Hope compels us forward.

## The Challenge: We Are Not Built to Wait

Yet we are not naturally suited for waiting. Immorality thrives on impatience. We want instant gratification. We argue and grumble. We spend on trivial things rather than stewarding what matters. We say, "It's just two dollars. It's just a holiday."

The hard truth the Bible addresses is this: death, sickness, persecution, temptations, and distractions test our resolve. Like children on a long car ride, we grow restless and complain.

Consider the miracle of compound interest. If you start with a single penny and double it every day for thirty days, you end up with over ten million dollars. But most growth happens in the final days. If you cash out five days early, you miss nearly ninety percent of the gain. What a tragedy to wait patiently and then give up just before the breakthrough.

## The Antidote: Grumble No More

Paul's command is direct: **Do everything without grumbling or arguing.**

Don't complain. Don't murmur. Don't argue. This isn't burdensome legalism—it's the pathway to freedom and joy. Those who serve faithfully in thankless tasks—our photocopying teams, our cleanup crews—exemplify this spirit. They are shining lights.

Why does this matter? Because we are **blameless and pure** through what Christ has done for us. When we truly grasp this identity, we don't blame others, nor do we complain or grumble. Our actions naturally flow from our identity.

## We Shine Now, Not Just Then

Notice Paul's language carefully. In verse 15, he writes "you will shine among them like stars" with no future tense in the Greek. This isn't something we become later—it's who we are now, shining lights in a warped and crooked generation, even as we wait.

Waiting is not about idleness or twiddling our thumbs. **Waiting is preparation.** During this season, God is working in us—shaping our character, deepening our faith, and preparing us for what's coming. Friends are praying for us. The word of God simmers and stews within us. We grow.

## A Call to Commitment

As we look ahead to another year together, I encourage you to participate fully. Attend the carol service, join the January study series, celebrate our fifteen years together as a congregation. We don't know how many seasons of waiting remain before Christ returns.

But this I know: just as the majority of compound interest arrives in the final days, so the greatest blessing may come just when we think the wait is too long. Don't cash out now from exhaustion. Don't abandon the wait just before the breakthrough.

We hold firmly to the word of life—this promise that all will be made right. Let's wait another year together in faithful preparation, serving without complaint, shining as lights of hope.

And then suddenly, it will be here.
