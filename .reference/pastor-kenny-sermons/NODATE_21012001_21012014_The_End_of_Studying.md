# Ecclesiastes 12:1-14

**Title:** The End of Studying
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Opening Reflection

As we approach the final days of intensive studying, there's a palpable weariness setting in. The writer of Ecclesiastes—known throughout Scripture as "the Teacher"—captures this reality with striking honesty. He speaks directly to what many of us are experiencing right now.

## The Reality of Endless Books

> 12 Be warned, my son, of anything in addition to them. Of making many books there is no end, and much study wearies the body.

The Teacher makes two crucial observations about studying:

**First: There is no end to books.** You can never finish studying. At the beginning of the school year, you start with textbooks. By the end, you've accumulated folders of notes, exercise books, past papers, and worksheets—stacks that seem to reach the ceiling. Even after an exam, you walk out thinking you could have answered one more question, filled in one more equation. The material is endless, and there's always more to learn.

This speaks to a deeper human reality: we can never achieve complete knowledge or mastery. Even when someone achieves a near-perfect score—like the girl from a private school who earned 99.95 ATAR when perfect scores were still possible—it's still not enough. She actually sued the Department of Education over those last few points. There's an inherent dissatisfaction in the pursuit of knowledge through studying alone.

**Second: Studying is physically wearying.** Day after day, your body bears the cost. Your hands are smudged with pen marks. Your eyes ache from headaches caused by hours of reading. You contort your body into uncomfortable positions for six hours straight. You carry heavy textbooks from place to place. It's exhausting—both mentally and physically.

## The Hope: There Is an End

But the Teacher offers us hope in the midst of this weariness. He speaks of an end coming to studying—and this operates on two levels.

**First: The immediate end.** The studying season will finish. The misery will end. Yes, it's going to be tough for a little while longer, but you will learn what you can, take those exams, and it will be done. There is a finish line.

**Second: The ultimate end—there is a limit to studying.** And this is where the Teacher's wisdom deepens. Studying our textbooks can only yield a limited amount of knowledge. Life requires more. God's Word tells us:

> 13 Now all has been heard; here is the conclusion of the matter: Fear God and keep his commandments, for this is the duty of all mankind.
>
> 14 For God will bring every deed into judgment, including every hidden thing, whether it is good or evil.

## The Teacher's Conclusion

This is the crucial insight: **judgment comes not for what we know, but for what we do.** You could be brilliant in science and mathematics, understand the complexities of calculus and chemistry, but if you don't know God, you've missed the essential thing.

The Teacher calls us to fear God and keep His commandments—this is the foundation of true wisdom. Into our weariness, into our exhaustion from endless pursuit of knowledge, God sent His Son, Jesus, into the world. He came to reveal God's wisdom for how to live this life and the next. This knowledge is found in the Bible. And to our weary world, He says, "My yoke is easy and my burden is light" (Matthew 11:30).

## The Invitation

So as you continue to study, remember two truths: there is an end to it, and there is a limit to it. Academic knowledge has its place, but it's not ultimate. Perhaps take a break from the weariness and open a Bible instead. In God's Word, you'll find the wisdom that truly matters—not just for passing an exam, but for living a life that honors God and stands before Him in the day of judgment.
