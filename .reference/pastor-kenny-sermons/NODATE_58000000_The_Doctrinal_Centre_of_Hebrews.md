# Hebrews

**Title:** The Doctrinal Centre of Hebrews

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## Overview: What is Hebrews About?

Hebrews is a letter (or possibly a sermon) that presents Jesus with unprecedented theological depth and clarity. Several distinctive features characterize this epistle:

- **Comparative language throughout**: The constant refrain of "better," "more," and "superior" shapes how we understand Christ's person and work
- **Doctrine and application woven together**: Teaching flows naturally into exhortation, making this both intellectually rigorous and practically demanding
- **Christology at the center**: Jesus is presented as the High Priest, the Son of God, the prophetic voice, and the ultimate sacrifice

The theological heart of Hebrews can be approached from several angles:

- **The Sonship of Christ** (mentioned 12 times): This embraces both His eternal relationship as Son and His incarnational humanity. Christians are drawn into participation in this sonship.
- **The Supremacy of Christ**: Every "better" statement reinforces that Jesus surpasses all prior revelation and all competing claims.
- **The New Covenant**: Chapters 8-10 systematically show how Christ fulfills and supersedes the Mosaic covenant.
- **Christian life as pilgrimage**: Like Israel's wilderness journey, believers are called to press forward with perseverance and faith toward the promised rest.

## The High Priesthood of Christ (Chapters 3-10)

The capstone statement appears in Hebrews 8:1: "The main point of what we are saying is this: We do have such a high priest." This declaration crystallizes the entire argument.

The development of Christ's priesthood dominates the central section:
- **Chapters 1-2** lead into the theme
- **Chapters 3-10** develop it fully
- **Chapters 11-13** explore its consequences for Christian living

Priestly language saturates the epistle, showing us that Jesus fulfills what the Levitical priesthood could only foreshadow.

## Five Movements in Hebrews

### I. Jesus: The Better Revelation (Hebrews 1:1-2:4)

**The Reality**: Jesus is the complete and final revelation of God. There is no other word we need, no additional message beyond Him.

**The Contrast**: In the past, God spoke "in various times and in various ways" through the prophets to the fathers. But "in these last days He has spoken to us by His Son" (1:1-2). This is the climax of redemptive history.

**Why Jesus is the Better Revelation**:
- Jesus is "the exact representation of God's being" (1:3a)—not a copy, but the very expression of God's nature
- Jesus has "completed his work" through His finished redemption (1:3b)—He is the final word, the ultimate communication

**The Call to Listen**: This demands response. "We must pay the most careful attention to what we have heard" (2:1). The stakes are eternally high.

**A Practical Warning**: We live in an age of competing voices—charismatics, Mormons, Muslims, Jehovah's Witnesses, liberals, pagans—all offering alternative revelations and spiritual shortcuts. But God calls us to trust His Word as sufficient and final. The Bible is not a magical conduit to be used arbitrarily; it is the authoritative voice of God Himself. If we truly believe Jesus is God's final word, we will prioritize knowing Him through Scripture.

---

### II. Jesus: The Better Man (Hebrews 2:5-3:1)

**The Setup**: Psalm 8 speaks of humanity's intended glory—"what is man that you are mindful of him?" God created mankind to rule over creation. Yet we do not see this realized.

**The Reality**: We do not see humanity in charge of the world. Sin has fractured our dominion. We are weak, vulnerable, and subject to death.

**The Resolution**: "But we do see Jesus" (2:9). Here is the ultimate Man, the representative of redeemed humanity, ruling at God's right hand. Jesus is the Man who accomplishes what Adam failed to do.

**How Jesus Helps Us**:

1. **He frees us from eternal death** (2:10-17): Through His incarnation, suffering, and resurrection, Jesus became our High Priest who "is able to save completely those who come to God through him, because he always lives to intercede for them" (7:25). He has triumphed over death itself.

2. **He helps us now** (2:18): "Because he himself suffered when he was tempted, he is able to help those who are being tempted." Jesus knows us intimately. He is not distant from our struggles. He loves us personally and stands ready to strengthen us in our present battles.

This is the practical heart of the gospel: Jesus knows your pain, understands your temptation, and genuinely wants to help—if only you will let Him.

---

### III. Jesus: The Better Rest (Hebrews 3:2-4:11)

**The Warning from History**: Israel experienced God's deliverance from Egypt, yet they never entered God's rest. Why? Because they hardened their hearts and refused to believe (3:7-19). They were so close to the promise, yet unbelief kept them out.

**The Application for Us**:
- Do not harden your hearts like they did (3:12)
- Encourage one another daily while it is still "today," while the opportunity remains (3:13-14)

**What is Rest?**

Rest is not merely the Levitical Sabbath—that was only a shadow. God declares, "This is not their rest but my rest" (4:3b-5). The true rest is not in the past, completed in creation. Rather, it is future and coming—the eternal Sabbath of God's completed redemption, when all labor ends and we dwell in perfect peace with our Creator.

**The Exhortation**: "Let us, therefore, make every effort to enter that rest" (4:11). Take your eyes off the present difficulties. Keep your gaze fixed on Jesus and the promise of His rest.

---

### IV. Jesus: The Better Sacrifice (Hebrews 8:13-9:15; 10:11-25)

**The Old System**: The Levitical priesthood, temple, and sacrificial system were designed by God but always temporary. They were prototypes—shadows pointing to something greater (10:1-4). God Himself declared this system "obsolete" (8:13). It was never meant to work permanently.

**The New System**: Jesus is the perfect Priest, the true Temple, and the once-for-all Sacrifice. When Jesus died, Mark tells us that "the curtain of the temple was torn in two from top to bottom" (Mark 15:37-39). The barrier between God and humanity was removed. The old system's work was finished.

**Our Response**: We do not repeat the old system or create modern substitutes. We do not build new temples or institute new sacrificial rituals. Instead, we trust completely in Jesus' finished work.

**How We Live in Light of the Better Sacrifice**:
- **Faith in God** (10:19-23): "Let us draw near to God with a sincere heart and with the full assurance that faith brings" (10:22)
- **Love for one another** (10:24): "And let us consider how we may spur one another on toward love and good deeds"
- **Regular gathering and encouragement** (10:25): "Not giving up meeting together, as some are in the habit of doing, but encouraging one another"

The true purpose of the church is not religious ceremony but genuine devotion to Jesus, authentic community, sacrificial love, and mutual encouragement.

---

### V. Jesus: The Better Assurance (Hebrews 10:26-11:2; 12:1-13)

**The Gravity of the Warning**: Deliberate rejection of the Son of God carries serious consequences (10:26-31). God takes rebellion against His grace with utmost seriousness.

**Yet Hope Remains for the Faithful**: Those who persevere in faith share the same experience as the heroes of the faith throughout history (11:1ff). Abel, Enoch, Noah, Abraham, Moses—all walked by faith, and their faith sustained them through impossible circumstances.

**Sonship Brings Discipline**: Christian perseverance is not merely our own effort. God disciplines His children, as a father corrects sons he loves (12:5-13). "The Lord disciplines the one he loves, and he chastens everyone he accepts as his son" (12:6). This discipline, though painful, proves our adoption into God's family and produces righteousness in us.

**Real Assurance**: The warnings in Hebrews are not there to undermine our confidence but to strengthen it. They are the very instruments God uses to preserve us. A genuine believer takes these warnings seriously and allows them to deepen his commitment to persevere in faith.

**The Dual Reality**:
- God's preservation of His people is absolutely certain
- Our perseverance is genuinely required and matters eternally
- These are not contradictory but complementary truths

We can have full assurance of salvation because Jesus is the High Priest who prays for us, and we are called to walk faithfully with Him to the end.

---

## Conclusion

Hebrews reveals Jesus as supremely better—better revelation, better representation of humanity, better promise of rest, better sacrifice, better ground of assurance. Every doctrine serves one purpose: to establish the superiority and sufficiency of Christ. In Him, we have everything we need for salvation, perseverance, and eternal hope.
