# 1 Peter 1:3-2:3

**Title:** Birth Rights

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Christians Are Born Into Living Hope

### An Inheritance (v. 3-4)

Christians possess a living hope through the resurrection of Jesus Christ. This hope is not wishful thinking but a confident expectation rooted in Christ's victory over death. With this hope comes an inheritance—a concept deeply woven through Scripture.

The Old Testament repeatedly emphasizes inheritance as God's covenant gift to His people. In Genesis, the promised land was Abraham's inheritance. In Exodus, God claimed Israel as His own inheritance. Even the Levites, who received no land, were told that God Himself was their inheritance. This inheritance theme carries forward to us: we are born into something precious and enduring that cannot be destroyed or fade away.

### Protected by God's Power (v. 4-8)

Our inheritance is not fragile or temporary. It is "kept in heaven for you" (v. 4)—protected by God's power itself. Nothing on earth can touch it. While we live as aliens in this world, vulnerable and often suffering, our true treasure is secure in God's hands.

This protection extends to us personally. We are "shielded by God's power through faith" (v. 5). Our faith itself is strengthened through trials. When we endure hardship, our trust in God deepens, and we discover that His faithfulness is real and proven.

### A Living Hope (v. 9)

The culmination of our inheritance is salvation itself—the full realization of redemption when Christ returns. This is not abstract theology but the goal toward which our entire Christian life moves.

## Christians Are Born From the Living Word

### The Word in Scripture (v. 23-25)

We are "born again...through the living and enduring word of God" (v. 23). This is not mere information or human wisdom. God's word is alive and active, accomplishing what He purposes.

### The Word in the Past (v. 10-12)

The Old Testament prophets spoke of grace coming to us. They didn't fully understand their own words—they were revealing mysteries about Christ's suffering and the glory that would follow. Their words, spoken centuries before Christ came, testify to the reliability and foresight of God's revelation.

### The Word in the Present (v. 18-20)

We were "redeemed...with the precious blood of Christ, a lamb without blemish or defect" (v. 18-19). This redemption was not an afterthought. Christ was "chosen before the creation of the world" (v. 20). God's plan of salvation was established before time itself began.

### The Word in the Future (v. 24-25)

"The grass withers and the flowers fall, but the word of the Lord endures forever" (v. 24-25). Everything temporary will fade—our physical bodies, earthly possessions, human kingdoms. But God's word remains. This is the word that gives us new birth and sustains us through all seasons.

## Living as Aliens in God's Kingdom

### A New Identity (2:1-3)

Having been born into this living hope through God's living word, we now live with a fundamentally different identity. We are no longer merely shaped by our cultural surroundings or earthly citizenship. We are aliens and strangers in this world, our true allegiance belonging to God's kingdom.

This identity transforms how we live. We shed the old ways—malice, deceit, hypocrisy, envy, slander (v. 1)—and instead grow in our desire for God's word, like newborns craving milk (v. 2-3). Our citizenship is heavenly. Our inheritance is secure. Our word is eternal. We live accordingly.
