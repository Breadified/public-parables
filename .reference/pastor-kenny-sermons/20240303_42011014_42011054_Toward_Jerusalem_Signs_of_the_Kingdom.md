## 1. A House Divided (11:14-23)

### The Miracle and the Opposition

> Jesus was driving out a demon that was mute. When the demon left, the man who had been mute spoke, and the crowd was amazed.

Interestingly, both the demon and the man are described as mute. Perhaps a mute demon caused the man's muteness. The demon wanted to possess and rule this person, causing a disability that was not common in society. When Jesus cast out the demon, the man could speak again, and the crowds were astonished.

Luke presents this as one of Scripture's briefest miracle accounts—not dwelling on the supernatural itself, but on its significance: demonic forces are being defeated.

### Jesus Responds to False Accusations

> Some of them said, "By Beelzebul, the prince of demons, he is driving out demons." Others tested him by asking for a sign from heaven.

"Some of them"—as we'll discover, these are Pharisees and teachers of the law. They accused Jesus of casting out demons only because he was the prince of demons, leading all the other demons. "Beelzebul" is a variant name for Satan himself, the king of demons.

**But this accusation is illogical.**

> Jesus knew their thoughts and said to them: "Any kingdom divided against itself will be ruined, and a house divided against itself will fall. If Satan is divided against himself, how can his kingdom stand? I say this because you claim that I drive out demons by Beelzebul."

Jesus's logic is simple: no leader would sabotage his own forces. Why would the prince of demons command his followers to retreat and fail? A divided house collapses. A divided kingdom falls. It's like demolishing your own walls, cutting off your own supplies, shooting your own soldiers. The kingdom would crumble.

The Pharisees' argument was fundamentally inconsistent.

### Jesus's Second Argument: Inconsistency

> "If I drive out demons by Beelzebul, by whom do your followers drive them out? So then, they will be your judges."

Apparently, exorcism was common in those days. Some Pharisees were doing the same thing Jesus was doing. Later, Acts 19 describes Jewish exorcists attempting this:

> Some Jews who went around driving out evil spirits tried to invoke the name of the Lord Jesus over those who were demon-possessed. They would say, "In the name of Jesus, whom Paul preaches, I command you to come out." Seven sons of Sceva, a Jewish chief priest, were doing this.

If other Jewish exorcists were casting out demons, were they also servants of Beelzebul? The Pharisees seemed to attack only Jesus, not these other practitioners. They didn't actually believe their own accusation—it was merely an excuse to oppose him.

### The Kingdom Has Come

> "But if I drive out demons by the finger of God, then the kingdom of God has come to you."

This miracle isn't a sign that Jesus is the prince of demons—it's a sign that he is the King. God himself has come to rescue his people.

The prophet Isaiah predicted this seven hundred years before Jesus came:

> "Say to those with fearful hearts: 'Be strong, do not fear; your God will come, he will come with vengeance; with divine retribution he will come to save you. Then will the eyes of the blind be opened and the ears of the deaf unstopped. Then will the lame leap like a deer, and the mute tongue shout for joy.'"

The prayers of Jesus and his disciples were being answered. The kingdom was arriving. The mute man had already begun to speak!

When you see the mute speaking, the blind seeing, the lame leaping like deer—this means God is here. Jesus has come.

### The Finger of God

The phrase "finger of God" appears in Exodus 8:19, in the account of the plagues on Egypt:

> "The magicians said to Pharaoh, 'This is the finger of God.' But Pharaoh's heart was hard and he would not listen."

Pharaoh's magicians admitted the plagues were God's finger—pointing to God's effortless power over nature. "Finger" means doing something with ease. If I can lift a brick with just my finger, it means either the brick is light or I am very strong. Jesus is extremely strong.

### The Stronger Man Overpowers

> "When a strong man, fully armed, guards his own house, his possessions are safe. But when someone stronger attacks and overpowers him, he takes away the armor in which the man trusted and divides up his plunder."

The strong, fully-armed man is Satan. His possessions are the people he controls through demonic possession. But then someone stronger arrives—Jesus overpowers him, strips away his armor, making him weak, and divides his plunder with one finger. Jesus is distributing Satan's household.

### Two Sides, No Neutrality

> "Whoever is not with me is against me, and whoever does not gather with me scatters."

This is war. We are called to choose a side. There is no neutral position. "Whoever is not with me is against me."

This may sound harsh. What about neutrals? What about civilians?

Yes, there are neutral people, agnostics, and non-combatants. We should befriend them. But ultimately, war transforms everyone into soldiers. In any conflict, the final logic is that everyone becomes part of one side or the other.

In the spiritual realm, some belong to Jesus. The rest belong to Beelzebul.

**Application:** To whom do you belong? If you are neutral, you will eventually have to choose. I hope you choose Jesus. If you want to choose Jesus today, check the first box on your response card.

## 2. The Empty House (11:24-26, 37-41)

### The Danger of Surface Cleansing

But choosing Jesus once isn't enough. It requires true commitment.

> "When an impure spirit comes out of a person, it goes through arid places seeking rest and does not find it. Then it says, 'I will return to the house I left.' When it arrives, it finds the house swept clean and put in order."

The impure spirit that left the mute man wanders through "arid places"—desert regions, places without water or people. Finding no suitable habitation, it returns to its original home.

When the demon first left, it had left the house in shambles. The man couldn't speak; he was sick. The furniture was broken, walls had holes, doors hung on a single hinge, mold was everywhere. But then Jesus came, cast out the demon, and the house was swept clean—"swept" (saroi) in Greek—and put in order.

"Put in order" comes from the Greek word cosmos, which we use today to mean the universe or the orderly arrangement of things. The mute man was free. He could speak. His disability had vanished. His life was restored. He was whole again.

**But there's a problem.**

> "Then it goes and takes with it seven other spirits more wicked than itself, and they go in and live there. And the final condition of that person is worse than the first."

Why does a swept, ordered house attract more unclean spirits?

The word "ordered" can also mean "decorated" or "arranged cosmetically." Think of cosmetics—surface appearance. The house received only surface cleaning.

**The house was swept, but this was merely superficial.**

Imagine someone selling a house: cheap paint over mold, the cheapest replacement handles, a spray-painted roof. In photos it looks fine, but within six months the hardware falls apart, the mold returns, the paint peels. Just cosmetic renovation. Beautiful on the outside, nothing substantial within.

This describes someone who superficially follows Jesus. Perhaps they call themselves Christian, do some religious things, accept certain values, abandon certain habits—but that's all. It's cosmetic treatment.

**Don't misunderstand me. I'm not saying you shouldn't call yourself Christian if you want to, or that considering yourself Christian is bad, or that we're all at different stages of maturity—we all begin immature. But I am saying that surface change is insufficient. We must pursue maturity. We must ensure that behind our desire to change stands a genuine love for Jesus.**

Because if Jesus isn't in the house, the house remains empty. Perhaps cleaner, but the evil spirits will eventually find the house again. Delighted that it's been swept for them, they bring more friends. Seven spirits move in. The house is destroyed.

God condemns such people.

### The Example of the Pharisees

> Jesus had finished speaking, and a Pharisee invited him to eat with him; so he went in and reclined at the table. But the Pharisee, noticing that Jesus did not first wash before the meal, was surprised.

Then Jesus said: "Now then, you Pharisees clean the outside of the cup and dish, but inside you are full of greed and wickedness. You foolish people! Did not the one who made the outside make the inside? But give what is inside the dish to the poor, and everything will be clean for you."

The Pharisees were a group of Jewish leaders concerned with how they appeared outwardly rather than what they were like inwardly. They washed cups and plates, but didn't clean the inside. They looked clean on the outside—what people could see—but inside was unclean. Even though God created both the external and internal, God cares about both.

Our society is full of Pharisees, especially in the West, where there's a strong desire to appear moral while being immoral simultaneously. A society of virtue signaling. This is peculiar to Western society. Other nations simply do what suits them—Japan protects its culture without large-scale immigration, without pretending diversity is their strength. Afghanistan has its views on women and doesn't hide them. But we have a desire to pretend we're doing good while doing wrong.

We say murder is wrong, then condemn Israel for killing children in Gaza while happily aborting babies in the womb and euthanizing our elderly. We claim to believe in marriage while destroying it through no-fault divorce and redefinition. We say we believe in women's equality but exempt ourselves from it in sports and bathrooms.

That's because we're a post-Christian nation. We have the ideals, the shell, the external form of our Christian past, but we've lost the biblical substance, conviction, and wisdom. We're not good. We just look good.

Having a house that looks nice but is empty is insufficient. We must fill it.

## 3. A House Filled with Light (11:27-36)

### Hearing and Obeying

> "As Jesus was saying these things, a woman in the crowd called out, 'Blessed is the mother who gave you birth and nursed you.' But he replied, 'Blessed rather are those who hear the word of God and obey it.'"

The mute man couldn't merely hear God's word. He must also obey it. He needs to repent.

> "As the crowds increased, Jesus said, 'This is a wicked generation. It asks for a sign, but none will be given it except the sign of Jonah.'"

Earlier in verse 16, the crowd had demanded a sign. They saw it. The mute man spoke.

Jesus tells two groups of people who listened and obeyed:

> "For as Jonah was a sign to the Ninevites, so also will the Son of Man be to this generation. The Queen of the South will rise at the judgment with the people of this generation and condemn them; for she came from the ends of the earth to listen to Solomon's wisdom, and now something greater than Solomon is here. The men of Nineveh will stand up at the judgment with this generation and condemn it; for they repented at the preaching of Jonah, and now something greater than Jonah is here."

The ancient Queen of the South—perhaps from what is now Ethiopia or Yemen—visited Solomon and listened to his wisdom. The ancient Ninevites—in what is now Syria—received Jonah's message and obeyed. Now someone greater than Jonah, greater than Solomon, is here. Jesus is present. The stronger man.

We have even greater reason to listen and obey.

### Filled with Light

> "No one lights a lamp and puts it in a place where it will be hidden, or under a bowl. Instead they put it on its stand, so that those who come in can see the light. Your eye is the lamp of your body. When your eyes are healthy, your whole body also is full of light. But when they are unhealthy, your body also is full of darkness. See to it, then, that the light within you is not darkness. Therefore, if your whole body is full of light, and no part of it dark, it will be just as when a lamp shines its light on you."

Following the house metaphor, a person filled with light is a house filled with light. A house fully occupied.

Your house full of chips and soda—your children will be overweight. Your house full of vegetables, meat, milk, and fruit—your children will eat well. Your house full of televisions, devices, game consoles, toys—you'll have distracted children. Your house full of books, pencils, pens, and paper—you'll have inquisitive children.

Your house filled with Jesus's light, filled with God's word, where residents listen and obey—you and your family will be healthy. There's no threat of demons returning.
