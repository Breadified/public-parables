# Galatians 5:16-23

**Title:** Life by the Spirit
**Date:** 10 July 2007
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## Introduction: What Does Spirituality Really Look Like?

When we think of spiritual people, various images come to mind—the Dalai Lama, the Pope, Mother Teresa, monks in desert monasteries. In Christian circles, we might point to worship leaders with their eyes closed and hands raised, or someone who prays for half an hour on their knees, or those who fast regularly.

There's an enormous demand for spirituality in our culture. But before we pursue it, we must ask: **What does the Bible mean by spiritual life? What does it truly mean to live by the Spirit?**

This is the crucial question Paul addresses in Galatians 5:16-26.

---

## 1. Live by the Spirit: Freedom From Sin

In the previous chapters, Paul explained how Jesus completed the law and freed us from its slavery. We are no longer trying to earn God's approval through our own efforts—an impossible task in any case. But this freedom isn't freedom to do nothing or to live lawlessly. Rather, it's freedom *for* something: freedom to love, freedom to be other-centered.

This is what the word "so" in verse 16 connects to:

> **Galatians 5:16-18** — So I say, live by the Spirit, and you will not gratify the desires of the sinful nature. For the sinful nature desires what is contrary to the Spirit, and the Spirit what is contrary to the sinful nature. They are in conflict with each other, so that you do not do what you want. But if you are led by the Spirit, you are not under law.

The spiritual life is the necessary consequence of being justified. We move from law to Spirit. This passage establishes a contrast: living by the Spirit versus gratifying sinful desires, being led by the Spirit versus being under law.

**What spiritual living is not:** It's not mysticism. It's not achieving emotional highs or feelings of oneness with something greater. It's not about looking spiritual through meditation, closed eyes, and ethereal expressions. These things may be emotionally real and moving, but they aren't necessarily biblical spirituality.

We can't simply slap a "Christian" label on something and make it biblical. A beautiful moment at a funeral isn't spirituality. An emotionally stirring song isn't spirituality. A Christian mug isn't more spiritual than a regular mug—that's just marketing.

**What spiritual living actually is:** It's the antithesis of sin. It's living according to the way of the Spirit rather than according to sinful desires. It's freedom from the tyranny of sin, not freedom to indulge it.

Paul uses the phrase "by the Spirit," but a clearer translation might be "**according to** the Spirit"—God's way, the sin-free way. Biblical spirituality is an alternate way of life entirely. If you're a Christian, you've chosen to live according to the Spirit's desires, according to what pleases God who is Spirit.

## 2. The Negative Examples: What We Must Leave Behind

> **Galatians 5:19-21** — The acts of the sinful nature are obvious: sexual immorality, impurity and debauchery; idolatry and witchcraft; hatred, discord, jealousy, fits of rage, selfish ambition, dissensions, factions and envy; drunkenness, orgies, and the like. I warn you, as I did before, that those who live like this will not inherit the kingdom of God.

Paul groups these sins into four categories—I call them the four S's:

**1. Bad Sex:** Sexual immorality, impurity, debauchery
- This includes sleeping around, multiple partners, and any sexual relationship that doesn't honor both people's dignity and commitment.

**2. Superstition:** Idolatry and witchcraft
- Bowing to false gods and relying on curses or magic.

**3. Spite:** Hatred, discord, jealousy, fits of rage, selfish ambition, dissensions, factions, envy
- These stem from prioritizing our own interests at the expense of others.

**4. Shortcuts to Self-Gratification:** Drunkenness, orgies
- Self-indulgent living as if we're the only ones who matter.

### Bringing These to Our Context

**Bad Sex:** Young people especially need to hear this. Dating practices that blur boundaries, holding hands that becomes emotional intimacy before you're ready for marriage—these complicate things unnecessarily. Why risk breaking friendships and emotional devastation if you're not ready to commit? There's an appropriate time to move from brother-and-sister relationships, but why rush it?

**Superstition:** We're too sophisticated to bow to ceramic idols, but superstition takes subtler forms in our lives:
- Lucky charms and superstitious exam rituals ("I won't do well unless I pray before the test")
- Quiet times treated as magical guarantees of a good day
- The language around church—"entering God's presence" on Sunday—as if God is more present then than in the toilet on Tuesday morning

These are spiritual slavery dressed up as spirituality. Jesus on the cross brought us into God's presence permanently. A song leader can't manufacture it.

**Spite:** For guys, this often shows up as a pecking order—bagging each other out to establish dominance. For girls, it's gossip—sharing stories about others so people will like you at their expense. Before you gossip, ask: "Would this person be happy if she found out?" If not, stop talking about it.

**Shortcuts to Self-Gratification:** Why go to nightclubs? Often, guys go for girls and girls go for guys, with alcohol as the excuse for self-indulgence and bad sexual decisions. Once you're there with your friends all drinking, you can't simply choose not to drink. You've yoked yourself to peer pressure and temptation. Why start?

## 3. The Positive Examples: What We Become Instead

> **Galatians 5:22-26** — But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness and self-control. Against such things there is no law. Those who belong to Christ Jesus have crucified the sinful nature with its passions and desires. Since we live by the Spirit, let us keep in step with the Spirit. Let us not become conceited, provoking and envying each other.

Rather than leaving us in despair, Paul presents the positive alternative—the fruit of the Spirit:

**Instead of bad sex: Faithfulness**
- Faithfulness means I'm committed to you for my whole life, which is why intimacy belongs only in marriage. If you're not ready to commit your whole life, why start the intimacy? It's not exploiting each other or keeping a backup plan.

**Instead of spite: Peace and patience**
- I don't have to solve the world's problems by gossiping about everyone's faults. God is King. When things go wrong or seem unfair, I don't have to react in anger or overreach. Peace means looking out for one another, being friends because I care about you—not because you help my self-esteem. It means welcoming newcomers because they need help, not because it grows my church.

**Instead of superstition: Joy**
- Freedom from the tyranny of fear and control. True freedom.

**Instead of shortcuts to self-gratification: Self-control**
- Delayed gratification. I don't have to have it today; tomorrow is fine. And whatever I miss in this life, the next life will supply.

The spiritual life isn't about multiplying gimmicks, mysticism, emotions, or Christian trinkets. **It's about doing away with sin.** You can pray with fervent tears and quote the Bible feverishly, but if you still indulge in hatred, illegitimate pleasures, and superstition, what's the point? The measure of your spirituality is whether you're abandoning sin and loving as you've been freed to do.

## 4. The Now and Not Yet: A Realistic Perspective

Here's the tension: If being saved leads to good works, does failing to live perfectly mean I've fallen out of favor with Christ?

No. The relationship between salvation and good works is **asymmetrical**: Being saved leads to good works, but good works don't lead to being saved.

### Not Yet: The Reality of Ongoing Sin

We live in the tension of "now and not yet." Sin is still a reality because we haven't yet been fully transformed. Even Paul admits this struggle:

> **Romans 7:14-25** — We know that the law is spiritual; but I am unspiritual, sold as a slave to sin. I do not understand what I do. For what I want to do I do not do, but what I hate I do... For what I do is not the good I want to do; no, the evil I do not want to do—this I keep on doing... What a wretched man I am! Who will rescue me from this body of death? Thanks be to God—through Jesus Christ our Lord!

The struggle is real. Sin hasn't been eradicated; sinlessness isn't yet. So don't be alarmed if you're not changing as quickly as you'd like, or if you see brothers and sisters struggling.

### Now: The Reality of Forgiveness

But here's what's true *now*:

- We are no longer under law.
- We are no longer condemned.
- Our sins don't count against us.

God's forgiveness isn't mystical or magical—it's according to a way of life that flows from grace. Through Jesus Christ, you can sin boldly, knowing you're forgiven. (Though I wouldn't recommend treating God like an idiot.)

**The miracle isn't that we become perfect overnight; it's that we don't *want* to sin anymore.** We feel more guilt, not less, because we're now motivated by the Spirit's desires, not by fear of judgment.

### Illustration

Think of it like making a sports team. The day before I was selected for the volleyball team and the day after—nothing had physically changed about me. I was the same poor player. But everything had changed: my status, my motivation, my identity. I wanted to be better now. I wanted to leave recreational sports behind.

Similarly, the moment you believe in Christ, your title changes, your identity shifts, and your motivation transforms—even if you're still the same person working through the same struggles.

---

## Conclusion: Your New Identity

Thank God He doesn't judge us on the basis of our daily progress chart! One day, in the new creation, we will be truly, physically transformed. Our bodies will be raised and remade. Then, yes, sinlessness will be complete.

Until then, here's what you need to know:

**If you're struggling:** Good. That means you care about changing. Keep at it, seek help, remain confident that you'll reach the end. Remember: you are forgiven by the blood of Christ who loves you.

**If you're looking at others who struggle:** Be generous. Thank God they're struggling rather than sinning in ignorance. Look to your own life. We belong to a people who want to change, who want to abandon sin and love.

**For today:** You're still the same Kenny—the same person you were before and after becoming a Christian. But one massive difference exists: God has forgiven you through Jesus, the ultimate spiritual man who never sinned and loved unto death.

This isn't a mystical change. It's like a marriage, an adoption, a prisoner being set free. Nothing internal has changed about you, but something external has—**someone views you differently and has given you freedom.** And that freedom changes everything.

> **Galatians 5:16** — So I say, live by the Spirit, and you will not gratify the desires of the sinful nature.

You follow the way of the Spirit now. That's your identity. You love because that's what you've been freed *for*.
