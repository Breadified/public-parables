# Luke 1:57-80

**Title:** The Birth of John the Baptist and Zechariah's Prophecy
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Birth of John and the Naming

Elizabeth's time came, and she gave birth to a son. Her neighbors and relatives rejoiced with her, knowing the Lord had shown her great mercy. On the eighth day, when they came to circumcise the child, everyone assumed he would be named after his father, Zechariah. But Elizabeth spoke up firmly: "No! He is to be called John."

The relatives were puzzled. They protested that no one in their family bore that name. So they turned to Zechariah, making signs to him to ask what he wished to name the child. He called for a writing tablet and, to everyone's amazement, wrote: "His name is John." In that very moment, Zechariah's mouth was opened and his tongue was loosed. He began to speak, praising God.

This event filled the neighbors with awe. Throughout the hill country of Judea, people talked about what had happened. Everyone wondered, asking one another, "What then is this child going to be?" They sensed that the Lord's hand rested upon him.

## Zechariah's Prophecy: The Benedictus

Filled with the Holy Spirit, Zechariah prophesied over his son. His words, known as the Benedictus (the Song of Zechariah), reveal the heart of God's redemptive plan:

> "Praise be to the Lord, the God of Israel, because he has come and has redeemed his people. He has raised up a horn of salvation for us in the house of his servant David (as he said through his holy prophets of long ago), salvation from our enemies and from the hand of all who hate us—to show mercy to our fathers and to remember his holy covenant, the oath he swore to our father Abraham: to rescue us from the hand of our enemies, and to enable us to serve him without fear in holiness and righteousness before him all our days."

## John's Role: Forerunner and Prophet

Zechariah then spoke directly to his son:

> "And you, my child, will be called a prophet of the Most High; for you will go on before the Lord to prepare the way for him, to give his people the knowledge of salvation through the forgiveness of their sins, because of the tender mercy of our God, by which the rising sun will come to us from heaven to shine on those living in darkness and in the shadow of death, to guide our feet into the path of peace."

John's calling was to prepare the way for the Lord—to give God's people the knowledge of salvation through forgiveness of sins. He would be the forerunner, the prophet of the Most High.

## John's Preparation

The child grew and became strong in spirit. He lived in the desert until he appeared publicly to Israel, a man set apart and prepared by God for his unique role in salvation history.
