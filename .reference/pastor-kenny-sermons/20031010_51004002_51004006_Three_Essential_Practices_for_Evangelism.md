# Colossians 4:2-6

**Title:** Three Essential Practices for Evangelism
**Date:** October 10, 2003
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction

Evangelism is simply telling people about Jesus—proclaiming the gospel to those who haven't yet heard it. As we study these verses, Paul gives us three practical foundations for sharing our faith effectively.

## 1. Watchful Prayer (verses 2-4)

> Devote yourselves to prayer, being watchful and thankful.

The foundation of all evangelism is watchful prayer. Prayer represents our dependence upon God expressed through words. When we pray, we acknowledge His sovereignty and His personal presence in our lives. We admit that we rely on Him for everything.

**What does "watchful prayer" look like?**

In Colossians 1:3-12, Paul describes a church that practices this. The Colossians heard the gospel, understood it, and lived it out through their love for one another—love expressed in the Spirit. They had grasped God's grace in its fullness.

However, by chapter 2, we see the danger: the Colossians were forgetting to remain watchful. False teachers were introducing hollow philosophies, trying to convince them that Christ alone wasn't sufficient. Paul reminded them that Christ is the head over all powers and authorities, having disarmed them through the cross. We are raised with Christ and complete in Him.

**The Application for Us**

We must watch our own lives carefully. Don't be deceived about what you're relying on. Keep your eyes fixed on Jesus. Be watchful of what's happening around you—the philosophies and systems vying for your allegiance. And as Paul requests, pray for opportunities to share your faith and watch for them when they come.

## 2. Wise Opportunities (verse 5)

> Be wise in the way you act toward outsiders; make the most of every opportunity.

"Outsiders" refers to those outside the faith. The key here is *character*—our behavior and the consistency of our lives. When we demonstrate wisdom and integrity in our actions, opportunities naturally emerge.

Consider this: If you refuse to use pirated software or decline an offered bootleg CD, your friends may initially think you're foolish. But in their better moments, they'll recognize your character. They'll see you have no double standards. When they later ask you why you live differently, they'll already know from your other actions that you'll give them an honest answer.

Don't waste these moments. Make the most of every opportunity to be different—to live out what you believe. Your attitude toward teachers, toward the opposite sex, toward your parents, your refusal to join in mocking others—these all communicate something powerful about what matters to you.

## 3. Witty Conversations (verse 6)

> Let your conversation be always full of grace, seasoned with salt, so that you may know how to answer everyone.

The third practice is grace-filled conversation. Not everyone is gifted as an evangelist in the traditional sense—standing before strangers and explicitly preaching the gospel. But we can all have genuine conversations.

When Paul says "witty," he doesn't mean you need to be hilariously funny or talk constantly. Rather, your speech should be naturally seasoned—memorable, substantive, engaging. It's perfectly natural for all of us to steer conversations toward what we know best, what we're passionate about, what makes us tick. If Christ is truly central to our lives, this will naturally come through in our conversations.

**Common Barriers to These Conversations**

Many of us struggle with fear of rejection or shyness. We often don't think deeply about what makes our non-Christian friends tick—their interests, their struggles, their questions. And we miss opportunities by not genuinely engaging in conversation with them in natural settings like lunch or after class.

Consider this: Christians should be the most meaningfully sociable people. Not superficially friendly, but genuinely present and interested in others. Why not make it a goal to have real conversations with non-Christian friends? Watch for natural openings where you might invite them to church or simply share what matters to you.

## Summary

Three essential practices for evangelism:

1. **Watchful Prayer** — Depend on Jesus; remain alert to opportunities
2. **Wise Opportunities** — Let your character and consistency open doors
3. **Witty Conversations** — Speak with grace; let your passion for Christ naturally emerge
