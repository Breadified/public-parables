# Ephesians 6:10-20

**Title:** Spiritual Warfare and the Armor of God  
**Date:** 14 June 2015  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Reality of Spiritual Warfare

Our culture is fascinated with spiritual conflict. Horror shows about demons and vampires—from *Twilight* to *Buffy the Vampire Slayer*—captivate audiences, yet they often miss the deeper reality that Scripture reveals.

Last week we read Revelation 12, which speaks of Satan's rage unleashed on earth. There is genuine spiritual warfare happening, and for many, this truth is terrifying—not in the way a spider attack is scary, but in the way entering a room full of invisible webs is scary. We cannot see it. It's like terrorism or pandemic disease: a hidden threat that can strike anywhere, anytime.

Even among Christians, this fear manifests in troubling ways. Some pray over bridges to cleanse spiritual territories. Others believe depression, illness, or epilepsy are forms of demon possession. Some wonder if Christians themselves can be demonized and need deliverance rituals. The anxiety is real: something invisible is waging war against me, my family, and all I hold dear. I cannot see the enemy.

## Paul's Answer: Put On Christ (Ephesians 6:10-16)

The Apostle Paul, writing from prison where he suffered for Jesus, gives us clear instruction in two parts:

1. **Put on Christ**
2. **Stand firm**

### The Armor is Jesus Himself

Paul repeats the command twice (verses 10 and 13): "Put on the full armor of God."

He then describes this armor in verses 14-16:

> Stand firm then, with the belt of truth buckled around your waist, with the breastplate of righteousness in place, and with your feet fitted with the readiness that comes from the gospel of peace. In addition to all this, take up the shield of faith, with which you can extinguish all the flaming arrows of the evil one.

Don't get caught up in the metaphorical details—why truth is a belt rather than a shield, or why faith isn't righteousness instead. The genius of Paul's imagery comes from the Old Testament prophecies about the Messiah, the Divine Warrior who would come to defeat evil and judge the earth.

Consider these prophecies about Jesus:

- **Belt of truth:** "Righteousness will be his belt and faithfulness the sash around his waist" (Isaiah 11:5)
- **Breastplate of righteousness:** "He puts on righteousness as his breastplate" (Isaiah 59:17)
- **Feet fitted with the gospel of peace:** "How beautiful are the feet of those who bring good news, who proclaim peace, who proclaim salvation" (Isaiah 52:7)
- **Helmet of salvation:** "He puts on the helmet of salvation on his head" (Isaiah 59:17)
- **Sword of the Spirit:** "He will strike the earth with the rod of his mouth; with the breath of his lips he will slay the wicked" (Isaiah 11:4)
- **Shield of faith:** "Like birds hovering overhead, the LORD Almighty will shield Jerusalem; he will shield it and deliver it, he will 'pass over' it and will rescue it" (Isaiah 35:1)

Paul is not telling us to wear literal armor or engage in spiritual cosplay. **He is telling us to put on Jesus—the Divine Warrior himself.** And putting on Jesus is simply another way of saying: **Be Christian.**

### The Themes Throughout Ephesians

These pieces of armor aren't arbitrary. They echo Paul's teaching throughout Ephesians:

- **Belt of truth:** Know and speak the truth (1:13, 4:15, 4:25)
- **Breastplate of righteousness:** Be righteous; put on the new self (4:24)
- **Feet of peace:** We have peace with God and each other (2:14, 4:3)
- **Shield of faith:** Believe that Christ can save us (1:13, 4:13)
- **Helmet of salvation:** Salvation is by grace through faith (2:5, 1:13)
- **Sword of the Spirit:** The word brings salvation; build one another up through Scripture (1:13, 5:18-19)

### What It Means to Wear Christ's Righteousness

Ephesians 4:22-24 explains the transformation:

> You were taught, with regard to your former way of life, to put off your old self, which is being corrupted by its deceitful desires; to be made new in the attitude of your minds; and to put on the new self, created to be like God in true righteousness and holiness.

This new self wears a righteousness that is not our own. We are profoundly sinful. We all have skeletons in our closets—things we've done that should disqualify us. But Christ replaces those skeletons with new clothing: himself.

This new dress came at an unimaginable cost. To clothe us in his righteousness, Christ had to be stripped bare. He was made vulnerable, tortured, lashed, bloodied, crowned with thorns, and crucified—naked and judged so that God could condemn our sin in him.

Now we wear the attributes of Christ. Though we are filthy, we are clothed in his righteousness. When God the Judge looks at us, he sees us dressed in Christ's righteousness.

**So be a Christian.**

## The Nature of Our Danger

We face real danger. As Paul writes:

> As for you, you were dead in your transgressions and sins, in which you used to live when you followed the ways of this world and of the ruler of the kingdom of the air, the spirit who is now at work in those who are disobedient. (Ephesians 2:1-2)

> For our struggle is not against flesh and blood, but against the rulers, against the authorities, against the powers of this dark world and against the spiritual forces of evil in the heavenly realms. (Ephesians 6:11-12)

This world is like *Transformers*—there is more to reality than meets the eye. Evil is far more than what appears in the news. The symptoms we see—murder, corruption, greed—are manifestations of deeper spiritual forces.

### The Retreat of Christianity in the West

Consider the trajectory of Western civilization. In the 1st century, Western Europe was barbaric. When Saint Augustine brought the gospel to England in 597 AD, the people lived in caves, committed rape and infanticide, waged constant war. The civilizing influence of Christianity transformed society. Laws were created reflecting the Ten Commandments. Sexual union was given sacred meaning through marriage. Society developed moral restraint.

But now, as Christianity retreats from the West (British Christianity is projected to be statistically extinct by 2067—perhaps in your lifetime), we are becoming barbarians again. What drives this regression is evil.

We must not think the world knows better or that we should "shift with the times." The world is not wiser than God's Word.

If you are not yet a Christian, understand this: you may recognize the world is in decline, but you remain part of that world. You belong to the prince of the air. You are still enslaved to sin. You belong to the devil. Wake up. Pay attention. Get out. **Be a Christian.** This is the way to be protected. The alternative is to stand naked—without the armor that protects.

## Put Off Sin (Ephesians 6:17-20)

### Our True Battle Is Within

Now, let me be clear about what our fight actually is.

Our main battle is not with government, though we should participate in the civic process. Our main battle is not directly against the devil himself—that is Christ's work as the Divine Warrior who has already fatally maimed Satan.

We do not perform exorcisms. The word "exorcist" appears only once in the Bible—when the seven sons of Sceva attempted unsuccessfully to cast out demons (Acts 19:13-16), and they failed spectacularly. Jesus never performed exorcism rituals or used special words to cast out demons. This concept did not come from Scripture but from movies. The rise of exorcism and deliverance ministries in Christianity traces directly back to 1973—the year *The Exorcist* was released. The early church's first business was shutting down magicians, amulet sellers, seers, and idol makers.

As Paul writes:

> No, in all these things we are more than conquerors through him who loved us. For I am convinced that neither death nor life, neither angels nor demons, neither the present nor the future, nor any powers, neither height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God that is in Christ Jesus our Lord. (Romans 8:37-39)

Christ does the fighting. He is our Michael of Revelation 12. The battle against cosmic evil is his responsibility, not ours.

**Our true fight is against sin within ourselves.**

### Personal Holiness Is the Real Warfare

Paul writes:

> Therefore each of you must put off falsehood and speak truthfully to his neighbor, for we are all members of one body. In your anger do not sin: Do not let the sun go down while you are still angry, and do not give the devil a foothold. (Ephesians 4:25-27)

Notice that these commands are deeply personal. They address individual sin, not cosmic battles.

Consider sexuality and marriage. Yes, we should defend biblical truth about marriage in the public square as long as possible. But the main battle is within us—to cultivate sexual integrity. What's the point of defending marriage laws if inwardly we've lost the meaning of marriage itself? If we've reduced it from a covenantal act oriented toward children to merely a romantic relationship between consenting adults? The real fight is in how we date, our faithfulness in marriage, and our orientation toward family and future generations.

This is how the devil gains a foothold—not through possession, but through personal compromise. Our fight is against the schemes of the devil and his accusations, and it happens in the quiet places of our hearts.

### Standing Firm Together

Paul writes:

> Therefore put on the full armor of God so that when the day of evil comes, you may be able to stand your ground, and after you have done everything, to stand. Stand firm then. (Ephesians 6:13-14)

Picture a Roman phalanx—rows of soldiers standing shield to shield. No enemy can break through. They hold the ground indefinitely. We are like that chain of soldiers, supporting each other. That unity is profoundly powerful.

Our weapon in this fight is not garlic or crosses. **It is the word of God.**

> Take the helmet of salvation and the sword of the Spirit, which is the word of God. And pray in the Spirit on all occasions with all kinds of prayers and requests. With this in mind, be alert and always keep on praying for all the saints. Pray also for me, that whenever I speak, words may be given me so that I will fearlessly make known the mystery of the gospel, for which I am an ambassador in chains. (Ephesians 6:17-20)

The word of God reminds us that Christ is victorious over evil. As the Divine Warrior, he has conquered Satan. All of Satan's accusations are lies. Christ has borne the burden of the war, and we are given peace—peace with God and peace with each other. We are forgiven.

## Living Without Fear

Horror movies are just entertainment, provided they don't shape your worldview. As for demons: don't worry about them. Christ has defeated them. They may roam, but they cannot kill you. Ignore them. Instead, concentrate on putting on the armor of Christ and putting away your own sin.

**Be Christian, and you will be immune from the devil's accusations.**

**Be Christian, and you will be free from the powers of darkness.**

**Be Christian, and you will be saved when the day of evil comes.**
