# Philippians 3:7-11
**Title:** How Can We Know God Better?
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Christian Dilemma of an Invisible God

A question from my year 8 scripture class stumped me recently. Some girls kept asking how they could really know Jesus—not just know about him, but truly know him personally. It's the difference between knowing facts about someone and actually being their friend, sharing meals together, doing life together.

As people, we naturally desire relationship over mere academic knowledge. This longing to know Jesus personally is perfectly natural, but it's also an incredible source of frustration for Christians. Why?

- He is invisible
- He is inaudible
- Jesus walked the earth 2000 years ago, not today
- We can't see, touch, feel, hear, or smell him—yet we are sensory beings
- We can't have coffee together, play tennis, meet at recess, share late-night conversations, or do all the things we normally do with friends

## Paul's Longing for Us to Know Christ

In this passage, Paul writes to the Christians in Philippi. They have a great relationship—partners in the gospel, working together in telling people about Jesus. Paul has deep affection for them, and they show their concern by supporting him with money and gifts.

Now Paul is in chains, under house arrest, probably in Rome. Yet he tells his readers to "rejoice in the Lord!" How can he say that?

Because he has come to know Jesus (Philippians 3:8). The privilege and value of that relationship far outweighs anything he had before. "Everything is a loss compared to the surpassing greatness of knowing Jesus, for whose sake I have lost all things."

Paul wants his readers—us—to know Christ. His prayer at the beginning of Philippians (1:9) asks that "your love may abound more and more in knowledge and depth of insight."

To help us, Paul talks about holding onto the gospel of Christ—the good news of Jesus in the Bible.

## The Best Way to Know God: The Gospel

I'm sorry to repeat this, but the obvious answer is: read the Bible.

At ISCF, we continually emphasize that God speaks through the Bible—his word. Here's why this matters:

**The Bible tells us everything about Jesus we need to know.** Through Scripture we learn that God became man, was obedient to the Father, suffered and died for us, then rose from the dead as King over the universe. No other historical document will tell you the significance of this. The good news of Jesus saving you—the gospel—is in the Bible.

**We can only know Jesus through the Bible.** Looking around us at creation—the sun, trees, newborn babies—we may recognize that there is a God deserving our respect and thanks. But we can only know Jesus through Scripture.

**The Bible isn't magic.** It's not that the words are some sort of mantra that, pronounced correctly, invokes power and material blessing from God. The Bible simply and plainly tells us about Jesus and what he has done on the cross that we could never do ourselves.

## Paul's Example: Righteousness Through Faith

Paul was a Jew of the Jews. If he were at James Ruse, he'd be the first to complete all assignments—even the optional ones for extra credit. He'd be on the debating team, math olympiad, and the titration team. He'd attend every musical rehearsal, perform at multicultural nights, and yes, even show up to every working bee with farm boots and gloves ready!

Yet Paul considers all that rubbish in order to gain Christ. Even though he followed the law and all the rules perfectly, he still could not be in right relationship with God. He could no longer depend on "a righteousness of my own that comes from the law, but rather that which is through faith in Christ—the righteousness that comes from God and is by faith."

What a great gospel! No matter how hard we try, we can never earn our way to heaven—but God does it for us in Jesus. This is the gospel we must put our trust and confidence in.

## Why We Must Constantly Remind Ourselves

We need to constantly remind ourselves of the gospel because we are forgetful and sinful people.

We forget the marvelous thing Jesus has done and instead search for something more exciting—like goldfish in a bowl, swimming around looking for something new and interesting.

We forget the greatest miracle—the most unrepentant sinner becoming a Christian—and instead look for lesser miracles of healing or spectacular magic.

We forget that in and through Christ we have the greatest blessing of relationship with God, yet continue to seek lesser blessings in our earthly successes.

We forget our citizenship in heaven and instead settle for membership in professional associations or worldly honors.

## Waiting in Trust

Being content with what he has already given us and waiting for him is hard, isn't it? Having to wait until we can see God face to face, talk face to face, touch, smell, feel.

How does Paul wait? He talks about pressing on—persevering—to "take hold of that for which Christ Jesus took hold of me" (v. 12). "Forgetting what is behind and straining toward what is ahead, I press on toward the goal to win the prize for which God has called me heavenward in Christ Jesus" (v. 14). "Our citizenship is in heaven and we eagerly await a Savior from there" (v. 20).

So stand firm in this relationship as we wait for that coming day.

## The Temptation of Mysticism

As we wait for Jesus to come again, there can be a temptation to shortcut knowing Jesus. Instead of knowing him through reading the Bible, we might try to short-circuit our connection through mystical means:

- Hearing Jesus whispering in my ear
- Eagerness to see the supernatural and miraculous
- Jesus making impressions on my heart
- Feeling his presence through warm, fuzzy feelings and sensations
- "Touching heaven"

Some say these are evidence of knowing Jesus better. I don't think this is true. Besides being very subjective (if you feel warm and fuzzy, try turning the air conditioner off!), it could be any sort of spirit—evil or otherwise—communicating. After all, Christians aren't the only ones who claim spiritual experiences. Pagan religions, fortune tellers, the occult—all claim spiritual awareness and experience in the supernatural.

## The Hard Work of Truth

To be honest, I'd prefer to have a voice tell me straight out what to do. I'd prefer to feel God physically now rather than wait. I'd prefer to see signs and miracles for guidance.

Why? Because I'm lazy. I'd rather watch sport than read about it. I'd rather buy the summary book than check out the textbook. I'd rather watch the movie than read the novel.

English comprehension is hard! Bible study—ugh! Who has the time? I'd rather have instant visions than put in the hard work to understand.

But aside from the terrible consequences of making up our knowledge of Jesus, we'd miss out on all that God has to say in the Bible. We'd miss reminding ourselves what a great thing he has done. We'd miss the encouragement he has given us. We'd miss out on knowing Jesus better!

## Fellowship Through Suffering

That's not to say we have no experience of a personal relationship with Jesus. The knowledge of Jesus is something more (3:10) than just academics. Of course it is personal and dynamic, rather than the plain activity of reading the Bible.

But that experience and emotion will come through—it will become real—when we start obeying Jesus and actually following him. You will feel it when you have to suffer for the gospel.

At school, there isn't a lot of suffering. We can enjoy reading the Bible today—it's fun, we're with friends, no problems. But it won't always be like this. One day we'll have to make decisions about who we really trust:

- **HSC is coming.** Do I spend several hours studying for 4 Unit math, or do I help out with leading Bible study groups, reading the Bible, talking to that non-Christian—even if it means scoring a little less on my UAI?

- **When terminally ill,** will you continue to trust the Lord or curse God?

- **Will you break off that relationship** with a non-Christian because you know it's unhelpful to both of you—to your relationship with Christ and to the other person's potential relationship with Christ?

- **What will you do** when it doesn't feel good to be a Christian anymore? Your old ISCF friends have moved away, your new friends ridicule you for knowing Jesus, the music is no longer exciting, you have little energy, when it's no longer cool to be an ISCF leader or member?

Are you feeling it?

Stand firm in the gospel. Trust in the invisible God revealed in the Bible. Keep reminding ourselves. Don't trust anything else as we keep persevering and wait for the last day. Don't cheat by shortcutting through cheap experiences.

**We know Jesus by continuing to trust in his death on the cross, no matter what.**

## A Challenge for Non-Christians

If you are not a Christian, there is a bigger dilemma. You don't know Jesus at all. Yes, you may know him theoretically, but you are missing out on a relationship with him.

In a sense, "knowing Jesus better" is a little misleading. There is no knowing Jesus better. Either you know him or you don't. Either you have a personal relationship with him or you don't.

The challenge is this: Are you going to rely on your previous misconceptions about Christianity, or will you put in the hard yards in reading and understanding what the Bible is saying? What's stopping you? Bring your questions to Focus group and get them answered.
