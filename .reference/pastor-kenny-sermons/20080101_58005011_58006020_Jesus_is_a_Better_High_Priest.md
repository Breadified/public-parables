## The Weight of Regret

We all know the sting of regret—those moments when we realize we've lost something precious and cannot get it back. A decision made, a choice not taken, and suddenly the door closes forever. The sliding doors moments that haunt us: the girl we never asked out, the investment we didn't make, the opportunity we let slip through our fingers.

This passage speaks directly to that profound regret—the impossibility of turning back from a path once chosen. It warns us about falling away from Christ and the consequences of such a choice.

---

## Part One: Don't Fall Away

### The Warning

> It is impossible for those who have once been enlightened, who have tasted the heavenly gift, who have shared in the Holy Spirit, who have tasted the goodness of the word of God and the powers of the coming age and who have fallen away, to be brought back to repentance. (Hebrews 6:4-6)

The author uses five interconnected words to describe what these believers have experienced. To understand their weight, we must look back to Israel's wilderness experience—the original template for falling away.

### Enlightened—Revelation and Salvation

The first word is *enlightened*. Light represents both revelation and salvation.

> "I am the light of the world. Whoever follows me will never walk in darkness, but will have the light of life." (John 8:12)

For Israel, this enlightenment was literal and undeniable:

> By day the Lord went ahead of them in a pillar of cloud to guide them on their way and by night in a pillar of fire to give them light, so that they could travel by day or night. (Exodus 13:21)

God showed them the way. There was no ambiguity, no room for doubt about His presence.

### Tasted—Experienced the Goodness of God

To *taste* means to experience. As Peter writes:

> Like newborn infants, crave pure spiritual milk, so that by it you may grow up in your salvation, since you have tasted that the Lord is good. (1 Peter 2:2-3)

For the Israelites, this wasn't metaphor—it was literal provision:

> Then the Lord said to Moses, "I will rain down bread from heaven for you. The people are to go out each day and gather enough for that day." (Exodus 16:4)

They ate manna from heaven. They drank water from the rock. They experienced God's tangible care in the wilderness.

### Shared—Partakers of God's Covenant

To *share* or be a *partaker* means to take part in something sacred. All Israel participated in the Passover—the defining moment of their liberation:

> The whole community of Israel must celebrate it. A foreigner residing among you who wants to celebrate the Lord's Passover must have all the males in his household circumcised; then he may take part like one born in the land. (Exodus 12:47-48)

They celebrated their freedom together. They were marked as God's people.

### Tasted the Goodness of God's Word

The word of God came to Israel as the Ten Commandments—God's direct speech at Sinai:

> "I am the Lord your God, who brought you out of Egypt, out of the land of slavery. You shall have no other gods before me." (Exodus 20:1-3)

They heard the voice of God. They received His law. They knew His character.

### Yet They Fell Away

Despite all this—enlightenment, tangible experience, covenant participation, the very words of God—they fell away.

> In this wilderness your bodies will fall—every one of you twenty years old or more who was counted in the census and who has grumbled against me. (Numbers 14:29)

At Kadesh Barnea, when they stood at the border of the promised land, they refused to enter. They preferred slavery's memory to freedom's uncertainty. God said, "Very well. You will not enter." For forty years they wandered, and that generation never possessed what God had promised them.

**The logic is devastating:** If you have experienced all of God—His guidance, His provision, His covenant, His Word—and you still turn away, there is no return. You have cut yourself off from everything.

## Part Two: The Hebrew Christians' Situation

The author addresses the Jewish Christians reading this letter. He calls them:

- "Holy brethren, partakers of a heavenly calling" (3:1)
- "Partakers of Christ" (3:14)
- "Beloved" (6:9)

He uses "we" and "us" throughout. These are converted people. They know Scripture. They understand doctrine.

Yet they seem stuck spiritually:

> Therefore let us move beyond the elementary teachings about Christ and be taken forward to maturity, not laying again the foundation of repentance from acts that lead to death, and of faith in God; instruction about cleansing rites, the laying on of hands, the resurrection of the dead, and eternal judgment. (Hebrews 6:1-2)

### What They Already Knew

Don't let "elementary teachings" deceive you. These believers understood:

- Repentance from dead works
- Faith in God
- Baptism and cleansing
- The laying on of hands
- The resurrection of the dead
- Eternal judgment
- The crucifixion and resurrection of Christ

They weren't ignorant. They knew the gospel.

> For I resolved to know nothing while I was with you except Jesus Christ and him crucified. (1 Corinthians 2:2)

We are not saved by theological degrees. We are saved by the simple knowledge that Christ has died for us—and these Hebrew Christians possessed that.

### The Real Problem: Immaturity, Not Ignorance

The issue isn't knowledge. It's maturity:

> In fact, though by this time you ought to be teachers, you need someone to teach you the elementary truths of God's word all over again. You need milk, not solid food! Anyone who lives on milk, being still an infant, is not acquainted with the teaching about righteousness. But solid food is for the mature, who by constant use have trained themselves to distinguish good from evil. (Hebrews 5:12-14)

They knew the truth but didn't live it. They possessed knowledge but lacked the character of mature believers. They heard God's Word but didn't apply it, so they had to be reminded again and again of what they should have already integrated into their lives.

**Hebrews pushes application, not information.** The writer calls them to stand firm, be faithful, and stop drifting back to Judaism. There is no gospel 2.0. What they know is sufficient. The time for action has come.

### How Good Christians Fall Away

We've all witnessed it. A believer who once seemed solid suddenly drops off. What happens?

1. The world becomes more attractive than following Christ
2. They don't constantly apply the Word they know
3. They lose the ability to distinguish between good and evil
4. Gradually, they begin to slip
5. They grow critical, looking for excuses: "The church is this... the pastor is that..."

But these are mere excuses. If it were truly about a particular church or personality, they'd simply move to another congregation. Instead, they give up altogether. They fall away completely.

Here's the tragedy: **They know it's wrong.** But as rational beings, we need reasons for our choices, so we invent them. We rationalize our rebellion.

### The Ultimate Consequence

When someone turns from Christ after knowing Him, something grave happens:

> To their loss they are crucifying the Son of God all over again and subjecting him to public disgrace. (Hebrews 6:6)

By falling away, they deny the cross. They reject the work Christ accomplished. They take the gift of life that Jesus died to give them, look at it, and throw it away. They subject Christ to public disgrace.

The result mirrors Israel's fate:

> Land that drinks in the rain often falling on it and that produces a crop useful to those for whom it is farmed receives the blessing of God. But land that produces thorns and thistles is worthless and is in danger of being cursed. In the end it will be burned. (Hebrews 6:7-8)

The faithful enter God's rest. The unfaithful never do.

### The Broader Warning Pattern in Hebrews

This passage fits within Hebrews' larger warning structure. Throughout the letter, the author calls believers to hold fast:

**Hebrews 2:1-4** – Pay attention so you don't drift away from so great a salvation.

**Hebrews 3:12-14** – Don't have an unbelieving heart that turns from the living God. Encourage one another daily. Hold your original conviction to the very end.

**Hebrews 10:26-31** – If we deliberately reject the knowledge of truth, only judgment remains. How much more serious to trample the Son of God underfoot?

**Hebrews 10:35-39** – Don't throw away your confidence. Persevere so you receive what God has promised. We are not among those who shrink back and are destroyed, but among those who have faith and are saved.

**Hebrews 12:25-29** – Don't refuse Him who speaks from heaven. We are receiving an unshakable kingdom.

## Part Three: You Won't Fall Away

Here's where hope enters:

> Even though we speak like this, dear friends, we are convinced of better things in your case—the things that have to do with salvation. God is not unjust; he will not forget your work and the love you have shown him as you have helped his people and continue to help them. We want each of you to show this same diligence to the very end, so that what you hope for may be fully realized. We do not want you to become lazy, but to imitate those who through faith and patience inherit what has been promised. (Hebrews 6:9-12)

The author's logic seems paradoxical: **"Yes, Christians can fall away... but not you."**

### Three Theological Perspectives

Theologians have wrestled with this passage:

**First View:** Christians can be saved and lost. If you fall away, you forfeit salvation.

But this creates tension with passages like Romans 8:37-39:

> No, in all these things we are more than conquerors through him who loved us. For I am convinced that neither death nor life, neither angels nor demons, neither the present nor the future, nor any powers, neither height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God that is in Christ Jesus our Lord.

And John 6:39: "This is the will of him who sent me, that I shall lose none of all those he has given me, but raise them up at the last day."

Jesus died effectively for His elect. He won't take that back. Does that mean God's sovereignty is compromised by the first view?

**Second View:** Those who fall away were never truly Christian. They were merely professing Christians who appeared to believe but never genuinely did.

But the author doesn't speak this way. He addresses these people as believers, calls them "beloved," includes them in "we," and assures them of his conviction about them.

**Third View: The Hypothetical (Amyraldian)** – The warning is real. You *can* hypothetically fall away. But you *won't*.

This matches how the Bible actually speaks. In every passage about falling away, the warnings are directed at *others* or presented in conditional clauses:

> If we deliberately keep on sinning after we have received the knowledge of the truth, no sacrifice for sins is left. (Hebrews 10:26)

> If you do not remain in me, you are like a branch that is thrown away and withers. (John 15:6)

Just because a stop sign exists doesn't mean you will run it. The warning is genuine, but if you're reading this, if you're wrestling with God's Word, if you're part of Christ's flock—you won't fall away.

### The Critical Distinction

The fate of others doesn't have to be your fate. Not because you're strong, but because Christ holds you.

When I encounter Christians who have fallen away, I don't assume they were fake. I believe them when they say they were sincere. But I also believe that God hasn't abandoned them. And I call on them to return—to remember their baptism, to go back to the foundations of faith, to grow up and mature.

For our friends and family who have drifted away, we don't stop praying. We don't assume they were never real. Instead, we long for their return and trust that God doesn't abandon His elect.

## A Word About Taking Breaks from God

I often hear Christians say, "I need a break from God. Ministry is stressful. I'm disappointed. I'm struggling."

Taking a break from church? From ministry? From Bible study? In the right context, these can be healthy. Try to maintain fellowship and accountability. Stay connected to others who can encourage you.

But **never take a break from God. Never take a break from His Word.**

God is the source of life and eternal life. His Word is alive and active. Do you understand what taking a break from life means? You die.

> "I'll just give my heart a break from pumping blood."
> "I'll just give my lungs a break from breathing."
> "My brain's been working all my life—I'll give it a break from oxygen."

**What will happen? You will die.** And there will be no coming back from that.

## Hope in the Darkness

Even in our deepest despair, there is hope. When all is lost and we feel only regret, hope remains. If you're struggling as a Christian today and on the verge of driving through that stop sign, don't do it.

> Even though we speak like this, dear friends, we are convinced of better things in your case—the things that have to do with salvation. God is not unjust; he will not forget your work and the love you have shown him as you have helped his people and continue to help them. We want each of you to show this same diligence to the very end, so that what you hope for may be fully realized. (Hebrews 6:9-11)

There is a promise here: God sees your work. He remembers your love. He won't forget. And if you remain faithful, you will fully realize what you hope for.

You won't regret following God.
