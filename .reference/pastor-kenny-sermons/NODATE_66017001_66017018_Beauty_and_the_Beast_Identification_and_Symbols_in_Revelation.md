# Revelation 17:1-18

**Title:** Beauty and the Beast: Identification and Symbols in Revelation
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Understanding the Woman: Her Essential Attributes

Rather than asking "who is this woman," Scripture invites us to understand her through her attributes. Let's examine what Revelation reveals about her character:

**Influence** (vv. 1, 2, 15, 18)
The woman exercises vast influence over the kings and peoples of the earth. She has enthroned herself over all nations, multitudes, and languages—her reach is universal and pervasive.

**Evil** (v. 3; cf. 12:3, 13:1)
She bears the same imagery as the dragon and the beast before her—symbols of cosmic rebellion against God's authority.

**Attractiveness** (v. 4)
Adorned in purple and scarlet, glittering with gold, precious stones, and pearls, she presents an alluring appearance. Her beauty is calculated to seduce.

**Repulsiveness** (v. 6)
Yet beneath her splendor lies something abhorrent: she is drunk on the blood of the saints and the blood of the witnesses to Jesus. Her beauty masks a deeply corrupting nature.

## The Vision's Setting

John is taken to the wilderness to see this vision (v. 3). In previous studies, we've learned that the wilderness is where God tests His people and where spiritual realities are laid bare—a place of revelation and clarification.

## The Mystery Revealed Through Seven Dimensions

The meaning of Babylon is not given through straightforward explanation, but through seven layers of understanding that focus our attention on the nature and scope of this reality:

**1. The Scarlet Beast (v. 8): Its Career, Not Merely Its Appearance**
We're drawn initially to its visual horror, but the critical insight is its career: "was, now is not, and yet is to come" (v. 8). We've encountered this precise pattern before—it echoes the false claim of resurrection that mocks Christ's true resurrection. This beast embodies a rebellion that has existed, vanished from immediate view, yet persists in returning.

**2. The Sevenfold Head/Hill (v. 9): Dual Interpretation**
The passage allows for two complementary interpretations: the heads represent both geographical mountains (physical kingdoms and empires) and the spiritual authority that sustains them. Both dimensions are simultaneously true—the physical and spiritual are interwoven in this vision.

**3. Seven Kings (vv. 10-11): Political Power Across Generations**
If the head represents authority and the mountain represents durability, then the kings represent political power. This principle applies to every Christian generation: worldly political systems rise and fall, yet the underlying ideology of godlessness persists. We see this in our own time—specific regimes change, but the fundamental rebellion against God's rule continues.

**4. The Horns (vv. 12-14): Distinct from the Kings**
Unlike the seven kings, the horns represent a different order of power—more recent, more coordinated, more unified in their rebellion. They haven't yet fully manifested their authority but are preparing to do so.

**5. The Waters (v. 15): Universal Dominion**
The waters upon which the woman sits represent "peoples, multitudes, nations, and languages"—the entire human race. Her influence is not regional or temporary; it's comprehensive and ongoing.

**6. The Split in the Ranks of Evil (vv. 16-17; cf. Mark 3:23-26): A Peculiar Victory**
Here we encounter something remarkable: the ten horns themselves turn on the woman and destroy her. This echoes Jesus's teaching that a kingdom divided against itself cannot stand. The powers of darkness, unified in their opposition to God, are nevertheless divided among themselves. 

What's strange—what should arrest our attention—is that God accomplishes His purposes through this very split. He puts it into the hearts of these evil powers to devour the woman (v. 17). God doesn't need to intervene directly; He simply allows the internal contradictions of rebellion to work themselves out. Evil contains within itself the seeds of its own destruction.

**7. The Core Reality: Godlessness Enthroned**
For most of human history, the satanic ideology of godlessness has governed the powers of this world. These worldly powers, in turn, dominate mankind. In Revelation's terms, the woman is enthroned on the beast, and the beast rides upon many waters—a complete system of rebellion and control, from ideology down through political structures to the masses of humanity.

## Personal Reckoning: Where Have We Been Corrupted?

This teaching demands honest self-examination. The ideology of godlessness—the claim that we can order our lives without reference to God's authority—has subtly infiltrated our thinking. Consider where this corruption may have taken root in your own heart:

- **Money**: Have we embraced the lie that wealth is security, that accumulation is the path to significance?
- **Lust**: Have we accepted the world's narrative that sexual desire defines us, that gratification is a fundamental right?
- **Pride**: Have we been seduced into believing we are self-made, answerable to no one but ourselves?
- **Corruption**: Have we rationalized small compromises of integrity as necessary or justified?
- **Idolatry**: Have we substituted lesser goods—status, comfort, human approval—for the worship of God alone?

## A Call to Repentance and Prayer

Let us repent of the ungodly attitudes and actions we've allowed to take root. Let us turn from the subtle ways we've been shaped by a godless ideology. And let us pray earnestly that God will deliver us from this corruption, that He will realign our hearts toward His kingdom and His righteousness, that His rule might be evident not just in our circumstances but in the very fabric of how we think and live.
