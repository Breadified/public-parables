# 1 Corinthians 10:31–11:1

**Title:** Serving  
**Date:** 26 January 2011  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## What Is Glory?

> So whether you eat or drink or whatever you do, do it all for the glory of God. (1 Corinthians 10:31)

Glory means living for the good of many. It's not about ourselves, but about the welfare and salvation of those around us. Paul illustrates this principle beautifully in his own life—he doesn't seek his own comfort or advantage, but constantly considers how his actions affect others.

## Do Not Cause Anyone to Stumble

> Do not cause anyone to stumble, whether Jews, Greeks or the church of God—even as I try to please everybody in every way. For I am not seeking my own good but the good of many, so that they may be saved. (1 Corinthians 10:32–33)

Paul's passion here is clear: our choices matter not just for us, but for the spiritual welfare of those around us. When we live for God's glory, we prioritize the salvation and growth of others over our own preferences and freedoms. This is the heart of Christian servanthood.

## Follow as I Follow Christ

> Follow my example, as I follow the example of Christ. (1 Corinthians 11:1)

Paul doesn't point people to himself, but through himself to Christ. This is the ultimate call—to follow leaders who themselves are following Jesus. True leadership is a relay of devotion, where each generation passes on not their own wisdom, but their example of following Christ.

> I praise you for remembering me in everything and for holding to the teachings, just as I passed them on to you. (1 Corinthians 11:2)

The church at Corinth is commended for their faithfulness in preserving and living out the apostolic teaching. This is the foundation of Christian community—holding fast to the truth and passing it on with integrity to the next generation.
