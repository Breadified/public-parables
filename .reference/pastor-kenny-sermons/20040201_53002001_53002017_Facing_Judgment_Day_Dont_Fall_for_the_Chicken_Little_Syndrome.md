# 2 Thessalonians 2:1-17

**Title:** Facing Judgment Day: Don't Fall for the "Chicken Little" Syndrome
**Date:** 22 February 2004
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction: The "Chicken Little" Problem

We live in an age of anxiety about the end times. Every global crisis—September 11, war, famine, disease—becomes "proof" that judgment day has arrived. Flyers appear on street corners proclaiming that Jesus is coming now, pointing to materialism, globalization, and world suffering as unmistakable signs.

But here's the thing: the Thessalonians faced this exact dilemma in AD 60. They were a model church—their faith was growing abundantly, their love for one another was increasing, and they endured persecution with remarkable steadfastness. Yet they had become convinced that the Day of the Lord had already begun. Paul writes to calm their fears and correct their misunderstanding.

---

## 1. The Day of the Lord Has Not Come (verses 1-2)

> Now concerning the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ and our being gathered together to him, we ask you, brothers, not to be quickly shaken in mind or alarmed, either by a spirit or a spoken word, or a letter seeming to be from us, to the effect that the day of the Lord has come.

### Understanding Their Hope

The Thessalonians were waiting for the *parousia*—the appearance or return of Jesus Christ—when believers would be gathered to him. This expectation wasn't new. It had deep roots in Jewish history.

Five hundred years before Jesus, Israel had been scattered in exile—the northern kingdom destroyed by Assyria, the southern kingdom taken captive to Babylon. The prophets of that era looked forward to a day when God would restore His people and gather them from all nations:

> In that day the Lord will extend his hand yet a second time to recover the remnant that remains of his people, from Assyria, from Egypt, from Pathros, from Cush, from Elam, from Shinar, from Hamath, and from the coastlands of the sea. He will raise a signal for the nations and will assemble the banished of Israel, and gather the dispersed of Judah from the four corners of the earth. (Isaiah 11:11-12)

Though the Jews returned from exile under the Persians, the restoration was incomplete. The temple was less glorious, foreign powers still ruled, and the people remained scattered. That longing for final restoration—when God would gather all His people and establish His kingdom—persisted.

### Why They Thought the End Had Come

The Thessalonians were a suffering community, persecuted by Roman authorities. In their pain and desperation, they began interpreting their suffering as signs that the final age had begun. Every hardship became evidence that Jesus would come any moment. Their expectation exceeded their patience.

It's like being in love when you're desperate: every accidental touch becomes a sign, every glance holds meaning, even the absence of attention becomes "playing hard to get." When you're suffering terribly, you long for heaven so intensely that you start seeing evidence of its arrival everywhere.

### The Problem with Sign-Seeking

Paul's message is simple: **the Day of the Lord has not come.**

We see the same mistake today. Some point to global crises as proof of the end. Others argue that a improving world proves Jesus is here; still others claim a deteriorating world proves the same. Every age seems "unique" when you're living in it. Some interpret personal success as God's direction—a good exam result means pursue medicine; a good job offer means you must take it. Others treat failure the same way.

But Jesus Himself said the day would come like a thief in the night—unexpectedly. We cannot and will not know. God doesn't guide us through interpreting signs in world events or personal circumstances. He guides us through His Word.

The man handing out flyers in Redfern was earnest and well-intentioned. His desire was genuine—he wanted people to know Jesus and understand judgment day. But his conclusion was wrong. The day is not here.

## 2. A Rival Is Coming (verses 3-12)

> Let no one deceive you in any way. For that day will not come, unless the rebellion comes first, and the man of lawlessness is revealed, the son of destruction, who opposes and exalts himself against every so-called god or object of worship, so that he takes his seat in the temple of God, proclaiming himself to be God.

### The Apparent Contradiction

Paul seems contradictory here. He just told the Thessalonians not to look for signs, yet now he speaks of a "man of lawlessness" to be revealed. But Paul is not saying, "Watch for this man so you'll know the end is near." Rather, he's explaining the order of events: *another* appearing will come before Christ's appearing.

### Who Is This Man of Lawlessness?

Paul acknowledges that he had explained this in person:

> Do you not remember that when I was still with you I told you these things? And you know what is restraining him now so that he may be revealed in his time.

We don't have that conversation recorded. We can only speculate that this figure—the "son of destruction"—might be a single person (an agent of Satan), a metaphor for rebellious humanity, or both (the representative of human rebellion in contrast to Christ, the obedient one).

What we do know:
- He opposes and exalts himself above every god and object of worship
- He proclaims himself to be God
- He is already at work in hidden ways—"the mystery of lawlessness is already at work"
- A restrainer currently holds him back, and when that restrainer is removed, the lawless one will be fully revealed

### His Brief, Anticlimactic End

And then what happens? An anticlimax:

> And then the lawless one will be revealed, whom the Lord Jesus will kill with the breath of his mouth and bring to nothing by the appearance of his coming.

Imagine the hype of a championship boxing match—months of media coverage, expectations building, crowds gathering. The bell rings. And in thirty seconds, it's over. Mike Tyson lands one punch and the challenger is knocked out.

So it is with the enemy of God. After all the buildup and restraint, the spirit that opposes God is destroyed by Jesus's own spirit—His very breath. There's no prolonged battle. The victory is complete and decisive.

This echoes Isaiah's prophecy of the coming Messiah:

> There shall come forth a shoot from the stump of Jesse, and a branch from his roots shall bear fruit... And the Spirit of the Lord shall rest upon him, the Spirit of wisdom and understanding... and he shall strike the earth with the rod of his mouth, and with the breath of his lips he shall kill the wicked. (Isaiah 11:1-4)

Jesus is that descendant of David. He carries the Spirit of the Lord, the Spirit of wisdom and might. He is the righteous Judge who will rule the earth with equity and justice. And He will destroy the wicked with His word.

### Two Appearings, One Real Winner

In reality, there's only one true coming that matters—Jesus's return. The man of lawlessness's appearance is defeated by Christ's appearance. They don't unfold as two equal rivalries; one crushes the other completely.

The consequences for those who align with the losing side are severe:

> The coming of the lawless one is by the activity of Satan with all power and false signs and wonders, and with all wicked deception for those who are perishing, because they refused to love the truth and so be saved. Therefore God sends them a strong delusion, so that they may believe what is false, in order that all may be condemned who did not believe the truth but had pleasure in unrighteousness.

Those who reject God's truth are handed over to deception. They get what they choose—a life built on lies. And in the end, they face condemnation.

### A Choice Before Us

Here's the fundamental question: **Whose appearance are you cheering for?**

One side has God, Jesus, and His Spirit. The other has Satan and the man of lawlessness. There is no middle ground, no neutral position.

The losing side is the perishing side, the side of deception, the side that chose pleasure in unrighteousness over love of truth. If you're not yet a Christian, tonight is a good night to change your allegiance. Find out how you can move to the winning side.

But if you are on God's side, then you know something crucial: the ultimate outcome is already determined. When that day comes, you will be with the One who wins.

## 3. In Any Case, Don't Worry (verses 13-17)

> But we ought always to give thanks to God for you, brothers beloved by the Lord, because God chose you as the first fruits to be saved, through sanctification by the Spirit and belief in the truth. To this he called you through our gospel, so that you may obtain the glory of our Lord Jesus Christ.

### Why Worry When You're Chosen?

Paul turns to reassurance. Thessalonians, why are you shaken or alarmed? You have every reason to be confident.

First, you are **beloved by the Lord**. God has chosen you—not as an afterthought, but as the "first fruits" of the resurrection age. Just as Christ rose from the dead as the firstfruits, so will all believers rise to eternal life. You are being **sanctified by the Spirit**—made holy, transformed daily to be more like Christ. You have the **truth that brings salvation**—not deception, but the gospel itself.

It's like telling the top students at an elite school, "Why are you panicking about your exams? You scored first in the trials. You've been acing exams since kindergarten. What could possibly be alarming about your results?"

If you are a Christian, you belong to the side that wins. Christ already paid the penalty for your sin through His death. Because He was perfectly obedient, God raised Him from the dead and crowned Him King of the universe. When He returns, He will destroy every rule, authority, and power that opposes Him, then deliver the kingdom to God the Father. You will be part of that victory.

### The Call to Stand Firm

> So then, brothers, stand firm and hold to the traditions that you were taught by us, either by our spoken word or by our letter.

Don't be shaken by sensational claims about the end times. Don't be alarmed by flyers proclaiming the sky is falling. Instead, **stand firm in what you have been taught from Scripture**.

How do you stand firm?

- **Learn the Bible.** Attend Bible studies, theological education, Bible camps. Know God's Word deeply.
- **Trust God's guidance through His Word, not through signs.** He doesn't speak to us through interpreting world events or personal coincidences. He speaks through Scripture.
- **Be stable in your faith.** Don't be tossed about by every new crisis or theory. You have the God of the universe on your side.

That man in Redfern, for all his good intentions, lacked confidence that God would actually follow through. He thought Satan might actually be a threat to God's plans. But Satan is not a threat. His defeat is already certain.

### The Final Word: Comfort and Strength

> Now may our Lord Jesus Christ himself, and God our Father, who loved us and gave us eternal comfort and good hope through grace, comfort your hearts and establish them in every good work and word.

Paul's closing prayer says it all. You have Jesus. You have a Father who loves you. You have eternal comfort and good hope, given freely by grace. In that security, your hearts are comforted and strengthened for every good work and word.

---

## Summary

Three truths to hold onto:

1. **The Day of the Lord has not come.** Don't fall for sensationalism or sign-seeking. Jesus will return, but we cannot know when.

2. **A rival will appear before Christ's final return—but Christ will defeat him completely.** There's only one true outcome. Choose the winning side.

3. **If you belong to Jesus, don't worry or be shaken. Stand firm in Scripture.** You are chosen, loved, and sanctified. The victory belongs to your King.
