# Proverbs 2:10–2:17

**Title:** Messaging the World Through Progeny  
**Date:** 24 May 2020  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction: The Challenge of Parenthood

Parenthood is genuinely difficult. Teaching our children, disciplining them, navigating the many challenges—it all takes intentional effort and sacrifice.

Modern life compounds these difficulties. Parents are separated from their children for most waking hours: work, school, tutoring, and transport consume the day. When parents and children are together, their sinfulness often surfaces—arguments and conflict instead of connection.

Beyond our own struggles, external forces work against healthy families. Governments, attempting to protect children, often alienate families instead. Expanded definitions of domestic violence and the language of "rights" within what should be an unconditional relationship create new problems. Cultural shifts—particularly the feminist revolution undermining paternal roles and policies encouraging mothers into the workforce—further destabilize families.

Yet God has a purpose for parenthood that extends far beyond our individual families. How does parenthood fit into God's larger mission to bring His glory to the ends of the earth?

## 1. God as Father: The Ultimate Model (Malachi 2:10)

> Do we not all have one Father? Did not one God create us? Why do we profane the covenant of our ancestors by being unfaithful to one another?

When understanding fatherhood, we must begin not with human fathers but with God Himself. God is the ultimate model.

### What Makes a Father?

A father is fundamentally a **creator**—one who gives life and has ownership. The "father's house" refers to all those given life by him (Genesis 31:30).

A father is also a **provider and protector**. When Job says, "I was a father to the needy," he means he protected and provided for those who had no one (Job 29:16). When Joseph became the one who provided food for all Egypt, Pharaoh called him "father" (Genesis 45:8). Conversely, to be fatherless is to be vulnerable, needy, and without provision.

### The Biblical Patriarchy

Ephesians 3:14–15 reminds us that all fatherhood in heaven and earth derives its name from the Father. God is the ultimate father, and the family structure rotates around the father—just as Israel revolved around their one God.

The word "patriarch" has taken on negative connotations in our culture. Many associate it with abuse and domination. But that's not God's definition of patriarchy. God's patriarchal design is rooted in biological reality and responsibility. The crucial question in any society has always been whether the *father* will be present. Single motherhood is the most vulnerable family condition, not because mothers are inadequate but because responsibility for provision falls on one person.

### Why Fathers Matter

Mothers are naturally present at a child's birth and early life. But fathers must choose to stay, to provide, to lead. This isn't about diminishing mothers—mothers and fathers work together (the Greek word *goneis* means "parents"). But the responsibility primarily rests on fathers.

God looks to fathers. The Bible unashamedly presents a patriarchal structure because it reflects God's good design, not human corruption of that design. The solution to bad fatherhood is not the elimination of fatherhood but the raising up of better fathers.

## 2. Human Parents: Breaking Faith (Malachi 2:11–16)

> Judah has been unfaithful. A detestable thing has been committed in Israel and in Jerusalem: Judah has desecrated the sanctuary the Lord loves by marrying women who worship a foreign god.

### Unfaithfulness Through Intermarriage

The Israelites broke faith with God through intermarriage with unbelievers. This wasn't merely about marrying people of different races—it was about marrying those who worshiped foreign gods and drew the covenant people away from exclusive loyalty to the God of Israel (Ezra 9:1–2).

The consequences were severe. Children of mixed marriages lost their heritage. In Nehemiah's account, children didn't even speak the language of Judah, cut off from their people's spiritual identity (Nehemiah 13:23–24). Even Solomon—the wisest and most favored king—was led into idolatry by his foreign wives (Nehemiah 13:26).

**Application Today:** When choosing a marriage partner, think Christianly. Everyone believes in some god—whether idols, parents, ancestors, celestial bodies, government, or themselves. You belong to an exclusive God who says, "You shall have no other gods before me. I am a jealous God." Your God does not want you in marriage with an unbeliever (1 Corinthians 7:39).

If you are unmarried and free to choose, marry a Christian. If you're already in a mixed marriage, remain faithful—God's promise is that the unbelieving spouse is "sanctified" through the believing one, and your children are "holy" (1 Corinthians 7:12–14). But if you have freedom to marry again, marry within the faith.

### Unfaithfulness Through Divorce

> You have been unfaithful to her, though she is your partner, the wife of your marriage covenant... The man who hates and divorces his wife does violence to the one he should protect.

Marriage is a covenant—a binding contract witnessed by God Himself. When you divorce, you break that covenant and commit violence against your spouse, against the vows made before God.

To divorce is to hate. Modern culture frames divorce as an act of love ("I love you too much to stay"), but this contradicts God's definition of love. God's love is steadfast, loyal, and faithful—He does not break promises. Our love should reflect His nature, not our feelings.

Divorce devastates not just the spouse but the children. They are ripped from one parent, from extended family, from a unified household. If it takes a village to raise a child, you have just removed half that village. You cannot bridge the distance between two homes.

**A Word of Grace:** Some have experienced divorce. I don't judge your past. But I warn about the future: If you are married, do not divorce. Stay faithful even if your spouse is unfaithful—you made a promise before God.

> I hate divorce, says the Lord.

## 3. Seeking Godly Offspring: God's Purpose (Malachi 2:15)

> Has not the one God made you? You belong to him in body and spirit. And what does the one God seek? Godly offspring.

### The Immediate Context: The Promised Seed

For Israel, "godly offspring" had specific meaning. God promised Abraham descendants, and specifically one descendant—the Seed who would reverse sin's effects and defeat Satan (Galatians 3:15–16). Every birth was watched with anticipation: Is this the one? Each generation had to maintain the covenant line, preserve the faith, and keep the project alive.

Intermarriage jeopardized this. A child of believers marrying unbelievers could not be part of the lineage that would produce the Messiah. Divorce twisted family lines so badly they couldn't be traced back to the promise.

This is why God's punishment made sense: to be cut off from the covenant meant exclusion from this great project.

### Our Children: Qualitative and Quantitative

The Christ has come. We're no longer waiting for the Messiah. But another project has replaced it—the same one announced in Malachi 1:11: God's name will be great among all nations, from sunrise to sunset.

Children remain central to this work.

**Qualitatively:** Your children are the next generation of gospel proclaimers. They will teach their friends, become the next preachers, share the faith with their generation. The gospel is one generation away from extinction—we hold that responsibility as parents.

**Quantitatively:** Demographics is destiny. Growth happens not just through conversion but through biological increase. Early Christians understood this. While pagan Romans practiced infanticide and abortion, Christians loved children, did not abort them, and reproduced. The pagan population declined; the Christian population exploded. By the 4th century, Christianity moved from a tiny sect to the official religion of Rome—simply because Christians out-birthed pagans.

This pattern can repeat. In 50 years, through biological growth alone, Christians could transform the cultural and political landscape. We're already seeing this in Asia, where Christians comprise 10% of the population versus Australia's 1.9%.

### Raising Godly Offspring

As parents, your responsibility is immense:

- **Choose God for your children while you can.** Prioritize Bible over badminton, Scripture class over secular options, Sunday School over social activities, youth group over tutoring.
- **Make Christianity easy for your children, not harder.** Don't let extracurriculars conflict with church attendance or Bible reading.
- **Guard their friendships and media carefully.** You are responsible for their spiritual formation, not the TV, school, or strangers.
- **Be regular at church.** Don't gamble with their spiritual walk.
- **Don't divorce.** Divorce devastates their spiritual development.

God wants your children. He seeks godly offspring to carry His message to the world.

## 4. The Promise of Real Justice (Malachi 2:17–3:18, 4:1–6)

> You have wearied the Lord with your words. "How have we wearied him?" By saying, "All who do evil are good in the eyes of the Lord, and he is pleased with them. Where is the God of justice?"

The people grew cynical. They saw the wicked prosper and questioned whether God cares about justice.

God's answer comes in the form of promise: He will come as a refiner's fire, purifying His people and judging the evildoers. Justice will not be delayed forever. The righteous will be distinguished from the wicked. Those who fear God's name will be His treasured possession.

Meanwhile, God calls His people to faithfulness: **Return to me. Keep the covenant. Be faithful in your families. Don't rob Me of tithes and offerings—these are acts of trust and covenant-keeping.** A faithful family life is itself a witness to God's justice and His redemptive work.

## Closing Word: To Every Age

**To Parents:** Don't divorce. Raise your children in the faith. You are God's instrument for passing the gospel to the next generation.

**To Young People Contemplating Marriage:** Don't intermarry. Find a spouse who shares your faith. Have children. Think biologically. You are part of God's long-term strategy to transform the world.

**To Teenagers and Children:** Be the godly offspring God is looking for. Form prayer groups at school. Be faithful in your generation. Your parents' protection of your faith isn't restrictive—it's loving. One day you'll understand that their insistence on church, their limits on entertainment, their prioritization of Scripture—these are gifts.

God is looking for faithful families to message the world with His glory. Will you be part of that work?
