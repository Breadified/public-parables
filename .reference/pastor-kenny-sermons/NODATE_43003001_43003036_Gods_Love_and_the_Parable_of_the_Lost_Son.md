# John 3:1-36

**Title:** God's Love and the Parable of the Lost Son
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Story of Adam and Eve

God created the world—the earth, the sun, the stars, the garden, and all its flowers. He created people: a man named Adam and a woman named Eve. He gave them a beautiful home in the garden.

Like all good fathers, God gave Adam and Eve rules:

1. Eat from any tree in the garden—anything you want
2. Don't eat from the tree of the knowledge of good and evil

Lots of freedom. Just one boundary.

But they ate the forbidden fruit anyway. It's like when your mum says, "Don't eat the cake I'm cooling on the bench—eat anything else in the kitchen," and what do you do? You eat the cake!

As a result, Adam and Eve were cursed and punished. They were kicked out of the beautiful garden. No more plenty—now they had to work hard just to survive.

### What They Really Did Wrong

The deeper issue wasn't just eating fruit. They decided they didn't trust what God knew about good and evil. They decided for themselves what was right and wrong. They thought eating the fruit was good, so they ate it.

### How We're Like Adam and Eve

Think about how we treat our parents. They tell us what's right and wrong—"Go to sleep"—and we ignore them. Then we wake up grumpy.

Think about how we treat our teachers. They tell us rules—"No running in the hallways"—and we ignore them. Then we fall.

Think about how we treat God. Jesus tells us to love one another, to listen to His voice, and we don't.

As a result, God is angry at us.

### The Solution

When a teacher or parent is angry with us, what do we do? We say sorry! We need to say sorry to God too.

## John 3:16—God's Love for the World

> For God so loved the world that he gave his only son, so that whoever believes in him should not perish but have eternal life.

### God Loved the World

God made the world and committed Himself to it. He made people and committed Himself to them. Even after Adam and Eve disobeyed and were kicked out of the garden, He didn't let them die immediately. He gave them clothes and food. He cared for them.

### God Gave His Only Son

But God does more than provide life outside the garden. The real key is getting back to being friends with God—getting back into the garden. To do this, God gave His Son.

His Son was precious. His only Son. The Son paid the price for the way back to God. Jesus swapped places with us so we can be friends with God again.

### Believe in Him

All we have to do is believe that God has done this. That's it.

### The Result: Not Perish

When we believe in Jesus and His sacrifice, we don't perish. We have eternal life.

## The Parable of the Lost Son

Jesus once told a story that shows us exactly what this love looks like.

There was a boy on a farm who said to his father, "Give me my inheritance." Think about what he was really saying: *I wish you were dead.* You only get an inheritance when your father dies.

But the father gave him what he asked for. The boy ran off and spent the money however he wanted. He stayed in hotels, bought the latest games and clothes, ate and drank, and didn't work. He bought toys and enjoyed himself—until one day he ran out of money.

He looked in his wallet. Nothing. He looked in his pocket. Nothing. He checked his bank account. Nothing.

No more toys. No more hotels. Not even food.

He ended up working in a piggery, where they raised pigs. The conditions were terrible. He was so hungry that he even stole food from the pigs.

### The Boy's Change of Heart

Then he thought to himself: *Wait a minute. The workers on my father's farm are treated better than this. I'll go back and ask him for a job.* 

He wasn't going back because he loved his father—he was sure his father didn't love him after he'd basically cursed him to death. He was going back because his father's workers had it better than this.

### The Father's Response

As the boy walked toward the farm, something remarkable happened. The father—who had been anxiously watching the road ever since his son left—saw him and ran to him.

This was shocking. The father was old. He was wealthy and powerful. Running looked funny. Rich, powerful men don't run. But he did. He ran to his son, hugged him, and kissed him. He was so glad his son was home.

> My son was lost, but now he is found.

### The Father's Forgiveness

This is stunning, isn't it? You'd think that if someone had wished you dead, you'd never forgive them. But the father forgave his son completely.

And that's exactly what our Father in heaven is like. He will accept us. We just need to run back to Him. We just need to say sorry.
