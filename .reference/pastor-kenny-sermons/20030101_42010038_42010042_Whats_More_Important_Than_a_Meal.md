# Luke 10:38-42

**Title:** What's More Important Than a Meal?

**Date:** January 2003

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Setting

Jesus has just sent out seventy-two disciples with specific instructions about hospitality and receiving meals from those who welcome them. Following the feeding of the five thousand, this teaching about sustenance takes on deeper meaning. When Jesus arrives at Martha and Mary's house, the stage is set for a profound lesson about priorities.

## Martha: Distracted by the Wrong Things

Martha welcomes Jesus into her home with genuine hospitality. She's the ideal convert—opening her home, preparing food, doing everything right according to the cultural rules of hospitality. But Luke describes her as "distracted" by all the preparations, while Jesus describes her as "worried and upset about many things."

The word "distracted" means literally "drawn away." Martha's anxiety mirrors the anxious mindset Jesus warns against throughout Luke 12:11-26. Though she's physically present, her attention has been pulled away from what matters most.

> 40 But Martha was distracted by all the preparations that had to be made. She came to him and asked, "Lord, don't you care that my sister has left me to do the work by myself? Tell her to help me!"

Her frustration reveals something crucial: she's made the meal the centerpiece of hospitality rather than the relationship. The meal has become so important that its absence feels like rejection—"Don't you care?" she asks Jesus. But this reveals a fundamental misalignment of priorities.

### The Problem with Anxiety Over Material Things

We live in a world of perpetual dissatisfaction. Despite living in one of the most prosperous, beautiful countries with the best food and cleanest beaches, we remain anxious and depressed. We've confused needs with endless wants:

- Instead of clothes, we chase fashion and celebrity status
- Instead of shelter, we build houses with more bathrooms than people, then fill them with possessions
- Instead of food, we demand restaurant-quality experiences where "the cows have been massaged and vegetables sung to"
- Instead of gathering with family and friends, we travel halfway around the world to eat the same fast food franchise so we don't "miss out"

We demand the right weight, the right look, the right image, the right style, the right course, the right job. This is anxious work—exhausting, endless, never satisfied. Yet Jesus calls us away from this trap.

## Mary: Choosing What Is Better

Mary sits at Jesus's feet, listening to his word. This isn't passivity—it's discipleship in its most active form.

> 39 She had a sister called Mary, who sat at the Lord's feet listening to what he said.

"Sitting at the Lord's feet" is the position of a disciple, a place of humility and learning. It echoes the example of the sinful woman in Luke 7 who washed Jesus's feet with her tears, and Jairus who fell at Jesus's feet pleading for help. Mary understands something Martha has forgotten: the privilege of being in the presence of Jesus.

The Queen of Sheba traveled to the ends of the earth to hear Solomon's wisdom. Yet here, something far greater has come to Mary—the King of the universe himself. She's not in the kitchen. She's not anxious. She's not distracted. She's peacefully present, listening.

### The Fundamental Truth: Life Is More Than Food

Deuteronomy 8:3 establishes the principle that resonates through Scripture:

> Man does not live on bread alone but on every word that comes from the mouth of the Lord.

Food sustains us for the next few hours. God's word sustains us for life itself—both for how to live now and for eternal life. A baby can be fed and kept warm, but without attention, eye contact, and relationship, something essential is missing. Similarly, we cannot live on bread alone.

This doesn't mean food is unimportant—clothes are important, shelter is important, food is important. But we often assign them far too high a value. We've inflated legitimate needs into desperate, anxiety-driven pursuits. The critical shift is this: **life is more important than these material things**.

Jesus points directly to Mary's choice:

> 42 But few things are needed—or indeed only one. Mary has chosen what is better, and it will not be taken away from her.

The word of God won't be taken away. It endures. It transforms. It saves. Material possessions? They're temporary. Anxiety about them? Fruitless.

## The Practical Application

### Reordering Our Priorities

- **Read the Bible.** This is the primary way we listen to Jesus's word now that he's ascended. Don't pretend Scripture doesn't exist; don't hide from it behind endless distractions.

- **Keep the important things important.** Gather with others around God's word. On Sundays, hear the Bible read and explained. Let your children experience the joy of learning Scripture with friends. Food can help facilitate these gatherings—but it's not the point.

- **Recognize that the word of God has built Western civilization** through Jesus's wisdom and offers hope beyond this life through his death and resurrection. This should relieve our anxiousness.

- **Don't become a restaurant.** Yes, prepare food for gatherings. Yes, celebrate with meals. Whether it's Korean food, Mexican, Chinese for Lunar New Year, or hot dog competitions for Australia Day—these can all have their place. But let them serve the purpose of drawing people together around God's word, not become the main event.

- **Extend invitations with the right motivation.** Invite people not primarily for the meal, but so you might speak more of God's word together. When young people prepare food for gatherings, it's designed to help people linger, to stay, to engage—not to showcase culinary skill.

- **Remember what camps and gatherings are really for.** The effort required is significant, but the intention is clear: gather people so they might hear God's word, bring their friends, and experience community centered on Scripture.

### The Heart of the Matter

Man does not live by bread alone. Nor by nachos or tortillas. He lives by the word of God. As we enjoy meals together, let this truth anchor us: **we are sorted out for this life through the wisdom of God's word, and we are sorted out for the next life through the death and resurrection of Jesus.**

This is what makes life more important than any meal.
