# Romans 2:1-29

**Title:** The Judgment of God  
**Date:** November 11, 2012  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Problem: Incongruency Between Compliance and Judgment

> Therefore you have no excuse, O man, every one of you who judges. For in passing judgment on another you condemn yourself, because you, the judge, practice the very same things. (2:1)

Paul opens with a radical challenge: those who judge others while committing the same sins stand condemned by their own standard. This applies particularly to the Jewish audience, who believed their covenant status exempted them from judgment.

> We know that the judgment of God rightly falls on those who practice such things. Do you suppose, O man—you who judge those who practice such things and yet do them yourself—that you will escape the judgment of God? (2:2-3)

The rhetorical question exposes a dangerous illusion: that performing the role of judge somehow grants immunity from judgment itself.

> Or do you presume on the riches of his kindness and forbearance and patience, not knowing that God's kindness is meant to lead you to repentance? But because of your hard and impenitent heart you are storing up wrath for yourself on the day of wrath when God's righteous judgment will be revealed. (2:4-5)

Paul identifies the root problem—hearts hardened against repentance, stubbornly accumulating judgment rather than responding to God's patient kindness.

## God's Congruency: Performance and Judgment

> He will render to each one according to his works: to those who by patience in well-doing seek for glory and honor and immortality, he will give eternal life; but for those who are self-seeking and do not obey the truth, but obey unrighteousness, there will be wrath and fury. (2:6-8)

God's judgment operates on a consistent principle: alignment between what you practice and what you receive. Those who persistently do good receive eternal life; those who pursue unrighteousness receive wrath.

> There will be tribulation and distress for every human being who does evil, the Jew first and also the Greek, but glory and honor and peace for everyone who does good, the Jew first and also the Greek. For God shows no partiality. (2:9-11)

Notice the repetition of "Jew first and also the Greek"—God's judgment bracket applies universally, with no exceptions based on ethnic privilege or covenant status. God shows no favoritism.

### The Universal Standard: Obedience to the Law

> For all who have sinned without the law will also perish without the law, and all who have sinned under the law will be judged by the law. For it is not the hearers of the law who are righteous before God, but the doers of the law who will be justified. (2:12-13)

Paul establishes a crucial principle: everyone will be judged according to the light they've received. The Gentiles will face judgment according to conscience and natural law; the Jews according to the written Torah. What matters is not hearing the law but keeping it. Intellectual assent is worthless without obedience.

## Case 1: Gentiles Who Obey by Nature

> For when Gentiles, who do not have the law, by nature do what the law requires, they are a law to themselves, even though they do not have the law. They show that the work of the law is written on their hearts, while their conscience also bears witness, and their conflicting thoughts accuse or even excuse them on that day when, according to my gospel, God judges the secrets of men by Christ Jesus. (2:14-16)

This remarkable passage affirms that Gentiles—God-fearers and believers without Torah—demonstrate the law's requirements through their nature. The law is inscribed on their hearts through the Spirit. Their conscience witnesses to this obedience. Even their internal conflicts between accusing and defending thoughts reveal that they understand God's moral standard.

On judgment day, Christ will reveal the hidden motivations behind every action, vindicating those who truly obeyed.

## Case 2: Jews Who Fail to Obey

> But if you call yourself a Jew and rely on the law and boast in God and know his will and approve what is excellent, because you are instructed from the law; and if you are sure that you yourself are a guide to the blind, a light to those who are in darkness, an instructor of the foolish, a teacher of children, having in the law the embodiment of knowledge and truth—(2:17-20)

Paul paints a portrait of the Jewish teacher: confident in their role as moral guide, instructed in God's will, entrusted with the law as embodied truth. Their position is genuinely privileged.

> But you then who teach others, do you not teach yourself? While you preach against stealing, do you steal? You who say that one must not commit adultery, do you commit adultery? You who abhor idols, do you rob temples? You who boast in the law dishonor God by breaking the law. (2:21-23)

Yet this confidence crumbles in the face of hypocrisy. The one who teaches others to abstain from stealing steals. The one who condemns adultery commits it. The one who denounces idolatry robs temples. Their boasting in the law becomes active dishonoring of God.

> For, as it is written, The name of God is blasphemed among the Gentiles because of you. (2:24)

The consequence is severe: God's name is defamed among the very Gentiles the Jews claim to instruct. Their disobedience negates their witness.

## Circumcision: Sign of Obedience or Self-Deception?

> For circumcision indeed is of value if you obey the law, but if you break the law, your circumcision becomes uncircumcision. So, if a man who is uncircumcised keeps the precepts of the law, will not his uncircumcision be regarded as circumcision? Then he who is physically uncircumcised but keeps the law will condemn you who have the written code and circumcision but break the law. (2:25-27)

Circumcision, the sign of God's covenant people, possesses value only through obedience. The moment you break the law, circumcision becomes meaningless—in effect, uncircumcision. Conversely, the Gentile who keeps the law's requirements will, on judgment day, condemn the circumcised Jew who possesses the written code but disobeys it. The sign means nothing without the reality it represents.

## The True Jew: Inward Reality Over Outward Status

> For no one is a Jew who is merely one outwardly, nor is circumcision outward and physical. But a Jew is one inwardly, and circumcision is a matter of the heart, by the Spirit, not by the letter. His praise is not from man but from God. (2:28-29)

Paul's conclusion redefines what it truly means to be God's people. Being a Jew is not an identity inherited through birth or marked by external ritual. True Jewishness is inward—a transformed heart circumcised by the Spirit, not just the flesh. The genuine mark of God's people is not the approval of others but the approval of God Himself.

This teaching—delivered with pastoral urgency and intellectual precision—calls every hearer to examine whether their obedience matches their profession, whether their practice aligns with their privilege, and whether their hearts have truly been circumcised by the Spirit of God.
