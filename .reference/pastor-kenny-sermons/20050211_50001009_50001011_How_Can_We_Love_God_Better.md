# Philippians 1:9-11

**Title:** How Can We Love God Better?

**Date:** February 11, 2005

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## The Core Principle

> And this is my prayer: that your love may abound more and more in knowledge and depth of insight, so that you may be able to discern what is best and may be pure and blameless until the day of Christ, filled with the fruit of righteousness that comes through Jesus Christ—to the glory and praise of God. (Philippians 1:9-11)

**Main Idea:** We can love God better by pursuing knowledge and understanding of Him.

---

## Paul's Deep Affection for the Philippians

The apostle Paul writes to the Christians in Philippi with genuine, heartfelt love. Notice how his affection runs throughout this passage:

- "I thank my God every time I remember you" (v. 3)
- "In all my prayers for all of you, I always pray with joy" (v. 4)
- "It is right for me to feel this way about all of you, since I have you in my heart" (v. 7)
- "God can testify how I long for all of you with the affection of Christ Jesus" (v. 8)

From this foundation of love, Paul makes his prayer: that their love for God would **abound more and more**.

---

## Love Must Be Guided by Knowledge

Paul doesn't pray for mere emotion or feeling. He prays that their love would grow "in **knowledge and depth of insight**." This is crucial: true love for God is educated and intelligent, not superficial.

### What Kind of Knowledge?

The knowledge Paul speaks of is **the knowledge of how to please God**—understanding what is right and best in His eyes. It means:

- Knowing what it means to be pure and blameless before God
- Discerning between right and wrong
- Understanding God's character and purposes revealed in Scripture
- Being equipped to live righteously through Jesus Christ

This knowledge produces **the fruit of righteousness**—a life that glorifies God through obedience and moral clarity.

---

## A Personal Illustration: Learning What Love Really Means

Early in my marriage, I wanted to show my wife how much I cared. I planned what I thought would be the perfect day: we'd browse garage sales in our neighborhood, then enjoy cheap Chinese noodles at a food court—a bargain I was proud of!

I was sincere. I tried my best. I paid for it. But my wife hated it. She found the garage sales boring, the food court stuffy and noisy (which meant we couldn't talk), and she doesn't even particularly like Chinese food. What I loved, I assumed she would love. What I thought was thoughtful, she found disappointing.

**What went wrong?** I didn't *know* her. I didn't take time to ask what she actually wanted. I didn't have the knowledge and insight necessary to love her well.

**The same is true with God.** We cannot love Him better without understanding who He is and what pleases Him. That understanding comes from Scripture.

---

## Where Does This Knowledge Come From?

This isn't mysterious or hidden knowledge—it comes from the **Bible**. Yet we're often tempted to neglect it. We think we could sing instead of studying, play a game instead of reading, or that Bible study takes away from Christian fellowship.

But here's the truth: **Time invested in knowing God through Scripture is time well spent.** It is through the Bible that we come to know and love God more deeply. And as our love for God grows, we're equipped to love others better—both Christians and non-Christians.

### Loving Non-Christians Well
When we know Scripture well, we can explain the gospel accurately and give people a true picture of who God is. What could be more loving than that?

### Loving Christians Well
As we understand God's plans and purposes revealed in Jesus, we're able to help one another discern right from wrong. We become less vulnerable to the world's deceptions and can stand together in truth until Christ's return.

---

## True Love Is Obedience, Not Emotion

Jesus Himself teaches us what loving God really means:

> Whoever has my commands and obeys them, he is the one who loves me. He who loves me will be loved by my Father, and I too will love him and show myself to him. (John 14:21)

Notice: The one who loves God is **not necessarily** the one with the most feelings or the most emotional expression. It's **not** the person who wears crosses or speaks religiously with fervor.

**The one who truly loves God is the one who knows His commands and obeys them.** It's the one who understands what pleases the Father and does it.

---

## Application: Make Knowing Scripture Your Priority

If you want to love God better and love each other better, then **know and understand the Bible.**

Yes, this will be hard work. But Jesus has already done the hardest part for us. He came to earth to reveal God to us. He showed us God's justice and love on the cross. He has given us the complete revelation of God's character and will in Scripture.

We have everything we need. The question is: will we invest the time and effort to study it, understand it, and act on it?

---

## The Lesson for My Marriage

Now I understand what went wrong that day. I tried to love my wife, but without knowing her—without the knowledge and depth of insight into what she actually wanted and needed. I should have asked first. I should have listened.

And so it is with God. **Let us prioritize the knowledge of Scripture—not to be proud or judgmental, but so that we can love God better and love each other better.**

The next time someone questions why you're going to Bible study, answer with confidence: *"So that I can love you better."*
