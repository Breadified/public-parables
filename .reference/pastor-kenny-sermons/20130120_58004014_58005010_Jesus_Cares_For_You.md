# Hebrews 4:14–5:10

**Title:** Jesus Cares For You

**Date:** January 20, 2013

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## The Problem: Useless Advice

Sometimes Christianity can feel useless. We celebrate the past—Jesus came for you, Jesus died for you. We anticipate the future—heaven is wonderful. But what about today? Is Christianity just "pie in the sky when you die"? What about living here and now? What good is faith if it doesn't help us in our present struggles?

This is where many people feel abandoned by religion. They're told what happened and what will happen, but they're left wondering: Does God actually care about my life right now?

## 1. God Is On Our Side

> Therefore, since we have a great high priest who has gone through the heavens, Jesus the Son of God, let us hold firmly to the faith we profess. (Hebrews 4:14)

In the time of Jesus, the Jewish high priest served as a mediator between God and people. Because humanity had sinned, God's ears were closed to them. The high priest would perform sacrifices at the temple to bridge that gap—making atonement so that God would hear them again.

When you faced a serious problem, you needed an expert. A legal problem? You need a lawyer. A car problem? You need a mechanic. A spiritual problem? You needed the high priest to bring a sacrifice on your behalf.

Jesus is that high priest—but infinitely greater. He stands on our side, mediating between us and God.

## 2. He Is Like Us

> Every high priest is selected from among men and is appointed to represent them in matters related to God, to offer gifts and sacrifices for sins. He is able to deal gently with those who are ignorant and are going astray, since he himself is subject to weakness. (Hebrews 5:1–2)

For the high priest to represent the people effectively, he had to be one of them. He had to understand their struggles, their weaknesses, their temptations. Only someone who knows what it's like can truly sympathize.

> For we do not have a high priest who is unable to sympathize with our weaknesses, but we have one who has been tempted in every way, just as we are—yet was without sin. (Hebrews 4:15)

Jesus knows what we face because He became fully human. Consider what Jesus experienced:

- Persecution and abuse
- Bullying and unfair treatment
- Poverty
- Betrayal by His closest friend
- Trouble with the law
- Being single
- Living with siblings
- Death in the family (His father Joseph)
- Career uncertainty and change

Jesus knows what it's like to be human. He understands every temptation we face. He knows how hard some things are, and how easy others feel. We must never think that God is aloof, distant, or unmoved by our humanity. When Jesus says He understands, it's not mere sympathy—He means it. He has walked this earth. He knows exactly what we're going through.

> For this reason he had to be made like them, fully human in every way, in order that he might become a merciful and faithful high priest in service to God, and that he might make atonement for the sins of the people. Because he himself suffered when he was tempted, he is able to help those who are being tempted. (Hebrews 2:17–18)

Because of this, we can approach the throne of grace with confidence:

> Let us then approach the throne of grace with confidence, so that we may receive mercy and find grace to help us in our time of need. (Hebrews 4:16)

We find an understanding ear—not judgment, not useless advice, but genuine compassion. As Peter reminds us:

> Cast all your anxiety on him because he cares for you. (1 Peter 5:7)

## 3. He Is Not Like Us

Here's the problem: A sympathetic friend who shares my struggle can empathize, but can they actually fix it? An electrician who appreciates my electrical problem but can't fix it is useless. I need someone who understands *and* can solve the problem.

Jesus must be both like us *and* different from us.

> For we do not have a high priest who is unable to sympathize with our weaknesses, but we have one who has been tempted in every way, just as we are—yet was without sin. (Hebrews 4:15)

Without sin. This is crucial. Every high priest of old had to atone for his own sin first. But how could the blood of bulls and goats ever truly bridge the gap between sinful humanity and the righteous God? How could a mere human priest—one who couldn't even get himself right with God—get others right with God? It's like a man in prison offering solutions to escape prison. If his solution worked, he wouldn't be in prison.

The old priests kept offering sacrifices day after day, year after year—proof that their system didn't work. They were experts in their own failure.

But Jesus is without sin. He is the true expert on sin, not because He practices it, but because He resisted every temptation. He understands sin deeply—its power, its seduction, its consequences—and He is the one who can deal with it effectively. He is the one who can teach us how to overcome it.

> No one takes this honor upon himself; he must be called by God, just as Aaron was. So Christ also did not take upon himself the glory of becoming a high priest. But God said to him, "You are my Son; today I have become your Father." And he says in another place, "You are a priest forever, in the order of Melchizedek." (Hebrews 5:4–6)

Jesus wasn't just another priest in the line of Abraham's descendants. Melchizedek was an ancient high priest from the time of Abraham, a mysterious figure to whom Abraham himself showed deference. This means all the Israelite priests were subordinate to Melchizedek. Jesus is claiming to be a priest in that greater order—not just a better priest, but the greatest priest.

> Although he was a son, he learned obedience from what he suffered and, once made perfect, he became the source of eternal salvation for all who obey him and was designated by God to be high priest in the order of Melchizedek. (Hebrews 5:8–10)

Jesus didn't just sympathize with our condition—He became the sacrifice Himself. The old priests brought lambs and bulls and goats. Jesus gave Himself on the cross. This is why we can now be free. Jesus took our place. He paid the price we could not pay.

## 4. Jesus Cares for Us—Right Now

> During the days of his life on earth, he offered up prayers and petitions with loud cries and tears to the one who could save him from death, and he was heard because of his reverent submission. (Hebrews 5:7)

Here is the God who hears prayer. In the Garden of Gethsemane, Jesus prayed for us with loud cries and tears. God heard Him. Jesus intercedes for you.

**Finally, a mediator who understands.** Finally, a priest who can actually fix the problem. No more useless advice.

Even when you don't know what to pray, Jesus prays for you. He cares for each of you deeply. He knows the anxieties you face—the secret struggles you only tell a select few, the things you haven't told anyone about. Jesus knows. And He prays for you.

This is not just "pie in the sky when you die." This is steak on your plate while you wait. Jesus cares for you right now, today, in the midst of your real struggles. He is with you. He understands. And He is able to help.
