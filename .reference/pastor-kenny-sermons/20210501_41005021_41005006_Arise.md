# Mark 5:21-5:6

**Title:** Arise
**Date:** 29 May 2021
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Fear and Faith in Crisis

Throughout Mark 4:35-6:6a, we encounter a profound theme that weaves through every encounter with Jesus: the struggle between fear and faith. The disciples tremble in the storm. A woman bleeds in silence, afraid to speak. A synagogue ruler despairs when his daughter seems lost. Yet in each situation, Jesus responds not with condemnation, but with a gentle invitation to trust.

What is remarkable is that fear doesn't disqualify us from faith—it often precedes it. The woman who touched Jesus' cloak was afraid, yet her very fear drove her to seek Him. The synagogue ruler's despair became the doorway through which his daughter was healed. Even the disciples, terrified in the boat, were learning to recognize who stood among them.

## Who Is This Man?

This entire passage invites us to grapple with one essential question: Who is Jesus, really? 

Different people arrived at different answers:
- The disciples saw a teacher who commanded even the wind and waves
- The demon-possessed man recognized Him as the Son of God
- The woman recognized Him as a healer
- His own hometown saw only a carpenter's son

Yet here is the peculiar irony: Those closest to Him—His family, His neighbors—struggled most to see Him clearly. Meanwhile, those society had written off—the demoniac, the bleeding woman, the desperate father—recognized His power and submitted to His authority.

## Compassion Over Judgment

What strikes us most deeply in these passages is Jesus' consistent response: He never rebuked the fearful. Instead, He met them with compassion. To the bleeding woman, He said, "Daughter, your faith has healed you; go in peace" (5:34). To Jairus, faced with the news of his daughter's death, Jesus said simply, "Don't be afraid; just believe" (5:36).

This reveals Jesus' true character. He is not a harsh judge waiting to condemn our weakness. He is a shepherd who understands human fear and invites us, gently and persistently, to trust Him despite it.

## The Power of Belief

The feeding miracles in this section carry a deeper significance. When Jesus fed the five thousand with five loaves and two fish, He was demonstrating something essential: abundance flows from trust in His provision. When He later fed the four thousand, He was reinforcing the same lesson—that faith in His character produces impossible outcomes.

Yet notice how the disciples struggled to remember these miracles when fear returned. Jesus had to remind them: "Do you still not understand?" (6:52). We are not alone in this struggle. The disciples walked with the risen Lord and still fought doubt. The question is not whether we will face doubt, but whether we will, like them, gradually learn to trust.

## A Living Faith

True faith is not passive agreement with doctrine. It is an active, lived confidence in Jesus' character. The woman didn't just believe *about* Jesus—she believed *in* Him enough to push through a crowd. Jairus didn't just hope—he moved toward Jesus despite receiving terrible news.

As we read Mark's account, we are invited into the same journey. What would it look like to move toward Jesus in our fear rather than away? What would it mean to claim His promise: "The kingdom of God belongs to such as these" (Mark 10:14)?

## For Reflection

- What fears most commonly steal your peace?
- Can you identify a time when fear actually led you closer to Jesus?
- How would your daily choices change if you truly believed Jesus had authority over every circumstance you face?
- What "proof" of Jesus' character do you need to see more clearly today?
