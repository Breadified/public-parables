# Matthew 5:33-40

**Title:** Keeping Promises  
**Date:** 3 June 2018  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Law and the Heart

Jesus continues the pattern established in the Sermon on the Mount—taking Old Testament law and pressing deeper into the heart of God's intention.

> "You have heard that it was said, 'You shall not commit adultery.'" (Matthew 5:27)

The commandment against adultery is clear. But Jesus reveals how we rationalize and find loopholes in God's law, trying to keep the letter while ignoring the spirit.

> "But I tell you that anyone who looks at a woman lustfully has already committed adultery with her in his heart." (Matthew 5:28)

Jesus isn't condemning natural attraction. He's addressing *deliberate, lustful looking*—the intention to possess what isn't ours. Adultery begins in the mind long before it manifests in action.

## The Radical Call to Action

> "If your right eye causes you to stumble, gouge it out and throw it away. It is better for you to lose one part of your body than for your whole body to be thrown into hell. And if your right hand causes you to stumble, cut it off and throw it away. It is better for you to lose one part of your body than for your whole body to go into hell." (Matthew 5:29-30)

This isn't literal instruction—it's hyperbolic language meant to shock us into seriousness. Jesus illustrates with the image of gangrene: a small infection that spreads, threatening the entire body. When left untreated, it destroys everything.

## The Application

The call is urgent: do something about the sources of temptation in your life. Remove what leads you away from God. Be willing to sacrifice temporary comfort for the health of your soul. The stakes are eternal.
