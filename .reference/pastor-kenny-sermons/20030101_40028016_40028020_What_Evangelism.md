## The Great Commission

> Then the eleven disciples went to Galilee, to the mountain where Jesus had told them to go. When they saw him, they worshiped him; but some doubted. Then Jesus came to them and said, "All authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me. Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, and teaching them to obey everything I have commanded you. And surely I am with you always, to the very end of the age." (Matthew 28:16-20)

---

## What Is Evangelism?

Evangelism flows from two foundational truths about Jesus revealed in this passage.

### 1. Jesus Has All Authority

Jesus comes to his disciples and declares: *"All authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me."*

Notice that some of the disciples doubted (v. 17), yet Jesus's authority remains absolute. Doubt does not diminish truth. This authority is real because of what has just happened—the Resurrection. Jesus has been raised from the dead, seated at the right hand of God, and will be the Judge of all humanity. This is the gospel's core message.

As Paul wrote:

> For what I received I passed on to you as of first importance: that Christ died for our sins according to the Scriptures, that he was buried, that he was raised on the third day according to the Scriptures. (1 Corinthians 15:3-4)

Because Jesus rose from the dead, those who follow him will also follow him into heaven and receive eternal life. This is the good news we proclaim.

**Application:** Is Jesus the ruler of your life?

### 2. Jesus Gives a Command

Because Jesus has authority, he gives a clear command to his disciples—and to us:

> Go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, and teaching them to obey everything I have commanded you. And surely I am with you always, to the very end of the age.

The command is specific:
- **Go** and make disciples wherever you are
- **Baptize** them—converting and cleansing them through the symbolism of baptism
- **Teach** them to obey everything Jesus has commanded

This is not a suggestion to have a nice life, build nice buildings, or do whatever we want. His final instruction to the church is singular in focus: *make disciples*.

**Application:** Those who follow Jesus as Lord obey his commands. He wants us to make disciples at school, among our siblings, in our neighborhoods. Evangelism doesn't require a special place or special time. It happens wherever the gospel message is spoken—whether reading Scripture with someone one-on-one, sharing briefly with a stranger, or raising the issue of faith in conversations with friends.

Evangelism is simply passing on the message of hope. The good news. The promise of living forever.

---

## Why Evangelism Matters

We might think of someone like Buster Martin, a 104-year-old marathon runner who defied the odds of longevity through sheer determination. Yet even he eventually died. Every person, no matter how long they live, faces the reality of death.

But because Easter has happened—because Jesus rose from the dead and defeated death—we evangelize. We have a message that matters eternally. We share not just earthly hope, but the hope of eternal life through Christ.

This is what evangelism is.
