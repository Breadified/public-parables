# Philippians 1:3-11

**Title:** Being Thankful (for the Partnership)  
**Date:** 2 November 2004  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Partnership in the Gospel (vv. 3-5)

> I thank my God in all my remembrance of you, always in every prayer of mine for you all making my prayer with joy, because of your partnership in the gospel from the first day until now.

Paul opens his letter to the Philippians with profound gratitude. He thanks God not occasionally, but every time he remembers them and every time he prays for them. His thanksgiving flows from one central reality: the Philippians have been his partners in the gospel from the very first day until the present moment.

What does partnership in the gospel mean? It is the working together toward a common goal—the proclamation of Christ. This is not a solo endeavor. God has intentionally placed Christians among other Christians so they might labor together.

The Philippian partnership with Paul took concrete form:
- Paul traveled the Mediterranean preaching the gospel
- The Philippians shared in his troubles and hardships
- They supported him financially when no other group would
- They supplied all his material needs, even when separated by distance
- They gave generously and continuously to sustain his ministry

This is partnership in action—a church supporting its missionary with both prayer and provision, making possible what one person alone could not accomplish.

## Partners in Grace (vv. 6-7)

> And I am sure of this, that he who began a good work in you will bring it to completion at the day of Jesus Christ. It is right for me to feel this way about you all, because I hold you in my heart, for you are all partakers with me of grace, both in my imprisonment and in the defense and confirmation of the gospel.

Paul's confidence rests not on the Philippians' abilities, but on God's character. God has begun a good work in them, and He will bring it to completion when Christ returns. This is Paul's assurance.

The Philippians are more than pleasant churchgoers doing a good thing on the weekend. They are partners in the deepest sense—partakers together of the same grace. When Paul was imprisoned, facing trial and potential death, the Philippians did not abandon him. They stood firm. They did not renounce their faith to save themselves. Instead, they defended and confirmed the gospel even when it cost them socially and personally.

This commitment reveals the true basis of their partnership: not activity or friendship, but the shared grace of Christ himself—the grace that comes from trusting in Jesus, whose death secured forgiveness for sinners and whose coming judgment will one day judge all creation. That reality transcends any earthly partnership.

## A Prayer for Growing Love (vv. 9-11)

> And it is my prayer that your love may abound more and more, with knowledge and all discernment, so that you may approve what is excellent, and so be pure and blameless for the day of Christ, filled with the fruit of righteousness that comes through Jesus Christ, to the glory and praise of God.

Paul's prayer for the Philippians is not that their activities multiply, but that their love increase. This is not romantic sentiment, but a committed, intelligent love—the kind that stood by the imprisoned Paul, that makes sacrifices, that thinks carefully about the good of others.

Love grounded in knowledge and discernment is the guide. Without understanding Scripture and spiritual wisdom, we cannot truly love others well. We need both heart and mind—affection guided by truth.

Paul's ultimate prayer is that they will be pure and blameless when Christ returns, overflowing with the fruit of righteousness that only comes through Jesus Christ. All of this brings glory and praise to God.

## Application for Our Partnership

When we gather as a church community, we are not primarily a voluntary organization doing good works. We are partners in the gospel—united in the same grace that saves us, standing together for the truth of Christ's death and resurrection, and committed to proclaiming His name.

A church full of activities, friendships, and fun is not enough. If we have not been faithful to the gospel itself, we are merely a social club. The gospel is what makes us distinct—and the gospel must remain our foundation.

Our partnership finds expression in:
- Opening Scripture together and proclaiming Christ's death and coming judgment
- Enduring hardship and rejection as we share our faith
- Standing firm in faith even through personal sorrow and loss
- Sacrificing our pride and comfort to invite others into God's kingdom

As we pray for one another, let us not focus on what remains undone. Instead, let us be grateful for the partnership we have been given—both in our shared activities and in our common faith. And let us pray that our love for one another may abound more and more, guided by knowledge and discernment, so that we remain faithful to the gospel that has made us one family in Christ.
