# John 4:1-54

**Title:** The Woman at the Well and Living Water
**Date:** 22 February 2015
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Samaritan Context

Understanding Jesus's encounter with the Samaritan woman requires knowing the deep cultural and religious division between Jews and Samaritans.

The Samaritans recognized only the Pentateuch (the first five books of Moses) as authoritative Scripture. This meant that all the later Old Testament writings that commanded worship at the Jerusalem temple held no binding authority for them. They actually incorporated the obligation to worship at Mount Gerizim into their understanding of the Decalogue itself.

The rift between these two groups only deepened over time. After the Jewish people returned from the Babylonian exile, the Samaritans actively opposed the restoration of Jerusalem and its temple. The tension finally erupted into open conflict during the second century B.C., when the Samaritans sided with Syrian monarchs against the Jews—a betrayal the Jews answered by destroying the Samaritan temple on Mount Gerizim in 128 B.C.

These were indeed "worship wars"—a centuries-long conflict rooted in competing claims about where God should be worshipped and which Scripture should be trusted.
