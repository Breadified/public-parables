# Romans 8:1-39

**Title:** Belonging to God
**Date:** 11 August 2013
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Benefits of Belonging

When we belong to something, we gain privileges. Think of membership clubs—American Express cardholders enjoy frequent flyer points and protection. Gym memberships grant access to facilities. Golf clubs offer special privileges. Even a simple 10% card at the local grocery shop provides tangible benefits.

In the same way, Christians belong to God's exclusive club, and the benefits are incomparably greater than any earthly membership.

> Therefore, there is now no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus, because through Christ Jesus the law of the Spirit of life set me free from the law of sin and death. (Romans 8:1-2)

This is the privilege of belonging: **no condemnation from God** and **freedom from the law of sin and death**.

### A Living Picture of Membership

Imagine immigrating to a new country. The moment you arrive, you gain immediate benefits—free education, social welfare, protection from persecution, access to opportunity. I was born in Malaysia, where my future seemed limited. University felt unlikely, international travel seemed distant. But coming to Australia opened doors I never expected. Suddenly I had the chance to earn three degrees, to travel freely, to build a life of possibility.

For refugees, the benefits were even more immediate—legal protection, access to courts, housing, basic provisions from the government itself.

When we join God's club through Christ, we gain immeasurable benefits. And we gain them through an act of divine love that deserves our deepest gratitude.

## How We Gained These Privileges

### Jesus Joined Our Club First

The foundation of our membership rests on one profound truth: **Jesus became human so we could become His**.

> For what the law was powerless to do in that it was weakened by the sinful nature, God did by sending his own Son in the likeness of sinful man to be a sin offering. And so he condemned sin in sinful man. (Romans 8:3)

Jesus entered humanity—our club, our condition—for a singular purpose: **to be our sin offering**. He became the sacrifice we could never be.

Here lies the heart of the gospel: sin demands punishment. Two options existed—punish us, or punish Him. Jesus chose to take our place. He **paid the subscription fee** with His own blood, making our entry into God's family possible.

### Now We Must Become Like Him

Belonging to this club brings implications. Verse 4 reveals the profound purpose:

> So that the righteous requirements of the law might be fully met in us, who do not live according to the sinful nature but according to the Spirit. (Romans 8:4)

What does this mean? Yes, Christ fulfilled the law's demands for us through His substitution. But notice the second part: it describes *righteous people*—those who don't live by the sinful nature but by the Spirit. We are called to embody the very righteousness Christ demonstrated.

## Living as Club Members

### The Two Paths Before Us

> Those who live according to the sinful nature have their minds set on what that nature desires; but those who live in accordance with the Spirit have their minds set on what the Spirit desires. The mind of sinful man is death, but the mind controlled by the Spirit is life and peace. The sinful mind is hostile to God. It does not submit to God's law, nor can it do so. Those controlled by the sinful nature cannot please God. (Romans 8:5-8)

Paul presents a stark contrast:

- **Once:** Our minds were set on the desires of the flesh  
- **Now:** Our minds can be set on what the Spirit desires

- **Once:** We had the mind of death  
- **Now:** We can have the mind of life and peace

- **Once:** We were hostile to God, unable to please Him  
- **Now:** We're free to follow Him fully

### The Obligation of Membership

Think of joining an exclusive school or sports team. Once admitted, don't you act as a member should? Don't you attend its activities, follow its rules, show up to practice, and pursue its higher standards? Of course you do. To join and then ignore membership would be absurd.

We have entered God's exclusive club. We can now follow Him—and we must live accordingly.

> You, however, are controlled not by the sinful nature but by the Spirit, if the Spirit of God lives in you. And if anyone does not have the Spirit of Christ, he does not belong to Christ. (Romans 8:9)

The Spirit of Christ marks us as His. And because we belong to Him, we share in His life:

> But if Christ is in you, your body is dead because of sin, yet your spirit is alive because of righteousness. And if the Spirit of him who raised Jesus from the dead is living in you, he who raised Christ from the dead will also give life to your mortal bodies through his Spirit, who lives in you. (Romans 8:10-11)

### Put to Death the Misdeeds of the Body

> Therefore, brothers, we have an obligation—but it is not to the sinful nature, to live according to it. For if you live according to the sinful nature, you will die; but if by the Spirit you put to death the misdeeds of the body, you will live. (Romans 8:12-13)

We are no longer enslaved to our old nature. We've been adopted into God's family:

> Because those who are led by the Spirit of God are sons of God. For you did not receive a spirit that makes you a slave again to fear, but you received the Spirit of sonship. And by him we cry, "Abba, Father." The Spirit himself testifies with our spirit that we are God's children. (Romans 8:14-16)

Like a child adopted into a new family, we must learn to live differently—with new values, new allegiances, new habits. We put away envy, malice, and the deeds of our former life.

### Persevere Through Suffering

Yet belonging to God's family brings not only blessing but also suffering:

> Now if we are children, then we are heirs—heirs of God and co-heirs with Christ, if indeed we share in his sufferings in order that we may also share in his glory. (Romans 8:17)

This is the full reality of membership. We share the good times *and* the difficult times. How can we accept the benefits but reject the costs? A family member cannot simply enjoy family privileges while refusing to contribute or sacrifice for the family's good.

Sometimes this means pain. But Paul calls us to keep perspective:

> I consider that our present sufferings are not worth comparing with the glory that will be revealed in us. (Romans 8:18)

The entire creation groans under the weight of decay, waiting for God's children to be fully revealed (Romans 8:19-21). We, too, groan as we wait for our final redemption:

> Not only so, but we ourselves, who have the firstfruits of the Spirit, groan inwardly as we wait eagerly for our adoption as sons, the redemption of our bodies. For in this hope we were saved. But hope that is seen is no hope at all. Who hopes for what he already has? But if we hope for what we do not yet have, we wait for it patiently. (Romans 8:23-25)

### God Never Leaves Us Alone in Suffering

In our weakness and confusion, the Spirit intercedes for us:

> In the same way, the Spirit helps us in our weakness. We do not know what we ought to pray for, but the Spirit himself intercedes for us with groans that words cannot express. And he who searches our hearts knows the mind of the Spirit, because the Spirit intercedes for the saints in accordance with God's will. (Romans 8:26-27)

God will help us even when we're confused, even when unbelief nags at us in the face of suffering. We should see this suffering as discipline—short-term pain that produces long-term good.

### God Works All Things for Our Ultimate Good

> And we know that in all things God works for the good of those who love him, who have been called according to his purpose. For those God foreknew he also predestined to be conformed to the likeness of his Son, that he might be the firstborn among many brothers. And those he predestined, he also called; those he called, he also justified; those he justified, he also glorified. (Romans 8:28-30)

What is "the good" God works toward? It is *not* financial wealth, health, intelligence, or promotions. It is not a bigger house, another holiday, or your children's admission to prestigious schools.

**The good is this: to become like Jesus.** To put away the misdeeds of the body as He did. To love sacrificially as He loved. To suffer patiently as He suffered.

Jesus is the firstborn among many brothers, and we are being conformed to His image.

*Application:* If you find yourself in pain, remember that God is there. Don't curse God. If your life feels hectic and disorganized, the solution isn't to abandon God, church, or Scripture. Rather, slow down other parts of your life. Stop chasing money, success, and the perfect image. Instead, rest in God and rely on your Christian brothers and sisters.

## The Best Club in the World

We belong to a club of unconditional love and acceptance. Best of all, we can never be kicked out—because the club admitted us when we were still sinners. Jesus opened the door with His blood.

> No, in all these things we are more than conquerors through him who loved us. For I am convinced that neither death nor life, neither angels nor demons, neither the present nor the future, nor any powers, neither height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God that is in Christ Jesus our Lord. (Romans 8:37-39)

In light of this unbreakable security, let us:

1. **Put away the misdeeds of the body** —live as members of God's exclusive family
2. **Persevere through suffering** —remembering that our present pain cannot compare to the glory awaiting us

This is what it means to truly belong to God.
