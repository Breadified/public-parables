# John 6:48-54

**Title:** I Am the Bread of Life

**Date:** 7 March 2025

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Understanding Jesus' Metaphor

> 48 I am the bread of life.
>
> 49 Your ancestors ate the manna in the wilderness, yet they died. 50 But here is the bread that comes down from heaven, which anyone may eat and not die.
>
> 51 I am the living bread that came down from heaven. Whoever eats this bread will live forever. This bread is my flesh, which I will give for the life of the world.
>
> 52 Then the Jews began to argue sharply among themselves, "How can this man give us his flesh to eat?"
>
> 53 Jesus said to them, "Very truly I tell you, unless you eat the flesh of the Son of Man and drink his blood, you have no life in you. 54 Whoever eats my flesh and drinks my blood has eternal life, and I will raise them up at the last day.
>
> 63 The Spirit gives life; the flesh counts for nothing. The words I have spoken to you are spirit and they are life.

When Jesus claims "I am the bread of life," He's not speaking literally. Instead, He invites us to understand what this metaphor actually means by looking back to the Exodus narrative—one of the foundational stories of Israel.

## The Old Testament Context: Manna in the Desert

During the Exodus (around 1462 BC), Moses led the Israelites out of Egypt toward the promised land. Because they disobeyed God on the journey, they were condemned to wander in the desert for forty years. Yet in this barren wilderness, God provided miraculously. Each morning, manna—small flakes of bread-like substance, white like coriander seed and tasting like wafers made with honey—appeared on the ground like dew, sustaining the entire nation.

Jesus draws a deliberate comparison between that ancient bread and Himself. Both are sources of nourishment and life in spiritually dry places. Both come from heaven. Just as Jesus had just fed five thousand, He repeats the pattern of divine provision to show that His generation, like their ancestors, is spiritually wandering and in need of sustenance.

But there is a crucial difference: the manna sustained only physical life. The people in the desert eventually died, and so will everyone who ate at Jesus' miracle feast. Jesus offers something infinitely greater—bread that gives eternal life, life that transcends death itself.

## The Two Metaphors

### Metaphor #1: Bread = Life-Giving Sustenance

Just as physical bread nourishes the body, Jesus sustains the soul and spirit. He is not merely sustenance for survival; He is the source of eternal existence.

### Metaphor #2: Eat Flesh = Believe His Words

When Jesus speaks of eating His flesh and drinking His blood, He is not calling for cannibalism—something prohibited by Old Testament law and, frankly, repugnant. Rather, He is using the most intimate physical act (consuming food) as a picture of spiritual union.

Look to verse 63 for the interpretation: "The Spirit gives life; the flesh counts for nothing. The words I have spoken to you are spirit and they are life."

**The flesh equals His words.** Jesus clarifies that He is not talking about literal consumption but about embracing His teaching. Just as food becomes part of you through digestion, Jesus calls us to make His words part of our very being through belief.

Consider how biography works: through someone's words, you can know them intimately even if you've never met them face-to-face. We have never physically seen Jesus, but His words remain alive in Scripture. The words are not mere information—they are His presence, His character, His authority speaking directly to us.

Later, Peter captures this understanding: "Lord, to whom shall we go? You have the words of eternal life" (John 6:68).

## The Call to Believe

**To eat Jesus is to believe in Him.** Just as eating bread makes it part of your physical body, believing Jesus' words makes Him part of your inner being. This is not mysticism or ritual—it is rational, cognitive faith rooted in understanding and trust.

Jesus invites us to:

- Believe His words when He says He knows the Father
- Believe His words when He warns that God's anger is coming against sin
- Believe His words that He protects us from that judgment through His sacrifice
- Believe Him when He promises eternal life

## How to "Eat" Jesus' Words in Practice

**For the non-Christian:** You have an invitation. Taste and see that His words are good. Believe them and gain eternal life.

**For the believer:** Continue to feed on Jesus' words. Think of it this way: you are what you eat. Consume His words voraciously. Read them. Think hard about them. Use the minds God gave us—reason, logic, discussion, debate. Digest Scripture deeply.

As you internalize His words, let them become your DNA:

- Let them govern your thoughts and neural pathways
- Share them with one another, encouraging others through Scripture
- Let them energize your actions, directing you toward ethical living and love
- Let His words set the trajectory and motion of your entire life

The practical starting point: read the Gospel of John. It contains only twenty-one chapters. Carry it with you—on the bus, train, in your car, during free moments. Metaphorically eat Jesus by consuming His words, believing them, and letting them transform how you live. In this way, you will truly have eternal life.
