# Galatians 3:27-3:37

**Title:** Marriage as an Act of Faith
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Setting the Scene

William and Ling were married during unprecedented calamity. The entire world was gripped by pandemic. People wore masks everywhere. Economies crashed. Families separated across continents. Yet here stood two people committed to marriage in the midst of it all—a beautiful reminder of what it means to trust God when the future is uncertain.

This scene echoes an ancient moment in Scripture:

> People were eating and drinking, marrying and being given in marriage, up to the day Noah entered the ark. (Luke 17:27)

In Noah's time, God was about to judge the world through flood for humanity's sins. Imagine the calamity—not just one storm, but rain falling continuously for forty days. The terror must have been overwhelming. Yet even as judgment approached, people continued to marry. This seems almost irrational. Why commit to another person when so much uncertainty hangs over the future?

## Why Marry in Times of Calamity?

We might reasonably ask: What compels two people to marry when:
- You might get sick
- Jobs might disappear
- Careers might be derailed
- Children might struggle
- Love might fade
- Everything feels uncertain

The answer reveals something profound about the nature of Christian marriage.

## Marriage as an Act of Faith

Marriage is fundamentally **an act of faith**—not primarily faith in another person (though we do depend on each other), but **faith in God and his promises**.

Two sinners will inevitably disappoint each other. This is not pessimism but realism. Yet marriage works because it rests on something deeper: a trust that God is sovereign, that he is in charge regardless of circumstances, and that he will sustain us through calamity and disaster.

As Christians, William and Ling confess that God sent his only Son, Jesus, to live on earth and die on the cross. Through Christ's death and resurrection, they have been secured eternal life. This is not theoretical comfort—it is the foundation of everything. Because God has conquered death itself, no earthly calamity can ultimately separate us from his care.

## The Practical Outworking

This faith takes concrete form: William and Ling will entrust life's uncertainties to God. In return, they commit to obey his commands—to be fruitful, to multiply, to raise the next generation in faith and godliness.

## Their Journey

What a remarkable journey these two have traveled:
- Meeting each other
- Building a relationship
- Getting engaged
- Planning a wedding during lockdown—in a driveway with five people, celebrated over Zoom, with wedding photos taken in an overgrown neighboring lot
- Rescheduling and reorganizing through endless complications
- Facing weather and unexpected obstacles even this week

And there will be more challenges ahead. Marriage is not easier during good times, let alone during calamity. Yet William and Ling have already proven their readiness through their actions. When everything around them urged caution and hesitation, they moved forward. When others held back, they committed. When the world paused, they pressed on.

## A Challenge and a Blessing

It is brave to marry in times of calamity. It is brave to have children when the future seems uncertain. These acts declare that you trust God more than you fear the world. William and Ling, your commitment today shows you are ready for this journey. May God grant you grace, perseverance, and the joy of building a life together that honors him and blesses the generations to come.
