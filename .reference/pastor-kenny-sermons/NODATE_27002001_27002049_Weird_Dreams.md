# Daniel 2:1-49

**Title:** Weird Dreams  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction: The Power of Dreams

We all have vivid dreams that stay with us. Some are so real we're left wondering whether they actually happened or not. But the Bible contains dreams far more significant than our personal nighttime musings. Today, we encounter King Nebuchadnezzar's dream—a vision so troubling it cost him sleep and set in motion events that would reveal God's sovereignty over kingdoms and kings.

## 1. The King's Dream (2:1-11)

In the second year of his reign, Nebuchadnezzar, king of Babylon (modern-day Iraq), was troubled by a dream. His mind was anxious and sleep eluded him. He summoned his court advisors—the magicians, enchanters, sorcerers, and astrologers (called Chaldeans in the text)—demanding they tell him what he had dreamed and interpret its meaning.

### The Futility of Human Wisdom

In ancient societies, these "wise men" were the most important people—more influential than prime ministers or farmers. Every culture had them: shamans in Native American societies, priests in Hindu religions, witchdoctors in pagan cultures, the oracle of Greece. They claimed to read the unknowable—the weather, a child's sex, fortune itself. People desperately relied on them because so much felt beyond human control.

The astrologers initially responded respectfully, asking the king to tell them his dream so they could interpret it. But the king, proving himself shrewder than average, saw through their scheme. He issued an ultimatum: "Tell me what I dreamed and what it means, or I will have you executed and your houses turned to rubble. But if you succeed, you'll receive great rewards and honor."

The astrologers protested. The king repeated his demand. Finally, they admitted the truth: "There is no one on earth who can do what you ask. Only the gods can reveal such things, and they do not live among humans."

### Why Human Mysticism Always Fails

This exposes the fundamental problem with mysticism, superstition, magic, and astrology. Anyone can interpret dreams because interpretations are subjective. Anyone can read a palm line and claim it means long life or short life—it depends entirely on how you squint at it and what you want to believe.

Ancient cultures were filled with such superstitions. Romans studied the livers of sacrificed animals (haruspicy) and read grit left in wine goblets for fortunes. They feared breaking mirrors because they believed mirrors told fortunes and breaking them brought seven years of bad luck. The phrase "got up on the wrong side of the bed" comes from Roman beliefs that the left side was evil (the Latin word for left is *sinister*).

**The crucial point:** If these spiritual practitioners were truly powerful, why couldn't they simply speak the king's dream? Why can't horoscopes be specific with names, times, and places? Why must they rely on vague statements and cold reading?

The answer is always the same—they're making it up.

Even today, superstition thrives. A British survey found that 77 percent of people admit to being at least somewhat superstitious. We see horoscopes in major newspapers claiming to describe your personality and future. Every horoscope sounds like you if you only read your own sign—that's the Barnum effect at work.

We watch television mediums like Tyler Henry or John Edwards perform "cold reading"—making high-probability guesses, watching for signals, throwing out vague statements like "I'm sensing a fatherly figure." When they're wrong, they blame the audience's cooperation. When they're right, people believe in their power. It's the same technique magicians use, and atheists and Christians alike fall for it.

Remarkably, atheists who criticize Christianity as superstitious are often the first to believe in these charlatans. We care deeply about the non-physical—the question is simply which spirituality we choose to believe in.

The king's advisors were right about one thing: "There is no one on earth who can do what the king asks... no one can reveal it except the gods."

They were partly correct, but they didn't know the true God.

## 2. The God of Dreams (2:12-23)

Nebuchadnezzar's anger was kindled. He ordered the execution of all the wise men of Babylon. As the executioner went out to carry out the decree, Daniel—one of the Jewish exiles serving the king—showed wisdom and tact. He asked Arioch, the commander of the guard, why the king had issued such a harsh decree.

After hearing the explanation, Daniel went to the king and asked for time, promising he would interpret the dream. But Daniel didn't spend that time gathering magical potions, burning incense, or performing rituals. He went home and prayed.

He gathered his friends—Hananiah, Mishael, and Azariah—and urged them to plead for mercy from the God of heaven concerning this mystery, so they would not be executed with the rest of Babylon's wise men.

### Prayer: Simple and Powerful

Prayer is not mysticism. It's not magical words or chanting or formulas. The word "pray" comes from Old English simply meaning "to ask." It's requesting something from someone. If God is real and powerful, then seeking His help is entirely reasonable—especially when facing execution.

That night, the mystery was revealed to Daniel in a vision.

Daniel responded not with self-congratulation but with praise:

> Praise be to the name of God for ever and ever; wisdom and power are his. He changes times and seasons; he deposes kings and raises up others. He gives wisdom to the wise and knowledge to the discerning. He reveals deep and hidden things; he knows what lies in darkness, and light dwells with him.

He continued:

> I thank and praise you, God of my ancestors: You have given me wisdom and power, you have made known to me what we asked of you, you have made known to us the dream of the king.

### The God Who Reveals

Success depends not on the act of praying but on *whom* you pray to. You can pray to a piece of wood, to the air, to yourself, to other people—but it's useless unless they're powerful enough to answer.

Who knows the dreams of a powerful king? Not Daniel. Not the king's advisors or his wife. No one except the one who placed that dream in the king's mind in the first place.

This is the fundamental difference between human wisdom and God's wisdom. We humans think we're clever, but our natural state is ignorance. We're primitively superstitious, fearing the dark and susceptible to anyone confident enough to deceive us.

**It is the light of the gospel that dispels superstition. It is the Bible—the record of God's activity on earth—that dispels ignorance. God doesn't hide; He reveals.**

### Christianity and the End of Superstition

When Christianity spread through the Roman Empire, superstition diminished. The emperor Constantius II encouraged legislation banning temple sacrifices. An imperial decree stated: "Superstition shall cease; the madness of sacrifices shall be abolished."

But when civilization drifted from Scripture during the Dark Ages, superstition returned. The printing press changed everything. In 1455, Gutenberg printed the first book ever made: a Latin Bible. Soon after came the Renaissance, the Age of Enlightenment, and the Scientific Revolution.

There's a common misconception that Christians are superstitious while atheists are scientific. The opposite is often true. The first scientists were Christians—often clergy with time to pursue their inquiries. Louis Pasteur, the founder of microbiology, proved that life comes only from life. He challenged the popular theory of spontaneous generation and showed it was false.

Pasteur declared: "No circumstances are known in which microscopic beings come into the world without germs, without parents similar to themselves."

Darwin had proposed that life arose spontaneously from a "warm little pond" with ammonia and phosphoric salts. But Pasteur's experiments disproved this completely.

The answer to the origin of life wasn't mysterious—**it was revealed in Genesis 1:1: "In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth."**

The more you truly know God, like Pasteur did, the less superstitious you become. God reveals; He doesn't hide.

## 3. The Revelation of the Gospel (2:24-49)

Daniel went to Arioch and told him not to execute the wise men. He would interpret the dream for the king. Arioch brought him before Nebuchadnezzar, and the king asked, "Are you able to tell me what I saw in my dream and interpret it?"

Daniel replied: "No wise man, enchanter, magician or diviner can explain to the king the mystery he has asked about, but there is a God in heaven who reveals mysteries."

### The Four Kingdoms

Daniel then described the dream. Nebuchadnezzar had seen an enormous, dazzling statue. Its head was pure gold, chest and arms of silver, belly and thighs of bronze, legs of iron, and feet partly iron and partly baked clay.

As the king watched, a rock—cut out by no human hands—struck the feet and shattered the entire statue. The iron, clay, bronze, silver, and gold became like chaff blown away by wind. But the rock became a huge mountain that filled the whole earth.

Daniel explained: "Your Majesty, you are the king of kings. The God of heaven has given you dominion. You are the head of gold."

After Babylon would come a second kingdom, inferior to yours. Then a third kingdom of bronze ruling the whole earth. Finally, a fourth kingdom as strong as iron, crushing all others.

These kingdoms are:
- **Babylon** (Nebuchadnezzar's own empire)
- **Medo-Persia** (which we'll see in chapter 6)
- **Greece** (detailed in chapters 8 and 10)
- **Rome** (not named in Daniel but described, predicting a fourth world power after Greece)

The fourth kingdom was divided—partly strong like iron, partly brittle like clay—representing Rome's division into east and west.

### The Kingdom That Never Ends

But the crucial element wasn't the statue. It was the rock.

Daniel explained:

> In the time of those kings, the God of heaven will set up a kingdom that will never be destroyed, nor will it be left to another people. It will crush all those kingdoms and bring them to an end, but it will itself endure forever.

This rock—cut from no earthly mountain—represents God's kingdom led by a new king. Jesus Himself referenced this passage:

> He who falls on this stone will be broken to pieces, but he on whom it falls will be crushed. (Matthew 21:44)

The chief priests and Pharisees understood He was speaking about them.

### The Eternal Kingdom

Luke records the angel's announcement to Mary:

> You will conceive and give birth to a son, and you are to call him Jesus. He will be great and will be called the Son of the Most High. The Lord God will give him the throne of his father David, and he will reign over Jacob's descendants forever; his kingdom will never end.

Isaiah prophesied:

> For unto us a child is born, unto us a son is given, and the government will be upon His shoulders. And He will be called Wonderful Counselor, Mighty God, Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace. Of the increase of His government and peace there will be no end. He will reign on the throne of David and over his kingdom, to establish and sustain it with justice and righteousness from that time and forevermore.

**The mystery revealed is this: Jesus is King of Kings. He brings a kingdom that will never end. He began His work at His first coming and will complete it at His second coming.**

Over 300 Old Testament prophecies foretold His coming—Jewish prophecies without bias. He would be born in Bethlehem of a virgin mother. He would enter His death riding a donkey. He would be crucified, with none of His bones broken. He would rise on the third day. And soon He will come again to judge the living and the dead.

Paul wrote:

> Now to him who is able to establish you in accordance with my gospel, the message I proclaim about Jesus Christ, in keeping with the revelation of the mystery hidden for long ages past...

The author of Hebrews declares:

> In the past God spoke to our ancestors through the prophets at many times and in various ways, but in these last days he has spoken to us by his Son, whom he appointed heir of all things, and through whom also he made the universe. The Son is the radiance of God's glory and the exact representation of his being, sustaining all things by his powerful word.

**This is God's last message. If you want to know God's thoughts, go to God's Word.**

### The King's Response

King Nebuchadnezzar fell prostrate before Daniel and paid him honor. He ordered that an offering and incense be presented to him and declared: "Surely your God is the God of gods and the Lord of kings and a revealer of mysteries, for you were able to reveal this mystery."

He elevated Daniel to a high position, lavished gifts upon him, made him ruler over the entire province of Babylon, and placed him in charge of all its wise men. At Daniel's request, he appointed Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego as administrators over the province as well.

## Conclusion: Where Is Your Faith?

You may be here today for various reasons. Perhaps you've had a vivid dream or saw our church mentioned on Facebook and took it as a sign. Maybe you had a strange experience that felt spiritual.

These are just circumstances. They matter far less than you might think.

**God's message for you is not found in dreams, signs, or feverish experiences. It's found in His Word.**

The greatest scientists and thinkers throughout history—from Louis Pasteur to countless others—discovered that faith in God and devotion to understanding His creation went hand in hand. Their conviction wasn't mystical superstition but confident trust in a God who reveals Himself.

Like King Nebuchadnezzar, consider what God has revealed. Don't settle for the empty promises of human wisdom or the deceptions of superstition. Seek the true God who speaks through His Word.

The solution to life's mysteries isn't found in the dark. It's found in the light of the gospel. Reconcile with your Maker while you can. Get right with this King before it is too late.
