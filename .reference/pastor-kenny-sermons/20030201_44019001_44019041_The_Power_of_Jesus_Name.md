# Acts 19:1-41

**Title:** The Power of Jesus' Name
**Date:** 23 February 2003
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## What Makes a Good Name?

We often think of a good name as one with power—a name that opens doors, influences people, and commands respect. We might value academic credentials, titles, or brand recognition. But what does God consider a good name?

In Acts 19, Paul encounters this same question when he meets some disciples of John the Baptist in Ephesus. Their encounter reveals a profound truth: there is only one name that truly matters for salvation.

## Point 1: Only Jesus' Name Brings True Repentance

**Scripture:** Acts 19:1-7

John the Baptist was arguably the greatest prophet who ever lived. Born miraculously, filled with the Holy Spirit from birth, he was the forerunner prophesied in Scripture. Yet Jesus said:

> "Among those born of women there has not risen anyone greater than John the Baptist; yet he who is least in the kingdom of heaven is greater than he." (Matthew 11:11)

When Paul meets some of John's disciples at Ephesus, he asks what baptism they had received. They answered, "John's baptism." Paul then explains:

> "John's baptism was a baptism of repentance. He told the people to believe in the one coming after him, that is, in Jesus." (Acts 19:4)

These disciples had received John's baptism—a true baptism of repentance—but they had not yet encountered Jesus. Their faith was incomplete because it was not centered on the One John pointed to. When they were baptized in Jesus' name and Paul laid hands on them, the Holy Spirit came upon them, and they spoke in tongues and prophesied.

This passage is sometimes used to argue that signs and wonders mark true conversion or a second baptism of the Holy Spirit. But that misses the point. Throughout Acts, we see that special miracles accompany the gospel when it breaks new ground—at Pentecost when the church was born, among the Samaritans when they entered the kingdom, and here in Ephesus as Asia was being reached. These twelve disciples parallel the twelve apostles sent to reach the nations. There is only one true baptism that counts, and only one name under which true repentance occurs: the name of Jesus.

**The Application:** Repenting under the wrong name is useless. You cannot be truly Christian by praying to Allah, bowing to false gods, or following any name but Jesus. The wrong name brings no Holy Spirit, no salvation, no hope. But the right name—the name of Jesus—opens the doors of heaven and brings the indwelling Holy Spirit. The difference between being a Christian and not being a Christian rests on this single, crucial name.

## Point 2: Merely Using Jesus' Name Does Not Fool God

**Scripture:** Acts 19:11-16

If John's name—great as he was—is not sufficient, what about simply invoking Jesus' name? Perhaps we think that using the right name is enough, even without true knowledge of Jesus.

Luke describes extraordinary miracles being worked through Paul. Even handkerchiefs and aprons that had touched him were taken to the sick, and their illnesses were cured and evil spirits left them. Some Jewish exorcists, seeing this power, decided to use the same formula. They would approach those who were demon-possessed and say:

> "In the name of Jesus, whom Paul preaches, I command you to come out." (Acts 19:13)

But these were not believers. They were Jews who had refused to believe in Jesus. The sons of Sceva, a Jewish chief priest, were among those attempting this. They had seen the power associated with Jesus' name and wanted to exploit it—like a magic formula or incantation. But when they tried to cast out a demon this way, the evil spirit answered them:

> "Jesus I know, and I know about Paul, but who are you?" (Acts 19:15)

Then the demon-possessed man overpowered all seven of them, beating them so severely that they ran out of the house naked and bleeding.

Here is the crucial difference: Paul's authority came from genuine relationship with Jesus. The sons of Sceva possessed only the outward formula without the inward reality. Paul's humble work clothes—mere handkerchiefs and aprons—carried more power than the religious credentials of seven sons of a chief priest.

**The Application:** We cannot deceive God through outward labels or name-dropping. Someone may be a third-generation Christian, raised in Sunday school, baptized, wearing a cross—yet have no personal knowledge of Jesus and no real relationship with Him. 

Jesus taught this clearly:

> "Not everyone who says to me, 'Lord, Lord,' will enter the kingdom of heaven, but only he who does the will of my Father who is in heaven. Many will say to me on that day, 'Lord, Lord, did we not prophesy in your name, and in your name drive out demons and perform many miracles?' Then I will tell them plainly, 'I never knew you. Away from me, you evildoers!'" (Matthew 7:21-23)

What makes someone truly Christian is not the external trappings but real, personal knowledge of Jesus. It is knowing what He has done for us on the cross, trusting that fact, and living according to it. Without genuine knowledge and genuine obedience, name-dropping Jesus has no saving power.

## Point 3: Jesus' Name Endures When All Others Fail

**Scripture:** Acts 19:23-36

As influential as Artemis was in Ephesus—worshiped throughout the province of Asia and the world, so powerful that an entire economy depended on her shrines—her name could not withstand the proclamation of Jesus. 

When the silversmith Demetrius realized that Paul's preaching was turning people away from Artemis, he incited a riot. The crowds filled the theatre, shouting for hours:

> "Great is Artemis of the Ephesians! Great is Artemis of the Ephesians!" (Acts 19:34)

The entire city was in an uproar. If you had stood in that theatre that day, you would have thought Artemis' influence was unshakable, eternal, invincible. Yet today, Ephesus lies in ruins. The temple of Artemis was destroyed in 267 AD. No one worships Artemis anymore. Her name, once so powerful, has vanished from history.

Meanwhile, in just two years, the name of Jesus reached every corner of Asia—an entire province transformed by the gospel. Though Artemis had centuries of influence behind her, the young name of Jesus spread with irresistible power.

**The Application:** As Christians, we live in a world where other names seem more powerful, more successful, more influential. We see Islam with its millions of followers. We see our friends pursuing wealth and career with apparent success, ignoring Jesus and thriving by worldly standards. It can feel daunting to remain faithful to Jesus when the competition looks so formidable.

But we must trust what Scripture teaches: in the end, Jesus' name will prevail. His way will endure. This means:

- For students: being honest in your assignments rather than plagiarizing
- For workers: trusting God to provide honest employment rather than backstabbing your way up the ladder
- For young people facing relationships: trusting that remaining single and lonely is better than being happily married to someone who doesn't follow Jesus

We cannot see how this works out now. We cannot foresee the heartache that comes when you are married to someone who doesn't share your faith—the arguments about raising children in church, the different value systems, the spiritual isolation. But God's wisdom will be vindicated.

Keep confidence that Jesus' way is the way that works. It is His way that will ultimately influence the world. It is His way that will endure.

## Conclusion: The True Power of Jesus' Name

The real power of Jesus' name is not in using it as a formula or spell. It is not in following worldly ways or external religiosity. The power is in what that name represents: the living God, dead but now alive, raised to glory because of His obedience to the cross.

When the people of Ephesus encountered the reality of Jesus' power, something remarkable happened:

> "When this became known to the Jews and Greeks living in Ephesus, they were all seized with fear, and the name of the Lord Jesus was held in high honour. Many of those who believed now came and openly confessed their evil deeds. A number who had practiced sorcery brought their scrolls together and burned them publicly. When they calculated the value of the scrolls, the total came to fifty thousand drachmas." (Acts 19:17-19)

That sum represented approximately 135 years of wages—an enormous cost. Yet people willingly paid it because the Holy Spirit had convicted their hearts and brought genuine repentance.

This is what the power of Jesus' name accomplishes: not magical signs or impressive displays, but transformed hearts. People who meet the living Jesus, who understand what He accomplished on the cross through His obedience, who receive His Spirit—these people are changed from the inside out.

> "In this way the word of the Lord spread widely and grew in power." (Acts 19:20)

Keep Jesus' name in high honour. It is greater than any other name—greater than John, greater than Artemis, greater than every power this world can offer. In it alone is salvation, transformation, and eternal life.
