# Romans 8:34-39

**Title:** Inseparable: The Love That Cannot Be Separated  
**Date:** 17 October 2019  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## Some Things Belong Together

There are certain pairings in life that simply go together. Chocolate and mint. Bread and coffee. Peanut butter and jam. Bacon and eggs. A great chef understands this instinctively—as Remy says in Ratatouille, "Each flavor was totally unique. But combine one flavor with another, and something new was created!"

Some pairings are inseparable by nature. A bride and groom. Two people created for companionship, bound together not just by shared interests—like a love of exploring new restaurants—but by something deeper.

In today's passage, Paul reveals another pairing that should never be separated: humanity and the love of God.

---

## The Original Partnership: Created for Relationship

We were made to be in relationship with God. In the garden of Eden, there was perfect fellowship—God walking in the cool of the day, enjoying the company of His creation, and mankind enjoying the presence of their Creator. It was compatible, natural, meant to be.

Even those who struggle to believe in God often sense hints of something beyond the material world. When we witness the birth of a child, we glimpse something miraculous—not merely survival of the fittest. When we see love at a wedding, we recognize something deeper than chemistry or selfish genes. These are signs that we possess worth beyond what can be measured economically or genetically—a worth that comes from having a Creator.

The Bible teaches us that we are valuable, that we belong with God. As Paul writes:

> **Romans 8:39** Neither height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God that is in Christ Jesus our Lord.

---

## Sin Ruins the Pairing

Yet something has gone terribly wrong. There is separation—not of distance, but of relationship.

Consider a married couple lying in the same bed, physically close but emotionally distant because of betrayal. They may be intimate in proximity yet as far apart as the earth and moon in their hearts. This is what sin does. It separates.

When Adam and Eve ate from the tree, they didn't just break a rule—they rebelled against God Himself. They denied the fundamental compatibility that should exist between Creator and creation. That act of disobedience created a gap: height separating, depth separating. No plane or submarine, no human effort can bridge it.

It's troubling that in our suffering, we cry out, "God, where are you?" But the problem isn't God's existence or absence. The problem is ours—the reality of sin. We ignore Him, use His name as a curse, hurt His creation, then complain when He allows us to face the consequences of our choices.

Sin has ruined what was meant to be the perfect pairing. Like a bride and groom torn apart by selfishness and infidelity, God and humanity stand separated—incompatible because of our rebellion.

Worse still, this separation is infinite. We sin against an infinite God, so the judgment is infinite. It's not about the quantity of sin we commit but against *whom* we sin. A finite offense against an infinite God deserves infinite consequence. The separation is eternal—separated by infinite height, infinite depth.

---

## The Solution: Christ as Mediator

But God does not want this separation to continue.

> **Romans 8:34** Who then is the one who condemns? No one. Christ Jesus who died—more than that, who was raised to life—is at the right hand of God and is also interceding for us.

Consider who Christ claims to be. A real person. The wisest teacher on earth. Our modern ethics and laws are built on His teachings. And He claims to be God. Two thousand years ago, God showed up—Christmas, 33 BC.

What did He do?

**He died.** This is sacrificial love. God Himself took on human form and gave His life to absorb the punishment we deserve. Forgiveness means taking the wrong done against you and bearing that hurt yourself instead of demanding justice.

**He was raised to life.** Because He is the infinite God, He swallowed our death and pain and defeated death itself—once and for all.

**He intercedes for us.** Now, through His sacrifice, Jesus stands before God pleading on our behalf. He asks not only for the forgiveness of our past sins but also for our future ones. When we repent and ask, Jesus presents His payment to God, and we are forgiven.

As a result, we have been brought back together with God. We are compatible once again.

---

## Inseparable: The New Partnership

Think of it this way: that bride and groom I mentioned earlier—they weren't brought together by their shared love of restaurants. They were brought together because they both know Jesus. They are two people forgiven by God. Something far greater than compatibility binds them: redemption itself. They met at church, served together, got to know each other as followers of Christ. Now they are inseparable.

In the same way, we are inseparable from God.

> **Romans 8:35** Who shall separate us from the love of Christ? Shall trouble or hardship or persecution or famine or nakedness or danger or sword?

Paul lists the things that threaten to tear us apart: persecution, famine, nakedness, danger, the sword. Many Christians today—particularly in Africa, Asia, and the Middle East—face these very threats. They are the most persecuted people in the world. Yet nothing can separate them from God's love.

> **Romans 8:37** No, in all these things we are more than conquerors through him who loved us.

> **Romans 8:38** For I am convinced that neither death nor life, neither angels nor demons, neither the present nor the future, nor any powers...

Marriage itself is a symbol of this inseparability—though we often denigrate and abuse it. But God's intention is profound: marriage points to something greater. It shows that God's will is stronger than human will. It demonstrates the bringing together of two seemingly disparate beings, two who might be enemies, now united. This is the mark of a master craftsman.

---

## The Chef's Mark: Making the Incompatible Compatible

A great chef can combine seemingly incompatible flavors: chocolate and chili, kimchi and cheddar cheese, tea and cherry jam, sea salt and caramel. Things that shouldn't work together create something beautiful.

Jesus is that Chef. Through His sacrifice, He takes the dirt—our sin, our shame, our brokenness—and bears it all Himself. It clings to Him like stains on a tea towel, on an apron, on the chef's hands. All of it is on Him.

And yet, through that selfless act, He brings together two incompatible things: sinful humanity and a holy God. He makes us compatible when we should be forever separated. He creates beauty from brokenness.

This is what Christ has done for us. This is why Paul can say with absolute confidence that nothing—not death, not life, not angels, not demons, not height, not depth, not any power in all creation—will be able to separate us from the love of God in Christ Jesus our Lord.

We belong together with God. And that bond is inseparable.
