# Romans 5:6-8

**Title:** Fake News vs. Good News: You Are Worth Enough  
**Date:** 27 January 2019  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Part 1: The Fake News - You Will Never Be Enough

### How Do We Measure Worth?

We constantly ask ourselves this question: What is something worth? We evaluate the worth of textbooks before buying them, the value of a car, the price of a house. But this question becomes infinitely more profound when we ask: What is a human life worth?

Consider a $100 note. One side declares its value: $100. Yet it's merely a piece of plastic, worth perhaps a few cents to produce. The disconnect reveals something crucial: worth isn't determined by what something is made of, but by what it represents.

### Mankind's Worth According to Scripture

The Bible presents a sobering view of human worth. Look at how Scripture describes us:

**We are powerless.** Romans 5:6 states: *"You see, at just the right time, when we were still powerless, Christ died for the ungodly."* We lack the power to save ourselves, to overcome sin, to achieve righteousness on our own.

Compared to God, our weakness is absolute. As 1 Corinthians 1:25 reminds us: *"For the foolishness of God is wiser than human wisdom, and the weakness of God is stronger than human strength."*

Compared to angels—beings who can roll back stone tombs and open prison doors—we are insignificant. Compared to creatures in nature—the eyesight of an eagle, the strength of an ant carrying 10-50 times its body weight, the spider's web ten times stronger than Kevlar—we possess no superhuman abilities.

Biologically, a human being is merely a few dollars' worth of carbon, water, and organic chemicals. We are just clumps of cells, easily disposable when we no longer provide economic or social value.

**But it gets worse. We are not just physically worthless—we are morally worthless.**

### We Are Morally Corrupt

In verse 8, Paul says: *"While we were still sinners, Christ died for us."* And in verse 10, we are called *"enemies of God."*

What does it mean to be a sinner? It doesn't mean everything we do is evil, but that we are fundamentally inconsistent and corrupt. We're like a corrupt-minded politician: not incapable of good, perhaps even entering public service with noble intentions, but structurally compromised. Given the right opportunity, we take advantage of the system.

We are more sinful than we pretend. We are more sinful than we think.

As a result, we become enemies—not just of each other, but of God Himself. We fight against His purposes and undermine His agenda.

### The Human Condition: Weak and Evil

This combination—weakness paired with moral corruption—is far worse than weakness alone. A weak creature might be tolerable. But a weak and evil creature? That's us. We are compromised, corrupted, never certain whose side we're on, living for self-gratification, in a genuinely pathetic state.

### The Self-Esteem Illusion

Our generation has embraced a self-esteem movement. Everything centers on you: you are the most important person. iPhone. Me Bank. MyHealth. We've become radically individualistic and self-centered.

But here's the truth: we are not that great. Instead of an iPhone, imagine a Weakphone—representing our true condition. We are weak, powerless, and sinful. Not worth much at all.

This is why James 4:6 calls us to humility: *"God opposes the proud, but gives grace to the humble."* Humility before God is the only realistic response to our actual condition.

## Part 2: The Good News - You Are Worth Enough to Die For

### The Astonishing Exchange

Here is where the Gospel becomes incomprehensible: *"Christ died for us."*

In the context of our absolute worthlessness, our powerlessness, our moral corruption—Christ died. Why? We're not worth dying for.

Verse 7 establishes the normal pattern of human sacrifice: *"Very rarely will anyone die for a righteous person, though for a good person someone might possibly dare to die."* People might die for someone virtuous, someone exceptional, someone on the positive spectrum of human goodness.

But Jesus died for the ungodly. His enemies. The opposite end of the spectrum. For the worthless, Jesus paid the ultimate price—His own life.

### The Worth of Christ

How much is Christ worth? The Scripture declares His immeasurable value:

- *"The Word became flesh and made his dwelling among us. We have seen his glory, the glory of the one and only Son, who came from the Father, full of grace and truth."* (John 1:14)

- *"For in Christ all the fullness of the Deity lives in bodily form."* (Colossians 2:9)

- *"To you who believe, this stone [Jesus Christ] is precious."* (1 Peter 2:7)

- *"You are worthy, our Lord and God, to receive all glory, honour and power."* (Revelation 4:11)

Christ possesses immeasurable, infinite worth. Yet while we were worthless, Christ—who alone is worthy—died for us.

### We Gain New Worth Through Exchange

The astonishing result: we are given new worth. But notice something crucial—this is not an intrinsic worth. Our worth is *derivative*. We gain worth through an exchange of status.

Mankind gains worth by swapping with Christ's status. And since Christ is precious, so are we. This is the redemption of mankind—the restoration of the image of God as it was always meant to be.

Scripture declares this transformation:

- *"So if you have been raised with Christ, seek the things that are above, where Christ is, seated at the right hand of God... for you have died, and your life is hidden with Christ in God. When Christ who is your life is revealed, then you also will be revealed with him in glory."* (Colossians 3:1-4)

- *"And we all, who with unveiled faces contemplate the Lord's glory, are being transformed into his image with ever-increasing glory."* (2 Corinthians 3:18)

- *"He made Him who knew no sin to be sin on our behalf, so that we might become the righteousness of God in Him."* (2 Corinthians 5:21)

### A Paradox of Money

Consider how modern currency works. A $100 note is literally paper, chemically worth perhaps 15 cents. Yet we don't discard it. Why? Because its worth isn't determined by its material composition, but by what it can be exchanged for. You can trade it for goods, services, assets. Whether it's denominated in AUD, HKD, or Renminbi, the denomination matters less than the exchange value.

Similarly, it doesn't matter that our bodies are biologically worthless. What matters most is what we exchange our identity for. Our worth becomes determined by our exchange rate—by what we trade ourselves for.

### The Application to Our Lives

Our generation, despite all our technology, feels deeply worthless. We see depression, anorexia, suicides—perhaps driven by impossibly high expectations, the loss of community support, or the cruel irony that those raised with participation trophies and the message that everyone's a winner are crumbling at the first real failure.

Stop looking for your worth in your studies, your athletic achievements, your physical appearance, your Instagram likes. Those things will fail. They always do.

Instead, put your hope in God—the God who died for you. Get your worth from that exchange. And because that death for you is eternal, your worth is eternal. It can never fade or perish.

Does it matter if no one likes your Facebook post? Not if God likes you. Would you prefer one popular person's approval over a hundred losers' approval? Of course. And here's the truth: God likes you, even if no one else does.

Will you swap your powerless, sinful, weak life—your 15-cent existence—for His precious, worthy life? It's worth it.
