# Ephesians 4:17-32

**Title:** Put Off the Old Self, Put On the New Self
**Date:** 20 October 2019
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Pagan Lifestyle: The Old Self (vv. 17-19)

> So I tell you this, and insist on it in the Lord, that you must no longer live as the "Gentiles do, in the futility of their thinking." (v. 17)

Paul begins by contrasting two ways of living. The word "Gentiles" here literally means "the nations"—those outside God's covenant. In this context, it refers to pagans, barbarians, and those living without God's revealed truth.

In the ancient world, the Jews possessed something the surrounding nations lacked: the law, history, ancestry, and God's covenants. They were civilized. The Gentiles, by contrast, were considered uncivilized and crude. Similarly, when Germanic tribes invaded Britain centuries later, they were unsophisticated warriors while China possessed paper, poetry, education, and philosophy. Paul's point is that living apart from God's revelation results in a corrupted, fragmented existence.

### The Problem: Darkened Understanding

> They are darkened in their understanding and separated from the life of God because of the ignorance that is in them due to the hardening of their hearts. (v. 18)

The pagan corruption involves three aspects:

**The Mind** – "futility of thinking" and "understanding": Without God's truth, human thinking becomes empty and circular.

**The Will** – "hardening of their hearts": The heart, in Scripture, represents the will and decision-making faculty. Like callused fingers that no longer feel, a hardened heart loses moral sensitivity. When we repeatedly choose sin, we eventually cannot feel that we are doing wrong anymore. This is not education's job to fix—it is a problem of willful rebellion.

> Having lost all sensitivity, they have given themselves over to sensuality so as to indulge in every kind of impurity, and they are full of greed. (v. 19)

This spiritual hardening leads to moral blindness. As Romans 1:21 describes: *"Although they knew God, they neither glorified him as God nor gave thanks to him, but their thinking became futile and their foolish hearts were darkened."* God's response is to give people over to their desires, allowing them to experience the natural consequences of rejecting Him.

## The Christian Life: The New Self (vv. 20-32)

> That, however, is not the way of life you learned when you heard about Christ and were taught in him in accordance with the truth that is in Jesus. (vv. 20-21)

This is not how followers of Christ are to live. The gospel saves us—and the same truth that saves us also delivers us from sin.

### Change of Mind

> You were taught, with regard to your former way of life, to put off your old self, which is being corrupted by its deceitful desires; to be made new in the attitude of your minds; and to put on the new self, created to be like God in true righteousness and holiness. (vv. 22-24)

Transformation begins with how we think. Notice that Paul emphasizes being *taught*—change comes through truth, not through counseling techniques, psychology, or even exercise and dopamine alone (though these may help). The solution is fundamentally theological, not therapeutic.

Right thinking about God produces right behavior. We must replace the pagan worldview with the Christian worldview, letting the truth of the gospel renew our minds daily.

### Change of Action

The new self manifests in concrete behaviors reflecting God's character:

**a. Speak the truth**

> Therefore each of you must put off falsehood and speak truthfully to your neighbor, for we are all members of one body. (v. 25)

Truth is the foundation of any functioning society. Lies corrupt everything. This echoes the ninth commandment: *"You shall not give false testimony against your neighbor."* When we belong to one body in Christ, honesty becomes not just a moral duty but an act of love for the community.

**b. Do not steal**

> Anyone who has been stealing must steal no longer, but must work, doing something useful with their own hands, that they may have something to share with those in need. (v. 28)

The eighth commandment forbids theft. But Paul goes deeper—work itself becomes redemptive. Instead of taking what isn't theirs, believers should labor so they can give to others. Honest work transforms the heart and enables generosity.

**c. Do not sin in anger**

> "In your anger do not sin": Do not let the sun go down while you are still angry, and do not give the devil a foothold. (vv. 26-27)

Anger itself is not sinful. Even God experiences righteous anger. But unresolved anger gives the devil an opportunity. The imagery of the sun going down suggests urgency—we must deal with anger quickly before it metastasizes into bitterness and becomes a stronghold in our hearts. Cain's anger at his brother Abel became murder because he let it fester (Genesis 4:6-8).

**d. Speak only what builds others up**

> Do not let any unwholesome talk come out of your mouths, but only what is helpful for building others up according to their needs, that it may benefit those who listen. (v. 29)

Our words should serve others' spiritual growth, not tear them down.

### The Completion: Put Off and Put On

> Get rid of all bitterness, rage and anger, brawling and slander, along with every form of malice. Be kind and compassionate to one another, forgiving each other, just as in Christ God forgave you. (vv. 31-32)

Paul summarizes: the old self—with all its destructive patterns—must be completely removed. Like old, worn-out clothes that no longer fit and are uncomfortable, we should discard the old life entirely. Sometimes we foolishly put those old clothes back on, but that is not who we are anymore.

Instead, we put on the new self: kindness, compassion, and forgiveness. We forgive as Christ forgave us—freely, completely, without holding back.

### Two Spiritual Forces

The passage reveals a fundamental choice: **follow the devil or follow the Spirit of God.**

The devil is the father of lies (John 8:44), crafting elaborate schemes to deceive us. When we grieve the Holy Spirit through sin, we lose His protection and presence (Isaiah 63:10). But when we resist the devil, he flees from us (James 4:7).

## Application: Two Contrasting Worlds

Paul illustrates his teaching with a striking contrast: Kanye West exemplifies the pagan, unredeemed life—arrogance, sexual depravity, materialism, and destructive speech. Yet recently, he released the album *"Jesus is King,"* a genuinely Christian work expressing surrender to Christ.

This transformation—from the old self to the new—is possible because the gospel changes minds and hearts.

Meanwhile, Western civilization, built on Christian foundations, is increasingly abandoning those foundations. Modern paganism takes new forms: spirituality without God, the law of attraction, mindfulness practices, and the worship of self. Migrants come to the West seeking its prosperity, yet they often find a spiritually empty civilization.

### A Challenge

If such transformation is good enough for a society, it should be good enough for you. Christians are made new. Why would we continue wearing the old self—those uncomfortable, ill-fitting clothes of sin?

Put away that sin. It no longer belongs to you. Put on righteousness and holiness instead. This is the calling of every believer: to be transformed by the renewing of your mind, to put off the old self and put on the new, created in the image of God.
