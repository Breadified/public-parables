# Romans 8:34-39

**Title:** The Inseparable Bond: God and Us
**Date:** 17 October 2019
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## The Perfect Pairing

There are some things in life that simply belong together—bread and cheese, peanut butter and jam, bacon and eggs. A skilled chef understands that when you combine the right flavors, something entirely new and beautiful emerges. Just as a groom and bride form an inseparable pairing, there is another pairing in Scripture that should never be separated: humanity and the love of God.

> "Neither height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God that is in Christ Jesus our Lord." — Romans 8:39

This relationship between God and us—between Creator and creation—is the fundamental pairing for which we were designed. In the garden of Eden, God walked with humanity in the cool of the day. We were made for fellowship with our Creator. God enjoyed the company of His creation, and we were designed to enjoy His company in return.

## The Problem: Sin Ruins Compatibility

Yet we live in a world where this perfect pairing has been broken. Sin has separated us from God—not a separation of distance, but a separation of relationship.

Consider a married couple lying in the same bed yet worlds apart in their hearts because of betrayal or wrong. They may be physically close, yet emotionally separated by an infinite distance. In the same way, sin—our rebellion against God—creates a chasm between us and our Creator.

When Adam and Eve ate the forbidden fruit, they made a choice that symbolized something far greater: they denied the very nature of their relationship with God. They rejected their identity as creation before their Creator. This act of disobedience created a separation that no effort of our own could bridge.

Sin separates us not by distance but by relationship. We might ask, "God, where are you?" But the real problem isn't God's absence—it's our sin and its consequences. When we ignore Him, use His name as a curse, hurt His creation, and then complain when we face the results of our choices, we reveal the depth of our separation.

The tragedy is this: separated from an infinite God, that separation becomes infinite. It would be eternal—separated by infinite height and infinite depth. This seems harsh until we understand that the severity of sin isn't measured by its quantity but by whom we sin against. When we sin against an infinite God, the consequences are infinite.

## The Solution: Jesus Brings Us Together

But God has not left us in this state of separation. He has given us a solution:

> "Who then is the one who condemns? No one. Christ Jesus who died—more than that, who was raised to life—is at the right hand of God and is also interceding for us." — Romans 8:34

Jesus came as God incarnate—the wisest teacher the world has ever known, whose ethical teachings form the foundation of our laws and societies. But His claims were far greater: He claimed to be God Himself.

What Jesus did for us breaks down into three essential truths:

**He died.** Through His death, Jesus offered sacrificial love—God Himself taking upon Himself the punishment we deserved. Forgiveness means taking the wrong against you and, instead of judgment, absorbing that hurt into yourself. Jesus did this for all humanity.

**He rose to life.** Because Jesus is the infinite God, He could swallow all our pain, all our hurt, all our death—and defeat death itself, once and for all. His resurrection proves that His sacrifice was sufficient and accepted.

**He intercedes for us.** Now, through His sacrifice, Jesus pleads with God on our behalf—not just for our past sins, but for our future sins as well. When we repent and ask, Jesus intercedes before God based on His payment, securing our forgiveness.

Through Christ's work, we have been brought back together with God. We are compatible once again. Something greater than natural affinity binds us—the grace of God through Jesus Christ.

## Inseparable: The Marks of His Love

> "Shall trouble or hardship or persecution or famine or nakedness or danger or sword?... No, in all these things we are more than conquerors through him who loved us. For I am convinced that neither death nor life, neither angels nor demons, neither the present nor the future, nor any powers... will be able to separate us from the love of God that is in Christ Jesus our Lord." — Romans 8:35-39

Once we are united with God through Christ, nothing can separate us. Not trouble, hardship, persecution, famine, nakedness, danger, or even death itself. Christians throughout the world—in Africa, Asia, and the Middle East—face persecution daily, yet nothing can sever our bond with God's love.

This inseparability is so central to God's design that He gives us marriage as a symbol of it. Marriage, at its best, reflects the inseparability between God and His people, between Christ and His church. It is God's intention that two people—even those who might seem incompatible—are brought together as one, just as God has brought together seemingly incompatible things: sinful humanity and a holy God.

## The Chef's Masterpiece

A great chef knows how to combine even the most unexpected ingredients—chocolate and chili, kimchi and cheddar cheese, sea salt and caramel. These strange pairings create something beautiful and new.

Jesus is that Master Chef. He has taken all the dirt—all our sin, shame, and brokenness—and placed it upon Himself. The stains are on Him: on His apron, on His hands, embedded in His sacrifice. Yet through His work, He has created something beautiful: a restored relationship between humanity and God.

We are a strange pairing, it's true. We are incompatible by nature—sinful creatures before a holy God. Yet through the work of Christ, we have been brought together. And it is this Master Chef, through His sacrificial love, who holds us inseparably together, even in our weakness and incompatibility.
