# Malachi 1-4

**Title:** The Lord Loved; You Despised - The Covenant Restored

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## The Foundation: God's Love and Our Response

> I have loved you, says the Lord.

God opens His message through Malachi with a declaration of love, yet His people respond with skepticism: "How have You loved us?" This question reveals the heart of Israel's problem—they had forgotten God's faithfulness and begun to question His character.

To answer their doubt, God points to His choice of Jacob over Esau. This wasn't arbitrary favoritism. God's love for Jacob was intentional, covenantal love. He gave Jacob the inheritance that should have been Esau's, transforming Edom's mountains into a wasteland. Yet even when Edom attempted to rebuild, God would demolish what they constructed. The Israelites would witness this with their own eyes and recognize: "The Lord is great, even beyond the borders of Israel."

## The Scandal of Contempt

God addresses the priests directly with a cutting question:

> A son honors his father, and a servant his master. But if I am a father, where is My honor? And if I am a master, where is your fear of Me?

The priests had despised God's name through their worship practices. What should have been sacred had become contemptible in their hands.

### The Corruption of Sacrifice

The specific sin: they brought defiled offerings to God's altar. When challenged, they asked, "How have we defiled You?" God's answer was blunt—by treating His table as contemptible, by presenting blind, lame, and sick animals for sacrifice.

This wasn't mere carelessness. God pointed out the absurdity: "Would you dare present such an animal to your governor and expect favor?" Yet they brought these inferior offerings to the God of the universe. The priests were essentially saying through their actions: God isn't worthy of our best; He'll accept whatever we're willing to discard.

God's response was severe: "I wish one of you would shut the temple doors, so you would no longer kindle a useless fire on My altar! I am not pleased with you, and I will accept no offering from your hands."

### God's Name Will Be Honored

Yet God revealed His intention: though Israel had profaned His name, His name would be great among all nations. From sunrise to sunset, incense and pure offerings would be presented in His name in every place. Where Israel had failed to honor God, the nations would rise up and give Him glory.

## The Corruption of the Priesthood

The priests had become teachers of corruption rather than guardians of truth. God reminded them of the original covenant with Levi—a covenant of life and peace. The priest's role was sacred:

> True instruction was in his mouth, and nothing wrong was found on his lips. He walked with Me in peace and fairness and turned many from sin.

The lips of a priest should guard knowledge, and people should seek instruction from him because he is the messenger of the Lord of Hosts.

But these priests had abandoned the way. They caused many to stumble through their false instruction and violated the covenant of Levi by showing partiality—giving one judgment to the rich and another to the poor, compromising truth for advantage.

As a result, God made them despised and humiliated before all the people. Their unfaithfulness bore the bitter fruit of shame.

## The Broken Covenant at Home

The corruption extended beyond worship into the very fabric of family life. God asked a piercing question:

> Dont all of us have one Father? Didnt one God create us? Why then do we act treacherously against one another, profaning the covenant of our fathers?

Specifically, Judah had married daughters of foreign gods, profaning the Lord's sanctuary that He loves. The Israelites covered God's altar with tears—weeping and groaning—because He no longer respected their offerings.

God explained why: "The Lord has been a witness between you and the wife of your youth. You have acted treacherously against her, though she was your marriage partner and your wife by covenant."

The covenant of marriage is sacred. God made them with a remnant of His own life-breath, and what does He seek? A godly offspring. To break covenant with one's spouse is to break faith with God Himself, to cover oneself with injustice.

## The Weariness of God

Finally, God revealed the deepest wound:

> You have wearied the Lord with your words.

When challenged—"How have we wearied Him?"—God pointed to their cynical theology: "Everyone who does what is evil is good in the Lord's sight, and He is pleased with them. Where is the God of justice?"

They had lost faith in God's moral character. They looked at the wicked prospering and the righteous suffering, and concluded: God doesn't care. Justice is meaningless. Righteousness doesn't matter.

## The Coming Judgment and Purification

### The Messenger Arrives

God announced His response:

> See, I am going to send My messenger, and he will clear the way before Me. Then the Lord you seek will suddenly come to His temple, the Messenger of the covenant you desire—see, He is coming, says the Lord of Hosts.

But His coming would not be gentle:

> Who can endure the day of His coming? And who will be able to stand when He appears? For He will be like a refiner's fire and like cleansing lye.

God would come as a refiner and purifier—burning away impurity like fire refines silver and gold. The sons of Levi would be purified so they could finally present offerings in righteousness. Then Judah and Jerusalem's offerings would please the Lord as they had in days of old.

### The Judgment Against Injustice

God's justice would fall on:

- Sorcerers and adulterers
- Those who swear falsely
- Those who oppress the widow and fatherless
- Those who cheat the wage earner
- Those who deny justice to the foreigner

> They do not fear Me, says the Lord of Hosts.

Yet God reminded His people: "Because I, Yahweh, have not changed, you descendants of Jacob have not been destroyed." His covenant love endured even when they broke faith.

## The Call to Return and Test God

God issued a call and a challenge:

> Return to Me, and I will return to you, says the Lord of Hosts.

But they asked, "How can we return?" 

God answered with a direct accusation:

> Will a man rob God? Yet you are robbing Me!

When they asked how they were robbing Him, He revealed the specific sin: "By not making the payments of the tenth and the contributions."

They were under a curse, yet continued robbing God. His invitation was radical: "Bring the full tenth into the storehouse so that there may be food in My house. Test Me in this way, says the Lord of Hosts. See if I will not open the floodgates of heaven and pour out a blessing for you without measure."

God would rebuke the devourer so the land's produce would not be ruined, and their vines would bear fruit. All nations would consider them fortunate, for they would be a delightful land.

## The Division: Those Who Fear God

Yet even in judgment, God noticed something beautiful:

> At that time those who feared the Lord spoke to one another. The Lord took notice and listened. So a book of remembrance was written before Him for those who feared Yahweh and had high regard for His name.

These faithful remnant would be God's special possession. He would have compassion on them as a father has on his son who serves him. In that day, all would see the difference between the righteous and the wicked, between one who serves God and one who does not.

## The Final Day

The book closes with the vision of the end:

> For indeed, the day is coming, burning like a furnace, when all the arrogant and everyone who commits wickedness will become stubble. The coming day will consume them, says the Lord of Hosts, not leaving them root or branches.

But for those who fear God's name, the sun of righteousness will rise with healing in its wings. They will go out and playfully jump like calves released from the stall. They will trample the wicked, for they will be ashes under their feet.

God's final word calls them back to the foundation: "Remember the instruction of Moses My servant, the statutes and ordinances I commanded him at Horeb for all Israel." And He promises to send Elijah the prophet before the great and awesome Day of the Lord to turn the hearts of fathers to their children and the hearts of children to their fathers—restoring family covenant as the bridge between judgment and restoration.
