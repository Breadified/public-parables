# 1 Corinthians 6:11-20

**Title:** A Church for Sexual Purity  
**Date:** 30 August 2015

## The Illusion of Youth

When we're young, our bodies seem invincible. We can eat whatever we want without gaining weight, skip exercise and still feel energized, recover instantly from exhaustion, and bounce back from poor financial decisions. We have unlimited optimism—no matter how stressed or dire the situation, we still finish that assignment.

We can do anything with our bodies.

Yet this same youthful perspective sometimes carries over to how we view our spiritual lives. After all, we are the righteous. We've been washed, sanctified, and justified in the name of the Lord Jesus Christ by the Spirit of our God (1 Corinthians 6:11). Surely that freedom extends to how we use our bodies?

---

## Paul's Logical Challenge (verses 12-13)

Paul faces what the Corinthians saw as logical conclusions:

> "I have the right to do anything," you say—but not everything is beneficial. "I have the right to do anything"—but I will not be mastered by anything.

> You say, "Food for the stomach and the stomach for food, and God will destroy them both."

Paul's response is clear: this reasoning is flawed. Three truths emerge:

- Not everything that's permissible is beneficial
- Freedom means not being mastered by anything
- Our bodies are not meant for sexual immorality but for the Lord

> The body, however, is not meant for sexual immorality but for the Lord, and the Lord for the body.

What you do in your body counts. This is not an insignificant detail of Christian life—it matters deeply.

## Reason 1: God the Father Will Raise Our Bodies (verses 11-14)

The first reason we must be careful with our bodies is that they will be raised to eternal life.

> By his power God raised the Lord from the dead, and he will raise us also.

There is continuity between our current bodies and our future bodies. As John Piper observes: "The old body will become a new body. But it will still be your body."

We know this because Christ was raised in the same body He had before death. The tomb was empty (Luke 24:1-6), and His resurrected body retained the scars of crucifixion (John 20:25, 27). His disciples recognized Him. Since Christ's resurrection is the pattern our resurrection will follow (Philippians 3:20-21; 1 Corinthians 15:49), we too will be raised with the same body.

When we speak of resurrection as raising the dead, we mean something that was dead is made alive—the body is reanimated, not replaced. Jesus Himself spoke of resurrection as people coming forth from their tombs: "An hour is coming, in which all who are in the tombs will hear His voice, and will come forth; those who did the good deeds to a resurrection of life, those who committed the evil deeds to a resurrection of judgment" (John 5:28-29). It is renewal, not reorganization. Resurrection, not reincarnation.

Therefore, in heaven we will be physical beings in our own bodies. If you have black hair now, you'll likely have black hair in heaven—perhaps shinier, perhaps less bald, but the same color. If you're a certain height, that will probably be your height. If you're male, you remain male; if female, you remain female. Mankind is male and female, not androgynous. If you have a scar, you'll likely have it in heaven.

All of this teaches us that what you do to your body matters. What you do to your mind matters. The body is both mind and body together—like a SIM card and phone, inseparable in God's design.

But the Greek philosophy familiar to the Corinthians divided these two, treating the mind as important while dismissing the body as insignificant. This led to two dangerous attitudes: asceticism (destroy or neglect the body) and libertarianism (do anything with it because it doesn't matter). Both are foolish. This body you're mistreating today is the one you'll inhabit forever—upgraded and perfected at resurrection, but unmistakably yours.

### Delay Bodily Gratification

If we have bodies in heaven, we don't need to live in FOMO in this life. All the bodily experiences you enjoy now, you can probably enjoy in the next one. But there is one thing you cannot do in heaven: proclaim the gospel and save lives from hell. Once the gates close and the chasm opens, that work is finished.

So do this work now. Know that the fruits of your bodily labor done in service to God count for eternity. Paul speaks of a "crown that lasts." The bridge you engineer may burn up on the last day, but the colleague who believed in Christ because you invited him to a Christian lunchtime meeting—the one who heard about Jesus's love on the cross and changed his life—that work will not burn up. It will last.

For good or evil, what you do in your body today echoes in eternity. Make your body count.

## Reason 2: United with Christ the Son (verses 15-18)

The second reason to be careful with your body is that sexual immorality can harm your unity with Christ.

> Do you not know that your bodies are members of Christ himself?

"Members" doesn't mean merely belonging to a club. The word means to be limbs of Christ—literally, as in dismemberment. Jesus said:

> If your right eye causes you to stumble, gouge it out and throw it away. It is better for you to lose one part of your body than for your whole body to be thrown into hell. (Matthew 5:29)

We are meant to be limbs of Christ, doing Christ's work. Romans 6 reminds us:

> In the same way, count yourselves dead to sin but alive to God in Christ Jesus. Therefore do not let sin reign in your mortal body so that you obey its evil desires. Do not offer any part of yourself to sin as an instrument of wickedness, but rather offer yourselves to God as those who have been brought from death to life; and offer every part of yourself to him as an instrument of righteousness. (Romans 6:11-14)

We are to be instruments of righteousness—weapons for good, like Wolverine armed with blades. Yet instead, we go rogue and use these limbs of Christ to do evil. This is what the Corinthians were doing.

> Shall I then take the members of Christ and make them members of a prostitute? Never!

The original text carries profound weight often lost in translation. Paul asks: "Shall I take the members of Christ and make them members of a prostitute?" Members of Christ are meant to serve Christ. Members of a prostitute serve the prostitute and her destructive purposes.

### The Reality of Prostitution

Prostitution is a terrible thing, whether legal or not:

- In legal prostitution, 60% of women are physically assaulted, 70% are threatened with physical assault, 40% experience sexual violence, and 40% have been coerced into the profession.
- Pimps don't become nice people when prostitution is legal. Legal Amsterdam brothels have three panic buttons in every room because clients regularly attempt to rape and strangle women.
- Prostitution increases organized crime. In Amsterdam, trafficking increased 80% after legalization; 80% of women in Dutch prostitution have been trafficked.
- It disproportionately harms poor neighborhoods. After New Zealand decriminalized prostitution, battles still rage over whose neighborhood it will invade.
- Child trafficking has increased since decriminalization, especially of ethnic minority Maori children.

If you visit a prostitute, you participate in evil and support criminals.

But there's something we rarely hear anymore: being a prostitute is also evil. A prostitute wrecks families, lures men with false promises, seduces them in webs of deceit and fantasy, and takes their money.

Proverbs describes the prostitute's path:

> Many are the victims she has brought down; her slain are a mighty throng. Her house is a highway to the grave, leading down to the chambers of death. (Proverbs 7:26-27)

> For the lips of the adulterous woman drip honey, and her speech is smoother than oil; but in the end she is bitter as gall, sharp as a double-edged sword. Her feet go down to death; her steps lead straight to the grave. (Proverbs 5:3-5)

> The mouth of an adulterous woman is a deep pit; a man who is under the LORD's wrath falls into it. (Proverbs 22:14)

> For a prostitute is like a deep pit; a harlot is like a narrow well. Indeed, she lies in wait like a robber, and increases the unfaithful among men. (Proverbs 23:27-28)

Some argue that prostitutes are just trying to survive, that circumstances force them into it. While pressure and limited options are real, good intentions don't make wrong actions right. Drug dealers face similar pressures—they have children to feed, they're products of crime—yet they're still ruining a generation through addiction and slavery. Likewise, a prostitute who ruins other people's families to save her own is not noble. There is almost always a choice. The trafficked woman brought on false pretenses can say no and return home rather than ruin families for money.

### Don't Become One Flesh with Prostitution

> Do you not know that he who unites himself with a prostitute is one with her in body? For it is said, "The two will become one flesh." (1 Corinthians 6:16)

This doesn't mean you're married to a prostitute—that would make rape equivalent to marriage. Marriage is a comprehensive union involving consent and public declaration for the purpose of raising children in a healthy environment.

This statement from Genesis reminds us that sex is part of the comprehensive union found in marriage—that's where two truly become one. So why would you wreck that? Marriage is hard enough when both partners are innocent and pure. Do you think prostitution will make it easier?

Think about confessing this to your spouse. Consider the Texas police chief who took his own life when his email from a cheating website was leaked.

This behavior, once started, is hard to erase—like alcoholism. In AA meetings, people say "I am an alcoholic" even after twenty years of sobriety, because it's a demon inside. Like cancer, you're never cured—you're in remission. Once you've had cancer, your risk increases. Once you've indulged in sexual immorality, the damage to your mind and spirit is profound.

### Flee Sexual Immorality

> Flee from sexual immorality. All other sins a person commits are outside the body, but whoever sins sexually, sins against their own body. (1 Corinthians 6:18)

Flee—run away! Like Joseph did when Potiphar's wife tempted him. He had everything to gain by staying—the most powerful woman in the world on his side, advancement through the ranks. Yet he faced death as a slave for refusing. But he fled anyway.

Don't put yourself in harm's way. Don't put yourself in temptation. Put away not just evil deeds but evil desires. Your sexual practices affect who you become. Even how you date shapes who you are.

If I steal, I can return it and make restitution. But sexual immorality takes something that can never be recovered—your innocence, your mental purity. The brain's plasticity means these experiences reshape you permanently.

## Reason 3: Temple of the Holy Spirit (verses 19-20)

The third reason to honor your body is its sacred status.

> Do you not know that your bodies are temples of the Holy Spirit, who is in you, whom you have received from God? You are not your own; you were bought at a price. Therefore honor God with your bodies.

This echoes an earlier truth: "Don't you know that you yourselves are God's temple and that God's Spirit dwells in your midst?" (1 Corinthians 3:16)

### The Ripple Effects of Sexual Immorality

Sexual immorality doesn't just hurt you—it damages everyone around you.

**The john's family:** If prostitution is legal, it gives men an excuse to find sex outside marriage when relationships are difficult. This does nothing to enhance trust between husband and wife. Marriages break down, trust is lost, diseases spread, and children suffer.

**The prostitute's family:** The idea that "mom's job" is having sex with strangers sets a terrible tone for family life. It hurts the woman and her children. Seventy percent of those in prostitution are mothers.

**The church:** When a church member is revealed to live a double life, the entire congregation suffers. We are united to one another—what one member does affects us all.

### The Crisis of Sexual Immorality

Why are the problems in society so severe today? Not from lack of equality or discrimination. We have more equality and less discrimination than ever in human history. Yet suicides are higher than ever.

It's because sexual immorality has never been so flagrant in 2,000 years. TV, media, magazines—we celebrate sexual license openly. As people leave God and abandon family structures, sexual immorality leaves vulnerable children who act out their parents' models but worse. The cycle deepens.

### The Price Paid for Us

Yet there is hope in God's reversal:

> You were bought at a price.

God has brought forgiveness for our sexual sins. The mercy He showed to Rahab and Mary Magdalene—He also offers to us, whether we've prostituted ourselves to people or to alternate gods. Jesus told the woman caught in adultery, "Sin no more" (John 8:11)—not condemnation, but grace and restoration.

The solution begins with the gospel. It starts with you. It starts with the church. Nothing is inevitable except doing nothing. We must speak and live out God's vision of sex—where males are male and females are female, where our bodies glorify God and belong to Him.

That price bought all of you. Not just your mind. Not just your soul. Not just your spirit. It bought your body. This lowly piece of flesh—and God says, "That's mine. My Son paid His flesh for this flesh."

You are not your own. You must use this body for Him.

**Honor God with your bodies.**
