# Joshua 1:1-18

**Title:** Be Strong and Courageous

**Date:** June 12, 2003

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## The Promised Land (1:1-5)

After forty years of wandering in the wilderness, the Israelites finally stand at the threshold of God's promise. Moses, their deliverer and leader, has died. Now Joshua, his aide, receives the call to lead the people across the Jordan River into the land God swore to give their ancestors.

> "After the death of Moses the servant of the Lord, the Lord said to Joshua son of Nun, Moses' aide: 'Moses my servant is dead. Now then, you and all these people, get ready to cross the Jordan River into the land I am about to give to them—to the Israelites.'" (Joshua 1:1-2)

This promise reaches back centuries to Abraham in Genesis 15—a covenant God made entirely on His own terms, requiring nothing from Abraham but faith. God is in complete control. The promise has passed through the generations and now rests with Joshua.

### The Extent of the Land

The territory extends far beyond what most imagine: from the desert in the south to Lebanon in the north, from the Euphrates in the east to the Mediterranean in the west. This vast inheritance was meant for them forty years ago, and now—after the wilderness years—they are finally ready to claim it.

God's assurance is absolute:

> "I will give you every place where you set your foot, as I promised Moses. Your territory will extend from the desert to Lebanon, and from the great river, the Euphrates—all the Hittite country—to the Mediterranean Sea in the west." (Joshua 1:3-4)

### God's Unwavering Commitment

At the heart of this promise is something fundamental about God's nature:

> "No one will be able to stand against you all the days of your life. As I was with Moses, so I will be with you; I will never leave you nor forsake you." (Joshua 1:5)

When God makes a promise, He fulfills it. He takes care of all the details. He provides. He protects. He sees it through.

Think of it like having a wealthy, generous relative—someone who insists on paying whenever you gather together. You don't worry about the bill because you know them. Their generosity is certain. When God invites you to the feast of heaven, He pays. We are simply called to accept the invitation and enjoy what He has already provided.

## Be Strong and Courageous (1:6-9)

This command appears repeatedly in God's charge to Joshua, and there's a reason. The Israelites had failed before.

Forty years earlier, when spies returned with reports of the Promised Land, the people panicked. They saw fortified cities, giants, and powerful armies. Fear overwhelmed them. Instead of trusting God's promise, they believed the obstacles were too great. That generation's unbelief—their cowardice—cost them everything. They wandered the wilderness for forty years until only Joshua and Caleb remained from that generation.

Now, standing at the same threshold, Joshua receives a clear command:

> "Be strong and courageous, because you will lead these people to inherit the land I swore to their ancestors to give them. Be strong and very courageous. Be careful to obey all the law my servant Moses gave you; do not turn from it to the right or to the left, that you may be successful wherever you go. Keep this Book of the Law always on your lips; meditate on it day and night, so that you may be careful to do everything written in it. Then you will be prosperous and successful." (Joshua 1:6-8)

### What Courage Looks Like

The Hebrew words for "strong" and "courageous" are nearly synonymous. They speak of confidence, firmness, boldness, and alertness. But notice what courage is grounded in: **obedience to God's Word**. Not confidence in military strength or strategy, but loyalty to what God has commanded.

Courage doesn't mean the absence of fear. It means trusting God despite what you see. The dinner has been paid for. The victory has already been secured. You are simply called to show up with confidence and take what is yours.

This isn't about Joshua conquering the land through his own strength. It's about obedience. God will do the impossible. Joshua's role is to believe and obey.

### The Foundation: God's Word

Success comes through meditation on Scripture—keeping it on your lips, turning it over in your mind day and night. This transforms how you think. When you know what God has said, you can trust what He will do.

## Courage in Community (1:12-18)

There's a striking detail in Joshua's charge. Before leading the people across the Jordan, Joshua turns to the Reubenites and Gadites—the tribes who had chosen to settle east of the Jordan because the land was suitable for their livestock.

These warriors had secured their own inheritance early. They could have simply stayed put. Instead, God's word to Joshua was clear: they must march with their brothers first. They cannot rest while others are still fighting. They must be the first wave, supporting the conquest of the land west of the Jordan.

> "Remember the command that Moses the servant of the Lord gave you after he said, 'The Lord your God will give you rest by giving you this land.' Your wives, your children and your livestock may stay in the land that Moses gave you east of the Jordan, but all your fighting men, ready for battle, must cross over ahead of your fellow Israelites. You are to help them until the Lord gives them rest, as he has done for you." (Joshua 1:13-15)

The Reubenites and Gadites understood something critical: discouragement spreads. Years earlier, when the spies brought back a faithless report, fear rippled through the entire nation. Courage and cowardice are both contagious.

The response from these tribes is striking:

> "Whatever you have commanded us we will do, and wherever you send us we will go. Just as we fully obeyed Moses, so we will obey you. Only may the Lord your God be with you as he was with Moses. Whoever rebels against your word and does not obey it, whatever you may command them, will be put to death. Only be strong and courageous!" (Joshua 1:16-18)

Notice the irony: as they encourage Joshua to be brave, they themselves commit to courageous obedience. They pledge to support the full conquest before settling into their own rest.

## The Challenge: Obedience When It Costs

In our own time, we face a similar challenge. Following God's Word isn't always popular. Sometimes it requires courage to stand for truth when the culture opposes it. Sometimes it means standing alone, or watching others—even fellow Christians—criticize you for taking a stand.

True Christian courage is "the willingness to say and do the right thing regardless of the earthly cost, because God promises to help you and save you on account of Christ."

This applies to evangelism. God hasn't called us to manipulate or force belief. We're called to speak truth with boldness, knowing the elect belong to God. If people aren't interested, that's not our failure—it's God's sovereign choice. We don't need to be afraid of rejection.

It applies to standing for biblical truth in a culture increasingly hostile to it. Not with arrogance, but with conviction. When friends mock your faith, do you stand firm or shrink back? When injustice surrounds you, do you speak up or stay silent? When others won't take a stand, do you encourage them to join you—or do you discourage them further by criticizing from the sidelines?

The most discouraging thing is when Christians shame other Christians for taking a stand while remaining silent themselves. Like the Reubenites and Gadites if they had mocked the conquest instead of supporting it. We need to encourage one another, sharpen one another, and fight together.

> "Be on your guard; stand firm in the faith; be men of courage; be strong." (1 Corinthians 16:13)

## The Ultimate Example: Jesus the True Joshua

Jesus is the fulfillment of Joshua's name and calling. He is the one who was truly strong and courageous.

He faced the ultimate enemy—not earthly armies, but Satan himself, sin, and death. He took all the fire, all the ammunition. Satan threw everything at Him. And Jesus, in His courageous obedience, died and rose again.

That's the decisive victory. Satan is mortally wounded. He has no more ammunition. His teeth are gone.

Now we live in the aftermath of victory. Jesus has already won. The beachhead is established. Those who follow Him are entering into a land already conquered, already secured by His blood.

How foolish it would be to turn back now. How shameful to doubt after such a sacrifice has been made on our behalf. The challenge isn't to fear the enemy—it's to have the courage to step into what Christ has already won.

We obey God not because we fear defeat, but because we've already been given victory. We stand firm in His Word. We encourage one another. We take what rightfully belongs to us as God's people.

**Be strong and courageous. The Lord your God is with you wherever you go.**
