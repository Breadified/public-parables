# Zechariah 14:1-21

**Title:** A New Day
**Date:** 25 December 2016
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The End of the Year

As we approach the end of the year, we find ourselves in different places. Some of us are surprised by how quickly time has passed, scrambling to complete unfinished projects and commitments. Others have been waiting for this moment—hoping to put a difficult year behind us and begin again.

The ancient Israelites, two and a half thousand years ago, also longed for such a day. Through the prophet Zechariah, God speaks to them in their hopes and disappointments with a promise: **"Return to me and I will return to you."** This final chapter of Zechariah closes his prophecy with a message for both the ancient Jews and for us.

Zechariah presents his vision in two key movements: a day of escape from judgment, and a day of peace when God reigns over all the earth.

## 1. The Day of Escape (Zechariah 14:1-8, 12-19)

### The Siege and the Suffering

The day begins with terrible judgment. Jerusalem faces a catastrophic siege—armies gather against the city, plunder its wealth, divide its treasures within the very walls meant to protect it. The scope of suffering expands from the city itself to individual homes to the most personal tragedies: the capture of the city, ransacking of houses, and violation of women. Half the population will be taken into exile, the other half left to suffer in their devastated home.

Yet it is in the midst of this darkest hour that God intervenes.

> 1 A day of the Lord is coming, Jerusalem, when your possessions will be plundered and divided up within your very walls.
> 
> 2 I will gather all the nations to Jerusalem to fight against it; the city will be captured, the houses ransacked, and the women raped. Half of the city will go into exile, but the rest of the people will not be taken from the city.
> 
> 3 Then the Lord will go out and fight against those nations, as he fights on a day of battle.

### The Miraculous Escape

What follows is a geological upheaval of stunning proportions. God's feet stand on the Mount of Olives, east of Jerusalem—and the mountain splits in two from east to west. Half moves north, half moves south, creating a great valley. For those trapped in the besieged city on the western side, with enemies approaching from higher ground, this becomes their escape route.

> 4 On that day his feet will stand on the Mount of Olives, east of Jerusalem, and the Mount of Olives will be split in two from east to west, forming a great valley, with half of the mountain moving north and half moving south.

This imagery echoes the ancient exodus from Egypt. When the Israelites fled Pharaoh's army, they too were backed into a wall—trapped between the pursuing Egyptian forces and an impassable sea. Yet God divided that water in two, and they crossed to safety on dry ground. Here, God divides the mountain to provide the same kind of miraculous rescue.

> 5 You will flee by my mountain valley, for it will extend to Azel. You will flee as you fled from the earthquake in the days of Uzziah king of Judah. Then the Lord my God will come, and all the holy ones with him.

And just as the waters came crashing down to destroy Egypt's army, so too will water fill this valley—destroying the enemies who pursued God's people.

> 8 On that day living water will flow out from Jerusalem, half of it east to the Dead Sea and half of it west to the Mediterranean Sea, in summer and in winter.

### Backed Into a Wall

This image speaks directly to us. All of humanity stands backed into a wall—a wall of our own making, impenetrable, with no escape. We face judgment with seemingly no possibility of deliverance. Our lives are hemmed in by sin, regret, and the weight of consequences we cannot reverse. We have run out of options.

But God's word is clear: when there is no hope, a way will be opened. An escape route appears. A solution is provided. A chance to repent, to change, to return.

## 2. The Day of Peace (Zechariah 14:9-11, 20-21)

### Universal Reign and Security

With the enemies defeated and the people rescued comes a new reality. Jerusalem is raised up high and secure.

> 9 The Lord will be king over the whole earth. On that day there will be one Lord, and his name the only name.
> 
> 10 The whole land, from Geba to Rimmon, south of Jerusalem, will become like the Arabah. But Jerusalem will be raised up high from the Benjamin Gate to the site of the First Gate, to the Corner Gate, and from the Tower of Hananel to the royal winepresses, and will remain in its place. 11 It will be inhabited; never again will it be destroyed. Jerusalem will be secure.

No more siege. No more suffering. Instead, there is prosperity and peace. Jerusalem stands elevated—so high that it needs no walls because it is beyond the reach of enemies. It will never be destroyed again.

### Everything Made Holy

The final vision of Zechariah reveals something even more profound. In this restored world, holiness is no longer confined to the temple or the sacred priesthood. Everything becomes holy.

> 20 On that day holy to the Lord will be inscribed on the bells of the horses, and the cooking pots in the Lord's house will be like the sacred bowls in front of the altar. 21 Every pot in Jerusalem and Judah will be holy to the Lord Almighty, and all who come to sacrifice will take some of the pots and cook in them.

Think about this: not just the temple vessels, but ordinary cooking pots. Not just the priest's equipment, but the bells on horses. Even your everyday tools become sacred. When God makes something holy, everything it touches is transformed. The profane becomes precious. The ordinary becomes set apart for God's use.

This reaches its fulfillment through Christ's death. His sacrifice is so effective that all who accept it are made holy. We become vessels fit for God himself. One day, the entire creation—every place, every object, every moment—will be sanctified.

## The Day Has Come (Matthew 27:45-54)

When did this day arrive? It came in Jesus.

> 45 From noon until three in the afternoon darkness came over all the land.
> 
> 50 And when Jesus had cried out again in a loud voice, he gave up his spirit. 51 At that moment the curtain of the temple was torn in two from top to bottom. The earth shook, the rocks split 52 and the tombs broke open. The bodies of many holy people who had died were raised to life.
> 
> 54 When the centurion and those with him who were guarding Jesus saw the earthquake and all that had happened, they were terrified, and exclaimed, "Surely he was the Son of God!"

The parallels are unmistakable:
- Darkness covered the land (Zechariah 14:6-7)
- The earth shook and rocks split (Zechariah 14:4)
- The dead were raised—the holy ones came forth (Zechariah 14:5)

These are not literal physical earthquakes in the manner Zechariah describes—no valley opens from Dead Sea to Mediterranean. Yet they point to something far greater: a spiritual escape from sin and judgment.

Humanity was backed into a wall, with no way out. When there was no hope, the wall was broken open. A way was made. On the cross, Jesus became that solution. When He gave His last breath, the barrier between God and humanity was torn in two. The way home was opened.

## Faith in God's Promise (Matthew 17:20-22, 21:21-22)

Jesus taught His disciples about the power of faith:

> If you have faith as small as a mustard seed, you can say to this mountain, 'Move from here to there' and it will move. Nothing will be impossible for you.

This is not about positive thinking or wishful believing. It is not about controlling God like Santa Claus, demanding what we want and expecting to receive it. God is not Santa. He is Father. Like all good fathers, He listens to our requests but ultimately decides what is best for us.

This passage teaches us to believe in something far greater: that Jesus will rescue us. To have faith in God's judgment and in Christ's power to save. To believe that if we ask for salvation, if we pray for escape from sin, we will receive it—because that is God's perfect will for us.

## Living Between the Mountains

We have been given a remarkable gift. All humanity faced an impossible wall—judgment we could not escape, sin we could not undo. Decisions made cannot be reversed. Regrets cannot be erased.

But the valley has been opened. When Jesus died and rose again, the path to redemption was made clear. We walk between the mountains now, in faith, toward a day when all things will be made new and holy.

As we end this year and begin the next, whatever walls have trapped us—whatever failures, regrets, and impossible circumstances we face—we do not walk alone. The One who split mountains stands with us. His sacrifice has opened the way.

## A Call to Return

Zechariah's word rings true across the centuries: **"Return to me and I will return to you."**

For thousands of years, God's people waited for this day. Zechariah waited five hundred years. Then Christmas came.

> For to us a child is born, to us a son is given, and the government will be on his shoulders. And he will be called Wonderful Counselor, Mighty God, Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace.

Jesus has come once. He will come again. The day has not finished. We await the final conclusion when peace will reign on all the earth.

Put your trust in Jesus. Not in yourself, not in circumstance, not in the passing year—but in the One who conquered death and opened the way for all who believe.
