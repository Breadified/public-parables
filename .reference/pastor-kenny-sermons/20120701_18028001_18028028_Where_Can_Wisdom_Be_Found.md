# Job 28:1-28

**Title:** Where Can Wisdom Be Found?
**Date:** 13 July 2012
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Question

> "But where can wisdom be found? Where does understanding dwell?" (Job 28:12)

This is the central question that frames Job's profound meditation on wisdom. Where does true understanding come from?

## Part 1: We Can Dig Up the Treasures of the Earth

Job begins by describing humanity's remarkable ability to extract wealth from the ground. We have mines for silver and gold. We tunnel deep into the earth, searching in the blackest darkness, pursuing ore in places forgotten by man. We break through rock and lay bare the roots of mountains. We search the sources of rivers and bring hidden things to light.

Consider the real-world example of mining in Australia. It has become a massive source of job growth and has created billionaires. Fortunes like those of Gina Rineheart—who turned an inheritance of $75 million into billions—demonstrate humanity's capacity to find and extract earthly treasures.

We are remarkably skilled at acquiring material wealth. Yet this raises the critical question: if we can find gold and silver, why can't we find wisdom?

## Part 2: We Cannot Dig Up the Treasures of Heaven

Here is where Job's teaching takes a decisive turn.

**Wisdom Cannot Be Found on Earth**

> "Man does not comprehend its worth; it cannot be found in the land of the living. The deep says, 'It is not in me'; the sea says, 'It is not with me.'" (Job 28:13-14)

**Wisdom Cannot Be Bought**

No amount of money can purchase wisdom. It cannot be bought with the finest gold, nor can its price be weighed in silver. Gold of Ophir, precious onyx, sapphires—none of these can compare with wisdom. Coral and jasper are not worthy of mention. The price of wisdom is beyond rubies.

This is the fundamental difference between earthly treasures and heavenly wisdom. Earthly wealth can be acquired through human effort and exchange. Heavenly wisdom remains hidden from the eyes of every living thing. Even destruction and death know only rumors of it.

## Part 3: Real Wisdom Comes from God

The answer to "Where is wisdom?" is simple yet transformative: wisdom belongs to God alone.

> "God understands the way to it and he alone knows where it dwells, for he views the ends of the earth and sees everything under the heavens. When he established the force of the wind and measured out the waters, when he made a decree for the rain and a path for the thunderstorm, then he looked at wisdom and appraised it; he confirmed it and tested it." (Job 28:23-27)

**The Heart of Wisdom**

Job concludes with God's own declaration:

> "And he said to man, 'The fear of the Lord—that is wisdom, and to shun evil is understanding.'" (Job 28:28)

This is the message from heaven: **the fear of the Lord is true wisdom**.

Paul echoes this truth in 1 Corinthians:

> "Where is the wise man? Where is the scholar? Where is the philosopher of this age? Has not God made foolish the wisdom of the world? For since in the wisdom of God the world through its wisdom did not know him, God was pleased through the foolishness of what was preached to save those who believe. Jews demand miraculous signs and Greeks look for wisdom, but we preach Christ crucified: a stumbling block to Jews and foolishness to Gentiles, but to those whom God has called, both Jews and Greeks, Christ the power of God and the wisdom of God. For the foolishness of God is wiser than man's wisdom, and the weakness of God is stronger than man's strength." (1 Corinthians 1:20-25)

## What Does Fearing the Lord Look Like?

The fear of the Lord is not terror—it is an attitude of humility and reverence. It is recognizing that we are not that wise, that God's wisdom far exceeds our own.

**The Call to Action:**

We are invited to get this wisdom from God through:
- Reading the Bible personally and together
- Living out this wisdom to see if it works in our lives
- Building our ethics and life choices on God's truth
- Stopping the pursuit of the "foolish gold" that consumes so many around us

**A Hard Truth:**

The fear of the Lord results in persecution and suffering. Many of us say we want to follow Jesus, yet we resist the suffering that inevitably comes with truly fearing God. Are we actually trying to see His wisdom work in our lives? Or are we giving up too easily when the cost becomes real?

The meaning of life is found in Jesus—in the crucified Christ who judges, who makes all things new, where gold and silver count for nothing. The fear of the Lord is paramount.
