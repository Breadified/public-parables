# James 3:13-18

**Title:** Heavenly Wisdom  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## What Is Wisdom?

We often hear the word "wisdom" thrown around—associated with good decisions, maturity, knowledge, or success. But what does true wisdom really mean? Is it the person who knows everything? The one who achieves the highest marks? The old sage sitting on a mountain?

According to James, the wise person is one "who shows it by his good life, by deeds done in the humility that comes from wisdom" (James 3:13). True wisdom isn't merely intellectual—it's demonstrated through how we live.

## Wisdom from Heaven

The readers of James were struggling. Their behavior revealed they lacked wisdom. So James tells them: "If any of you lacks wisdom, he should ask God, who gives generously to all without finding fault, and it will be given to him" (James 1:5).

But what does this mean? Does our IQ suddenly increase by a hundred points? Does our memory improve? No. **Heavenly wisdom is the knowledge of the gospel—God revealing himself to us through Jesus Christ.**

> "Jews demand miraculous signs and Greeks look for wisdom, but we preach Christ crucified: a stumbling block to Jews and foolishness to Gentiles, but to those whom God has called, both Jews and Greeks, Christ the power of God and the wisdom of God." (1 Corinthians 1:22-24)

> "It is because of him that you are in Christ Jesus, who has become for us wisdom from God—that is, our righteousness, holiness and redemption." (1 Corinthians 1:29-30)

The wisdom of the world operates as if God doesn't exist. But the Christian's wisdom is rooted in the reality of the gospel.

### A Tale of Two Gameplans

Imagine you're on a sports field, ball in hand, moving toward victory. But imagine two different games with two different ways of winning:

**The Materialist's Game:** "He who has the most toys at the end wins." But for those who know the gospel, this is foolish—because material things perish.

**The Hedonist's Game:** "He who has the most fun and pleasure wins." But for those who know the gospel, this too is foolish—because pleasure is temporary, but heaven is eternal.

When we understand the gospel, we're playing an entirely different game. We've changed our gameplan. This is why heavenly wisdom seems like foolishness to those still playing by worldly rules.

## Transformation Through the Gospel

If we know the gospel and believe it, then we are wise. When we are in Christ, we have access to the wisdom of God himself. But this wisdom demands something of us: **we must forsake worldly views of reality and begin seeing our world through the lens of the gospel.**

This is transformation. It happens through:

- **Study of God's Word** — "Be transformed by the renewing of your mind" (Romans 12:2). As we study Scripture, we become more aware of what Christ has done for us.
- **Prayer** — When James tells us to ask God for wisdom, he doesn't mean asking to be cleverer. He means asking to know "the hope to which he has called you" and to understand "how great it really is" (Ephesians 1:17-23; Ephesians 3:16-19).

## The Proof Is in the Life

Knowledge alone isn't enough. You can't claim to be wise and then live foolishly—that's like Homer Simpson singing "I am smart, S-M-A-R-T" while demonstrating the opposite.

> "Do not merely listen to the word, and so deceive yourselves. Do what it says!" (James 1:22)

The criterion for true wisdom is simple: **by your good life and humble deeds done in wisdom** (James 3:13). Wisdom is deeply practical.

## Two Kinds of Wisdom

James presents a stark contrast between two types of wisdom:

### Earthly Wisdom
- **Origins:** Earthly, unspiritual, devilish
- **Characteristics:** Bitter envy, selfish ambition, jealousy
- **Result:** Disorder and every evil thing

At its root, earthly wisdom is driven by selfishness—prioritizing our own needs above others. It manifests in envy, jealousy, and quarrels. Whatever you accomplish through this wisdom ultimately comes to nothing.

### Heavenly Wisdom
- **Origins:** From above, from heaven
- **Characteristics:** Pure, peace-loving, considerate, submissive, full of mercy and good fruit, impartial and sincere
- **Result:** A good life and a harvest of righteousness

When we possess heavenly wisdom rooted in the gospel, our actions reflect it:

- **Pure:** Holy in our relationships, especially with those of the opposite sex—not changing who we are when no one's watching
- **Peace-loving:** Free from gossip, slander, and quarreling
- **Considerate:** Putting others' needs before our own, remembering that Christ sacrificed himself for us
- **Submissive:** Respecting authority—parents, teachers, leaders—recognizing that Jesus is Lord over all
- **Full of mercy:** Looking out for the weak and vulnerable
- **Impartial and sincere:** Treating everyone fairly, not showing favoritism based on popularity

This isn't mystical. It's practical, observable, real.

## Living Out Heavenly Wisdom

So how do we make decisions? The answer is clear: **Because you are in Christ, the will of God is known.** God's goal is to bring everything under Christ's rule. Now we respond appropriately to that reality.

This doesn't require waiting for mystical guidance or feelings. Instead:

1. **Study the Bible** — As we understand what Christ has accomplished, we make decisions that align with that gospel reality
2. **Pray with understanding** — Ask God to deepen your grasp of His work in your life
3. **Act accordingly** — Work out the implications of the gospel in your specific situation—at school, at home, in your relationships

## The Challenge

Who is wise and understanding among you? Let him show it by his good life, by deeds done in the humility that comes from wisdom. The world will have to see. Your life will be the proof.
