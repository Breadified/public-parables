# Ephesians 3:1-13

**Title:** God's Secret Revealed

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## Introduction: The Secret

Last year, a film called *The Secret* became wildly popular, promoted by Oprah and featured on bestseller lists. It presented what they called the "Law of Attraction"—the idea that your thoughts and feelings attract circumstances into your life. If you think about money, money comes to you. It's a simple concept wrapped in mystery, and of course, it came with a price tag of $34.95.

But Paul's letter to the Ephesians reveals a *different* secret—one that isn't hidden and costs nothing at all. It's not the law of attraction, but the abolishment of the law. It's a secret freely given and openly accessible to all.

---

## 1. God's Secret Is Revealed (v.6)

> This mystery is that through the gospel the Gentiles are heirs together with Israel, members together of one body, and sharers together in the promise in Christ Jesus. (Ephesians 3:6)

### The Great News

Through the gospel, something revolutionary has happened:

- **Gentiles are heirs together with Israel** — Those outside God's chosen people are now joint heirs
- **Members together of one body** — No separation, no hierarchy
- **Sharers together in the promise** — Full access to what was promised to Abraham

This is Paul's central message: the Gentiles are saved too, alongside the Jews. Throughout Ephesians 1-2, this theme echoes:

- In chapter 2, all are raised together with Christ (v.6)
- Those who were once foreigners to the covenant, without hope and without God, are now inheritors (v.11)
- The separated are brought near to God, and the two are made one (vv.13-14)
- In chapter 1, all things in heaven and on earth are brought together under one head, Christ (v.10)

### Why Was This a Secret?

**It was previously unknown.** This plan was hidden in God for ages past, not made known to previous generations. It was "unsearchable," described as a "mystery," "hidden in God" until the Spirit revealed it to His apostles and prophets.

**It was completely unpredictable.** No one saw this coming. Consider the history between Israel and the Gentile nations:

For two thousand years, they were enemies. They enslaved one another. Egypt enslaved Israel; Israel enslaved the Canaanites. When Israel entered the promised land, they were commanded to destroy all the nations living there. Even from Israel's beginning, the message was clear: you are set apart. Leviticus 20:24-26 established Israel as holy, separated from all other nations—no alliances, no intermarriage, no treaties with foreign powers.

When Israel broke this pattern and allied with the nations—when Solomon married foreign wives and made treaties—disaster followed. The Gentile empires eventually conquered and exiled Israel: the Assyrians, the Babylonians, the Greeks, the Romans.

Israel's policy was protectionist. Internationally, they were isolationists. Socially, they said: "You may integrate and become like us, but we will never become like you." They were xenophobic, unwilling to share their inheritance with outsiders.

### The Wall of Hostility

After centuries of this relationship, a great dividing wall of hostility separated Jews from Gentiles. This wasn't merely cultural—it was literal. The temple in Jerusalem was physically divided into three sections: one for the high priest, one for Jews, and one for Gentiles. That courtyard for Gentiles? It was like the parking lot of a shopping center compared to the holiness within.

Even when Gentiles wanted to integrate and join, they were treated as second-class citizens.

**Consider a parallel:** In Australia, the White Australia Policy wasn't abolished until 1973. For nearly two hundred years, anyone who wasn't white and European was considered inferior. Indigenous Australians, Asians—all discriminated against. It took an apology from the government just last year. Two hundred years of ingrained suspicion created deep damage that persists today.

But imagine that pattern extended to two thousand years. That's the mental and spiritual landscape Paul's readers inherited. It was unthinkable that Gentiles—the "unclean and unwashed"—would share anything with Jews except servitude.

**And then came the secret.** The revelation that overturned everything.

Now the privileges of Israel belong to the Gentiles too. They are no longer confined to the courtyard. They stand with the best. The dogs now eat at the table with the children. It's radical. It's revolutionary. It was crazy talk.

## 2. Paul Is Given the Secret from Christ (vv.1-5)

> For this reason I, Paul, the prisoner of Christ Jesus for the sake of you Gentiles... (Ephesians 3:1)

Paul pauses mid-sentence here. He begins with "For this reason, I..." but doesn't finish until verse 14. Instead, he digresses to explain what "for the sake of you Gentiles" means.

> Surely you have heard about the administration of God's grace that was given to me for you, that is, the mystery made known to me by revelation... which was not made known to men in other generations as it has now been revealed by the Spirit to God's holy apostles and prophets. (vv.2-5)

### How Paul Received the Secret

This secret was revealed to Paul directly by Christ. In Acts 9, we see his commission:

> "Lord," Ananias answered, "I have heard many reports about this man and all the harm he has done to your saints in Jerusalem. And he has come here with authority from the chief priests to arrest all who call on your name." But the Lord said to Ananias, "Go! This man is my chosen instrument to carry my name before the Gentiles and their kings and before the people of Israel. I will show him how much he must suffer for my name." (Acts 9:13-16)

Paul was a persecutor—the most zealous opponent of Christians among the Jews. How could such a man become God's vessel for revealing salvation to the Gentiles? The answer lies in his encounter on the Damascus Road. A light blinded him. Jesus confronted him. He repented, trusted Christ, and was transformed.

Paul's credentials are extraordinary. God Himself commissioned him. He has been given grace to preach to the Gentiles the unsearchable riches of Christ and to make plain this mystery that was hidden for ages.

### Why This Matters

Some Christians today prefer Jesus but are skeptical of Paul. "Paul was sexist," they say, or "homophobic." "Jesus was all-loving; Paul twisted things." But Paul's letters are apostolic authority—they speak to us today because they carry God's Word.

Paul isn't establishing a succession of apostles to pass down this secret. Instead, he sees his role as the key person revealing it to the Gentiles. His mission was to make it public, to shout it from the rooftops through his letters. Ephesians still speaks today, telling Gentiles everywhere that the good news is for them—that they have full access to the Jewish inheritance without becoming Jewish.

**Listen to Paul today.** Not to angels, not to mystical revelations, not to tea leaves in cups. Listen to the apostles, the prophets, and the Word of God.

## 3. God's Secret Revealed: He Loves the World (vv.9-10)

> Although I am less than the least of all God's people, this grace was given me: to preach to the Gentiles the unsearchable riches of Christ, and to make plain to everyone the administration of this mystery, which for ages past was kept hidden in God, who created all things. (Ephesians 3:7-9)

### Salvation Is for All

Paul was appointed by God to preach and make plain this mystery—not to keep it hidden, but to open it to everyone. The wall that said "Gentiles keep away" is demolished. The barrier that confined Gentiles to the outer courtyard is shattered.

> In him and through faith in him, we may approach God with freedom and confidence. (Ephesians 3:12)

Gentiles now have equal access to the very presence of God through Christ. They approach with freedom, with boldness, with the confidence of beloved children.

### The Cosmic Purpose

> His intent was that now, through the church, the manifold wisdom of God should be made known to the rulers and authorities in the heavenly realms, according to his eternal purpose which he accomplished in Christ Jesus our Lord. (Ephesians 3:10-11)

When Paul says "through the church," he doesn't mean one local congregation. He means the unified body of Gentiles and Jews gathered together in Christ. By bringing these two warring groups into one people, God displays His manifold wisdom to the heavenly powers—to the rulers of the air themselves.

God is saying to the cosmic powers arrayed against Him: "I have found a way to bring blessing to all nations. I have secured salvation for the entire world." Satan accused God: "You committed Yourself to saving the world through Abraham and Israel, but Israel failed to bring the nations into salvation." But one Jew—the Lord Jesus—proved it could be done. He went to the Syrophoenician woman. He freed the demon-possessed man called Legion. He looked beyond ethnic and cultural boundaries to embrace the outsider.

Through the church—through this impossible union of Jew and Gentile—God demonstrates His power to bring all things under Christ's feet.

## Application: God Loves You

"Salvation for all" is another way of saying: **salvation is for you.**

Jesus didn't die only for the Jews, His own race. He died for those outside, those cut off from the privileges of being Jewish. He died for the marginalized, the outsider, the one who feels rejected.

- The gay person the world rejects
- The Aboriginal person discriminated against
- The sick and suffering
- The Asian who knows what it feels like to be an outsider

Imagine being excluded for two thousand years—and then suddenly welcomed as a full heir. We Asians understand this experience of being outsiders. That's why the message of Ephesians is so powerful.

**If you are not yet a Christian,** I invite you to become one today. The secret is yours to claim. God loves you. No charge. No conditions. Just come.

**If you are a Christian,** be assured: no matter how marginalized you feel, no matter how rejected you believe yourself to be, God loves you with the love that brought Gentiles and Jews together at impossible cost.

The secret is open. God loves the world. And that includes you.
