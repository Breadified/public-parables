# Revelation 12:1-18

**Title:** A Night of the Dragon  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Understanding Revelation 12:1-6

Rather than asking simply "Who is the woman, the dragon, and the child?" we should explore deeper questions: What do these figures symbolize, and what are they doing?

### The Woman
The woman in this vision carries symbolic weight drawn from Old Testament imagery. Consider Genesis 37:9-11, where Joseph's dream featured celestial symbols representing his family. Here, the woman represents God's people—the church protected and preserved through the ages.

### The Dragon
The dragon demands careful attention to its features. What do the heads, horns, and crowns represent? Recall how Egypt functioned as a symbol of evil and opposition in Revelation 11. The dragon echoes Old Testament imagery of chaos and rebellion found in Ezekiel 29 and 32, and Psalm 74:13—representing Satan himself, the ultimate source of opposition to God's kingdom.

### The Child
This child is the fulfillment of Psalm 2:7. He is none other than Jesus Christ, the one destined to rule all nations with an iron scepter. His life is described in terms of authority and triumph.

### The Desert Sanctuary
The desert becomes a place of divine refuge. Throughout Scripture, the desert represents both testing and protection—recall the wilderness wanderings in Exodus 15-16, or Elijah's flight to the desert in 1 Kings 19. Here, God provides sanctuary for the woman, preserving His people during times of persecution.

### The Dragon's Fury
The dragon's anger is directed at the woman and her offspring—those who follow Christ and keep God's commandments. This reveals the true nature of Satan's rebellion: opposition to God's people and God's purposes.

## The Heavenly War (Revelation 12:7-16)

### Michael and the Conflict
Michael is the warrior angel identified in Daniel 10:21 and 12:1 as the great prince who stands guard over God's people. The war in heaven is not merely a supernatural conflict—it plays out in the Old Testament through the struggles of God's people against opposing forces.

### How the Dragon is Defeated
The dragon is overcome through the blood of the Lamb and the word of our testimony. This is not primarily a military victory in a physical sense, but a spiritual triumph accomplished through Christ's redemptive work. We participate in this victory through faithful witness—not shrinking back even when facing death, but proclaiming the truth of the gospel.

### Eagles' Wings and Divine Protection
When God brings the woman to Himself on eagles' wings, we are reminded of His protection at Mount Sinai (Exodus 19:4) and His constant care for His people. The eagle's wings represent divine strength and the tender care of our Father.

### The Flood and Escape
The serpent spews a flood from his mouth to destroy the woman, but the earth opens to swallow it. This vivid imagery speaks to how God's purposes cannot be thwarted by satanic opposition. What the enemy intends for harm, God transforms through His sovereignty.

## Two of Seven Visions (Revelation 13:1-15:4)

From Revelation 13:1 through 15:4, John records seven successive visions. Tonight we examine two of them; the remaining five will follow in our next study.

### The Beast from the Sea
This beast represents political power and governmental authority aligned against God's kingdom. The beast from the sea symbolizes human government operating under satanic influence—wielding power without regard for God's justice or mercy.

### Our Response to Oppressive Government
We are called to patient endurance and faithfulness. Rather than rebelling through violence or compromise, we maintain our allegiance to Christ, knowing that His kingdom will ultimately prevail.

### The Beast from the Earth
The second beast is described as having the appearance of a lamb but speaking like a dragon—a wolf in sheep's clothing. This represents false religion and deceptive authority that mimics the true faith while serving demonic purposes.

### The Mark and False Worship
This beast compels people to receive a mark and to worship the image of the first beast. The significance lies in absolute allegiance: you cannot buy or sell, cannot participate in society, without this mark. But this directly contradicts Deuteronomy 13:1-3, which warns against following signs and wonders that would lead us away from the true God.

### The Number 666
In Revelation, numbers and pictures together unlock symbolic meaning. The number 666 represents human rebellion and incompleteness. Where God's number is 7 (completion and perfection), 666 falls eternally short—a parody of divine perfection, a number of human pride and godlessness.

## Our Calling in the Midst of Opposition

The repeated refrain challenges us directly: Are we being wise? Are we patiently enduring? Are we remaining faithful even in the midst of this type of persecution?

This is not abstract theology. In Revelation, John addresses churches facing real pressure to compromise—to worship Caesar, to participate in economic systems built on idolatry, to deny Christ for the sake of safety. His message then is our message now: hold fast to Jesus. He has already won the victory. Our faithfulness, even costly faithfulness, testifies to His triumph.
