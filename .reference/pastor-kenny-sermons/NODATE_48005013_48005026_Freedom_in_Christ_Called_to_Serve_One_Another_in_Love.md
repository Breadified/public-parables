# Galatians 5:13-26

**Title:** Freedom in Christ: Called to Serve One Another in Love

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## Introduction

Last week we explored freedom from the law and freedom in Christ. A natural question follows: if we're free in Christ, does that mean we can do anything? Today we're answering that crucial question.

**Main Point:** Christians are free not to sin—and this freedom exists so we can love each other.

---

## Point 1: Christians Have Freedom From Sin

> You, my brothers, were called to be free. But do not use your freedom to indulge the sinful nature; rather, serve one another in love. (Galatians 5:13)

### Understanding "Flesh"

When Paul speaks of the "sinful nature" or "flesh," he doesn't mean we should hate our physical bodies—that's a Greek idea, not a Christian one. Rather, "flesh" refers to:

- The ways of death and decay
- Our sinful desires and impulses
- A kind of slavery to our base nature

To sin is to live by the flesh. But now that we're free from the flesh through Christ, Paul's urgent point is this: **don't go back to it.**

### The Illustration

Imagine you're desperately sick with food poisoning. You can't eat; you don't want to eat. Then you vomit violently, expelling what was poisoning you. You're finally free! The poison is gone. You're free to eat again.

But then—inexplicably—you go back and eat the vomit. You seek out that festered box of spoiled food and eat from it again.

That's what Paul means when he warns against returning to sin. Now that you're free, don't stupidly go back to what was killing you.

### What Is "the Vomit"?

> The acts of the sinful nature are obvious: sexual immorality, impurity and debauchery; idolatry and witchcraft; hatred, discord, jealousy, fits of rage, selfish ambition, dissensions, factions and envy; drunkenness, orgies, and the like. I warn you, as I did before, that those who live like this will not inherit the kingdom of God. (Galatians 5:19-21)

These acts are obvious. We know them when we see them:
- Sexual immorality—what we watch on the internet
- Selfish ambition and pride
- Envy and jealousy
- Selfishness in all its forms

---

## Point 2: Christians Are Free *For* Service

> Rather, serve one another in love. (Galatians 5:13b)

### Freedom Redefined

Here's the critical insight: we're not just free *from* something; we're free *for* something else.

Contrary to popular thinking, Christian freedom is not the freedom to do anything we want. Rather, we're freed from the petty legalism of Old Testament law so we can follow the true law—the law that sums up everything God wants:

> The entire law is summed up in a single command: "Love your neighbor as yourself." (Galatians 5:14)

### Illustrations of True Freedom

**The sacrifice of parents.** When immigrant parents sacrifice their homeland and security for their children's education, those children aren't freed to dishonor that sacrifice. They're freed to excel—to use their education and opportunity well. We honor their sacrifice by working hard and taking full advantage of the freedom they bought for us.

**The ANZAC sacrifice.** Australian soldiers fought and died to give this country freedom—freedom from foreign aggressors, freedom to live in peace. That freedom wasn't given so Australians could terrorize the country, steal, or kill. It was given so we could build a better nation and serve it well.

**Christ's sacrifice.** But someone has done far more for us than even our parents or our soldiers. Christ died to free us from the greatest problem of all time—the penalty of sin itself. This freedom wasn't given so we could serve ourselves and our petty desires, nor so we could return to sin. It was given so we could be freed from evil and **serve rightly**.

## Point 3: The Spirit-Filled Life

> But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness and self-control. Against such things there is no law. Those who belong to Christ Jesus have crucified the sinful nature with its passions and desires. Since we live by the Spirit, let us keep in step with the Spirit. Let us not become conceited, provoking and envying each other. (Galatians 5:22-26)

### What Spirit-Filled Living Looks Like

Notice Paul speaks of the fruit of the Spirit (singular, not plural). It's one fruit with many expressions, all rooted in love:
- Love, joy, peace
- Patience, kindness, goodness
- Faithfulness, gentleness, self-control

Here's the beautiful irony: **there is no law against these things.** You can't be prosecuted for loving too much, serving too generously, or showing too much kindness.

We've died to the old law that restricted our capacity to love, because Christ has been crucified—and through him, we've crucified our sinful nature with all its passions and desires.

### The Mark of the Spirit

"They will know you are my disciples by your love" (John 13:35).

How can you tell if someone has God's Spirit? Look for service. Look for love lived out. Look for kindness and self-control. A Spirit-filled person isn't marked by ecstatic experiences or special knowledge—they're marked by their willingness to serve one another in love.

---

## Conclusion

Spirit-filled Christianity is said to mean many things in our day, but here's what it truly means: **serving one another in love is what living by the Spirit is all about.**

We are dead to the law that once stopped us from loving. Christ Jesus has been crucified, and through him, we're free. So let us now live what we've been set free to do: **serve one another in love.**
