# John 8:32-47

**Title:** Truth and Freedom: Light Against Darkness

**Date:** 26 February 2023

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Part One: The Light That Sets Us Free (8:12-32)

### The Context: Opposition and Truth Claims

Jesus stood in Jerusalem during the Feast of Tabernacles, facing intense opposition. The religious authorities challenged his authority and origins, claiming he couldn't be the Messiah because he came from Galilee. Yet Jesus responded with a profound claim about his identity and mission.

> **12 When Jesus spoke again to the people, he said, "I am the light of the world. Whoever follows me will never walk in darkness, but will have the light of life."**

### Why Light? Understanding the Metaphor

Jesus invoked light deliberately for multiple reasons. First, the Feast of Tabernacles itself commemorated Israel's deliverance from Egypt, and the prophets associated this feast with both water and light. Isaiah 9 spoke of a great light coming to Galilee—the very region Jesus inhabited—fulfilling Scripture even in his geographic origin.

Second, light itself serves as the perfect metaphor for truth. Light illuminates, revealing things as they actually are. Unlike darkness that conceals, light shows reality without distortion. This is why Jesus uses light to represent truth: it exposes what is real.

### The Testimony Debate

When the Pharisees challenged Jesus's self-testimony, he responded with remarkable authority:

> **14 Jesus answered, "Even if I testify on my own behalf, my testimony is valid, for I know where I came from and where I am going. But you have no idea where I come from or where I am going."**

Jesus claimed something extraordinary: his testimony stands because he knows his origin—he is from above, from the Father. The Jews couldn't fathom this claim because they had no framework for understanding someone sent from God himself.

> **23-24 But he continued, "You are from below; I am from above. You are of this world; I am not of this world. I told you that you would die in your sins; if you do not believe that I am he, you will indeed die in your sins."**

### The Truth That Sets Free

Despite opposition, those who believed Jesus made him a stunning promise:

> **31-32 To the Jews who had believed him, Jesus said, "If you hold to my teaching, you are really my disciples. Then you will know the truth, and the truth will set you free."**

But what does this freedom mean? Consider economics as an illustration. A free market works because prices communicate truth—the sum total of information from millions of people about what something is truly worth. When governments manipulate prices through artificial controls, they distort this truth. Minimum wage laws restrict hiring freedom. Land-use restrictions prevent development and artificially raise housing costs. Government subsidies for first-time homebuyers only drive prices higher.

Whenever truth is distorted, bad outcomes follow. In the spiritual realm, the same principle applies: truth brings freedom, while deception brings slavery.

## Part Two: Slavery to Sin and the Devil (8:33-47)

### The Blindness of Self-Deception

The Jews indignantly rejected Jesus's offer of freedom:

> **33 They answered him, "We are Abraham's descendants and have never been slaves of anyone. How can you say that we shall be set free?"**

This claim was factually absurd—they were celebrating the Feast of Tabernacles to commemorate their slavery in Egypt. Yet Jesus pointed to a deeper slavery that affects us all:

> **34 Jesus replied, "Very truly I tell you, everyone who sins is a slave to sin."**

We all face a bondage worse than any physical oppression: enslavement to sin itself. And there is a terrible consequence:

> **35 Now a slave has no permanent place in the family, but a son belongs to it forever. So if the Son sets you free, you will be free indeed.**

### The Father of Lies

The Jews claimed descent from Abraham, yet their actions contradicted this lineage. They sought to kill Jesus—the one who told them the truth from God. Their true father was revealed through their behavior:

> **44 You belong to your father, the devil, and you want to carry out your father's desires. He was a murderer from the beginning, not holding to the truth, for there is no truth in him. When he lies, he speaks his native language, for he is a liar and the father of lies.**

This echoes the very beginning of human sin in Genesis 3, where the serpent deceived Eve with three characteristic lies:

**The Serpent's Strategy:**

1. **Exaggerate the truth to make it sound unreasonable.** He asked, "Did God really say you must not eat from *any* tree?" making God's one reasonable restriction sound like total deprivation.

2. **Deny the consequences of truth.** He declared, "You will not certainly die"—a direct contradiction of God's warning.

3. **Invert morality, claiming disobedience is good.** He promised, "You will be like God, knowing good and evil"—as if breaking God's covenant would elevate rather than debase them.

This pattern repeats throughout human history. With sexual morality, the devil employs the same three tactics: exaggerate God's boundaries as oppressive, deny the real consequences of sexual immorality, and claim that sexual "freedom" is actually liberation and self-fulfillment.

The evidence contradicts this lie. Sexual immorality brings measurable harm: damage to the brain's capacity for bonding, destruction of marriages, exploitation of others, addiction, and ultimately spiritual death. God's boundaries around sexuality aren't restrictive—they're protective, designed for our true flourishing.

### Understanding True Freedom

We often accept the Enlightenment lie that we're "born free" and that God makes us slaves. Rousseau declared, "Man is born free, but everywhere he is in chains." But Scripture reveals the opposite: we are born enslaved to sin. Children don't need to be taught to lie—it comes naturally. We inherit not freedom but the inclination toward deception and destruction.

True freedom doesn't come from rejecting all boundaries. Consider a train—its freedom lies in staying on the tracks. A fish's freedom is in water. Freedom always operates within reality. Like Thomas the Tank Engine derailing himself, or a fish gasping on dry land, stepping outside God's truth is not liberation but self-destruction.

## Part Three: Seeing in the Light (9:1-41)

### The Sign: Blind Eyes Opened

Jesus's teaching about being the light of the world is immediately followed by a sign that demonstrates his power:

> **9:5 While I am in the world, I am the light of the world.**

He encounters a man born blind and heals him—not through prayer or words alone, but through active intervention, sending him to wash in the Pool of Siloam (meaning "Sent"). The blind man obeys and receives his sight.

### Two Types of Sight

The account presents a striking contrast. The religiously educated Pharisees claim to see and know the truth, yet they remain spiritually blind, unable to recognize God's work. The formerly blind man, stripped of all pretense and authority, sees clearly:

> **25 He replied, "Whether he is a sinner or not, I don't know. One thing I do know. I was blind but now I see!"**

When pressed by the authorities, this simple man displays remarkable theological insight:

> **30-33 The man answered, "Now that is remarkable! You don't know where he comes from, yet he opened my eyes. We know that God does not listen to sinners. He listens to the godly person who does his will. Nobody has ever heard of opening the eyes of a man born blind. If this man were not from God, he could do nothing."**

### The Cost of Truth

For his testimony, the man suffers rejection:

> **34 To this they replied, "You were steeped in sin at birth; how dare you lecture us!" And they threw him out.**

Yet Jesus finds him and completes his healing with full revelation:

> **35-38 Jesus heard that they had thrown him out, and when he found him, he said, "Do you believe in the Son of Man?" The man asked, "Who is he, sir? Tell me so that I may believe in him." Jesus said, "You have now seen him; in fact, he is the one speaking with you." Then the man said, "Lord, I believe," and he worshiped him.**

The man who physically saw darkness now sees the true light—Jesus himself, the light of the world.

## The Choice Before Us

> **32 Then you will know the truth, and the truth will set you free.**

This isn't mere intellectual knowledge. Following Jesus means embracing reality as God defines it, not as we wish it to be. It means acknowledging our slavery to sin and accepting the freedom that comes through his atoning sacrifice on Easter.

The choice is stark: take the blue pill and remain captured by the devil's lies, or take the red pill and see reality clearly. Jesus calls us to see the truth—about ourselves, about sin, about God's character, about his love demonstrated in the cross.

**If you follow Christ, keep your eyes open to truth.** If you don't yet follow him, will you consider opening your eyes to see the light of the world?
