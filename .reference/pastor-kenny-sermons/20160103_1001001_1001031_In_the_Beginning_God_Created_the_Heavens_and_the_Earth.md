## A Classic Opening

Genesis 1:1 is the most classic opening in all of Scripture. A classic is an outstanding example of enduring worth that has proven itself over time and become the standard by which others are measured. Just as classical music represents the pinnacle of the musical tradition, Genesis 1:1 stands at the beginning of the Bible—the world's best-selling and most widely distributed book, with over 5 billion copies printed to date.

> In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth.

This verse has shaped not just individual believers but entire civilizations. The Bible Society estimates that 160 million people accessed Bible search websites in 2015 alone, with over 1.6 billion page views recorded. God has given us this foundation for hope, progress, rational thought, science, and human dignity.

## Three Reasons This Verse Changed Everything

### 1. IN THE BEGINNING: A Linear Concept of Time

**The Problem with Cyclical Thinking**

Our natural intuition often tells us that time is endless and cyclical. The ancient Greeks—Aristotle and Plato—believed time either always existed infinitely or was merely a "moving image of eternity," fundamentally timeless. Eastern philosophies like Hinduism and Buddhism taught that life consists of infinite cycles of death and rebirth, where your next life depends on your karma in this one. Even the Mayan calendar moved in endless cycles, each completing and beginning again.

This cyclical worldview has devastating implications. If life is an endless cycle with no true beginning or progress, then life is fundamentally meaningless—as the Preacher says in Ecclesiastes:

> Meaningless! Meaningless! Everything is meaningless. What do people gain from all their labors? Generations come and generations go, but the earth remains forever. The sun rises and sets, and hurries back to where it rises. All streams flow into the sea, yet the sea is never full. There is nothing new under the sun.

When life is perceived as an endless recycling of the same events, there is no hope for genuine improvement or progress. This belief system profoundly shapes a culture's ability to advance. India, despite being one of the world's oldest civilizations, has struggled to develop economically. While many factors contribute to this, one significant reason is the cyclical worldview embedded in Hinduism. If karma has already determined your station in life, why work to change it? Why help others improve when the cosmos has already spoken their fate? There is no incentive for progress when everything will eventually return to where it was.

The despair this creates is so profound that the greatest hope Eastern philosophy offers is Nirvana—literally "to be blown out or extinguished like a candle." To cease existing as a person. To escape the futility of cyclical life is to stop existing altogether.

**God Said: In the Beginning**

But into this ancient world, the Jewish and Christian worldview introduced something radically different: time is not circular but linear. A line has a beginning. From that beginning point, time moves forward in one direction—never cycling back, always progressing forward. This single statement reframed how humanity understands itself and its future.

The ancient Jews counted years not by cycles but by a linear progression from creation itself. Later, when the world became Christian, we began counting again from the pivotal moment when God entered history as a baby—Jesus Christ. We now mark time as Anno Domini, "the Year of the Lord." This year number never recycles; it continually grows larger because time is linear and moving forward.

> In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God. He was with God in the beginning... The Word became flesh and made his dwelling among us. We have seen his glory, the glory of the one and only Son, who came from the Father, full of grace and truth.

This birth was a once-in-eternity event, never before and never to be repeated again. It marks the climax of creation itself.

**Why This Matters to Your Life**

This seemingly abstract concept has concrete, life-changing implications. A linear view of time makes progress possible. It allows us to believe that life can genuinely improve from one year to the next—that the world is not merely an illusion cycling endlessly but is actually moving toward something better.

Christians believe poverty can be alleviated, life expectancy can improve, and illness can be healed. If this life fails us, there is a heaven to look forward to. We make New Year's resolutions because we believe that change is possible.

Consider the United States. While it's fashionable to criticize America today, it's worth remembering that this nation—settled just 300 years ago and begun by colonists who starved in their first winter—has produced the technological innovations, medicines, and patents that benefit the entire world. India had a 4,000-year head start, yet the Christian hope embedded in American culture created unprecedented progress and innovation.

Americans are characterized by an almost unrealistic optimism—the belief that you can accomplish anything with effort. This "can-do" mentality, which others find almost naive, springs from a Christian worldview of linear time and genuine progress. It's a mark of unrestrained hope in the future.

### 2. GOD CREATED: An Ordered Universe

> God created the heavens and the earth.

The second great truth from Genesis 1 is that God made an ordered and perfect world.

**The Structure of Creation**

As we read through Genesis 1, we see a remarkable pattern of order:
- **Days 1-3:** God creates the environment (day/night, sky/waters, land/vegetation)
- **Days 4-6:** God fills these environments (sun/moon/stars, sea creatures/birds, land animals/mankind)

The very structure of the account shows us that God is an orderly Creator. He doesn't simply speak things into being haphazardly; He creates with intention and system.

Notice the literary clues that underscore this perfection:
- The phrase "And God said" is repeated in the same rhythmic pattern throughout
- God declares His creation "good" multiple times (showing satisfaction with order and design)
- In Hebrew, the first verse contains exactly seven words; God is mentioned 35 times (7 × 5); there are seven days—the number of completeness in Jewish tradition

**Why This Contradicts Ancient Pagan Worldviews**

This was revolutionary knowledge for the ancient Israelites. The other religions of the ancient Near East taught that the world was chaotic—that it had been created from the leftovers of a cosmic war, a jumbled mess of divine conflict. But Genesis declares something utterly different: the world is not random, not the product of warring gods, not a collection of unpredictable forces. It is the work of one intelligent, orderly Mind.

**The Foundation for Science**

One of the most significant implications is this: science is possible because creation is ordered.

We often hear science presented as the antithesis of faith in God, but this is backwards. Science actually presupposes belief in God. To even begin a scientific investigation, you must assume that creation is ordered—that there are laws of nature rather than random chaos. You must believe that the world makes sense, that consistent reasons explain why things happen, and that your formulas and measurements apply the same way in Australia as in Hong Kong as on the moon.

This assumption is not arbitrary. It only makes sense if there is an orderly Creator. Without God, why should we expect the world to make sense at all? If the universe is merely the random collision of unordered matter, then there are no "laws"—only chaos. Reading the periodic table would be no different than reading a jumble of random letters. But if there is meaning, there must be an author.

Even the atheist philosopher Thomas Nagel acknowledges this: "Evolutionary naturalism implies that we shouldn't take any of our convictions seriously, including the scientific world picture on which evolutionary naturalism itself depends."

Furthermore, if there is no rational Mind behind creation, what makes us think we ourselves are rational? If my mind is composed of random, unordered chemicals, how can I trust my mind to discern truth? The scientist's ability to discover truth rests on the fact that we are created rational by a rational God, with faculties designed to understand creation. When we discover a law of nature, we don't say we invented it—we say we discovered it, because it was already created by God.

**The Victory Over Superstition**

This ordered worldview liberated humanity from superstition. No more sacrificing children into volcanoes to appease harvest gods. No more burning living widows with their dead husbands to placate the gods. An ordered creation revealed by reason freed us from these atrocities.

**The Historical Record**

It should not surprise us that modern science emerged from Christian civilization. The founders of modern science were Christians:
- **Francis Bacon** (founder of the modern scientific method): "Knowledge is the rich storehouse for the glory of the Creator and the relief of man's estate"
- **Isaac Newton** (who wrote more on theology than science): "It is inconceivable that accidents alone could be the controller of this universe... the most beautiful system of the sun, planets and comets could only proceed from the counsel and dominion of an intelligent and powerful Being"
- **Galileo Galilei, Johann Kepler, Michael Faraday, Lord Kelvin, James Clerk Maxwell**—all devout Christians
- Even **Charles Darwin**, a self-confessed agnostic, said: "In my most extreme fluctuations I have never been an atheist in the sense of denying the existence of a God"
- **Francis Collins**, director of the Human Genome Project, is a devout Christian

**The Nobel Prize Record**

The data is striking: In the first 100 years of Nobel Prizes (1901-2000), Christians won 65.4% of all Nobel Prize awards. Christians have won 78.3% of all Peace prizes, 72.5% in Chemistry, 65.3% in Physics, 62% in Medicine, 54% in Economics, and 49.5% of Literature awards—all while comprising only 33.2% of the world's population.

The vast majority of the remaining Nobel laureates have been Jewish (about 20% of prizes despite Jews comprising less than 0.2% of the world's population). Atheists and agnostics account for roughly 5% of Nobel Prize winners.

The pattern is unmistakable: belief in an ordered Creation by an intelligent Creator correlates strongly with scientific discovery and advancement.

### 3. FOR MANKIND: Human Dignity and Purpose

> So God created mankind in his own image, in the image of God he created them; male and female he created them. God blessed them and said to them, "Be fruitful and increase in number; fill the earth and subdue it. Rule over the fish of the sea and the birds of the sky and over every living creature that moves on the ground."

**Mankind as the Climax of Creation**

Day Six—when mankind is created—is the climax of the entire creation account. Several details underscore this:
- The description of this day is longer and more detailed than the previous days
- God doesn't simply say "Let there be man" but speaks more personally: "Let us make man in our image"
- God's evaluation changes from "good" to "very good"

**Made in God's Image**

Mankind alone is created in God's image. We are divine representatives, created to exercise God's careful rule over creation and to carry out His purposes. This gives us tremendous purpose, position, respect, and honor. God entrusts humanity with a task: to properly rule the world under Him.

**God's Love for Mankind**

Contrast this with pagan religions, which teach that mankind exists to serve the gods—to be a slave to their needs. But Genesis reveals something radically different: God serves mankind. He provides food; He sustains us; He loves us.

> Then God said, "I give you every seed-bearing plant on the face of the whole earth and every tree that has fruit with seed in it. They will be yours for food."

**The Foundation of Human Rights**

This is the only possible foundation for human rights and human dignity. If humanity is merely another animal, then why do we treat humans differently? In nature, animals kill each other, commit sexual violence, eat their offspring, and leave the weak to die. "Survival of the fittest" is the law of the wild. And indeed, ancient societies and even modern ones have often operated by this principle—sacrificing humans, enslaving humans, murdering humans without moral constraint.

But Genesis 1 declares something that changes everything: mankind is created in the image of God. Therefore:
- Slavery is morally wrong
- Murder is morally wrong
- The weak deserve protection even at our expense
- Darwin's "survival of the fittest" does not apply to humans

This single verse is the moral foundation for all human rights. Without it, there is no philosophical basis to say that all humans have equal value, dignity, and worth. The image of God is the only ground for universal human rights.

## God's Promise: Jesus and the New Creation

God has created a world that can be genuinely improved. It is not now perfect, and it can never be perfected in this age. But God loves us.

Despite our repeated failure to remember that we bear His image, despite our forgetfulness of Him as Creator, despite our failure to acknowledge the time we live in, despite our tendency to take God and His blessings for granted—God loves us. He proved this in another once-in-eternity, never-to-be-repeated event.

Jesus Christ died for the sins of the world, taking upon Himself the judgment of God on behalf of all humanity. He is recreating us to be in His image again—if we will but listen to Him.

> He loves man so much that he died on the cross to pay for our penalty. He promises to one day return to judge, and after that He will bring a new heavens and a new earth.

In that new creation, there will be no evil, no decay, no wars, no disease. This is the Christian future. We don't have to guess what God's purpose for us is—the new creation itself is God's purpose. Meanwhile, we are called to wait and invest our lives toward things of eternal value.
