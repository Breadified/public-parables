# 1 John 4:7-11

**Title:** Love: God's Definition
**Date:** 27 May 2017
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## What Is Love?

Today is a wonderful celebration—the culmination of months of planning, the melding of two families, the marriage of Andrew and Pamela. At its heart, this is a celebration of love. But what kind of love are we celebrating? The world offers us many definitions.

**The evolutionary definition** suggests that love is rooted in physical attraction. Science tells us that for men, a woman's waist-to-hip ratio signals her ability to bear children. For women, a man's broad chest and shoulders suggest strength and provision—the ability to provide and protect.

**The chemical definition** is equally fascinating. Research shows that testosterone and estrogen "get you out looking for anything." Then, when you find "the one," a cascade of neurochemicals floods your system: dopamine makes you feel good, adrenaline sets your heart racing and makes you sweat, and serotonin temporarily makes you feel like you're losing your mind. Finally, attachment hormones—oxytocin and vasopressin—bond you to your beloved. This is love as biology.

But there is a deeper definition of love—**God's theological definition**.

## 1. God Is Love

God has authority on this matter because He is the source and very substance of love itself. Look at what John writes:

> Dear friends, let us love one another, for love comes from God. (1 John 4:7)

God is not merely the source of love—He is love itself. John continues:

> Whoever does not love does not know God, because God is love. (1 John 4:8)

This is not a mask that God wears and removes when no one is looking. Love is not a fickle attribute that comes and goes with His mood. No—God **is** love. His very nature is love. Every fiber of His being is love. That is His desire, His motivation, His identity.

Think of it this way: if you took a chisel and chipped off a piece of God, it would be love. If you operated on Him and examined His deepest parts, you would find love. If God sweated, He would sweat love. If you cut Him, He would bleed love.

When God came to earth, we see Jesus—the perfect embodiment of who God is.

## 2. Jesus Is Love

> This is how God showed his love among us: He sent his one and only Son into the world that we might live through him. (1 John 4:9)

Two thousand years ago, God sent His one and only Son to a small patch of earth in the Middle East. In Jesus, we see God's love made visible.

> This is love: not that we loved God, but that he loved us and sent his Son as an atoning sacrifice for our sins. (1 John 4:10)

Jesus was crucified—hung on a cross for crimes He did not commit. He sacrificially died on behalf of our sins. God did bleed. God was cut. All for our sake.

By contrast, humanity is not love. While we are capable of it, love is not intrinsic to us. Dig beneath the surface, behind the masks we wear, and what do we find? Selfishness, hatred, apathy—toward each other and toward God. When pressure comes, we look out for ourselves.

But Jesus showed us a different kind of love—an other-centered love. He revealed to us:

- God's love
- Sacrificial love
- Patient and kind love
- The love of a God slow to anger and abounding in mercy

Those who have experienced this generosity of God in Jesus understand what it truly costs to love another human being.

## 3. Love One Another

> Dear friends, since God so loved us, we also ought to love one another. (1 John 4:11)

God's love flows to those who know His love, and that love is meant to overflow into our relationships with others. When one person's love becomes infectious, it spreads. Just as love has the power to transform hearts, so too does it have the power to transform marriages.

For Andrew and Pamela, I pray that it is this theological definition of love—not the chemical rush that fades, not the evolutionary impulse that shifts—but the love of Jesus that will sustain their marriage.

May you both reflect Jesus' sacrificial, other-centered love to each other. Your vows declared "I will"—not merely "I do" for today, but a commitment extending into tomorrow.

This is a love that is unconditional. It is a love that persists even when the hormones no longer work, when physical attraction fades, when your spouse wrongs you, when he's difficult and she's demanding, when she's messy and he's withdrawn.

This is the love that God first showed us in Jesus—a love that remains faithful through every season.

Dear friends, since God so loved us, we also ought to love one another.
