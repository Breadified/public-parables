# Hebrews 1:1-3

**Title:** Find Out About God Through Jesus
**Date:** 31 January 2014
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction

For thousands of years, people have searched for ways to know God—through the stars, horoscope and zodiac signs, tea leaves, and fortune tellers. This wasn't unique to our modern age. Even in ancient Israel's history, people looked everywhere for divine knowledge.

But Scripture shows us something far better.

## The Past: Fragmented Revelation

> In the past God spoke to our forefathers through the prophets at many times and in various ways. (Hebrews 1:1)

Throughout Israel's history, God revealed Himself through diverse means. Moses encountered Him in a burning bush. Joseph and Pharaoh received divine messages through dreams. Ezekiel experienced powerful visions. Daniel witnessed writing on the wall. Even Balaam's donkey became an instrument of God's word. The prophets heard voices from above and received visions through which God made His will known.

These were all legitimate ways for an eternal God to communicate with finite human beings—how else could the divine reveal Himself except through our senses: sight and sound?

Yet something greater was coming.

## The Present: The Son

> But in these last days he has spoken to us by his Son, whom he appointed heir of all things, and through whom he made the universe. (Hebrews 1:2)

The shift from "the past" to "these last days" is dramatic. God's final and supreme revelation has arrived: His Son, Jesus Christ. Why is this better? Two reasons.

### a. Authority

Jesus is "appointed heir of all things"—a position of ultimate authority and significance. Think of it this way: when your friend tells you to clean your room, it's one thing. When your teacher announces a test is coming, you listen more carefully. But when the principal sits you down personally, you sit up and pay attention. The source of the message carries weight.

Jesus carries the highest authority—He is the heir appointed by God Himself. He is the co-creator through whom the universe was made.

**Application:** We must listen to Jesus. We find His words in the reliable biblical records of His life. Pay attention to His warnings, His comfort, His guidance.

But authority alone isn't enough. Does He actually know what He's doing?

### b. Ability

> The Son is the radiance of God's glory and the exact representation of his being. (Hebrews 1:3)

Jesus is not merely a messenger about God—He *is* God. The words and deeds of Jesus are the words and deeds of God Himself. God came down to earth as Jesus Christ, God in human form.

How can we be certain the message is authentic? How do we know God's message hasn't been distorted? Simple: God Himself came to deliver it personally.

Serious matters demand face-to-face attention. When you're buying something precious—a computer with specific requirements, or a formal dress that must fit perfectly—you go in person because you know exactly what you need. The HSC exam, that crucial final test: you show up yourself because you understand precisely how to answer. You would never send a substitute.

Jesus knows God exactly because He *is* God. He came as God's exact representation.

**Application:** We sometimes say, "If God were real, let Him appear to me." The Bible's answer is direct: He did. Two thousand years ago, in a small corner of the Middle East, God appeared in the flesh. Had you been there, you would have seen Him. Heard Him speak Aramaic with His own accent. Touched His skin. Smelled the dust of the Palestinian climate on Him. You would have encountered God Himself.

## The Purpose: Redemption

> After he had provided purification for sins he sat down at the right hand of the Majesty in heaven. (Hebrews 1:3)

Jesus came to earth for one reason: to rescue sinners—you and me—from God's judgment. We are all guilty of wrongdoing against God, against each other, against those we love. That wrongdoing carries a death sentence, the strictest penalty.

But God loves us. And so Jesus provides the purification for sins. He took upon Himself the death we deserve and died on the cross in our place.

## Conclusion: Trust in Jesus

How do we find out about God?

Not through tea leaves. Not through horoscopes. Not through trusting in horses and chariots.

> Some trust in chariots and some in horses, but we trust in the name of the Lord our God. (Psalm 20:7)

Instead, listen to Jesus. Find Him in the pages of Scripture. That's where we discover what God is truly like, what He has done, what He will do, and what He calls us to become.
