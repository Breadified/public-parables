## A Trap: The Question About Taxes

Jesus faced a carefully constructed political trap. His opponents sent spies pretending to be sincere, hoping to catch him in something he said so they could hand him over to the governor's authority.

> They were watching him closely and sent spies who pretended to be sincere. They hoped to catch Jesus in something he said, so that they might hand him over to the power and authority of the governor. So the spies questioned him: "Teacher, we know that you speak and teach what is right, and that you do not show partiality but teach the way of God in accordance with the truth. Is it right for us to pay taxes to Caesar or not?" (Luke 20:20-22)

The trap was brilliant: an either/or question with no winning answer. If Jesus said no to paying taxes, he would appear disloyal to Caesar and could be arrested for sedition. If he said yes, he would appear to be an apologist for Roman rule—compromising God's kingdom by endorsing Caesar's authority. It was a question about two loyalties with seemingly incompatible answers.

But Jesus, as he often did, transcended the false choice with a remarkable response:

> He saw through their duplicity and said to them, "Show me a denarius. Whose image and inscription are on it?" "Caesar's," they replied. He said to them, "Then give back to Caesar what is Caesar's, and to God what is God's." They were unable to trap him in what he had said there in public. And astonished by his answer, they became silent. (Luke 20:23-26)

## Give to Caesar What Is Caesar's

Jesus's teaching established a biblical framework for understanding government's legitimate role in society.

### Government Has Authority from God

The coin bore Caesar's image and inscription. By that logic, if something bears Caesar's mark, it belongs to Caesar's jurisdiction. Jesus affirmed what Paul would later teach: governments exist because God has established them.

> Let everyone be subject to the governing authorities, for there is no authority except that which God has established. The authorities that exist have been established by God. Consequently, whoever rebels against the authority is rebelling against what God has instituted, and those who do so will bring judgment on themselves. (Romans 13:1-2)

### Government's Purpose: Restraining Evil

Government is not equipped to save souls or transform hearts, but it serves a vital function: restraining the worst expressions of human evil through the power of the sword.

> For rulers hold no terror for those who do right, but for those who do wrong. Do you want to be free from fear of the one in authority? Then do what is right and you will be commended. For the one in authority is God's servant for your good. But if you do wrong, be afraid, for rulers do not bear the sword for no reason. They are God's servants, agents of wrath to bring punishment on the wrongdoer. (Romans 13:3-4)

The "sword" in Scripture consistently refers to the power to inflict capital punishment—the right to use force, including deadly force, to maintain law and order. This is government's unique authority among human institutions. They maintain courts, prisons, police, armies, and border security. They punish murder and rape, restrict dangerous substances, and protect citizens from criminal activity.

### Why We Pay Taxes

Our taxes fund this legitimate governmental work. When we pay taxes, we compensate government officials for the service they provide to society.

> Therefore, it is necessary to submit to the authorities, not only because of possible punishment but also as a matter of conscience. This is also why you pay taxes, for the authorities are God's servants, who give their full time to governing. Give to everyone what you owe them: If you owe taxes, pay taxes; if revenue, then revenue; if respect, then respect; if honor, then honor. (Romans 13:5-7)

Even imperfect governments serve this restraining function. A corrupt government will still keep law and order to some degree. When government disappears—as when military intervention removes dictators without establishing alternative authority—what results is chaos. Government, for all its flaws, does restrain the worst evils.

### The Christian's Responsibility

We should respect government authorities, pay our lawful taxes, and give them the honor due to their office. We have a duty to be informed citizens and, where possible, to participate in the political process with wisdom and integrity. Some of you are called to sacrifice and enter politics yourselves. That is not too late for many of you. Armed with God's wisdom and under his sovereignty, you can bring sanity to our world and preserve freedom for the rest of us.

## Give to God What Is God's

But Jesus's statement contained a radical second half: "give to God what is God's." This means not everything belongs to Caesar.

### What Bears God's Image?

Using Jesus's own logic about the coin—that what bears Caesar's image belongs to Caesar—we must ask: what bears God's image?

> Then God said, "Let us make mankind in our image, in our likeness, so that they may rule over the fish in the sea and the birds in the sky, over the livestock and all the wild animals, and over all the creatures that move along the ground." (Genesis 1:26)

Every human being bears God's image. Moreover, God writes his laws on our hearts:

> This is the covenant I will make with them after that time, says the Lord. I will put my laws in their hearts, and I will write them on their minds. (Hebrews 8:10)

Even those outside the faith carry God's inscription within them:

> When Gentiles, who do not have the law, do by nature things required by the law, they are a law for themselves, even though they do not have the law. They show that the requirements of the law are written on their hearts, their consciences also bearing witness. (Romans 2:14-15)

Every person has God's barcode written on them: "Belongs to God." Therefore, all of us—all of humanity—belong ultimately to God, not to Caesar.

### Jesus Is Lord

The teachers of the law challenged Jesus's authority. In response, Jesus posed a question from Psalm 110:

> David himself declares in the Book of Psalms: "The Lord said to my Lord: Sit at my right hand until I make your enemies a footstool for your feet." David calls him 'Lord.' How then can he be his son? (Luke 20:41-44)

This was revolutionary. The Jewish people revered King David—a great military and political leader who expanded Israel's territory. They longed for another "son of David" who would drive out the Romans. Yet David himself called the Messiah "Lord," acknowledging a superior authority. Even this greatest of earthly kings was subject to the Messiah.

Jesus is Lord—not just of the spiritual realm, but of all reality. He existed before time, created all things, sustains all things, redeems his people, and will judge all people. He holds authority over every sphere of life.

### Two Critical Implications

**First implication:** We owe God our complete allegiance. Every breath, every ounce of energy, all our loyalty and faithfulness belongs to him.

> You are worthy, our Lord and God, to receive glory and honor and power, for you created all things, and by your will they were created and have their being. (Revelation 4:11)

To be his disciple means absolute surrender:

> Whoever wants to be my disciple must deny themselves and take up their cross daily and follow me. (Luke 9:23)

**Second implication:** By contrast, human government is limited and temporary. While government deserves respect and obedience, it does not deserve ultimate loyalty. Caesar is mortal; he owes God an account just as everyone does.

> Let everyone be subject to the governing authorities, for there is no authority except that which God has established. (Romans 13:1)

### The Idolatry of the State

Here is where many of us go astray. We have not truly grasped that government is limited. We worship Caesar.

When problems arise, where do we turn? We instinctively appeal to government. Housing affordability? The government can fix it—just adjust this tax, regulate that. Racism? The government can solve it—more legislation, more intervention. Poverty? The government. Unemployment? The government. Health? The government. Even global warming—we trust the government to control the temperature of the earth itself.

We trust government to decide what we earn, what we can say, how our children are educated, what we can do with our land. We give them 30-50% of our earnings without complaint. We entrust our children to state schools. We give Monday through Saturday to government and Sunday to Jesus. When things go wrong, we blame government more than we blame God.

Consider where our actual loyalties lie: We give the state control over economics, finance, philosophy, education, freedom of speech, wage levels, interest rates, school curriculum, and what our children learn. Yet we give God our church attendance and prayer time—perhaps 1% of our financial resources.

This is idolatry of the state. This is atheistic thinking. When too many people think this way, we get dangerous ideologies. Hegel wrote, "The State is the Divine Idea as it exists on earth... the State is the march of God through the world." This philosophy influenced Karl Marx and led to communism and socialism. Mussolini's motto captured it perfectly: "Everything within the State, nothing outside the State, nothing against the State." Hitler declared, "The party is all-embracing. It rules our lives in all their breadth and depth. There will be no license, no free space, in which the individual belongs to himself."

This is what happens when we forget that government is limited. This is what happens when we elevate Caesar above God.

### Government Cannot Do What God Does

The hard truth is that government is powerless in the things that matter most:

- Government cannot create. It can only redistribute.
- Government cannot provide true morality or transform hearts.
- Government cannot rehabilitate sin or offer forgiveness.
- Government cannot care or love.

By contrast, through his Son Jesus, God creates. God guides. God teaches. God forgives. God transforms. God cares. God loves.

Government also cannot solve fundamental human problems. It has no wisdom superior to the collective wisdom of millions of citizens making daily choices. Government officials are ordinary people—no more intelligent than the general population, and often less so when disconnected from God's wisdom. They cannot balance budgets like ordinary households do. They have only one concern: getting elected again.

Even in bad governments, the solution is not anarchy. But neither should we expand government's role infinitely. As the writer of 1 Samuel warned us long ago:

> This is what the king who will reign over you will claim as his rights: He will take your sons and make them serve with his chariots and horses... He will take your daughters to be perfumers and cooks and bakers. He will take the best of your fields and vineyards and olive groves and give them to his attendants. He will take a tenth of your grain and of your vintage and give it to his officials and attendants... You yourselves will become his slaves. (1 Samuel 8:11-17)

Do not put your trust in princes:

> Do not put your trust in princes, in human beings, who cannot save. When their spirit departs, they return to the ground; on that very day their plans come to nothing. (Psalm 146:3-4)

## Understanding Church and State: Different Spheres, One Kingdom

We must clarify what "separation of church and state" actually means. It does not mean that religion and government operate in completely separate universes with entirely different moral frameworks. All laws are inherently moral statements—they reflect a view of what is right and wrong, good and bad.

A housing policy reflects a moral judgment about homelessness. Laws about Sunday work reflect a moral view about family time and rest. There is no truly "neutral" law. Every law reflects someone's morality.

The false dichotomy—that we should "elect Christians to government but keep religion out of politics"—does not work. One kingdom, one Lord Jesus, one standard of morality across all of life.

### Multiple Authorities, Same Source

Scripture reveals that God ordained different authorities for different purposes, each with legitimate power in its sphere:

**The Family:** Parents have authority over children. The family creates, nurtures, feeds, and teaches the next generation—something government cannot do.

**The Church:** Church leadership has authority over the spiritual growth and discipline of believers. The church dispenses the gospel and transforms hearts.

**Government:** Civil authorities maintain law and order, punish wrongdoing, and protect citizens—using the sword when necessary.

**Business:** Employers have authority over employees in the work relationship.

Each institution has its own tools, methods, and goals. Government cannot procreate, feed, nurture, or teach as families do. Church leaders cannot punish criminals or wage war. Families cannot establish courts or maintain armies. Each has its proper sphere.

As the Dutch theologian Abraham Kuyper wrote:

> The various entities... which God called into being by his creative powers and to which he apportioned power, are almost all, in whole or in part, of a moral nature. There is a distinctive life of science; a distinctive life of art; a distinctive life of the church; a distinctive life of the family... Next to and alongside all these entities stands the institution of the state. Not above them, but alongside them. For each of these organizations possesses "sphere-sovereignty," that is to say, derives the power at its disposal not as a grant from the state but as a direct gift from God.

Fathers have authority from God, not as a grant from government. The state's role, at most, is to codify these God-given rights and protect them when violated.

### The Danger of Unlimited Government

You do not want government to have unlimited power because government officials have guns. They are the only institution with the legitimate power to use deadly force. Concentrating too much power in such hands invites tyranny. History shows us repeatedly what happens when the state becomes a steamroller across everyday life, crushing every other institution beneath it.

This is why civilized societies limit government to specific functions. When government overreaches into spheres where it has no authority—controlling speech, dictating family decisions, determining education entirely, managing every aspect of the economy—it becomes tyrannical.

### When Institutions Conflict

In our world, we have multiple overlapping authorities: multiple layers of civil government (local, state, federal), household governments, and church governments. All should aim at the same goal under the same Lord and the same Scripture.

When an institution oversteps its boundaries, Christians may be called to civil disobedience. Scripture is clear:

> Peter and the other apostles replied: "We must obey God rather than human beings!" (Acts 5:29)

But this requires caution and humility. The principle is: when authorities command what God forbids or forbid what God commands, we obey God. However, we should also be willing to face the consequences of our disobedience, as the early disciples did.

## The Challenge Before Us

We live in a Christian nation—Australia was founded on Christian principles. Our constitution acknowledges God. Parliament begins each day with the Lord's Prayer. This inheritance is precious: it contains built into its DNA the understanding that government cannot take everything, that it offers protection from tyranny, and that citizens have freedom.

Yet this heritage is fragile. Because Christians became distracted and some adopted bad theology that separated the sacred from the secular, we neglected politics. Now we see the damage. The system that took 200 years to build is showing signs of strain.

Nothing is inevitable. We need to be better informed. Some of you are called to sacrifice and enter government. It is not too late for many of you. Armed with God's wisdom and under his sovereignty, you can bring sanity to our world and preserve freedom for the rest of us.

The fundamental question remains: Who will you trust? Who is your God?

> Government or God?

Choose wisely.
