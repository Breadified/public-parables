# John 15:1-17

**Title:** I Am the Vine
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Garden Metaphor

Jesus uses the image of a vineyard to teach us about our relationship with him and the Father. There are three key characters in this garden: the Gardener, the Vine, and the Branches.

## The Gardener: God the Father (1-2)

> I am the true vine, and my Father is the gardener. He cuts off every branch in me that bears no fruit, while every branch that does bear fruit he prunes so that it will be even more fruitful.

God the Father holds complete authority over the Vine and all its branches. His pruning serves two essential purposes:

1. **To remove what is unproductive** — branches that bear no fruit are cut off entirely
2. **To increase fruitfulness** — productive branches are pruned to bear even more fruit

Pruning may sound harsh, but it's the mark of a skilled gardener. Just as cutting grass frequently causes it to grow stronger and fuller, the Father's pruning in our lives produces greater spiritual fruit. This answers the deep question of suffering in the Christian life — God's hand of discipline is always aimed at our greater fruitfulness.

## The Vine: Jesus Christ (3-4)

Jesus declares, "I am the true vine." The word *true* means genuine, dependable, and authentic. Throughout John's Gospel, Jesus uses this "I am" language to reveal his identity:

- I am the bread of life
- I am the light of the world
- I am the gate
- I am the way, the truth, and the life
- I am the good shepherd
- I am the resurrection and the life
- I am the true vine

> You are already clean because of the word I have spoken to you.

The disciples are cleansed not through external ritual, but through faith in Jesus's words. His words carry divine authority—when he speaks, things change. We too are cleaned by believing and receiving his words. The disciples once said to Jesus, "Where else would we go? You have the words of eternal life." It is through faith in Jesus and his teachings that we are made clean.

## The Branches: Believers (4-8)

The cleansed ones—those who have put their faith in Christ—have a clear calling: **remain** and **bear fruit**.

> Remain in me, and I will remain in you. No branch can bear fruit by itself; it must remain in the vine. Neither can you bear fruit unless you remain in me.

> I am the vine; you are the branches. If a man remains in me and I in him, he will bear much fruit; apart from me you can do nothing. If anyone does not remain in me, he is like a branch that is thrown away and withers; such branches are picked up, thrown into the fire and burned.

The word "remain" carries both a passive and active dimension:

- **Passively**, it means staying connected to Christ, trusting him, letting his life flow through you
- **Actively**, it means deliberately choosing to listen to Jesus with your ears, your heart, your hands, and your will

> If you remain in me and my words remain in you, ask whatever you wish, and it will be given you. This is to my Father's glory, that you bear much fruit, showing yourselves to be my disciples.

**Fruit is not optional.** It is the natural overflow of abiding in Christ. And what is this fruit? Love. The evidence that we belong to Jesus is our capacity to love as he has loved us.

## Called to Love (9-17)

> As the Father has loved me, so have I loved you. Now remain in my love. If you obey my commands, you will remain in my love, just as I have obeyed my Father's commands and remain in his love. I have told you this so that my joy may be in you and that your joy may be complete.

> My command is this: Love each other as I have loved you. Greater love has no one than this, that he lay down his life for his friends. You are my friends if you do what I command.

Notice the progression: we are no longer servants kept at a distance from our Master's purposes. Instead, Jesus calls us his friends. A servant doesn't know the master's business, but a friend is brought into intimate knowledge and partnership.

> I no longer call you servants, because a servant does not know his master's business. Instead, I have called you friends, for everything that I learned from my Father I have made known to you. You did not choose me, but I chose you and appointed you to go and bear fruit—fruit that will last. Then the Father will give you whatever you ask in my name. This is my command: Love each other.

## Practical Application

As you prepare for holidays and seasons of life, prepare also to remain in Jesus's words. As you schedule extra study and plan your activities, plan your Bible reading with equal intentionality. As you pursue your hobbies and interests, pursue Christian books and discipleship with the same dedication.

Holidays offer a unique opportunity to bear fruit. When you're not caught up in the busyness of daily schedules, you have time to love your family more deeply, invest in your friendships, and serve others generously. If you've never committed to a Christian community, this is the time to explore it. Reach out to a leader or pastor to learn more about joining a Bible study or fellowship group.

**The call is simple: remain in Christ, and you will bear fruit that glorifies the Father.**
