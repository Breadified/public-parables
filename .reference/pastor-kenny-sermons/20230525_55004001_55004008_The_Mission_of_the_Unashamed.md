# 2 Timothy 4:1-8

**Title:** The Mission of the Unashamed  
**Date:** May 25, 2023 (PAC Hills)  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## The Vision of Steadfast Faith

> My pace is set, my gait is fast, my goal is heaven, my road is narrow, my way is rough, my companions few, my guide is reliable, and my mission is clear. I cannot be bought, compromised, detoured, lured away, turned back, deluded or delayed.

This vision epitomizes what Paul calls us to in 2 Timothy 4. The passage opens with the gravity of God's judgment and Christ's appearing—the ultimate reality that frames everything we do.

## The Charge: Preach the Word (vv. 1-2)

> In the presence of God and of Christ Jesus, who will judge the living and the dead, and in view of his appearing and his kingdom, I give you this charge: Preach the word; be prepared in season and out of season; correct, rebuke and encourage—with great patience and careful instruction.

Paul's charge is straightforward: make the Word of God your reliable guide. This is not optional or seasonal work—we are to share Scripture "in season and out of season," anytime and anywhere, with both patience and careful instruction.

This is the Great Commission lived out. Christians around the world labor to draw others toward heaven through Christ, inviting them to join in this mission of service. It is a privilege to be in a setting where this mission can be pursued openly. Many work in contexts where such witness is restricted or discouraged. Here at this Christian school, you have the freedom to speak truth—use it well.

## The Mission of the Few (vv. 3-5)

> For the time will come when people will not put up with sound doctrine. Instead, to suit their own desires, they will gather around them a great number of teachers to say what their itching ears want to hear. They will turn their ears away from the truth and turn aside to myths. But you, keep your head in all situations, endure hardship, do the work of an evangelist, discharge all the duties of your ministry.

Notice the contrast: the masses (plural) will seek teachers who tell them what they want to hear. But *you*—singular—are called to stand firm even when you stand alone.

This is the reality of following Christ. In the world at large, Christians are a minority. The majority do not hold to biblical truth. Coming to faith events, worshiping, and standing for Christ when no one else does trains you to face that world. It prepares you for a life where you cannot depend on peer validation. 

During your teenage years, peer pressure shapes so much. Your decisions often rest on what others around you do. This is part of growing up—leaving behind the inherited faith of your parents and exploring for yourself. But the goal is not to remain in that phase. The goal is to move toward *owning* your faith—becoming convinced of its truth not because your parents believe it, not because your friends do, but because *you* do.

When you reach that conviction, the crowd no longer determines your path. Your mission becomes clear regardless of who walks beside you.

## The Example of Paul (vv. 6-8)

> For I am already being poured out like a drink offering, and the time for my departure is near. I have fought the good fight, I have finished the race, I have kept the faith. Now there is in store for me the crown of righteousness, which the Lord, the righteous Judge, will award to me on that day—and not only to me, but also to all who have longed for his appearing.

Paul embodies the vision he calls us to. Though he faced abandonment—friends who turned away, even those who caused him harm—he did not waver. He kept fighting the good fight. He kept the faith.

His steadfastness reflects that of the righteous Judge himself. Jesus stood alone on the cross, bearing the sins of the world, even as the Father turned his face away. In his loneliness and obedience, he became our model.

## The Fellowship of the Unashamed

One young man from this school, Chris, exemplified something of this unashamed faith. Due to his learning differences, he lacked the self-consciousness that often holds us back. He would approach a stranger at church without hesitation, extend his hand, and say hello—sometimes too forward, but always genuine. He was not ashamed.

We need that kind of courage: a fellowship of the unashamed. Not recklessness, but freedom from the paralyzing fear of what others think.

When your mission is clear—when your goal is heaven, your guide is God's Word, and your companions are few but faithful—you cannot be bought, compromised, detoured, or delayed. You simply walk the path before you, whether anyone walks it with you or not.
