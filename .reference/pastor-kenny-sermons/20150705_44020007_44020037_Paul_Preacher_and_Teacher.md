## Understanding Paul's Authority

In contemporary debates about sexuality and marriage, some Christians suggest we should reject Paul's teachings because Jesus never explicitly addressed these topics. They argue that Paul's "stricter" teachings aren't authoritative. But this approach misunderstands Paul's place in Scripture and his apostolic authority.

The Book of Acts—written by Luke, not Paul—gives us compelling reasons to trust Paul's words as God's truth. Acts repeatedly demonstrates that Paul functions with the same prophetic authority as the great prophets of Israel.

### Paul Operates with Prophetic Authority

When Eutychus falls asleep during Paul's sermon and falls from a window to his death, Paul's response mirrors the miracles of Elijah (1 Kings 17) and Jesus (Luke 7).

**The parallels are striking:**
- Both involve an upper room
- Both involve a young man apparently dead
- Both involve the prophet/apostle throwing himself on the body
- Both result in resurrection to life

Luke deliberately draws these connections to show that Paul carries the authority of Israel's prophets—and ultimately, Jesus himself. Just as Elijah's raising of the widow's son confirmed he was "a man of God," and Jesus's raising of the widow's son at Nain proved "a great prophet has appeared among us," Paul's miracle demonstrates the same divine authority.

**This means Paul's words carry absolute authority.** He speaks with God's authority to instruct us in God's ways—an authority greater than government, courts, or social opinion.

### Receiving Paul's Authority with Confidence

We cannot pick and choose which parts of Scripture to follow. The Bible is not a buffet where we accept Jesus's words but reject Paul's, or accept the Gospels but dismiss the epistles. If we submit to an authority, we must submit to it as a whole, unconditionally.

This doesn't mean blind obedience. We study, reflect, and consider carefully. But after that process of learning, we follow with confidence in the absolute authority of God's Word—the complete counsel of God recorded by the prophets, in the Gospels, and through Paul's letters.

**Because we follow Paul as Paul follows Christ.**

## Paul's Suffering and the Suffering Servant

Paul's journey to Jerusalem is not a casual visit or business trip. It is a deliberate march toward death—a pattern that mirrors Jesus's own path.

### The Pattern of Self-Sacrifice

Just as Jesus "set his face to Jerusalem" (Luke 9:51) knowing death awaited Him, so Paul resolves "in the spirit" to go to Jerusalem. The parallels continue:
- Jesus sends disciples ahead; Paul sends helpers ahead to Macedonia
- Jesus faces opposition from adversaries; Paul faces opposition from the Ephesians
- Jesus sets His face toward death; Paul sets his face toward the same fate

Paul explicitly acknowledges this: "Compelled by the Spirit, I am going to Jerusalem, not knowing what will happen to me there. I only know that in every city the Holy Spirit warns me that prison and hardships are facing me."

Yet he declares: "I consider my life worth nothing to me; my only aim is to finish the race and complete the task the Lord Jesus has given me—the task of testifying to the good news of God's grace."

### Paul as Suffering Servant

Paul sees himself as continuing the role of the suffering servant. In Romans 15:21, he applies Isaiah 52:15—a passage about the Messiah's sacrifice—to his own ministry:

> "Those who were not told about him will see, and those who have not heard will understand."

The suffering servant's disfigurement and rejection are redemptive, making the gospel known to the nations. Paul embraces this same sacrificial role, becoming a living continuation of Christ's mission.

Church tradition records that Paul was eventually tortured and beheaded by Emperor Nero in Rome in A.D. 67. He was not executed for money or power or to deceive. He died for a truth he believed with absolute certainty. This is evidence of his sincerity and the trustworthiness of his testimony.

## Paul's Complete Proclamation

Paul's authority is sealed not just by miracles but by his faithful, comprehensive proclamation of God's truth.

### His Method

Notice what Paul tells the Ephesian elders about his ministry:

- "I have not hesitated to preach anything that would be helpful to you"
- "I taught you publicly and from house to house"
- "I have declared to both Jews and Greeks that they must turn to God in repentance and have faith in our Lord Jesus"
- "I have not hesitated to proclaim to you the whole will of God"
- "For three years I never stopped warning each of you night and day with tears"

Paul held nothing back. He preached in public gatherings and in homes. He addressed both Jewish and Greek audiences. He proclaimed the complete counsel of God without fear or favoritism.

### His Content

What was this "whole will of God" that Paul proclaimed? It is the message that:
- There is a God
- We have sinned against Him
- Judgment is coming
- We can escape judgment through repentance and faith in Jesus
- Those who have accepted Him must live in godliness while waiting for His return
- We must call others to do the same

This is the comprehensive teaching Paul embodied and transmitted.

### His Legacy and Our Call

Paul takes on Israel's prophetic role—he becomes, in his own person, the one who announces Jesus to the world. This is the role Isaiah 49:6 describes: "I will also give You as a light to the Gentiles, that You should be My salvation to the ends of the earth." Jesus fulfilled this mission; Paul continues it until Jesus returns to heaven.

But the mission doesn't end with Paul. It passes to the next generation—to the Ephesian elders, and through them, to all Christians in every age.

**The stakes are serious.** Church can feel like a comfortable hobby—a good pattern in your week, fellowship with friends, shared meals. But it is far more. It is a 2,000-year mission to proclaim Christ's lordship and gather the elect before His return.

In each generation, the teaching of Jesus through Paul is guarded, taught, and obeyed. What will you do with this inheritance?

## Guarding the Gospel for the Next Generation

As Paul prepares to leave the Ephesian elders, his parting charge is clear:

> Keep watch over yourselves and all the flock of which the Holy Spirit has made you overseers. Be shepherds of the church of God, which he bought with his own blood. Even from your own number men will arise and distort the truth in order to draw away disciples after them.

### The Internal Threat

Paul warns that the greatest danger often comes from within the church. History bears this out. Around A.D. 140, Marcion rejected Paul in favor of Peter, then discarded Matthew, Mark, and John, rewriting Luke to fit his own theology. Modern movements continue this pattern—adding Gnostic texts or removing books from Scripture.

**Removing Paul removes the gospel of grace itself.** Paul proclaims what we cannot earn but can only receive: justification by faith alone, forgiveness available to all through Christ's sacrifice. "While we were sinners, Christ died for us" (Romans 5:8).

### The Call to Vigilance

This task of guarding and transmitting truth applies not only to church leaders but to:
- Husbands and fathers as leaders of their families
- Older women teaching younger women
- All believers passing on what Christ has taught us

You have received the apostolic deposit. The question is: what will you do with it?

**Will you be salt and light to the world?** Will you personally sacrifice for this truth as Paul did?

If you've grown weary or disappointed, it's time to wake from slumber and realize your role: to share with friends the truth that transforms lives. Consider serving in the church. Engage with the full counsel of God through Scripture. When you encounter teachings that seem difficult, seek help—there are no new questions under the sun. Write them down. Bring them to those who can help you work through them.

The love and tears of the Ephesian elders as they said goodbye to Paul—grieving that they would never see him again—testifies to the reality of his ministry. But he left them a mission: guard the gospel and pass it to the next generation.

That mission is ours today.
