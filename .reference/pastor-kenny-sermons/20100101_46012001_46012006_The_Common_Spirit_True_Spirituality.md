# 1 Corinthians 12:1-6

**Title:** The Common Spirit: True Spirituality
**Date:** 10 January 2010
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## What Is True Spirituality?

We all carry different images of what spirituality looks like. Some picture the televangelist with spectacular miraculous gifts. Others think of the meditating monk, the missionary who abandoned everything, or the passionate worshipper with raised hands and tears flowing. Today's evangelical churches often blend these pictures—we want the emotional intensity of charismatic worship combined with evangelical theology. But is this what true spirituality actually is?

The Corinthian church faced this exact question. Corinth was a bustling harbor city on a narrow strip of land connecting northern and southern Greece, with two ports opening toward Asia and Italy. Wealth flowed through constantly, bringing trade, people, and—inevitably—sexual immorality, idolatry, and pagan worship. It was a hedonistic place, and the church there reflected it.

Paul had to confront them directly:

> Brothers, I could not address you as spiritual but as worldly—mere infants in Christ. (1 Corinthians 3:1)

The problems were staggering: sexual immorality, divisions, lawsuits between believers, incest, idolatry, drunkenness at communion, greed so severe that some went hungry. Others reacted by swinging to the opposite extreme—harsh asceticism, claiming the body and food were evil. But neither extreme captured true spirituality.

## Understanding "The Spirituals"

Paul begins chapter 12 with a crucial phrase: "Now about the spirituals, brothers, I do not want you to be ignorant."

The Greek word here is simply the adjective *ta pneumatika*—"the spirituals"—not "spiritual gifts" as many translations suggest. This distinction matters deeply.

### Why Not "Spiritual Gifts"?

The popular interpretation links spirituality directly to special gifts. But several clues suggest Paul means something different:

- The term "spiritual gift" appears only once in Romans 1:11 throughout the New Testament
- In 1 Corinthians itself, chapter 1:7 describes the Corinthians as lacking no gifts, yet makes no mention of the Spirit
- Chapter 2 extensively discusses the work of the Spirit but contains no discussion of gifts
- The Trinity, not just the Holy Spirit, distributes these abilities (12:6, 11)

The phrase "spiritual gifts" creates a false impression—that gifts belong exclusively to the Spirit, making the Spirit like "Santa Claus of the Trinity." But gifts flow from the entire Godhead.

### What Are "The Spirituals"?

Paul is addressing something more specific: **spiritual truths and their communication to one another.**

To understand this, we must first understand what "spiritual" means. The Greek word *pneuma* literally means wind or breath—the breath of God. Look at how Paul uses it in chapter 2, contrasting the spirit of the world with the Spirit of God:

> For I resolved to know nothing except Jesus Christ and him crucified. (1 Corinthians 2:2)
>
> But with a demonstration of the Spirit's power. (1 Corinthians 2:4)
>
> We speak of God's secret wisdom, a wisdom that has been hidden and that God destined for our glory. (1 Corinthians 2:7)
>
> This is what we speak, not in words taught us by human wisdom but in words taught by the Spirit, expressing spiritual truths in spiritual words. (1 Corinthians 2:13)

The contrast is clear: worldly wisdom versus God's wisdom. Eloquence and persuasion versus God's power. Human teaching versus Spirit-taught truths. And what is this spiritual wisdom? **The gospel—Christ crucified.**

> Jews demand miraculous signs and Greeks look for wisdom, but we preach Christ crucified: a stumbling block to Jews and foolishness to Gentiles, but to those whom God has called, both Jews and Greeks, Christ the power of God and the wisdom of God. (1 Corinthians 1:22-24)

The "spirituals" are the truths about God revealed through His breath—His Word. They are the gospel spoken and lived out among God's people.

## The Foundation of True Spirituality

### 1. Don't Be Ignorant of Spiritual Truth

> Now about the spirituals, brothers, I do not want you to be ignorant. Therefore I tell you that no one who is speaking by the Spirit of God says, "Jesus be cursed," and no one can say, "Jesus is Lord," except by the Holy Spirit. (1 Corinthians 12:1, 3)

In the world, "spirituality" often means embracing ignorance—following horoscopes, empty meditation, psychic readings, or whatever feels right. It's anti-intellectual, sometimes even sensational, chasing after miracles or emotional highs.

But Christian spirituality is not ignorance. Paul explicitly tells us not to be ignorant. We are called to know the truth. Nor is it mere emotionalism. Emotions have their place, but they are not the measure of spirituality. A passionate worship experience doesn't make you spiritual. Only understanding and embracing the gospel does.

### 2. The Ultimate Spiritual Truth Is the Gospel

> There are different kinds of gifts, but the same Spirit. There are different kinds of service, but the same Lord. There are different kinds of working, but the same God works all of them in all people. (1 Corinthians 12:4-6)

Paul's radical claim: **If you believe the gospel, you have the Spirit. You are spiritual.**

This truth has been obscured in some Christian circles. Years ago, a false teaching circulated claiming there were two baptisms: a baptism of water (when you become Christian) and a baptism of the Spirit (when you become truly powerful and able to speak with confidence). According to this error, you weren't a "real Christian" until you received the second baptism.

But this destroys the gospel's power. When you first committed your life to trusting that Jesus died for you—that moment, the Spirit came to you. Whether you felt it or not. Whether you've had emotional experiences or not. The gospel is the common possession of all believers.

Recently, someone criticized Sydney Anglicans for believing only in "the Father, Son, and Holy Bible." The jab was meant to suggest we don't have the Spirit. But this misses something crucial: the Holy Bible is inseparable from the Holy Spirit. Is the Word of God not the Sword of the Spirit? If you want the Spirit, He speaks through His Word.

The criticism really targeted our lack of emotionalism—our simple music, our quiet worship style, my refusal to raise my hands and cry from the pulpit. But all of that confuses emotionalism with spirituality. **Emotion is not the measure of the Spirit.**

Many today believe that if you're passionate, you're spiritual. If you feel it, it's real. But this heresy—dressed in new clothes—still undermines the most amazing gift of all: eternal life through Christ. All Christians share this common Spirit, whether emotionally expressive or quietly committed.

### 3. True Spirituality Shows in Lordship

> Therefore I tell you that no one who is speaking by the Spirit of God says, "Jesus be cursed," and no one can say, "Jesus is Lord," except by the Holy Spirit. (1 Corinthians 12:3)

The true sign of spirituality is not emotion or feeling. **It is wholehearted commitment to the Lordship of Christ.**

This isn't reciting a formula or chanting words. It's a genuine declaration that Jesus is Lord of your life—that He has supreme authority over you. The gospel itself is the good news that through His death and resurrection, Jesus is Lord of the universe.

If you refuse to submit to His lordship, you do not have the Spirit. You are not a Christian. I'm not saying Christians live perfectly—we stumble and fall. But we should desire to follow Him, to bring our lives in line with His will.

Some live a double life: Sunday worship followed by Monday cheating. Saturday parties followed by Sunday piety. Such people have not genuinely confessed Jesus as Lord. Piety and emotionalism cannot substitute for obedience.

## What Does This Look Like?

Some might think our church isn't "spiritual" because we lack elaborate formalities, our music is simple and down-to-earth, and our pastor doesn't come forward with raised hands or tears. But that's a shallow judgment.

True spirituality is a place where:
- The Bible is read and proclaimed
- Spiritual truths are spoken to one another
- People are submitting to the Lordship of Christ
- The Word cuts through self-deception, convicting us of sin and our need to repent
- Day by day, members commit more deeply to God's agenda and God's vision

I've spoken with some of you this year and asked you to sacrifice time, money, and effort for the sake of others and the growth of God's kingdom. Many of you said yes to greater suffering, deeper commitment, and real sacrifice. **That is true spirituality.**

If you haven't made that commitment, will you now? Will you submit to Christ's lordship, follow His will, love one another, and care for God's mission?

As we do this together—as we speak the gospel to one another, as we live under the Lordship of Christ, as we love and serve—this becomes a place where true spirituality dwells. And the world will know we are His disciples.
