# Colossians 1:24-2:3

**Title:** Mystery Revealed  
**Date:** 8 May 2022  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## Opening: The Mystery of Motherhood

On this Mother's Day, we celebrate the remarkable transformation that motherhood brings. A woman becomes a "supermum" overnight—not just physically, but neurologically. When a mother first sees her baby, her reward network floods with dopamine and oxytocin, creating instant love. Her emotional regulation and threat-detection systems activate in response to her child's needs. This mysterious rewiring of the brain is nothing short of miraculous.

Yet today we explore another mystery—one far greater than motherhood itself, though the parallel will illuminate both.

## The Mystery Revealed (Colossians 1:24-28; 2:2-3)

### What Is This Mystery?

> 25 I have become its servant by the commission God gave me to present to you the word of God in its fullness— 26 the mystery that has been kept hidden for ages and generations, but is now disclosed to the Lord's people.

The word "mystery" here doesn't mean spooky or inexplicable. It simply means hidden—like a murder mystery where the murderer's identity remains unknown until the very end when all is revealed. This mystery was hidden for ages and generations, thousands of years of human history, yet like any good mystery, God left clues throughout the Old Testament.

### The Clues in the Old Testament

God made promises to Abraham: land, offspring, and blessing. Israel would inherit physical territory and become a great nation, blessed and prosperous—a land flowing with milk and honey. Even when Israel stumbled and needed restoration, the prophets promised God would reshape the earth itself to fulfill these promises. And the prophets hinted that a new king would come—one who would transform the cosmos itself. For centuries, Israel wondered: who is this person?

### The Answer Revealed

> 2:2 My goal is that they may be encouraged in heart and united in love, so that they may have the full riches of complete understanding, in order that they may know the mystery of God, namely, Christ, 3 in whom are hidden all the treasures of wisdom and knowledge.

The mystery is **Christ**. His birth, life, teachings, death, and resurrection—this is the "word of God in its fullness" and the "full riches of complete understanding."

### How Christ Solves the Deepest Problem

God faced an eternal dilemma. On one hand, we have sinned and deserve God's wrath—His justice demands punishment for our rebellion. Yet on the other hand, God's mercy wants to forgive us rather than condemn us to eternal death. How could God fulfill both justice and mercy simultaneously?

Enter Jesus.

God took upon Himself the requirements of righteousness. He bore the penalty for our sin. And through that self-sacrifice, He is now able to forgive us.

Think of a mother whose young child destroys her favorite photo, flushes away her hard-earned savings, draws on newly painted walls, wrecks her career. As the child grows, they curse at her, even strike her in anger. The child deserves punishment. But instead, the mother pays for the damage herself, swallows her own dreams, and forgives her daughter or son unconditionally.

This is what Jesus did. God took the abuse that we deserved. And He forgives us.

This is the gospel mystery: that the powerful God would lower Himself to our level and die for us selfish mortals. This is good news. This is revealed in God's word. And we must want to discover more.

### Application: Our Attitude Toward Scripture

We possess the secrets of the universe in our hands—past, present, and future. Yet we scroll through Netflix and social media instead of studying Scripture. If someone gave you the winning lottery numbers for the next two years, you would memorize them. If someone gave you the answers to a major exam, you would study them diligently.

Scripture is infinitely more valuable. Make it a priority. Bring a hard copy to Bible studies and church. Take notes. See the greater context. Before you check social media, spend a few minutes in God's word. Before you go to YouTube, read Scripture.

Life is like an eighty-year exam that you must pass to survive. Here is the cheat code. Here are the secrets to success. Now that we know it, we must pass it on to others.

## The Mystery Made Known (Colossians 1:27-2:1)

### Made Known to the Gentiles

> 1:27 To them God has chosen to make known among the Gentiles the glorious riches of this mystery, which is Christ in you, the hope of glory.

The mystery is not only that Christ came. The mystery also includes that Christ came for the gentiles—for all nations, not just Israel.

> **Ephesians 3:6** This mystery is that through the gospel the Gentiles are heirs together with Israel, members together of one body, and sharers together in the promise in Christ Jesus.

It seemed at first that salvation was exclusively for the Jews—God's chosen nation. After all, Israel occupied a privileged position as God's children. Yet God intended from the beginning that the gospel would reach all nations.

God gave Abraham a hint:

> **Genesis 12:1-3** "Go from your country, your people and your father's household to the land I will show you. I will make you into a great nation, and I will bless you; I will make your name great, and you will be a blessing. I will bless those who bless you, and whoever curses you I will curse; and all peoples on earth will be blessed through you."

Abraham's descendants were not merely God's favored children—they were meant to grow up and serve the world. God wanted them to be priests of the gospel to the rest of humanity.

### The Pattern: Suffering for the Gospel

> 1:24 Now I rejoice in what I am suffering for you, and I fill up what is still lacking in regard to Christ's afflictions, for the sake of his body, which is the church.

This doesn't mean Christ's atoning work was incomplete. Rather, Paul understood his calling to continue the pattern of suffering that Christ established. Christ suffered for us; Paul suffers for others so that the gospel can spread to the gentiles.

> 1:28 He is the one we proclaim, admonishing and teaching everyone with all wisdom, so that we may present everyone fully mature in Christ. 1:29 To this end I strenuously contend with all the energy Christ so powerfully works in me.

> 2:1 I want you to know how hard I am contending for you and for those at Laodicea, and for all who have not met me personally.

Paul adopts the language of motherhood to describe this labor:

> **Galatians 4:19** My dear children, for whom I am again in the pains of childbirth until Christ is formed in you.

> **1 Thessalonians 2:7-8** Just as a nursing mother cares for her children, so we cared for you. Because we loved you so much, we were delighted to share with you not only the gospel of God but our lives as well.

### Application: Our Commitment to Gospel Work

For many of us, sharing the gospel is merely a hobby—something we do occasionally when we feel nostalgic. But Paul calls us to make it a career choice. We must commit ourselves fully to gospel proclamation.

This may not be immediately realistic for everyone. You may have a family, work responsibilities, and financial obligations. But let that be your aim: to free your life up for gospel work. If you live modestly and plan wisely, you may achieve this sooner rather than later.

In the meantime, treat gospel proclamation not as a hobby, but as a side-gig. A side-gig is real work—a second job. It requires showing up faithfully.

### A Special Word for Mothers

Mothers have a unique opportunity to embody this calling sooner rather than later. Homemaking is not merely child-rearing in isolation—it is full-time ministry within the home and community. Through neighborhood initiatives, teaching Scripture in schools, running playgroups for neighbors, visiting the sick, and supporting families in crisis, a mother who is freed from external employment (when her husband's income permits) can engage in essentially full-time gospel work.

This requires sacrifice. As Jesus gave His body for us, mothers give their bodies for their children. Not "my body, my choice," but "my body for their body"—following Christ's example.

Motherhood offers no degree, no manual, no paycheck, no promotions, no formal reward system. The first child will be hard. The second harder still. But by the third and fourth, you develop wisdom and achieve economies of scale.

This is to "fill up what is still lacking in regard to Christ's afflictions." You are doing God's will. You are fulfilling Paul's vision.

**Well done, mothers, for attempting this sacred work. Keep at it. Our time is short. We can spend our skills making corporations wealthy, or we can spend our lives making Christ known among the nations—especially to our own children—so that they become rich in the knowledge of God.**

## The Fullness of Christ

> 2:2 My goal is that they may be encouraged in heart and united in love, so that they may have the full riches of complete understanding, in order that they may know the mystery of God, namely, Christ, 3 in whom are hidden all the treasures of wisdom and knowledge.

All the treasures of wisdom and knowledge are hidden in Christ. Everything we need to understand life, purpose, suffering, joy, and eternity is found in Him.
