# 2 Timothy 3:10-17

**Title:** Listening to God
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Scripture Foundation

> 2 Timothy 3:10-17 (NIV)
> 
> You, however, know all about my teaching, my way of life, my purpose, faith, patience, love, endurance, persecutions, sufferings—what kinds of things happened to me in Antioch, Iconium and Lystra, the persecutions I endured. Yet the Lord rescued me from all of them. In fact, everyone who wants to live a godly life in Christ Jesus will be persecuted, while evil men and impostors will go from bad to worse, deceiving and being deceived. But as for you, continue in what you have learned and have become convinced of, because you know those from whom you learned it, and how from infancy you have known the holy Scriptures, which are able to make you wise for salvation through faith in Christ Jesus. All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness, so that the man of God may be thoroughly equipped for every good work.

## Where We've Been: God's Grace in Two Tenses

Paul reminds Timothy that our salvation operates in two dimensions:

**We are saved from God's future wrath** (Ephesians 2:4-5)
> But because of his great love for us, God, who is rich in mercy, made us alive with Christ even when we were dead in transgressions—it is by grace you have been saved.

**We are being transformed to live righteously now** (Titus 2:11-14)
> For the grace of God that brings salvation has appeared to all men. It teaches us to say "No" to ungodliness and worldly passions, and to live self-controlled, upright and godly lives in this present age, while we wait for the blessed hope—the glorious appearing of our great God and Savior, Jesus Christ, who gave himself for us to redeem us from all wickedness and to purify for himself a people that are his very own, eager to do what is good.

This creates a beautiful tension: we are secure in Christ's finished work, yet actively called to pursue righteousness as we await His return.

## The Central Question: How Do We Know What Is Right?

With salvation secured and a calling to live righteously, we face an essential question: **How do we discern what is actually righteous and good?** The answer lies in understanding Scripture itself.

### What Is Scripture?

Scripture is God-breathed—not merely human wisdom about God, but God's own Word breathed out to us. It is divinely inspired, carrying His authority and truth.

### What Purpose Does It Serve?

Scripture equips us for every dimension of the Christian life:
- **Teaching**: Shows us what is true
- **Rebuking**: Confronts us when we're wrong
- **Correcting**: Realigns us toward righteousness
- **Training in righteousness**: Develops godly character and practice

Through these functions, Scripture makes us "wise for salvation through faith in Christ Jesus" and thoroughly equips us "for every good work."

## Why Read the Bible?

We read Scripture because it is our guide for knowing God's will and living according to His purposes. Without it, we have no reliable standard for discerning right from wrong. The Bible is not optional—it is essential for those who desire to live a godly life.

## How Do You Read Your Bible?

This is the practical question that transforms knowledge into living faith. Reading the Bible is not merely an intellectual exercise but a spiritual practice that shapes our entire lives.
