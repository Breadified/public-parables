# Genesis 9:1-20

**Title:** Beginning of Government
**Date:** 24 January 2021
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## A. Recreation of the Heavens and Earth

The flood account in Genesis 8-9 mirrors the creation account of Genesis 1-2 in striking ways. God is essentially re-establishing His order after the world has been corrupted by sin.

### Parallels Between Noah and Adam

Both men were given dominion over the animals. Noah took charge of them as they left the ark, just as Adam named them in the garden. Both are described as men connected to the soil—Adam formed from dust, Noah described as "a man of the soil" who planted a vineyard.

### Dry Land Appearing

Just as God caused dry land to appear on the third day of creation, so the earth emerged again from the floodwaters in Noah's time. The world had to be recreated because it had become severely wicked. Humanity, separated from the tree of life, had descended into evil—from Cain's murder of Abel to Lamech's boasting of his own violence.

### Why Judgment Became Necessary

> Genesis 6:5 The Lord saw how great the wickedness of the human race had become on the earth, and that every inclination of the thoughts of the human heart was only evil all the time.

The second law of thermodynamics describes how systems tend toward chaos and disorder. Humanity, inclined toward evil from childhood, was devolving into complete chaos. The world needed a reset—like a corrupted software system that must be rebooted.

But this reset strategy raises a question: Is it sustainable? God established a covenant that suggests He will not continue an endless cycle of destruction and recreation.

### The Covenant: "Never Again"

> Genesis 9:8-11 Then God said to Noah and to his sons with him: "I now establish my covenant with you and with your descendants after you and with every living creature that was with you—the birds, the livestock and all the wild animals, all those that came out of the ark with you—every living creature on earth. I establish my covenant with you: Never again will all life be destroyed by the waters of a flood; never again will there be a flood to destroy the earth."

God commits to a new approach. Rather than destroying humanity repeatedly, He will work with the reality of human sinfulness.

### The Paradox of Human Nature

> Genesis 8:21 The Lord smelled the pleasing aroma and said in his heart: "Never again will I curse the ground because of humans, for even though every inclination of the human heart is evil from childhood. And never again will I destroy all living creatures, as I have done."

This is remarkable: the very description of human nature that justified judgment in Genesis 6:5—"every inclination of the human heart is evil from childhood"—becomes the reason for salvation in Genesis 8:21. How can the same inclination be both cause for destruction and cause for mercy?

### Understanding Human Nature

Many philosophical traditions teach that human nature is inherently good. The Confucian teaching states: "People at birth, are naturally good." But this contradicts both Scripture and observable reality. Children do not need to be taught to be selfish or cruel; we must teach them to be kind. They instinctively cry out for themselves, demand what they want, and seem naturally capable of inventing new ways to harm and manipulate others.

The inclination toward evil is not merely about deeds we commit—it's about the bent of our hearts. We are restrained by culture, education, and training, but given opportunity and freedom from external constraints, we invent new ways to do evil.

> Romans 1:28-30 Furthermore, just as they did not think it worthwhile to retain the knowledge of God, so God gave them over to a depraved mind, so that they do what ought not to be done. They have become filled with every kind of wickedness, evil, greed and depravity. They are full of envy, murder, strife, deceit and malice. They are gossips, slanderers, God-haters, insolent, arrogant and boastful; they invent ways of doing evil.

In our present mortal state, this inclination is fundamentally unchangeable. Good teaching, faith, a good church, and a good country can improve our behavior significantly, but we await new bodies to be rid of sin entirely.

### Mercy as the Ultimate Solution

Perhaps this is why the description "inclined to do evil from childhood" serves as both the reason for judgment and the reason for salvation. God will be the one to save because we are inclined to do evil. God will be the one to save because He alone can. We cannot save ourselves.

Rather than relying on us to obey His laws or change our hearts, God deals with the facts of life: we sin. He finds a solution that incorporates the problem of sin itself. God will come as a human—one who is not inclined to sin—and take upon Himself the sin of those who are inclined to sin. This is the message of the cross.

In Noah's time, the rainbow was the sign of this covenant.

### The Rainbow: A Sign of Peace

> Genesis 9:12-17 And God said, "This is the sign of the covenant I am making between me and you and every living creature with you, a covenant for all generations to come: I have set my rainbow in the clouds, and it will be the sign of the covenant between me and the earth. Whenever I bring clouds over the earth and the rainbow appears in the clouds, I will remember my covenant between me and you and all living creatures of every kind. Never again will the waters become a flood to destroy all life. Whenever the rainbow appears in the clouds, I will see it and remember the everlasting covenant between God and all living creatures of every kind on the earth."

God establishes a sign. The Hebrew word for "rainbow" is simply "bow"—the same word used for a weapon of war. God uses the bow for judgment throughout Scripture. But after the judgment of the flood, with its violent rain and lightning, God hangs up His weapon of judgment.

It's possible that before the flood, rain had never fallen (Genesis 2:5 says God had not caused it to rain). The rainbow may be a new thing—a phenomenon created by water in the air refracting light. But whether old or new, the rainbow carries a new meaning: it is a symbol of peace between God and humanity.

In our time, the rainbow has been appropriated to mean something else entirely. But when we see it, we should remember God's message: even to those inclined toward evil—whether through sexual sin, adultery, or the emptiness of modern "hookup culture"—the rainbow speaks hope. When you find yourself immersed in deep sin, look up and see the rainbow. It is a message that God has not abandoned you, that His judgment was satisfied at the cross, where He pointed His arrow away from the earth and toward Himself. Jesus experienced death so that we might live. He loves you.

## B. Recreation of the Rule of Man

God repeats the creation mandate to Noah, establishing that humanity's role remains unchanged: to be fruitful, multiply, and fill the earth.

### The Command Repeated

> Genesis 1:28 God blessed them and said to them, "Be fruitful and increase in number; fill the earth and subdue it."

> Genesis 9:1 Then God blessed Noah and his sons, saying to them, "Be fruitful and increase in number and fill the earth."

But the terms have changed. In creation, God gave humanity dominion over the animals and plants for food. Now, after the flood, new elements appear—elements required by a degraded world.

### Fear and Dread: A New Relationship with Animals

> Genesis 9:2-3 The fear and dread of you will fall on all the beasts of the earth, and on all the birds in the sky, on every creature that moves along the ground, and on all the fish in the sea; they are given into your hands. Everything that lives and moves about will be food for you. Just as I gave you the green plants, I now give you everything.

Originally, the relationship between humans and animals was not based on fear. Humans ruled with authority, but it was benign. Now, animals will fear humans because humans are given authority to kill them for food. This is the first time meat-eating is explicitly permitted.

Why this change? One theory points to degradation—humanity, increasingly distant from the tree of life, needed sustenance that only meat could provide. Another perspective connects it to the new covenant administration, which would emphasize sacrifice and the shedding of blood as a way to approach God.

### Restrictions on Eating Meat

> Genesis 9:4-5 But you must not eat meat that has its lifeblood still in it. And for your lifeblood I will surely demand an accounting. I will demand an accounting from every animal.

The restriction against eating meat with blood still in it likely reflects a concern about bloodthirstiness and cruelty to animals. Cruelty to animals can lead to cruelty to people. History shows us that those who delight in the torture of animals often graduate to cruelty toward humans. The prohibition is designed to cultivate compassion and respect for life, even as humans are given authority to take animal life.

### The Foundation of Government: Capital Punishment

> Genesis 9:5-6 And for your lifeblood I will surely demand an accounting. I will demand an accounting from every animal. And from each human being, too, I will demand an accounting for the life of another human being. Whoever sheds human blood, by humans shall their blood be shed; for in the image of God has God made mankind.

This is significant: God introduces human justice. Prior to this, God directly judged sin—Cain was marked, Lamech's boast was recorded, and the flood itself was God's direct judgment. But now, humans are given the responsibility to exact justice. This marks the beginning of government.

> Romans 13:4 For the one in authority is God's servant for your good. But if you do wrong, be afraid, for rulers do not bear the sword for no reason. They are God's servants, agents of wrath to bring punishment on the wrongdoer.

Government exists because human hearts are inclined toward evil. Without government—without men with the authority and power to punish evil—society descends into anarchy. The alternative to government is chaos. Government is the lesser of two evils, and its primary responsibility is to punish murder, the greatest crime.

At the climax of crime is murder. At the climax of justice is capital punishment. This is not cruelty; it is justice. When an animal kills a human, we euthanize it. When a human murders another human, the same principle applies—the accounting must be paid.

### Why Government Matters

> Genesis 9:7 As for you, be fruitful and increase in number; multiply on the earth and increase upon it.

How can humanity multiply if murder artificially decreases the population? Murder works against God's purpose. The image of God is stamped on every human being, making murder not just an offense against a person, but an offense against God Himself.

Government does not need to involve itself in many areas—not in sexuality, not in education (parents bear that responsibility), not in dictating environmental policy or financial systems. But government has one essential job: to kill murderers and protect the innocent. Without this, there is no order.

The irony of our modern age is stark: we protect those convicted of murder while simultaneously permitting the killing of the unborn. We debate the death penalty while legalizing abortion and euthanasia. We have called good evil and evil good.

### The Message for Australia Day

As we approach Australia Day, we should pray for government—that it would fulfill its God-given purpose and not turn against those who call upon the name of Christ. We should proclaim the message of the rainbow: that God is not seeking to destroy us, but calls us to obedience to Him and love for one another.

Human government is imperfect because humans are imperfect. But it is necessary. Without it, we cannot fulfill God's mandate to be fruitful and multiply in safety. We need government to do its job: to restrain evil and protect the innocent.
