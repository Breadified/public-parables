# 1 Corinthians 2:6-16

**Title:** Wisdom  
**Date:** 26 July 2016  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Seeing the Big Picture

Understanding requires perspective. Without seeing the bigger picture, we become confused, anxious about the wrong things, and careless about what truly matters. This applies to major societal decisions about marriage, economics, and justice—and equally to personal decisions about education, relationships, and raising children.

Think of it like watching a film where confusion reigns until the final reveal transforms your understanding. Or consider temperature graphs: zooming from 100 years to 2,000 years to 800,000 years completely changes what the data means. As the timeframe expands, perspective shifts dramatically.

The gospel provides this crucial bigger picture. It gives us the tools to see reality clearly—not just in "religious" matters, but in every area of life.

## Part 1: Human Wisdom vs. Spirit Wisdom

### The Wisdom We Speak (v.6)

> We do, however, speak a message of wisdom among the mature, but not the wisdom of this age or of the rulers of this age, who are coming to nothing.

Paul speaks of wisdom—the gospel message about Jesus's death and resurrection. This is contrasted with earthly wisdom, which the "rulers of this age" (both ancient Romans and modern leaders) neither understand nor recognize.

> 1 Corinthians 1:22-24: Jews demand signs and Greeks look for wisdom, 23 but we preach Christ crucified: a stumbling block to Jews and foolishness to Gentiles, 24 but to those whom God has called, both Jews and Greeks, Christ the power of God and the wisdom of God.

> 2:2 For I resolved to know nothing while I was with you except Jesus Christ and him crucified.

The gospel is God's complete revelation, summarized around five essential truths:

**Knowledge of God as Creator**  
God is the loving ruler of the world who made us to steward creation under his authority.

**Knowledge of Sin**  
We all reject God's rule by trying to live our own way, but we fail—both individually and societally.

**Knowledge of Judgment**  
God will not permit rebellion forever. Death and judgment await those who refuse his rule.

**Knowledge of Redemption**  
Out of love, God sent Jesus, who lived perfectly under God's rule. Through his death, Jesus took our punishment and brought forgiveness. Justice and mercy meet at the cross.

**Knowledge of Resurrection**  
Jesus rose from the dead, conquering death and offering new life. He will return as ruler to judge all things.

This framework is logically coherent: it identifies the Creator, explains why we need him, outlines the consequences, and offers the solution—leaving us with a choice.

### This Wisdom Was Hidden (v.7-9)

> 7 No, we declare God's wisdom, a mystery that has been hidden and that God destined for our glory before time began.

The gospel is a mystery—hidden and destined for our glory in eternity. This makes sense because:

> 8 None of the rulers of this age understood it, for if they had, they would not have crucified the Lord of glory.

The rulers—Jewish and Roman authorities—did not comprehend what they were doing. And this remains true: those of this age, whether ancient or modern, operating from purely human wisdom, cannot understand God's wisdom. If they did, they would not reject Christ or persecute his church.

> 9 However, as it is written: "What no eye has seen, what no ear has heard, and what no human mind has conceived"—the things God has prepared for those who love him.

The gospel transcends human knowledge channels. We cannot:
- **Think** our way to the gospel through reason
- **Work** our way to it through effort  
- **Rationalize** our way to it through logic
- **Experience** our way to it through intuition

God's wisdom is inaccessible to human potential in any form. It can only be received as a gift.

### We Are Given the Gospel (v.10-11)

> 10 These are the things God has revealed to us by his Spirit. The Spirit searches all things, even the deep things of God.

God has revealed the gospel to us. He spoke first to the apostles, who recorded it. That same revelation is available to us today.

> 11 For who knows a person's thoughts except their own spirit within them? In the same way no one knows the thoughts of God except the Spirit of God.

Just as only a person's own spirit knows their thoughts, only God's Spirit knows God's mind. Yet this hidden wisdom has been revealed to us—a privilege beyond measure.

**This is incredible news!** It proves the gospel wasn't invented by humanity through trial and error. Human wisdom tends toward survival of the fittest, justifying violence and oppression. Only the gospel teaches "love one another" and protects the vulnerable. Look at societies untouched by Christianity: witness how they treat women, children, and minorities—often with barbarity. The gospel transforms culture, and no natural evolutionary process explains this transformation.

### Given to the Mature (v.6)

> We do, however, speak a message of wisdom among the mature.

This wisdom is for the mature—those who see the bigger picture. Maturity means thinking long-term, considering consequences, and grasping eternal implications. 

The frontal lobe, which enables long-term decision-making and consequence assessment, doesn't fully develop until age 25—which is why car insurance is expensive for younger drivers. Similarly, spiritual maturity comes from understanding God's long view: eternity is the ultimate long-term perspective.

The gospel matures us by teaching us to think beyond this moment, this day, this life. It redirects anxiety away from temporal concerns toward eternal reality. With this perspective, we make wiser decisions about education, relationships, parenting, and how we steward our influence.

Receiving the gospel as a gift also requires gratitude. We don't critique God's choices; rather, like children receiving a gift, we receive with thanks. God isn't accountable to us for his design of creation—we're accountable to him for our choices. He's the potter; we're the clay.

## Part 2: Human Interpretation vs. Spirit Interpretation

We receive not only the gospel's content but also the means to understand it. We're given special glasses to interpret Scripture itself.

> 12 What we have received is not the spirit of the world, but the Spirit who is from God, so that we may understand what God has freely given us.

> 13 This is what we speak, not in words taught us by human wisdom but in words taught by the Spirit, explaining spiritual realities with Spirit-taught words.

> 14 The person without the Spirit does not accept the things that come from the Spirit of God but considers them foolishness, and cannot understand them because they are discerned only through the Spirit.

Scripture is self-contained—it comes with its own interpreter. Like a can of sardines with its own opener, or cup of noodles with its own fork, or the Rosetta Stone that unlocks hieroglyphics, Scripture provides within itself the framework for understanding itself.

The gospel seems strange to our natural ears because our perception has been corrupted by sin. We're all spiritually color-blind, unable to perceive what we haven't been shown. Romans 1 illustrates this:

> Romans 1:18-20: The wrath of God is being revealed from heaven against all the godlessness and wickedness of people, who suppress the truth by their wickedness, since what may be known about God is plain to them, because God has made it plain to them. For since the creation of the world God's invisible qualities—his eternal power and divine nature—have been clearly seen, being understood from what has been made, so that people are without excuse.

God has made himself visible in creation. Yet:

> Romans 1:21-22: Although they knew God, they neither glorified him as God nor gave thanks to him, but their thinking became futile and their foolish hearts were darkened. Although they claimed to be wise, they became fools.

Once people reject God's revelation, a spiral of distorted thinking begins:

> Romans 1:24-28: God gave them over in the sinful desires of their hearts... They exchanged the truth about God for a lie... God gave them over to shameful lusts... God gave them over to a depraved mind, so that they do what ought not to be done.

When people suppress truth repeatedly, they lose the basic tools to recognize truth at all. It's like starting to use drugs knowing it's harmful, but eventually—across generations—the distortion becomes normal. Your children inherit a twisted worldview without even knowing an alternative exists.

So in verse 14, "cannot understand" means a genuine cognitive inability, not mere refusal. The unbeliever is trapped—not because they won't believe, but because they can't. Their interpretive framework has been compromised.

### The Epistemological Gap

Many assume that with enough logical proof, historical evidence, and philosophical argument, unbelievers will believe. Yet this overlooks a fundamental problem: unbelievers operate from different presuppositions. They don't share our values, logic, or criteria for evidence. They seek signs and earthly wisdom—not the gospel.

Debates often reveal this. One side assumes logical discourse; the other weaponizes offense. The distance between two people operating from different epistemologies—different ways of knowing and valuing truth—is unbridgeable by argument alone.

Consider the contradictions that arise when the gospel is abandoned:

- Christianity (which grants women voting rights and autonomy) is called sexist, while Islam (which mutilates female genitalia and forces women into submission) is defended.
- Christians are accused of discrimination for calling a boy a boy and a girl a girl, while transgender ideology is celebrated for insisting a boy is a girl and a girl is a boy.
- Homosexuality is called innate and unchangeable, yet gender is declared fluid and chosen—using opposite logic simultaneously.
- Israel (a democracy protecting minorities, warning enemies of attacks) is condemned, while Iran (pursuing genocide, funding terrorism) is trusted with nuclear weapons.
- The Church is attacked for 0.000003% of members being abusers, while 40-50 million annual abortions are ignored—a disparity of millions to one in proportionality.

The logic has collapsed. Once you lose the gospel's presuppositions, you lose the framework for logic itself. You lose:

- **Law**: Without a creator-judge, law becomes power maintaining power. Without transcendent accountability, "might is right."
- **Sin**: Without the gospel, we deny human nature is sinful and blame external factors—poverty, oppression. Then we're shocked when permissiveness produces chaos instead of virtue.
- **Justice**: Without God, justice becomes arbitrary. Yet even secular society recognizes justice as intrinsically valuable—prosecuting ancient war criminals, trying senile perpetrators. Why? Because justice matters.
- **Forgiveness**: Without the gospel's redemptive model, resentment calcifies. We can't love enemies or forgive deeply.
- **Hope**: Without resurrection, we're left with "eat, drink, and be merry, for tomorrow we die." Purpose evaporates.

These aren't peripheral to the gospel—they're foundational to civilization itself. When the gospel fades, its presuppositions fade with it, and society unravels.

### You Have the Mind of Christ

> 15 The person with the Spirit makes judgments about all things, but such a person is not subject to merely human judgments.

> 16 "Who has known the mind of the Lord so as to instruct him?" But we have the mind of Christ.

Christians possess wisdom to judge all things—not just theology, but politics, science, history, economics, relationships, parenting. You have access to God's perspective on reality itself.

This isn't arrogance; it's confidence rooted in receiving God's interpretive framework. When you have the mind of Christ, you can discern truth across every domain.

Consider migration patterns over 200 years. People—atheists, Muslims, Buddhists—flee to societies shaped by Christian heritage. Why? Christian societies developed:
- Safety and order
- Justice and accountability  
- Protection for minorities
- Care for women and children
- Rule of law

Even as Christian belief fades, these societies still live on inherited capital. The effects persist. But when the gospel is abandoned entirely, that capital depletes.

To be truly wise—as an engineer, accountant, parent, student, or leader—you need the gospel. It provides the ultimate perspective, the biggest picture, the tools to see reality as it is.

The gospel is a gift. Receive it in faith, and suddenly God's wisdom opens before you. You'll have the Spirit of God and the mind of Christ—everything you need to navigate this world with genuine understanding.
