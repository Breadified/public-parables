## Introduction: The Overlooked King

Proverbs 31 is famous for the "Proverbs 31 woman"—the portrait of an ideal wife and mother. But we often overlook the beginning of this chapter, which presents us with the "Proverbs 31 man."

> The sayings of King Lemuel—an inspired utterance his mother taught him. Listen, my son! Listen, son of my womb! Listen, my son, the answer to my prayers! *(son of my vows)*

This passage frames a mother's wisdom to her son, a king named Lemuel, which means "devoted to God." The book of Proverbs opens and closes with the same emphasis: do not forsake your mother's teaching. Though there is no historical record of a King Lemuel (some believe this refers to Solomon, others to a foreign figure), the inspired collectors of Scripture deemed this an oracle—God's Word to us about what constitutes true manhood and leadership.

His mother warns her son about two great dangers: women and wine. These warnings apply to all men who would lead—not just kings, but fathers, Christian men, and those called to influence their communities.

## Warning 1: Don't Spend Your Strength on Women

> Do not spend your strength on women, your vigor on those who ruin kings.

The word "strength" carries multiple meanings throughout Scripture—it can mean power, wealth, military force, or the totality of your mind and livelihood. Essentially, the mother is saying: "Stop letting thoughts of girls and sex dominate your existence."

### Why This Matters

It's natural for young men to think about the opposite sex. Testosterone is real. But this predisposition—our natural inclination—is being systematically exploited to sap our strength.

**Wealth.** Advertising weaponizes attraction. An ordinary pair of jeans on a pretty model becomes desirable. A plain car, ordinary food, an unremarkable holiday destination—all transformed into objects of desire through association with beauty. This drives wasteful spending. The porn industry alone generates between $15 billion and $97 billion annually in the USA—more than the combined revenues of ABC, NBC, and CBS combined, and more than the NFL, NBA, and MLB combined.

**Relationships.** Serial monogamy—moving from one "serious girlfriend" to another—is practicing divorce and remarriage. This lifestyle fragments your ability to commit deeply.

**Time.** Hours spent ruminating and dreaming are hours not spent building. The majority of workplace affairs (some estimates suggest 75-90%) occur at work, where men and women spend long hours together.

### The Biblical Pattern: Kings Ruined by Women

History and Scripture show us men brought down by their attraction to women:

- **Adam** was drawn to Eve at her most appealing
- **Samson's** legendary strength was weakened by Delilah
- **King David** committed adultery with Bathsheba
- **Solomon** himself married many wives, despite his wisdom
- **King Edward VIII** abandoned the throne for a woman, then collaborated with Nazis hoping to reclaim power
- **Prince Harry** became a victim of his own choices

But Lemuel's mother offers the antidote:

> Charm is deceptive, and beauty is fleeting; but a woman who fears the LORD is to be praised.

**Don't be thirsty.** In Gen Z language: stop being desperately eager for the attention of women. Strength means not being manipulated by beauty.

### Application: Marriage as Multiplication or Division

When choosing a wife, the question isn't about sexual compatibility (if you're male and female, you're already compatible). The real question is: **Is she ready for marriage?** Is she ready for the work, the service, the responsibility? Is she ready to submit to her husband as Christ leads the church?

There's an adage—perhaps I invented it, as it doesn't appear online—that "the right wife doubles your strength, but the wrong wife halves it." A compatible marriage multiplies your power. An incompatible one divides it.

**For unmarried men:** If you find a godly woman who is genuinely ready for marriage, marry quickly. But if the godly women don't take you seriously, if you're not yet marriageable, then take your time. Don't be desperate.

Many young Christian men are too eager for marriage as a solution to sexual desire. They misread 1 Corinthians 7:

> But if they cannot control themselves, they should marry, for it is better to marry than to burn with passion.

In the original Greek, "passion" doesn't appear. The verse simply says: "if it is better to marry than to burn." The word "burn" throughout Scripture typically refers to judgment, not sexual desire. Paul is addressing those already committing sexual immorality—widows and widowers already sleeping together—who would "burn" under God's judgment. For them, marriage is the solution.

But sexual desire is never solved by marriage. It's solved by self-control—the fruit of the Spirit. It comes from having God's mind, from dedication to righteousness and living God's way.

**Use the time wisely.** Build up your strength, your power, your self-control. Become a more godly man, a better man. You are not the weak. You are not those without hope. You are sons of the covenant, and God has promised: "Never will I leave you, never will I forsake you."

## Warning 2: Stay Alert—Don't Drink

> It is not for kings, Lemuel—it is not for kings to drink wine, not for rulers to crave beer, lest they drink and forget what has been decreed (the law), and deprive all the oppressed of their rights.

Wine and beer in moderation can be sources of joy (Deuteronomy 14:26; Psalm 104:14-15; Isaiah 25:6-8). But their abuse brings danger:

> Wine is a mocker and beer a brawler; whoever is led astray by them is not wise.

### The Problem: Loss of Clarity

Wine is the opposite of self-control. Its entire purpose is to change your mood, to lower your guard, to make you forget. But a king—a leader, a Christian man—cannot afford to forget God's law.

Consider Uriah the Hittite. When King David tried to trick him into sleeping with his wife, Uriah refused:

> The ark and Israel and Judah are staying in tents, and my commander Joab and my lord's men are camped in the open country. How could I go to my house to eat and drink and make love to my wife? As surely as you live, I will not do such a thing!

Uriah remembered he was at war. He controlled both his sexual desire and his drinking. As a soldier, he needed clarity.

**You are in a war.** The apostle Peter writes:

> Be alert and of sober mind. Your enemy the devil prowls around like a roaring lion looking for someone to devour.

You are driving a car down a windy road. You cannot afford to take your eyes off the road, to be distracted, to lower your guard. Leaders must remain alert.

### A Side Note: For Those Without Hope

There is a merciful exception in verses 6-7:

> Let beer be for those who are perishing, wine for those who are in anguish! Let them drink and forget their poverty and remember their misery no more.

This is not a policy recommendation for free beer for the poor. Rather, it acknowledges that for those with no hope—the dying, those with nothing to live for—alcohol serves as morphine. It dulls the pain when there's nothing left to hope for.

But that's not you. You have hope in the resurrection. You are not the powerless. You are not the weak. You are not those with no hope. As Paul writes:

> If the dead are not raised... "Let us eat and drink, for tomorrow we die."

But that's not our faith. We have hope.

## The Task of a King: Righteousness

> Speak up for those who cannot speak for themselves, for the rights of all who are destitute. Speak up and judge fairly; defend the rights of the poor and needy.

The word "speak up" is repeated—speak and act. As 1 John reminds us:

> Dear children, let us not love with words or speech but with actions and in truth.

Those who "cannot speak" are the mute, the voiceless, the vulnerable. Those who are "destitute" are appointed to die—echoing the earlier warning about those "perishing" without hope. A king doesn't give them drinks to forget; he rescues them through righteous judgment.

### Justice, Not "Social Justice"

Here we must be careful with language. The call is for righteous judgment—treating everyone equally according to God's law—not "social justice," which often means forcing equal outcomes regardless of different circumstances.

We cannot and should not aim for identical results. Every person is different. One beautiful musician earns a billion; another equally hardworking and talented musician cannot get an audition. Who can account for timing, genetics, location, connections, risk, delayed gratification, and yes—dumb luck? 

What we seek instead is that everyone is treated equally by the standard of God's law. Speak up for:

- The unborn who cannot speak for themselves
- The innocent falsely accused
- Those bullied by those who break the law

### The Three Estates: Household, Church, Society

Martin Luther spoke of three "estates" where Christian men must lead: the household, the church, and society.

**The household.** Christian fathers must lead their families. Marriages built only on romance must mature into partnerships capable of producing and raising the next generation.

**The church.** Men must serve sacrificially, using their gifts and time.

**Society.** Men must engage in workplaces, school boards, local committees, political parties, voting booths—wherever decisions are made. As Proverbs 31:23 shows us:

> Her husband is respected at the city gate, where he takes his seat among the elders of the land.

He is not drunk. He is not consumed with thoughts of self-gratification. He is self-controlled, remembering the law, and judging righteously.

### A Warning and a Call

On Father's Day, we saw thousands protest in the city—10,000 people venting frustration about inflation, housing, and changing social norms. I'm sympathetic. No one wants their suburb transformed overnight. And I'd be skeptical of media portrayals labeling everyone who disagrees with their narrative as extremists. You and I have been called that too.

Good for them for speaking up. Behind every man protesting is someone trying to protect his wife, daughters, mothers, and the brothers, sons, and friends he loves. They endure media hatred and police scrutiny while others receive different treatment.

But it cannot stop at protest. Complaining and whinging are not enough. What action will follow?

### The Root Problem

Immigration itself is not the problem—it's a solution to another problem: the native population has stopped having children. When you lack children born to you, the only way to maintain prosperity and the workforce is immigration. For thirty years, Australia had zero recession until the lockdowns.

But Australians want prosperity without children. Cut immigration without having your own children, and soon you'll be protesting again—about lack of jobs, opportunity, and future.

### The Real Solution

There is hopelessness because Australia has lost the faith of its youth. Instead of marching on a Sunday, they should be at church—worshipping the God of their ancestors, the God who transformed this country from barren desert to the metropolis it now is.

**Here is the warning:** If you do not build your strength, marry the right people, and care about righteousness, you will be replaced by others who will. They will occupy the workplaces, the school committees, the boards, the voting booths, the political parties.

**And here is the call:** Fathers and future fathers, you need to let go of your hobbies, your indulgences, your holidays, your temptations, and your distractions. You no longer have time for those. We need strong Christian men to lead the way.

The world is broken. Your strength, your wisdom, and your knowledge of God's law are desperately needed.
