# Titus 2:1-10

**Title:** Be Who You Are!
**Date:** November 7, 2008
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Foundation: Grace Has Appeared

> For the grace of God that brings salvation has appeared to all men. (Titus 2:11)

God's grace has made its appearance in Jesus Christ. This grace that saved us while we were still sinners is complete—all the work has been done. We live now in the light of that finished salvation, knowing that the next appearance of grace will be at Christ's return for judgment.

The question naturally arises: "What will I be?" The answer Paul gives through Titus is not about our careers, our wealth, or our social status. It's about who we are becoming in Christ. Everything in this passage flows from one central truth: **be who you are!**

## Living Out What Grace Has Done

> It teaches us to say "No" to ungodliness and worldly passions, and to live self-controlled, upright and godly lives in this present age. (Titus 2:12)

God's grace doesn't simply rescue us from sin—it teaches us how to live. The grace that appeared to save us now instructs us to live in a completely different way.

This means two things:

**First, reject ungodliness and worldly passions.** Self-control stands against the passions of this world. Uprightness means living by God's values, not the world's. Godliness means ordering our lives around what pleases God, not what pleases our desires.

**Second, do good works.** Notice in verses 2-10 how Paul illustrates this for every group: older men are to be temperate and self-controlled; older women are to teach what is good; younger women are to be self-controlled and pure; young men are to show integrity and seriousness of speech; even servants are to be trustworthy and honest in their work. In every relationship and every circumstance, we live out the reality of our redemption through our conduct.

As Paul says in Ephesians, we are God's workmanship, "created in Christ Jesus to do good works, which God prepared in advance for us to do."

## The Motivation: You Have Been Redeemed

> Who gave himself for us to redeem us from all wickedness and to purify for himself a people that are his very own, eager to do what is good. (Titus 2:14)

Here is the heart of it: Christ gave himself for you. Do you know what you cost? You cost the cross.

This is why Paul's exhortations to right living are not burdensome commands—they flow from something far deeper. When you receive an invaluable gift, why would you treat it carelessly? When you make a team you've longed to join, why would you skip training? When you've been redeemed at such infinite cost, how could you not live in a way that honors that redemption?

You are a purified people—set apart for God's own purposes. The works you do, the integrity you show, the self-control you exercise, the kindness you demonstrate—these are not ways to earn God's favor. They are the natural expression of what you already are in Christ. Good works flow from salvation, not the other way around. Sometimes this consistency takes time as you grow into who you truly are, but gradually, the reality of your redemption should shape every area of your life.

**Be who you are!** You are redeemed, purified, and loved at immeasurable cost. Live like it.
