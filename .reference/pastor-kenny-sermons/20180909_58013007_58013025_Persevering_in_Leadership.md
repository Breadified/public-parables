# Hebrews 13:7-25

**Title:** Persevering in Leadership  
**Date:** September 9, 2018  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction: Learning from Leaders

Congratulations to those graduating. This recognition celebrates not just your achievements, but your dedication to what you've learned. You're stepping forward as leaders.

I didn't grow up in a church, but my parents sent me to Sunday school for about six years. I have vivid memories of Miss Kwok, the head Sunday school teacher—a kind, middle-aged woman who was there faithfully every Sunday, leading us in singing and prayer. I learned something crucial from her presence: there is a God, and He cares for me.

Later, in youth group, various leaders taught me Bible studies, shared the gospel, and loaned me books. Everything began to make sense. Each of them spoke the word of God to me.

> **Hebrews 13:7** Remember your leaders, who spoke the word of God to you. Consider the outcome of their way of life and imitate their faith.

This verse gives us three essential points about leadership in God's kingdom.

---

## 1. Remember the Word (Not Just the Leader)

> **Hebrews 13:7** Remember your leaders, who spoke the word of God to you.

What we remember about our leaders isn't their charisma, their appearance, or the programs they organized. We remember that **they spoke the word of God**.

### The Word Endures

> **Hebrews 13:8** Jesus Christ is the same yesterday and today and forever.

This doesn't mean everything works the same way now as it did 2,000 years ago—Jesus isn't physically on earth anymore. But it means the *message* remains unchanged. The moral will of God, His word to us, is eternally consistent.

> **Psalm 119:89** Your word, LORD, is eternal; it stands firm in the heavens.

> **1 Peter 1:25** But the word of the Lord endures forever. And this is the word that was preached to you.

### Leaders Come and Go

When Hebrews was written, the original leaders had likely been persecuted, some even martyred. Doubt filled the community. Perhaps some leaders had recanted their faith and fallen away. But the message remained: persecution doesn't change the word of God.

At my church, we recently experienced a change in senior leadership because unfortunately our former minister's life did not reflect the word of God. This teaches us something vital: **we judge leaders by whether they align with God's word; we don't judge God's word by the leader.**

> **Hebrews 13:9** Do not be carried away by all kinds of strange teachings.

False teachers—even charismatic ones—lead people away from Christ. The Hebrews faced Judaistic teachers trying to pull them back to ceremonial laws.

> **Hebrews 13:9** It is good for our hearts to be strengthened by grace, not by eating ceremonial foods, which is of no benefit to those who do so.

### The Word Speaks Louder Than Personalities

In 1 Corinthians, Paul addresses followers who were dividing over personalities:

> **1 Corinthians 1:12-17** One of you says, "I follow Paul"; another, "I follow Apollos"; another, "I follow Cephas"; still another, "I follow Christ." Is Christ divided? Was Paul crucified for you? Were you baptized in the name of Paul? ... For Christ did not send me to baptize, but to preach the gospel—not with wisdom and eloquence, lest the cross of Christ be emptied of its power.

Sometimes we get caught up in personality clashes between leaders. We might even lose interest in a sermon because we find it boring and then wonder how we would teach the passage instead. But the call remains the same: focus on the word, not the personality.

### Application: Gratitude and Growth

As followers, we remember leaders *to the extent that they stood firm in God's word*, and we're grateful for them. At graduation, we acknowledge not just the students' achievements, but the teachers who poured into them.

God has chosen to deliver His word through people. When you're in a leadership role, speak God's word. When you're being led, remember God's word. We're all simultaneously leaders and followers—whether formally as pastors, or informally as parents, spouses, or older Christians mentoring younger ones.

> **Ephesians 4:14-15** Then we will no longer be infants, tossed back and forth by the waves, and blown here and there by every wind of teaching and by the cunning and craftiness of people in their deceitful scheming. Instead, speaking the truth in love, we will grow to become in every respect the mature body of him who is the head, that is, Christ.

## 2. Consider the Outcome of Their Way of Life

> **Hebrews 13:7** Consider the outcome of their way of life and imitate their faith.

The word "outcome" means the trajectory—where a person's life actually lands. While the word of God is primary, the *outcome* of a leader's life matters too.

Think of a nutritionist who is morbidly obese, or a financial advisor who is poor. There's a disconnect. Similarly, the Old Testament gives us a test for prophets:

> **Deuteronomy 18:20-22** A prophet who presumes to speak in my name anything I have not commanded, or a prophet who speaks in the name of other gods, is to be put to death. You may say to yourselves, "How can we know when a message has not been spoken by the Lord?" If what a prophet proclaims in the name of the Lord does not take place or come true, that is a message the Lord has not spoken.

There must be correlation between what leaders *say* and what they *do*.

### Leaders Eat Last

> **Hebrews 13:10** We have an altar from which those who minister at the tabernacle have no right to eat.

This appears to mean that church leaders sacrifice their own comfort and provision. Leaders eat last.

Research on organizational success reveals something profound: the most successful teams, families, and businesses share a common trait—their leaders create a safe environment by sacrificing for others. When a leader gives time and energy generously, oxytocin (a chemical associated with safety and bonding) is released in their body *and* in those around them. Witnessing human generosity naturally triggers generosity in return.

**Leadership isn't a rank or position. It's a choice. It's deciding to serve the people on your right and your left.**

### The Ultimate Example: Jesus

> **Hebrews 13:11-12** The high priest carries the blood of animals into the Most Holy Place as a sin offering, but the bodies are burned outside the camp. And so Jesus also suffered outside the city gate to make the people holy through his own blood.

In the Old Testament sacrificial system, the blood of the animal cleansed sin, but the carcass had to be taken outside the city and burned completely—as if the sin remained with it, making it unclean.

> **Leviticus 6:30** Any sin offering whose blood is brought into the tent of meeting to make atonement in the Holy Place must not be eaten; it must be burned up.

Jesus was taken outside Jerusalem's walls—outside the camp—and executed like a criminal. The writer looks at us and says:

> **Hebrews 13:13-14** Let us, then, go to him outside the camp, bearing the disgrace he bore. For here we do not have an enduring city, but we are looking for the city that is to come.

Jesus Himself said in Luke 9:23: "If anyone would come after me, let him deny himself and take up his cross daily and follow me." **Being a Christian means going with Jesus outside the camp—accepting risk, potential uncleanness, and discomfort.**

The camp represented safety, sanctity, and cleanliness. Outside were dangers, unclean things, and exposure. But leaders leave the safety of the camp. They take the risk. They sacrifice themselves for others.

### Identifying True Leadership

How do you identify a true leader? When there's a problem and no one acts, the leader says, "I'll fix it." Not "Someone really should do something about that"—that's a consultant. That person makes noise, writes a report, and leaves.

Picture a dirty toilet at church. If you think, *That's terrible; I hope someone fixes it*, you're a follower (nothing wrong with that). If you grab toilet paper and clean it yourself, you have the qualities of a leader.

> **Mark 10:45** Jesus called them together and said, "You know that those who are regarded as rulers of the Gentiles lord it over them, and their high officials exercise authority over them. Not so with you. Instead, whoever wants to become great among you must be your servant, and whoever wants to be first must be slave of all. For even the Son of Man did not come to be served, but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many."

## 3. Imitate Their Faith

> **Hebrews 13:17** Have confidence in your leaders and submit to their authority, because they keep watch over you as those who must give an account. Do this so that their work will be a joy, not a burden, for that would be of no benefit to you.

The Greek word here (*peitho*) can mean both "obey" and "be persuaded." We follow leaders not through coercion, but through being convinced of their character and their alignment with God's word.

### The Sacrifice of Praise

> **Hebrews 13:15-16** Through Jesus, therefore, let us continually offer to God a sacrifice of praise—the fruit of lips that openly profess his name. And do not forget to do good and to share with others, for with such sacrifices God is pleased.

"Sacrifice of praise" is sometimes read as singing alone. But in context, after calling us to "go outside the camp" with Jesus and bear His disgrace, this is about *speaking*—openly professing His name, even at cost. The "fruit of lips" is the fruit of the message leaders proclaim. Leaders don't just talk the talk; they walk the walk.

And verse 16 explains verse 15: doing good and sharing with others is the *action* that makes the sacrifice praiseworthy.

**Jesus saw a problem. He came to earth. He underwent suffering and persecution. He ate last.**

And therefore, we follow Him. We imitate the faith of leaders who follow Him.

## Closing

> **Hebrews 13:20-21** Now may the God of peace, who through the blood of the eternal covenant brought back from the dead our Lord Jesus, that great Shepherd of the sheep, equip you with everything good for doing his will, and may he work in us what is pleasing to him, through Jesus Christ, to whom be glory for ever and ever. Amen.

**Leaders lead.** Step forward, degree by degree. Remember the word. Consider the outcomes. Imitate their faith. Become the leader who eats last.
