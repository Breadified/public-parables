# John 6:30-44

**Title:** Jesus as the Bread of Life

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Question of Food and Satisfaction

Food symbolizes so much in our lives—our profession, our career, the way we care for ourselves and others. But food points to something far deeper than mere physical sustenance.

The people Jesus fed were like sheep without a shepherd, hungry and seeking. Yet even after witnessing His miraculous provision, they remained unsatisfied. This reveals a profound truth: physical food alone can never truly satisfy the human soul.

## Jesus: Greater Than Moses

The crowd looked back to Moses and the manna in the wilderness, but Jesus presents Himself as something infinitely greater. Where Moses provided temporary physical bread, Jesus offers the word of salvation—spiritual and eternal feeding.

> He is the true bread that comes down from heaven to give life to the world.

## The Futility of Earthly Satisfaction

Consider how we chase satisfaction in this world:
- We study hard for exams, thinking this will fulfill us
- We pursue money and career advancement
- We look at the rich and think, "If only I had what they have"

Yet satisfaction through earthly means always falls short. The wealthy are no more satisfied than the poor. Material provision can never address the deeper hunger of the human heart.

## The Eternal Feeding

What Jesus offers is categorically different. He calls people to trust in Him as the true source of life—not merely for a day or a season, but for eternity. This is the feeding that satisfies, the bread that sustains the soul forever.
