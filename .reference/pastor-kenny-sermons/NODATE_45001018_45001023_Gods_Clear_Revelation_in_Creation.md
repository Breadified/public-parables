# Romans 1:18-23

**Title:** God's Clear Revelation in Creation
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction

We often find ourselves in a world awash with speculation and uncertainty. As we start this year, consider how our society treats God much like we treat UFO sightings—based on personal experiences, lacking concrete evidence, leaving everyone uncertain about whether God is truly real.

But is this really the case? What does Scripture tell us about the evidence for God's existence and our ability to know Him with certainty?

This series explores the core foundations of Christian faith. Today, we begin at creation itself—the foundational testimony to who God is.

## Point 1: Clear Evidence

> For since the creation of the world God's invisible qualities—his eternal power and divine nature—have been clearly seen, being understood from what has been made. (Romans 1:19-20)

God deliberately and intentionally reveals Himself to humanity. Unlike the hidden deity of popular imagination—think of the Wizard of Oz hidden behind a curtain—the God of Scripture is one who actively makes Himself known. He is not distant or impersonal, but a God who chooses to reveal Himself.

### His Eternal Power

Creation testifies to the power of its Creator. The psalmist captures this beautifully:

> The heavens declare the glory of God; the skies proclaim the work of his hands. Day after day they pour forth speech; night after night they reveal knowledge. (Psalm 19:1-3)

When we observe the endless expanse of the sky, the deep valleys carved between great mountains, the intricate complexity of human anatomy, the ordered laws of science and mathematics—all of this points unmistakably to the power of the Creator. The fact that this creative power has sustained itself since the beginning of time demonstrates that the Creator's power is eternal.

### His Divine Nature

Alongside creation's testimony to God's power comes the revelation of His divine nature—His "God-ness." Because we recognize creation's glory and the power it reflects, we are forced to acknowledge that no human or creature could accomplish such work. Whoever made all of this must be unlike any other being. He must stand alone. He alone is God—the one to whom all creation answers.

Job learned this when he questioned God, and God responded:

> Where were you when I laid the earth's foundation? Tell me, if you understand. Who marked off its dimensions? Surely you know! Who stretched a measuring line across it? ... Who shut up the sea behind doors when it burst forth from the womb, when I made the clouds its garment and wrapped it in thick darkness, when I fixed limits for it and set its doors and bars in place? (Job 38:4-10)

What human being was present at the world's foundation? What human can command the sea or govern the boundaries of creation? Only God possesses such power. The very nature of creation's design declares God's uniqueness and divinity. He alone is worthy of our praise and recognition.

### Application: A Logical Conclusion

Christians are often accused of being superstitious and illogical for believing in a Creator God. Yet the opposite is true.

Consider the atheistic worldview: everything in the universe emerged from nothing and randomly organized into the complex laws of nature and science we observe today. Complex realities like human consciousness and DNA point to random chance rather than intelligent design. But this worldview leads to an unsustainable conclusion—if everything is merely the product of cosmic accident, then nothing matters. All becomes nihilism.

Yet no one actually lives this way. Even the most committed atheist will embrace their family, enjoy a sunset, find meaning in beauty. The reason is simple: we cannot escape the logic that design demands a designer. When we look at creation, there is only one rational conclusion—not random chance, but deliberate, intelligent design.

A Creator God is the most logical explanation for the universe's origins. God is plainly evident in His creation.

## Point 2: Without Excuse

> For since the creation of the world God's invisible qualities—his eternal power and divine nature—have been clearly seen, being understood from what has been made, so that people are without excuse. (Romans 1:20)

God has given humanity clear evidence of His existence, power, and authority. This means we cannot claim ignorance. On that day when all stand before God, many will attempt to use ignorance as a defense: "God, I didn't know," or "Why didn't You give me more clues?" Some will even say, "God, I would have been Your best follower if only I'd known You existed!"

But this defense cannot stand, because the evidence is unmistakable.

### Illustration: Border Security and Obvious Signs

Years ago, there was a television show called *Border Security* that followed customs officers at Australian airports. In nearly every episode, some traveler would attempt to smuggle undeclared items—dried meat, vegetables with insects, or other biohazards. When caught, they'd always offer the same excuse: "I didn't know."

But their defense never worked, because as soon as they protested, the camera would pan to the wall displaying clear, unmistakable signs and instruction sheets specifying exactly what could and couldn't be brought into Australia. The evidence was everywhere. There was no room to plead ignorance.

In the same way, humanity cannot claim ignorance of God's existence. The signs are everywhere in creation. We have sufficient evidence to recognize God exists and that we are accountable to Him. We have no excuse.

### Application: Universal Knowledge

Even among Christians, we sometimes wonder if God is being unfair in His revelation. A common question arises: What about isolated tribes in the Amazon rainforest with no access to civilization or the Bible? How can God hold them accountable? Isn't it unreasonable?

But this misses the point. The signs are there for all peoples. If God has made Himself clearly visible through creation, then the excuse of ignorance fails. It's not that some societies have been given the chance to believe in a Divine Creator while others haven't. Every culture can look at the world and recognize evidence of a divine Creator. All have received knowledge of God through creation.

God is not being unreasonable. We have no excuse for failing to acknowledge Him and seek Him.

## Point 3: Choosing to Ignore

If God is so clearly revealed that humans have no excuse, why do so many reject Him? Why do different religions worship different gods?

The answer is not ignorance—it is willful ignoring.

> For although they knew God, they neither glorified him as God nor gave thanks to him, but their thinking became futile and their foolish hearts were darkened. (Romans 1:21)

Humans are not ignorant of God's existence. Rather, we know He exists and we do not like it. We reject the reality that God is ruler of our lives. We prefer to live as though He isn't there so we can do as we please. This rejection originates in our wickedness—we suppress truth through our sinfulness.

This heart condition traces back to humanity's beginning. Adam and Eve walked with God in Eden, experiencing His presence in ways we cannot fathom. Yet when God gave them a simple instruction—eat from any tree except one—they ignored Him. They listened to the serpent's deception and rebelled against their Creator. This first act of rebellion brought sin into the world.

Sin, biblically speaking, is precisely this: ignoring God as the rightful ruler of our lives and creation. Since Adam and Eve, all humanity has inherited this tendency to suppress truth about God and refuse to acknowledge His rightful authority.

### The Trap of False Gods

When we reject God, we create a vacuum. Because we deep down know there is a God, we inevitably construct our own idols to fill that space.

> Although they claimed to be wise, they became fools and exchanged the glory of the immortal God for images made to look like a mortal human being and birds and animals and reptiles. (Romans 1:22-23)

Adam and Eve did this first by heeding the serpent's voice—worshiping creation instead of the Creator. Now, though creation should point us to God's glory, we instead treat creation itself as God.

This plays out literally in many world religions. Visit a Thai restaurant and you'll see elephant gods, monkey gods, half-human hybrid deities adorning the walls. But the pattern extends beyond literal idols.

We are extraordinarily skilled at creating false gods in our own image—gods of money, career success, wealth, social status, and personal comfort. We even create gods from our human intellect: gods of science, technology, philosophy, and social progress.

Everyone worships something. The question is not whether, but which. If it's not the Creator God, then it's a false god of our own making—a powerless substitute we imagine will allow us to live as we please.

### Application: Knowledge vs. Will

If Scripture speaks truth here, then for anyone who doesn't believe in God, the problem isn't that you don't *know* God exists—it's that you don't *like* the God you know exists.

Perhaps acknowledging God means accepting accountability to your Creator rather than living freely as you wish. Perhaps it means surrendering the gods you've worshiped—money, career, status, comfort—and you're unwilling to let those go.

But here's the truth: everyone worships something. Either it's the God who created the world, or it's a cheap imitation we've constructed to replace Him and fill the void of the true God we've chosen to ignore.

Yet it doesn't have to be this way. God desires for you to know Him—not merely to know that He exists, but to have a genuine personal relationship with Him.

God revealed Himself again in history two thousand years ago, in a way far clearer than creation reveals Him. God came in the flesh as Jesus Christ, the full image of the invisible God—100% God incarnate.

In love, Jesus died on the cross to pay the price for our rebellion, our sin, our rejection of Him. He did this so our broken relationship with our Creator could be restored and renewed, if we trust in what He accomplished for us.

It's not too late. Even if you've suppressed the revelation of God in creation your entire life, even if you've knowingly worshiped false gods, God still loves you. He has revealed Himself to you through Jesus Christ, whose story we find in Scripture. He invites you to know Him.

## Conclusion

While we lack sufficient evidence to confirm extraterrestrial life, the evidence for God's existence is plainly seen by all. Creation bears an unceasing testimony to God's eternal power and divine nature, leaving humanity without excuse.

The choice before us is clear: Will we continue to ignore God, worshiping creation rather than Creator? Or will we choose to know Him through Jesus Christ?
