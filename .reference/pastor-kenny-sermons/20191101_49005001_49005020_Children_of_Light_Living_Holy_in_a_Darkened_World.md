# Ephesians 5:1-20

**Title:** Children of Light: Living Holy in a Darkened World
**Date:** 10 November 2019
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## A Crisis of Immorality

We live in a world shadowed by a crisis of sexual immorality. The Royal Commission into Institutional Child Sexual Abuse, which began in 2012 and concluded six months ago, revealed the depth of this darkness. Over 16,000 individuals came forward, sharing 8,000 personal stories of abuse.

The findings are sobering: 25% of complainants (2,186) came from schools, and of those, 75% (1,639) were from religious schools. Even more startling, half of all complainants (4,000) reported abuse within religious institutions—churches, Christian schools, and faith-based organizations.

This is not a problem we can dismiss or blame on secular society alone. The church itself has been implicated. Young men came to the church seeking guidance and help, only to encounter predators. Reports surfaced but were dismissed as mere hints, whispers not worth acting upon. We wanted to be accepting, to affirm rather than condemn. We failed to protect the vulnerable.

## 1. Darkness

> **For of this you can be sure: No immoral, impure or greedy person—such a person is an idolater—has any inheritance in the kingdom of Christ and of God.** (Ephesians 5:5)

Paul describes this world of darkness in verse 5, naming three sins that characterize those separated from God's kingdom:

**Immorality** (*porneia* in Greek) encompasses all sexual activity outside traditional marriage—premarital relations, adultery, prostitution, bestiality, and homosexual practice. It is the totality of sexual transgression.

**Impurity** speaks of uncleanliness—a defiled and corrupted state that extends beyond the physical act.

**Greed** or covetousness is the endless craving for more, the lust that never satisfies. Think of someone at an all-you-can-eat buffet, always reaching for more, never content.

### The Link to Idolatry

Paul makes a striking connection: these sins are not merely moral failures—they are *idolatry*. When we engage in sexual immorality, impurity, or greed, we are worshiping false gods.

> **Jeremiah 3:6** — "Have you seen what faithless Israel has done? She has gone up on every high hill and under every spreading tree and has committed adultery there."

> **Isaiah 57:3-8** — "You burn with lust among the oaks and under every spreading tree... Behind your doors and your doorposts you have put your pagan symbols. Forsaking me, you uncovered your bed..."

There is a direct connection between idolatry and sexual immorality. Some try to argue that biblical prohibitions against homosexuality only apply in the context of temple prostitution and idolatry, not to the practice itself. But this reasoning fails. Yes, there is a link between adultery and idolatry—but this does not mean there is "good adultery" and "bad adultery." There is a link between child sacrifice and idolatry—but we do not distinguish between acceptable and unacceptable forms of child sacrifice.

The practice of sexual immorality leads to idolatry, or idolatry leads to sexual immorality. They are intertwined.

> **Romans 1:22-27** — "Although they claimed to be wise, they became fools and exchanged the glory of the immortal God for images... Therefore God gave them over in the sinful desires of their hearts to sexual impurity... They exchanged the truth about God for a lie, and worshiped and served created things rather than the Creator... Because of this, God gave them over to shameful lusts... Men committed shameful acts with other men, and received in themselves the due penalty for their error."

The idolatry of today is the worship of self—the idolizing of the human body, the worship of power, money, pleasure, and popularity. Modern idolatry is the pursuit of self-satisfaction and short-term gratification at any cost.

### A Word of Grace for the Struggling

I must pause here and speak an important truth: these condemnations are not directed at Christians who struggle with same-sex attraction or other temptations. Many faithful believers of both genders struggle to varying degrees with unwanted desires.

We are all on an infinite spectrum of sin, because while there is one way to live according to God's standard, there are infinite ways to deviate from it. The Bible speaks judgment for all of us—we can act out sin in many ways and think sin in countless others.

But for the Christian—the one who is repentant and desires to live God's way—there is good news.

> **Follow God's example, therefore, as dearly loved children and walk in the way of love, just as Christ loved us and gave himself up for us as a fragrant offering and sacrifice to God.** (Ephesians 5:1-2)

Our identity is not defined by our struggles. We are not "homosexual-Christians" or "lustful-Christians." We are Christians who struggle with homosexuality, with lust, with temptation. These are not our identities—they are battles we face.

The condemnation is reserved for the unrepentant—those who have deliberately chosen this lifestyle despite warnings, the activists who encourage others to join in the corruption, those who silence the voices of warning, and the enablers who stand and applaud while darkness spreads.

The idolatrous person demands endless sacrifice: sacrifice of true sexuality, sacrifice of children, sacrifice of everything good. These false gods demand compulsive, unlimited devotion rooted in fear. For the sexually active person, each month brings anxiety—"Did I contract something this time?" The morning after brings shame and being used. A pregnancy becomes a sacrifice on the altar of the sex god—that relentless deity that keeps demanding and demanding.

But Jesus offers something different:

> **He gave himself up for us as a fragrant offering and sacrifice to God.**

Jesus says: stop the compulsive activities, the obsessions, the fixations, the fetishes, the fear of missing out. He will fix this with His blood. We struggle with the old self, but we are different now. Our identity is this: we are **dearly loved children** of God, set apart for His purposes.

## 2. Light

> **For you were once darkness, but now you are light in the Lord. Live as children of light** (Ephesians 5:8)

We are no longer of darkness. We are children of light.

Instead of immorality, impurity, and lust, the fruit of the light is **goodness, righteousness, and truth** (v.9). Our calling is to discover what pleases the Lord and walk in it.

### The Holiness Standard

> **But among you there must not be even a hint of sexual immorality, or of any kind of impurity, or of greed, because these are improper for God's holy people.** (Ephesians 5:3)

We are set apart—*holy*—and our standards must reflect that separation. This phrase—"not even a hint"—does not mean secrecy. It means we live so purely, so carefully, that there would be no occasion for anyone observing us to question our integrity.

An older man talking to a child in a closed room might be perfectly innocent—a teacher, an interview, seeking privacy from noise. But today, because of past abuse, we do not do such things anymore, even when innocent, because there must not be a hint. We have learned to be extra cautious.

Dating couples should ask themselves: Would anyone observing us wonder if something inappropriate is happening? For dating relationships, my counsel includes some radical thoughts:

- **Don't hang out at each other's homes or rooms without a chaperone.** Meet in public—a food court, a café. It is remarkably difficult to fall into sexual immorality in public.
- **Don't date at night when you are tired and self-control is low and no one is watching.** We are not people of darkness. Meet in the morning at a café. Open the Bible together.
- **Don't kiss until your wedding day.** One thing leads to another. Treat each other as brothers and sisters. If you would not kiss your sibling that way, do not kiss your date that way.
- **Keep the dating period short.**

### The Power of Our Speech

> **Nor should there be obscenity, foolish talk or coarse joking, which are out of place, but rather thanksgiving.** (Ephesians 5:4)

This extends to how we speak. We must avoid lewd jokes, sexual innuendo, and coarse banter—especially jokes that mock homosexuality or make light of sexual sin. Such jokes may deeply wound someone struggling silently with these very temptations. We do not joke about matters of sexual sin.

Locker room antics—butt slapping, crude joking—might seem innocent fun, but what message do they send to an outsider, to a child watching, to someone who has been abused? Our speech must reflect our holiness.

Instead of coarse joking, replace it with **thanksgiving**. In the locker room, thank your teammates for a great effort. In conversation, express gratitude for one another. When we meet, a firm handshake becomes a statement: "I am thankful for you." At year-end gatherings, let thanksgiving replace foolish talk. This may sound boring, like something Ned Flanders would say, but imagine the authenticity and brotherly love such speech would create.

### Children of Light: Expose the Darkness

> **Have nothing to do with the fruitless deeds of darkness, but rather expose them. It is shameful even to mention what the disobedient do in secret. But everything exposed by the light becomes visible—and everything that is illuminated becomes a light.** (Ephesians 5:11-13)

Light has remarkable properties. Sunlight disinfects. UV radiation kills pathogens. Mold dies in the sun. A quilt that smells musty becomes fresh again when exposed to light.

This is the Christian's role: **we are the disinfectant**. We are the cleansing agent in a contaminated world. Christ's light shines on us first, and we reflect His glory outward to the world. We expose darkness not through accusation alone, but through living so purely that the contrast becomes undeniable.

> **Wake up, sleeper, rise from the dead, and Christ will shine on you.** (Ephesians 5:14)

## 3. A Different Kind of Party

> **Do not get drunk on wine, which leads to debauchery. Instead, be filled with the Spirit, speaking to one another with psalms, hymns, and songs from the Spirit. Sing and make music from your heart to the Lord, always giving thanks to God the Father for everything, in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ.** (Ephesians 5:18-20)

When the Holy Spirit fills us, our speech becomes God-inspired proclamation. On Pentecost, the disciples were accused of drunkenness because they spoke with such boldness and joy:

> **Acts 2:11-15** — "We hear them declaring the wonders of God in our own tongues!... Some made fun of them and said, 'They have had too much wine.' Then Peter stood up... 'These people are not drunk, as you suppose. It's only nine in the morning!'"

But Peter and the disciples were filled with the Spirit, not wine. Their speech was bold, inspired, and transformative. So too must all our speech be God-inspired.

Instead of the world's parties—fueled by alcohol, sensuality, and hollow pleasure—we gather for something radically different. We gather with psalms, hymns, and songs. We make music from our hearts to the Lord. We give thanks for everything.

### The Root of Our Crisis

The crisis we face today did not emerge suddenly. Several factors converged:

**The absence of fathers.** In the aftermath of two world wars, fathers and grandfathers were simply gone. The void they left was profound.

**The sexual revolution.** When every immorality, impurity, and lust was displayed and celebrated, when marriage itself was denigrated, society failed its children.

**Bombardment of sexual imagery.** Our children are surrounded by sexual images in advertising, television, magazines, and the internet. Fashion trends display less and tighter clothing. Everywhere we turn, we see sex—more and more of it. We are surprised when predators emerge, yet we fail to acknowledge our own lax attitudes and permissive culture.

The church fell into the same trap. Rather than standing as a refuge for struggling young people, we tried to be like the world. Young men came seeking guidance during their hardest years—the teenage struggle to discover who they are. If surrounded by superheroes, they imagine becoming one. If surrounded by sexual immorality, those become the options they see. The church should have provided a different vision, different examples.

Yet older men—spiritual leaders—failed to set good moral examples. We failed to guide with our behavior, our words, our attitudes, and how we treated our sisters in Christ.

And now another sexual revolution is unfolding. Many churches across the world are falling for it again. In our rush to be politically correct, we are now affirming sin itself. There will be consequences. Another generation will be abused—perhaps not sexually, but surgically.

## The Call to Holiness

> **Be very careful, then, how you live—not as unwise but as wise, making the most of every opportunity, because the days are evil. Therefore do not be foolish, but understand what the Lord's will is.** (Ephesians 5:15-17)

The Lord's will is stated clearly: **not even a hint**. Our holiness must be so transparent that there could be no question about our purity.

We must get serious. If your television, Netflix, or social media feeds contain even a hint of sexual content, remove them. If your dating relationships contain even a whisper of impropriety, restructure them. If your speech contains even an innuendo of lust, change it.

The darkness is real. The abuse was real. But we are children of light, called to shine brilliantly in a darkened world.
