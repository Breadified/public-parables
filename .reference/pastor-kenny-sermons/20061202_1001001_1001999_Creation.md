# Genesis 1

**Title:** Creation  
**Date:** December 2, 2006  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## God's Power, Order, and Love

Genesis 1 addresses far more important questions than the details of how creation occurred. While we may have questions about the mechanism of creation, God is interested in revealing something deeper about himself. Genesis 1 tells us three fundamental truths about God:

1. God is powerful
2. God made creation perfect
3. God loves mankind

## One Powerful God

> In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth. (Genesis 1:1)

God is immediately established as the sovereign creator of all things—the heavens and the earth, everything. This was a crucial message for Israel as they emerged from Egypt into a land surrounded by pagan religions.

The neighboring cultures told radically different creation stories. In their mythology, multiple gods existed in the heavens and warred with one another. As the gods died in these cosmic battles, the victors took the corpses of the defeated and used their body parts to fashion the earth and humanity. Creation was built from death and divine conflict.

Genesis presents a stark contrast: there is only one God. He does not compete or fight. He requires no raw materials—no dead bodies, no cosmic debris. He speaks, and creation exists. He creates from nothing.

This God alone holds power. Where other religions attributed divine power to the sun and moon, Genesis identifies them as merely "the greater and lesser lights" (Genesis 1:16)—created objects under God's rule, possessing no independent power of their own. Creation has no authority; only God does.

### Application for Today

We face the same temptation the ancient Israelites did. We are drawn to worship false gods—the god of money, the god of power, the god of success—and we live as though they control our destinies more than the Christian God.

Genesis 1 declares that the Christian God alone is supreme. The other gods exist only in our imagination.

We also surrender to the created order when we should trust only God. Some seek guidance in the stars through astrology; others fear or covet numbers, believing they determine our fate. As Chinese people, we may avoid the number 4 or pursue 8, trusting that numbers hold our future. But Genesis teaches that God, not numbers or stars, controls what comes to pass. Horoscopes and astrological predictions are merely superstition.

This is liberating news. Around the world, people live in fear of the unknown, turning to tea leaves, palm reading, tarot cards, and feng shui for security. But the Christian is freed from such bondage. We are in relationship with our Father and Creator. We need not fear the future—we already know it. Our future is eternal relationship with God, and this truth determines how we live today, whether our earthly circumstances are pleasant or filled with suffering.

## God of Perfect Order

The second great truth Genesis 1 reveals is that God created an ordered and perfect world.

Several features demonstrate this deliberate ordering:

**The Literary Structure**
The narrative itself is carefully constructed. The repeated phrases "And God said," "and it was," and the marking of "the first day," "the second day," and so on create a rhythmic, orderly account of creation itself.

**Repeated Affirmation**
The word "good" appears throughout the account, emphasizing that everything God made was inherently good.

**Symbolic Completeness**
In Jewish literature, the number seven represents wholeness and completion. Genesis 1 is saturated with sevens: seven days of creation, seven words in the opening sentence, and God is mentioned thirty-five times (7 × 5). These numerical patterns underscore that creation is complete and perfect.

**The Architectural Pattern**
Most compelling is the elegant structure of the six days of creation:

| Days 1-3: Environment | Days 4-6: Inhabitants |
|---|---|
| Day 1: Light and darkness | Day 4: Sun and moon (lights for day/night) |
| Day 2: Waters and sky | Day 5: Fish and birds (fill waters and sky) |
| Day 3: Land and vegetation | Day 6: Animals and humanity (fill the land) |

God first prepares the spaces, then populates them with inhabitants perfectly suited to each domain. This is not randomness; this is intelligent design.

### Historical Context

The pagan religions surrounding Israel taught that the world was inherently chaotic—born from divine war and assembled from leftover cosmic debris. But Genesis declares that our universe is the product of one intelligent mind. It is ordered, purposeful, and good.

### The Problem of Evil

Some might ask: If God created a perfect world, why do we see war, famine, disease, and suffering?

The answer lies not with God but with humanity. God created a beautiful, perfect creation, but Adam—mankind—chose rebellion. Within just a few chapters, Adam lies to God and breaks his covenant. As a result, the entire creation falls under a curse. Suffering and hardship enter the world. The perfect creation becomes marred by human sin.

Yet God does not abandon us in our misery. He provides a way of salvation—but before we explore that, we must understand the third truth Genesis 1 proclaims: that God loves mankind.

## God Loves Mankind

The contrast between Genesis and the surrounding religions is most striking here.

The pagan stories claim that humanity was either:
- Created from the corpses of defeated gods (a shameful origin), or
- Fashioned as an afterthought—a minor creation made to serve the gods

But Genesis declares something radically different:

> Then God said, "Let us make man in our image, in our likeness, and let them rule over the fish of the sea and the birds of the air, over the livestock, over all the earth, and over all the creatures that move along the ground." So God created man in his own image, in the image of God he created him; male and female he created them. (Genesis 1:26-27)

Humanity is made in God's very image. We are not slaves to the gods; we are God's representatives on earth. We are commissioned to exercise careful stewardship over creation and to carry out God's purposes. We are, in a sense, God's prime ministers.

The sixth day—when humanity is created—stands out as the climax of creation. The narrative is extended and given special attention. God doesn't simply command "Let there be man" as with other creatures. Instead, he says, "Let us make man in our image." This elevation reveals the dignity and honor God bestows upon humanity.

Moreover, God provides for humanity:

> Then God said, "I give you every seed-bearing plant on the face of the whole earth and every tree that has fruit with seed in it. They will be yours for food." (Genesis 1:29)

God serves man by providing abundance. This demonstrates his deep love and care.

### Application: Sin and Redemption

But here we encounter the tragedy. Humanity, despite being given honor, power, and extraordinary gifts, rebels against God. We choose to rule the world according to our own selfishness and greed rather than according to God's love and generosity. We refuse the role God designed for us.

The curse that fell on creation affects us directly. It brings death, famine, and disease. But beyond the physical realm, it corrupts our relationships—with one another and with God. What was once "very good" is now broken. Good relationships become cursed relationships. The world is imperfect because we are sinners.

This is unbearably sad. Yet we are not left without hope.

### The Hope of Christ

God has provided a way through Jesus Christ. He is the only man who lived in complete obedience to God and ruled the earth properly. He even commands the seas to be silent, demonstrating his authority over creation.

More than this, Jesus loves us so deeply that he died on the cross to pay the penalty for our sin. He rose from the dead, conquering death itself. And he promises to return one day to judge the living and the dead. After that judgment, he will bring a new heavens and a new earth—a new creation to replace the old corrupted one.

This new creation will have no evil. It will not decay. It will have no wars, no diseases, no suffering. This is the Christian's future—not something we must guess or achieve, but something God promises to accomplish.

God has kept his original love for mankind, even though he was not obligated to do so. He demonstrates this love in the most powerful way possible:

> God demonstrates his love for us in this: While we were still sinners, Christ died for us. (Romans 5:8)

## Conclusion

Genesis 1 reveals three eternal truths about God:

1. **God is powerful**—the only God, sovereign over all creation
2. **God made creation perfect**—ordered, purposeful, and good
3. **God loves mankind**—created in his image, honored with dignity and purpose

God has been faithful to us and has saved us from the wrath we deserved. Let us respond by placing our trust in his perfect representative, Jesus the Son of God, and by living in obedience to him.
