# 1 Peter 1:1–2:3

**Title:** Alien Born

## Introduction: Birthplace and Privilege

Where you are born determines what privileges you inherit. I was born in Ipoh, Malaysia—a former mining town known more for its food than much else. Yet being born there gave me the privilege of sharing a birthplace with the actress Michelle Yeoh. Different lands confer different privileges: only those born in the United States can become President; those born in Hong Kong receive automatic citizenship under the Basic Law, which sparked an influx of mainland Chinese mothers seeking to give birth there for their children's citizenship rights.

Today's passage addresses two crucial realities for Christians: our spiritual birthplace and the extraordinary privileges that come with it.

## Part One: Christians Are Born Into Living Hope

### The Inheritance (1 Peter 1:3-4)

> Praise be to the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ! In his great mercy he has given us new birth into a living hope through the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead, and into an inheritance that can never perish, spoil or fade—kept in heaven for you.

In Scripture, "inheritance" carries a specific meaning: it refers to **land**, not money or temporary blessings. This promise originates with God's covenant to Abraham in Genesis 12 (around 2000 BC). Throughout the Bible, this inheritance language persists—Moses leads the people toward the promised land as their inheritance; the Levites alone receive no inheritance because they are called to serve the temple, not to possess land.

Peter uses this language deliberately. He writes to a people without land and without earthly privileges—scattered throughout Pontus, Galatia, Cappadocia, Asia, and Bithynia, facing persecution in the Roman Empire where citizenship brought tremendous security and power. Yet Peter reassures them: *you have an inheritance; you possess land.*

### A Hope Yet to Be Revealed (1 Peter 1:6-9)

This land is not yet visible. It awaits the final revelation of Christ:

> These have come so that your faith—of greater worth than gold, which perishes even though refined by fire—may be proved genuine and may result in praise, glory and honor when Jesus Christ is revealed. Though you have not seen him, you love him; and even though you do not see him now, you believe in him and are filled with an inexpressible and glorious joy, for you are receiving the goal of your faith, the salvation of your souls.

Revelation 21 shows us this future: a city descending from heaven itself. Abraham never possessed the land of Canaan, yet he purchased a grave site there, believing the promise. Joseph refused the honor of Egyptian burial, insisting his bones be carried into the exodus toward the promised land. Why? Because that land is where God's kingdom will be established and where the resurrection will occur.

**The certainty of this future promise is so absolute that we can speak of our salvation as though we already possess it.** I own my home in Castle Hill, though technically the bank holds the title. No one calls it "the bank's house." If we can be certain of future physical ownership despite economic uncertainty and job insecurity, how much more certain can we be of our spiritual inheritance?

### For Those Who Doubt

Some of us struggle with assurance of salvation. Perhaps you feel frustrated that you haven't sensed or experienced God directly. Yet verses 8-9 normalize this experience:

> Though you have not seen him, you love him; and even though you do not see him now, you believe in him and are filled with an inexpressible and glorious joy.

**This is the ordinary Christian experience:** to love Jesus without having seen him, to believe without sensory proof, to be filled with unexplainable joy even in the absence of emotional intensity.

Others worry because they don't emotionally *feel* God's joy. Yet notice verse 6: the early Christians could "greatly rejoice, though now for a little while you may have had to suffer grief in all kinds of trials." **It is possible to be sad and happy simultaneously**—sad about present troubles, yet joyful about tomorrow's salvation.

We all wrestle with doubt. The call is simply to *believe*. Come to church. Keep meeting with other Christians. This fellowship sustains faith.

### The Inheritance Is Protected (1 Peter 1:4-5)

While we await Christ's return, our inheritance is secure:

> An inheritance that can never perish, spoil or fade—kept in heaven for you, who through faith are shielded by God's power until the coming of the salvation that is ready to be revealed in the last time.

There is no inflation to erode its value. No thief can steal it. No sun can fade it. No obsolescence can diminish it. It will remain untouched by global warming, earthquakes, floods, or any earthly catastrophe. Unlike Bill Gates's children—who will inherit nothing because their father has pledged his $58 billion to charity—our inheritance from our Father in heaven cannot be given away.

## Part Two: Christians Are Born From the Living Word

### The Mystery: How Can Earthlings Inherit Heaven?

We are born of the earth, yet we inherit a heavenly land. We are aliens to heaven by birth, yet somehow we inherit it. The answer is this: **we are born not of this earth, but of the living word of God.**

> For you have been born again, not of perishable seed, but of imperishable, through the living and enduring word of God. (1 Peter 1:23)

The word of God is:
- **Imperishable** (not perishable)
- **Living** (not dead)
- **Enduring** (not temporary)

### The Living Word in the Past (1 Peter 1:10-12)

> Concerning this salvation, the prophets, who spoke of the grace that was to come to you, searched intently and with the greatest care, trying to find out the time and circumstances to which the Spirit of Christ in them was pointing when he predicted the sufferings of Christ and the glories that would follow. It was revealed to them that they were not serving themselves but you, when they spoke of the things that have now been told you by those who have preached the gospel to you by the Holy Spirit sent from heaven. Even angels long to look into these things.

The prophets spoke God's word in ages past. They didn't know the name of Jesus, yet what they spoke pointed directly to him. They predicted his sufferings, his redemptive blood, and his resurrection. The Spirit that indwelt them was always pointing toward Christ. Their words were never irrelevant—they were always intended for us, always pointing to God's ultimate plan in history.

### The Living Word in the Present (1 Peter 1:18-21, 25)

> For you know that it was not with perishable things such as silver or gold that you were redeemed from the empty way of life handed down to you from your forefathers, but with the precious blood of Christ, a lamb without blemish or defect. He was chosen before the creation of the world, but was revealed in these last times for your sake. Through him you believe in God, who raised him from the dead and glorified him, and so your faith and hope are in God. And this is the word that was preached to you.

If you inherit only perishable things, what's passed down is an empty way of life. But through the precious blood of Christ—chosen before the foundation of the world, yet revealed for our sake in these final days—we inherit the eternal.

We become Christians through various doorways. Some experience a spiritual awakening; others grow up in Christian families; still others encounter the faith through friends or compelling arguments. **Yet the true birth happens through one means: the gospel word.** Whatever our path, we ultimately hear the word of God. We hear that Christ was offered without blemish. We hear that his blood redeems us. We hear that he rose and ascended and that through him our faith rests in God.

**Have you been born of the gospel?** If so, believe that word. The word is imperishable. If you belong to the word, you are imperishable. Death no longer holds you. You are resurrected.

### The Living Word in the Future (1 Peter 1:24-25)

The word will endure forever:

> All men are like grass, and all their glory is like the flowers of the field; the grass withers and the flowers fall, but the word of the Lord stands forever.

#### Do We Believe This?

Two attacks challenge the permanence of God's word in our culture.

**First Attack: Selective Reinterpretation**

The pattern is predictable:
1. Proclaim ignorance—"The passage isn't clear; who knows what it means?"
2. Appeal to non-biblical authorities—cite early church fathers, Calvin, modern research, or cultural trends
3. Announce a "new understanding"—dismiss traditional readings and impose contemporary meanings

We see this clearly in the debate over homosexuality:
1. "The ancient laws are outdated; our culture has moved on. Who really knows what this means?"
2. "Studies show 10% of people are gay, so it must be acceptable. I have a gay friend who's wonderful; it must be permissible."
3. "Therefore, let's ordain gay pastors and redefine marriage."

**The biblical rules for interpretation are simple:**
- Scripture interprets scripture (not external authorities)
- When in doubt, hold to the conservative reading

**Second Attack: Filling the Gaps**

While some deny what Scripture says, others add to it. The Bible doesn't address which subjects to study at university, whom to marry, whether to buy an iPad. We're tempted to fill these silences with extra words from God—visions, dreams, voices.

Yet we must be careful. How do we know that the voice we hear is God's and not our own desire or peer pressure? Some ministries practice "treasure hunts"—small groups pray, receive visions about locations and descriptions, then evangelize the people they find. While evangelism is important, we shouldn't need supernatural words of knowledge to accomplish God's work. The Bible is always sufficient and relevant. If it doesn't answer a question, perhaps we're meant not to know, or meant to discern the answer ourselves.

**Instead of seeking words of knowledge, we need:**
- Better comprehension skills
- A humbler heart
- A repentant heart that recognizes when we twist Scripture to justify our sin
- Wisdom to discern what's already there in the text
- Strength to obey, even when it's hard

**Practical steps:**
- Find someone to read Scripture with you
- Bring your Bible to church and follow along with sermons
- Use physical Bibles at church and study (not just your phone) to minimize distraction
- Bring a pen and notebook to take notes
- Carry your Bible to school
- Study Scripture more regularly and prayerfully, asking God to speak through his word rather than to you outside it

## Part Three: Living as Aliens

> Therefore, rid yourselves of all malice and all deceit, hypocrisy, envy, and slander of every kind. Like newborn babies, crave pure spiritual milk, so that by it you may grow up in your salvation, now that you have tasted that the Lord is good. (1 Peter 2:1-3)

We are newborns in God's family. Like infants, we should crave the pure milk of Scripture—soaking it up, acting upon it, connecting ourselves to God through his word and to one another through fellowship. The hostile, competitive spirit has no place among us; we are not here to advance ourselves but to grow together in salvation.

### The Passport Illustration

When traveling in a foreign country, I carry my passport everywhere. **The Bible is our precious passport.** We are not yet in heaven; we are aliens in a foreign land. Let us keep Scripture close to us—physically present at church and study, mentally engaged with its truths, spiritually submitted to its authority.

---

## Conclusion: Alien Born

**Where are you born?** Peter answers: we are **Alien Born**. We are born not of this earth but of the eternal word. We are born of the blood of Christ.

Some of you face real material pressures: inadequate retirement savings, inability to enter the property market, anxiety about leaving an inheritance for your children. It's okay. **Thank God we are born of the Word of God into an inheritance that can never be destroyed.** That is our true birthright.
