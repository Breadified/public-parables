# John 15:26-16:15

**Title:** This is Love: Love Story

**Date:** 23 April 2023 (ARISE)

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## The Problem: Persecution Coming

Years ago as university students, my friends and I witnessed a motorcycle accident near UTS. We helped and provided witness statements to the authorities. To our shock, the motorcycle owner's insurance company sued us for thousands of dollars in damages—not with a junior solicitor, but with a barrister. We were terrified, just poor second-year students trying to do the right thing. We needed help. We needed a lawyer.

Today, Christians around the world face similar trials. In America, bakers and photographers are sued for declining to participate in events contrary to their conscience. In Australia, Christian organizations, schools, and leaders face legal action and public vilification for holding traditional Christian values. Leaders like Craig Thorburn and Israel Folau have been attacked not for harm they caused, but for their beliefs.

Jesus addresses this suffering directly in this passage. He offers two gifts: first, he explains the reason for persecution; second, he provides the solution.

## 1. Hate (John 15:18-25)

### Why Persecution Happens

> If the world hates you, keep in mind that it hated me first. (15:18)

The world persecuted Jesus. Therefore, it will persecute his followers. This is not accidental or surprising—it is the consequence of being chosen by him.

> I have chosen you out of the world. That is why the world hates you. (15:19)

We belong to Jesus' team, not the world's system. When the world attacks Jesus, it attacks everyone aligned with him.

> If they persecuted me, they will persecute you also. (15:20)

This is not a question of if, but when. We should not expect exemption from what our Master endured.

> They will treat you this way because of my name. (15:21)

The persecution targets our identity as Christians. In our culture, every other identity—race, gender, sexuality—receives protection and celebration. But the name "Christian" has become unacceptable in many circles. They attack us on our faith itself.

Specifically, they hate Jesus' teachings about sexuality, gender, and human dignity—the claim that God created us male and female, that sexuality has purpose within marriage. When they reject these teachings, they reject those who hold them.

### Why This Hate Is Irrational

> They hated me without reason. (15:25)

This persecution is not a rational response. Jesus was, by any secular account, a humble carpenter who dedicated his life to doing good. Yet the religious establishment hunted him down with fury.

Consider: if I were an unbeliever, I would logically want a Christian society. Christians build schools, orphanages, and hospitals. How many private atheist hospitals exist? And if I wanted to pursue my own sexual ethics, wouldn't I want others—at minimum—to maintain stable families, to have children who become nurses, teachers, and caregivers? These are overwhelmingly Christian occupations. Someone must sustain the social infrastructure. Why would you systematically eliminate those willing to bear that burden?

The persecution makes no logical sense. Yet it persists, and will intensify.

> The time is coming when anyone who kills you will think they are offering a service to God. (16:2)

What begins as social pressure escalates. Soft persecution—exclusion from leadership, silencing dissent, mockery, enforced conformity—gradually hardens into violence. History shows this progression. What seems impossible in democracies has already happened and will happen again.

### Soft Persecution in Our Time

In Australia, we experience "soft" persecution—not yet deadly, but still persecution:

- You cannot hold leadership positions in society
- You cannot voice dissent without being attacked
- You are not permitted to have opinions
- Media and culture mock and ridicule your beliefs constantly
- You must teach your children what the state demands, not what you believe
- The pressure builds: "...yet."

If we applied this same standard to race, we would call it what it is: systemic discrimination. Yet somehow, applying it to Christians is acceptable.

We must not be gaslit into thinking this is harmless. Some Christians, exhausted by pressure, have developed a Stockholm syndrome mentality:

> "They don't know what they're doing. They didn't mean it. It was only an accident. They apologized."

But what if it actually is hate? **Jesus certainly thinks it is.**

## 2. Help (John 15:26-16:15)

Facing this persecution, Jesus offers comfort. Our help comes from the Advocate—the Holy Spirit.

### The Advocate's Identity

> When the Advocate comes, whom I will send to you from the Father—the Spirit of truth who goes out from the Father—he will testify about me. (15:26)

The word "Advocate" (paraclete in Greek) means one who speaks or acts on your behalf in your favor. It can mean comforter, encourager, helper, witness—or lawyer. The common thread: someone advocating for you.

When Jesus ascended, he didn't abandon us. Instead, he sent a replacement advocate to continue his work on earth. Jesus intercedes for us in heaven (1 John 2:1; Romans 8:33-34), while the Holy Spirit advocates for us here.

> It is for your good that I am going away. Unless I go away, the Advocate will not come to you. (16:7)

Think of it this way: our main lawyer, Jesus, must leave Sydney to argue our case in the high court of heaven. While he's gone, he sends another advocate to represent us on earth. We now have two advocates—one in heaven, one on earth.

### The Advocate's Work: Truth

The Spirit's tool is truth. Not swords or armies, but the Word of God.

> The Spirit of truth will testify about me. When he comes, he will prove the world to be in the wrong about sin and righteousness and judgment. (15:26, 16:8)

The Spirit exposes truth in three dimensions:

**Sin:** People do not believe in Jesus (16:9). We are all sinners with insufficient faith in God's word.

**Righteousness:** Jesus goes to the Father, and we can see him no longer (16:10). Yet he is the righteous one—death and sin could not contain him. His resurrection proves his righteousness.

**Judgment:** The prince of this world stands condemned (16:11). Those who reject Christ face judgment. Those who repent are judged righteous based on Jesus' blood.

This is the gospel: We are sinners. Jesus is righteous. Which way will you live?

### The Weapon: God's Word

The Spirit works through Scripture. 

> All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness. (2 Timothy 3:16-17)

Scripture is God-spoken, God-spirited, recorded for us so we know how God works in believers.

> The words I have spoken to you are spirit and they are life. (John 6:63)

Jesus' words are not mere information—they are living, life-giving truth that the Spirit internalizes into our hearts.

> Take the helmet of salvation and the sword of the Spirit, which is the word of God. (Ephesians 6:17)

The Spirit's sword cuts through deception and reality itself.

> You also must testify, for you have been with me from the beginning. (15:27)

We are entrusted with the only weapon that truly helps: God's truth in Scripture. We must testify. We must speak it.

### Why Truth Defeats Persecution

When we face persecution, we might pray for an army, for superpowers, for our accusers to disappear. Instead, Jesus offers truth. It seems like a strange weapon—but it is the only one that works:

- **Truth sustains us:** We die for truth, not for lies. Conviction born from truth gives us the resolve to endure.

- **Truth exposes reality:** It prunes us, strips away illusions, prepares us for hardship.

- **Truth builds confidence:** It equips us to withstand persecution with the certainty that we stand on solid ground.

- **Truth converts enemies:** It will convict our accusers of their sin and call them to repentance. The love story of Jesus dying for their sins will melt their resistance. One by one, accusers will change their minds.

### The Story Continues

Years later, my friends and I sat in arbitration facing a QC barrister (charging perhaps $1,000 per hour to recover $5,000—this was about domination, not money). We had no lawyer. We were terrified.

But we had the truth.

I presented it simply: "You did not stop crossing the street. Why would you stand in the middle of the road staring at a high-powered motorcycle? Because it was high-powered. And you had to look."

The judge dismissed the case.

We won.

---

No matter what the world throws at us, we have the Spirit of Truth. We have Jesus with us in spirit. We have been armed with the only weapon that ultimately persuades, converts, and triumphs—the truth of God's word and God's love.
