# Daniel 4-5

**Title:** Heaven Rules! The Real King  
**Date:** July 17, 2011  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## An Ancient Letter of Recommendation

King Nebuchadnezzar opens chapters 4-5 with a remarkable letter to all the peoples, nations, and languages of the world. This is no ordinary communication—it marks a significant shift in Daniel's narrative.

Notice three crucial features of this letter:

First, it's written in the first person, a personal testimony from the king himself. Second, it's addressed to his subjects across the entire known world—all peoples, nations, and men of every language. Third, its placement within Daniel is theologically significant. While chapters 2-7 are written in Aramaic (the international lingua franca of that era), chapters 4-5 form the crucial middle section of this Aramaic portion, positioning it as a message to the Gentile world about God's sovereignty.

> **Daniel 4:1-3**
> 
> King Nebuchadnezzar, To the peoples, nations and men of every language, who live in all the world: May you prosper greatly! It is my pleasure to tell you about the miraculous signs and wonders that the Most High God has performed for me. How great are his signs, how mighty his wonders! His kingdom is an eternal kingdom; his dominion endures from generation to generation.

## Heaven Rules!

This is the king's central message: **Heaven rules!** 

The greatest king of the greatest empire on earth is recommending and praising the Christian God. He acknowledges a kingdom greater than his own, a dominion that supersedes his earthly rule, and a king who is superior to himself.

### God's Sovereignty Over Kings and Kingdoms

How did Nebuchadnezzar come to this realization? Through suffering and humiliation. God gave him a troubling dream—a great tree reaching to heaven that sheltered all creatures, suddenly cut down by a heavenly messenger, leaving only a stump.

Daniel interprets the dream: "You, O king, are that tree." The tree represents Nebuchadnezzar himself, and the judgment represents his coming humiliation.

The dream carries a critical message about divine sovereignty:

> **Daniel 4:17**
> 
> The decision is announced by messengers, the holy ones declare the verdict, so that the living may know that the Most High is sovereign over the kingdoms of men and gives them to anyone he wishes and sets over them the lowliest of men.

For millennia, kings believed in the divine right—that God had mandated their rule permanently. In a sense, they were correct: God does place rulers over nations. But they forgot a crucial truth: what God grants, God can revoke. He installs kings and deposes them with equal ease.

The king must learn that **heaven rules over earth.** When he acknowledges this, his kingdom will be restored:

> **Daniel 4:26**
> 
> The command to leave the stump of the tree with its roots means that your kingdom will be restored to you when you acknowledge that Heaven rules.

Daniel urges Nebuchadnezzar to repent:

> **Daniel 4:27**
> 
> Therefore, O king, be pleased to accept my advice: Renounce your sins by doing what is right, and your wickedness by being kind to the oppressed. It may be that then your prosperity will continue.

### A Perspective on God and Humanity

Compare God to humanity as we compare the heavens to earth. The universe is infinite, expansive, powerful, majestic, and eternal. The Hubble Telescope, gazing at a single spot for eleven days, revealed billions of galaxies spanning 78 billion light-years. By contrast, earth is microscopically tiny—limited, temporary, changing. Its landscapes shift, climates transform, and its inhabitants return to dust.

So it is with humankind. We are nothing compared to God. The God we worship is not the God of temporary earthly concerns but the eternal God of the heavens. This is the acknowledgment Nebuchadnezzar must make.

## Those Who Refuse to Acknowledge Heaven's Rule

### Nebuchadnezzar's Pride and Fall

Twelve months after his dream, Nebuchadnezzar took a walk on his palace roof. Seeing his empire spread before him, he spoke with breathtaking arrogance:

> **Daniel 4:30**
> 
> Is not this the great Babylon I have built as the royal residence, by my mighty power and for the glory of my majesty?

His words contain a fatal truth wrapped in pride: yes, he built the city, amassed the wealth, created the power. But he had forgotten who stands behind all human achievement. He had failed to acknowledge God.

Judgment came immediately:

> **Daniel 4:31-33**
> 
> The words were still on his lips when a voice came from heaven, "This is what is decreed for you, King Nebuchadnezzar: Your royal authority has been taken from you. You will be driven away from people and will live with the wild animals; you will eat grass like cattle. His body was drenched with the dew of heaven until his hair grew like the feathers of an eagle and his nails like the claws of a bird.

The great king was reduced to living like cattle—humiliated, stripped of power, and broken by his own pride.

### Belshazzar's Greater Judgment

Worse judgment falls upon Belshazzar, Nebuchadnezzar's grandson. Where Nebuchadnezzar's sin was self-exaltation, Belshazzar's sin was idolatry. At a great banquet, he and his nobles praised the gods of gold, silver, bronze, iron, wood, and stone—and did so using sacred vessels stolen from the temple in Jerusalem.

In that moment, a disembodied hand appeared and wrote on the palace wall:

> **Daniel 5:5-6**
> 
> Suddenly the fingers of a human hand appeared and wrote on the plaster of the wall, near the lampstand in the royal palace. The king watched the hand as it wrote. His face turned pale, his knees knocked together and his legs gave way.

No amount of gold, silver, or power could save him now. "The writing is on the wall"—a phrase still used today to describe approaching doom.

Daniel refuses the king's rewards and delivers the judgment:

> **Daniel 5:22-24**
> 
> But you, his son, O Belshazzar, have not humbled yourself, though you knew all this. Instead, you have set yourself up against the Lord of heaven. You had the goblets from his temple brought to you, and you and your nobles, your wives and your concubines drank wine from them. You praised the gods of silver and gold, of bronze, iron, wood and stone, which cannot see or hear or understand. But you did not honor the God who holds in his hand your life and all your ways. Therefore he sent the hand that wrote the inscription.

That very night, Belshazzar died, and the kingdom passed to another.

### A Word to Modern Leaders and Nations

Our contemporary leaders praise not the Most High God but the gods of GDP, stock markets, economic growth, and material prosperity. We the people share this idolatry—we trust in wealth, status, possessions, and power. We bow before the gods of gold, silver, and bronze; of fast cars and fine houses; of awards and acclaim.

For those who place their ultimate trust in these things, judgment awaits. The writing is on the wall. No earthly power, no amount of wealth, no human authority can stand against the God who rules heaven.

## Mercy from the Merciful King

Yet God's response is not only judgment. When Nebuchadnezzar finally repented, raising his eyes toward heaven in genuine acknowledgment of God's sovereignty, his sanity was restored:

> **Daniel 4:34-37**
> 
> At the end of that time, I, Nebuchadnezzar, raised my eyes toward heaven, and my sanity was restored. Then I praised the Most High; I honored and glorified him who lives forever. His dominion is an eternal dominion; his kingdom endures from generation to generation. All the peoples of the earth are regarded as nothing. He does as he pleases with the powers of heaven and the peoples of the earth. No one can hold back his hand or say to him: "What have you done?" At the same time that my sanity was restored, my honor and splendor were returned to me for the glory of my kingdom. Now I, Nebuchadnezzar, praise and exalt and glorify the King of heaven, because everything he does is right and all his ways are just. And those who walk in pride he is able to humble.

God restored not only the king's mind but his throne and glory—greater than before. This merciful restoration points to Jesus Christ, the true King of Heaven.

> **Luke 1:29-33**
> 
> Mary was greatly troubled at his words and wondered what kind of greeting this might be. But the angel said to her, "Do not be afraid, Mary, you have found favor with God. You will be with child and give birth to a son, and you are to give him the name Jesus. He will be great and will be called the Son of the Most High. The Lord God will give him the throne of his father David, and he will reign over the house of Jacob forever; his kingdom will never end.

Unlike earthly kings who demand to be served and exalted, Jesus came to serve. He offers mercy to all who repent and acknowledge His kingship.

### The Call to Repentance

To those who feel powerless before unjust laws, corrupt leaders, and immoral systems: do not despair. God is greater. Trust Him rather than the kingdoms of earth.

To those in positions of leadership and power: remember that your authority exists only by God's permission. Your power is not your own. Your wealth is not yours to claim as ultimate treasure. You are not as important as you believe. Lead with humility, always subordinate to King Jesus.

To everyone: humble yourself before the King of Kings. Repent before He humbles you. That is the king's recommendation.
