# Romans 8:34-39

**Title:** A Pairing That Cannot Be Separated  
**Date:** September 2, 2023  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## The Perfect Pairing

Some things in life simply belong together. Bread and cheese. Peanut butter and jam. Bacon and eggs. Great chefs understand this—they know which flavors complement one another to create something beautiful and new.

As the character Remy says in Ratatouille, "Each flavor was totally unique. But, combine one flavor with another, and something new was created!"

This principle applies to more than just food. Some pairings are meant to go together—like Marcus and Cindy, who met at Pennant Hills Station and discovered they were perfectly matched for one another. They should not be separated.

In the same way, there is another pairing that should never be separated: humanity and the love of God.

## The Pairing That Was Broken

We were created in relationship with our Creator. In the garden of Eden, God walked with humanity in fellowship. We were designed for compatibility with Him—a Father and His children, made to enjoy one another's company.

But something happened. Sin entered the world.

When Adam and Eve disobeyed God in the garden, they symbolically rejected their role as creation under the Creator. They denied the compatibility that God had intended. This act of rebellion created a separation—not of distance, but of relationship.

Consider a married couple who lies in the same bed yet hates one another because of wrongs committed. They may be physically close, yet relationally as far apart as the moon. Sin works the same way between us and God.

> **Romans 8:34-39**  
> "Who then is the one who condemns? No one. Christ Jesus who died—more than that, who was raised to life—is at the right hand of God and is also interceding for us."

### The Nature of Sin's Separation

This separation is tragic. While a couple might be separated for only two weeks, as Marcus and Cindy were, the separation between sinful humanity and God is infinite. It is separation by infinite height and infinite depth—seemingly unbridgeable.

This raises a difficult question: If I have not committed infinite sin, why do I deserve infinite judgment?

The answer reveals the gravity of sin: it is not the *amount* of sin that matters, but *who* we sin against. We sin against an infinite God. When finite creatures rebel against the infinite Creator, the weight of that offense is infinite. What appeared to be a perfect pairing becomes seemingly incompatible.

## The Solution: Jesus Christ

But God does not want this separation to continue. He has provided a solution.

> **Romans 8:34**  
> "Christ Jesus who died—more than that, who was raised to life—is at the right hand of God and is also interceding for us."

Consider the claims of Jesus. He was a real person, the wisest teacher who ever lived. His ethics and teachings have shaped the laws and values of civilizations for two thousand years. And He claimed to be God Himself.

God showed up two thousand years ago—at Christmas, in 33 AD—in the person of Jesus Christ.

### What He Did for Us

**He died.** Jesus offered Himself as a sacrifice, taking upon Himself the punishment we deserve. This is sacrificial love—God Himself giving His life to bear the consequences of our sin. True forgiveness means taking the wrong against you and, instead of demanding judgment, absorbing that hurt into yourself.

**He was raised to life.** Because Jesus is the infinite God, He was able to swallow the hurt, pain, and death itself. He defeated death once and for all, breaking its power over us.

**He intercedes for us.** Through His sacrifice, Jesus now stands before God the Father, pleading on our behalf. When we repent and ask for forgiveness, Jesus pleads to God to forgive us—not just for our past sins, but for our future sins as well. His payment covers our debts.

### Restored Compatibility

As a result of Christ's work, we are brought back together with God. We are compatible with Him once again. The pairing that sin had broken is restored.

I like to think that what truly binds Marcus and Cindy together is not mere compatibility, but the fact that they are both people forgiven by God through Christ. Something greater than shared interests binds them—their shared redemption. They met and grew to know one another better as Christians, serving together at church.

In the same way, we have been brought together with God. The result is inseparability.

## Nothing Can Separate Us

> **Romans 8:35-39**  
> "Who shall separate us from the love of Christ? Shall trouble or hardship or persecution or famine or nakedness or danger or sword?... No, in all these things we are more than conquerors through him who loved us. For I am convinced that neither death nor life, neither angels nor demons, neither the present nor the future, nor any powers, neither height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God that is in Christ Jesus our Lord."

Paul lists the things that might seem to threaten our relationship with God:

- **Trouble and hardship** – life's difficulties
- **Persecution** – opposition for our faith
- **Famine** – deprivation and lack
- **Nakedness** – shame and exposure
- **Danger and sword** – physical threats and death

It is worth noting that Christians are the most persecuted people in the world today. Most Christians live not in Western nations but in Africa, Asia, and the Middle East, facing genuine hardship for their faith.

Yet none of these can separate us from God's love.

Paul goes even further:

- **Death nor life** – nothing in the span of our existence
- **Angels nor demons** – neither heavenly nor infernal powers
- **Present nor future** – no matter what time brings
- **Any powers** – no authority or force
- **Height nor depth** – you cannot fly to Hawaii or the moon to escape God
- **Height nor depth** – you cannot dive to the Mariana Trenches or dig to the earth's core to hide from God
- **Anything else in all creation** – nothing else exists that could separate us

This is the promise: once we are reconciled to God through Christ, nothing in creation can tear us apart.

## Marriage as a Symbol of Inseparability

Marriage itself is meant to be a symbol of this inseparability. While marriage can be denigrated, abused, and broken—that is not God's intention. Rather, it points to something greater: the unbreakable bond between God and His people, between Christ and His church.

It is God's will bringing together two seemingly incompatible things—sinful humanity and a holy God. It is the mark of a master chef to bring together things that should not work, yet create something beautiful.

Strange pairings can become wonderful when combined by the right hand:

- Chocolate and chili
- Kimchi and cheddar cheese
- Tea and cherry jam
- Strawberries and peas
- Sea salt and caramel
- Brie cheese and blueberry

The chef combines these unlikely ingredients, and the dirt and mess of cooking ends up on the chef's apron, the towels, and the chef himself. Jesus is that Chef. He has taken all of our sin, shame, and brokenness upon Himself.

## Conclusion

We are a strange pairing—sinful humanity and a holy God. Yet through the work of Christ, we can be brought together. And it is that Chef who will keep you together, even when you seem incompatible.

Nothing can separate you from the love of God in Christ Jesus our Lord.
