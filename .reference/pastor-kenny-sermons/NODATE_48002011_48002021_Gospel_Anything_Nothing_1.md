# Galatians 2:11-21

**Title:** Gospel + Anything = Nothing

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## The Question Behind the Question

Over the past couple of weeks, we've received many questions about denominations. It's a particularly popular question I've noticed, especially among those wrestling with their faith journey. But beneath the surface question—"Which denomination should I belong to?"—lies a deeper question about identity and belonging: *What label should I call myself? Which club should I join?*

This is a natural question, much like other identity questions teenagers face. But the first century church grappled with this same problem, and Paul's response to Peter in Antioch speaks directly to us today.

---

## 1. A Fight Over Clubs (Verses 11-13)

> When Peter came to Antioch, I opposed him to his face, because he was clearly in the wrong. Before certain men came from James, he used to eat with the Gentiles. But when they arrived, he began to draw back and separate himself from the Gentiles because he was afraid of those who belonged to the circumcision group. The other Jews joined him in his hypocrisy, so that by their hypocrisy even Barnabas was led astray.

Peter's mistake was profound: he stopped eating with Gentile Christians. A group of Jewish Christians pressured him, insisting that Gentiles weren't "real Christians" because they didn't follow Jewish rituals—circumcision, food laws, and other cultural practices. These rituals themselves weren't the issue; they were part of Jewish cultural heritage. But Peter's withdrawal of fellowship from Gentile believers was un-Christian, and it created a dangerous precedent.

---

## 2. Peter Sets Up Two-Tier Christianity (Verses 14-16)

> When I saw that they were not acting in line with the truth of the gospel, I said to Peter in front of them all, "You are a Jew, yet you live like a Gentile and not like a Jew. How is it, then, that you force Gentiles to follow Jewish customs?

Paul's anger was righteous. Peter himself had been told by Jesus to eat ceremonially unclean foods—he had lived like a Gentile. Yet now Peter was doing exactly what Jesus had dismantled: using Jewish customs as a marker of true Christianity. In doing so, Peter was creating a hierarchy of believers, suggesting that some Christians were more legitimate than others based on external practices.

---

## 3. A True Christian Is Justified by Christ Alone (Verses 15-21)

> We who are Jews by birth and not 'Gentile sinners' know that a man is not justified by observing the law, but by faith in Jesus Christ. So we, too, have put our faith in Christ Jesus that we may be justified by faith in Christ and not by observing the law, because by observing the law no one will be justified.

Paul cuts to the heart of the matter with two essential truths:

**First:** A Christian is *justified*—declared right with God—by faith in Jesus Christ alone. We believe that Jesus has done it all. He paid for our sins on the cross. He alone has the power and willingness to forgive freely. This is the gospel.

**Second:** A Christian is *not* justified by observing the law, rituals, or customs. It doesn't matter if you're Jewish by birth or a Gentile. Labels don't save. Membership in the right club doesn't save. Only faith in Christ saves.

This leads to an inescapable logic: **If the gospel plus something else saves, then the gospel alone doesn't save.** But if the gospel alone saves—and Paul affirms it does—then these are mutually exclusive categories. There is nothing to add to the gospel.

Adding anything to a perfect thing ruins it. Think of it like adding water to Coke, or creating a fictional "Book 8" to Harry Potter. The perfection is compromised.

Paul makes this devastatingly clear:

> I do not set aside the grace of God, for if righteousness could be gained through the law, Christ died for nothing!

---

## Application: Beyond Denominations

Let's return to today's question about denominations. The real problem isn't the existence of different churches—it's when we make our denominational identity more important than our Christian identity.

If we say you must follow certain customs and rules to be a "true Christian," we're saying Christ died for nothing.

If we say your Christian legitimacy depends on what denominational label you wear, then that's not the gospel anymore. **Gospel + Labels = Nothing.**

If your loyalty to your church or denomination equals or exceeds your loyalty to the gospel, then the gospel becomes worthless.

The principle is simple: **Be a Christian first. Everything else—your church, your denomination, even your church's particular customs—comes a distant second.**

---

## Accept One Another in Christ

Peter should have accepted the Gentiles as fellow Christians because of their shared faith in the gospel. Instead, he rejected them because of their label—they were Gentiles, uncircumcised, different from the Jewish Christians in his club.

But the gospel belongs to all people. It doesn't depend on your gender, race, or religious background. Right standing with God rests on one thing only: faith in Christ.

At Soul Purpose, we are united by one thing: the gospel. We don't divide over what club you came from. We accept each other as Christian brothers and sisters—those who have put their faith in Christ alone. As we approach Christmas, this is our chance to invite friends and tell them about this faith in Christ alone.

---

## Final Thought

Labels aren't necessarily bad—they can be useful shorthand for describing different groups of Christians. But they should never define your identity as a believer. In Scripture, the terms used are simple: "Christian," "Jesus follower," "believer." It's not about what club you belong to. It's about what you actually believe. It's about faith in Christ alone.
