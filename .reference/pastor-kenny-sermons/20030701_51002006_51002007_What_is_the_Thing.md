# Colossians 2:6-7

**Title:** What is the Thing?  
**Date:** 11 July 2003  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction

What is the thing we're really talking about in the Christian life? It's easy to get distracted by all the noise around us, but Paul wants us to understand the core of what it means to follow Christ.

## Point 1: We Have Received Jesus as Lord

> So then, just as you received Christ Jesus as Lord, continue to live in him. (Colossians 2:6)

When we became Christians, we received the word of truth through people like Epaphras, and most importantly, we received Christ Jesus as Lord. The word "Lord" means overseer, king over you—someone with ultimate authority.

But here's the thing: it costs to have a Lord.

### The Cost of Lordship

Think about medieval history—the relationship between lords and serfs, or in Rome, between patricians and plebeians. When you have a lord, you become like the lower class. You submit. You pay tribute. You serve. You sacrifice yourself. You offer loyalty.

When Jesus becomes our Lord, we're essentially saying yes to all of that. We're submitting our will to His. We're offering our lives as tribute. We're sacrificing our own desires for His kingdom. We're pledging our loyalty.

**You can see why being a Christian is hard.** Not because we have a harsh master—in fact, we have the very best master. It's hard because all around us, at school, at work, everywhere, our friends don't think like this. They live for themselves. They pursue their own plans. And we're swimming upstream.

## Point 2: Therefore, Walk in Him—Rooted and Built Up

> Continue to live in him, rooted and built up in him, strengthened in the faith as you were taught, and overflowing with thankfulness. (Colossians 2:6-7)

The Colossians were being tempted to abandon what they'd been taught. False teachers were coming with fine-sounding arguments, trying to convince them that there was deeper wisdom, higher truths, found outside of Christ. Paul addresses this directly:

> See to it that no one takes you captive through hollow and deceptive philosophy, which depends on human traditions and the basic principles of this world rather than on Christ. (Colossians 2:8)

Paul is drawing a line: there is no spiritual wisdom outside of Christ. None. If you want to grow spiritually, if you want true understanding, it comes through Him alone.

### What Does It Mean to Walk in Him?

When Paul says "walk in him," he's talking about growth. He uses two beautiful images: **rooted and built up**.

Picture a tree. Its roots go deep, anchoring it to the basics—to the foundation of Christ's teaching. From those roots, the branches and leaves grow outward, bearing fruit: love, faith, gratitude, joy. Walking in Christ means growing deeper in your foundation while bearing more fruit in your life and in the world around you.

This walking, this growth, happens both personally and as we influence others. The gospel grows through us.

### Our Responsibility

Here's what Paul is implying: **we have a responsibility to grow ourselves.** We can't be passive. We have to actively choose to be rooted in Christ, to feed ourselves on His Word, to deepen our faith.

But we also have **a responsibility to one another.** We're called to help other Christians walk in Him—to encourage them, to help them stay rooted, to strengthen their faith as we were strengthened.

The Thing is about this. It's about building up the body of Christ together, one person, one community at a time.
