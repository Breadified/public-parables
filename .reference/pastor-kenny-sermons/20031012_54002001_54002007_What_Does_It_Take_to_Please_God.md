# 1 Timothy 2:1-7

**Title:** What Does It Take to Please God?
**Date:** October 12, 2003
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction: Misconceptions About Pleasing God

When we ask what it takes to please God, we often think of external things: keeping the Ten Commandments, meditation and spiritual disciplines, charitable works, or simply being a good person who doesn't harm others. But these surface-level answers miss something far more profound.

Consider the tragedy of October 12, 2002. Two brothers, Amrozi and Ali Imron, believed they were pleasing God. They purchased a Mitsubishi van and filled it with a tonne of chemicals—potassium chlorate, sulfuric acid, and aluminium powder—a recipe for explosives. They removed the rear seats, replaced them with filing cabinets, stripped the vehicle of identifying features, and drove it from East Java to Bali. There, a young man wearing an unusual heavy vest stepped into Paddy's nightclub at 11:07 PM and detonated himself. Thirty seconds later, the van outside exploded with such force that the blast registered on Indonesian earthquake measuring instruments.

The fireball stretched eighty metres into the sky. Two hundred and two people perished—eighty-eight of them Australians. Three hundred and fifty more were horribly burned or injured. Entire football teams disappeared overnight. For Amrozi and Imron, this carnage was their understanding of pleasing God.

This tragic contrast sets the stage for what Scripture actually reveals about pleasing the true God.

## Point 1: Prayer Pleases the Personal God

> **1 Timothy 2:1-2** I urge, then, first of all, that requests, prayers, intercession and thanksgiving be made for everyone—for kings and all those in authority, that we may live peaceful and quiet lives in all godliness and holiness.

Paul begins by calling us to pray for all people, especially those in government and positions of authority. This instruction reveals something vital: God cares deeply about our peace and prosperity. He wants us to pray that our leaders govern wisely, so we can live quiet, dignified, peaceful lives.

We often take governments for granted, but they undergird our well-being, security, and dignity. Anarchy is chaos. Even oppressive governments preserve more peace than lawlessness. We saw this after Saddam Hussein's fall in Baghdad—the absence of authority brought social collapse. Citizens ransacked museums, looted stores, set fires, and created mayhem. Governments enable people to live meaningfully and make rational choices in safety.

God desires this peace for us, not because He is distant or indifferent, but because He is profoundly personal. He cares about how our leaders govern. He wants to hear from us. He desires relationship with us—like a father who says "Tell me everything about your day" rather than "Leave me alone."

The problem is we don't actually believe God cares. Our culture—and popular entertainment—paints Him as impersonal and vindictive. In one South Park episode, a character describes God as a bored deity who gives us gifts only to take them away, drinking our tears for power, manipulating us like a boy with ants under a magnifying glass. This portrayal captures a widespread fear: God is distant, unconcerned with our welfare, using us for His sick pleasure.

But this is profoundly wrong. The God revealed in Scripture is personal, loving, and genuinely invested in our peace and well-being.

## Point 2: People Being Saved Pleases God

> **1 Timothy 2:3-4** God our Savior...who wants all men to be saved and to come to a knowledge of the truth.

There is something God loves and desires even more than our peaceful governments: the salvation of all people. This is His supreme delight.

But what does it mean to be saved? Saved from what? We need to understand what a "sinner" truly is.

> **1 Timothy 1:15** Christ Jesus came into the world to save sinners.

A sinner is more than someone who breaks rules or acts immorally. To sin fundamentally means to rebel against God's authority. It's not merely breaking a speed limit—that's a symptom. The real issue is rejecting God's right to rule us. It's anarchy against the Almighty.

Like Amrozi, who rejected Indonesian authority and sought to impose his own rule through terror, we likewise rebel against God by ignoring Him and His rightful authority over our lives. We may not blow up buildings, but in our hearts, we commit treason against our Creator. We ignore His way of living and create our own rules. We do this when we speak harshly, steal, act selfishly, disobey authorities, cheat, or pursue greed. At the core, we're saying to God: "I don't accept Your rule over my life."

And there are consequences. Just as nations demand justice for terrorism, God demands justice for rebellion against Him. The consequence is death.

Listen to how the families of Amrozi's victims spoke at his sentencing:

David Spike Stewart lost his 29-year-old son, Anthony. His only birthday wish was that Amrozi would be executed. He said he would volunteer for the firing squad.

Maria lost her twin sister and bridesmaid on her honeymoon. They came back in body bags. She said: "I can't wait for their execution. I'd like some of my blood on the bullets used, on behalf of my sisters."

Danny Hanley lost two daughters, including Simone, who died from agonising burns fifty days after the bombing. He said: "We are happy and relieved. It was most important to us that Amrozi has received his just reward—death."

This righteous anger mirrors God's anger at sin. We deserve death. God's own Son was killed by sinners—by us. There is judgment not only on Amrozi but on all of us.

When God cleans up His creation, He does it thoroughly. He doesn't hide things in cupboards or under beds like we do. He cleans completely, down to the atom. It doesn't matter whether we've sinned greatly or slightly. Big clumps of dirt or small specks of dust—it all goes. God has promised to do away with this world and create a new one where sin and rebellion will not exist. We are all part of the old world. From that perspective, we are no different from Amrozi.

Yet God desires people to be saved from this judgment. How? Through knowledge of the truth.

## Point 3: Jesus Pleases God

> **1 Timothy 2:5-6** For there is one God and one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus, who gave himself as a ransom for all men—the testimony given in its proper time.

This is the truth that saves: Jesus Christ is the one mediator between God and humanity. A mediator is someone who acts between parties to effect agreement and reconciliation. Think of the UN Secretary-General mediating between hostile nations—going to different countries, looking at alternative solutions, using persuasion. The mediator tries to broker peace.

Jesus acts between us and God to effect peace. But the price is immense. The ransom is a payment demanded to redeem from captivity. God's justice demands death—we are under bondage to death. Jesus goes to the cross and pays that price. He is crucified, tortured, and dies. On those two beams, His infinite worth effectively and infinitely pays for our infinite sins.

Kofi Annan might arrange foreign aid or IMF assistance for peace, but he would never die himself to save a distant country. Jesus gives His life—His very self—to effect peace completely and perfectly. He satisfies God's justice.

Jesus is the unique mediator. There is one God and one mediator. Not Buddha, not Mohammad, not priests in robes, not confessional boxes, not your own goodness and efforts. There are not many ways to God. Jesus alone is the mediator.

And by this one act of sacrifice, God is pleased. Not pleased by death as if He were sadistic, but pleased that justice is finally done. Like a parent relieved when the criminal who hurt their child is put away, God is pleased when sin is finally judged and paid for. In fact, God is so pleased with Jesus that He raises Him from death and gives Him the best honour, the best titles and names, the best seat in heaven.

So here's the crucial truth: We cannot please God enough on our own. The prayers of sinners are not heard by God. We have no basis to approach Him. But through Jesus, the great mediator, we do. Only His prayers are effective. That is why we pray in Jesus' name. Our only access to God is through Him.

## Point 4: Pleasing God Through Jesus

We now understand what pleases God:
- **Prayer**, because He cares for us personally
- **Salvation of people**, because it's His deepest desire
- **Jesus**, who enables both prayer and salvation to be possible

We cannot please God on our own strength, but through Jesus, we now can. We can become part of God's desire for all people to be saved. This is good and pleasing to God.

Consider Amrozi's brother, Imron. He was equally guilty. He had bought the van with Amrozi. He was the better-trained terrorist, having studied in Islamic schools in Pakistan and militia camps in Afghanistan. He provided the know-how for the bombs. He drove the van full of explosives and two suicide bombers to the nightclub.

Yet Imron did not receive the death sentence. Why? Because he apologised. He was repentant. He didn't defend himself or appeal. He lodged an application for clemency, recognising his guilt and his inability to save himself. He begged for forgiveness.

We are likewise guilty. We must beg. Forget the appeals process. Don't try to defend yourself by questioning how God should have done things differently, or claiming He should prove Himself, or blaming Him for not making you a Christian. Put away all your objections. Beg for forgiveness, and do whatever God says. Don't rebel any longer. Put Jesus in charge as the Ruler of your life.

## The Moment We Live In

Imagine we could pause time at that moment in Paddy's nightclub. Before the bomb exploded, before two hundred and two lives were stolen, imagine if those victims had a chance to do things differently—to catch a different plane, go to another pub, make a different choice.

Friends, this is the moment we live in right now. The judgment of God has already been executed—Jesus paid the price on the cross. But in incredible mercy, God offers us a chance to get right with Him before all things end. Jesus is the one mediator through whom we can achieve peace with God.

Would those eighty-eight Australians in Bali have taken that chance if they could? Of course. Will you? Don't delay. Please God by coming to Him through Jesus.

## Prayer of Commitment

If you wish to please God through Jesus and want to make things right with Him tonight, consider praying this prayer:

> Dear God, my Saviour and Lord,
> 
> Thank you for our government, for the peace and dignified lives they provide, and we ask that they continue to govern wisely.
> 
> I am sorry that I have not pleased you in the past and see why you are angry with me.
> 
> I thank you for sending your Son, Jesus, to save me by pleasing you on my behalf.
> 
> Please help me now to please you by following Jesus as my Lord.
> 
> In Jesus' name, Amen.

If you've prayed this prayer, be confident that God has heard you and forgiven you. Angels are rejoicing. God is very pleased. Tell someone about your decision. Your Christian friends would like to know. We want to help you grow in your new life with Christ.
