# Colossians 2:6-7

**Title:** What is The Thing?  
**Date:** July 11, 2003  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction

What is The Thing? This is the central question we're exploring today as we look at how to live out our faith in Christ.

## Point 1: We Have Received Jesus as Lord

> So then, just as you received Christ Jesus as Lord, continue to live in him...

When the Colossians received the word of truth from Epaphras, they received Christ Jesus as Lord. The word "Lord" (Greek: *kyrios*) means overseer and king—the one who has authority over you.

But here's the reality: it costs to have a Lord.

### The Cost of Lordship

Think back to history class. Remember learning about medieval castles, serfs, and lords? Or the Roman distinction between plebeians and patricians? When Jesus becomes our Lord, we essentially take on the role of a servant—we become like the serf to our Master.

Having Jesus as Lord means:
- **Submission** to His authority
- **Tribute** with our lives
- **Sacrifice** of ourselves
- **Loyalty** to His kingdom

You can see why being a Christian is hard. Not because we have a harsh master—in fact, we have the best master—but because it's difficult when everyone around us, at school and in our social circles, doesn't think this way. Our friends live by different values, and that creates real tension in how we live out our faith.

## Point 2: Walk in Christ—Rooted and Built Up

> Continue to live in him, rooted and built up in him, strengthened in the faith as you were taught, and overflowing with thankfulness.

The Colossians were being tempted to abandon their faith in Christ. Paul addresses this directly:

> See to it that no one takes you captive through hollow and deceptive philosophy, which depends on human tradition and the basic principles of this world rather than on Christ. (Colossians 2:8)

There's a seductive lie that says spiritual wisdom exists outside of the Lord. Paul emphatically rejects this. There is no true wisdom apart from Christ.

### What Does It Mean to Walk in Him?

To "walk" in Christ is to grow in Christ—personally and then outward to others. It's portrayed beautifully in nature: a tree with roots going deep, anchored to the basics, while its branches and leaves grow upward, producing the fruit of love and faith.

### Our Responsibility

Growing in Christ is not a passive experience. We have two responsibilities:

1. **Personal responsibility:** We must cultivate our own growth in Christ, staying rooted in the gospel, deepening our understanding of Scripture, and developing stronger faith.

2. **Corporate responsibility:** We have a responsibility to one another—to help other Christians walk in Christ, to encourage them, to build them up in the faith.

### The Thing

The Thing—what we're discussing today—is how we fulfill these responsibilities together. It's about helping one another continue walking in Christ, rooted and built up in Him, strengthened in faith and overflowing with thankfulness.
