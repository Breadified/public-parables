# Hebrews 1:1–2:4

**Title:** Jesus is the Best Messenger of God
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew
**Date:** 5 October 2009

---

## The Objectivity of God's Message

When I was in school, I struggled with most subjects, but I persisted with Mathematics. Why? Because Math doesn't allow for ambiguity. One plus one equals two—not a well-structured answer with a nice introduction and conclusion. In Mathematics, you either know the answer or you don't. There's no adding and no subtracting.

This truth about Math mirrors something profound about Jesus and His message: you cannot add to it, and you cannot subtract from it. The message of Christ is final, complete, and exact—like a mathematical equation.

---

## Jesus Reveals God Better Than Past Prophets

The writer of Hebrews makes his case by comparing Jesus to all the messengers who came before Him. Look at how the text itself reveals the contrast:

> In the past God spoke to our forefathers through the prophets at many times and in various ways, but in these last days he has spoken to us by his Son, whom he appointed heir of all things, and through whom he made the universe. (Hebrews 1:1–2)

Notice the structure:
- **When:** In the past → In these last days
- **To whom:** To our forefathers → To us
- **How:** Through prophets, in various ways → Through His Son

In ancient times, God revealed Himself through burning bushes (Moses), dreams (Joseph, Pharaoh), visions (Ezekiel), writing on walls (Daniel), and even through a donkey (Balaam). These were legitimate ways for God—who exists outside our world—to communicate with mortal beings using our senses: sight and sound.

But now, in these last days, God has spoken through His Son Himself.

### What Do "The Last Days" Mean?

The prophets of ancient Israel all pointed forward to an end-time renewal. Isaiah and Micah predicted a day when God's temple would be truly established and people—both Jews and Gentiles—would draw near to God, walking in His ways. Hosea spoke of Israel's return to their land and their true King. Jeremiah and Peter both prophesied that in the last days, God's Spirit would be poured out on all people, and everyone who calls on the Lord's name would be saved.

Yet we also live in the age described in 2 Peter and 2 Timothy: the last days are marked by scoffers and wickedness. This present age is that foretold time.

And two thousand years ago, at Christmas, the Son came and spoke.

---

## Why Jesus Is Better: Two Reasons

### 1. Jesus Is the Exact Representation of God

Consider the contrast: Moses was like the George Washington of Israel. Through miracles and military might, he liberated the nation, established law, and organized an entire people into a disciplined community that wandered the Sinai and Levant. Jesus? A carpenter who spent time around a river.

Yet Jesus is infinitely superior. Here's why:

> The Son is the radiance of God's glory and the exact representation of his being. (Hebrews 1:3)

Jesus *is* God. He is:
- The heir of all things
- The one through whom God made the universe
- The one who sustains all things

Jesus lived a human life and died a human death as God Himself incarnate. The words and deeds of Jesus are the words and deeds of God Himself.

The author strengthens this truth by comparing Jesus to angels—the highest of God's created messengers:

> For to which of the angels did God ever say, "You are my Son; today I have become your Father"? Or again, "I will be his Father, and he will be my Son"? And again, when God brings his firstborn into the world, he says, "Let all God's angels worship him." (Hebrews 1:5–6)

Notice what the author emphasizes:
- Jesus is called God's Son twice—an intimate relationship of Father and Son
- These titles are never applied to angels
- Angels are commanded to worship Him

Therefore, Jesus is the exact representation of God's being. If you had seen Jesus in first-century Palestine, you would have seen God in the flesh. You could have touched Him. You would have heard His Aramaic accent. You would have encountered God in history, embodied in the one who perfectly reveals His Father.

When our non-Christian friends ask, "If God is real, why doesn't He reveal Himself?"—Hebrews answers: He has. Jesus is the exact representation of the being of God.

### 2. Jesus Is the Final Representation of God

The second reason Jesus surpasses all previous messengers is that His revelation is final.

> After he had provided purification for sins he sat down at the right hand of the Majesty in heaven. (Hebrews 1:3)

When someone sits down, they stop working. Jesus came to earth, said what needed to be said, did what needed to be done—especially through His death providing purification for our sins—and then He sat down. His work is finished.

I worked retail in Year 10, standing for four-hour shifts. When I finally sat down, it meant one thing: my shift was over. I was done.

When Jesus sat down at God's right hand, it means: *It is finished.* His work is complete, and so is His message.

Jesus Himself declared on the cross: "It is finished" (John 19:30). If His work is completed, then His message is completed too.

Consider the parable of the tenants: the vineyard owner sent his son, saying, "Perhaps they will respect him." But they killed him, thinking, "This is the heir. The inheritance will be ours." What does the owner do next? He doesn't send another messenger. He brings judgment.

The point is clear: Jesus is the heir. His message is final. The prophecies of Isaiah, Jeremiah, Micah, and Hosea find their fulfillment in Him. Jesus is the "Amen" to all their hopes. He is the "Yes" to every prediction. He is the climax of the entire story.

Therefore, God's revelation is finished. He has done the job perfectly and completely. Jesus is not just a better messenger than the prophets—He is the final messenger. No more burning bushes, no more dreams and visions, no more indirect revelation. God Himself has arrived.

### 3. Jesus Is Permanent

Beyond being exact and final, Jesus is also permanent. The author contrasts Him with angels:

> In speaking of the angels he says, "He makes his angels winds, his servants flames of fire." But about the Son he says, "Your throne, O God, will last for ever and ever." (Hebrews 1:7–8)

Angels are described as winds and flames—powerful but temporary. They come and go like the seasons. But the Son's throne lasts forever. He existed at the beginning:

> In the beginning, O Lord, you laid the foundations of the earth, and the heavens are the work of your hands. (Hebrews 1:10)

And as He was there at the beginning, He will be there at the end:

> They will perish, but you remain; they will all wear out like a garment. You will roll them up like a robe; like a garment they will be changed. But you remain the same, and your years will never end. (Hebrews 1:11–12)

Angels, like the earth and heavens themselves, are part of creation and will pass away. But the Son stands outside creation. He is eternal and unchanging. His revelation of the Father will not become outdated in a few generations. It is permanent, everlasting, a permanent record.

Think of it this way: the Old Testament is like a series of draft documents with revisions. Jesus is the final document—the one you hand in to your boss or client. That's it.

## The Application: Listen and Do Not Drift

Given all this—that Jesus is the exact, final, and permanent revelation of God—the author's command is urgent:

> We must pay more careful attention, therefore, to what we have heard, so that we do not drift away. For if the message spoken by angels was binding, and every violation and disobedience received its just punishment, how shall we escape if we ignore such a great salvation? (Hebrews 2:1–2)

The logic is simple: if even the message delivered by angels carried binding authority, how much more binding is the message delivered by the superior messenger—God's own Son?

> This salvation, which was first announced by the Lord, was confirmed to us by those who heard him. (Hebrews 2:3)

The apostles heard Jesus directly and confirmed His words through their teaching and writing. This is our revelation in written form: the Bible.

Therefore, there is no further revelation coming. No other news. No plot developments. This is it. For two thousand years, there has been silence from heaven—not because God has abandoned us, but because He has said all that needs to be said. Salvation is set. It is revealed. There is no other Messiah coming, no other way to God.

Some of you today might be hesitating, waiting for further revelation or further confirmation. There is none expected to come. This is as good as it gets. The Bible—for all its alleged faults—is the only way to know Jesus, the only way to hear His message, the only way to know the Messenger.

If you've been waiting to hear the word of salvation, here is the answer: Jesus has provided purification for sins through the cross. Our sins are forgiven. He sits at the right hand of God. And one day He will return to judge. But for those who trust in Him, salvation is certain.

## The Bible Speaks Today

A problem immediately arises: Jesus lived two thousand years ago in first-century Palestine. How does His message speak to us today?

The answer is this: Jesus' words and deeds remain with us today through the apostles' teaching in the Bible. Those who followed Him recorded all that He did for our benefit. They wrote letters to one another about how to apply His teachings. The writer of Hebrews himself reflects on how Jesus is the final message of God.

**The written word is not a lesser form of revelation.** Yes, there's a difference between hearing Jesus speak in person and reading His words on a page. But the difference is only in medium, not in substance or power. A love letter from someone special carries the same force as their voice—the medium changes, but the message doesn't.

Words are powerful. Read Barack Obama's inauguration speech and feel your spine tingle. Listen to testimonies and feel the weight of truth. The apostles' testimonies—those who were there, who saw Jesus, who confirmed His resurrection—are powerful.

Moreover, one could argue that the written word was *necessary* for Jesus to sit at God's right hand. He cannot be in two places at once. He cannot appear personally to every individual in every generation. But through the written Word, He speaks to us today. If you wonder why God hasn't personally shown up for you—He did, once, two thousand years ago. He cannot do it again and again for each person. But He hasn't left you in darkness. Through the Bible, He speaks to you today.

## Two Commands: Do Not Subtract, Do Not Add

From this foundation, the application becomes clear.

### Do Not Subtract

> We must not subtract from what he says. (Hebrews 2:1)

To subtract from God's Word is to subtract from what God Himself has said.

Some subtract the resurrection or Jesus' miracles because they don't fit our modern presuppositions about how the world should work. Others subtract teachings on marriage because they don't align with contemporary family structures. Still others subtract commands about stealing because they think, "That doesn't apply to me—I'm just a student."

This is dangerous. We must listen to what Jesus has said and not diminish it.

### Do Not Add

If revelation is finalized in Jesus, then we must stop searching for further revelation.

> We should not be looking for other ways of knowing God or knowing God's will. (Hebrews 1:1)

God does speak to individuals through dreams and visions—these are possible. But Hebrews makes clear that Jesus is the final revelation, which means the past methods of receiving God's Word have given way to something greater. We are not to act or pray or speak as though what God has already said is insufficient.

This applies especially to guidance. Some pursue additional information from God through supposed words of knowledge or modern prophets claiming special revelation. But consider a survey of a Christian school where nearly every student felt "called" to become a doctor or lawyer—the prestigious professions—but very few felt "led" to become nurses or garbage collectors. If God were speaking through these personal callings, wouldn't His guidance be more varied?

The truth is: God has told you all you need to know to make wise decisions. When choosing a university course or career path, you don't need a mystical calling. You need wisdom. Choose a course you can pursue in a way that honors God while you wait for Jesus' return—one you can pass without abandoning relationships with family and friends, one that doesn't require you to do something unethical, one that won't destroy your health or your relationship with God through prayer and Bible study. Beyond these principles, you have freedom.

What about coincidences, strong feelings, or dreams? I don't deny their reality. But they are neutral. Natural instincts and intuitions come to Christians and non-Christians alike. A coincidence doesn't carry divine guidance; you must still decide the right course in wisdom.

Suppose you score exactly the ATAR needed for a course. Does that mean you should enroll? No—the score itself says nothing. You must decide wisely. Or perhaps you miss a course by a fraction. Does that mean God is saying no? No—it's neutral. You decide based on godly principles whether to pursue it further or move on.

Charles Spurgeon, the great Baptist preacher, tells of a night when an angel supposedly visited him. The angel said, "Charles, I'm here to tell you whether you're in the Book of Life or not." Spurgeon's response? "Get behind me, Satan!" You might think he'd be thrilled, but Spurgeon understood something vital: "Before you came, I trusted the words of Christ in the Bible for my salvation. Why would I now trust the words of an angel instead?"

The words of the exact representation of God are enough. Nothing can improve upon them. Jesus has come, spoken, and acted. His word is final. Any other revelation can only downgrade what He has already said.

---

## Conclusion

We must face a critical fact: revelation is final. Therefore, we must stop looking for additional revelation. Jesus is not only better than past messengers—He is also better than any future pretenders (with the exception of His return in glory).

The Jesus of history, the one revealed in Scripture, is the superior message. There is no need to resort to any other. What can alternate revelation offer? At best, it repeats what we already know. At worst, it tempts us to place our hopes in something other than the Christ of history.

The Word of God is like mathematics:
- **Don't add,** because that dilutes what He has already accomplished.
- **Don't subtract,** because that denies what He has already accomplished.

The message is exact. The message is final. The message is permanent.

Listen to it. Don't drift away.
