# Daniel 10-12

**Title:** A Heavenly War
**Date:** 14 August 2011
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Spiritual Reality Behind Earthly Events

As we come to chapters 10-12 in Daniel, we encounter a vision of war. But this is not merely an account of earthly military conflict—it reveals something far more significant: the spiritual battle that determines all earthly outcomes.

> At that time Michael, the great prince who protects your people, will arise. There will be a time of distress such as has not happened from the beginning of nations until then. (Daniel 12:1)

The earthly wars described in these chapters—particularly the conflicts between the Seleucid and Ptolemaic empires with Israel caught between them—mirror a greater war being waged in the heavenly realm. Daniel receives a detailed prophecy so accurate that skeptics have assumed it was written after the events occurred. But the vision points beyond mere political history to the cosmic conflict between God and Satan.

### A War Behind the Curtains

In chapter 10, Daniel encounters angelic beings engaged in spiritual combat:

> The prince of the Persian kingdom resisted me twenty-one days. Then Michael, one of the chief princes, came to help me... Soon I will return to fight against the prince of Persia, and when I go, the prince of Greece will come. (Daniel 10:13, 20)

The language reveals this is not simply about earthly generals, but about spiritual powers. Like Job's experience, something is happening behind the curtain—a reality that determines our earthly destiny.

The modern world makes a critical mistake: materialism. We assume that only what can be seen, tested, measured, and observed is real. But this denies the reality of love, relationships, history, and God himself. The metaphysical realm is as real as the physical. And the war being waged there—between the forces of God and Satan—is the war that truly matters.

> For our struggle is not against flesh and blood, but against the rulers, against the authorities, against the powers of this dark world and against the spiritual forces of evil in the heavenly realms. (Ephesians 6:12)

All earthly wars are proxy wars—clashes of values and ideologies. The final outcome will not be determined by education, money, weapons, or military might. It will be determined by what happens in heaven.

## Michael: The Prince of Heaven

Michael appears throughout Scripture as the great prince who protects God's people. He is the guardian of Israel, the intercessor for the Jews. The figure of Michael foreshadows—or perhaps embodies—the pre-incarnate Christ.

### Jesus, the Stronger Man

In his earthly ministry, Jesus fought and defeated Satan:

> No one can enter a strong man's house and carry off his possessions unless he first ties up the strong man. Then he can rob his house. (Mark 3:27)

Jesus confronted this reality directly. The teachers of the law accused him of colluding with the devil, claiming he cast out demons through Satanic power. Jesus answered with perfect logic: Satan would not attack his own house. Instead, Jesus is the stronger man who comes, binds Satan, and rescues those held captive. This is what happens when someone turns to Christ—the demon must depart.

### The Cross: Victory Over Evil

But the great battle was yet to come. The final war between Michael and the dragon was fought at the cross:

> War broke out in heaven. Michael and his angels fought against the dragon, and the dragon and his angels fought back. But he was not strong enough, and they lost their place in heaven. The great dragon was hurled down—that ancient serpent called the devil, or Satan, who leads the whole world astray. He was hurled to the earth, and his angels with him. (Revelation 12:7-9)

That war *is* the cross. Satan thought he had won when Jesus died, but he had lost everything. Jesus disarmed the powers and authorities and made a public spectacle of them, triumphing over them through the cross (Colossians 2:15). Christ has risen and ascended to God's right hand, with all angels, authorities, and powers in submission to him (1 Peter 3:22).

It is finished. The victory is won.

## Choosing a Side

Yet we live in the period between Christ's victory and its final consummation. We remain at war.

> Those whose names are written in the book will be delivered. Multitudes who sleep in the dust of the earth will awake: some to everlasting life, others to shame and everlasting contempt. Those who are wise will shine like the brightness of the heavens, and those who lead many to righteousness, like the stars for ever and ever. (Daniel 12:1-3)

Two destinies await. Two ways to live. The righteous will inherit everlasting life; the wicked will experience everlasting shame.

### The Problem of Christian Neutrality

Here lies a persistent problem among Christians: the desire to remain neutral. We want to see both sides, appreciate the merits of different positions, sit comfortably between camps. This posture may seem wise in times of peace, but in times of war, neutrality is not an option. When the allies fought Germany, no one could afford to be 60% for one side and 40% for the other. To sit on the fence was to be a traitor.

The spiritual war demands a clear choice: Whose side are you on? There is no neutral ground.

> Do everything without complaining or arguing, so that you may become blameless and pure, children of God without fault in a crooked and depraved generation, in which you shine like stars in the universe as you hold out the word of life. (Philippians 2:14-16)

Those who belong to Christ must stand firm, shine brightly, and lead others toward righteousness. We must put on the full armor of God so that when the day of evil comes, we can stand our ground (Ephesians 6:11-13).

## The War Continues—But Victory Awaits

The future has not yet fully arrived. We live in the "already/not yet"—the victory is already won, but its final manifestation is not yet here.

> From the time that the daily sacrifice is abolished and the abomination that causes desolation is set up, there will be 1,290 days. Blessed is the one who waits for and reaches the end of the 1,335 days. (Daniel 12:11-12)

These numbers—time, times, and half a time (3.5 years)—represent a short period. Half a week. We are, spiritually speaking, on humpday—halfway to the goal, in the brief span between Christ's first coming and his return.

### Refined Through the Fire

During this time, spiritual refinement continues:

> Many will be purified, made spotless and refined, but the wicked will continue to be wicked. None of the wicked will understand, but those who are wise will understand. (Daniel 12:10)

We live as soldiers in an active war zone. The wicked will remain wicked; the wise will grow wiser and more righteous. We experience temptation and trial, spiritual warfare within and around us. But the victory has already been secured. Our weapons are not physical but spiritual—the truth of God's Word that demolishes arguments and takes every thought captive to obedience to Christ (2 Corinthians 10:3-6).

### The Prize at the End

> As for you, go your way till the end. You will rest, and then at the end of the days you will rise to receive your allotted inheritance. (Daniel 12:13)

The promise sustains us: rest awaits. The seventh day. The sabbath. Resurrection life and an inheritance in God's kingdom. But first, we must stand firm. Perhaps we will face persecution. But the inheritance makes this present life bearable—worth the suffering, worth the struggle.

We are called to be vigilant, faithful, and unshakeable. The heavenly Prince has already won the war. Our task is to endure until he calls us home.
