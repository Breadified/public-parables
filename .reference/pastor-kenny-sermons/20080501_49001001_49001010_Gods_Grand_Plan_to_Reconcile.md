# Ephesians 1:1-10

**Title:** God's Grand Plan to Reconcile  
**Date:** 25 May 2008  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Grand Plans

We all make grand plans. Whether it's a summer vacation to places like Las Vegas, Thailand, or Hong Kong, or something as simple as painting a wall in our home—these plans give us direction and vision for how we live. They help us prepare: save money, plan our routes, recruit help.

Paul's letter to the Ephesians is itself a grand plan. He presents to the church at Ephesus (in modern-day Turkey) a sweeping vision of what God intends for the world. It begins with a statement about God—a kind of title for everything that follows.

## 1. We Have Complete Blessing from God (3-5a)

### Praise Be to God the Father (v. 3)

> Praise be to the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who has blessed us in the heavenly realms with every spiritual blessing in Christ. (Ephesians 1:3)

The Greek word translated "praise" literally means "to speak well of." It's the same word used for "blessed," so Paul is saying: Blessed is God the Father. But why praise God? What makes Him worthy of our speaking well of Him?

The answer: **because God has blessed us.** And God's blessing is not mere words. When God speaks blessing, it carries real power and real benefit. When God says "blessed be Kenny," there are actual consequences.

### What Is This Blessing?

At first, Paul doesn't spell out what this blessing is. But looking ahead to verses 4 and 7, we discover it: **the blessing is forgiveness of sins. It is reconciliation between God and humanity.**

### A Complete Blessing on All Dimensions

This blessing of forgiveness is complete—not partial or lacking. Paul emphasizes this through three dimensions:

**Geographically complete:** "In the heavenly realms" (v. 3b). The forgiveness extends throughout the entire cosmos. No matter where you are, forgiveness is available. You cannot escape it.

**Numerically complete:** "Every spiritual blessing" (v. 3). All spiritual blessings are yours. The full measure is there—not held back, not rationed.

**Temporally complete:** "He chose us in him before the creation of the world" (v. 4). God's blessing extends from eternity past. Before you were born, before I was born, before the dinosaurs—God had already chosen to bless us.

This is radically different from partial blessing. God has given us something so complete that we cannot be outside of it. We cannot be short in number, nor in place, nor in time. We simply cannot be out of it.

This reminds us of Paul's magnificent declaration in Romans 8:28-39: nothing—not death, not life, not angels, not demons, not the present, not the future, not any powers, not height, not depth, nor anything else in all creation—can separate us from the love of God in Christ Jesus.

### Complete Forgiveness, Complete Assurance

**Reflection:** How do you feel about forgiveness as a Christian? Do you truly believe you have complete forgiveness, or do you harbor thoughts that you're only partially forgiven?

Some of us might genuinely feel only partially forgiven. This deserves careful thought:

First, if you feel guilty, it might be because you haven't actually repented and become a Christian. In that case, yes—you should feel guilty, because you haven't been forgiven by God. The invitation is to confess your sins and be reconciled.

Second, even as Christians, it's perfectly normal to feel guilty about the wrong things we've actually done. We really did commit those sins we're ashamed of now. These guilty feelings are genuine because we are objectively guilty. The Christian difference is this: **though we are guilty, our penalty has been transferred to another.** Objectively, in God's sight, we are forgiven—regardless of how we subjectively feel.

If guilty feelings persist, they can motivate genuine restitution. If you stole something years ago, go pay for it. If you hurt someone, apologize. It makes no difference to God's forgiveness, but it may bring healing to you and to the victim.

Over time, another feeling should grow to replace guilt: **the feeling of relief and gratitude.** I once braked hard at a crossing because I saw an elderly woman starting to cross while I was speeding. Someone hit me from behind. By every measure, it was my fault—I was speeding and reacted poorly. But because the other driver hit me from behind, legally and technically, the fault shifted to him. Though guilty, I felt relieved! That's the Christian experience: though we are guilty, someone else took the punishment, and we are free.

## 2. The Blessing Comes Through Jesus (5b-8)

### How Forgiveness Is Possible

> He predestined us to be adopted as his sons through Jesus Christ, in accordance with his pleasure and will, to the praise of his glorious grace, which he has freely given us in the One he loves. In him we have redemption through his blood, the forgiveness of sins, in accordance with the riches of God's grace that he lavished on us with all wisdom and understanding. (Ephesians 1:5b-8)

The blessing is made possible **through Jesus Christ**. The entire passage pulses with references to Christ's role in obtaining this blessing:

- "in Christ" (v. 3)
- "in the One he loves" (v. 6)
- "in him we have redemption through his blood" (v. 7)

**Atonement** is the heart of this. Christ died on our behalf. The guiltless Jesus took the punishment that belongs to guilty me. He paid the price through His blood. As a result: forgiveness of sins.

### How Forgiveness Is Complete

Forgiveness is complete because Christ completely redeems us. Look at the language Paul uses to emphasize fullness:

- "Freely given" (v. 6)
- "In accordance with the riches of God's grace" (v. 7)
- "Lavished on us with all wisdom and understanding" (v. 8)

The conclusion is unmistakable: **complete blessing.**

### The One and Only Spiritual Blessing

If forgiveness is now complete in Christ, it's hard to imagine another spiritual blessing to be gained in this lifetime. While we do await physical blessings and the return of Christ, there is no other spiritual blessing remaining. Christians have already received it—all of it. We have redemption. We have been chosen. We are forgiven.

Therefore, **forgiveness of sins is the one and only spiritual blessing from God.** There is no "second blessing" that comes later to bring empowerment, special ability, new holiness, or the gift of speaking in tongues. No such thing exists. The only spiritual blessing given to Christians is the complete one—and therefore there is no other.

## 3. This Blessing Is in Accordance with God's Purposes (9-10)

> And he made known to us the mystery of his will according to his good pleasure, which he purposed in Christ, to be put into effect when the times will have reached their fulfillment—to bring all things in heaven and on earth together under one head, even Christ. (Ephesians 1:9-10)

### To Glorify Christ (v. 10)

Why did God choose to bless us? The primary reason is **to glorify Christ.** The goal is stated in verse 10: "to bring all things in heaven and on earth together under one head, even Christ."

God loves His Son—and His Son is obedient and worthy, having put Himself out for others. God has orchestrated everything so that His Son will be in charge of all things. This has been His plan from before the creation of the world. Everything moves toward the exaltation of Christ.

### Just Because (v. 9)

There's a second reason, and it's wonderfully simple: **God wanted to.** Look at the repeated language:

- "According to his good pleasure" (v. 9)
- "In accordance with his pleasure and will" (v. 5)
- "In accordance with the riches of God's grace" (v. 6)
- "In love" (v. 4)

God wanted to, out of love for us. Why do you love chocolate? Because you do. Because you love it. There's no deeper argument required. I don't need to explain the reason; I simply love it. That's how God is with us. He wants to bless us, and so He makes it happen.

### A Word on Predestination and Fatalism

Some may feel uncomfortable with God being so sovereign and in control. It might seem that God is arbitrary—doing what He wants simply because He wants to. He's planned and arranged everything from before the creation of the world. For those not yet Christians, it might feel like they never had a chance—always destined to be outside His plan.

But here's the crucial distinction: **While it is true that God is sovereign and has worked out all things, we don't know the specifics of His will regarding who exactly His elect are.**

God has revealed His will to us through Paul and this letter: it is His desire to love and to redeem. But I do not know His will as to which specific individuals are chosen. The way to find out? **Become a Christian.** Confess your sins, repent, and you will discover your eternal destiny. You have that chance right now.

## Get in Line with God's Grand Plan

We might have grand plans for this summer, or for life after high school, or for our careers and education. We may have grand plans for ministry or for our church.

But God has an even grander plan—one thought out from eternity to eternity. That plan is **reconciliation between God and humanity through Christ.**

The question for each of us: Are you preparing for it?

In the age to come, all things will be under Christ. But will you bow willingly, recognizing His lordship with joy? Or will you be forced to your knees? Will you join God's grand plan willingly or unwillingly?

The invitation is to align your life with God's purpose. Join His grand plan.
