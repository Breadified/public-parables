# Philippians 1:1-11

**Title:** Partners in the Gospel
**Date:** Friday, February 4, 2011
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction: What Makes a Great Partnership?

Think about the great partnerships we admire: Michael Jordan and Scottie Pippen in basketball, passing and executing plays with such precision they seemed to read each other's minds. Torvill and Dean in ice skating. Batman and Robin. Even brand partnerships like Tiffany & Co.

What made these partnerships work? They had common ground—shared training, shared vision, shared purpose.

But there's a partnership far greater than any of these: partnership in Christ. And Paul's letter to the Philippians teaches us what this means.

---

## Context: Paul's Relationship with Philippi

Paul, a follower of Jesus, writes to the Christians in Philippi, a Greek city. He begins with deep thanksgiving:

> I thank my God every time I remember you. In all my prayers for all of you, I always pray with joy because of your partnership in the gospel from the first day until now. (Philippians 1:3-5)

Notice the pattern: *every time I remember*, *in all my prayers*, *always pray with joy*. Paul clearly loved these people. But why was he so grateful? Two reasons shape his thanksgiving.

---

## The Gospel: Our Common Ground

**The first reason Paul was thankful** was for their partnership in the gospel.

The gospel is the good news: that Jesus sacrificed himself on the cross to take the penalty of our sin upon himself. It's gracious forgiveness from God at the cost of his own Son. We don't earn it—we receive it humbly.

Paul and the Philippians shared this gospel from the first day, beginning with Paul's visit to Philippi recorded in Acts 16. This wasn't a casual connection; it was the bedrock of everything.

### Application: Our Unity in Christ

As we gather together—whether you've been a Christian your whole life or you're just hearing about Jesus for the first time—what binds us? The gospel. That's the one thing that unites us with every Christian across time and geography.

Don't take this for granted. Some of you are hearing about Jesus for the first time at school or through Scripture classes. Others have grown up hearing it repeated and may think, "Not the cross again!" But consider: there are those here who desperately need to hear it. Those struggling with doubts need to be renewed by God's love. All of us—yes, all of us—need to hear it again because we're forgetful people.

Just as parents need to remind their children they're loved, we need to remind each other. The gospel is God saying, "I love you." It sustains us.

This isn't just a task for church leaders or study groups—it's for all of you. Remind each other at lunch or recess: "What did you learn today? How's your Christian life going? Did you read your Bible? How did that impact you?" We're like rowers in a boat, keeping rhythm together, reminding each other of the beat.

---

## Partnership in Action and Support

**The second reason for Paul's thanksgiving** was the Philippians' physical and practical partnership.

> It is right for me to feel this way about all of you, since I have you in my heart; for whether I am in chains or defending and confirming the gospel, all of you share in God's grace with me. God can testify how I long for all of you with the affection of Christ Jesus. (Philippians 1:7-8)

Paul wasn't alone in his mission. The Philippians walked with him through everything. When Paul was imprisoned, they were imprisoned with him in spirit. When Paul defended the gospel, they defended it too. Their partnership wasn't merely spiritual—it was active, tangible, costly.

Paul traveled planting churches and strengthening believers across Asia and Europe. He faced Roman imprisonment awaiting trial. The Philippians stood with him through it all.

### Application: Partnership in Ministry

Our gatherings here aren't just places to hear about the gospel—they're places of ministry where we serve together. We're encouraged so we can go proclaim Christ during lunch and recess, inviting friends and witnessing to others.

Here's the beautiful reality: when one of you invites someone to hear about Jesus, we're all inviting them. When one of you shares the gospel, we're all supporting you. When one of you sees someone turn to Christ, we all rejoice with you. And if one of you faces opposition or persecution, we all face it together.

That's worth giving thanks for.

---

## Paul's Prayer for the Philippians

Paul's thanksgiving naturally flows into his prayer:

> And this is my prayer: that your love may abound more and more in knowledge and depth of insight, so that you may be able to discern what is best and may be pure and blameless until the day of Christ, filled with the fruit of righteousness that comes through Jesus Christ—to the glory and praise of God. (Philippians 1:9-11)

Paul prays for two essential things:

**Knowledge and Discernment:** That their love would grow not in sentiment alone, but in understanding. That they'd develop the wisdom to distinguish what's truly good from what merely appears good.

**Godliness and Fruitfulness:** That they'd live pure and blameless lives, overflowing with the fruit of righteousness that only comes through Jesus Christ.

---

## The Key to Christian Partnership

Just as Michael Jordan and Scottie Pippen trained together for years, learning the same moves and tactics, so Christians are unified through the gospel. We're united in one truth. We stand together under the banner of Christ and his righteousness.

This is what makes partnership possible—and this is what we celebrate together.

---

## Prayer

Father, I thank you for this fellowship of believers. I thank you for our partnership from the first day we've met until now. Thank you for the fruit of righteousness that comes through Jesus Christ's blood.

Because of that, I pray that our love may abound more and more in knowledge and depth of insight so that we may discern what is best, that we may be pure and blameless in view of the day of Christ.

Amen.
