# Micah 2:1-13

**Title:** God's Church
**Date:** 10 October 2024 (ARISE)
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## 1. Woe to Those Who Plan Evil (verses 1-3a, 8-9)

> Woe to those who plan iniquity, to those who plot evil on their beds! At morning's light they carry it out because it is in their power to do it.

Notice the insidious nature of this sin: they plot evil "on their beds"—lying awake at night, devising schemes for wrongdoing. This describes a comprehensive commitment to evil, a 24/7 dedication to wickedness.

> Lately my people have risen up like an enemy.

What do you think about when you lie down to sleep? Most of us think about dreams, regrets, or stress. But these ancient Judahites were plotting evil, constantly inventing new ways to do harm. This mirrors our own world:

> They have become filled with every kind of wickedness, evil, greed, and depravity. They are full of envy, murder, strife, deceit, and malice. They are gossips, slanderers, God-haters, insolent, arrogant, and boastful. They invent new forms of evil. (Romans 1:29-30)

Evil at night means evil all day. No rest. This is 24/7 evil.

When morning comes, they act on these schemes:

> They covet fields and seize them by violence, and houses, and take them. You strip off the rich robe from those who pass by without a care, like men returning from battle.

Their violence extends to stealing people's only protection—their garments, their basic necessities. It's as if they took someone's umbrella in the rain, their hat in scorching sun, just because they could. They literally woke up and chose violence.

> They defraud people of their homes. You drive the women of my people from their pleasant homes. You take away my blessing from their children forever.

When they rob homes, they rob families of their inheritance—not just physical property, but their theological inheritance. They destroy the promise of God's blessing for future generations.

God's people were meant to think differently. This was the command:

> These commandments that I give you today are to be on your hearts. Impress them on your children. Talk about them when you sit at home and when you walk along the road, when you lie down and when you get up. (Deuteronomy 6:6-7)

### Application

What do your children think about at night? Do they end their day with God's word, or do they drift to sleep with their minds filled with endless screen content—games, videos, and pagan material?

What do *you* think about at night?

> Each person is tempted when they are dragged away by their own evil desire and enticed. Then, after desire has conceived, it gives birth to sin; and sin, when it is full-grown, gives birth to death. (James 1:14-15)

Are you planning violence? Dreaming of idolatry? Researching your next big purchase? Or are you:

- Dreaming of serving God
- Thinking about who to tell the gospel to
- Considering how to love your neighbor
- Planning how to keep your children from screen addiction

**What's your real priority?**

Go to sleep with prayer. Wake up with God's word. Or do the reverse. Either way, let God's kingdom consume your thoughts, day and night.

## 2. God's Judgment (verses 3b-5, 10)

> Therefore, the Lord says: "I am planning disaster against this people, from which you cannot save yourselves. You will no longer walk proudly, for it will be a time of calamity."

If you seize homes, God will seize your family. If you show no regard for family, God will not give you one. You do evil; I will bring evil upon you.

> In that day people will ridicule you; they will taunt you with this mournful song: "We are utterly ruined; my people's possession is divided up. He takes it from me! He assigns our fields to traitors."

Their inheritance is reversed. What they stole from others, invaders will steal from them. Their own fields will be given to enemies.

> Therefore you will have no one in the assembly of the Lord to divide the land by lot.

This cuts deeper than physical loss. To have no place in God's assembly means exclusion from God's community and God's promises.

> Get up, go away! For this is not your resting place, because it is defiled, it is ruined, beyond all remedy.

### Application

We live in an age that celebrates autonomy and individualism. But the judgment here reveals a harsh truth: when we live only for ourselves, we end up alone.

If we constantly make selfish decisions, thinking only of ourselves rather than others, our families, or our communities—we will ultimately find ourselves isolated. That single unit you protect becomes all you have. When you need help, no one will be there.

The church is a community of choice, not compulsion. No one forces attendance. Church is for those who want to pray and read Scripture before sleep and upon waking, and for those who want to be challenged and to serve others.

Yet because church is voluntary, we treat it as optional. It becomes the last thing on our priority list. We attend only when everything else is cleared from our schedule—no work, no shopping, no household repairs, no vacations, perfect weather. Then, if we feel like it, we might show up.

But this is the danger: if you invest nothing, you receive nothing. If you treat church as an afterthought, you will complain that no one knows you, that you don't fit in, that people don't meet your needs. Your children will learn the habit of skipping Sunday. Eventually, you'll blame the church, switch congregations, blame God for abandoning you—never asking whether you were there for Him.

By then, it's too late. The judgment becomes self-fulfilling: "There is no one in the assembly of the Lord" for those who refused to be present.

## 3. The False Remedy vs. God's Word (verses 6-7, 11)

Some would rather not hear the truth at all:

> "Do not prophesy," their prophets say. "Do not prophesy about these things; disgrace will not overtake us."

They demand comforting lies. "Don't tell us we're wrong. Don't speak of judgment. Prophesy that our path is fine."

> If a liar and deceiver comes and says, "I will prophesy for you plenty of wine and beer," that would be just the prophet for this people!

They want false prophets who promise abundance and ease—the "wine and beer" of worldly comfort. Anything that encourages them to keep sinning, keep pursuing wealth and luxury, keep living for themselves. The message is simple: *you do you*. Follow your authentic self. Look like the world, have its comforts, but still call yourself Christian—with no sacrifice.

But this is a lie, born from the father of lies himself:

> So the great dragon was cast out, that serpent of old, called the Devil and Satan, who deceives the whole world. (Revelation 12:9)

> You belong to your father, the devil, and you want to carry out your father's desires. He was a murderer from the beginning, not holding to the truth, for there is no truth in him. When he lies, he speaks his native language, for he is a liar and the father of lies. (John 8:44)

### The Real Remedy

The true remedy is God's word:

> "Do not my words do good to the one whose ways are upright? You descendants of Jacob, should it be said, 'Does the Lord become impatient? Does he do such things?'"

The question is penetrating: Do you not trust God? That:
- His timing is perfect
- His will is best
- When we surrender our wishes to His desires, all will be well
- The path of the godly is the path of sacrifice, not comfort
- The way to eternal life is narrow, not broad
- He chooses spouses, opportunities, and callings radically different from what the world chooses
- Instant gratification lasts only an instant, but delayed gratification lasts forever

You fear you'll miss out on the good. But the promise is true: **Do not His words do good to those whose ways are upright?**

God is gathering a righteous people—not people who are morally perfect, but people who depend on God in everything, who want to live His ways, whose direction is set by Him, not by themselves.

## 4. The Great Gathering (verses 12-13)

> I will surely gather all of you, Jacob; I will surely bring together the remnant of Israel. I will bring them together like sheep in a pen, like a flock in its pasture; the place will throng with people.

By righteous, God doesn't mean morally perfect people. Jacob himself was a deceiver. "Sheep" are foolish creatures. Rather, God wants people who follow His orders as one unified flock—not independent rebels, but soldiers marching in unity to serve His gospel.

The "remnant" is small—a narrow gate, not many.

> The One who breaks open the way will go up before them; they will break through the gate and go out. Their King will pass through before them, the Lord at their head.

This prophesies the Messiah—the King who goes before His people, who breaks through the barriers of exile and death itself. Jesus, who went to the cross against all odds and rose victorious on the third day.

### A Greater Example

Consider how one man, despite overwhelming opposition, refused to compromise his convictions. Though experts said it was impossible, though celebrities, media, and elites opposed him, though courts and institutions fought against him, he stood alone. Not pandering to the powerful, not changing his message to please people. He kept the same conviction, and eventually, the people changed their minds.

We have an infinitely greater hope in Jesus. Yet we think that if we just blend in with the world—wearing their clothes, taking their holidays, buying their houses, driving their cars, attending their schools—we'll be accepted. It doesn't work that way.

You are accepted by Christ. That is enough.

It's lonely standing apart. But we have each other. And we will prevail despite the odds, because our King leads the way.
