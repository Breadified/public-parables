## Introduction: How We Begin

There's an interesting Chinese tradition that whenever the Lunar New Year arrives, how you spend that first day determines how the rest of your year will unfold. The day before, families clean the house thoroughly—not out of mere tidiness, but out of superstition. If you clean on New Year's Day itself, you'll be doing chores all year. If you go hungry that day, you'll starve all year. If you fight, you'll fight all year. Don't get sick, don't cry, or you're marked for the same struggles ahead.

The superstition carries a profound truth: our beginnings shape our futures.

Many of us feel we've started on the wrong foot. Perhaps we came out of our mothers' wombs crying, wet, and cold—hardly an auspicious beginning. We look at our lives now—depressed, constantly fighting, perpetually sick, or just sleepwalking through days in a blur. We wonder: where do I fit in God's plan? It doesn't look like He's tidying up the universe in my life.

Paul's letter to the Ephesians answers this question beautifully. He shows us that God has a destiny for us, but it didn't begin on the day we were born. It began before the foundation of the world.

## 1. You Are God's Possession—Security Through the Seal

> 13 And you also were included in Christ when you heard the message of truth, the gospel of your salvation. When you believed, you were marked in him with a seal, the promised Holy Spirit, 14 who is a deposit guaranteeing our inheritance until the redemption of those who are God's possession—to the praise of his glory.

When Paul says "those who are God's possession," he's talking about us—those who have heard the gospel, believed, and are now marked with God's seal.

### The Ownership Seal

In ancient times, a seal was a mark of ownership. When someone sealed something, they were saying: "This is mine." God doesn't own you through some distant corporate arrangement. He personally owns you directly, and He puts His name on you lovingly.

But how does this work with predestination? Paul explains:

> 11 In him we were also chosen, having been predestined according to the plan of him who works out everything in conformity with the purpose of his will, 12 in order that we, who were the first to put our hope in Christ, might be for the praise of his glory.

We are chosen. Predestined. Chosen before the beginning of time—personally known by God before we even existed, before we were a twinkle in our parents' eyes.

This raises a natural question: How can God choose us when we chose to believe? The answer isn't quietism (passive fatalism where God does everything) or Pelagianism (we do everything and God isn't in control). The answer is **compatibilism**: God freely chooses us to freely choose Him. Our wills are compatible with His will.

God is the initiator. He chooses us. And through that choice, we freely respond. God will save the victim through you rescuing her. God will save that person through you sharing the gospel. God will provide food through you working. You are not passive; you are His chosen instrument.

### What This Means

You are not an unwanted gift sitting next to a Christmas tree, wondering how to get rid of you. You are not an accident of your parents, nor a Christian merely because your parents are Christian. You are not simply a by-product of your experiences or the chance invitation to church.

You are like Woody with "Andy" written on the sole of your boot—owned, loved, marked as His. Your name is written in His book, written in blood through redemption.

**Application:** If you've ever felt like a nobody—not chosen for the school team, not picked for leadership, not selected for the formal—know this: God has chosen you to be blameless and His child. Not because of your goodness, but because of His love. This is an amazing privilege.

The second implication of predestination is humility. Any good thing you do comes from God. It is by His will alone that you live in right relationship with Him. When you see others struggling to obey God, don't look down on them. It is only by God's blessing and grace that you are any different.

## 2. He Is Your Possession—Access to Heavenly Benefits

We get more than assurance. We receive both heavenly and earthly benefits. Because we are possessed by God, we possess God.

### The Holy Spirit as God's Presence

> When you believed, you were marked in him with a seal, the promised Holy Spirit, 14 who is a deposit guaranteeing our inheritance...

The Holy Spirit is God's presence dwelling in us. God is three persons: the Father is everywhere, the Son was located in Jesus, and the Spirit is located in every believer. This means part of Him is in us.

### The Deposit—A Guarantee of Inheritance

A deposit is a partial payment that promises the full payment to come. When you buy a house or put something on layaway, you pay a deposit. That 10% tells the world: "This is mine. Hands off—I've claimed it."

God says the same thing about us. Everyone hands off—this child is mine. And unlike people who might lose interest or run out of money, God never changes His mind nor runs out of resources. He always keeps His promises and cannot break them. If He's put a deposit on you, it's for real—as good as 100% sold.

### What We Inherit

A heavenly inheritance awaits us. All material possessions are waiting in heaven. As Revelation tells us:

> 7:16 Never again will they hunger; never will they thirst. The sun will not beat down on them, nor any scorching heat.

We sometimes act as though our possessions in this life are the end-all and be-all. We hoard, we cry over lost things, we grieve like children over broken toys. But the mature understand: if you lose something here, you'll get it back in heaven. Those bad days? They're only this life. The next will be better.

Paul wants us to appreciate this deeply. He prays:

> 15 For this reason, ever since I heard about your faith in the Lord Jesus and your love for all God's people, 16 I have not stopped giving thanks for you, remembering you in my prayers. 17 I keep asking that the God of our Lord Jesus Christ, the glorious Father, may give you the Spirit of wisdom and revelation, so that you may know him better. 18 I pray that the eyes of your heart may be enlightened in order that you may know the hope to which he has called you, 18 the riches of his glorious inheritance in his holy people.

## 3. The Power That Belongs to You

> 19 And his incomparably great power for us who believe. 20 That power is the same as the mighty strength he exerted when he raised Christ from the dead and seated him at his right hand in the heavenly realms.

The power that raised Jesus from the dead is the same power available to us today. How do we possess this power? Primarily through the Bible.

### The Power of the Resurrection Through God's Word

The Bible is the means by which the eyes of our heart are enlightened. The Holy Spirit works through God's Word. His Word convicts us of sin. It convicts us of the truth of the gospel. It continues to convince us that what we read is true, moving us to act upon it and become holy and blameless.

> Ephesians 6:17 And take the helmet of salvation and the sword of the Spirit, which is the word of God.

> 2 Peter 1:21 For prophecy never had its origin in the human will, but prophets, though human, spoke from God as they were carried along by the Holy Spirit.

> John 14:26 But the Advocate, the Holy Spirit, whom the Father will send in my name, will teach you all things and will remind you of everything I have said to you.

The power of the resurrection raises us from spiritual death to spiritual life. In the future, it will raise us from physical death to physical life. And today, it can raise us from a corrupt and dead way of life to life itself.

### This Power Is for Now, Not Just Later

> 21 Far above all rule and authority, power and dominion, and every name that is invoked, not only in the present age but also in the one to come.

Notice: the power operates "in the present age"—not just in the age to come. Western civilization itself is evidence of this power. Through knowledge and enlightenment rooted in Christian values, societies flourish. The Bible is the one document that gave rise to America. It's the crib sheet that enables you to pass the selective exam of life. It's the contract that gives a hundredfold return.

## 4. Yet We Ignore What We Possess

We possess God. And yet we ignore Him.

The most important call for 2019: **Read the Bible.** Paul prays for this specifically—that the eyes of your heart would be enlightened. The way forward is to sit under God's Word.

> Isaiah 66:2 These are the ones I look on with favor: those who are humble and contrite in spirit, and who tremble at my word.

### How to Engage with Scripture

- Bring your Bible to church. If you don't have one, let us know.
- Bring notes and pencils. The act of writing keeps you awake and engaged.
- Respect the reading of Scripture. When the Bible is read aloud, stop what you're doing. It's like prayer, like the national anthem—we pause and listen.
- If you see someone dozing off, gently nudge them awake.

### For Leaders and Parents

When you're reading Scripture to young people, in Sunday school or to your children, first read it to yourself. Read it with reverence. Then share it with others.

### Community Reading

Form groups to read Scripture together:
- Campus Christian groups and ISCFs
- Scripture programs in primary schools
- Small groups in our church

Here is access to the power of resurrection. We possess it!

## Conclusion: Your True Beginning

What did you do on the first day of Lunar New Year? Perhaps you went to school or work. Perhaps you were decluttering, or getting sick, or busy with a hundred other things. But that's not your destiny. That's not God's plan.

Your ultimate plan will not end with misery and death. Instead, on that first day—even before you were born—you were **chosen**. And therefore, that will be your destiny for the rest of eternity.

Your life didn't begin on the day of your birth. It began before that, predestined in God's heart. So start this new year on the right foot:

- Pray
- Encourage one another
- Welcome people into God's family
- Gather together

God has marked you as His own. You possess Him, and He possesses you. Live like it.
