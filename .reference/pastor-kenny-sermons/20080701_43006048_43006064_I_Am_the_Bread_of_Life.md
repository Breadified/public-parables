# John 6:48-64

**Title:** I Am the Bread of Life
**Date:** 25 July 2008
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Bread: The Source of Life

Bread is deceptively simple—just flour, water, yeast, and salt—yet it sustains billions of people worldwide. In biblical times, bread wasn't merely one food among many; it was *food* itself. No bread meant no life. A breadwinner was the one who brought sustenance home to the family.

This fundamental truth about bread sets the stage for understanding Jesus' teaching. Jesus had just miraculously fed five thousand people with only two loaves and five fishes. Now he begins to explain the deeper meaning of that sign.

## "I Am the Bread of Life" (v. 48)

> **John 6:48** I am the bread of life.

Jesus isn't speaking literally—he's not made of flour or crusty on the outside and soft on the inside. This is a metaphor, and to understand it, we must look at the verses that follow.

## The Bread That Gives Life (vv. 49-50)

> **John 6:49-50** Your forefathers ate the manna in the desert, yet they died. But here is the bread that comes down from heaven, which a man may eat and not die.

Jesus reaches back into Israel's history—the Exodus, around 1462 BC. Moses led the Israelites out of Egypt toward the promised land, but because they disobeyed God, they wandered the desert for forty years. In that barren wilderness, God provided miraculous food called manna: white flakes like coriander seed that tasted like wafers made with honey, appearing each morning like dew on the ground.

Jesus invites a comparison between then and now:

- **Both provide nourishment and life** in emptiness and need. The manna sustained the Israelites in the desert; Jesus claims to give life itself.
- **Both come from heaven.** The manna appeared supernaturally; Jesus multiplied bread from thin air.
- **Both repeat God's provision.** Jesus echoes the Exodus miracle to show that his generation, like their ancestors, wanders spiritually and desperately needs sustenance.

But there's a crucial difference:

> **John 6:51** I am the living bread that came down from heaven. If anyone eats of this bread, he will live forever. This bread is my flesh, which I will give for the life of the world.

The forefathers ate manna and yet died. Everyone who ate the bread Jesus provided at the feeding would eventually die. But Jesus promises something more—a bread that offers not just earthly sustenance but **eternal life**.

## Eating Flesh: A Metaphor for Belief

> **John 6:53-56** Jesus said to them, "I tell you the truth, unless you eat the flesh of the Son of Man and drink his blood, you have no life in you. Whoever eats my flesh and drinks my blood has eternal life, and I will raise him up at the last day. For my flesh is real food and my blood is real drink. Whoever eats my flesh and drinks my blood remains in me, and I in him."

This language shocked Jesus' listeners. Literal cannibalism? That was prohibited by Old Testament law and utterly repulsive. Besides, Jesus was standing before them alive—he wasn't offering his actual body to eat.

The key to understanding this metaphor lies further in the passage:

> **John 6:63** The Spirit gives life; the flesh counts for nothing. The words I have spoken to you are spirit and they are life.

And Peter later says:

> **John 6:68** Lord, to whom shall we go? You have the words of eternal life.

**Flesh = words.** Jesus isn't talking about literal cannibalism. He's talking about consuming his words. Just as physical bread must be eaten to nourish the body, Jesus' words must be received to nourish the soul.

Think of it this way: a biography is words that describe a person. Even if you've never met someone, you can know them intimately through their words. Today, we haven't seen Jesus physically, but his words remain with us in Scripture. Through his words recorded in the Bible, we can know him.

**Eat = believe.** Just as eating bread makes it part of you, Jesus calls us to believe his words—to make them part of our inner being, our identity, our very essence.

## Application: Make Jesus' Words Your Nourishment

**For those not yet Christian:** The invitation is to metaphorically eat and taste. Believe Jesus' words when he says he knows the Father. Believe when he warns that God's anger is coming. Believe that he can protect you from that judgment through his sacrifice. The words of eternal life stand before you. Eat and live.

**For those already Christian:** Continue eating. You are what you eat. Consume Jesus' words until they become the DNA of your thinking. Let his words pump through your veins and fire through your synapses. Let Scripture govern your entire worldview.

As you digest his words:
- **Share them.** Encourage one another with the words of Jesus.
- **Live them.** Let the energy from his words move your muscles toward ethical action, godliness, and love.
- **Think deeply.** We know Jesus not through mystical experiences or rituals, but cognitively—through reading, comprehension, logical thinking, reason, discussion, and debate. Use your mind to know him. Then believe what you understand to be true and align your actions with that truth.

Make Jesus' words the very essence of you.
