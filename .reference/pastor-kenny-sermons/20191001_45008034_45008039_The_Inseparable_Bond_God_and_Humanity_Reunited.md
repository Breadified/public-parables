# Romans 8:34-39

**Title:** The Inseparable Bond: God and Humanity Reunited
**Date:** 17 October 2019
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Perfect Pairing

> 39 neither height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God that is in Christ Jesus our Lord.

Some things in life belong together. Great chefs understand this—they know which flavors complement each other to create something entirely new. Chocolate and mint, bacon and eggs, peanut butter and jam—these pairings are inseparable by nature.

But there's a pairing far more profound than any culinary combination: humanity and the love of God. We were created to be in relationship with our Creator—a fellowship between God and his people, walking together in intimate companionship. We were meant to belong to each other.

## When Sin Separates

Yet something has gone terribly wrong. Sin has fractured this perfect pairing.

When Adam and Eve ate from the forbidden tree, they weren't simply breaking a rule. They were rebelling against the fundamental reality of creation—that God is Creator and we are his creation, that we belong to him. Their disobedience created a separation, not one of distance but of relationship. A married couple can lie in the same bed yet be emotionally worlds apart when trust has been broken. So it is with humanity and God.

Sin creates an infinite chasm. We sin not against something small, but against the infinite God himself. The separation becomes eternal—separated by infinite height, separated by infinite depth. No human effort can bridge this gap. No plane can fly high enough, no submarine can dive deep enough to heal the breach we've created through our rebellion.

Even more troubling, we often fail to recognize the real problem. When disaster strikes and we cry out "God, where are you?"—we miss the point. God's silence isn't the issue; our sin is. We ignore him, use his name carelessly, hurt his creation, and then blame him when he allows our choices to run their course. The separation we experience is one we've created ourselves.

## The Divine Solution

But God does not want this separation to continue. He refuses to leave us alienated from him.

> 34 Who then is the one who condemns? No one. Christ Jesus who died—more than that, who was raised to life—is at the right hand of God and is also interceding for us.

God himself showed up two thousand years ago. Jesus, the infinite God, entered human history and made an extraordinary claim: he is God. And then he did something that defies all logic—he died for us.

**He died:** This is sacrificial love in its purest form. God himself takes upon himself the punishment we deserve. Forgiveness isn't merely pardoning a wrong; it's absorbing the hurt into yourself rather than returning judgment. Jesus bore our condemnation.

**He was raised to life:** Because he is the infinite God, he swallowed death itself and defeated it once and for all. Death could not contain him. In his resurrection, he conquered the power that enslaves us all.

**He intercedes:** Now, seated at God's right hand, Jesus pleads our case before the Father. Having paid the price for our sins through his sacrifice, he intercedes for us—not just for past sins, but for all our failures yet to come. When we repent and ask for forgiveness, Jesus brings our petition before God, and God forgives us based on his payment, his shed blood.

Through Christ's work, the impossible has become possible. We who were incompatible with God have been made compatible again. The infinite chasm has been bridged. We are reconciled.

## Inseparability Restored

As a result of Christ's sacrifice, we are brought back together with God—and this reunion creates an inseparable bond.

> 35 Who shall separate us from the love of Christ? Shall trouble or hardship or persecution or famine or nakedness or danger or sword?

Paul lists the forces that might threaten to tear us from God's love:
- Famine
- Nakedness  
- Danger
- Sword
- Persecution

And yet through all these trials, we remain inseparable from his love. Christians throughout history and around the world have faced unimaginable hardship, yet their bond with Christ remains unbreakable. Most persecuted Christians live not in the West but in Africa, Asia, and the Middle East—yet their faith endures.

> 37 No, in all these things we are more than conquerors through him who loved us. 38 For I am convinced that neither death nor life, neither angels nor demons, neither the present nor the future, nor any powers,

Nothing—no power in heaven or earth, no force in time or eternity—can sever us from God's love once we're united to him through Christ.

## The Beautiful Mystery

Marriage, when understood rightly, is a symbol of this inseparability. It represents God's will joining two people together so firmly that no human power can truly undo what God has done. But marriage itself points to something greater: the inseparable bond between Christ and his Church, between God and his redeemed people.

This is the mark of a master chef—to bring together seemingly incompatible things and create something beautiful. Chocolate and chili. Kimchi and cheddar cheese. Salt and caramel. Strange pairings, yet when combined by a skilled hand, they become magnificent.

So it is with God and us. We are an unlikely pairing—the infinite Creator and finite sinners, holiness and rebellion, purity and corruption. Yet through Jesus, God has brought us together. He takes our dirt—our sin, our shame, our brokenness—upon himself. Like a chef who bears all the mess of creating a beautiful meal, Jesus bears all our mess so that we might be clean and fit for fellowship with God.

That is the power of the gospel: two enemies brought together. Two seemingly disparate realities—heaven and earth, divine and human, holy and sinful—joined forever through the sacrificial love of Christ.

**And it is this love that binds us inseparably to God.**
