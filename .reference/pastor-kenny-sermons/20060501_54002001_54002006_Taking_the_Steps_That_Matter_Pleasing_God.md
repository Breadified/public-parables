## What Does It Take to Please God?

Many people wonder what truly pleases God. Some assume it's keeping the Ten Commandments—avoiding theft, coveting, and other moral failures. Others imagine it comes through meditation and chanting, as various religions teach. Still others believe that performing charitable works and good deeds will earn God's favor. Or perhaps, like many in our culture, we simply hope that being decent people who don't harm others is enough.

But what if our understanding of pleasing God is fundamentally wrong?

### A Sobering Reality

On the night of October 12, 2002, two men—Amrozi and his brother Imron—believed they were pleasing God. They purchased a Mitsubishi van and filled it with a tonne of potassium chlorate, sulfuric acid, and aluminum powder. They removed the rear seats to make room for filing cabinets containing these chemicals, stripped the air conditioning unit for resale, and filed off the chassis number to conceal the vehicle's identity.

They drove from East Java through Surabaya to Bali, where they had carefully selected their target: Legian Street. One of their recruits wore a suicide vest into Paddy's nightclub. At 11:07 PM, he detonated himself. Thirty seconds later, the van exploded in a fireball so massive it registered on Indonesian earthquake-measuring equipment.

In an instant, 202 people died. Eighty-eight were Australians. Three hundred fifty more were horribly burned or injured. The Kuta nightclub district was devastated. Amrozi sat at home in East Java and cheered when he heard the news on television. Imron drove in circles at the scene, awed by the destruction they had wrought.

For these men, this was what pleasing God meant—or so they believed.

The question haunts us: What truly pleases God?

## What Actually Pleases God

Scripture gives us a clear answer. Paul writes to Timothy about four essential things that please God.

### 1. Prayer Pleases the Personal God

> **1 Timothy 2:1-3 (ESV)**
> 
> First of all, then, I urge that supplications, prayers, intercessions, and thanksgivings be made for all people, for kings and all who are in high positions, that we may lead a peaceful and quiet life, godly and dignified in every way. This is good, and it is pleasing in the sight of God our Saviour.

Paul commands us to pray for all people—especially for kings and government leaders—so that we might live in peace and dignity. This reveals something profound: **God cares deeply about our daily lives and welcomes our prayers.**

We often underestimate how crucial governments are. Even imperfect governments are better than anarchy. Consider what happened in Baghdad when Saddam's regime fell and no authority remained in power: citizens looted, rioted, destroyed, and created chaos. The absence of order led to widespread suffering. Governments, despite their flaws, enable us to live in peace, make meaningful decisions, and experience dignity.

God desires for us to ask Him to care for our leaders—not because He doesn't know what will happen, but because **He wants relationship with us**. He is not a distant deity who demands we leave Him alone. Rather, He is like a father who genuinely wants to hear about your day, who cares about your peace and well-being.

It pleases this personal God when we bring our concerns to Him in prayer, trusting that He cares about our circumstances and our world.

### 2. People Being Saved Pleases God

> **1 Timothy 2:4**
> 
> God desires all people to be saved and to come to the knowledge of the truth.

There is something far greater that moves God's heart: **the salvation of people.** God's deepest desire is that all would be saved.

But saved from what? The apostle Paul answers:

> **1 Timothy 1:15**
> 
> The saying is trustworthy and deserving of full acceptance, that Christ Jesus came into the world to save sinners.

When we speak of sin, we often think only of moral wrongs—not stealing, not swearing, not drinking. But sin is far more fundamental than bad behavior. **Sin is rebellion against God's authority.** It's not merely breaking a speed limit; it's treason against the King of the universe.

Consider Amrozi's actions: He didn't simply commit murder. He rejected Indonesian authority and sought to establish his own rule through terror. Similarly, we all rebel against God. We ignore His right to rule our lives. We create our own moral codes and live by them instead of His. We chase our own desires rather than His will. And like any ruler facing rebellion in his kingdom, **God's justice demands a response. The consequence of rebellion against God is death.**

This seems harsh to us, yet we understand it when faced with earthly injustice. Listen to the words of those who lost loved ones in Bali:

David Spike Stewart lost his 29-year-old son. On his 56th birthday, his only wish was to hear that Amrozi would be executed. If he could ask for more, he wanted to join the firing squad himself.

Maria celebrated her first wedding anniversary with the deaths of her twin sister, elder sister, bridesmaid, and cousin. They died in the bombings. She said: "I can't wait for their execution. I'd like some of my blood on the bullets used, on behalf of my sisters."

Danny Hanley's two daughters died in the explosions. His daughter Simone suffered for more than 50 days from agonizing burns before she died. "We are happy and relieved with that decision," he said. "Amrozi has killed over 200 people, including two of my daughters, my only daughters, and he has got his just reward ... death."

The anger these families feel is righteous. They demand justice.

**This is how God sees us.** God is likewise angry at our rebellion. We deserve death. His own Son was killed by sinners—by us. When God cleans house, He doesn't compromise. He doesn't hide the dirt in a cupboard or under a bed as we do. He cleanses completely, down to the atomic level. He promises to remake creation, removing every trace of sin and rebellion.

We are no different from Amrozi. Our sin, whether great or small, separates us from a holy God and brings death.

Yet God does not desire our death. He desires our salvation. And salvation comes through one path: **the knowledge of the truth.** What is this truth?

### 3. Jesus Pleases God

> **1 Timothy 2:5-6**
> 
> For there is one God, and there is one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus, who gave himself as a ransom for all, which is the testimony given at the proper time.

**Jesus is the mediator—the peace broker between God and humanity.**

Imagine the United Nations Secretary General Kofi Annan attempting to mediate between George Bush and Saddam Hussein. Saddam has rebelled against America. Bush demands punishment. Sanctions are in place. Annan travels between nations, seeks alternatives, makes appeals, and desperately works for peace. It's an extraordinarily difficult task.

Jesus stands between us and God in a similar role, but infinitely greater. We have rebelled against our Creator. God's justice demands death. But Jesus voluntarily steps forward. He goes to the cross and pays the ransom for us. He is tortured and crucified. On those two beams, His infinite worth satisfies God's infinite justice and pays for our infinite sins.

Kofi Annan might arrange foreign aid, coordinate with the IMF, offer money—but he would never die for a distant nation he barely knows. **Jesus gives His life to effect peace completely and permanently.** He is satisfied. God's justice is satisfied. Peace is made.

Moreover, **Jesus is the unique mediator.** There is one God and one mediator. There are no alternative paths to God. Your good works cannot broker peace with God. Your moral efforts cannot satisfy His justice. There are not many ways to reach Him. Jesus is the one and only way.

And because of Jesus' perfect sacrifice, **God is pleased—not with death itself, but with the fact that justice is finally done.** God raises Jesus from death and gives Him the highest honor, the best titles, and the best seat in heaven.

Only Jesus can truly please God. His prayers are heard. His sacrifice is accepted. Through Him alone, sinners have access to the Father.

### 4. We Can Please God Through Jesus

This is the incredible truth: We cannot please God on our own. Our prayers as rebellious sinners are not heard. Our good works cannot satisfy His justice. **Only Jesus can please God—and through Him, we can too.**

God says these things please Him:
- Prayer, because He cares for us personally
- People being saved
- Most of all, Jesus, who makes salvation and prayer possible

When we trust in Jesus as our Lord and Mediator, **we become part of God's great desire to save all people.** And this is good and pleasing to God.

## The Decision Before Us

Consider Imron, Amrozi's brother. Imron was equally guilty. He had purchased the van with Amrozi. He was the better-trained terrorist, educated in Pakistan's Islamic schools and trained in Afghan militia camps. He had built the bombs and driven the suicide bombers to their target. By all measure, he was more culpable than Amrozi.

Yet Imron did not receive the death sentence. Why? **Because he repented.** He apologized. He admitted his guilt, abandoned his appeals, and begged for mercy.

We too are guilty. We have rebelled against God. And like Imron, we must do the same: **beg for forgiveness.** Stop making excuses. Stop questioning how God should have done things differently. Stop waiting for Him to prove Himself to you. Recognize your guilt and your helplessness.

Ask God for mercy. Turn from your sin. Put Jesus in charge as the Ruler of your life.

### For Those Already Following Jesus

If you are a Christian here tonight, continue following Jesus. He is your Lord and the source of your salvation.

### For Those Hearing This for the First Time

If this is your first time hearing the good news of Jesus, take your time to understand. Let it sink in. But know that understanding requires action. It may mean finding a church to attend regularly. It may mean finally joining that Bible study your Christian friend has been inviting you to. It may mean having a conversation with that friend who has been trying to share their faith with you. They are not claiming to be perfect—they simply want to share the truth that pleases God: that He desires to save you through Jesus.

### For Those Ready to Decide

For some of you, this may be the moment. You understand the good news. You've thought about it. You're wondering how to get right with God. 

**Tonight is your night.** Do not try to justify your past actions. Do not delay. We live in peace and freedom—a gift from God. We are not in war or chaos. We are not facing our final moment. This is the best time to decide.

Recognize that judgment has already occurred—Jesus took it upon Himself at the cross. But through His death, there is now a moment of grace, an opportunity to get right with God before all things end. **Will you take it?**

## A Prayer That Pleases God

If you want to please God through Jesus, pray this prayer with genuine intention. It's not a magical formula, but an honest expression of your desire to turn to God. You can pray this anytime, anywhere—you don't need me to lead you. But if you're ready tonight, here's what to say:

> **Dear God my Saviour and Lord,**
>
> Thank you for our government, for the peace and dignified lives they provide, and we ask that they continue to govern wisely.
>
> I am sorry that I have not pleased you in the past and see why you are angry with me.
>
> I thank you for sending your Son, Jesus, to save me by pleasing you on my behalf.
>
> Please help me now to please you by following Jesus as my Lord.
>
> In Jesus' name we pray, Amen.

If you've prayed this prayer with a sincere heart, know this: **God has heard you. He has forgiven you.** It may not feel like it yet, but the angels are rejoicing. God is pleased.

Tell someone about your decision. Your friends would want to know. I would like to know. We will connect you with someone who can help you grow in your faith as a Christian.
