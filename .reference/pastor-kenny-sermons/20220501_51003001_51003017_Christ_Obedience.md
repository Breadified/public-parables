# Colossians 3:1-17

**Title:** Christ Obedience
**Date:** 22 May 2022
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction: True Unity

During election season, politicians speak of unity, but their actions reveal deep divisions. Yet the Bible speaks of a different kind of unity—not political or imposed, but spiritual and transformative. This passage teaches us what it means to be truly united in Christ.

## 1. United in Position (3:1-4)

### The Foundation: Raised with Christ

> Since, then, you have been raised with Christ, set your hearts on things above, where Christ is, seated at the right hand of God. Set your minds on things above, not on earthly things.

When Paul says we have been "raised with Christ," he means we share in his resurrection. Christ himself was raised by God's power and is now seated at the right hand of God as judge. This Thursday is Ascension Day—the day we commemorate Christ's ascent to the Father's right hand.

If we are united with Christ, then we too are raised with him. We are no longer of the earth, of the ground, of the soil—that place symbolizing death. Instead, we have died to our old selves and been buried with him.

> For you died, and your life is now hidden with Christ in God.

"Hidden" carries the image of being buried—lost from sight, gone from view. Yet this is not a tragic end, but a glorious transition.

### The Tension: Already and Not Yet

There seems to be a contradiction here. Verse 1 speaks of being raised in past tense, yet verse 4 promises we will appear in glory—future tense. Are we raised or not?

The answer lies in understanding the "already, but not yet" reality of our salvation. Our spiritual resurrection is already accomplished—we have died and been raised with Christ. But our physical resurrection is still to come. We continue to struggle with sin; we live in a world filled with conflict where we often vote "for the lesser of two evils" rather than the ideal.

Yet here is the power of the gospel: even though the future promise is not yet realized, we can start living as if it is. Just as a homeowner with a mortgage from the bank calls it "my house" and lives accordingly—treating it as their own, following certain rules, caring for it—so too we can live now as those who have already been raised with Christ.

> When Christ, who is your life, appears, then you also will appear with him in glory.

The key is that this future reality should shape our present choices. Where will you be seated? There are only two kinds of tickets left: free entry (register your interest), or outside the kingdom. Just because something is future doesn't mean it doesn't affect us now.

### The Mindset: Set Your Hearts and Minds

> Set your minds on things above, not on earthly things.

The Greek word *phroneo* means more than just thinking—it refers to attitude, understanding, mindset, the way you frame reality. It's the root of words like "frenetic" and "phrenology." We're not called to physically leave the earth, but to adopt a heavenly perspective in how we think and act.

Since we have been raised with Christ, the natural response is to order our lives accordingly—to seek treasures in heaven (Matthew 6), to live with a mindset aligned with heaven's values, not earth's.

## 2. United in Morality (3:5-17)

Paul now provides concrete guidance on how this heavenly mindset works out in our daily lives. He references three prohibitions from Colossians 2:21—"Do not handle! Do not taste! Do not touch!"—and reinterprets them for Christian living.

### A. Do Not Touch: Sexual Purity (3:5-7)

The command "do not touch" speaks to sexual morality. Some fear sexual immorality so much that they forbid marriage altogether, thinking celibacy is the solution. But Paul's answer is not avoidance—it's proper expression through marriage.

> Put to death, therefore, whatever belongs to your earthly nature: sexual immorality, impurity, lust, evil desires and greed, which is idolatry.

**Sexual immorality** (*porneia*) means sex outside of marriage—the root of our word "porn." Fornication is sexual sin before marriage; adultery is sexual sin after marriage. This is why Mary was accused of fornication before her betrothal to Joseph.

**Impurity** literally means uncleanliness. Modern media and medicine hide the diseases associated with hookup culture, but it remains a destructive habit. Jesus compared the hypocritical Pharisees to "whitewashed tombs, which look beautiful on the outside but on the inside are full of the bones of the dead and everything unclean" (Matthew 23:27). Medical science confirms that more than 30 different bacteria, viruses, and parasites are transmitted through sexual contact—eight major pathogens linked to sexually transmitted diseases, four curable and four incurable (hepatitis B, herpes, HIV, HPV).

**Lusts, evil desires, and covetousness** all point to unrestrained sexual desire. Greed is called idolatry because when we worship our desires, we make gods of them.

> Because of these, the wrath of God is coming. You used to walk in these ways, in the life you once lived.

The emphasis falls on "once lived"—that's your past. Many of us here have struggled with sexual sin or the things that lead to it: making out, time alone in bedrooms, late nights in cars. We are the grandchildren of the sexual revolution, and while our bodies remain capable of sin, that's not who we are anymore.

> We repent and no longer live this way. We go to the feet of Christ, and by his blood we are picked up from the tomb—that place of death with all its uncleanness—and brought to his side.

The call is clear: stay away from sexual immorality.

### B. Do Not Taste: Guard Your Words (3:8-11)

The command "do not taste" speaks to what comes out of our mouths. Jesus taught that what comes out of the mouth reveals the heart:

> Whatever enters the mouth goes into the stomach and then out of the body. But the things that come out of the mouth come from the heart, and these make a person unclean. For out of the heart come evil thoughts, murder, adultery, sexual immorality, theft, false testimony, and slander. (Matthew 15:17-19)

Therefore:

> But now you must also rid yourselves of all such things as these: anger, rage, malice, slander, and filthy language from your lips. Do not lie to each other, since you have taken off your old self with its practices and have put on the new self, which is being renewed in knowledge in the image of its Creator.

We must be careful with our words—always polite, always respectful. We must avoid ad hominem attacks, gossip, foul language, and abusive speech. When you're frustrated and angry, your spontaneous words reveal your true heart. If swear words come out in a moment of stress, that's concerning enough. But if those same words come out when you're frustrated with a person, understand that you're preparing yourself to hurt them—setting yourself up to injure your brother or sister, your spouse, your child, or your parent.

> Here there is no Gentile or Jew, circumcised or uncircumcised, barbarian, Scythian, slave or free, but Christ is all, and is in all.

In Christ, all earthly distinctions fade. Our unity transcends culture, status, and background.

### C. Do Not Handle: Draw Near! (3:12-17)

The third command "do not handle" might suggest we should stay away, keep our distance. But Paul's interpretation is the opposite—he calls us to draw near.

In the Old Testament, certain places were holy and untouchable. Moses trembled at Mount Sinai, where "if even an animal touches the mountain, it must be stoned to death" (Hebrews 12:20). The barrier between God and humanity was absolute.

But now:

> You have come to Mount Zion, to the city of the living God, the heavenly Jerusalem. You have come to thousands upon thousands of angels in joyful assembly, to the church of the firstborn, whose names are written in heaven. You have come to God, the Judge of all, to the spirits of the righteous made perfect, to Jesus the mediator of a new covenant, and to the sprinkled blood that speaks a better word than the blood of Abel. (Hebrews 12:22-24)

When you gather with the church, you come to the earthly expression of the heavenly assembly. This may not feel that way, but it is true. Hebrews 13 confirms this: we are called to love one another, show hospitality to strangers (who might be angels), and remember those in prison and those mistreated as if we were with them.

Rather than avoid each other, we must draw near and work through conflict together. The solution isn't isolation but engagement and reconciliation.

> Therefore, as God's chosen people, holy and dearly loved, clothe yourselves with compassion, kindness, humility, gentleness and patience. Bear with each other and forgive one another if any of you has a grievance against someone. Forgive as the Lord forgave you.

We are not yet in heaven, so we will disagree. We will fight. We will sin against each other. But we go through it together with resilience. We don't always do well, but we're a bunch of sinners huddling together, seeking shelter from the guilt of sin—sinners serving other sinners. I don't think we will ever get it perfectly right, but if we persevere, we'll get closer each time.

> Let the peace of Christ rule in your hearts, since as members of one body you were called to peace. And be thankful. Let the message of Christ dwell among you richly as you teach and admonish one another with all wisdom through psalms, hymns, and songs from the Spirit, singing to God with gratitude in your hearts. And whatever you do, whether in word or deed, do it all in the name of the Lord Jesus, giving thanks to God the Father through him.

## True Unity

> And over all these virtues put on love, which binds them all together in perfect unity.

This is true unity—not the political unity politicians promise, but unity rooted in obedience and submission to the risen Christ. Freedom from the penalty of sin does not mean freedom from his rules.

- Do not touch—not about marriage, but about rejecting sexual immorality
- Do not taste—not about food, but about refusing foul language
- Do not handle—not about staying away, but about drawing near!

True unity is found when believers covenant together to live under Christ's lordship, supporting one another through grace, bearing with one another in love, and advancing together toward the day when Christ appears in glory and we appear with him.
