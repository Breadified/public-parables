# Daniel 10-12

**Title:** A Heavenly War
**Date:** August 14, 2011
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Vision: A Heavenly War Accompanies Earthly War

Daniel receives his final vision during a time of personal fasting and repentance. On the twenty-fourth day of the first month, standing by the Tigris River, he encounters a heavenly being—a man clothed in linen with a belt of finest gold. The vision is so overwhelming that Daniel loses all strength, yet this angelic messenger recognizes him as "highly esteemed," affirming that his prayers have been heard since the first day he sought understanding and humbled himself before God.

The messenger comes to explain what will happen to Daniel's people in the future. But there is more at work here than mere human history.

## The Spiritual Realm: A War Beyond What We See

We live in a world where materialism—the belief that only what we can measure and observe is real—has taken hold. But this is a profound mistake. Love, relationships, history, and God Himself cannot be placed in a beaker or tested in a laboratory, yet they are deeply real. There exists a metaphysical dimension to reality that our materialistic age refuses to acknowledge.

As Paul writes: "Our struggle is not against flesh and blood, but against the rulers, against the authorities, against the powers of this dark world and against the spiritual forces of evil in the heavenly realms" (Ephesians 6:12).

In Daniel's vision, the angelic messenger speaks of warfare in this spiritual realm. He has been resisted by "the prince of the Persian kingdom" for twenty-one days until Michael, one of the chief princes, came to help him. The messenger warns that he will return to fight against the prince of Persia and then the prince of Greece. This is not merely political conflict between earthly kingdoms—it is spiritual warfare at the highest level.

This vision echoes what John would later see in Revelation: "There was war in heaven. Michael and his angels fought against the dragon, and the dragon and his angels fought back. But he was not strong enough, and they lost their place in heaven. The great dragon was hurled down—that ancient serpent called the devil, or Satan" (Revelation 12:7-9).

## The Earthly Conflict: Kingdoms at War

Chapter 11 provides detailed prophecy of wars to come. After Alexander the Great's death, his empire is divided among his generals. The Seleucid kingdom in the north and the Ptolemaic kingdom in the south will wage war repeatedly, and Israel—positioned between them—will be caught in the middle, eventually coming under the rule of Antiochus Epiphanes IV.

But these earthly wars, though they seem significant to us, are merely symptoms of the greater spiritual battle being waged in heaven. Wars have always been with us: first men with clubs and sticks raiding villages, then peasants with fire, then knights in armor, then cannons and military formations, then aircraft, and in the twentieth century, nuclear weapons and space-age technology. Each generation thinks its warfare is ultimate, yet new forms continually emerge. The cycle continues endlessly.

Yet none of these earthly conflicts truly matter compared to the war in heaven.

## Michael: The Heavenly Prince

Daniel's vision points to a pivotal figure: Michael, "the great prince who protects your people."

> "At that time Michael, the great prince who protects your people, will arise. There will be a time of distress such as has not happened from the beginning of nations until then. But at that time your people—everyone whose name is found written in the book—will be delivered." (Daniel 12:1)

Michael appears throughout Scripture as Israel's advocate and defender, the one who fights against Satan himself. But there is something more here. The language suggests that Michael points to Jesus Christ, the ultimate deliverer and the one who will secure final victory over Satan.

Jesus Himself testified to this spiritual warfare. When accused by the teachers of the law of being demon-possessed, He responded with a parable:

> "No one can enter a strong man's house and carry off his possessions unless he first ties up the strong man. Then he can rob his house." (Mark 3:27)

Jesus is that stronger man. Where He goes, demons flee. Those held captive by sin are set free. Satan's kingdom is being dismantled from within.

## The Victory: Jesus and the Cross

Jesus fought the final battle against Satan—and won. That victory was won on the cross.

> "Having disarmed the powers and authorities, [Christ] made a public spectacle of them, triumphing over them by the cross." (Colossians 2:15)

Satan believed he had triumphed when Jesus died. But the cross was not Satan's victory—it was his defeat. Jesus rose from the dead, ascended to heaven, and now sits at God's right hand "with angels, authorities and powers in submission to him" (1 Peter 3:22).

It is finished.

## For Us Now: Living in the Victory

For those who belong to Jesus, the great prince, there is peace. The victory He won extends to all who trust in Him.

**We are delivered.** We are no longer under Satan's dominion or threat. Our names are written in the book of life.

**We are raised.** Daniel's vision promises that "multitudes who sleep in the dust of the earth will awake: some to everlasting life, others to shame and everlasting contempt. Those who are wise will shine like the brightness of the heavens, and those who lead many to righteousness, like the stars for ever and ever" (Daniel 12:2-3).

**We are at peace with God.** We need not fear death, the occult, or demons. As Paul assures us:

> "Neither death nor life, neither angels nor demons, neither the present nor the future, nor any powers, neither height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God that is in Christ Jesus our Lord." (Romans 8:38-39)

Christians cannot be demon-possessed. The battle has been fought on our behalf, and we are on the winning side.

## For Us Now: Living in the Not Yet

And yet we must remember: we live between the times.

The heavenly war has been won. Satan has been defeated. But the full manifestation of that victory has not yet come to earth. We live as those who have received news of victory, but we still see the remnants of the defeated enemy's resistance.

Consider the story of Lieutenant Hiroo Onoda. In 1944, he was sent to conduct guerrilla warfare on a remote Philippine island. Unfortunately, no one ever told him the war had ended. For twenty-nine years, Onoda continued to live in the jungle, hiding and surviving, unaware that his nation had surrendered. When he finally emerged in 1972, the war had been over for nearly three decades.

Satan is like that soldier. He has not yet fully received the message that he is defeated. He wanders the earth toothless, in a frayed uniform held together by vines, eating bananas with a rusty rifle containing no bullets. He cannot kill you, but he can still cause damage—a good gumming with what remains.

Therefore, we must be vigilant. Christians are still at war, though victory is assured.

## The Armor of God

Paul calls us to take our stand against the devil's schemes:

> "Put on the full armor of God so that you can take your stand against the devil's schemes. For our struggle is not against flesh and blood, but against the rulers, against the authorities, against the powers of this dark world and against the spiritual forces of evil in the heavenly realms. Therefore put on the full armor of God, so that when the day of evil comes, you may be able to stand your ground, and after you have done everything, to stand. Stand firm then, with the belt of truth buckled around your waist, with the breastplate of righteousness in place, and with your feet fitted with the readiness that comes from the gospel of peace. In addition to all this, take up the shield of faith, with which you can extinguish all the flaming arrows of the evil one. Take the helmet of salvation and the sword of the Spirit, which is the word of God. And pray in the Spirit on all occasions with all kinds of prayers and requests. With this in mind, be alert and always keep on praying for all the saints." (Ephesians 6:11-18)

This armor is not mystical or strange. It is grounded in the gospel, in truth, and in prayer. Our weapons are not the weapons of the world—not politics, power, or earthly force. Rather:

> "Though we live in the world, we do not wage war as the world does. Our weapons are not the weapons of the world. On the contrary, they have divine power to demolish strongholds. We demolish arguments and every pretension that sets itself up against the knowledge of God, and we take captive every thought to make it obedient to Christ." (2 Corinthians 10:3-5)

Our mission is persuasion through the gospel. We take captives for Christ, not through force but through the power of God's Word.

## In a War-Torn World

It is indeed a war zone out there. The news reports of violence, looting, and destruction remind us that we live in a fallen world. Soldiers die in distant lands. Innocent people suffer. The cycle of human conflict continues.

Yet we know something the world does not: that the true battle—the one that determines all others—has already been won in heaven. The Prince has triumphed. Satan has been defeated. And those who stand behind Jesus Christ live in the peace and security of His victory, even as earthly wars rage around us.

We may not understand everything, as Daniel himself admitted. We still have many questions. But we can trust three truths:

1. **God is the true King.** He rules not through earthly power but through His Son.
2. **He rules through the Son of Man coming in the clouds.** His kingdom is not of this world, yet it will ultimately encompass all things.
3. **This Man protects His people.** Death itself cannot separate us from His love and protection.

Get behind your Prince. Live in His victory. And remain vigilant until He comes.
