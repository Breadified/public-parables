# Romans 1:1-17

**Title:** God's Project  
**Date:** 21 October  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Power of a Vision

The Gospel is transformative news—news with the power to move nations and people toward action. Consider Singapore's remarkable story: fifty years ago, it was a swamp with no natural resources, no clean water, and no hope. Yet because of a vision—Lee Kuan Yew's belief in what Singapore could become—the nation transformed itself from third-world to first-world status. Today it ranks among the wealthiest nations per capita, commands fierce loyalty from its citizens, and serves as a model of what vision-driven belief can accomplish.

For Christians, we have our own founding story that drives us forward: the Gospel, the good news of Jesus Christ. The Book of Romans has always been this kind of transformative text—it drove Martin Luther's reformation, it shapes how we understand God's plan, and it conscripts us into God's great project.

The central question Paul raises in Romans is this: **What is the good news that drives Christians?**

## Jesus: Both Human and Divine

> **Romans 1:3-4 (ESV)**  
> concerning his Son, who was descended from David according to the flesh and was declared to be the Son of God in power according to the Spirit of holiness by his resurrection from the dead, Jesus Christ our Lord

The Gospel centers on Jesus Christ, and this good news requires understanding two essential truths about him:

**Jesus is Human**

Jesus descended from David according to the flesh. He is fully human, which matters profoundly: *what is not incarnated is not redeemed.* Jesus needed to be human because it is humans who have sinned. For atonement to be just, a human must bear the penalty of human sin. He speaks our language, takes our form, identifies with our condition. This is good news because God meets us where we are.

**Jesus is God**

Yet Jesus cannot be merely human, for a human alone is powerless to save. Jesus is divine—declared to be the Son of God in power through his resurrection. This is essential because only God can forgive sin. Sin is ultimately an offense against God himself, so only God can bear its penalty. If a third party paid the price, justice would not be satisfied. But when God himself bears the cost, taking on human flesh and dying in our place, the debt is paid completely.

As C.S. Lewis beautifully expressed it: *"The Son of God became a man that men might become sons of God."*

This is the heart of the Gospel: Jesus is both the Judge and the one judged, both God and man, so that we might be redeemed.

## The Gospel Promised from the Beginning

> **Romans 1:2 (ESV)**  
> which he promised beforehand through his prophets in the holy Scriptures

The good news is not new. God promised this Gospel long ago through the prophets in the holy Scriptures. The entire Old Testament points toward Jesus. What God promised gradually throughout history, he finally and fully revealed in Jesus Christ. The Old Testament is not separate from the New—it is the promise moving toward its fulfillment, all pointing to Christ.

## The Gospel Available to All

> **Romans 1:16 (ESV)**  
> For I am not ashamed of the gospel, for it is the power of God for salvation to everyone who believes, to the Jew first and also to the Greek.

This good news is available to you. It is God's power for salvation—not just to the Jews, but to everyone who believes. There is no shame in this Gospel. We should only be ashamed of things we have no confidence in. But the Gospel of Jesus is the power of God. With such power behind it, how could anyone be ashamed?

## Paul: Set Apart for God's Project

> **Romans 1:1, 5-7 (ESV)**  
> Paul, a servant of Christ Jesus, called to be an apostle, set apart for the gospel of God... through whom we have received grace and apostleship to bring about the obedience of faith for the sake of his name among all the nations, including you who are called to belong to Jesus Christ. To all those in Rome who are loved by God and called to be saints: Grace to you and peace from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.

Paul was set apart by God for a specific task: to call people from all nations to the obedience that comes from faith. He received grace and apostleship—not because he was special in some exclusive sense, but because God had conscripted him into his missionary work. Paul burned with urgency about this task. He prayed constantly for the churches, he longed to visit Rome to strengthen them spiritually, and he felt obligated to preach the Gospel to Greeks and non-Greeks, wise and foolish alike.

Why? Because the Gospel is the power of God, and with that power comes both privilege and obligation.

## We Are Also Set Apart

This is not Paul's task alone. When we believe in the power of the Gospel, we also receive grace and apostleship. We are sent to do the same work Paul did. We are conscripted into God's project.

God's project is this: to call people to faith in Jesus Christ through the Gospel. It is to proclaim the good news to our friends, our families, our communities—so that they too might have faith and obey Jesus Christ our Lord.

This requires confidence. The Gospel is not some run-down, outdated vehicle we should be embarrassed to be seen in. It is a sleek, powerful instrument of God's salvation. When you truly understand what you have in the Gospel, you drive it with your windows down, your head held high. You feel the power of it. There is nothing to be ashamed of.

**We need this confidence for God's project.** If every believer brings one person to faith, our churches would double. Our influence would multiply. God's kingdom would advance. There is nothing stopping us except our own timidity. The Gospel is the power of God. Let us be those who, like Paul, are unashamed and eager to share it.

> **Romans 1:17 (ESV)**  
> For in it the righteousness of God is revealed from faith for faith, as it is written, "The righteous shall live by faith."

This is our calling. This is God's project, and you are needed.
