# Exodus 40:17-38

**Title:** The End of an Era and the Beginning of a New Life

**Date:** September 27, 2024

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## 1. The End of Disobedience

> **Exodus 40:17** So the tabernacle was set up on the first day of the first month in the second year.

The tabernacle—a special portable tent where God's presence dwelled—was finally complete. After months of construction detailed in previous chapters, Moses assembled it according to God's exact specifications.

> **Exodus 40:18-21** When Moses set up the tabernacle, he put the bases in place, erected the frames, inserted the crossbars and set up the posts. Then he spread the tent over the tabernacle and put the covering over the tent, as the Lord commanded him. He took the tablets of the covenant law and placed them in the ark, attached the poles to the ark and put the atonement cover over it. Then he brought the ark into the tabernacle and hung the shielding curtain and shielded the ark of the covenant law, as the Lord commanded him.

The furniture was placed in order: the table for bread, the lampstand for light, the gold altar for incense, the burnt offering altar, and a new addition—the bronze basin for washing.

> **Exodus 40:33** Then Moses set up the courtyard around the tabernacle and altar and put up the curtain at the entrance to the courtyard. **And so Moses finished the work.**

But something remarkable happened beyond the completion of construction:

> **Exodus 39:42-43** The Israelites had done all the work just as the Lord had commanded Moses. Moses inspected the work and saw that they had done it just as the Lord had commanded. So Moses blessed them.

### A Stunning Reversal

Throughout their journey, the Israelites had been rebellious:

- **Before rescue:** When facing Pharaoh's wrath, they blamed Moses: "May the Lord look on you and judge you! You have made us obnoxious to Pharaoh" (Exodus 5:20-21).

- **After rescue:** When the Egyptian army approached, they despaired: "Was it because there were no graves in Egypt that you brought us to the desert to die? It would have been better for us to serve the Egyptians than to die in the desert!" (Exodus 14:10-12).

- **Complaints about food:** "If only we had died by the Lord's hand in Egypt! There we sat around pots of meat and ate all the food we wanted, but you have brought us out into this desert to starve" (Exodus 16:2-3).

- **Complaints about water:** They quarreled with Moses multiple times, even questioning whether God intended to kill them in the wilderness (Exodus 15:23-24, 17:1-2).

- **The golden calf:** In their deepest rebellion, they fashioned an idol while Moses received God's law on Mount Sinai.

And yet, after God's discipline and restoration, something transformed. The Israelites finally **obeyed**. They did exactly what God commanded.

### Baptism as Symbolism

Baptism represents this same kind of ending and beginning. Going down into the water symbolizes death—not just any death, but a specific one:

- In ancient understanding, water represented chaos, darkness, and the abyss, as seen in the flood narrative and Jonah's drowning.

- Descending into water meant going down to the grave.

When Christians are baptized, they symbolically die to:
- Death to self
- Death to the world's system
- Death to the ruler of this world

Coming up from the water represents rising to new life, leaving behind earthly desires to seek God's kingdom.

### The Application

Christians are those who declare: "I am done with my old life of sin." Just as the Israelites served under Pharaoh—a cruel master who used them, abused them, and then cast them away—sin is a master that enslaves us. Repentance means recognizing that we've been a pain to God, to everyone, and to ourselves. Through baptism, we symbolically end that era and begin a new one.

## 2. The Start of a New Journey

> **Exodus 40:17** So the tabernacle was set up on **the first day of the first month in the second year.**

Notice the timing: "first month." This was nearly a full year after they left Egypt.

> **Exodus 12:1-2** The Lord said to Moses and Aaron in Egypt, "This month is to be for you the first month, the first month of your year. Tell the whole community of Israel that on the tenth day of this month each man is to take a lamb for his family, one for each household."

A year earlier, they had eaten a hasty Passover meal while preparing to flee slavery. Now, with the year complete and God's dwelling place established, they would celebrate Passover properly. The tabernacle was ready. God's presence was about to dwell with them. They were prepared for the journey ahead.

### The Willing Heart

A critical detail emerges in Exodus 35. After the golden calf incident—after their greatest failure—something changed. God called the people to contribute materials for building the tabernacle:

> **Exodus 35:21, 22, 26, 29** Everyone who was willing and whose heart moved them came and brought an offering to the Lord... All who were willing came and brought gold jewelry... All the women who were willing and had the skill spun the goat hair... All the Israelite men and women who were willing brought to the Lord freewill offerings for all the work the Lord through Moses had commanded them to do.

The Hebrew phrase "willing heart" speaks to the seat of will and commitment. After their failure, the Israelites chose differently. They committed to:
- Living according to God's ways
- Submitting to His laws
- Serving His kingdom
- Making His cause their cause

### The Journey Ahead

Christians understand that baptism is not the end—it's the beginning of a new journey. Coming up from the water, we emerge as new creations:

> **2 Corinthians 5:17** Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation; old things have passed away; behold, all things have become new.

> **1 Peter 1:3** Praise be to the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ! In his great mercy he has given us new birth into a living hope through the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead.

> **Ezekiel 36:26** I will give you a new heart and put a new spirit in you; I will remove from you your heart of stone and give you a heart of flesh.

The Israelites faced challenges ahead—they would encounter obstacles, moments of doubt, and a 40-year wilderness journey that tested their commitment. But we, too, are on a journey. Some have just started; others have walked for years. We have decided to follow God, and in times of doubt, He helps us.

## 3. God's Presence With Us

> **Exodus 40:34-38** Then the cloud covered the tent of meeting, and the glory of the Lord filled the tabernacle. Moses could not enter the tent of meeting because the cloud had settled on it, and the glory of the Lord filled the tabernacle. In all the travels of the Israelites, whenever the cloud lifted from above the tabernacle, they would set out; but if the cloud did not lift, they did not set out—until the day it lifted. So the cloud of the Lord was over the tabernacle by day, and fire was in the cloud by night, in the sight of all the Israelites during all their travels.

Throughout the journey, God did not leave them alone. His presence was made visible through:

- **The cloud** (verse 34): God's glory filling the tabernacle
- **Guidance** (verse 36): The cloud lifting to signal when to move forward
- **Fire by night** (verse 38): God's presence and light during darkness

This was like having a personal GPS and a guide visible to the entire community as they wandered through the wilderness.

### A Different Privilege Today

There was a significant limitation in that era: "Moses could not enter the tent of meeting because the cloud had settled on it."

Moses, the greatest prophet of the Old Covenant, was barred from God's immediate presence. But we have been given a special privilege that superseded even Moses' access:

Through Jesus Christ, God's presence dwells within our hearts. Jesus went through death for us, was resurrected, defeated death on our behalf, and now calls us to new life. He is our continuing guide in the present.

### The Application: God's Guidance Today

God's presence is available to us through:

- **His Word:** We can read Scripture for ourselves to receive guidance.

- **Prayer and reflection:** Meditating on God's Word opens channels for His direction.

- **Community:** God has placed us in an extended community—the church—which serves as a repository of God's wisdom and truth.

Dwell in God's Word. Read it for yourselves. Or come to church each Sunday, where we gather to encounter God's presence together through His Word and through one another.

This is the privilege of baptism: entering into a new life where God's presence guides us, sustains us, and transforms us every day of our journey.
