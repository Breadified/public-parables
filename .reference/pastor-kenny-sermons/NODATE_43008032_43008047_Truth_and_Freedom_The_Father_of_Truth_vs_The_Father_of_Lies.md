# John 8:32-47

**Title:** Truth and Freedom: The Father of Truth vs. The Father of Lies  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## Introduction: Facing Reality

Kenny opens with a personal story from his school days in Galton Pool during Year 7 swimming week. Despite having had swimming lessons, he confidently jumped into the 25-meter pool only to discover the truth: he couldn't swim. As he sank, reality hit hard—he had never actually dived, freestyled, or completed a lap before. He was pulled to safety by other students and assigned to remedial swimming classes.

This story illustrates a profound truth: **facing reality is tough**. Whether it's university not matching high school success, struggling in a new job, discovering marriage is harder than expected, or realizing your skills don't match your assumptions—reality eventually catches up. And it hurts.

Yet this painful confrontation with truth is precisely what sets us free.

## Part One: The Father of Truth (John 8:32-39)

> "Then you will know the truth, and the truth will set you free." (8:32)

The Jewish listeners immediately object: "We've never been slaves to anyone. Why do we need to be set free?" This response ignores an obvious historical reality—they're celebrating the Feast of Tabernacles, commemorating their exodus from slavery in Egypt. But Jesus speaks of a deeper slavery.

### Slavery to Sin

> "Everyone who sins is a slave to sin." (8:34)

Just as Pharaoh's slaves endured harsh, bitter labor and ruthless treatment (Exodus 1:14), sin enslaves us with the same brutality. Sin is a tyrant boss—it lures us in, uses us to commit dark deeds, and discards us when finished. As James describes it:

> "Each person is tempted when they are dragged away by their own evil desire and enticed. Then, after desire has conceived, it gives birth to sin; and sin, when it is full-grown, gives birth to death." (James 1:14-15)

The outcome is always the same: slavery leads to death.

### Only the Free Son Can Save Us

> "Now a slave has no permanent place in the family, but a son belongs to it forever. So if the Son sets you free, you will be free indeed." (8:35-36)

Slaves cannot free themselves. The enslaved people of America did not and could not free themselves—approximately 360,000 soldiers from the North died to liberate them, demonstrating that freedom requires someone from outside the system of slavery. They had no guns, no money, no resources.

Similarly, we cannot free ourselves from sin. We need a Son—someone with power and authority, not bound by the slavery that binds us. Only Jesus, the free Son, can ransomed us from sin's tyranny.

### The Source of Freedom

Jesus possesses this authority because He comes from the Father, the source of all truth and reality itself. As He declares:

> "My testimony is valid" (8:14)
> "My other witness is the Father, who sent me" (8:18)

Those freed from slavery are those who align themselves with Jesus by receiving His word. Those with "room for my word" (8:37) are the ones who experience liberation.

In our age of "my truth, your truth," this passage presents a radical alternative: **only Jesus's truth counts—the truth that sets us free.**

### The Liberating Truths

Several hard realities must be faced:

- We are slaves to sin
- We will be punished for sin
- Our sin brings suffering and death

But facing these truths opens the door to a second reality: **our sins are forgiven by Jesus. Our sin is not the final judgment on our lives. We are now free.**

### A Practical Pattern for Freedom

This truth operates like recovery programs. In Alcoholics Anonymous, the first step is admitting: "I am an alcoholic." Facing reality is the first step toward healing.

Similarly, if we have the "cancer of sin," we must:
1. Admit and confess our condition
2. Accept the painful treatment (repentance)
3. Receive forgiveness through Jesus
4. Experience freedom and new life

Just as someone with cancer cannot pretend the disease doesn't exist and hope it disappears, we cannot pretend about sin. We must face it, confess it, and accept Christ's forgiveness.

**Key Application:** What reality are you avoiding? What truth about sin—in your own life or in our world—are you unwilling to confront? Only by facing reality can we begin the process of healing.

## Part Two: The Father of Lies (John 8:40-47)

> "You belong to your father, the devil, and you want to carry out your father's desires. He was a murderer from the beginning, not holding to the truth, for there is no truth in him. When he lies, he speaks his native language, for he is a liar and the father of lies." (8:44)

### The Language Reveals the Father

In the New Testament, sons apprenticed under their fathers and learned their trade. You could identify someone's father by their work—a blacksmith's son was a blacksmith; a butcher's son was a butcher. The same principle applies spiritually.

Jesus speaks truth because He comes from the Father of Truth. Those who speak lies reveal that they come from the Devil, the father of lies. As He says:

> "Why is my language not clear to you? Because you are unable to hear what I say." (8:43)

The listeners cannot hear Jesus because they don't belong to God. They speak the Devil's language—and that language is deception.

### Three Classic Lies from the Garden

The Devil's deceptive strategy is ancient. In Genesis 3, we see three patterns of lies that became humanity's template for deception:

**Lie #1: Exaggerate the truth to make it sound unreasonable**

> "Did God really say, 'You must not eat from any tree in the garden'?" (Genesis 3:1)

The serpent twists God's simple command into something that sounds absurdly restrictive. God only forbade one tree; the serpent makes it sound like God forbade everything. The implication: "Doesn't God care about you? How will you eat?"

**Lie #2: Deny the truth (claiming consequences are unenforceable)**

> "You will not certainly die." (Genesis 3:4)

The serpent flatly contradicts God's warning. You can disobey without consequence. The penalty isn't real.

**Lie #3: Claim disobedience is good and liberating**

> "For God knows that when you eat from it your eyes will be opened, and you will be like God, knowing good and evil." (Genesis 3:5)

Disobedience becomes freedom. Breaking God's law becomes enlightenment. And thus:

> "When the woman saw that the fruit of the tree was good for food and pleasing to the eye, and also desirable for gaining wisdom, she took some and ate it...Then the eyes of both of them were opened, and they realized they were naked." (Genesis 3:6-7)

Sin entered the world through a lie. And sin brings death.

### Modern Deception: The Sexual Immorality Example

The same three-part deception operates today around sexuality. God's clear boundary is simple: sexual relations are for marriage between a man and woman.

The Devil's modern campaign follows the ancient pattern:

**Exaggeration:** "Did God really say sex is bad? He's such a party pooper! Is He so frightened of people having fun that He forbids all sexual expression?"

**Denial:** "You can engage in sexual immorality—pornography, sleeping around, adultery, prostitution, homosexual acts—without consequence. It's just a minor thing. It won't damage your thinking, your future marriage, your children, or society."

The data contradicts this lie. Every welfare marker shows life improves when sex is regulated within committed marriage. Sexual immorality:
- Exploits people, particularly women
- Kills marriages and family joy
- Damages the brain and creates addiction
- Brings slavery and death

**Justification:** "Sexual immorality is good for you! You have repressed feelings inside. You must be free to express them. Sexual freedom is liberation. Love is love."

And like Adam and Eve, we die—in this life and the next.

### Lies Lead to Murder

The Devil is "a murderer from the beginning." Lies and murder are inseparable. The father of lies is also a killer because his deceptions always lead to death—spiritual death, relational death, physical death.

### Who Will You Follow?

The choice is stark:

> "Whoever obeys my word will never see death." (8:51)

This echoes the choice between the blue pill and red pill: remain in comfortable illusion, or take the red pill and see reality as it truly is. Following Jesus means choosing truth, always standing for truth, even when it's costly.

But there is hope. Just as God redeemed Israel from Pharaoh's slavery through mighty acts and blood sacrifice (Exodus 4:22-23), so Jesus's death and resurrection is God's mighty act by which He ransoms His people from the Devil's grip.

## Part Three: Seeing in the Light (John 9:1-41)

> "While I am in the world, I am the light of the world." (9:5)

Jesus's teaching about truth and freedom is immediately followed by a sign: a man blind from birth receives his sight. Light is the perfect metaphor for truth—it illuminates what is hidden, shows things as they really are, not as we wish them to be.

The contrast is sharp:

> "Night is coming, when no one can work." (9:4)

Nighttime, darkness, and blindness represent inability to see reality. This is Jesus's work: to give sight to the blind, to illuminate truth.

### The Blind Man Who Sees

When the authorities interrogate the man who was healed, he becomes an unexpected hero. Facing pressure to denounce Jesus, he refuses:

> "Whether he is a sinner or not, I don't know. One thing I do know. I was blind but now I see!" (9:25)

Later, when the authorities ridicule him for being Jesus's disciple, he argues from logic:

> "Now that is remarkable! You don't know where he comes from, yet he opened my eyes. We know that God does not listen to sinners. He listens to the godly person who does his will. Nobody has ever heard of opening the eyes of a man born blind. If this man were not from God, he could do nothing." (9:30-33)

His reasoning is irrefutable. When the authorities lose the argument, they resort to censorship—throwing him out of the synagogue. As Kenny notes, "liars love censoring people when they lose an argument."

But Jesus finds him and reveals Himself:

> "Do you believe in the Son of Man?...You have now seen him; in fact, he is the one speaking with you." (9:35-37)

The man's response is complete faith:

> "Lord, I believe," and he worshiped him. (9:38)

### The Irony of Blindness

> "For judgment I have come into this world, so that the blind will see and those who see will become blind." (9:39)

The formerly blind man now sees the light of the world. The Pharisees, who claim to see clearly, are revealed as spiritually blind because they reject truth.

### The Call to Open Your Eyes

The passage concludes with Jesus's original promise:

> "Then you will know the truth, and the truth will set you free." (8:32)

The chain of sin that binds us—the causation between sin and death—has been broken. This is Easter's victory.

## Theological Foundations

### God's Boundaries Are Liberating, Not Oppressive

Some argue that God is a "party pooper" imposing restrictions on human freedom. But this misunderstands freedom itself. Consider:

- If Thomas the Tank Engine leaves the rails to be "free," is he truly free?
- If a fish leaves its tank to be "free," is it liberated or dying?
- If a toddler wanders onto a highway, is that freedom or danger?

God's guidelines aren't chains—they're the conditions for genuine flourishing. **Truth and reality bring freedom, not the pseudo-freedom that leads to death, but freedom from sin.**

The Enlightenment thinker Jean-Jacques Rousseau declared, "Man is born free but everywhere he is in chains." This sentiment has animated modern thinking: if only we could be free from kings, obligations, morals, God...

But Rousseau was wrong. We are not born free; we are born enslaved to sin. We naturally lie, and our lies lead to murder. The German citizen of 1939 was not uniquely evil—given the opportunity, all of us are capable of darkness.

### The Gospel's Reversal

The Gospel reverses our fundamental misunderstanding:

We assume we're born free and God makes us slaves.

The truth is: **we're born enslaved to sin, and God's truth sets us free.**

We assume God's boundaries restrict us.

The reality is: **God's boundaries keep us on the rails, in the water where we can live, away from the highway's danger.**

## Application and Call to Response

**For Those Who Follow Jesus:** Continue to keep your eyes open to truth. Don't retreat into comfortable illusions. Defend truth even when costly.

**For Those Not Yet Following Jesus:** Will you consider opening your eyes? Will you face the reality of sin and freedom? Will you receive the forgiveness and liberation that Jesus offers?
