# 2 Peter 1:1-11

**Title:** Everything We Need For Eternity

## Introduction

Consider MacGyver, the old TV show about an FBI agent who refuses to carry a gun. Instead, he solves impossible problems using only his Swiss pocket knife, duct tape, and whatever is at hand. Trapped in a jail cell, he fashions a bomb. Locked in a freezer, he uses a light bulb to melt ice, channels the water to a lock, and lets it refreeze and expand to break free.

Among those of us who grew up watching, "MacGyvering" became a verb—the ability to take everyday objects and accomplish extraordinary feats with minimal resources.

The question Peter asks us today is simple: **Do we, as Christians, have everything we need?** Many of us feel lacking. We sense powerlessness, feel something is missing. But Peter's answer is decisive: we have everything necessary—not just for survival, but for eternity.

---

## 1. The Gospel Is All We Need (1:1-4)

> His divine power has given us everything we need for a godly life through our knowledge of him who called us by his own glory and goodness. (2 Peter 1:3)

Notice how verses 2, 3, and 4 work in parallel:

- **Verse 2:** We receive grace and peace in abundance through knowledge of God and Jesus  
- **Verse 3:** God's divine power gives us everything we need for life and godliness through our knowledge of him  
- **Verse 4:** Through these he has given us his precious promises, so we may participate in the divine nature

What have we been given? **The gospel itself.** In verse 1, Peter speaks of "a faith as precious as ours"—the good news of salvation through Christ's righteousness.

The gospel is:
- **Grace**—unmerited favor
- **Peace**—reconciliation with God  
- **Promise**—the Old Testament fulfilled in Christ

The gospel tells us that Jesus takes our place on the cross. We deserve God's wrath due to our sin, but Christ paid that price. Though we face death in this life, we receive eternal life.

### The Gospel Compared to a Smartphone

A smartphone contains everything you once needed separately: camera, map, encyclopedia, watch, compass, entertainment, television, radio. Similarly, the gospel—testified by all of Scripture—contains everything you need from now until eternity.

- **For comfort:** In grief and loss
- **For learning:** Understanding the world and God
- **For wisdom:** Guidance in decision-making  
- **For rebuke:** Correction and growth
- **For curiosity:** Answers to life's deepest questions

The world tells a different story. Every magazine, billboard, and social media post whispers: *You don't have enough. Buy this. Get that. Wear this. Experience that.* Your friends help along, posting pictures of food, holidays, and entertainment, suggesting that only if you had these things would you be complete.

**The world is wrong.**

What we think we desperately need are things we merely want. Consider how many iPhones sit unused in your drawer. How many games you bought but forgot about. How many clothes you never wear. These things don't last. Yet Christians in countries with no food, no clothes, and no peace survive and flourish because they possess the gospel first.

**Nothing is crucial except the cross.** As Paul said, "I resolve to know nothing except the cross of Christ."

## 2. We Have Everything We Need to Know (1:3)

How do we obtain the gospel? Through **knowledge**.

> His divine power has given us everything we need for a godly life through our knowledge of him who called us by his own glory and goodness. (1:3)

Peter emphasizes knowledge throughout his letter:

> Grace and peace be yours in abundance through the knowledge of God and of Jesus our Lord. (1:2)

> If they have escaped the corruption of the world by knowing our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ and are again entangled in it and overcome, they are worse off at the end than they were at the beginning. (2:20)

### Christianity Is Not Anti-Intellectual

This is vital: **the gospel comes to us through knowledge.** God has not left us in darkness. He took the initiative, sending messengers and ultimately Christ himself, who said, "If you know me, you know the Father" (John 14:7).

The accusation that Christianity is anti-intellectual is false. Knowledge and learning are woven into the Christian DNA:

- The first modern scientists were Christians—many were clergy  
- The first universities were Christian seminaries
- The first school in New South Wales was founded by a clergyman
- A remarkable percentage of Nobel Prize winners have been Christians

This is why our churches rightly have Bible studies, sermons, catechism classes, and Sunday school. Church leaders must be teachers. Yet even today, churches face pressure to dilute these very things. In our zeal to be "relevant and exciting," have we forgotten the foundational question: **How do we actually come to know God?**

The essential tools of the church aren't guitars or candles, but pen and paper and Scripture. Once we know God's Word, we can put that learning to music with the guitar and use the candle to read the Bible.

Some argue that God is bigger than knowledge and books, so constant study isn't proper. But while it's true that God transcends books, **He chooses to be known through the Book.** As Hebrews declares:

> In the past God spoke to our ancestors through the prophets at many times and in various ways, but in these last days he has spoken to us by his Son, whom he appointed heir of all things, and through whom also he made the universe. The Son is the radiance of God's glory and the exact representation of his being, sustaining all things by his powerful word. (Hebrews 1:1-3)

### The Gospel Can Be Known by All

Notice something remarkable: **you don't need a PhD to be a full Christian.** You don't need a university degree or even to have finished high school. A poor, uneducated laborer in rural China can be as complete a Christian as a Nobel laureate. There is no spiritual advantage to advanced education.

As Martin Luther said, "Even a farmboy can understand the gospel."

The gospel is not secret knowledge (gnosticism) or subjective interpretation. It is **objective truth available to all.**

When Peter uses the word "knowledge" in verse 3, he employs the Greek term for "full knowledge" (epignōsis). This means **Christians possess everything we need to know.** Not that we know everything—we don't know what everyone is thinking or have Wikipedia in our heads. But we possess the tools to interpret the world aright. We have the proper glasses through which to see life in focus.

The further the world drifts from God, the crazier it becomes. But Christians have the framework to make sense of it all.

## 3. We Have Everything We Need to Do (1:5-11)

Knowledge must never remain confined to academic towers. It must flow into ordinary life and shape how we live. This is the purpose of Peter's knowledge:

> His divine power has given us everything we need for **life and godliness** through our knowledge of him. (1:3)

### From Knowledge to Godliness

Earlier we asked: what does it mean to "participate in the divine nature"? Peter's answer: **becoming godly—reflecting God's holiness and character.**

This is not theosis—we don't receive God's substance. We don't become filled with some mystical force. Our biology doesn't change when we become Christians. Rather, we grow in godliness through the indwelling Spirit.

With this understanding, look at verses 5-11:

> For this very reason, make every effort to add to your faith goodness; and to goodness, knowledge; and to knowledge, self-control; and to self-control, perseverance; and to perseverance, godliness; and to godliness, mutual affection; and to mutual affection, love. For if you possess these qualities in increasing measure, they will keep you from being ineffective and unproductive in your knowledge of our Lord Jesus Christ. (1:5-8)

This is a list of qualities to cultivate:
- Goodness
- Knowledge  
- Self-control
- Perseverance
- Godliness
- Brotherly kindness
- Love

There's no strict order here—the emphasis is not on sequence but on **constant effort: "make every effort" (verse 5) and "be all the more eager" (verse 10).**

### The Purpose: Productive Living

Why add these qualities?

> For if you possess these qualities in increasing measure, they will keep you from being ineffective and unproductive in your knowledge of our Lord Jesus Christ. (1:8)

This doesn't mean "ineffective in getting to know Jesus," though that's commendable. Rather, Peter says: **You already have knowledge of Jesus and the gospel. Now use it.** Don't be sterile and barren in that knowledge. Be productive.

Think of productive land: it requires fertilizer and seed to grow. Similarly, knowledge without godliness is dead—it must produce fruit in how we live.

### Your List vs. God's List

Summer is approaching. We make lists: Xbox games to play, movies to watch, jobs to secure, licenses to get, places to visit, shopping trips. These are temporary pursuits.

Meanwhile, God's agenda is:
- Add goodness
- Add knowledge
- Add self-control
- Add perseverance
- Add godliness
- Add mutual affection
- Add love

### Why This Matters

> Therefore, my brothers and sisters, make every effort to confirm your calling and election. For if you do these things, you will never stumble, and you will receive a rich welcome into the eternal kingdom of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ. (1:10-11)

We have everything we need to live for eternity. We possess all the tools.

Yet how many of us make excuses?
- "I'll be more godly if only I had more money..."
- "...if only I felt more secure..."
- "...if only I had more time..."
- "...if only my parents were normal..."
- "...if only my church were better..."
- "...if only my friends were more supportive..."

We search for formulas and shortcuts. We wish our circumstances were different.

But Peter cuts through this: **We have everything we need right now.** We have the gospel. We have knowledge of Christ. We have the Holy Spirit. 

Like MacGyver, trapped in impossible situations but finding victory through ingenuity and available resources, we too must stop complaining and get to work. See the wonderful provision already given to you. Be godly. You can do this because you possess knowledge of the gospel.

We have everything we need for eternity. We have everything we need to know. We have everything we need to do.

**Live accordingly.**
