# 1 Thessalonians 5:1-11

**Title:** Readiness for Judgment

**Date:** September 14, 2014

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## A Season of Getting Ready

Life is full of moments requiring preparation—students preparing for exams and scholarships, families preparing for new chapters, and in the church, we prepare for significant transitions. Yet there is one preparation that matters above all others: getting ready for Jesus' return.

This passage speaks directly to the living, instructing us how to prepare for Christ's coming. It builds on last week's teaching about what happens to those who have already died when Jesus returns. Now Paul addresses the practical question: **How do we get ready for Jesus' return?**

## 1. The Coming Will Be a Surprise (vv. 1-3)

### No One Knows the Time

> **1 Thessalonians 5:1-2** Now, brothers and sisters, about times and dates we do not need to write to you, for you know very well that the day of the Lord will come like a thief in the night.

Paul writes that there is no need to discuss when Christ will return because **no one knows.** We cannot calculate the year, the month, the hour, or the minute. All we can know with certainty is that it will come suddenly, without warning.

### Two Powerful Metaphors

**The Thief in the Night**

A thief does not announce their arrival. They don't send appointment cards or schedule their theft. The very nature of a thief's success depends on surprise. Similarly, Jesus will come unexpectedly.

**Labor Pains on a Pregnant Woman**

> **1 Thessalonians 5:3** While people are saying, "Peace and safety," destruction will come on them suddenly, as labor pains on a pregnant woman, and they will not escape.

Just as labor pains arrive without scheduled notice—sometimes weeks earlier than expected—the day of the Lord will overtake the unprepared. While the world goes about saying "Peace and safety," judgment will arrive suddenly with no escape.

### A Warning Against False Prophets

History is littered with false predictions. The first president of what became the Jehovah's Witnesses calculated 1874 as the year of Christ's return. Joseph Smith prophesied 1891. Various groups throughout history have pointed to wars, social upheaval, and catastrophes as signs of the end. None were correct.

> **Deuteronomy 18:22** If what a prophet proclaims in the name of the Lord does not take place or come true, that is a message the Lord has not spoken. That prophet has spoken presumptuously, so do not be alarmed.

In our modern age, this same pattern continues—not always as explicit predictions, but as constant alarmism. Climate catastrophe. Y2K. Economic collapse. The media amplifies these fears, creating a perpetual sense that we are "beyond the point of no return." Yet none of these are signs of Christ's coming.

**The True Sign**

The only sign we need is the sign of the cross—it has already been given. Christ died and rose. The world is condemned and awaits demolition, yet the tradies are slow at their work. The building stands, though its fate is sealed. One day, with no warning, it will be gone.

### What This Day Will Be Like

The two metaphors—thief and labor pains—reveal different dimensions of Christ's coming:

**Destruction for the Unprepared**

Like a thief, it represents destruction. The day of the Lord brings mourning, cataclysm, and dramatic reversal of fortunes. It is the day when God visits the earth in judgment (Isaiah 13:6, 9; Joel 2:11, 31; Amos 5:18-20; Zephaniah 1:14, 18). Jesus Himself used this same thief metaphor to describe the sudden appearing of the Son of Man (Matthew 24:43-44; Luke 12:39-40).

**Salvation for the Prepared**

Yet like birth pains, it also represents deliverance of something good. Though painful, birth brings gladness and joy. Justice will come. Righteousness will prevail. Evil will be removed from the earth.

### Application: Don't Be Complacent

- Don't delay spiritual decisions or plan for deathbed conversions. You cannot time Jesus' return—He may come while you're still young.
- Don't be fatalistic, thinking we have no time left. Use the time we have to warn others that Christ is coming.

## 2. Yet for Believers, It's Not a Surprise (vv. 4-8)

### An Apparent Contradiction

> **1 Thessalonians 5:4** But you, brothers and sisters, are not in darkness so that this day should surprise you like a thief.

This seems to contradict what Paul just said. The resolution: **While the timing will be surprising, the fact of Christ's coming is no surprise to believers.**

While the world will be caught off guard, Christians know that judgment day is coming. We are not caught at our weakest point. We are like someone waiting by the window with a stick, having watched that spot for years with all the lights on.

Or, to use the labor pain metaphor: we don't know exactly when the baby will come, but we know a baby is coming. There is no shock in that.

**This allows us to respond with wisdom rather than panic.**

### People of the Light

> **1 Thessalonians 5:5-7** You are all children of the light and children of the day. We do not belong to the night or to the darkness. So then, let us not be like others, who are asleep, but let us be awake and sober. For those who sleep, sleep at night, and those who get drunk, get drunk at night.

Paul draws a contrast between light and darkness, day and night. These are not merely poetic—they represent fundamentally different ways of living.

**Darkness and Shame**

Darkness is representative of deeds of shame: crimes, sexual assault, theft, drunkenness, and all forms of moral failure. Research consistently shows that people behave differently under cover of darkness:

- Approximately 63% of sexual assaults occur at night; 72% of motor vehicle thefts occur at night.
- Stanford psychologist Philip Zimbardo's famous 1969 experiments found that subjects wearing dark hoods and baggy clothes administered electric shocks more readily than those in normal clothing.
- Psychology research shows that darkness creates a "false sense of concealment," giving us license to behave badly. People cheat more in dimly lit rooms than bright ones. Those wearing sunglasses showed less generosity and fairness than those with clear lenses.

The pattern is clear: **darkness both enables and encourages immoral behavior.** When we cannot be seen, we believe we can act without consequence. When self-interest is hidden, we become more selfish.

**The Deeds of the Dead**

Paul speaks of those who "sleep" and engage in deeds of darkness as "asleep"—a different term than the euphemistic sleep referring to death in chapter 4. Here it means spiritually lazy, or perhaps worse: spiritually dead.

The deeds of darkness are the deeds of those who are spiritually dead—deeds that lead to death. Drunkenness literally kills brain cells. Sexual permiscuity leads to spiritual death. These people "fail to see the approaching judgment of God and so are unprepared. As good as dead" in their trajectory toward judgment.

**Light and Goodness**

> **1 Thessalonians 5:8** But since we belong to the day, let us be sober, putting on faith and love as a breastplate, and the hope of salvation as a helmet.

In contrast, light represents goodness, alertness, and sobriety. To belong to the day means to live with watchfulness and clarity.

### The Armor of Readiness

The imagery here is military—the armor of someone ready for battle, protected against thieves and plunderers:

- **Sober:** Clear-headed, alert
- **Faith and love as a breastplate:** Protecting the heart, the center of life
- **Hope of salvation as a helmet:** Protecting the mind from despair

This language echoes Isaiah 59:17:

> **Isaiah 59:17** He put on righteousness as his breastplate, and the helmet of salvation on his head; he put on the garments of vengeance and wrapped himself in zeal as in a cloak.

God Himself dons the armor of war to execute justice against the wicked. **To put on this armor is to join God's team by faith.** We align our lives with His purposes and place ourselves under His gracious protection.

Those who sleep in darkness, unprepared, will be caught unaware. But we who have put on Christ's armor are ready. We belong to the day.

## 3. The True Surprise: We're Appointed for Salvation (vv. 9-11)

### Appointed for Salvation, Not Wrath

> **1 Thessalonians 5:9** For God did not appoint us to suffer wrath but to receive salvation through our Lord Jesus Christ.

Here is the stunning truth beneath everything Paul has been saying. While destruction awaits the unprepared—those "asleep" and spiritually dead—**Christians are appointed not for wrath, but for salvation.** We are appointed to *receive* salvation, to possess it fully.

This is what the armor prepares us for. This is what being awake and sober is about.

### United with Him in Life

> **1 Thessalonians 5:10** He died for us so that, whether we are awake or asleep, we may live together with him.

"Awake or asleep" refers to the two groups of believers from chapter 4—both those living when Christ returns and those who have already died. The promise is the same for both: **we will live together with him forever.**

This is the victory. Not escape from judgment, but union with Christ in eternal life.

### Encouragement and Mutual Strength

> **1 Thessalonians 5:11** Therefore encourage one another and build each other up, just as in fact you are doing.

Paul's final word is about community. This is *why* we gather as the church. Encouragement is the lifeblood of perseverance.

When we see one another remaining faithful despite hardship, it strengthens our resolve to do the same. When we see others slack off, we drift with them. **Our presence with one another matters.** Our shared commitment to Christ sustains each other.

---

## Final Question: Are You Ready?

The question is not whether you're ready to enjoy eternal life—of course that's appealing. The real question is: **Are you ready to give an account of your life?** Are you ready for that examination?

Being ready for Christ's return means living now as children of the light—awake, sober, clothed in faith, love, and hope. It means belonging to the day in how we live today.
