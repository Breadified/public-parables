# Ephesians 5:21-33

**Title:** Marriage as Covenant—Submission and Sacrificial Love  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## The Tension with Submission

The word "submit" jars our modern ears. When we hear it in the context of marriage, our minds immediately conjure troubling images—slavery, exploitation, domestic abuse. We recoil at the idea of one person subjugating another against their will.

Yet here is the bride, an intelligent, educated woman, choosing freely to place herself under the groom's authority for life. This seems backwards to us. How can she do this? Why would she want to?

The answer lies in understanding what biblical submission actually means and why God designed marriage this way.

## Submission Enables Leadership

> Wives, submit to your husbands as to the Lord. For the husband is the head of the wife as Christ is the head of the church, his body, of which he is the Savior. Now as the church submits to Christ, so also wives should submit to their husbands in everything. (Ephesians 5:22-24)

The submission of a wife is not arbitrary—it serves a specific purpose. It creates the space for the husband to lead, and leadership itself requires something to lead.

Notice the disproportionate instruction in this passage: wives receive one sentence. Husbands receive an entire paragraph. And what is the husband called to do?

> Husbands, love your wives, just as Christ loved the church and gave himself up for her to make her holy, cleansing her by the washing with water through the word, and to present her to himself as a radiant church, without stain or wrinkle or any other blemish, but holy and blameless. In this same way, husbands ought to love their wives as their own bodies. (Ephesians 5:25-28)

This is radical. The husband's job is to love sacrificially—to die for his wife, just as Christ died for the church. This means:

- **Physically**: To protect, to be willing to stand between danger and his family
- **Materially**: To provide food and shelter, to set aside pride for the sake of provision
- **Emotionally**: To initiate reconciliation, to sacrifice personal ambitions for family wellbeing
- **Spiritually**: To guide the family toward holiness, to shepherd them toward God

This is enormous responsibility. But here is the crucial insight: *The groom cannot do any of this without the bride's support. He cannot lead without her submission.*

Consider a team or organization you've led. It's frustratingly impossible to lead when your followers won't follow. You can make decisions, but without submission—without people willing to let you lead—you're simply frustrated. By definition, leadership requires followers who have chosen to follow.

When the bride agrees to submit, she is making room for the groom to lead. Her submission is part of the beautiful symmetry of their covenant. Notice the order: the bridegroom promises first to love sacrificially. Only then, knowing his intention, does the bride agree to follow.

The man initiates. She accepts. And so begins a beautiful dance.

## Submission as a Tiebreaker

But what happens when husband and wife genuinely disagree? When compromise seems impossible?

In everyday decisions, there is room for give-and-take. One person yields for the sake of harmony. He lets her choose the restaurant. She lets him enjoy the meal. Neither needs to enforce their will. This is normal married life.

But God's instruction on submission addresses something deeper: the moment of *irreconcilable difference*. When one insists on Brisbane and the other on Melbourne. When both have good reasons. When no amount of discussion resolves the deadlock.

What do you do then? You cannot vote—a tie remains a tie. You cannot appeal to parents without sacrificing your independence. So what provides the tiebreaker?

Under modern law, irreconcilable differences lead to divorce. But this couple have committed to marriage as a covenant—something greater than their feelings, stronger than their disagreements, worth fighting for.

God provides a structure for exactly this moment. Someone must give way. God says: let the husband make the final call, and let the wife submit. He could have reversed it. Either way, someone yields. But God says this is how it will work.

This is not arbitrary oppression. It is God's mercy. He sees a couple in love, committed to covenant, and says: *I will help you. I will structure your marriage so you never have to divorce when you face irreconcilable differences. There is a tiebreaker. There is a way forward.*

God wants to preserve their love. He does not want them separated by geography or circumstance. He wants them together.

## Submission to Christ

This instruction about marriage reveals something even larger:

> This is a profound mystery—but I am talking about Christ and the church. (Ephesians 5:32)

God is asking each of us to submit to Christ as the church submits to its head. This is not submission to an authoritarian tyrant. It is submission to one who has already proven his love through sacrifice—who gave himself up for us.

It is the choice to let him lead, so we might live healthier, more fulfilled lives, both in this world and the next.

---

**To the couple:** Remember these instructions when you face irreconcilable differences. May you reflect Christ's love and the church's trust to all who know you.
