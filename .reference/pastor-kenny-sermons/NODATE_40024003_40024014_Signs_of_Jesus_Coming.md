# Matthew 24:3-14, 26-31

**Title:** Signs of Jesus' Coming
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Disciples' Question

When Jesus sat on the Mount of Olives, His disciples came to Him privately asking: "When will this happen, and what will be the sign of your coming and of the end of the age?"

The Greek word *parousia* (coming) literally means "appearance" or "visitation"—like when a king would visit a city in his kingdom. The disciples weren't confused about Jesus being present with them; they were asking: When will you appear as the King? When will you come in power to make things right?

This same question echoes through our generation. Looking at the constant stream of disasters—hurricanes, economic collapse, political instability, wars—we find ourselves asking: **When will God finally come and rescue us from this chaos?** Surely things getting worse means He'll be forced to act?

## Beware of False Signs and False Kings

Jesus' first warning is clear:

> Watch out that no one deceives you. For many will come in my name, claiming, "I am the Messiah," and will deceive many.

Throughout history, false messiahs have emerged promising to deliver us from our anxieties. They point to false signs—wars, rumors of wars, nation rising against nation, famines, earthquakes—and claim these herald their coming. But Jesus tells us:

> You will hear of wars and rumors of wars, but see to it that you are not alarmed. Such things must happen, but the end is still to come. Nation will rise against nation, and kingdom against kingdom. There will be famines and earthquakes in various places. All these are the beginning of birth pains.

### Understanding "Birth Pains"

Jesus uses a powerful metaphor. Before a baby is born, a mother experiences Braxton Hicks—false contractions that feel like labor but signal nothing imminent. The world today experiences similar false alarms. Events are real, yes, but they don't indicate we're nearing the end. They're just the normal pain that has always characterized human history.

Consider how we describe our present moment: we live in "unprecedented" times. Yet every generation has thought the same. Look back through the years—we panicked about bushfires, Hong Kong's political crisis, elections described as "the most important ever," contaminated strawberries, Supreme Court rulings. Before that: wars, plagues, famines going back centuries. Yet if you compared newspapers from fifty years ago to today, you'd find the same categories of crisis, just different details.

### The Problem of Alarmism

Why does the media hype these stories? Because newspapers need to sell copies, and politicians need your votes and attention. Each year becomes "the most critical," yet nothing fundamentally changes. The danger isn't that crises exist—they always have—but that our panic makes us susceptible to false saviors.

Today we have leaders promising rescue from the climate, from natural disasters, from disease itself. These promises grow more grandiose: soon they'll promise we'll never die. Yet the reality is simple: even if they found the right person, solving something that big would take decades. They'd be voted out in three years, backstabbed by their own party, or the crisis is simply too large for any single person to address.

**So let's reject alarmism.** Do not panic. Face the next challenge with self-control and reason, not emotion. Don't be swayed by fake news or false messiahs.

## The Real Sign Has Already Come

Jesus speaks of a "great distress" (tribulation) unlike anything before or after. But here's what most miss: **this event has already happened.** It happened on the cross.

> Immediately after the distress of those days: "The sun will be darkened, and the moon will not give its light; the stars will fall from the sky, and the heavenly bodies will be shaken."

These cosmic signs already occurred:
- **Sun darkened, moon without light:** Matthew records darkness covering the land at Jesus' crucifixion
- **Heavenly bodies shaken:** An earthquake split rocks at His death

The circumstances of Jesus' capture match this prophecy perfectly: rumors of war, disciples with swords, Roman soldiers, false rumors about the Messiah, betrayal. Then, Jesus appeared—or rather, ascended:

> Then will appear the sign of the Son of Man in heaven. And then all the peoples of the earth will mourn when they see the Son of Man coming on the clouds of heaven, with power and great glory.

Note: The Greek word here isn't *parousia* but *erkomai*—which can mean both "coming" and "going." Jesus ascended on clouds, taken up before their eyes, hidden from sight (Acts 1:9).

**Therefore, we don't have to search for signs because they've already come. Jesus has appeared.**

## What Remains: Stand Firm

What's left is the gathering of the elect. Jesus promises to send His messengers—angels, or ordinary people like you and me—to gather His chosen from every corner of the earth: from the Americas to Europe, from Africa and Australia to China and Asia. Christians have gone to the ends of the earth spreading the gospel.

But disciples face opposition. Jesus warned:

> You will be handed over to be persecuted and put to death, and you will be hated by all nations because of me. At that time many will turn away from the faith and will betray and hate each other, and many false prophets will appear and deceive many people. Because of the increase of wickedness, the love of most will grow cold.

The real danger isn't external circumstances—it's that fear and pressure will turn believers against each other. In times of alarm, Christians have historically compromised their faith and betrayed one another to seem respectable, to secure funding, to comply with hostile powers. But Christian organizations exist to *protect* believers, not to protect themselves at the expense of Christians.

Yet Jesus offers this encouragement:

> The one who stands firm to the end will be saved.

Imagine standing on a roof during floods, knowing rescue helicopters are coming. You know what to expect. You know things are bad. But you don't panic—you stand firm. Don't sell out God when times get tough. Don't betray your brothers and sisters in Christ for comfort or safety.

## The Gospel Will Be Preached to All Nations

> This gospel of the kingdom will be preached in the whole world as a testimony to all nations, and then the end will come.

The end will come like lightning flashing across the sky from east to west—visible everywhere at once. It cannot be hidden or localized to one place. Where there's a carcass, vultures gather. Where judgment comes, it will be unmistakable.

But here's the essential truth: **on the cross, the end has already begun.** We don't wait for the king to come because the King has already come. We don't search for signs because the ultimate sign has been given.

## The Call to Faith

So don't worry about the circumstances of life. Don't panic over world events. Instead, worry about the judgment of Jesus. The cross is the real sign. On that cross, the abomination that causes desolation. On that cross, judgment pronounced upon the world—declared the end, named condemned.

But on that cross also stands forgiveness and salvation. 

Jesus came into His city on a donkey, receiving greetings befitting a king: "Hosanna, Son of David... Hosanna in the highest." He has visited. He has appeared.

Stand firm. Trust the King who has come.
