# Proverbs 3:5-6

**Title:** Fully Rely on God
**Date:** July 2, 2010
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

> Trust in the LORD with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding; in all your ways acknowledge him, and he will make your paths straight. (Proverbs 3:5-6)

## The Foundation: Understanding Trust

When we speak of relying on God, we're talking about **faith**—who do you trust? This is the essential question.

We live in a skeptical society. Trust has become difficult. We hide from our neighbors. We lock our doors and even our cars in church parking lots. Postmodernism tells us there is no objective truth, that all knowledge is merely manipulation by those in power. So we're taught: don't trust teachers, don't trust politicians, don't trust anything—including the Bible.

But here's the truth: **trust itself is not the problem.** We are relational beings created to trust one another. We *must* trust to survive. The real problem is that **we trust in the wrong things.**

## Where We Place Our Trust (And Why It Fails)

We trust media corporations and advertisements that promise happiness through consumption. We believe that success means becoming a doctor or lawyer, worth sacrificing our relationships and integrity. We ignore God, who has given us life itself, and refuse to acknowledge Him.

The Scripture diagnoses this perfectly: "lean not on your own understanding." These are the lies that tell us we can live best without God.

## The Answer: Trust Jesus

So who *can* we trust? Jesus provides the answer.

Jesus said:

> For I did not speak of my own accord, but the Father who sent me commanded me what to say and how to say it. I know that his command leads to eternal life. So whatever I say is just what the Father has told me to say. (John 8:49-50)

**Why trust Jesus?** His life proves His integrity. He was born poor and died poor. His disciples, who recorded His words, had no hidden agenda—they too ended in poverty and suffering. They had nothing to gain by lying.

What does Jesus reveal to us?

- **His birth** tells us God is still interested in a world that has ignored Him
- **His death** tells us God loves us, willing to forgive and reconcile us through His sacrifice
- **His resurrection** tells us God is judge, and we will be judged based on whom we trust

## The Promise

> In all your ways acknowledge him, and he will make your paths straight. (Proverbs 3:6)

"Acknowledge" means more than casual recognition—it means *know Him*. When we know God, we see clearly where we're going and how to get there. Like a torch lighting a dark street, His knowledge guides us. This is eternal life: to know God and to walk His straight path, stumble-free.

## A Word to Our Students

**The invitation stands:** Pick up a Bible today and learn about Jesus. Trust in Him completely, in all your ways, and watch how He straightens your path.
