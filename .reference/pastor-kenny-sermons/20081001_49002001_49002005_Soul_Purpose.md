# Ephesians 2:1-5

**Title:** Soul Purpose
**Date:** 24 October 2008
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## Our Spiritual Condition: Dead in Sin (1-3)

> As for you, you were dead in your transgressions and sins, in which you used to live when you followed the ways of this world and of the ruler of the kingdom of the air, the spirit who is now at work in those who are disobedient. All of us also lived among them at one time, gratifying the cravings of our sinful nature and following its desires and thoughts. Like the rest, we were by nature objects of wrath.

The Bible diagnoses our condition with stark clarity: we are spiritually dead. Not merely wounded or sick, but dead—unable to save ourselves, completely helpless.

It's like being trapped in a nightmare you know you're having but cannot wake from. You shout at yourself, pinch yourself, but nothing works. Or imagine being paralyzed while fully conscious, watching evil work against you with no power to resist. This is the human condition apart from Christ.

### The Root Cause: Rebellion Against God

Sin takes two forms, and both reveal the same fundamental problem: we have turned away from God.

**Active sin** consists of breaking God's commands—stealing, lying, hatred, swearing, spreading false rumors. These are visible transgressions we can point to.

**Passive sin** is more subtle and more dangerous. We can live respectable, law-abiding lives—succeed at school, raise good children, volunteer in our community—all while ignoring God entirely. Like terrorists who appear as model citizens while harboring evil in their hearts, we can ignore our Creator without obviously breaking any laws.

The deeper issue isn't the symptom; it's the disease. Active sin is merely a symptom of our fundamental rebellion: **we treat God as irrelevant**. He is the Creator of all, yet we ignore Him.

We try to fix the symptoms—anger management classes, self-help courses, stronger laws, positive thinking—and we might experience temporary improvement. But these approaches never address the real problem: our broken relationship with God and our enslaved will. We remain spiritually dead because we are disobedient to God, slaves to the "ruler of the air" (Satan himself), and objects of God's righteous wrath.

### A Sobering Reality

Our great "superpower"—the ability we seem to master most easily—is the capacity to do evil and hurt others. Without God's intervention, this is all we have.

---

## God's Rescue: Alive in Christ (4-5)

> But because of his great love for us, God, who is rich in mercy, made us alive with Christ even when we were dead in transgressions—it is by grace you have been saved.

The word "but" marks a dramatic turning point. Despite our hopelessness, God acts with breathtaking generosity.

### What God Has Done

Notice the richness of what Scripture tells us about God's action:

- He loves us with great love
- He is rich in mercy—overflowing with compassion
- He has made us alive with Christ
- We have been saved by grace

Jesus is the true superhero of this story. Unlike the villains who steal power, Jesus willingly takes our burden upon Himself. He absorbs the poison of our sin and evil, drinking it completely so we might be cleansed. He bears the wrath of God that we deserve. And in the ultimate twist of redemption, through resurrection power, He overcomes death itself and returns—not for Himself, but so that we might live.

### Grace, Not Works

God had no obligation to rescue us. We are the rebels; He is the wronged party. We ignored Him; He didn't ignore us. Yet "while we were still sinners, Christ died for us."

This salvation is by **grace**—God's unmerited generosity toward us. It is not something we earned or can earn. It is a gift, freely given.

---

## Our Response: Trust and Surrender

We face three possible responses to this gospel:

**Denial:** Continue believing we're not really that sinful. "I'm not perfect, but I'm not a sinner." This ignores the diagnosis entirely.

**Self-reliance:** Acknowledge the problem but believe we can fix it ourselves through good works, church attendance, or moral effort. This still ignores God as the source of salvation.

**Faith:** Fully trust Jesus. Admit our complete helplessness and rely entirely on His sacrifice. Believe that through His death, He took God's wrath upon Himself, freeing us from condemnation.

The call is clear: **Admit your helplessness and seek help from Jesus alone.**

This is what true faith means—not optimism or positive thinking, but absolute dependence on Christ's finished work for our salvation.
