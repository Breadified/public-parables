# Hebrews 3:1-6

**Title:** Commitment
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Faithful Jesus

> Therefore, holy brothers, who share in the heavenly calling, fix your thoughts on Jesus, the apostle and high priest whom we confess. He was faithful to the one who appointed him, just as Moses was faithful in all God's house. Jesus has been found worthy of greater honor than Moses, just as the builder of a house has greater honor than the house itself.

Jesus was a faithful man who kept his commitment to God. The apostle—one sent from God on a mission—took on an impossible task. As high priest, he dealt with the problem of sin not through animal sacrifice, but through the ultimate sacrifice of himself. This was truly a great task.

## Jesus Greater Than Moses

Moses was a faithful servant of God. His faithfulness made him the greatest person in Jewish history. He listened to God, confronted the most powerful ruler of his time, and demanded freedom for enslaved people. To the Jews, Moses was as central as Martin Luther King Jr. was to Black Americans or Nelson Mandela to South Africans.

Yet Jesus is greater than Moses because he held an even bigger role and carried even greater responsibility.

> Moses was faithful as a servant in all God's house, testifying to what would be said in the future. But Christ is faithful as a son over God's house. And we are his house, if we hold on to our courage and the hope of which we boast.

## The Difference Between Servant and Son

A servant in the house has full access to every room—the parents' bedroom, the bathroom, the intimate places—but enters to clean them, to serve. A son also has full access, but for a different reason. He enters not to serve, but to belong. He can jump on the bed, use the toilet freely, and on Saturday mornings crawl in to cuddle his parents.

Jesus had the greater, bigger task. He was appointed to this responsibility and saw it through to the end. He takes charge over our welfare. Jesus takes responsibility to sacrificially care for the world. For our sake, he did what no one else could do—the unblemished lamb of God died for us. Therefore, Jesus was faithful to the end.

## What This Means for Us

If we follow Jesus, we too must be faithful to God.

### To Those Committed to God

Stay faithful. Keep the commitments you have made to God. Many things will distract you from this commitment—friends, social events, social media, and academic pressures like the HSC. These demand your time and money, and you must prioritize carefully.

**Why consistency matters:** Keep the discipline of turning up to youth group and church. This is where you can naturally pray for each other, where opening the Bible feels natural, where more mature Christians look after you, and where you learn to serve and love one another—whether through conversation or practical help.

But none of this works if you don't show up. You can't start games, plan activities, or invite friends if people aren't there. So prioritize Sunday. Really own it. Follow through on your commitment, or you may live to regret it.

**Be faithful to God because you have made the commitment. You are his house.** I know it's hard, but you are the brightest, smartest, and most social people I know—you can do this. I see many of you serving and loving each other already. I've seen some of you make waffles for other groups!

### To Those Not Yet Committed

I want to congratulate you on coming today. You have no obligation to commit to us, yet you came anyway. That speaks well of you, and I hope we'll see you again.

If you're looking for a group that genuinely commits to each other's welfare, this is it. If you want to follow Jesus, who loves us and commits to us, then come join us. We meet regularly, and next term we're studying Moses and the Exodus. Come, and we can fix our eyes on Jesus together.
