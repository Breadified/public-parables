# Ephesians 1:1-14

**Title:** Christians Are Blessed!  
**Date:** July 17, 2005  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## How Are Christians Blessed?

> Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who has blessed us in Christ with every spiritual blessing in the heavenly places. (Ephesians 1:3)

The word "blessing" is often used carelessly in our culture. People say "God bless you" as though repetition itself brings blessing. Others use it merely as a farewell, or only in response to a sneeze. But what does it truly mean when Scripture says God has blessed us?

The word blessing simply means to bestow good things. When you behave well in class, you bless your teacher. When you help with dishes, you bless your parents. But God's blessing is far deeper—He has blessed us with *every spiritual blessing in Christ*.

The rest of Ephesians 1:1-14 spells out exactly what these spiritual blessings are. There are three principal things God has done for us, and remarkably, each begins with the letter P.

## 1. God Has Predestined Us (Verses 4-5)

> For he chose us in him before the creation of the world to be holy and blameless in his sight. In love he predestined us to be adopted as his sons through Jesus Christ, in accordance with his pleasure and will. (Ephesians 1:4-5)

God chose us before the foundation of the world. Before anything existed, before we were even a thought in our parents' minds, He knew us personally and selected us to be His children. This is an incredible reality.

God has predestined us for three things:

**To be holy.** Holiness means to be set apart, to be different. Like special chopsticks reserved for a particular purpose—not ordinary, but sacred—we have been chosen to be different from the rest of the world.

**To be blameless.** When we look in a mirror, we see physical imperfections. But if we could see ourselves spiritually as God sees us, we would see far worse—our sins, our failures, our brokenness. Yet God blesses us by deciding before time itself to make us blameless before Him. All our faults, all our flaws, are wiped clean in His sight.

**To be adopted as His children.** It is God's will that we become His sons and daughters. What a privilege! We can relate to God as a child relates to their father and mother—with trust, dependence, and the confidence that He has the power to provide all we need and knows how best to care for us.

### What This Means for Us Today

First, remember how special you are in God's eyes. You may feel like a nobody at school. You may be chosen last for sports teams or passed over for leadership roles. But know this: God has chosen you to be His child, to be holy and blameless—not because of your goodness, but because of His love. This is an amazing privilege.

Second, we must be humble. Any good we do comes from God, not from ourselves. It is by His will alone that we live in right relationship with Him. When you see others struggling to obey God, don't look down on them. It is only by God's blessing and grace that you are any different.

## 2. God Has Provided Redemption (Verse 7)

> In him we have redemption through his blood, the forgiveness of sins, in accordance with the riches of God's grace. (Ephesians 1:7)

We are a people who have rebelled against God. We have trespassed against Him by disobeying and living our own way. We don't deserve to be blessed—we deserve the opposite. We deserve to be cursed, to be cut off from God. Our actions prove we are slaves of sin, not children of God.

But Jesus has redeemed us from that slavery. Redemption means being purchased back, being bought out of bondage. We belonged to sin and Satan, but Jesus paid the price to set us free. That price was His own blood. We deserved to lose our blood through death as punishment for our sins, but Jesus took that punishment in our place.

### What This Means for Us

To be a Christian is to trust in this redemption—to believe that Jesus has done it all, that we did nothing to earn it. Just as we didn't choose ourselves (God predestined us), we didn't redeem ourselves (Jesus redeemed us).

For those who trust in Christ: continue to remember God's grace. When you're tempted to complain about life's difficulties—exams, demanding parents, the struggles of daily living—remember that these pale in significance compared to the blessing of Christ's redemption. Yes, life is hard, but we have been blessed with *every spiritual blessing in Christ*.

For those who don't yet trust in Christ: I urge you to do so. God has sent His Son into the world. It is by trusting in Him alone that you can be spiritually blessed, made right with God, holy and blameless in His sight.

## 3. God Has Proven It Through the Holy Spirit (Verses 13-14)

> And you also were included in Christ when you heard the word of truth, the gospel of your salvation. Having believed, you were marked in him with a seal, the promised Holy Spirit, who is a deposit guaranteeing our inheritance until the redemption of those who are God's possession—to the praise of his glory. (Ephesians 1:13-14)

How can we be certain that God's promises will come to pass in the future? The answer is found in the Holy Spirit, whom God gives to everyone who believes.

When are we sealed by the Spirit? Not when we have an emotional experience or see a mystical light. Look at verse 13: we are sealed when we *hear the word of truth and believe*. The moment we embrace the gospel as truth, God marks us with the Holy Spirit.

The Holy Spirit doesn't give us magical powers. Rather, He convicts us of our sins and reveals the truth of the gospel to us. He works continuously in us, convincing us that what Scripture says is true, empowering us to act on that truth, and transforming us to become holy and blameless. You know you have the Holy Spirit if you believe the gospel is true and you commit yourself to that truth.

The Holy Spirit is a deposit—a down payment on your heavenly inheritance. When you place a deposit on a house or put something on lay-away, you're making a partial payment that signals your commitment. It tells others, "This is mine," and it guarantees you'll complete the purchase. Similarly, God places His seal on you and says, "This child is Mine." While we might lose interest or run out of resources, God never changes His mind and never runs out of power. If He has placed a deposit on you, it's as good as 100% certain.

### What This Means for Us

We can be confident as Christians that God will keep His promise. We can be certain because the way into this promise is secured by God's own work—He causes us to believe. He sealed it with His Holy Spirit.

## The Complete Picture of Blessing

God has **predestined** us—making blessing a possibility. God has **provided** redemption in Christ—making blessing a reality. God has **proven** our inheritance through the Holy Spirit—guaranteeing our blessing will be fulfilled.

This is why Christians are indeed blessed.

## Spiritual Blessing vs. Material Blessing

Yet we must ask the hard question: If Christians have every blessing, why do we still suffer? Why do we get sick? Why do we fail? Why do we struggle?

The answer is found in verse 3: God has blessed us with *every spiritual blessing*—not every blessing, but every *spiritual* blessing. In our relationship with God through Christ, we lack nothing. If you trust in Jesus, you are in perfect relationship with God, whether you feel it or not.

However, we still live on earth, and our daily physical problems remain. We will still sin. We will still be sick. We will still struggle. God has fixed our biggest problem—our separation from Him—but not all our problems.

Therefore, we must be patient. We must not be anxious or demanding. We must wait for Jesus to return. The Holy Spirit is our deposit, our guarantee, pointing us forward to the full inheritance when everything will be made perfect—not just our spiritual relationship with God, but every aspect of our existence.

We look forward to a day when we will be blessed spiritually, physically, and materially in every way—when there will be no more sin, no more tears, no more suffering. Until that day, we live as those who have tasted God's blessing and eagerly await its completion.
