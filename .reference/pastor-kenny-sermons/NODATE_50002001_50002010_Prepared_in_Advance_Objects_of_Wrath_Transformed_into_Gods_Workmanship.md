# Philippians 2:1-10

**Title:** Prepared in Advance: Objects of Wrath Transformed into God's Workmanship
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## Introduction

We begin a series on the radical Christian life as we follow Jesus. At the start of the year, with many newcomers joining us, this is a perfect time to reset ourselves spiritually. The radical life calls us to pursue not merely the good, but the best—and the best is the gospel itself.

Let me start with an illustration. In Malaysia, I noticed they don't have council pickup days like we do here. In our cities, the council leaves certain days when household items are placed on the street for collection—old furniture, worn appliances, things deemed worthless. They sit there, destined for the garbage truck. I've seen beautiful pieces of furniture, perfectly usable items, reduced to rubble in moments. A toaster that can't be given away. These objects await destruction.

This image captures something profound about our spiritual condition before Christ.

---

## 1. Objects of Wrath (Ephesians 2:1-3)

Let's read verses 1-3:

> As for you, you were dead in your transgressions and sins, in which you used to live when you followed the ways of this world and of the ruler of the kingdom of the air, the spirit who is now at work in those who are disobedient. All of us also lived among them at one time, gratifying the cravings of our sinful nature and following its desires and thoughts. Like the rest, we were by nature objects of wrath.

The conclusion is stark: **we are objects of wrath.** We are dead. When someone says "you are dead meat," they mean you are condemned, marked for destruction.

### Why God's Anger Is Justified

God is angry at us because we:

**1. Have committed transgressions and sins** (v. 1)
We have broken God's laws. We know what is right, yet we do wrong.

**2. Have followed the ways of the world** (v. 2a)
We've made our own laws, created our own moral standards, lived by the world's values rather than God's.

**3. Have followed the ruler of the kingdom of the air** (v. 2b)
This refers to Satan—the personification of evil. In heaven's past, Satan rebelled against God, lost that battle, and was cast down. Yet he was permitted to rule the earth for a time (Revelation 12). The "air" is that realm between earth and heaven, and the world remains under his dominion. We have betrayed our Creator and followed His archenemy.

**4. Have gratified the cravings of our sinful nature** (v. 3)
We pursue pleasure, lusts, materialism—following what feels right rather than what is right. Modern culture constantly tells us to "follow your heart" and "trust your feelings." But when our nature is sinful, following our feelings leads us astray. Feelings are a poor compass when the compass itself is broken.

### We Deserve Condemnation

Rightly, we are objects of wrath. Condemned. Ready for destruction.

But here's the difficulty: it's hard to imagine ourselves as objects of wrath. We think, "I'm not that bad. I haven't murdered anyone. I haven't stolen. I care for my family and friends. Surely I don't deserve God's judgment."

We think of ourselves like Mr. Bean—clumsy, foolish perhaps, but fundamentally harmless. Yet watch Mr. Bean closely, and you'll see something darker: he is profoundly selfish. He ignores authorities and his mother's worries. He neglects those in his care to pursue his own pleasures. He cheats, he hoards, he makes others wait. We laugh because the chaos he creates seems contained, because luck and script intervention prevent real tragedy. But absent God's grace protecting those around us from our selfishness, we would cause far greater harm.

We are not objects of wrath because we're obviously evil. We're objects of wrath because we're sinful, and the consequences of sin are far-reaching—affecting people we don't even know, people we would never hurt intentionally, yet whom our selfish choices harm nonetheless.

## 2. Objects of Mercy (Ephesians 2:4-9)

But then comes the turning point. Verses 4-5:

> But because of his great love for us, God, who is rich in mercy, made us alive with Christ even when we were dead in transgressions—it is by grace you have been saved.

That word **"but"** is everything. The trajectory of your life changes in that one word.

If you are a Christian, you are no longer marked for destruction. Rather than being dead in the future, you will be alive—eternally alive in God's presence.

Listen to verses 6-9:

> And God raised us up with Christ and seated us with him in the heavenly realms in Christ Jesus, in order that in the coming ages he might show the incomparable riches of his grace, expressed in his kindness to us in Christ Jesus. For it is by grace you have been saved, through faith—and this not from yourselves, it is the gift of God—not by works, so that no one can boast.

God doesn't just rescue us and leave us broken on the curb. He restores us completely. He cleans us, fills the broken places, sands away the damage, and applies layer after layer of grace until we shine again. And this restoration continues throughout eternity—in the coming ages, God will continue to display the incomparable riches of His grace toward us in Christ.

### Three Responses to Our Condition

When we realize we are like those items destined for the garbage truck, there are three possible responses:

**1. Ignore the problem**
We can pretend we're not that sinful. "I'm not perfect, but I'm surely not wicked." This is self-deception.

**2. Work to fix ourselves**
We can acknowledge the problem but believe we can solve it through our own efforts—through good works, church attendance, religious devotions, trying to pay back our sin debt. But this is impossible. The consequences of our sin are too far-reaching, touching people we don't even know. One act of anger can ripple outward, causing others to respond with anger, which harms their families, which affects their communities. We cannot undo what we've broken.

**3. Trust in Jesus**
The third response is to simply have faith. Faith means putting your trust entirely in someone else—in Jesus, who took God's wrath upon Himself through His death so that we are now free from the penalty of sin.

Consider a lifeguard on the beach. If I'm caught in a dangerous rip current, I have three options: pretend I'm not in trouble and ignore it, or exhaust myself trying to swim out. Both lead to drowning. The only wise response is to raise my hand, call out for help, and trust the lifeguard to rescue me. That is faith in Jesus. It is not work—it is the opposite of work. It is admitting I cannot save myself and asking for help.

### Grace, Not Merit

Notice the timing in verse 5: God saved us "when we were dead in transgressions." Not when we were thinking positively. Not when we were doing wonderful deeds. Not when I aced a test or was feeling spiritually strong. No—He saved us when we were dead, helpless, undeserving.

"For it is by grace you have been saved, through faith—and this not from yourselves, it is the gift of God—not by works, so that no one can boast."

If you are not yet a Christian, today is the day. Recognize that you are marked for destruction, that you cannot save yourself, and that Jesus has already paid your debt. All that remains is for you to put your trust in Him. The garbage truck is coming—we are in the final minutes before it reaches our house. The time to decide is now.

## 3. Objects of God's Workmanship (Ephesians 2:10)

But this is not the end of the story. We are not rescued merely to sit safely in the garage, unused and purposeless.

> For we are God's workmanship, created in Christ Jesus to do good works, which God prepared in advance for us to do.

The word "workmanship" suggests a masterpiece—something crafted with skill and intention, an engineering marvel, a work of art. You are God's creation, His handiwork, designed for a specific purpose.

### Rescued for Purpose

Before Christ, we had no purpose. We were destined for destruction. Now, rescued and remade, you are meant to become a vessel of good works. You are designed to serve, to love, to help, to rescue others as you have been rescued.

This is not burdensome duty. You don't have to do good works to earn God's love—He has already given you that love freely. Good works are not obligation but opportunity. They are your grateful response to grace.

Imagine a neighbor helps you in a time of crisis—you don't serve them out of debt but out of genuine gratitude. In the same way, we serve God not from guilt but from love, recognizing what He has done for us.

### The Year Ahead

What does serving look like practically? Consider:

**1. Supporting the mission financially**
God has entrusted resources to you. Consider pledging what you can—even a small amount makes a difference.

**2. Giving time and effort**
There are many ways to participate: serving in various capacities, encouraging one another in faith, caring for those in need.

**3. Looking after the poor and vulnerable**
Those who lack food lack the basic stability to hear and respond to the gospel. Feeding the hungry is not separate from spiritual work—it opens the door for spiritual life.

**4. Offering your unique gifts**
If you have skills we need—whether organizing events, leading worship, mentoring others—please let us know. We're always looking for those willing to serve.

## Conclusion

In Malaysia, they don't have council pickup days, but they have waste services—garbage stinking in the heat, useless plastic, soiled scraps of newspaper destined for burning.

God didn't pick us up when we were pristine furniture needing only a bit of sanding. He picked us up as useless, disposable plastic—wet and dirty scraps of newspaper. He saw us at our worst and loved us anyway. He rescued us.

And now He calls us to serve Him—not out of guilt, not as payment for what we've been given, but out of loving gratitude. You have been rescued. You have been restored. You have been given purpose. Live accordingly.
