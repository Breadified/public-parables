# Ephesians 5:21-33

**Title:** The Ideal Husband?
**Date:** November 11, 2007
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## What Is God's Ideal Husband?

We often picture the ideal husband through the world's lens—a pop star's popularity, a romantic hero's charm, physical attractiveness, intellectual brilliance, financial success, or leadership prowess. But Scripture offers us a different standard entirely. God's ideal husband is defined not by external qualities, but by two essential characteristics: he rules with authority, and he loves sacrificially.

## The Husband Leads: Authority and Responsibility

### The Foundation of Headship

> Wives, submit to your husbands as to the Lord. For the husband is the head of the wife as Christ is the head of the church, his body, of which he is the Savior. Now as the church submits to Christ, so also wives should submit to their husbands in everything. (Ephesians 5:22-24)

The word "head" means leadership and responsibility. A head—like a headmaster or department head—holds a position of authority. It's not a complicated concept; it simply means having charge over something.

### Understanding the Metaphor: Christ and the Church

Paul illustrates this relationship by comparing the husband-wife dynamic to Christ and the Church. When Scripture speaks of the church, it refers to the great gathering of all believers—past, present, and future—assembled before God on the final day. The church is also described as Christ's body, meaning:

- **Indivisibility**: Christ as head and the church as body are inseparably united and cannot be divided.
- **Leadership**: Just as the head directs the body, Christ directs the church. He is in charge, making decisions about where the body goes and what it does.

### Head as Authority, Not Merely Source

Some suggest that "head" simply means source, like the head of a river. While it's true that in Genesis woman came from man, this interpretation has problems:

1. When we examine Christ's relationship to the church in this very passage, Christ is clearly not just the source—He holds authority over the church, with all things under Him.
2. Scholars of Greek have found that "head" rarely means source in biblical usage.

Paul intends for us to understand that the husband is the leader of his wife. As leader, he:

- Makes the decisions
- Calls the final shots
- Leads his family's direction
- Bears responsibility for outcomes—he is ultimately accountable

### Why Male Headship? The Practical Answer

The concept of male headship challenges our modern democratic sensibilities. Why shouldn't the wife lead? Why not share power equally?

The answer lies in practical reality: marriage is a partnership of two people. Democracy requires more than two participants. When only two people must make decisions together, unanimous agreement becomes necessary—but this creates deadlock. Decisions get stuck. No direction emerges.

Consider something as simple as naming a baby: one spouse wants Mary, the other wants Martha. Without a tiebreaker, you're paralyzed. This dysfunction harms the family's function.

Therefore, God appointed a decisive structure: someone must have the final authority to break ties and move the family forward. While one could argue for either gender to hold this role, the result would be the same complaint from whichever gender was excluded. God's design eliminates endless debate and frees husbands and wives to focus on building their life together.

### Application for Men

Men, you are called to exercise authority actively and take responsibility over your wife and family. Don't wait passively for permission; initiate leadership.

Many men fail in leadership not through dominance, but through *absence*. They hide behind work, television, or newspapers, becoming passive bystanders in their own homes. This reflects a pattern in many Asian families, where fathers remain distant authority figures—intervening only in crises while mothers handle daily life. This is not leadership; it's abdication.

True leadership demands active involvement:

- **Family direction**: Initiate conversations about where your family is headed and what concerns need addressing.
- **Spiritual guidance**: Lead in prayer and Bible study. Don't leave this to your wife.
- **Emotional and physical care**: Be present and engaged.
- **Daily presence**: You cannot lead while reading the newspaper at dinner and retreating to television afterward, claiming exhaustion.
- **Conflict resolution**: When problems arise, initiate solutions rather than avoiding them.

### For Unmarried Men

This applies to you as well—not in ruling over women now, but in preparing for future leadership. Start practicing these skills today. Learn to exercise responsibility in your Bible study groups. If you don't initiate prayer or spiritual direction, others will fill the void. Many young women don't want to lead not because they're unable, but because young men have abdicated the responsibility so consistently.

## The Husband Leads: In Sacrificial Love

### The Standard of Love

But before men venture forth with grand plans about authority, we must understand *how* husbands are to lead. The answer is breathtaking: through sacrificial love.

> Husbands, love your wives, just as Christ loved the church and gave himself up for her to make her holy, cleansing her by the washing with water through the word, and to present her to himself as a radiant church, without stain or wrinkle or any other blemish, but holy and blameless. In this same way, husbands ought to love their wives as their own bodies. He who loves his wife loves himself. After all, no one ever hated his own body, but he feeds and cares for it, just as Christ does the church—for we are members of his body. "For this reason a man will leave his father and mother and be united to his wife, and the two will become one flesh." This is a profound mystery—but I am talking about Christ and the church. However, each one of you also must love his wife as he loves himself, and the wife must respect her husband. (Ephesians 5:25-33)

The standard is radical: love your wife as you love your own body. You instinctively feed and care for your body; you are to do the same for your wife with that same natural devotion.

### The Example: Christ's Love

Paul illustrates this through Christ's love for the church:

- Christ loved the church so intensely that He gave Himself up for her
- He went to the cross to make her holy, to cleanse her, to present her as radiant and blameless
- He is our Savior—He literally sacrificed His body so that His body (the church) might live
- This is other-centered love at its most costly

### Leadership Redefined: Servant Leadership

This model of leadership contradicts the world's understanding entirely. We typically envision leadership as power, dominance, and control. Leaders ask: How do I gain more power? How do I manipulate circumstances to serve my agenda? How do I secure my position and protect my ego?

We see this in school prefects, in political campaigns, in boardrooms. We vote for leaders who promise to serve our interests while they serve their own. Everyone walks away feeling they've made a selfish deal together.

Imagine instead voting for someone because he will care for everyone's welfare—even at his own expense. Imagine a leader who says, "I'll give up my tax cuts because I want everyone to benefit from better schools." This is revolutionary.

**True leadership in marriage is servant leadership—exercising authority for another person's benefit, not your own.**

It is not:
- Domineering or controlling
- Self-serving
- An ego trip or power play

It is:
- Love in action
- Service rendered willingly
- Authority exercised for her flourishing

### Application for Men

This requires sacrifice:

- Turn off the television for a family discussion, even when you're exhausted, because it's good for your family.
- Initiate prayer with your wife and lead her in learning Scripture.
- Request submission rather than demand it. The Bible never commands you to force obedience.
- Lead by being first—first to serve, first to fix the problem, first to initiate what is beneficial.
- Lead in godliness.

### For Unmarried Men

- Pray with your girlfriend and lead spiritually.
- Be the first to address problems, the first to serve, the first to start something good.
- Build a pattern now of servant leadership.

### A Word to Women

This calling to servant leadership is extraordinarily difficult. Leading is hard enough; leading with a mindset of love is harder still. The job description reads: "Die for the other person. Interested parties apply."

Men will make mistakes—sometimes significant ones—not out of malice but from simple human frailty. Leadership is burdensome, with competing priorities and self-esteem constantly threatened. Men need support from their wives.

This is where submission becomes essential. Submission means understanding that your husband *will* make mistakes but choosing not to undermine his ability to lead. When a wife constantly corrects him ("I told you so") or threatens his authority, his ego suffers deeply. He withdraws. The impulse to lead dies, replaced by resignation and television.

If you choose a husband who loves you sacrificially, submission becomes far easier. So choose wisely.

## God's Ideal Husband

Who, then, is the ideal husband according to Scripture?

It's not about physical attractiveness, intelligence, coolness, romantic flair, sexiness, or wealth. It's about **loving leadership and responsibility**. He simply needs to be adequate to lead one woman faithfully—and from her perspective, she needs to be able to submit to this one man.

Yet even the best husband will fall short of this standard in this lifetime. Husbands are fragile men with fragile egos, prone to failure and weakness. There is no ideal husband among us.

But there is one who perfectly fulfills this calling: **Jesus Christ is the ideal husband.**

He did not delegate His responsibility or run from His duty. He sacrificed His life for His bride, the church. His self-sacrifice ensures her welfare and her glory. Every aspect of His leadership flows from love so deep that He poured out His life.

> I want to submit to this husband. (Ephesians 5:24)

So the call stands: **Husbands, love your wives as Christ loved the church. Wives, submit to your husbands as to the Lord.**
