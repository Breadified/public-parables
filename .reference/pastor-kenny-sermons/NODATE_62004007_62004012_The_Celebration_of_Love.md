# 1 John 4:7-12

**Title:** The Celebration of Love  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## What Is Love?

A wedding celebration is a wonderful thing—months of planning culminate in the melding of two families, the union of a man and woman. But beneath all the festivities lies something profound: **the celebration of love.**

Yet what kind of love are we really celebrating? There are many definitions.

**The Evolutionary Definition:** Biology teaches us that love is rooted in physical attraction—a woman's waist-to-hip ratio signaling fertility, a man's broad shoulders suggesting strength and provision. It's survival of the species dressed in romantic language.

**The Chemical Definition:** Science reveals that love is orchestrated by neurochemistry. Testosterone and estrogen drive the initial search. When we find "the one," dopamine floods our system, making us feel euphoric. Adrenaline quickens our heartbeat. Serotonin temporarily clouds our judgment. Finally, oxytocin and vasopressin bond us to our beloved. Love, reduced to a cascade of hormones.

**The Theological Definition:** But God's definition stands apart from both evolution and chemistry.

---

## God Is Love

> 7 Dear friends, let us love one another, for love comes from God.

God is the source of all love. If love comes from anywhere, it comes from Him.

> 8 Whoever does not love does not know God, because God is love.

This is not merely an attribute God wears like a mask, something He removes when no one is looking. Love is not a fleeting mood that comes and goes. **God IS love.** His very nature is love. Every fiber of His being is love. It is His desire, His motivation, His identity.

If you could chisel a piece from God, it would be love. If you operated on Him and took a biopsy from His deepest core, you would find love. If God were to sweat, He would sweat love. If you cut Him, He bleeds love.

And when God came to this earth, we see this love incarnate in Jesus.

---

## Jesus Demonstrates Love

> 9 This is how God showed his love among us: He sent his one and only Son into the world that we might live through him.

Two thousand years ago, in a small corner of the Middle East, God demonstrated His love by sending His only Son into the world so that we might live through Him.

> 10 This is love: not that we loved God, but that he loved us and sent his Son as an atoning sacrifice for our sins.

Jesus was crucified for crimes He did not commit. He was hung on a cross as an atoning sacrifice—God's own blood spilled for our sake. This is love made visible.

By contrast, humanity is not inherently love. While we are capable of love, it is not woven into our nature. Dig beneath the surface, beneath the mask, and what do we find? Selfishness, hatred, apathy. When pressure mounts, we look out for ourselves.

But Jesus showed us something radically different—**other-centered love.** He demonstrated God's love, sacrificial love, patient and kind love. He showed us a God slow to anger and abounding in love.

---

## Love One Another

> 11 Dear friends, since God so loved us, we also ought to love one another.

Those who have experienced God's love through Jesus understand what it truly costs to love another human being. When we are loved this way, we are called to love others the same way.

God's love flows through us to those around us. In a marriage, it flows between husband and wife.

This theological definition of love is what should sustain a marriage—not the fading hormones or physical attraction, but a commitment to **sacrificial, other-centered love.**

This is the love of "I will," not merely "I do." "I do" means you love today. But marriage demands "I will"—a promise for tomorrow and all the tomorrows after that. It is love that transcends the chemical rush and remains when physical attraction fades.

It is unconditional love. It is love when you are no longer "in love." It is love when hormones cease to work, when physical beauty changes, when the other person wrongs you, when you both become difficult—when he's a jerk and she's nagging, when she's messy and he's aloof.

This is the love first shown to us by God in Jesus. This is the love that marriages are meant to reflect.

> 12 No one has ever seen God; but if we love one another, God lives in us and his love is made complete in us.

When we love one another with God's love, the world sees Jesus. God becomes visible through our love for each other.
