# Hebrews 10:24-25

**Title:** Meeting God's Family  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Foundation: Saved to Serve

In previous weeks, we've established that we are now saved from God's future wrath. This salvation is not earned—it's a gift of God's great love and mercy.

> But because of his great love for us, God, who is rich in mercy, made us alive with Christ even when we were dead in transgressions—it is by grace you have been saved. (Ephesians 2:4-5)

But salvation isn't meant to be a passive experience. We are called to be people created to act rightly and do good works. The grace that saves us also instructs us:

> For the grace of God that brings salvation has appeared to all men. It teaches us to say "No" to ungodliness and worldly passions, and to live self-controlled, upright and godly lives in this present age, while we wait for the blessed hope—the glorious appearing of our great God and Savior, Jesus Christ, who gave himself for us to redeem us from all wickedness and to purify for himself a people that are his very own, eager to do what is good. (Titus 2:11-14)

## How Do We Know What Is Righteous?

The answer lies in Scripture itself. God's Word is our guide for understanding righteousness and goodness:

> All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness, so that the man of God may be thoroughly equipped for every good work. (2 Timothy 3:16-17)

## Living Before Others: Outsiders and Insiders

Our righteous living has different expressions depending on who we're with.

**With Outsiders:**
> Be wise in the way you act toward outsiders; make the most of every opportunity. Let your conversation be always full of grace, seasoned with salt, so that you may know how to answer everyone. (Colossians 4:5-6)

**With Insiders (the Church):**

This brings us to our text. When Christians meet together, we have a sacred responsibility:

> And let us consider how we may spur one another on toward love and good deeds. Let us not give up meeting together, as some are in the habit of doing, but let us encourage one another—and all the more as you see the Day approaching. (Hebrews 10:24-25)

## Key Questions for Reflection

- What should Christians do when they meet together?
- Why is it good for Christians to meet together?
- What opportunities do you have to meet with other Christians, and what should you do when you do?
