## Why We Live Godly Lives: God's Appearance

> "For the grace of God has appeared that offers salvation to all people. It teaches us to say 'No' to ungodliness and worldly passions, and to live self-controlled, upright and godly lives in this present age, while we wait for the blessed hope—the appearing of the glory of our great God and Savior, Jesus Christ, who gave himself for us to redeem us from all wickedness and to purify for himself a people that are his very own, eager to do what is good." (Titus 2:11-14)

There's a story behind every truth. Many assume Christianity is simply about following moral rules—good behavior leads to heaven, bad behavior to hell. But the deeper story reveals something far more transformative: Christians live godly lives not through external obligation, but through the appearing of Jesus Christ and what His grace accomplishes in us.

---

## The Appearance of God's Grace Teaches Us to Be Godly

### God's Grace Has Appeared

Grace means generosity—God's undeserved kindness. This grace appeared supremely when Jesus Christ came to earth 2,000 years ago, died on the cross, and offered salvation to all people. When God "did not spare his own son, but gave him up for us all"—that's when God's grace appeared most clearly for everyone to see.

### God's Grace Appeared to Teach

Paul acknowledges a fundamental human reality: we don't naturally know how to be good. Teaching implies imparting knowledge to someone ignorant—and we are ignorant of godliness by nature.

Consider Titus 1:16: "They claim to know God, but by their actions they deny him. They are detestable, disobedient and unfit for doing anything good." This describes our natural state. Most of us avoid comparing ourselves to God's standards, instead measuring ourselves against others. But when we compare ourselves to God's holiness, we realize we are ungodly beings who crash through life not knowing what truly is right and wrong.

In the chaos of human ignorance, God appears to teach us how to live in order, how to live in love. That's why Titus repeatedly emphasizes teaching:

> "You, however, must teach what is appropriate to sound doctrine." (2:1)

> "Teach the older men... Teach the older women...teach what is good." (2:2-3)

> "Our people must learn to devote themselves to doing what is good." (3:14)

### What Grace Teaches Us

God's grace teaches us three things:

**Say "No" to Ungodliness and Worldly Passions**

Ungodliness means denying or disobeying God. Worldly passions means living materialistically, consumed by the things of this present world. The Cretan churches faced a culture known for these very vices—as one of their own prophets said: "Cretans are always liars, evil brutes, lazy gluttons" (Titus 1:12).

**Say "Yes" to Self-Controlled, Upright, and Godly Living**

- **Self-controlled** means sensible, clear-minded, and ruled by wisdom
- **Upright** means righteous, just, and blameless
- **Godly** means faithful to God's truth

### Teaching Godliness Across Different Life Stages

**For Older Men (2:2):** Be sober-minded, dignified, self-controlled, sound in faith, in love, and in steadfastness.

Naturally, men can be ruled by career, women, money, power, or pride. But godliness calls men to maturity—to be rock-like and steady anchors in their communities. They must be faithful not because it's easy, but because they belong to Christ. They become models of good works, showing integrity and sound speech.

**For Younger Men (2:6-8):** Be self-controlled and set an example by doing what is good. Show integrity, seriousness, and soundness of speech.

Young men are called to the same maturity, proving to those who oppose the faith that there is nothing bad to say about them.

**For Older Women (2:3-4):** Be reverent in behavior. Don't be slanderers or slaves to wine. Teach what is good to younger women.

Slandering—falsely accusing others—is universal across cultures and generations. Women's words can be dangerous weapons. Instead, older women model reverence and teach the younger generation what is good and helpful.

**For Younger Women (2:4-5):** Love your husbands and children. Be self-controlled, pure, and devoted to home. Be kind and submissive to your husbands.

In a culture of arranged marriages, and in our age of romanticism and easy divorce, young women need to be trained in what genuine, faithful love looks like. They must learn to be sensible with their resources, pure in thought and action, and to understand that managing a home is respectable and good work—whether done exclusively or alongside other vocations.

**For Slaves (2:9-10):** Be obedient, avoid talking back, and show yourself to be entirely trustworthy and honorable.

## The Appearance of God's Grace Purifies Us to Be a New People

Simply teaching godliness isn't enough—we lose motivation. We need a stronger reason to pursue holiness. That reason is found in what Christ's appearance accomplishes beyond teaching.

### Christ Purifies Us Through His Sacrifice

> "He gave himself for us to redeem us from all wickedness and to purify for himself a people that are his very own, eager to do what is good." (2:14)

Christ didn't come merely to teach, perform miracles, or prophesy. He came to give His life for ours. He came to the cross to redeem us—paying the price with His own death so we could be freed from all wickedness.

**We cannot purify ourselves.** Only Christ's death on the cross accomplishes this purification. When you redeem something, you pay a price. Christ paid the ultimate price.

### We Are Now God's Chosen People

We're not just improved moral beings following better rules. We're **redeemed and transformed people**—given new identity and new status. We are "a people that are his very own," His chosen people, His community.

Earlier in Titus 1, we saw people described as "liars, evil brutes, lazy gluttons, corrupted, detestable, disobedient and unfit for doing anything good." Christians were once like that. But through Christ's purification, we are no longer. We are eager to do good works—not to earn salvation, but because we've been transformed.

### Purification Creates Motivation

Consider a full mouth reconstruction in dentistry. After years of neglect, a patient's teeth are rotten, their gums and jaw destroyed, their bite completely warped. The reconstruction takes months or years and costs tens of thousands of dollars. But once completed, the patient has a functioning, orderly, healthy set of teeth. They wouldn't dream of returning to their old habits. The great cost and effort invested creates powerful motivation to maintain what's been restored.

Similarly, we are redeemed at infinite cost—the blood of Jesus Christ on the cross. We are His newly purified people. Why would we return to our old ungodly ways? The appearance of Christ's sacrifice creates irresistible motivation to live for Him and do good works.

---

## Living Out Our Purification

When you lack motivation to do good, when you're too tired or stressed, or when you honestly don't know how to live rightly—return to the cross. Look at what Christ has done for you. His grace has purified you. You are His people. His team. His own.

When you truly understand this deeply, you'll persevere in doing good. Not from fear of punishment or hope of earning heaven. But from gratitude for the One who already paid everything to make you His own.

The story behind every Christian's desire to live godly is this: **God has appeared in Jesus Christ. His grace teaches us how to live rightly. His sacrifice purifies us and gives us new identity. And that is why we eagerly do good.**
