# 1 Peter 1:1-2:3

**Title:** Alien Born
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Birth Rights

### 1. Christians Are Born Into Living Hope

> **1 Peter 1:3** Praise be to the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ! In his great mercy he has given us new birth into a living hope through the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead.

Our birthplace determines our privileges. Being born in a certain country brings automatic rights—citizenship, security, belonging. But as Christians, we have a different kind of birthplace altogether.

#### An Inheritance

In Scripture, inheritance means something specific: land. God promised Abraham not just blessing, but a great land—an inheritance. This promise continued through the centuries. Moses led the people toward this inheritance. David and Solomon saw its greatest expansion. Yet Israel eventually lost it in exile to Babylon.

But with Jesus, everything changes. We inherit a land too—a heavenly one.

Peter writes to a landless people scattered across the Roman Empire (1:1). They are strangers, foreigners, aliens in the world. They have no earthly inheritance, no Roman citizenship, no security or privilege. Yet Peter reminds them: **you have an inheritance**.

> **1 Peter 1:4** and into an inheritance that can never perish, spoil or fade—kept in heaven for you.

This is not earthly wealth that can be stolen or eroded. There is no inflation, no thief, no decay, no obsolescence. It is protected from every earthly threat—from global warming, natural disasters, time itself.

#### Protected and Eternal

> **1 Peter 1:5** who through faith are shielded by God's power until the coming of the salvation that is ready to be revealed in the last time.

Our inheritance is not just waiting; it's guarded. God's power shields it for us until the final revelation of salvation.

#### For Those Who Doubt

Some of us may struggle with this promise. We might feel worried because we don't emotionally sense God's joy. We might feel frustrated because we haven't seen Jesus with our own eyes.

> **1 Peter 1:6-7** In this you greatly rejoice, though now for a little while you may have had to suffer grief in all kinds of trials. These have come so that your faith—of greater worth than gold, which perishes even though refined by fire—may be proved genuine and may result in praise, glory and honor when Jesus Christ is revealed.

It's possible to be sad and joyful simultaneously: grieving present troubles while rejoicing in future salvation. Our faith, tested like gold in fire, is being proven genuine.

> **1 Peter 1:8-9** Though you have not seen him, you love him; and even though you do not see him now, you believe in him and are filled with an inexpressible and glorious joy, for you are receiving the goal of your faith, the salvation of your souls.

Not seeing Jesus is normal. The early Christians didn't see him either, yet they loved him, believed in him, and experienced overwhelming joy. This is the Christian experience: faith without sight, love without seeing, joy without physical proof.

We doubt our salvation sometimes. But we're called to believe anyway. The answer isn't to seek new visions or signs—it's to trust the promise already given.

#### A Future Hope

> **1 Peter 1:9** for you are receiving the goal of your faith, the salvation of your souls.

This inheritance is still to come, not some patch of earth but a heavenly reality. Yet the promise is so certain that we can speak of it as already ours—like calling a mortgaged house "my house." The future ownership is guaranteed, so we claim it now.

## Born From the Living Word

How is this possible? We are born of earth. Yet we inherit heaven. Those born on earth should be foreigners to heaven.

The answer: we are not actually born of this earth. Through Jesus, we are born again—we are alien born.

### 2. Christians Are Born From the Living Word

#### A Living Word in the Past

> **1 Peter 1:10-12** Concerning this salvation, the prophets, who spoke of the grace that was to come to you, searched intently and with the greatest care, trying to find out the time and circumstances to which the Spirit of Christ in them was pointing when he predicted the sufferings of Christ and the glories that would follow... It was revealed to them that they were not serving themselves but you, when they spoke of the things that have now been told you by those who have preached the gospel to you by the Holy Spirit sent from heaven. Even angels long to look into these things.

The prophets of old spoke under the Spirit's guidance. They didn't know the name of Jesus, but what they spoke pointed to him—his sufferings, his rescue through blood, his resurrection. Their words were always relevant, always pointing to our salvation.

#### A Living Word in the Present

> **1 Peter 1:23** For you have been born again, not of perishable seed, but of imperishable, through the living and enduring word of God.

> **1 Peter 1:25** And this is the word that was preached to you.

We have been born again through something radically different from ordinary birth. Our DNA is not perishable, not dead, not temporary—it is imperishable, living, and enduring. It is heavenly.

This DNA matters because our birth determines our privileges. If we were born of perishable things, we'd inherit an empty way of life. But we are redeemed not with silver or gold:

> **1 Peter 1:18-21** For you know that it was not with perishable things such as silver or gold that you were redeemed from the empty way of life handed down to you from your forefathers, but with the precious blood of Christ, a lamb without blemish or defect. He was chosen before the creation of the world, but was revealed in these last times for your sake. Through him you believe in God, who raised him from the dead and glorified him, and so your faith and hope are in God.

Under Roman law, citizenship could be inherited by blood (*Jus Sanguinis*), not just birthplace. Children of Roman citizens were citizens themselves, even if born abroad. Similarly, we are born not of land but of blood—the precious blood of Christ. We have citizenship in heaven, an inheritance awaiting us in a place we've never physically been.

Our conversion stories vary. Some had a spiritual awakening. Some grew up in Christian families. Some joined friends at Scripture class. Some were convinced by apologetics. But the real birth—the one that matters—is through the word of the gospel: Jesus came, lived perfectly, died undeservedly to pay for our sin, rose to new life, and promises the same to all who believe.

If you haven't been born of the gospel, I'd love to talk with you. If you have, believe that word. The word is imperishable. Belong to it. Then you are imperishable too. Death no longer holds you. You are resurrected.

#### A Living Word in the Future

> **1 Peter 1:24-25** "All men are like grass, and all their glory is like the flowers of the field; the grass withers and the flowers fall, but the word of the Lord stands forever."

Do we believe this? The Word of God will endure forever while everything earthly fades.

**The Word Under Attack**

The Bible faces two attacks today:

**First Attack: Cultural Pressure to Move Forward**

Some claim the Bible is outdated. "Culture has moved on. Who knows what these old laws mean?" They proclaim ignorance, appeal to other authorities (studies, personal relationships), then confidently declare answers that reshape ancient institutions.

But Scripture interprets Scripture. When in doubt, hold to the conservative reading. Don't let contemporary culture override God's Word.

**Second Attack: Filling the Gaps**

While some deny what Scripture says, others add to it. Many Bible passages don't address our specific questions: What subject should I study? Who should I marry? Should I buy an iPad?

It's tempting to fill these gaps with extra words from God—visions, dreams, voices. But how do we know these are truly God and not our own desires or peer pressure? Some have claimed visions that led to harm. Others have engaged in "treasure hunts"—waiting for visions to direct evangelism efforts.

There's nothing wrong with evangelism, but we must be careful. The Bible is always relevant. If it doesn't answer a question, perhaps we're not meant to know, or meant to work it out ourselves. Instead of seeking new revelations, we need better comprehension skills, humbler hearts, and repentant hearts that recognize when we're manipulating truth to justify our sin.

We don't need extra words. We've been born of this Word alone. If you feel lacking, don't ask for words of knowledge—ask for wisdom to discern what's already in the text. Ask for repentance to apply what it says. Ask for strength to keep doing so when it gets hard. Meet with a leader to read Scripture together. Bring your Bible to church, to study, to school—carry it like a passport. We're not in heaven yet; we're in a foreign land. Keep God's Word close to us physically, mentally, and spiritually.

## Living as Aliens

Now that we understand our alien birth and alien privilege, how do we live?

### 3. Do Not Assimilate

> **1 Peter 1:13-16** Therefore, prepare your minds for action; be self-controlled; set your hope fully on the grace to be given you when Jesus Christ is revealed. As obedient children, do not conform to the evil desires you had when you lived in ignorance. But just as he who called you is holy, so be holy in all you do; for it is written: "Be holy, because I am holy."

Holy means set apart. Aliens don't belong to the earth, so we shouldn't act like natives. We have a different DNA, a different accent, different values. We act strangely because we're born of something different.

This applies to everything: how we speak, what we do, whom we marry. Dating a non-Christian doesn't make sense for the alien-born. Our DNA has nothing to do with this world's behavior.

### Work Hard

> **1 Peter 1:17** Since you call on a Father who judges each man's work impartially, live your lives as strangers here in reverent fear.

Live as strangers now. This isn't home. Work hard for the gospel, remembering that we're living as foreigners in a land that isn't ours.

This is harder for the young. Peer pressure is immense. It's easier for older believers to resist assimilation—they worry less about what friends think. But youth is the time when everything revolves around acceptance. Stay faithful to your alien identity.

### Crave the Living Word

> **1 Peter 2:1-3** Therefore, rid yourselves of all malice and all deceit, hypocrisy, envy, and slander of every kind. Like newborn babies, crave pure spiritual milk, so that by it you may grow up in your salvation, now that you have tasted that the Lord is good.

A newborn instinctively cries for milk. We who are born again should crave God's Word with the same hunger. Through the living, enduring, imperishable Word, we continue to taste that the Lord is good. Nothing else can satisfy this hunger.

---

## Conclusion: Alien Born

Where are you born?

Peter says we are alien born. We are born not of this earth but of the eternal Word. We are born of the blood of Christ. We have been given new birth into living hope through the resurrection of Jesus Christ.

This is our privilege. This is our identity. This is how we live.
