# Hebrews 10:24-25

**Title:** Meeting God's Family  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Foundation: Saved by Grace, Called to Good Works

In our previous weeks together, we've established a crucial truth: we are saved from God's future wrath. This salvation comes not through our own merit, but through the riches of God's mercy.

> But because of his great love for us, God, who is rich in mercy, made us alive with Christ even when we were dead in transgressions—it is by grace you have been saved. (Ephesians 2:4-5)

Yet salvation is not merely about our standing before God. We have been called to live as people who act rightly and pursue good works. The grace that saves us also transforms us.

> For the grace of God that brings salvation has appeared to all men. It teaches us to say "No" to ungodliness and worldly passions, and to live self-controlled, upright and godly lives in this present age, while we wait for the blessed hope—the glorious appearing of our great God and Savior, Jesus Christ, who gave himself for us to redeem us from all wickedness and to purify for himself a people that are his very own, eager to do what is good. (Titus 2:11-14)

## Knowing What Is Right and Good

But how do we discern what is truly righteous and good? Scripture itself is our guide.

> All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness, so that the man of God may be thoroughly equipped for every good work. (2 Timothy 3:16-17)

God has given us His Word to equip us completely for living righteously in every context.

## How We Live Among Others

Our righteous living takes different forms depending on who we're with:

**With Outsiders:** We must be wise in how we conduct ourselves, making the most of every opportunity. Our conversation should be marked by grace, seasoned with salt—speaking truth in a way that invites genuine response.

**With Fellow Believers:** Our dynamic changes within the family of God. We have unprecedented access to Him through Christ's work.

> Therefore, brothers, since we have confidence to enter the Most Holy Place by the blood of Jesus, by a new and living way opened for us through the curtain, that is, his body, and since we have a great priest over the house of God, let us draw near to God with a sincere heart in full assurance of faith, having our hearts sprinkled to cleanse us from a guilty conscience and having our bodies washed with pure water. Let us hold unswervingly to the hope we profess, for he who promised is faithful. And let us consider how we may spur one another on toward love and good deeds. Let us not give up meeting together, as some are in the habit of doing, but let us encourage one another—and all the more as you see the Day approaching. (Hebrews 10:19-25)

## Meeting Together: Purpose and Practice

When Christians gather, we are called to:
- **Spur one another on** toward love and good deeds
- **Encourage one another** through our presence and words
- **Hold fast** to our hope together
- **Not neglect** gathering, especially as we await Christ's return

This gathering isn't optional—it's essential to our spiritual growth and perseverance. As we see the Day approaching, our need for community only intensifies.

## Questions for Reflection

- What should Christians do when they meet together, and why is this practice so vital?
- What opportunities do you currently have to meet with other believers, and how can you maximize them?
- Is our church community truly fulfilling this calling to encourage and spur one another toward good works?
