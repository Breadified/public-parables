# John 3:1-36

**Title:** On the Side of Truth  
**Date:** 22 February 2015  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## You Must Be Born from Above (3:1-6)

Nicodemus, a Pharisee and member of the Jewish ruling council, comes to Jesus at night. As a leader of Israel's people, he recognizes something extraordinary in Jesus—he sees a teacher sent from God, one whose miraculous signs reveal God's presence and power.

But Jesus cuts through Nicodemus's observations with a startling declaration:

> Very truly I tell you, no one can see the kingdom of God unless they are born again—born from above.

The Greek word *anothen* carries a double meaning: both "again" and "from above." Jesus isn't speaking about a second physical birth, but a birth that originates from heaven itself—a spiritual rebirth that comes from God.

Nicodemus takes Jesus literally, confused: "How can someone be born when they are old? Surely they cannot enter a second time into their mother's womb!"

But Jesus explains through the imagery of natural elements—water and Spirit:

> No one can enter the kingdom of God unless they are born of water and the Spirit.

This language echoes the Old Testament's vision of restoration. In Isaiah 44:3-5, God promises Israel:

> I will pour water on the thirsty land, and streams on the dry ground; I will pour out my Spirit on your offspring, and my blessing on your descendants.

Water and wind—these are the symbols of God's blessing, cleansing, and life-giving power. In Ezekiel 36, God promises spiritual renewal: "I will sprinkle clean water on you, and you will be clean; I will give you a new heart and put a new spirit in you." And in Ezekiel 37, dry bones come to life when God's breath enters them—a picture of resurrection, of being born again from spiritual death.

Jesus clarifies the nature of these two births:

> Flesh gives birth to flesh, but the Spirit gives birth to spirit.

The first birth—our natural birth into this world—belongs to the flesh. The second birth—our spiritual birth into God's kingdom—comes only from the Spirit. This is what it means to be born from above.

### The Sovereignty of the Spirit

> The wind blows wherever it pleases. You hear its sound, but you cannot tell where it comes from or where it is going. So it is with everyone born of the Spirit.

The Spirit moves with freedom and sovereignty, not bound by human logic or control. Just as we cannot direct the wind, we cannot master God's Spirit. Being born again is not something we accomplish—it is something God does in us.

## You Must Believe in the Son (3:7-17)

Nicodemus still struggles to understand. Jesus replies with gentle rebuke:

> You are Israel's teacher, and do you not understand these things?

Here stands Nicodemus, a ruler and teacher of God's people, yet ignorant of this foundational truth. Why? Jesus explains:

> Very truly I tell you, we speak of what we know, and we testify to what we have seen, but still you people do not accept our testimony. I have spoken to you of earthly things and you do not believe; how then will you believe if I speak of heavenly things?

The problem is not intellectual capacity but spiritual blindness—a refusal to believe, a failure to accept testimony about heavenly realities.

Yet Jesus offers a solution. Only one person has authority to teach about being born from above:

> No one has ever gone into heaven except the one who came from heaven—the Son of Man.

Just as you would trust a book about Japan written by someone who has actually been to Japan, so you must trust Jesus about heavenly birth. He alone has come from above; He alone can speak with certainty about what it means to be born of the Spirit.

Jesus then draws Nicodemus back to the Old Testament:

> Just as Moses lifted up the snake in the wilderness, so the Son of Man must be lifted up, that everyone who believes in him may have eternal life.

In the wilderness, when serpents attacked Israel, God provided a cure: Moses lifted up a bronze serpent, and anyone—rich or poor, wise or foolish—who looked upon it was healed. No qualifications. No prerequisites. Just look and live.

So it is with salvation. The cross is lifted high on Golgotha so that anyone might look to Jesus and receive eternal life. And the motive behind this salvation is breathtaking:

> For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life.

God's love is not selective. It embraces the entire world. And God's purpose is not condemnation but salvation:

> For God did not send his Son into the world to condemn the world, but to save the world through him.

There are no excuses. The light has come. The way is open. Believe in the Son and live.

## The Consequence of Refusing the Light (3:18-36)

The stakes of this choice are absolute:

> Whoever believes in him is not condemned, but whoever does not believe stands condemned already because they have not believed in the name of God's one and only Son.

Notice: we do not choose condemnation. We are naturally condemned by our refusal of Christ. Our default state is separation from God. Salvation is opting into life; rejecting Christ is remaining in death.

Jesus frames this as a verdict about light and darkness:

> This is the verdict: Light has come into the world, but people loved darkness instead of light because their deeds were evil. Everyone who does evil hates the light and will not come into the light for fear that their deeds will be exposed. But whoever lives by the truth comes into the light, so that it may be seen plainly that what they have done has been done in the sight of God.

Notice the detail: Nicodemus came to Jesus *at night*. He sought the darkness, unwilling to have his deeds exposed. In John 3, he remains in darkness.

But look what happens later. In John 19:38-39, after the crucifixion, Nicodemus emerges from the shadows:

> Joseph of Arimathea asked Pilate for the body of Jesus. He was accompanied by Nicodemus, the man who earlier had visited Jesus at night. Nicodemus brought a mixture of myrrh and aloes, about seventy-five pounds.

Nicodemus steps into the light. He owns his allegiance to Jesus publicly, preparing His body for burial. The man who once hid in darkness has been born from above.

### The Testimony of John the Baptist

The passage concludes with John the Baptist's witness, which reinforces the central message:

> A person can receive only what is given them from heaven. You yourselves can testify that I said, I am not the Messiah but am sent ahead of him. The bride belongs to the bridegroom. The friend who attends the bridegroom waits and listens for him, and is full of joy when he hears the bridegroom's voice. That joy is mine, and it is now complete. He must become greater; I must become less.

John understands what Nicodemus is struggling to grasp: Jesus is not merely another teacher or even another messenger. He is the one who comes from above, who stands over all earthly things and speaks the very words of God. The Spirit is given to Him without limit. Everything has been placed in His hands.

> Whoever believes in the Son has eternal life, but whoever rejects the Son will not see life, for God's wrath remains on them.

## Application: Never Too Late to Be Born Again

Are you too old? Too stained by sin? Too far gone? The gospel declares otherwise. The Spirit moves freely, sovereignly, unpredictably. God's love embraces the entire world. The light has come.

Perhaps you were born into a Christian home and have drifted into spiritual complacency, simply going through the motions of faith. Has there been a moment when you truly decided to follow Jesus? Have you taken ownership of your faith, or are you still riding on your parents' belief?

Perhaps you come from a completely different background—no Christian home, no Christian country, no Christian foundation. It doesn't matter. Being born again is not about your family tree or your cultural heritage. It transcends race and nationality. If you can look to Jesus, you can live.

Perhaps you feel weighed down by old habits, convinced you're too stuck in your ways to change. But the same Spirit that raised dry bones to life can resurrect your soul. The death of Christ cleanses us completely. If 40 can be the new 30, surely we can be born again at any age.

And perhaps you, like Nicodemus, have been hiding in the darkness, unwilling to let your deeds be exposed. But there is freedom in stepping into the light. Own your faith. Come openly before Jesus. Be born from above. Let the Spirit of God give you new life—not as an accessory to your existing life, but as a complete spiritual transformation that makes you a new creation in Christ.
