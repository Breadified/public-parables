# 1 John 2:7-29

**Title:** God's Love and Living in Truth  
**Date:** January 2003  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Perfect Love: God's Valentine

As Valentine's Day approaches, our culture celebrates romantic love. But today, I want to point you to the real source of love—God Himself.

### The Source of Love

> **1 John 4:7** Dear friends, let us love one another, for love comes from God. Everyone who loves has been born of God and knows God.

This is not strange or unnatural. Why? Because of what follows:

> **1 John 4:8** Whoever does not love does not know God, because God is love.

Love is not merely an attribute of God or a facade He wears. God *is* love. His very essence is love. If you could take a chisel and cut a piece of God away, or open His heart and dissect His deepest being, what would you find? Love. You cut God, and He bleeds love.

### The Prime Example: Christ's Sacrifice

How has God demonstrated this love to us?

> **1 John 4:9-10** This is how God showed his love among us: He sent his one and only Son into the world that we might live through him. This is love: not that we loved God, but that he loved us and sent his Son as an atoning sacrifice for our sins.

Two thousand years ago, a man walked up a little hill carrying a cross. The guards who surrounded Him placed Him on that cross, and there, hanging between earth and sky, He gave up His life so that those whom He loved—you and me—might be free. Free to love others.

An atoning sacrifice means a substitution, a swap. A man diving in front of a car to save his girlfriend becomes a hero. Jesus did this for us all, saving us from the wrath of God. What could be more perfect for Valentine's Day than someone who dies for another?

### You Become a Source of Love

> **1 John 4:11-12** Dear friends, since God so loved us, we also ought to love one another. No one has ever seen God; but if we love one another, God lives in us and his love is made complete in us.

Because God has loved us, we are called to love one another. This love takes many forms:

- **Family**: Be patient with your siblings and parents
- **Friends**: Show genuine care and support
- **Church community**: Love those gathered with you in worship
- **School**: Organizations like ISCF exist not just for games or food, but to provide a place where people come to love and be loved

But our love doesn't stop there. We extend it to:

- **Strangers**: Those we don't know
- **Sinners**: Those we might judge
- **The proud**: Those who seem self-sufficient
- **The poor**: Those in material need

Ultimately, we show love by telling people about God's love—the atoning sacrifice of Jesus. When we participate and commit ourselves to this mission, we become a source of love to our communities. We carry God's love into the world.

## Stick with Jesus in the Last Hour

As we look forward to what's coming, 1 John speaks to us about the "last hour" of this earth—a metaphorical term for the end times. This passage presents three realities we must understand.

### 1. The Anti-Christ

> **1 John 2:18** Dear children, this is the last hour; and as you have heard that the antichrist is coming, even now many antichrists have come. This is how we know it is the last hour.

When we hear "antichrist," we often imagine a singular, chaotic satanic figure. While a specific Antichrist is coming, John speaks here of "many antichrists"—a broader category of opposition to Christ.

> **1 John 2:19** They went out from us, but they did not really belong to us. For if they had belonged to us, they would have remained with us; but their going showed that none of them belonged to us.

By definition, an antichrist is anyone who is anti-Christ. Just as we might be anti-pollution, anti-discrimination, or anti-poverty, an antichrist is anyone who stands apart from Jesus—those who do not want to be identified with Him, those who do not belong to Him.

> **1 John 2:22-23** Who is the liar? It is the man who denies that Jesus is the Christ. Such a man is the antichrist—he denies the Father and the Son. No one who denies the Son has the Father; whoever acknowledges the Son has the Father also.

An antichrist denies Jesus as Christ—as Lord and King. This can happen actively through open rebellion against His rule, but more commonly it happens passively. We ignore the Son and Father's claims on our lives. We give silent treatments instead of love. We treat people poorly and ignore His teaching. In these quiet ways, we deny His lordship.

### 2. The Anointed Ones

On the other side stands those who belong to Jesus. They are called the anointed ones.

> **1 John 2:20-21** But you have an anointing from the Holy One, and all of you know the truth. I do not write to you because you do not know the truth, but because you do know it and because no lie comes from the truth.

In Scripture, anointing was reserved for kings, priests, and the Messiah—those set apart for God's purposes. John applies this term to ordinary believers. If you are Christian, you have been anointed. With what have you been anointed? With truth.

You know the truth. Truth has been poured over you. We live in an age that prefers to ignore truth, to indulge in delusions. Like the characters in *The Matrix* who prefer the comfortable illusion, many choose ignorance. Yet knowing the truth—even when painful—is far more valuable than pleasant deception.

### 3. Authentic Life: Remain in Him

> **1 John 2:26-27** I am writing these things to you about those who are trying to lead you astray. As for you, the anointing you received from him remains in you, and you do not need anyone to teach you. But as his anointing teaches you about all things and as that anointing is real, not counterfeit—just as it has taught you, remain in him.

Like a counterfeit Louis Vuitton that falls apart, versus a genuine bag with Egyptian cotton and correct stitching, there is a difference between what is authentic and what is fake. The anointing of the Holy Spirit is real, not counterfeit. It teaches you and remains in you.

> **1 John 2:23-25** No one who denies the Son has the Father; whoever acknowledges the Son has the Father also. See that what you have heard from the beginning remains in you. If it does, you also will remain in the Son and in the Father. And this is what he promised us—even eternal life.

The call is clear: remain in Him. Let what you have learned from the beginning stay with you.

> **1 John 2:28-29** And now, dear children, continue in him, so that when he appears we may be confident and unashamed before him at his coming. If you know that he is righteous, you know that everyone who does what is right has been born of him.

When Jesus returns, those who have continued in Him will stand confident and unashamed. Choose authenticity. Choose truth. Choose to remain in Jesus, for in Him alone is eternal life.
