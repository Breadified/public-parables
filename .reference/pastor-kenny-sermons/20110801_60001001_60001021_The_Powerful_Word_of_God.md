# 1 Peter 1:1-21

**Title:** The Powerful Word of God  
**Date:** 27 August 2011  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Powerful Word

Peter writes to persecuted Christians scattered throughout Asia Minor, reminding them of a foundational truth: the word of God is powerful. This power rests on two essential reasons found in verse 23.

> For you have been born again, not of perishable seed, but of imperishable, through the living and enduring word of God. (1 Peter 1:23)

### The Word Endures Forever

The first reason the word is powerful is that it is **enduring**. Unlike all earthly things, God's word lasts forever.

> All men are like grass, and all their glory is like the flowers of the field; the grass withers and the flowers fall, but the word of the Lord stands forever. (1 Peter 1:24-25)

Everything in creation withers and fades—our accomplishments, our possessions, our very lives. Yet God's word remains. This endurance means more than mere longevity; it means the word is eternally *relevant*. The wisdom it contains is not bound to a particular season or culture but speaks with power across all ages.

Consider how God's word sustained Israel throughout history. In creation, God spoke and the world came into being. To Abraham, He spoke new promises. Through Moses, He spoke deliverance: "Let my people go." These words were not merely words for their time—they were words spoken to *us*, preserved in Scripture and eternally applicable. The word is the foundation of both the past and the future.

This creates two critical challenges for us:

**First, cultural attacks on Scripture's authority.** We live in a culture that constantly pressures us to "move forward," suggesting that ancient biblical teaching is outdated or unclear. Some proclaim ignorance—"Who really knows what this passage means?"—while others selectively quote scholars, early church fathers, or contemporary research to suggest we should abandon traditional readings for new ones. In these moments of confusion, they claim special knowledge. But our response must be clear: if in doubt, stick to the conservative reading. Scripture must be interpreted by Scripture, not by external authorities.

**Second, the temptation to fill the gaps.** There are things God has genuinely not told us—whether we should change careers, accept a particular opportunity, or pursue a relationship. When facing these gaps, we're tempted to seek words of knowledge, visions, or prophetic insights. Some practice "treasure hunts"—small groups praying for guidance to approach strangers with supposedly Spirit-given revelations. While evangelism is vital, we must ask ourselves: are we adding to Scripture what God has not revealed?

The truth is simple: if God's word doesn't explicitly address a matter, we are either meant not to know, or meant to work it out ourselves through prayer, wisdom, and discernment. The word's enduring nature means it is always sufficient for what it addresses and honest about what it doesn't.

### The Word Brings Life

The second reason the word is powerful is that it **brings life**. This is the heart of the gospel.

> For you know that it was not with perishable things such as silver or gold that you were redeemed from the empty way of life handed down to you from your forefathers, but with the precious blood of Christ, a lamb without blemish or defect. (1 Peter 1:18-19)

The contrast is stark: perishable things versus imperishable life. An empty, inherited way of life versus the living power of the gospel. The word's power is not found in visions, mystical experiences, or filling our gaps with supernatural insights. Its power lies in what the word proclaims—the gospel of Jesus Christ.

Death is the ultimate perishable thing. Immortality is the ultimate power. Because Christ shed His blood for us, we are no longer held by death. We have been raised with Him.

> Praise be to the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ! In his great mercy he has given us new birth into a living hope through the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead, and into an inheritance that can never perish, spoil or fade—kept in heaven for you. (1 Peter 1:3-4)

The word that was preached to you—the gospel—is the very word that brought you to new birth. Because its source is the immortal, living Christ, everything that flows from it is also immortal. We are no longer in death's grip. We are resurrection people, shielded by God's power and kept by His grace.

**Application:** If you feel lacking in spiritual understanding, don't seek words of knowledge. Instead, ask God for better comprehension skills to grasp what's already revealed in Scripture. Ask for a humbler heart that isn't tempted to manipulate the truth to justify our own desires. Ask for a repentant spirit that recognizes our tendency toward spiritual pride. Ask for strength to apply what you already know, even when it's difficult.

Read Scripture carefully. Study it deeply. Let it encourage and equip you. The word of God is like oxygen—essential for life itself. We don't need it only when we're in crisis; we need it to sustain us continuously. Carry it with you. Meditate on it. Let others know where to find it and help them encounter it. In a world drowning in information, the word of God is the spiritual nourishment that actually gives life.

## Transformed by the Powerful Word

The word doesn't remain abstract doctrine—it transforms lives. Scripture shows us two profound effects of encountering God's word:

**Sincere love for one another.** When we're truly transformed by the truth, we naturally move toward genuine, heartfelt love for our brothers and sisters in Christ.

> Now that you have purified yourselves by obeying the truth so that you have sincere love for your brothers, love one another deeply, from the heart. (1 Peter 1:22)

**Spiritual growth and maturity.** Those transformed by the word shed malice, deceit, hypocrisy, envy, and slander—the spiritual toxins of our old way of life.

> Therefore, rid yourselves of all malice and all deceit, hypocrisy, envy, and slander of every kind. Like newborn babies, crave pure spiritual milk, so that by it you may grow up in your salvation, now that you have tasted that the Lord is good. (1 Peter 2:1-3)

This transformation extends to how we live. We live as strangers here, with reverent fear, knowing that our Father judges impartially. Our good works become a witness to those around us. The word transforms not just our inner lives but our entire way of being in the world.

You have been transformed by the word. And you have been transformed by people who themselves have been transformed by the word. This is the gospel's power working generationally through time.

## Speaking the Powerful Word

If the word is truly powerful and truly life-giving, then we have both a privilege and an obligation to speak it.

> If anyone speaks, he should do it as one speaking the very words of God. If anyone serves, he should do it with the strength God provides, so that in all things God may be praised through Jesus Christ. (1 Peter 4:11)

God chose to reveal Himself first through His word, then through His flesh in Jesus Christ, and then again through His word proclaimed by His people. We are the vessels through which this message continues. Yet many of us hesitate. We wait for the right moment that never comes. We tell ourselves we don't want to be pushy. We wait for a crisis, for a crisis, for a relationship to develop first. Sometimes patience is wisdom, but often patience is simply fear masquerading as prudence.

When you're genuinely passionate about something, you can't help but speak about it. When you truly care about others, you tell them what you believe they need to hear for their own good. When you have news that could save lives, you're compelled to share it. When you believe in your message, you don't obsess over the response you might receive—you simply tell anyone who will listen.

Shouldn't this be exactly our posture toward faith in Jesus Christ? The gospel is life. It is the message that transforms everything. Prepare your mind for action. Don't conform to the desires of ignorance. Be holy, for He who called you is holy. And speak—naturally, passionately, fearlessly—the word that alone gives life.
