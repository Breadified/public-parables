# Ephesians 6:10-24

**Title:** Spiritual Warfare and Peace
**Date:** 7 June 2009
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction: Understanding Spiritual Warfare

We arrive at the climax of Ephesians after a lengthy journey through Scripture. The passage before us is commonly titled "Spiritual Warfare," and the question naturally arises: Is there actually a war taking place?

Our culture certainly suggests it. Popular media is saturated with spiritual conflict—from television series like *Buffy the Vampire Slayer* and *Supernatural* to blockbuster thrillers and best-selling novels. Books on demons and deliverance ministries proliferate, and many speak confidently about territorial spirits and exorcism techniques.

This invisible warfare can feel genuinely frightening—as unsettling as a virus we cannot see attacking us and everything we hold dear. We sense something is happening beyond what we can observe, and that unknowing is deeply unsettling.

To this pervasive anxiety, Paul offers a simple but profound answer: **Be confident.**

## Be Strong in the Lord (v. 10)

> Finally, be strong in the Lord and in his mighty power.

To be strong means to trust God—to anchor your confidence in two fundamental truths: (1) He is Lord, and (2) He possesses incomparable power. Paul then instructs us in three practical ways to live out this strength.

## 1. Put on the Full Armor of God (vv. 11-16)

> Put on the full armor of God so that you can take your stand against the devil's schemes.

### Understanding the Armor: Putting on Christ the Divine Warrior

Paul is not commanding us to don literal clothing or magical armor from fantasy stories. Rather, he draws from Old Testament prophecies describing the Messiah—the Divine Warrior who will bring righteousness and defeat evil. Centuries before Roman centurions guarded Paul's prison cell, Isaiah foresaw the armor of the coming Savior:

- **Belt of truth:** "Righteousness will be his belt and faithfulness the sash around his waist" (Isaiah 11:5)
- **Breastplate of righteousness:** "He put on righteousness as his breastplate" (Isaiah 59:17)
- **Sandals of peace:** "How beautiful on the mountains are the feet of those who bring good news, who proclaim peace" (Isaiah 52:7)
- **Shield of faith:** "I am your shield, your very great reward" (Genesis 15:1)
- **Helmet of salvation:** "He puts on the helmet of salvation on his head" (Isaiah 59:17)
- **Sword of the Spirit:** "He will strike the earth with the rod of his mouth; with the breath of his lips he will slay the wicked" (Isaiah 11:4)

Paul's instruction is beautifully simple: **Put on Jesus.** This is another way of saying: **Be Christian.**

### The Armor as the Theme of Ephesians

This is not surprising when you understand Ephesians as a whole. Chapters 1-3 describe everything Christ has accomplished for us through His death and resurrection. Chapters 4-6 call us to respond by walking as Christ did, putting on His character in every aspect of life.

Throughout Ephesians, these armor metaphors reappear:

- **Belt of truth:** The gospel comes through hearing the word of truth; we speak truthfully to our neighbors (1:13; 4:15, 25)
- **Breastplate of righteousness:** The new self is created righteous; we put it on (4:24)
- **Feet fitted with the gospel of peace:** Christ is our peace with the Father and each other; we maintain unity through peace (2:14; 4:3)
- **Shield of faith:** We have received knowledge and believe in Christ for salvation (4:13; 1:13)
- **Helmet of salvation:** We are saved by grace and through faith in Christ (2:5; 1:13)
- **Sword of the Spirit/Word:** The word brings salvation; we build one another up through Scripture (1:13; 5:18-19)

To be strong = to be Christian. If you are Christian, you are strong.

## 2. Stand Firm Against the Schemes of the Devil (v. 14)

> Stand firm then, with the belt of truth buckled around your waist, with the breastplate of righteousness in place, and with your feet fitted with the readiness that comes from the gospel of peace. In addition to all this, take up the shield of faith, with which you can extinguish all the flaming arrows of the evil one.

This is the reason we put on Christ: to stand firm against Satan's schemes. We must understand what we're actually standing against:

> Our struggle is not against flesh and blood, but against the rulers, against the authorities, against the powers of this dark world and against the spiritual forces of evil in the heavenly realms.

### A Critical Balance: Two Extremes to Avoid

**Non-Christians give too little attention to the devil.** They fail to recognize that this world is more than meets the eye. Evil is not merely what appears in newspapers and nightly news broadcasts. As Paul writes:

> You were dead in your transgressions and sins, in which you used to live when you followed the ways of this world and of the ruler of the kingdom of the air, the spirit who is now at work in those who are disobedient.

If you are not a Christian, you belong to the prince of the air—you are enslaved to sin and under the devil's dominion. The call is urgent: Wake up. Pay attention. Escape this kingdom. Become Christian.

**But Christians often give the devil too much attention.** Many contemporary believers have adopted unbiblical practices and beliefs:

- Praying to cleanse neighborhoods and highways of demons
- Attributing depression, illness, and epilepsy to demon possession
- Claiming that Christians can be demonized and require repeated "cleansings"
- Employing elaborate exorcism protocols with named demons and mapped authority structures

These beliefs deserve careful scrutiny. Where exactly in Scripture do we find highways and suburbs under demonic territorial control? The Greek word for "exorcist" appears only once in Scripture—in Acts 19:13-16, where the seven sons of Sceva attempt unsuccessfully to cast out demons in a complete and utter failure. Jesus never performed exorcisms as elaborate rituals or with special words. Remarkably, the rise in modern exorcism and deliverance ministries traces back to 1973—the year *The Exorcist* film was released. Hollywood, ignorance, and tradition have created a misconception.

Christianity fundamentally opposes superstition, not promotes it. We drove out magical thinking from the ancient world, not back into it. Moreover, we do not fight the devil directly. Jesus does not duel with Satan; He has already defeated him. Our struggle is against sin itself—the schemes and forces through which evil operates in this world—not against the devil as a combatant we must face.

### The Victory Is Already Won

The fundamental truth: **The war is over. God has won.** All things have been reconciled to Him. There is no ongoing spiritual warfare for believers—there is spiritual peace.

What power remains to the devil? Only accusation. He is "the accuser" (Greek *diabolos*; Hebrew *Satan*). When we put on Christ's armor, these accusations cannot touch us. Paul writes:

> No, in all these things we are more than conquerors through him who loved us. For I am convinced that neither death nor life, neither angels nor demons, neither the present nor the future, nor any powers, neither height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God that is in Christ Jesus our Lord.

Do not give the devil a foothold through sin:

> Each of you must put off falsehood and speak truthfully to your neighbor. In your anger do not sin: Do not let the sun go down while you are still angry, and do not give the devil a foothold.

Christians are already strong. They can stand. **Stick with Christ. Ignore the devil.** Do not fall into superstition—ignore the heckler demanding attention, just as you ignore foolish advertising.

### Focus on Sin, Not Superstition

Read "spiritual warfare" in Ephesians not as conflict with demons but as ethical struggle against sin. The context is ordinary behavior issues—immorality, anger, falsehood. Our struggle is against sin and its schemes, not against demons summoned by superstition. Do not give the devil attention through superstition. Give sin much attention—your anger, your words, your choices.

## 3. Receive Salvation with a Prayerful Attitude (vv. 17-18)

> Take the helmet of salvation and the sword of the Spirit, which is the word of God. And pray in the Spirit on all occasions with all kinds of prayers and requests. With this in mind, be alert and always keep on praying for all the saints.

Take the helmet of salvation—truly receive it, not as something earned but as a gift. Take the sword of the Spirit—embrace God's word. Believe God when He says His Son has given His life for you. Do not take this for granted. Actively receive it.

Notice the common thread running through all three commands: Put on Christ. Stand firm in Christ. Take salvation. **They are all expressions of the same call: Be Christian.**

Be Christian, and you will be immune from the devil's accusations. Be Christian, and you will be free from the powers of darkness. Be Christian, and you will stand when the day of evil comes.

## The Power Available to You

Consider the incomparable power available to those who believe:

> I pray that the eyes of your heart may be enlightened in order that you may know the hope to which he has called you, the riches of his glorious inheritance in the saints, and his incomparably great power for us who believe. That power is like the working of his mighty strength, which he exerted in Christ when he raised him from the dead and seated him at his right hand in the heavenly realms, far above all rule and authority, power and dominion, and every title that can be given, not only in the present age but also in the one to come. And God placed all things under his feet and appointed him to be head over everything for the church.

If you are Christian, you have been resurrected from death—away from the power of the devil. Nothing can harm you spiritually anymore. Put away silly superstitions. Be Christian.

## Conclusion: Spiritual Peace

> Finally, be strong in the Lord and in his mighty power. Put on the full armor of God so that you can take your stand against the devil's schemes.

There is no warfare. The war is over. God has won. What remains is not conflict but **spiritual peace between us and God**—the deep assurance that we are loved, protected, and eternally secure in Christ.
