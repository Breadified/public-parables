# Romans 12:16-13:14

**Title:** Serve the Village  
**Date:** 10 March 2013  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction

Last time we looked at Romans 12:1, where Paul tells us that in light of God's mercy to us, we should offer our bodies as living sacrifices. But what does that sacrifice actually look like?

Over the next three talks, we'll explore this question by examining three dimensions of sacrifice:
1. Serve the village
2. Serve the weak
3. Serve the world

## Living with Our Neighbors

### Harmony Over Conflict

One Saturday evening, my neighbor's seven-year-old daughter was having a birthday party. There were cars honking, guests arriving, music blaring—all the joyful chaos of a children's celebration. It's hard to be annoyed at that kind of noise.

But I've also experienced the other side: teenage kids making trouble late at night, with alcohol and noise at midnight. Whether it's an innocent celebration or genuine disruption, the question becomes: how do we live with our neighbors in a way that honors God?

Scripture addresses this directly:

> **Romans 12:16** Live in harmony with one another. Do not be proud, but be willing to associate with people of low position. Do not be conceited.

> **Romans 12:18** If it is possible, as far as it depends on you, live at peace with everyone.

This applies to everyone—those of high or low position, rich or poor, married or single, regardless of their background. But this teaching comes with a crucial caveat:

> **Romans 12:17** Do not repay anyone evil for evil. Be careful to do what is right in the eyes of everybody.

### Rejecting Revenge

This echoes an earlier command in the same passage:

> **Romans 12:14** Bless those who persecute you; bless and do not curse.

This is a radical thought. Most civilizations teach us to take revenge, especially when our families are threatened. Even the Old Testament reflects this principle:

> **Genesis 12:3** I will bless those who bless you, and whoever curses you I will curse.

> **Exodus 21:23-24** But if there is serious injury, you are to take life for life, eye for eye, tooth for tooth, hand for hand, foot for foot.

But Jesus inaugurated a new age. In the New Testament, He teaches:

> **Matthew 5:38-39** "You have heard that it was said, 'Eye for eye, and tooth for tooth.' But I tell you, Do not resist an evil person. If someone strikes you on the right cheek, turn to him the other also."

Why this shift? We now live in the age of forgiveness and tolerance. This is not the final age of judgment; God reserves justice for Himself:

> **Romans 12:19** Do not take revenge, my friends, but leave room for God's wrath, for it is written: "It is mine to avenge; I will repay," says the Lord.

On the last judgment day, God will avenge all wrongdoing. But for now, we are called to live in peace—even with our enemies:

> **Romans 12:20-21** On the contrary: "If your enemy is hungry, feed him; if he is thirsty, give him something to drink. In doing this, you will heap burning coals on his head." Do not be overcome by evil, but overcome evil with good.

God's will is for a peaceful village and a peaceful neighborhood. This is more fundamental to Christian living than any other skill we might develop.

### The Priority of Character Over Achievement

We often think success means earning lots of money or being brilliant academically. But God would rather we be good citizens—people who know how to live together. What do we really want our children to excel at?

God never instructed us to be good at mathematics or genius at science. He has instructed us to live with one another. What's the point of being smart if we're rude? What's the point of studying hard if we don't know basic manners? Can we say thank you when someone gives us a ride? Can we say sorry when we're late? Can we explain ourselves when we break a promise? These are the life skills that matter.

There's no point in being a piano prodigy if we don't know how to serve our community.

### Practical Steps for Better Neighborliness

Living well with our neighbors requires small acts of tolerance and generosity—what I call "give and take." Often it will feel like we're giving more than taking:

- Take out the trash for neighbors who are away
- Help return shopping trolleys left in front of apartments
- Help with the local school canteen, especially when non-Christian parents are busy with their careers
- At work, resist selfish ambition and office politics; instead, live in harmony
- At church, be patient with each other. We interact in limited time and make mistakes—let's extend grace rather than take offense

## Living with the State

### God Establishes All Authority

Not only must we live well with each other; we must also live peacefully with our authorities:

> **Romans 13:1** Everyone must submit himself to the governing authorities.

Paul gives two reasons for this submission. The first is conscience—an awareness of what God wants, learned through years of studying Scripture. And what does God tell us?

> **Romans 13:1** Let everyone be subject to the governing authorities, for there is no authority except that which God has established.

This is repeated with emphasis throughout the passage:

> **Romans 13:1-2** The authorities that exist have been established by God. Consequently, he who rebels against the authority is rebelling against what God has instituted, and those who do so will bring judgment on themselves.

God has established good governments and bad governments. Whether Labour or Liberal, elected or dictatorial, hereditary monarchy or democratic republic—God is ultimately behind all legitimate authority. Even in a democracy where we choose our leaders, God remains sovereign. Even Nazi governments existed under God's ultimate authority, though this doesn't mean God approved of their actions.

The government is God's instrument. It is God's servant.

> **Romans 13:4** For he is God's servant to do you good. But if you do wrong, be afraid, for he does not bear the sword for nothing. He is God's servant, an agent of wrath to bring punishment on the wrongdoer.

When we obey authorities, we obey them as we would obey God. To rebel against government is not merely to rebel against an ordinary institution—it is to disobey God directly:

> **Romans 13:2** Consequently, he who rebels against the authority is rebelling against what God has instituted, and those who do so will bring judgment on themselves.

This has practical implications. A parking fine is against God. A speeding ticket is against God. Cheating the government system is cheating against God.

### Fear of Punishment as Restraint

The second reason for obeying authorities is the practical fear of punishment:

> **Romans 13:3** For rulers hold no terror for those who do right, but for those who do wrong. Do you want to be free from fear of the one in authority? Then do what is right and he will commend you.

Government exists for our good and society's good. Its primary function is to restrain sin. Yes, government itself is imperfect and sometimes sinful, but even imperfect government restrains sin better than anarchy. By definition, government creates order.

Consider the reality: there is no other earthly source of authority. God is not going to bring thunder and lightning to catch thieves. In this present age, the government is our only resort to justice. If we start undermining the government's right to govern, we're simply removing justice from the world. If we dilute it or subvert it, we're left with nothing—perpetual coups, like we've seen in some Pacific islands, Egypt, or Thailand.

### Supporting Government Through Taxes

Our village needs governance and authority. We should support it:

> **Romans 13:6** This is also why you pay taxes, for the authorities are God's servants, who give their full time to governing.

Supporting the government happens concretely through paying taxes. Often we ask, "What do I get out of taxes?" But that's the wrong question. Taxes aren't for you—they're for the government to govern.

This means:
- Declare your correct income and deductions
- Don't cheat on GST. I know mechanics and tradespeople often offer a 10% discount for cash—it seems like a win for both of us, but the government loses, and ultimately, God loses
- We may criticize public servants as overpaid or inefficient, but the solution lies within the system: become a public servant yourself, enter parliament, lobby for change. The solution is not to undermine the government. It's hypocritical to critique the quality of public servants while sending our brightest children away from the public sector to become lawyers and doctors instead.

Even if we live under an anti-Christian government, this is not an excuse for us to sin. Rather, we are called to change ourselves.

## The Call to Renewed Living

> **Romans 13:11-14** And do this, understanding the present time. The hour has come for you to wake up from your slumber, because our salvation is nearer now than when we first believed. The night is nearly over; the day is almost here. So let us put aside the deeds of darkness and put on the armor of light. Let us behave decently, as in the daytime, not in orgies and drunkenness, not in sexual immorality and debauchery, not in dissension and jealousy. Rather, clothe yourselves with the Lord Jesus Christ, and do not think about how to gratify the desires of the sinful nature.

Our calling is clear:
- Follow the law
- Do not take revenge
- Live in peace
- Put on the character of Christ
