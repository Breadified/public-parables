# John 13:36-14:23

**Title:** You Know the Way

**Date:** April 16, 2023 (ARISE) / March 13, 2015 (SMAC)

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## Going Away

We find ourselves in the Farewell Discourse—Jesus' final teaching with his disciples on the night before his crucifixion. He announces that he is going away, and naturally, the disciples are anxious. When someone close to us departs, insecurity rises. We worry: Where are they going? How will we manage without them?

Jesus' disciples felt this acutely. He tells them:

> "My children, I will be with you only a little longer. You will look for me, and just as I told the Jews, so I tell you now: Where I am going, you cannot come." (John 13:33)

Peter, characteristically direct, asks the obvious question:

> "Lord, where are you going?" (John 13:36)

> Jesus replies, "Where I am going, you cannot follow now, but you will follow later." (John 13:36)

Then Peter presses further:

> "Lord, why can't I follow you now? I will lay down my life for you." (John 13:37)

You can hear the concern in his voice—almost a plea.

---

## Section 1: Where Are You Going? (13:33-14:4)

Jesus' response is not a rebuke but reassurance:

> "Do not let your hearts be troubled. You believe in God; believe also in me. My Father's house has many rooms; if that were not so, would I have told you that I am going there to prepare a place for you?" (John 14:1-2)

Jesus is not abandoning them. He has a purpose for going away. He is preparing a place for them—just as he sent the disciples ahead to prepare the upper room for the Passover meal, so now he is going to make ready a dwelling for the disciples.

### Understanding "My Father's House"

The phrase "Father's house" appears elsewhere in John's Gospel. Three Passovers earlier, Jesus cleansed the temple:

> "In the temple courts he found people selling cattle, sheep and doves, and others sitting at tables exchanging money. So he made a whip out of cords, and drove all from the temple courts, both sheep and cattle; he scattered the coins of the money changers and overturned their tables. To those who sold doves he said, 'Get these out of here! Stop turning my Father's house into a market!'" (John 2:14-16)

Here, "my Father's house" refers to the temple of Jerusalem—the place where God dwells, the house of God the Father.

But immediately after this cleansing, Jesus says something mysterious:

> "Destroy this temple, and I will raise it again in three days." The Jews replied, "It has taken forty-six years to build this temple, and you are going to raise it in three days?" But the temple he had spoken of was his body. After he was raised from the dead, his disciples recalled what he had said. Then they believed the scripture and the words that Jesus had spoken. (John 2:19-22)

**The true temple is Jesus' own body.** This is the "Father's house"—Jesus himself, upon whom God's Spirit dwells.

### What Jesus Meant by "Preparing a Place"

When Jesus says he is going to prepare "the Father's house," he means he is preparing his own body. He is preparing himself for death. That very night, Jesus will be betrayed, arrested, and killed. He is truly going away.

This also explains why Peter "cannot follow" Jesus now. The sacrifice of atonement is something only Jesus can accomplish. Peter, despite his brave words, cannot lay down his life for the sins of many. No human can pay that price—only Jesus, the spotless Lamb of God, can atone for the world's sins.

After that sacrifice, Jesus will return for his disciples:

> "And if I go and prepare a place for you, I will come back and take you to be with me that you also may be where I am." (John 14:3)

Notice what matters here: the goal is not a distant location, but **being with Jesus himself**. "Where I am, there you will be also." The promise is intimacy with Christ, not just a geographical destination.

The word "place" (Greek *topos*) can mean position as well as location. Christ was preparing a **position** for each disciple—a place of belonging in his body, a status of acceptance before the Father.

## Section 2: How Do We Know the Way? (14:5-21)

Thomas expresses the disciples' confusion:

> "Lord, we don't know where you are going, so how can we know the way?" (John 14:5)

If the destination is Jesus himself, then the way to it must also be Jesus:

> "Jesus answered, 'I am the way and the truth and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me.'" (John 14:6)

### The Way Back to Chapter 1

This echoes John the Baptist's words from chapter 1:

> "John replied in the words of Isaiah the prophet, 'I am the voice of one calling in the desert, Make straight the way for the Lord.'" (John 1:23)

John was preparing the way for God's arrival. And who appears on that road? Jesus. For though the Father and Son are two distinct persons, they are one in purpose, character, and nature.

### Three Ways Jesus and the Father Are One

**a) To know Jesus is to know God:**

> "If you really know me, you will know my Father as well. From now on, you do know him and have seen him." (John 14:7)

**b) To see Jesus is to see God:**

> "Philip said, 'Lord, show us the Father and that will be enough for us.' Jesus answered, 'Don't you know me, Philip, even after I have been among you such a long time? Anyone who has seen me has seen the Father. How can you say, Show us the Father?'" (John 14:8-9)

**c) To believe Jesus is to believe God:**

> "Don't you believe that I am in the Father, and that the Father is in me? The words I say to you I do not speak on my own authority. Rather, it is the Father, living in me, who is doing his work. Believe me when I say that I am in the Father and the Father is in me; or at least believe on the evidence of the works themselves." (John 14:10-11)

Know, see, believe—these are the same thing in John's theology.

### The Intimacy Between Father and Son

Their relationship is marked by the language of mutual indwelling:

> "I am in the Father, the Father is in me." (John 14:10-11)

Jesus doesn't need to show the Father because the disciples have already seen Jesus. Jesus is the incarnation of God. The disciples have walked with him, touched him, known him intimately. And if Jesus knows God intimately, then those who know Jesus also know God.

Just as Jesus exclusively atones for sins, Jesus exclusively gives access to God the Father. This exclusive access is the gift Jesus offers.

**Illustration:** One of the most exclusive places in the world is the Coca-Cola vault in Atlanta, which holds the secret recipe—protected for over 130 years. Only two people know it, and they cannot travel together. Imagine if they allowed you into that vault and revealed the secret to you. That is what Jesus offers—exclusive access to the knowledge of God.

### The Benefit of Being a Christian

Through the Spirit, believers receive the same intimacy and exclusive access to God the Father that the Son possesses. This is the remarkable promise of verses 12-21:

> "Very truly I tell you, whoever believes in me will do the works I have been doing, and they will do even greater things than these, because I am going to the Father. And I will do whatever you ask in my name, so that the Father may be glorified in the Son. You may ask me for anything in my name, and I will do it." (John 14:12-14)

Just as the Father answers the Son, the Son will answer the prayers of those who believe. The same intimacy mirrored downward:

- The disciples will do greater works (v. 12)
- The Son will answer their prayers (v. 13)
- They will live because Jesus lives (v. 19)
- The mutual indwelling expands: "I am in the Father, you are in me, and I am in you" (v. 20)
- Those who love Jesus will be loved by the Father and by Jesus himself (v. 21)

### The Role of the Spirit

This intimacy becomes possible through the Spirit:

> "And I will ask the Father, and he will give you another advocate to help you and be with you forever—the Spirit of truth. The world cannot accept him, because it neither sees him nor knows him. But you know him, for he lives with you and will be in you." (John 14:16-17)

The word "advocate" (*paraclete*) means one who comes alongside to speak in your defense and provide counsel—a lawyer. Just as the Father sent the Spirit to Jesus, so Jesus sends the Spirit to the disciples.

> "I will not leave you as orphans; I will come to you." (John 14:18)

Through the Spirit, Jesus is present with his disciples always.

### Application

Many people believe in a god. But Jesus teaches that vague belief in "some higher power" is a twisted shadow of the truth. To really believe in God, we must know and believe in Jesus.

God has objectively revealed himself. There is no more speculation, guessing, or ignorance. There is no excuse. Believe in Jesus, and you are intimate with God the Father.

## Section 3: Why Not Show Yourself to the World? (14:22-23)

One final objection arises:

> "Then Judas (not Judas Iscariot) said, 'But, Lord, why do you intend to show yourself to us and not to the world?'" (John 14:22)

The logic seems obvious: If God is Jesus, and Jesus died for the world, shouldn't he reveal himself to everyone? Why restrict this intimacy to just the disciples?

Jesus' answer points to something profound:

> "Anyone who loves me will obey my teaching. My Father will love them, and we will come to them and make our home with them. Anyone who does not love me will not obey my teaching." (John 14:23-24)

### The Teaching: Love One Another

This recalls Jesus' original commandment from chapter 13:

> "A new command I give you: Love one another. As I have loved you, so you must love one another. By this everyone will know that you are my disciples, if you love one another." (John 13:34-35)

By the love of Christians for one another, the world will see Jesus. Love each other, and the world will see disciples. And seeing disciples, they will see Jesus.

**Illustration:** When a master craftsman trains an apprentice well, everyone appreciates the master through the quality of the apprentice's work.

### Application: Witnesses to the World

We are witnesses. Our task is to show Jesus to the world through how we love one another.

This means:
- **Put away envy and jealousy.** Stop comparing yourself with others. Put on generosity, kindness, and the desire for each other's good. Serve one another.
- **Put away apathy and self-centeredness.** Put on commitment to one another.
- **Put away thin-skinned oversensitivity.** Put on humility and willingness to be accountable.

As we live this way, alongside our words, we will tell others about this Way, the Truth, and the Life that leads to God.

The testimony of a loving Christian community speaks louder than any argument. One student newspaper editor, observing a Christian Union at her university, wrote:

> "They're really lovely and don't care who knows it... They do regular free lunches themed around philosophical discussion... Great job, CU!"

Through their love for one another, they won the ear of the irreligious. This is how Jesus shows himself to the world—through us.

---

## Summary

Jesus prepares a place for us through his sacrificial death. He is the way to God—the exclusive access point to intimacy with the Father. And through his Spirit, believers experience the same closeness to God that characterizes the Father-Son relationship.

But this gift comes with a calling: to witness to the world through our love for one another. In a fragmented age, a community that genuinely loves sacrificially becomes a living sign of Jesus' presence and power.

Where is your favorite place in the world? It should be wherever Jesus is—for in him, we find home.
