# John 2:1-4:54

**Title:** True Belief in Jesus  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## The Signs of the New Messianic Age

John presents five pivotal signs that reveal Jesus as the fulfilment of God's promises. Each sign points beyond itself to the arrival of salvation's age:

- **New Wine** (2:1-11) – The abundance of the kingdom has come
- **New Temple** (2:12-22, 4:15-24) – Jesus replaces the physical temple as God's dwelling place
- **New Birth** (3:1-21) – Spiritual regeneration through the Holy Spirit
- **New Baptiser** (3:22-30) – Jesus alone brings true cleansing and transformation
- **New Water** (4:1-14) – Living water that eternally satisfies

These signs announce one central truth: the age of salvation has arrived at last. The question John presses throughout is whether we truly believe this reality.

---

## What Does NOT Result in True Belief

### The Jews (2:23-25)

They witnessed Jesus's signs and were impressed. Yet John reveals the tragic gap between surface belief and genuine faith. They believed in the miracles but not in Jesus himself. They could recite what they saw but refused to surrender to who he claimed to be.

### Nicodemus (3:1-2, 3:11-12)

This learned Pharisee came to Jesus at night, acknowledging the signs with intellectual honesty: "Rabbi, we know that you are a teacher who has come from God; for no one can do these signs that you do apart from the presence of God." But intellectual recognition is not belief. 

Nicodemus struggled to understand spiritual rebirth because he approached it from a purely rational framework. True belief requires something more than mental assent—it demands transformation.

---

## What WILL Result in True Belief

### The Samaritan Woman (4:29, 4:39b)

This woman at the well represents the opposite trajectory. She encountered Jesus's words—his knowledge of her life, his revelation of himself as the Messiah. She didn't need to see a miracle. His words pierced through her defences and awakened genuine faith.

Notice how she moves from curiosity to conviction: "Come and see a man who told me everything I have ever done! He cannot be the Messiah, can he?" Yet her testimony drew others to belief.

### The Samaritans (4:41-42)

The woman's belief sparked a hunger in her entire community. They believed first through her word, then through Jesus's own words: "It is no longer because of what you said that we believe, for we have heard for ourselves, and we know that this is truly the Saviour of the world."

Their belief matured from secondhand testimony to personal encounter with Jesus's teaching.

### You (3:14-18a, 3:21, 3:31-36, 4:43-53)

The pattern is clear throughout these chapters: true belief comes through Jesus's words and through personal encounter with him. It is not manufactured by spectacular signs alone, but by the inward work of the Spirit as we hear and respond to his truth.

---

## The Central Question

John writes these signs so that you might believe that Jesus is the Messiah, the Son of God, and that believing you might have life in his name. But belief itself becomes the question: Is your faith rooted in what Jesus *does*, or in who Jesus *is*? 

The miracles point beyond themselves. They are meant to awaken us to the person they reveal. Many see the signs and miss the Saviour. Others hear his words and find him.

Which kind of believer will you be?
