# 1 John 2:1-46

**Title:** The True King vs. The Pretend King (Christmas Message)
**Date:** December 12, 2009
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Setting: Two Kings in One City

When Jesus was born, He came into a world with competing claims to authority. Herod ruled as king in Jerusalem, yet he was not truly from the royal family—he was a pretend king, a usurper who held power through political maneuvering rather than rightful claim. Into this fractured kingdom came news of a real king, a true king born in Bethlehem.

## Wise Men Seeking the True King

From distant lands, wise men arrived in Jerusalem searching for this newborn king. They had seen His star and recognized something the world had missed: that a genuine king had entered human history. When they asked Herod about this new king, they spoke with spiritual intuition. They were looking for someone worth worshiping—not just another ruler, but a true king whose authority came from heaven itself.

Herod's reaction revealed everything about his heart. The thought of another king terrified him. He could not bear the idea of sharing power, of submitting to someone else's rule. His throne, his crown, his authority—these were his gods. So he devised a deception: he would pretend to join the wise men in worship while secretly plotting to destroy this threat to his power.

## God Protects the True King

But God intervened through dreams. The wise men were warned not to return to Herod. Joseph was instructed to flee to Egypt with the Christ child. While Herod's paranoia drove him to massacre the innocent children of Bethlehem—a horrific attempt to eliminate the true king through brute force—Jesus was safe in God's protective care.

The contrast could not be sharper. Herod's desperation revealed the fragility of false authority. He could kill innocent children, but he could not touch the one he truly feared. His power was ultimately powerless against God's plan.

## The Unfolding Story: Death and Resurrection

As time passed, Herod aged and died—his pretend kingship ended as all earthly powers must. But Jesus, the true king, lived on. He grew into manhood and began His ministry. Yet the world's resistance to His kingship did not end with Herod. When Jesus came as an adult, people still did not want to acknowledge Him as king. They rejected His authority, and they killed Him.

But here lies the heart of the gospel: Jesus is God's king. Death could not hold Him. God raised Him from the dead, exalting Him as king over all creation. Every attempted assassination of His kingship—whether by Herod's sword, by the religious establishment's condemnation, or by death itself—ultimately failed. Jesus reigns.

## The Choice Before Us

This Christmas message carries an urgent spiritual question for every heart: **Who will you choose to follow?**

Will you be like Herod—fighting to maintain control of your own life, refusing to submit to anyone else's rule, treating your desires and ambitions as your true king? This is the path of the pretend king, the one who grasps for power and fears losing it.

Or will you be like the wise men—recognizing Jesus as the true king and bowing before Him in worship? This is the path of those who understand that real freedom comes not from self-rule, but from submission to the one true King who actually deserves our allegiance.

The choice shapes everything. Herod's rebellion against Jesus' kingship led only to desperation, destruction, and death. But those who recognize and honor Jesus as king find themselves under the care of the one who conquered death itself.

This season, as we remember Christ's birth, we are invited once again to make our choice: Will we resist like Herod, or will we worship like the wise men?
