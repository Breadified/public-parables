# 2 Peter 3:3-13

**Title:** 720 Hours: Living with Urgency in God's Timing
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction: Mistaken Perceptions of Time

We often deceive ourselves about how much time we actually have. There's a humorous story about Mrs. Casey describing her brother-in-law Pat's condition: despite having four different doctors each give him three months to live, she insists "he's good for a long time—he's got a year!" 

This captures something true about human nature. Year 12 students especially feel this acutely. You think you have time to study, then suddenly realize you have only 720 hours left—30 days. It feels shocking, yet that careless voice in your head whispers, "Two hours this afternoon won't really affect the outcome anyway."

The reality is this: **we can be profoundly mistaken about the time we actually have.**

## Section 1: Time Feels Long (Verse 4)

Peter addresses those who mock the promise of Christ's return. Listen to their argument:

> They will say, "Where is this 'coming' he promised? Ever since our fathers died, everything goes on as it has since the beginning of creation." (2 Peter 3:4)

The scoffers make a compelling case: our ancestors died, yet nothing changed. They point to two thousand years since Peter wrote these words, and the world still stands. The earth hasn't ended. It's like waiting for a political promise—we've been waiting thirty years for change that never comes.

Historically, they're not wrong. Years have accumulated. Generations have passed. The world continues.

This perception of long time is especially pronounced in modern Australia. Our life expectancy is 81.3 years (78 for men, 83 for women)—higher than most of the world. Japan averages 82.6, Hong Kong 82.2, while parts of Africa see only 40 years. With those extra decades, we compress enormous experiences into a single lifetime:

- By age 12, you've learned multiple musical instruments, accumulated tutoring hours, played countless games, collected hundreds of Facebook friends
- By 25, you've earned two degrees and held multiple jobs
- By 30, most Australians have traveled to Europe three times—after school, after university, on a honeymoon

All of this combines to reinforce a deep conviction: **time feels very long.**

## Section 2: Time Is Actually Short (Verse 8)

But like the Year 12 students feeling the HSC countdown right now, the truth is far different. **Time is actually short.**

Peter shifts our perspective with a single verse:

> But do not forget this one thing, dear friends: With the Lord a day is like a thousand years, and a thousand years are like a day. (2 Peter 3:8)

God's conversion rate is staggering. In God's perception, two thousand years is merely a blink—like two days in the lifetime of eternity. To the God who is eternal, this supposed "long time" is nothing. It's a stark reorientation that should completely reshape our perception of time.

But there's more to the shortness of time than God's eternal perspective. Peter reminds us:

> By God's word the heavens existed and the earth was formed out of water and by water. By these waters also the world of that time was deluged and destroyed. By the same word the present heavens and earth are reserved for fire, being kept for the day of judgment and destruction of ungodly men. (2 Peter 3:5-7)

The world was created by a word. Just as easily, by a word it can return to the formless emptiness of Genesis 1:2—darkness covering the deep, the Spirit hovering over the waters. God has done this before. In Noah's time, while scoffers mocked, judgment came suddenly.

The same timetable applies now. Our fate is spelled out. Reserved. Kept for fire because of sin. There is no escaping this destiny.

Here's the sobering reality: **we are not merely living out our days in comfortable Australia. We are like soldiers deployed to a high-risk zone.** Yes, life expectancy is eighty years, but that statistical comfort means nothing when you're in a war zone. The risk of sudden end is high. Despite appearances of safety and order, time is short. You write every letter like it's your last.

When will it come? Peter is clear:

> The day of the Lord will come like a thief. The heavens will disappear with a roar; the elements will be destroyed by fire, and the earth and everything in it will be laid bare. (2 Peter 3:10)

One moment, we're ordinary. The next moment—by a word, by a snap of God's fingers—the world ends.

## Section 3: Now Is the Time of Salvation (Verse 9)

> The Lord is not slow in keeping his promise, as some understand slowness. He is patient with you, not wanting anyone to perish, but everyone to come to repentance. (2 Peter 3:9)

Yes, judgment is coming. That judgment will be complete. That judgment will be final. But God says, "Wait just a moment."

He sent Jesus two thousand years ago to rescue his people. Jesus took all our wickedness, all our sin, and paid for it through his death on the cross. For two thousand years, he's been persuading us to follow him. He's patient—just one more soul, just a few more—waiting for the last person he has elected to finally walk through the doors of faith.

**For Those Not Yet Christian:**

If you're not yet a Christian, welcome. You've come to the right place. As you think about the HSC testing ahead, keep an eye on a far greater testing—the last judgment. As you count down the days to your exams, count down also the last days of this earth.

If you haven't repented, now is the time. Come visit our church and hear more about this Jesus who judges. If the HSC timeline makes it impossible right now, make it a priority afterward.

**For Those Already Christian:**

Peter asks those already saved, those who know where they're going:

> Since everything will be destroyed in this way, what kind of people ought you to be? You ought to live holy and godly lives as you look forward to the day of God and speed its coming. (2 Peter 3:11-12)

What should we do in this time? Several things:

- **Think of the bigger picture.** Study, yes, but remember: this earth is temporary. 
- **Pray for judgment.** Even Jesus taught us to pray, "Your kingdom come" (Lord's Prayer).
- **Tell someone about Jesus.** Time is short—not for the HSC, but for salvation.
- **Keep coming to church.** Stay rooted in community and truth.
- **Speed the day's coming.** Live with such holiness and urgency that you're actually cooperating with God's plan to bring his kingdom.

The countdown has begun. The question is: what will you do with the time you have?
