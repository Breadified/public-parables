# John 15:1-17

**Title:** Remain in Jesus

## The Vine

> "I am the true vine, and my Father is the gardener." (John 15:1)

Jesus uses an agricultural metaphor to describe the relationship between the Father, the Son, and us. In ancient Israel, vines and gardens were everywhere—the irrigation systems of the Jordan River brought life to the desert. This imagery would have been deeply familiar to His listeners.

Traditionally, the vine represented Israel itself. In Psalm 80, the psalmist recalls how God rescued Israel from Egypt and planted them in Canaan. The passage describes Israel as a spreading vine whose leaves covered mountains and whose branches stretched from the Mediterranean Sea to the River—a picture of a nation blessed with growth, territory, and influence. Yet despite this abundance, Israel failed morally. They possessed great blessings but lacked genuine spiritual fruit; they did not provide the nourishment God intended.

When Jesus says "I am the true vine," He is claiming to be what Israel was meant to be. He is the true Israel—not the failed, compromised nation of the past, but the perfect fulfillment of what God always envisioned. As the true vine, Jesus provides genuine nourishment. He offers not just material blessing, but spiritual sustenance for those who remain connected to Him.

This includes even those who are "unnatural" or outsiders—through Jesus, Gentiles and anyone far from God can be grafted in and become part of His people. This speaks to Easter and new life for all who believe.

### Application: Remain Connected to Jesus

> "Remain in me, as I also remain in you. No branch can bear fruit by itself; it must remain in the vine. Neither can you bear fruit unless you remain in me." (John 15:4)

Jesus is the source of your nourishment and life. The critical question is: where do you get your sustenance? Who or what are you plugged into? Without remaining connected to Jesus—and to His word, which He has left us—you are left dead and empty. This connection is vital not only for eternal life but for how you live right now.

Ask yourself honestly: am I truly remaining in Jesus?

## The Gardener

> "My Father is the gardener. He cuts off every branch in me that bears no fruit, while every branch that does bear fruit he prunes so that it will be even more fruitful." (John 15:1-2)

The Father is the Gardener, actively tending His vineyard with purpose and care. A gardener's work involves pruning—the selective removal of parts of a plant to stimulate healthier growth, more flowers, and more fruit.

### The Nature of Pruning

Pruning serves several purposes:

- **Encourages new growth:** Removing older stems directs the plant's energy into fresh, vigorous growth, keeping it young and vibrant.
- **Prevents disease:** Removing dead or damaged branches stops disease from spreading throughout the plant.
- **Increases fruitfulness:** Strategic pruning increases both the quantity and quality of fruit, flowers, and foliage.

The ultimate purpose is to keep the plant healthy and increasingly fruitful. It's not punishment for punishment's sake—it's loving discipline aimed at greater productivity.

### The Disciples Are Pruned

> "You are already clean because of the word I have spoken to you." (John 15:3)

The disciples have been cleaned—pruned by the word of Christ. The verb here means the gardener has taken his shears and trimmed away the dead growth, clearing away what doesn't serve fruitfulness.

### The Word Cleanses and Prunes

> "For the word of God is alive and active. Sharper than any double-edged sword, it penetrates even to dividing soul and spirit, joints and marrow; it judges the thoughts and attitudes of the heart." (Hebrews 4:12-13)

God's word is sharper than any scissors or pruning shear—sharper than a scalpel. It cuts into our innermost thoughts and attitudes, opening us to see what's truly in our hearts.

Like X-rays that expose what's hidden beneath the surface, the word lays bare:

- The excess indulgence that has fattened our hearts
- The shriveled, withered parts of our character
- The evil thoughts of revenge and destructive desires
- The illogical position of unbelief itself

When the word cuts us open and exposes everything to light, we see ourselves as we truly are. And when Christians encounter this piercing truth, the proper response is acknowledgment: *Yes, I am guilty.* We accept the conviction, not in despair, but in faith that the God who exposes our sin is also our Savior.

### Application: Stick with Jesus' Words

> "If you do not remain in me, you are like a branch that is thrown away and withers; such branches are picked up, thrown into the fire and burned. If you remain in me and my words remain in you, ask whatever you wish, and it will be done for you. This is to my Father's glory, that you bear much fruit, showing yourselves to be my disciples." (John 15:6-8)

A branch cut off from the vine loses its nourishment and dies. But those who remain—who stay connected to Jesus and let His words dwell in them—will bear much fruit and show themselves to be His true disciples.

## The Branches (The Disciples and All Believers)

> "I am the vine; you are the branches." (John 15:5)

We are the branches, entirely dependent on the vine for life and fruitfulness. Just as a branch cannot produce fruit by itself, we cannot bear spiritual fruit apart from Jesus.

### Bearing Fruit Through Remaining

> "If you remain in me and I in you, you will bear much fruit; apart from me you can do nothing." (John 15:5)

Jesus is not asking us to pick and choose parts of Him while discarding others. He calls us simply to **remain**—to stick with Him even when it's hard, even when we feel convicted of sin, even when we doubt. He is the sap of life flowing through us. Without His word, we are left dead and empty.

The path to fruitfulness is straightforward: remain in Him. Trust Him. Abide in His presence through His word.

### The Consequences of Disconnection

A branch that remains disconnected will wither and die, becoming useless. But those who remain connected are continuously pruned—refined and cleaned—so they become increasingly fruitful.

### What Does Fruitfulness Look Like?

> "As the Father has loved me, so have I loved you. Now remain in my love. If you keep my commands, you will remain in my love, just as I have kept my Father's commands and remain in his love. I have told you this so that my joy may be in you and that your joy may be complete. My command is this: Love each other as I have loved you." (John 15:9-12)

Fruitfulness is not primarily about activity or achievement—it's about love. The fruit of remaining in Jesus is the fruit of the Spirit: love that overflows to others.

Ask yourself: Why would someone prepare morning tea for church? *Love.* Why help pack up after the service? *Love.* Why arrive early to welcome others? *Love.* Why financially support the church or give to the poor? *Love.* Why invest your Sunday in getting to know others? *Love.* Why give lifts despite the cost of petrol? *Love.* Why would leaders spend hours meeting with others and leading groups? *Love.*

If you are doing these things, the word of God is working in you, changing you from the inside out. We should be deeply thankful for this transformation.

> "Greater love has no one than this: to lay down one's life for one's friends. You are my friends if you do what I command." (John 15:13-14)

Jesus calls us friends and invites us to love with the same self-giving love He has shown us.

### The Purpose: Lasting Fruit

> "You did not choose me, but I chose you and appointed you so that you might go and bear fruit—fruit that will last—and so that whatever you ask in my name the Father will give you. This is my command: Love each other." (John 15:16-17)

In the ancient world, gardens existed for a practical reason—to produce food. A tree that merely took up space was a waste. The purpose of gardening was to bear fruit, and that fruit was meant to sustain life.

Reading God's word is not merely a pleasant activity like reading a novel—though it may be enjoyable. Rather, it is a direct relationship with God that results in the fruit of love. God's word works in ways that no human literature can: it cuts us with truth, then heals us so we might grow stronger. It prunes away the deadwood and cleans us for greater fruitfulness.

## The Word Divides

The word of God creates a clear division among those who hear it:

**Those who accept it:**

> "If anyone loves me, he will obey my teaching. My Father will love him, and we will come to him and make our home with him." (John 14:23)

> "If you hold to my teaching, you are really my disciples." (John 8:31)

> "Whoever hears my word and believes him who sent me has eternal life and will not be condemned; he has crossed over from death to life." (John 5:24)

**Those who reject it:**

> "You are ready to kill me, because you have no room for my word." (John 8:37)

> "His word does not dwell in you, for you do not believe the one he sent." (John 5:38)

## The Word Gives Life

For those who accept it and repent, the word that convicts us of guilt is also the word of salvation. The word cuts away our guilt and shame, removing the excess so we might be renewed. We are given new life.

> "Do not let your hearts be troubled. Trust in God; trust also in me. In my Father's house are many rooms; if it were not so, I would have told you. I am going there to prepare a place for you." (John 14:1-3)

In the Gospel of John, Jesus speaks less about His coming betrayal and more about preparing a place for us and remaining in His love.

## The Word Is Undivided from the Messenger

> "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God." (John 1:1)

> "The Word became flesh and made his dwelling among us." (John 1:14)

The message is inseparable from the Messenger. You cannot accept Jesus while rejecting His words, nor can you claim to follow Him while picking and choosing only the teachings you prefer.

Some attempt to separate the two—to say, "I like Jesus, but I'm uncomfortable with His words." This approach is fundamentally flawed. If you don't embrace His words, you haven't truly embraced Him. There is no other reliable source for what Jesus said except Scripture. There is no way to separate them.

Historical example: Liberal German scholars in previous generations attempted to determine what Jesus "really said" by voting on the authenticity of each saying. They used colored balls dropped into a box—red for authentic sayings, pink for probably authentic, gray for close to His ideas, black for additions by later tradition. The result? Only about 15 sayings earned majority vote as truly from Jesus—roughly 6% of His recorded words. Ninety-four percent were deemed doubtful or false.

This arbitrary approach creates a Jesus of our own making rather than submitting to the Jesus who actually exists. We end up with a Jesus shaped by our preferences rather than the Jesus who claims to be the truth.

> "Your word is truth." (John 17:17)

## Therefore: Remain in Me

> "Sanctify them by the truth; your word is truth." (John 17:17)

> "My prayer is not for them alone. I pray also for those who will believe in me through their message." (John 17:20)

Reading the Bible is how we remain connected to the sap of life. Through God's word, we hear about His character, His purposes for the world, and His plans for us. This is how we relate to God when Jesus is no longer physically present.

### Practical Tips for Remaining

There is no magic formula or secret method:

- **Different seasons require different approaches:** Sometimes you read for the big picture; sometimes you focus on minute detail. Both are valuable. Don't be discouraged by variety.

- **Young people with structured lives** can establish strong disciplines and habits—high school years are ideal for building this foundation.

- **Self-motivated individuals** may thrive with Bible-in-a-year plans or similar frameworks.

- **As adults, scheduling becomes harder.** Different approaches work for different people:
  - Some are **need-driven**—studying Scripture for exams or to understand a current challenge
  - Some enjoy **book reading**—working through Scripture systematically
  - Some prefer **listening**—audio Bible recordings
  - Some benefit from **discussion**—wrestling through texts with others after church
  - Some prefer **intensive study**—camps or extended Bible study conferences where they can think deeply

- **Different seasons work for different life stages.** Just as children's needs change and their tastes evolve, so does your relationship with Scripture. Keep feeding on God's word; maintain variety; sometimes emphasize discipline, sometimes flexibility. Keep engaging with it.

### The Resolution

This year, resolve to read and understand the Bible. Let the word of God work in you, pruning away what doesn't bear fruit and nourishing what does. Remain in Jesus through His word, so that you might bear fruit—fruit that lasts—and show yourself to be His disciple.
