# Malachi 1:1-2:9

**Title:** Messengers of God's Word Through Priests
**Date:** 17 May 2020
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## The Heart of Malachi's Message

Malachi's name means "messenger," and his central message is beautifully simple yet profoundly challenging: we are called to love God, love each other, and love the world through our witness.

During times of uncertainty—when we're hungry for news and information—we have an even deeper need: to hear what God wants to tell us. That's what Malachi brings us over these three weeks.

---

## 1. God's Choosing Love

> "I have loved you," says the Lord. (Malachi 1:2)

God's love for Israel stretches back over a thousand years before Malachi's time. He speaks through Malachi to a people returned from exile, a people who questioned whether God truly loved them.

### The Meaning of "Love" and "Hate"

When God says He loved Jacob but hated Esau, He's not using these words in the violent sense we might assume. Love and hate here mean **to choose** or **to reject**. When you propose marriage saying "I love you," you're saying "I choose to commit to you for life"—not that you dislike everyone else. Similarly, "I hate chocolate" simply means you don't choose it.

This is about **election**—God's sovereign choice.

> Yet, before the twins were born or had done anything good or bad—in order that God's purpose in election might stand: "Jacob I loved, but Esau I hated." (Romans 9:11-13)

### God Has Chosen You and Me

> To God's elect... who have been chosen according to the foreknowledge of God the Father, through the sanctifying work of the Spirit, to be obedient to Jesus Christ and sprinkled with his blood. (1 Peter 1:1-2)

You are chosen. Before time itself, God set His affection on you. He paid the ultimate price for your redemption—the blood of Christ—not because you earned it, but because He loves you.

But notice the **purpose** of being chosen: **to be obedient to Jesus Christ**. We are chosen for a reason—to obey God's will, to embody His values, to love Him supremely.

### The Cost of Choosing God

> If anyone comes to me and does not hate his father and mother, his wife and children, his brothers and sisters—yes, even his own life—he cannot be my disciple. (Luke 14:26)

Jesus isn't asking you to hate your family in the ordinary sense. He's asking you to **choose God's interests over all other interests**—even those closest to you.

This is directed at adults who can make that choice. When you're young, listen to your parents. But when you mature and face conflicts between what your parents want and what God calls you to do, you must choose God. Your parents love you and want success for you—but they may not have God's perspective.

> Anyone who loves their life will lose it, while anyone who hates their life in this world will keep it for eternal life. (John 12:25)

The deepest sacrifice isn't about hating your family—it's about dying to self-interest and living for God's kingdom.

## 2. Israel Failed to Love God

> "A son honors his father, and a slave his master. If I am a father, where is the honor due me? If I am a master, where is the respect due me?" (Malachi 1:6)

This is the cry of a God taken for granted. The Israelites questioned God's love, but the real problem was their failure to **honor** Him.

### Contempt Through Half-Hearted Worship

> "By offering defiled food on my altar... By saying that the Lord's table is contemptible... When you offer blind animals for sacrifice, is that not wrong? When you sacrifice lame or diseased animals, is that not wrong?" (Malachi 1:7-8)

The priests were bringing defective animals to the altar. These weren't the best of their herds—they were the rejects. The blind, the lame, the diseased animals. Animals they didn't want. Animals that were going to die anyway.

The whole point of sacrifice is to understand that **sin is costly**. The penalty for sin is actually our own lives, so God graciously allowed animals to take our place. This required bringing something valuable—your finest livestock, your most prized possession. It was meant to shock you into understanding how serious sin is.

But the priests were defeating the entire purpose by bringing trash.

> "Now plead with God to be gracious to us. With such offerings from your hands, will he accept you?" (Malachi 1:8-9)

Of course He won't. It's a waste of time.

> "Oh, that one of you would shut the temple doors, so that you would not light useless fires on my altar! I am not pleased with you," says the Lord Almighty, "and I will accept no offering from your hands." (Malachi 1:10)

### Despising the Table of the Lord

> "But you profane it by saying, 'The Lord's table is defiled,' and, 'Its food is contemptible.'" (Malachi 1:12)

The priests had become so cynical about worship that they actually **despised** what they were supposed to serve. Their contempt was poisoning the people.

### Jesus: The True Temple and Priest

Now there is no physical temple. Jesus is the temple of God. He is also the priest who offers the perfect sacrifice—Himself.

> "See, I lay a stone in Zion, a chosen and precious cornerstone, and the one who trusts in him will never be put to shame." (1 Peter 2:4-6)

### We Are the Living Temple

> Don't you know that you yourselves are God's temple and that God's Spirit dwells in your midst? (1 Corinthians 3:16)

> You also, like living stones, are being built into a spiritual house to be a holy priesthood, offering spiritual sacrifices acceptable to God through Jesus Christ. (1 Peter 2:5)

We are not bricks and mortar. We are **living stones** being constructed into God's spiritual house. And we are **priests**—all of us.

### Our Spiritual Sacrifices

The sacrifices we offer today are not bulls and goats:

> Through Jesus, therefore, let us continually offer to God a sacrifice of praise—the fruit of lips that openly profess his name. And do not forget to do good and to share with others, for with such sacrifices God is pleased. (Hebrews 13:15-16)

We honor God's table by caring for those who sit around it. We honor the temple by building up the body of Christ. This means:

- **Preparing ourselves** to gather with God's people—not showing up exhausted and distracted
- **Giving your best time** to prayer and God's Word, not just the leftovers
- **Dressing with intention** for corporate worship—not as legalism, but as a sign that you're offering your best
- **Serving sacrificially** while you're young and strong, not waiting until retirement
- **Encouraging one another** with prepared hearts and fresh minds

If you treated Sunday church the way you treat Monday work, everything would change. But we often give the dregs—showing up late, unprepared, distracted by screens, unmotivated.

> Therefore, I urge you... to offer your bodies as a living sacrifice, holy and pleasing to God—this is your true and proper worship. (Romans 12:1)

## 3. God's Name Must Be Great Among the Nations

> "My name will be great among the nations, from where the sun rises to where it sets. In every place incense and pure offerings will be brought to me, because my name will be great among the nations," says the Lord Almighty. (Malachi 1:11)

This isn't just about Israel's private relationship with God. **God's reputation is at stake.** The nations are watching.

### Honor Culture and Representation

In an honor culture, you're never just an individual. You carry your family's name, your culture's reputation, your father's honor. When you wear a school uniform, you don't represent yourself—you represent the school. If you misbehave in that uniform, you don't just ruin your own reputation; you ruin the school's.

Our relatives often see us not as individuals but as our father's children, our family's representatives. When we act poorly, we don't just damage our own honor—we damage our family's name.

**This is exactly what Israel was doing to God.**

### The Priests Failed in Their Role

> "For the lips of a priest ought to preserve knowledge, because he is the messenger of the Lord Almighty and people seek instruction from his mouth." (Malachi 2:7)

The priest's job was to:
- **Know God's law** thoroughly
- **Teach it** faithfully to the next generation
- **Guard it** against corruption and compromise
- **Walk in it** with integrity and peace

The prophets came from outside to forewarn. The priests were meant to be the **guardians of the inside**—maintaining the system's integrity.

> "But you have turned from the way and by your teaching have caused many to stumble; you have violated the covenant with Levi," says the Lord Almighty. "So I have caused you to be despised and humiliated before all the people, because you have not followed my ways but have shown partiality in matters of the law." (Malachi 2:8-9)

### What the Nations See

When other nations looked at Israel, they weren't just seeing Israel—**they were seeing God.** And what God did they perceive?

- A God whose followers offer Him only half-hearted commitment
- A God who receives contempt instead of honor
- A God whose name is despised among His own people

Who would want to follow such a God? **God's entire mission to the nations was being undermined** by the failure of His priests to love and honor Him.

### We Are Called to Be Priests

> But you are a chosen people, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, God's special possession, that you may declare the praises of him who called you out of darkness into his wonderful light. (1 Peter 2:9)

Jesus is the true guardian of God's Word. He alone kept perfect integrity. He "had no regard for his brothers and sisters" in the sense that He prioritized God's will above family expectations—never married, never had children, wholly committed to the Father's work.

But now **we inherit the priestly calling.** We are meant to:

- Declare God's praises through our words and actions
- Guard God's truth in a world of lies
- Teach others about Jesus through our lives
- Make God's name great among the nations

### The Opportunity Before Us

During uncertain times, people are searching for truth and meaning. This is your moment:

- **At school**: Pray for your friends. Talk about God's perspective in conversations. Show genuine care.
- **At work and in your community**: Live with such integrity that people ask about your hope and your peace.
- **In casual encounters**: Be ready to speak about Jesus when doors open—at coffee shops, restaurants, in chance conversations.

> Make the most of your time. Spend your best, healthiest, most vibrant years expanding God's kingdom.

Invite friends to church. Offer spiritual sacrifices by pointing them to Christ. Make it easy for them to say yes to Jesus by showing them a community that honors God with our whole hearts.

---

## The Stakes: God's Name and God's Glory

Everything in Malachi flows from one central concern: **the honor of God's name among the nations.** God's sovereignty isn't exercised in a vacuum—it's exercised **for His glory.**

God chose Israel not because they were great, but to make His name great. When we fail to honor Him, we undermine His mission. When we honor Him with our whole hearts—our best time, our finest service, our whole lives—we advance His kingdom.

You are chosen. Chosen to love God. Chosen to love each other. Chosen to make His name great in a world that desperately needs to know Him.
