# John 1:1-14

**Title:** Invisible Not  
**Date:** 20 April 2008  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## God: Do You Really Exist?

We begin in a place of spiritual vulnerability. The question haunts many of us: How can we know God is real? What would He need to do to remove all doubt?

## God Addresses Our Need

The apostle John opens his Gospel not with a story, but with a profound theological statement. He addresses our deepest need—the need to know that God is not distant, not hidden, not unknowable. God is real, and He has made Himself visible.

### The Word: His Credentials

John introduces us to *the Word*—the Logos in Greek—God's self-expression and divine communication. This is no ordinary messenger. The Word existed from the beginning. The Word was with God. The Word *was* God. Here is God's ultimate credential: He is the source and sustainer of all that exists. Nothing created stands outside His authority or understanding.

### The Word Became Flesh

This is where faith becomes tangible. The divine entered the human. The infinite became finite. The invisible became visible. In Jesus, we do not encounter a distant deity or a philosophical concept. We encounter God in flesh—Emmanuel, God with us.

Jesus lived among us. He hungered, wept, prayed, and suffered. Yet in His humanity, He revealed the full character of God: His grace, His truth, His holiness, His mercy. Those who saw Jesus saw the Father.

### The Word Answers Our Doubts

Through Jesus, God answers our question: "Do you really exist?" More than that—He answers: "I love you. I know you. I came for you."

## So What Do We Do?

Our response must move through three dimensions:

**Facts:** Know what Jesus taught and accomplished. Know that He died and rose again.

**Faith:** Trust Jesus as your Lord. Believe His promise of forgiveness and eternal life.

**Feelings:** Allow the Holy Spirit to fill your heart with assurance and joy, flowing from facts believed and faith lived out.
