# Micah 4:1-13

**Title:** God's Day  
**Date:** October 24, 2024  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Peace in the Last Days (1-5)

> In the last days the mountain of the Lord's temple will be established as the highest of the mountains; it will be exalted above the hills, and peoples will stream to it. Many nations will come and say, "Come, let us go up to the mountain of the Lord, to the temple of the God of Jacob. He will teach us his ways, so that we may walk in his paths." The law will go out from Zion, the word of the Lord from Jerusalem.

### Understanding "the Last Days"

The phrase "last days" in Scripture refers to the era inaugurated by Christ's death and resurrection. This is the period when the old world's fate is sealed—like a rocket launched toward its target. The destruction is certain; we're living through the interval before impact strikes.

Jesus embodied this transition. When He raised Lazarus from the dead, He was demonstrating that the last day had already begun. With His resurrection, the clock began ticking toward the world's end.

### The Mountain Vision

In this passage, Micah envisions God's temple mountain becoming the highest place—not geographically, but in authority and supremacy. The Hebrew word *rosh* (highest/ruler) carries the sense of ultimate dominion.

Notice the movement described: peoples and nations "stream" to this mountain, seeking wisdom and instruction. This isn't passive—it's a deliberate pilgrimage. They come to learn the ways of God, to walk in His paths.

### Peoples and Nations

The text distinguishes between "peoples" (God's covenant people, the Jews) and "nations" (the Gentiles). This prophecy finds its fulfillment in the New Testament when Greeks came to see Jesus during the Passover feast—gentiles streaming to the temple, drawn by the fame of His works.

### Peace Through God's Authority

> He will judge between many peoples and will settle disputes for strong nations far and wide. They will beat their swords into plowshares and their spears into pruning hooks. Nation will not take up sword against nation, nor will they train for war anymore. Everyone will sit under their own vine and under their own fig tree, and no one will make them afraid, for the Lord Almighty has spoken.

When God rules as judge, warfare ceases. The transformation is radical: implements of destruction become tools of cultivation and provision. This isn't wishful thinking—it's the consequence of submitting to God's law and kingdom.

The image of sitting under one's own vine and fig tree represents security, prosperity, and freedom from conscription. People enjoy the fruits of their own labor, undisturbed and unafraid.

This peace is anchored in God's strength ("Lord Almighty"—literally, Lord of Hosts/Armies). Peace through strength: only when one power towers above all others can true peace be maintained. History confirms this. The past century of relative global peace has been enabled by military supremacy that has kept wars localized and contained.

### The Gospel Flourishes in Peace

> All the nations may walk in the name of their gods, but we will walk in the name of the Lord our God for ever and ever.

During this extended period of global peace, the gospel has spread across the world. Mission work flourishes when there is stability, security, and open borders. Yet ironically, we often use this peace for personal travel rather than for gospel proclamation.

**A challenging thought:** While people from every nation are immigrating to Christian-influenced countries—seeking the Christian wisdom reflected in our schools, hospitals, and institutions—many Christians are traveling outward for leisure, leaving the mission field.

We have been given unprecedented freedom and opportunity. The question demands our attention: Are we using this peace dividend to build God's kingdom, or to build our own comfort?

## War and God's Gathering (6-13)

> In that day," declares the Lord, "I will gather the lame; I will assemble the exiles and those I have brought to grief. I will make the lame my remnant, those driven away a strong nation.

### The Day of Judgment Approaches

The prophecy shifts from the extended peace of the last days to their culmination. In "that day"—the singular, final day—God will gather His people completely.

Notice who He gathers: the lame (those society considers useless), the exiles (the rejected, those without home or identity), and those brought to grief. God shows particular care for the broken and marginalized. For anyone carrying deep wounds or unfair suffering, this is a word of hope—God will make things right, not through revenge, but through His presence and restoration.

### The Restoration of Kingship

> The Lord will rule over them in Mount Zion from that day and forever. As for you, watchtower of the flock, stronghold of Daughter Zion, the former dominion will be restored to you; kingship will come to Daughter Jerusalem.

Even now, in the extended last days, the kingdom belongs to God's people under Christ's rule. But there remains a period of waiting, characterized by the birth pangs of travail.

### The Birth Pangs of History

> Why do you now cry aloud—have you no king? Has your ruler perished, that pain seizes you like that of a woman in labor? Writhe in agony, Daughter Zion, like a woman in labor, for now you must leave the city and camp in the open field. You will go to Babylon; there you will be rescued. There the Lord will redeem you out of the hand of your enemies.

The pain is real—exile, displacement, suffering. But like labor, it produces new life. The apparent absence of a king doesn't mean God has abandoned His people. The extended timeline of the last days allows for the gospel's proclamation and the salvation of the world.

This is why we pray for government leaders: that we may have peace and quiet in which to live godly lives and proclaim the gospel. The chaos of war makes spiritual growth and evangelism nearly impossible.

### Two Gatherings

God orchestrates two distinct gatherings:

**First:** He gathers His people like sheaves to be refined and redeemed.

> But now many nations are gathered against you. They say, "Let her be defiled, let our eyes gloat over Zion!" But they do not know the thoughts of the Lord; they do not understand his plan, that he has gathered them like sheaves to the threshing floor.

**Second:** He gathers His enemies as sheaves to be threshed and destroyed.

> Rise and thresh, Daughter Zion, for I will give you horns of iron; I will give you hooves of bronze, and you will break to pieces many nations. You will devote their ill-gotten gains to the Lord, their wealth to the Lord of all the earth.

### The Final Day

War is coming—not the contained conflicts of the last days, but the definitive judgment. When that final day arrives, God's judgment will fall completely and fully. No president or power will prevent it.

## The Challenge for Us

We stand in the last days with unique opportunity. The gospel message is freely accessible across the globe. Christian institutions shape culture and values worldwide. And yet we sometimes squander this season by prioritizing comfort and leisure over kingdom building.

Consider: Are we using this time to develop our character so that others see Christ in us? Are we inviting our families and friends to church? Are we thinking strategically about how to proclaim Christ in this window of peace?

The promise is sure—God will gather His people and rule over them forever. The question is not whether God's kingdom will come, but whether we will be faithful stewards of this present season.

Have you made peace with God? If you're uncertain whether you stand with Him or against Him, now is the time to resolve that question.
