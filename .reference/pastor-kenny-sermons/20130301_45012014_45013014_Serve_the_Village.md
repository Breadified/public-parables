# Romans 12:14–13:14

**Title:** Serve the Village

**Date:** 10 March 2013

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## Introduction

In the previous message, we explored Romans 12:1—Paul's call to offer our bodies as living sacrifices in light of God's mercy. But what does that sacrifice actually look like in practice?

Over the next three talks, we'll answer that question by examining what it means to:

1. Serve the village
2. Serve the weak
3. Serve the world

---

## 1. Live with Our Neighbours

### The Challenge of Community Living

One Saturday evening, my neighbour's daughter had a birthday party. Cars honked as guests arrived, children screamed in fun, and music played throughout the evening. It was a seven-year-old's party, so the noise was understandable. But another time, teenage kids down the street caused trouble with alcohol and noise at midnight. Perhaps your own neighbour has done something annoying to you.

Today's passage speaks directly to this: how we live with our neighbours and our community.

### Scripture on Harmony and Peace

> Romans 12:16 Live in harmony with one another. Do not be proud, but be willing to associate with people of low position. Do not be conceited.
>
> Romans 12:18 If it is possible, as far as it depends on you, live at peace with everyone.

Paul calls us to:
- Live in harmony with one another
- Live at peace with everyone
- Include all sorts of people—whether of high or low position, rich or poor, married or single, regardless of ethnicity

This applies universally. We are not to avenge wrongs that happen to us.

> Romans 12:14 Bless those who persecute you; bless and do not curse.
>
> Romans 12:17 Do not repay anyone evil for evil. Be careful to do what is right in the eyes of everybody.

### A Radical Departure from Culture

This teaching is revolutionary. Most civilisations teach that we should take revenge, especially when our families are threatened. Even the Old Testament contained the law of reciprocal justice:

> Genesis 12:3 I will bless those who bless you, and whoever curses you I will curse.
>
> Exodus 21:23-24 But if there is serious injury, you are to take life for life, eye for eye, tooth for tooth, hand for hand, foot for foot.

Yet Jesus transformed this ethic entirely:

> Matthew 5:38-39 You have heard that it was said, 'Eye for eye, and tooth for tooth.' But I tell you, Do not resist an evil person. If someone strikes you on the right cheek, turn to him the other also.

### Why This Transformation?

We live in the age of forgiveness and tolerance. This is not the age of judgment; that is coming.

> Romans 12:19 Do not take revenge, my friends, but leave room for God's wrath, for it is written: "It is mine to avenge; I will repay," says the Lord.

On the last judgment day, God will avenge all wrongdoing. For now, we are called to live in peace. Instead of revenge, we respond with generosity:

> Romans 12:20-21 If your enemy is hungry, feed him; if he is thirsty, give him something to drink. In doing this, you will heap burning coals on his head. Do not be overcome by evil, but overcome evil with good.

### The Priority of Citizenship

God's will is for a peaceful village and peaceful neighbourhood. This is more important than any other achievement. We often think success means earning lots of money or being brilliant academically. But God values something different: the ability to live well with others.

What do we want our children to be good at? God has never instructed us to excel at mathematics or genius-level science. He has instructed us to live with one another. What is the point of being clever if we are rude? What is the point of studying hard if we lack basic manners?

Real life skills include:
- Saying thank you when someone gives you a lift
- Saying sorry when you're late
- Giving a good explanation when you break a promise

There's no point being a piano prodigy if you cannot serve your community.

### Practical Application in Daily Life

How can we live better with our neighbours and village?

- **At home:** Show tolerance and give-and-take. Take out neighbours' trash when they're away. Help return shopping trolleys left in front of apartments.
- **At school and work:** Help with local school canteens. Live in harmony despite workplace politics and selfish ambition.
- **At church:** We must live patiently with one another. We spend limited time together, so we naturally make mistakes. Let's be patient, seek to understand, and live in peace.

## 2. Live with the State

### Submission to Authority

Not only must we live with each other, we must also live with our authorities.

> Romans 13:1 Everyone must submit himself to the governing authorities...

Paul gives two reasons for obedience:

> Romans 13:5 Therefore, it is necessary to submit to the authorities, not only because of possible punishment but also because of conscience.

### The First Reason: Conscience

Conscience—an awareness of what God wants—is developed through years of studying Scripture. What does God want us to know?

God has established government. Paul emphasizes this repeatedly:

> Romans 13:1 For there is no authority except that which God has established. The authorities that exist have been established by God.
>
> Romans 13:2 Consequently, he who rebels against the authority is rebelling against what God has instituted, and those who do so will bring judgment on themselves.

God has established good governments and bad governments. Whether Labour or Liberal, elected or dictator, hereditary crown or president—God ultimately stands behind every government. Even in a democracy where we choose our government, God is ultimately behind that choice. Even a Nazi government exists within God's sovereignty.

> Romans 13:4 For he is God's servant to do you good. But if you do wrong, be afraid, for he does not bear the sword for nothing. He is God's servant, an agent of wrath to bring punishment on the wrongdoer.

We are to obey authorities as we would obey God. Rebelling against government is not merely rebelling against an ordinary institution—it is disobeying God directly.

Consider the implications: A parking fine? That is against God. A speeding ticket? That is against God. Cheating the government system? You are cheating against God. This is a sobering reality that puts authority in its proper perspective.

### The Second Reason: Restraint of Sin

Government exists to restrain sin and maintain order:

> Romans 13:3 For rulers hold no terror for those who do right, but for those who do wrong. Do you want to be free from fear of the one in authority? Then do what is right and he will commend you. For he is God's servant to do you good. But if you do wrong, be afraid, for he does not bear the sword for nothing. He is God's servant, an agent of wrath to bring punishment on the wrongdoer.

Government may be sinful, yet it still restrains sin better than anarchy. By definition, government implies order. There is no other earthly source of authority to which we can appeal. God does not bring thunder and lightning to catch thieves. The government, imperfect as it is, remains our only resort to justice in this present age.

If we undermine government's authority, we remove justice itself. Dilute or subvert it, and we have nothing left—only perpetual coups like those we see in some Pacific islands, Egypt, or Thailand.

### Practical Obedience: Paying Taxes

Our village needs governance and authority. We support it through taxes.

> Romans 13:6 This is also why you pay taxes, for the authorities are God's servants, who give their full time to governing.

Taxes fund government service. Often we ask, "What do I get out of them?" The real answer is nothing—it's not for you. It's for the government to govern.

Practical obedience means:
- Declare the correct income and claim only legitimate deductions
- Pay GST honestly. Mechanics and tradespeople often offer discounts without GST, which seems like a win-win—but the government loses, and so does God
- Support public service, even if it seems inefficient. We may complain that public servants are overpaid or the system is inefficient, but the solution lies within the system: become a public servant, enter parliament, lobby for change. It is hypocritical to critique public service while sending our brightest children to become lawyers and doctors instead

### Living Righteously Under Imperfect Government

Even if we live under an anti-Christian government, this is not an excuse for us to sin. Rather, we are called to change ourselves.

> Romans 13:11-12 And do this, understanding the present time. The hour has come for you to wake up from your slumber, because our salvation is nearer now than when we first believed. The night is nearly over; the day is almost here. So let us put aside the deeds of darkness and put on the armor of light.

Our response to government should include:
- Following the law
- Not seeking revenge
- Living in peace

## 3. Live Differently—The Heart of the Matter

### Love as the Fulfilling of All Law

> Romans 13:7-8 Give everyone what you owe him: If you owe taxes, pay taxes; if revenue, then revenue; if respect, then respect; if honor, then honor. Let no debt remain outstanding, except the continuing debt to love one another, for he who loves his fellow man has fulfilled the law.
>
> Romans 13:9 The commandments, "Do not commit adultery," "Do not murder," "Do not steal," "Do not covet," and whatever other commandment there may be, are summed up in this one rule: "Love your neighbor as yourself."
>
> Romans 13:10 Love does no harm to its neighbor. Therefore love is the fulfillment of the law.

### The Problem of Debt

In Paul's time, debt was a serious matter. A neighbour who could not repay money would go hungry, and bankruptcy carried great social stigma. Debt remains serious today—not necessarily from the lender's perspective, but from the borrower's.

Excessive debt signals:
- An unsustainable lifestyle
- Lack of planning
- Overambition
- Phone contracts that cost more than they're worth
- Houses with more bathrooms than residents

The Global Financial Crisis revealed a deeper truth: unbridled debt reflects a lack of love for our neighbour. When we exploit credit systems without regard for consequences, we demonstrate a fundamental failure to love.

Excessive debt is not godly. We must live within our means and honour our obligations.

### A Call to Live Differently

> Romans 13:11-14 And do this, understanding the present time. The hour has come for you to wake up from your slumber, because our salvation is nearer now than when we first believed. The night is nearly over; the day is almost here. So let us put aside the deeds of darkness and put on the armor of light. Let us behave decently, as in the daytime, not in orgies and drunkenness, not in sexual immorality and debauchery, not in dissension and jealousy. Rather, clothe yourselves with the Lord Jesus Christ, and do not think about how to gratify the desires of the sinful nature.

We cannot control everything—an anti-Christian government, unjust laws, systemic evil. But there is much we can control. If non-Christians cannot distinguish your life from theirs, what value do you add to the world?

If you study the same way, party the same way, work the same way as unbelievers, then what is the difference? We must be people of the light.

Consider what this means practically: You're invited to a club party. If you're invited, you might go for a few minutes to support a friend. But if you're in control, would you organize a dark, pulsing disco party for children? Would you expose them to dance culture and its associations with immorality and excess? Or would you create something different—bright, joyful, wholesomely fun?

Our calling is to take our cues from God, not from the world. In school or university, don't feel shame when others look down on you for not swearing or drinking. You don't need cocktails to be sophisticated.

The greater change needed in this world cannot be legislated. It comes only through the gospel as it is proclaimed by you—supported by the integrity of your life.

---

## In Conclusion: Vindication

On that future day when our true King returns to judge, we will be vindicated. Those who lived righteously, who loved their neighbours, who honoured authority, who lived as children of light—they will be proven right.

Until then, we serve our village, we respect our authorities, and we live visibly, distinctly different from the darkness around us.
