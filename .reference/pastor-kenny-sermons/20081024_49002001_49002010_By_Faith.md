# Ephesians 2:1-10

**Title:** By Faith  
**Date:** October 24, 2008  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Problem: We Are Dead in Our Sins

To understand the gospel, we must first grasp the severity of our spiritual condition. Paul begins with a stark reality check.

> As for you, you were dead in your transgressions and sins, in which you used to live when you followed the ways of this world and of the ruler of the kingdom of the air, the spirit who is now at work in those who are disobedient. All of us also lived among them at one time, gratifying the cravings of our sinful nature and following its desires and thoughts. Like the rest, we were by nature objects of wrath. (Ephesians 2:1-3)

### Understanding Our Spiritual Death

Paul uses the word "dead"—not merely "sick" or "wounded," but completely helpless. Picture someone caught in a rip at the beach, unable to save themselves. Or imagine the character from *Heroes* with regenerative powers confronted by an enemy who could destroy her, leaving her helpless and trapped. That's our condition apart from God.

Paul makes clear that this deadness applies universally—to Gentiles and Jews alike, to all of us regardless of background. We are dead because of three influences:

- **External pressure:** We follow the ways of the world around us
- **Spiritual oppression:** The ruler of the kingdom of the air (Satan) actively works in those who are disobedient
- **Internal corruption:** Our sinful nature craves rebellion against God's design

### The Consequence: God's Wrath

The result of this condition is inescapable: "we were by nature objects of wrath." This isn't arbitrary anger. It's the righteous response of a holy God to human rebellion.

### A Critical Distinction

Many people today will readily admit they're "not perfect"—that they make mistakes, have flaws, and fall short of their goals. This is easily fixed: better education, counseling, motivation, self-help. But that misses Paul's point entirely.

Paul isn't saying we lack perfection. He's saying we are **sinful**—in rebellion against God, disobedient to His authority, following Satan instead of our Creator. We face divine judgment. And no amount of self-improvement, positive thinking, or external success can address this spiritual death.

---

## The Solution: Made Alive Through Grace

But there is hope. Paul's great "But" shifts the entire narrative.

> But because of his great love for us, God, who is rich in mercy, made us alive with Christ even when we were dead in transgressions—it is by grace you have been saved. (Ephesians 2:4-5)

### The Character of God's Rescue

Notice what God did not wait for:
- Our positive thinking
- Our good deeds  
- Our moment of achievement or success
- Our feeling ready to be saved

Instead, He acted precisely when we were dead—helpless, unable to save ourselves. This is pure grace: undeserved favor from a God who is "rich in mercy."

> And God raised us up with Christ and seated us with him in the heavenly realms in Christ Jesus, in order that in the coming ages he might show the incomparable riches of his grace, expressed in his kindness to us in Christ Jesus. (Ephesians 2:6-7)

### Three Possible Responses

When faced with the reality of our spiritual death, we have three options:

**1. Ignore It**  
We can pretend we're not really in trouble—that we're just "not perfect," nothing serious. This requires self-deception.

**2. Work to Fix It**  
We can believe a problem exists but convince ourselves we can solve it through our own effort: doing good works, attending church, keeping the rules. This is futile. Jesus teaches that even angry words toward someone constitute murder in God's eyes (Matthew 5:22). We cannot work our way out of this.

**3. Trust in Jesus**  
The third response is faith—not as intellectual belief alone, but as trust and dependence. When you're caught in a rip, you don't swim harder (that exhausts you). You raise your hand and call for help, placing yourself in the hands of the lifeguard. This is faith in Jesus.

> For it is by grace you have been saved, through faith—and this not from yourselves, it is the gift of God—not by works, so that no one can boast. For we are God's workmanship, created in Christ Jesus to do good works, which God prepared in advance for us to do. (Ephesians 2:8-10)

### Why Faith, Not Works?

Salvation is a gift, not an achievement. We cannot boast about earning God's grace because it's freely given to those who trust in Jesus. He bore God's wrath on the cross, freeing us from the penalty of sin. Our response is simple: to trust that He has done what we could never do.

---

## The Urgency of Now

We live in a strange in-between time. Spiritually, we are like a beheaded chicken that continues twitching and running for a few minutes—appearing alive while already dead. In God's eyes, we are dead in our sins. But we still draw breath. We still have time.

The future will bring finality: either eternal life through faith in Jesus, or eternal death. The time to seek rescue is now. Trust in Jesus while you still can.
