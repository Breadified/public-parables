# Hebrews 10:19-25

**Title:** Encouraging One Another

**Date:** January 2003

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Reason for Church

The passage opens with a profound declaration: we have access to God's presence because of Jesus Christ. This is revolutionary. In the Old Testament system, only the high priest could enter the Most Holy Place once a year. It was a forbidden, sacred space—utterly inaccessible to ordinary believers. But Christ's sacrifice changed everything.

> Therefore, brothers, since we have confidence to enter the Most Holy Place by the blood of Jesus, by a new and living way opened for us through the curtain, that is, his body, and since we have a great priest over the house of God, let us draw near to God with a sincere heart in full assurance of faith, having our hearts sprinkled to cleanse us from a guilty conscience and having our bodies washed with pure water. (Hebrews 10:19-22)

This "new and living way" is Jesus himself. His body was torn like the temple curtain, removing the barrier between God and humanity. We no longer approach God through rituals and sacrifices, but through direct access granted by Christ's blood. This is our confidence—not in ourselves, but in what Jesus accomplished.

## Four Commands for God's People

From this foundation of confidence, Paul gives us four imperatives that define church life:

### 1. Let Us Draw Near to God

We are invited—even commanded—to approach God with a sincere heart and full assurance of faith. This isn't tentative or fearful. Our consciences have been cleansed. Our guilt has been washed away. We can come boldly.

### 2. Let Us Hold Unswervingly to Hope

> Let us hold unswervingly to the hope we profess, for he who promised is faithful. (Hebrews 10:23)

Our hope rests not on our own consistency, but on God's faithfulness. He keeps his promises. In a world that shifts and changes, this anchor holds firm.

### 3. Let Us Consider How to Spur One Another On

> And let us consider how we may spur one another on toward love and good deeds. (Hebrews 10:24)

This is the heart of Christian community. We are not solitary believers. We exist to provoke, stimulate, and encourage one another toward Christlikeness.

### 4. Let Us Not Give Up Meeting Together

> Let us not give up meeting together, as some are in the habit of doing, but let us encourage one another—and all the more as you see the Day approaching. (Hebrews 10:25)

Gathering together is not optional. It's where encouragement happens. As the return of Christ draws near, our need for one another only increases.

## Understanding Christian Encouragement

Christian encouragement is speaking the truth in love with the aim of building believers up in Christ-likeness as we wait for Christ's return. This is what we do together as a church.

Notice that Barnabas—whose very name means "Son of Encouragement"—was not an apostle with special status. He was a believer who practiced encouragement as a spiritual gift. This teaches us something crucial: **you do not need a title to encourage others.** You need only yourself, your Bible, and God. Anyone can be an encourager.

## How to Encourage Others

### 1. Know Your Bible

The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom (Proverbs 9:10). When friends face difficult situations—exams, grief, bullying, broken relationships—we need more than sympathy. We need wisdom rooted in Scripture.

Always turn back to God's Word. His word and His Spirit work together. Read the Bible, think through the questions, pray about them, seek counsel if needed, and then speak the truth in love. God's Word carries power that our mere words cannot match.

### 2. Pray for Wisdom

Prayer is partnership with God. Pray for wisdom so you don't offer foolish counsel. Pray that God's Word will take root in the person you're encouraging. Even Paul never stopped praying for those he had ministered to (Ephesians 1:16-19). The power isn't in the prayer itself, but in the One who answers it. We plant seeds and water them, while God makes them grow.

### 3. Speak Up When Opportunity Comes

Barnabas endorsed Paul before the apostles when others were skeptical (Acts 9:26-27). When your friend questions evolutionary teaching, share the Gospel. When someone is suffering, offer comfort from Scripture and point them to Jesus. Don't let fear silence you.

### 4. Know When to Listen

Yet sometimes silence is golden. Job's friends sat with him in silence for a week, offering presence and comfort without words (Job 2:13). Listening helps you understand where your friend is truly struggling. It gives you time to think carefully about how to help. Sometimes being present is the deepest encouragement you can offer.

### 5. Be Humble in Your Approach

Don't overwhelm people with Bible passages or "Bible bash" them with multiple verses at once. Give advice in manageable portions so they have time to reflect and act. Wisdom and tact are gifts from God—use them with humility (Proverbs 17:27; 19:21). Your gentleness matters.

### 6. Let Your Life Back Up Your Words

Our actions reveal our character to those around us. If you claim to be a Christian but swear, steal, and act selfishly, no one will want what you have. Throughout the New Testament, Paul urges us to watch our conduct so that our Christian faith becomes attractive to others.

> Live such good lives among the pagans that, though they accuse you of doing wrong, they may see your good deeds and glorify God on the day he visits us. (1 Peter 2:12)

Words and actions work together for the progress of the Gospel and the encouragement of God's people. Don't ask friends to join you at church events if your life contradicts your invitation.

## Where Encouragement Happens

Encouragement isn't confined to formal settings. It happens everywhere:

- **In sports:** at swimming carnivals, athletics carnivals, cross country, during any competition
- **At school:** at recess, lunch, free periods, before and after school
- **On the way:** bus rides, car journeys
- **Through technology:** emails, Facebook messages
- **In writing:** handwritten letters and cards carry special weight because they show you invested time
- **In community:** welcoming newcomers at church, one-on-one conversations

Every moment is an opportunity to build someone up in Christ.
