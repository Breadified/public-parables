## The Confusion Over Parenthood Today

Families face unprecedented pressure. Parents and children are separated for most of their waking hours—through work, school, tuition classes, and transportation. Government policies increasingly intrude on parental authority. Families are further undermined by cultural shifts that have diminished the role of fathers while pushing mothers into the workforce.

The fundamental question we must ask is: What is the purpose of parenthood? How does family life fit into God's larger purposes to bring His glory to the ends of the earth?

To understand Christian parenthood, we must begin with the model of God as our Father.

---

## 1. God as Parent

> Have we not all one Father? Did not one God create us? Why do we profane the covenant of our fathers by breaking faith with one another? (Malachi 2:10)

Malachi begins this section by establishing God's foundational role. He is:

- **Creator**: The one who made all of us
- **Sustainer**: The one who upholds all creation
- **Provider**: The one who cares for His people

This is the model human fathers are called to follow.

### The Biblical Concept of Fatherhood

The word "patriarchal" has become a curse word in some circles, conjuring images of abusive fathers and misogynists. Many of us have experienced poor fathering. But we must not allow human failure to distort God's design. When the Bible speaks of fatherhood, it points us to God—not to broken human examples.

> For this reason I kneel before the Father, from whom his whole family in heaven and on earth derives its name. (Ephesians 3:14-15)

A father is one who:

**Creates and owns a household.** In biblical language, "father's house" refers to the family and relatives—all those given life by him. He is the creator of this unit.

**Redeems and commits to his children.** A father redeems his firstborn, signifying total commitment and care. Children are not a burden but a blessing.

> Sons are a heritage from the LORD, children a reward from him. Like arrows in the hands of a warrior are sons born in one's youth. Blessed is the man whose quiver is full of them. They will not be put to shame when they contend with their enemies in the gate. (Psalm 127:3-5)

This stands in stark contrast to the modern world's perspective, which often views children as inconveniences rather than blessings.

**Provides and protects.** A father is the protector and provider for his household.

> I was a father to the needy; I took up the case of the stranger. (Job 29:16)

Joseph exemplified this role when he became provider for all Egypt during famine:

> "So then, it was not you who sent me here, but God. He has made me father to Pharaoh, lord of his entire household and ruler of all Egypt." (Genesis 45:8)

Conversely, to be fatherless—without a protector and provider—is to be vulnerable and oppressed. This is why God identifies Himself as the Father of the fatherless (Psalm 68:5).

### A Warning About Redefining God's Fatherhood

There is an agenda, unfortunately shared by some Christians, to change how we understand God's nature. Some want to replace masculine language with gender-neutral terms, rewriting Scripture to obscure God's fatherhood and Jesus's sonship.

While some gender-inclusive language is appropriate in everyday use, it cannot be applied to biblical language without changing the text's meaning. When words like "father," "brother," and "son" are replaced with "parent," "sibling," and "child," the theological meaning shifts. When "He" becomes "someone" or "you," the personal relationship with God becomes abstract.

There is a purpose to the masculine imagery in Scripture: God is revealed as Father. Jesus is revealed as Son. This relationship model reflects the design God intends for human families—fathers and mothers caring for their children.

## 2. Unfaithful Parents: Breaking Faith

Despite this model, the Israelites broke faith in two devastating ways.

### First: Intermarriage with Unbelievers (v.11-12)

> Judah has broken faith. A detestable thing has been committed in Israel and in Jerusalem: Judah has desecrated the sanctuary the LORD loves, by marrying the daughter of a foreign god. (Malachi 2:11)

This passage operates on two levels: as a metaphor for idolatry and as a description of literal reality.

**Metaphorically**, marrying a daughter of a foreign god represents spiritual unfaithfulness—abandoning the exclusive worship of Israel's God.

**Practically**, the Israelites were actually marrying non-believers. Historical records confirm this:

> The people of Israel, including the priests and the Levites, have not kept themselves separate from the neighboring peoples with their detestable practices... They have taken some of their daughters as wives for themselves and their sons, and have mingled the holy race with the peoples around them. (Ezra 9:1-2)

> Men of Judah had married women from Ashdod, Ammon and Moab. Half of their children spoke the language of Ashdod or the language of one of the other peoples, and did not know how to speak the language of Judah. (Nehemiah 13:23-24)

The pattern repeats throughout history. King Solomon married foreign women to create political alliances and treaties. While interracial marriage itself is not wrong, the problem arose when each foreign wife brought her own gods and idols. Over time, Solomon abandoned the faith of his fathers and followed them.

Most worldly religions are syncretistic: "Your god is fine. My god is fine. Let's just all get along." This approach sounds tolerant, but it represents a fundamental rejection of God's exclusive claim.

### The Application Today

If you're considering marriage, think Christianly. An unbeliever won't typically demand you abandon your faith. Instead, they'll say, "I'm cool with whatever you believe. You believe what you want, I'll believe what I want." Most people hold syncretistic beliefs—they don't care much about truth and are happy to accept whatever works for them.

But you belong to an exclusive God:

> You shall have no other gods before me. (Exodus 20:3)

> I am a jealous God. (Exodus 20:5)

Your God does not want you in a marriage relationship with an unbeliever. This isn't legalism; it's protection. In ancient times, fathers arranged marriages for their children. While we don't practice arranged marriages today, God as your Father still claims His right to guide this crucial decision. **Do not marry an unbeliever.**

### Second: Divorce (v.14)

The second way they broke faith is found in verse 14:

> You ask, "Why?" It is because the LORD is acting as the witness between you and the wife of your youth, because you have broken faith with her, though she is your partner, the wife of your marriage covenant. (Malachi 2:14)

When a couple divorces, they break the covenant they made before God. God Himself serves as the witness and enforcer of that covenant. He will act.

> "I hate divorce," says the LORD God of Israel, "and I hate a man's covering himself with violence as well as with his garment," says the LORD Almighty. So guard yourself in your spirit, and do not break faith. (Malachi 2:16)

### Understanding God's Hatred of Divorce

When the Bible says God "hates" divorce, this is the stronger meaning—not merely rejection, but hatred of what is evil. God hates sin. He is not a God of love only; He is also a God of justice and holiness.

Modern culture has distorted "love" to justify almost anything: same-sex marriage because of love, polygamy because of love. "If it's done out of love," we hear, "it must be acceptable." But love does not determine morality—God does.

Some divorced parents tell their children, "Your mother and I love you very much, even though we're divorcing." This may be true emotionally, but divorce is not an act of love. It is an act of violence.

### The Violence of Divorce to Children

We often think of divorce as merely the end of a relationship between consenting adults. But we forget the children involved. Divorce rips children apart from their families. They lose daily access to one parent and are separated from grandparents, aunts, uncles, and cousins—the extended family that should surround them.

As the saying goes, "It takes a village to raise a child." Divorce tears that village apart. When parents live in different countries, as in some cases, the distance becomes unbridgeable. The child suffers the violence of that separation.

This destruction of family structure undermines God's intention for marriage and children entirely.

## 3. Seeking Godly Offspring

> Has not the LORD made them one? In flesh and spirit they are his. And why one? Because he was seeking godly offspring. So guard yourself in your spirit, and do not break faith with the wife of your youth. (Malachi 2:15)

Why does God unite a man and woman in marriage? **Because He is seeking godly offspring.**

### The Seed of God: Jesus Christ

The immediate theological context reveals that "godly offspring" ultimately refers to the Messiah, the seed promised to Abraham:

> The promises were spoken to Abraham and to his seed. The Scripture does not say "and to seeds," meaning many people, but "and to your seed," meaning one person, who is Christ. (Galatians 3:15-16)

God promised Abraham that through his descendants would come blessing to all the earth. But this promise focused on one particular descendant: Jesus Christ. He is the one who would:

- Reverse the effects of sin
- Repair God's name
- Bring land, offspring, and blessing to Israel and through Israel to all the world
- Defeat Satan

The genealogies recorded in Matthew and Luke trace Jesus's lineage all the way back to Abraham. For centuries, each generation of Israelites waited, wondering: Is this the one? Is this the promised Messiah? Every child born was, in a sense, a lottery ticket—could this be the seed of God?

### Why Unfaithfulness Jeopardizes the Promise

Now we understand why God takes intermarriage and divorce so seriously. Both threaten the continuation of the line through which the Messiah would come:

**Intermarriage:** The child of unbelievers in foreign gods cannot be the Christ. The Messiah cannot come through a family that has abandoned the God of Israel.

**Divorce:** Family disunity fractures the genealogical line. When divorce is followed by remarriage to unbelievers, the family structure becomes so twisted and corrupted that it can no longer be traced to the Messiah.

The punishment makes logical sense:

> As for the man who does this, whoever he may be, may the LORD cut him off from the tents of Jacob—even though he brings offerings to the LORD Almighty. (Malachi 2:12)

If someone's bloodline became so complicated and twisted that the promised seed could not come through them, they were cut off from the covenant. The promise—the lottery ticket—was taken away. They were no longer participating in God's project.

## 4. Messaging the World Through Parents and Children

Today, the promise of Christ has been fulfilled. Jesus has come. He has stomped on Satan's head and purchased salvation for all who believe. We are no longer under the old covenant, waiting for the Messiah.

But God has given us a new project, spoken of earlier in Malachi:

> My name will be great among the nations, from the rising to the setting of the sun. In every place incense and pure offerings will be brought to my name, because my name will be great among the nations. (Malachi 1:11)

And it's hard to believe, but children remain integral to this project. We are still seeking godly offspring—not to produce the Messiah, but to advance His kingdom.

This involves both qualitative and quantitative dimensions.

### Qualitative: Custodians of Truth

As we proclaim the gospel to our children, they become the gospel proclaimers to the next generation. They are custodians of God's truth, carrying it forward.

The gospel is one generation away from extinction. If we fail to pass our faith to our children, the project fails.

**To parents:** This is both a sobering responsibility and a privilege. Your job is hard, and I'm not criticizing. But you must actively choose God for your children while you still have influence.

Consider the choices you make: piano lessons or Bible reading? Badminton or Scripture class? The best secular school or a school that teaches God's Word?

The principle is simple: **Choose Bible, not badminton. Choose Scripture, not secular alternatives. Choose church, not concerts.**

Make it easy for your children to follow Christ, not harder. This means:

- Choosing their friends wisely
- Monitoring their media
- Being regular at church
- Finding a youth group they can belong to
- Removing obstacles that prevent them from engaging with God's people
- Not delegating their spiritual formation to TV, school, or strangers

I recently made choices about my young daughter's activities. Piano, ballet, violin—these are good things. But when they consume so much time that there's no room for Scripture, or when they conflict with church attendance or Christian fellowship, they become obstacles. As her father, I have a responsibility to help her choose God by removing those distractions.

**To young people:** Be the godly offspring God wants you to be. Form prayer groups at your school. Your generation can make a difference in messaging the world. Think about three friends right now. Write their names down. Pray for them at home, at school, at youth group. When your parents "force" you to church or restrict certain TV shows and movies, they're protecting you. That's love.

### Quantitative: Population Growth

Beyond the quality of our witness, there's a quantitative dimension: children are the population of tomorrow.

The Church grows not only through conversion but also through biological growth. We can increase the number of godly offspring.

This has happened before. In the fourth century, Christianity transformed from a small Jewish sect to the official religion of the Roman Empire. How? **Because Christians out-birthed pagans.**

The Romans practiced infanticide and abortion openly. Exposure of unwanted female infants and deformed male infants was legal and socially acceptable across all classes. Julius Caesar eventually had to legislate land grants to fathers of three or more children. Augustus offered political preference and financial benefits to men with three or more children, while imposing sanctions on the childless. Even these incentives failed. The Roman population declined.

Meanwhile, Christians rejected these practices. Following their God—who is a Father of orphans, who calls little ones to come to Him—Christians:

- Valued children as blessings, not burdens
- Refused abortion
- Reproduced

The result was exponential growth:

| Year | Estimated Christians | Percent of Population |
|------|----------------------|----------------------|
| 40   | 1,000               | 0.0017%             |
| 100  | 7,530               | 0.0126%             |
| 200  | 217,795             | 0.36%               |
| 250  | 1,171,356           | 1.9%                |
| 300  | 6,299,832           | 10.5%               |
| 350  | 33,882,008          | 56.5%               |

In just three centuries, Christianity grew from 0.002% to over 50% of the Roman Empire—not primarily through conversion, but through families having children.

Consider this: The Asian Christian population is around 10%. If that growth rate continues for 50 years, Christians could represent a significant majority simply through biological growth.

We live in a unique moment. Governments are offering cash incentives for having children (childcare subsidies, family benefits). Meanwhile, secular society is choosing not to have children. We have both the economic support and the demographic opportunity to influence nations.

**To those considering marriage:**

- Don't intermarry. Marry a believer.
- Think about biological growth. Be pro-children.
- Young women: Turn your hearts from what the world tells you. Find good fathers for your children.
- Young men: Find mothers for your children.
- Have as many children as the Lord provides.
- Outgrow the pagans. This is a long-term solution to kingdom growth.

This is a team effort. Some families are already doing their part. Hanson and Vinci, be ready. Set up your homes for children—buy secondhand furniture because kids will wreck it. Live counter-culturally. Live for the kingdom.

## Conclusion

This teaching is for everyone in this congregation. To those busy with other things, those who feel inconvenienced by these demands: You are the ones God is calling. The power is in your hands.

To the young people and teenagers—especially those not yet thinking about marriage: You are the next generation. You can be the godly offspring through whom God messages the world. One day, I hope to look back and see what God has done through you.

When your parents enforce church attendance, when they limit certain entertainment, when they make choices that feel restrictive—remember that it comes from love. They are protecting your spiritual future.

The choices you make about marriage, children, and faithfulness matter. They matter not just for your family, but for God's kingdom purposes. As you honor God in your relationships, you participate in His work to make His name great among all nations.
