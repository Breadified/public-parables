# 2 Timothy 4:1-8

**Title:** What Are You Chasing?  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Opening: The Question Before Us

Just recently, a 17-year-old girl named Sophia Park became the youngest person ever to pass the bar exam—the certification test required to become a lawyer. It's a remarkable achievement, the kind that makes every Asian parent's heart swell with pride.

But what struck me most was the cost. When interviewed, Sophia explained that being a lawyer had been her lifelong dream. For the past four years, she essentially speedran high school, college, and law school combined. She took extra credits, attended night classes, and worked part-time at a law office to gain hands-on experience. Every choice she made was sacrificed for one singular goal.

The question this raises is simple, yet profound: **What are you chasing after?** What is worth the sacrifice of your time, your energy, your life?

As we turn to 2 Timothy, God's Word speaks directly to this. Paul writes his final letter before his execution by the Roman Emperor Nero, and as he passes his last words of wisdom to his young friend Timothy, his deepest concern is this: *Will Timothy run after the right things in life?*

Paul illustrates two contrasting directions a person can choose. Today, we'll explore both—and discover which path truly leads to victory.

---

## Point 1: Living for a Lie

The first lifestyle Paul describes is one built on falsehood.

> **2 Timothy 4:3-4** For the time will come when people will not put up with sound doctrine. Instead, to suit their own desires, they will gather around them a great number of teachers to say what their itching ears want to hear.

Notice Paul's language about "desires." In the original Greek, this word means *lusts*—raw, consuming passions. Paul compares this to a wild animal that cannot be reasoned with. An animal simply chases its appetite, its impulses, without thought or reflection.

That's the picture Paul paints of human nature when left unchecked. We naturally gravitate toward living according to our impulses rather than listening to how God calls us to live. We seek out voices that scratch our itching ears—teachers and influences that affirm what we already want to believe rather than challenge us to something higher.

### Living for Yourself: The Modern Myth

Look around our world today. In the West, society whispers a seductive lie: *Life is all about you. Your individual wants. Your individual desires.* This self-centered thinking has seeped into all of us more deeply than we'd like to admit.

For some, it manifests as the chase for worldly success. We pursue prestigious degrees—law, medicine, engineering—not primarily because we want to serve others, but because we crave the approval and admiration that comes with these titles. How many young people are driven by the validation of telling relatives, "I'm studying law"?

For others, it's the pursuit of comfort and pleasure. We save and plan for exotic vacations, for that Instagram-worthy moment where we can announce to the world that we've "discovered ourselves" abroad.

Still others structure their entire lives around romantic intimacy. Notice how we speak of relationships: *I'm looking for my other half. Someone to complete me.* When marriage loses its spark, divorce seems acceptable because the other person isn't meeting our expectations anymore. Don't we see our selfishness reflected in how disposable we've made commitment?

**All of these are different expressions of the same lie: making life about you.**

### The Trap of "Itching Ears"

When people refuse to hear truth and instead chase selfish desires, Paul tells us they turn toward *myths*—fantasies we invent to justify our lifestyle.

Think about the empty slogans we're sold on social media:
- "Live your best life now"
- "Chase your dreams"
- "Get the bag"
- "Never believe anyone who says you don't deserve what you want"

They sound inspiring, don't they? But they're hollow. They're not grounded in any transcendent truth. They're modern-day myths designed to affirm our selfishness.

**The result? Emptiness.**

Consider King Solomon, the wisest king who ever lived. In the book of Ecclesiastes, he decided to test this philosophy himself—to truly "live his best life now."

> **Ecclesiastes 2:10-11** I denied myself nothing my eyes desired; I refused my heart no pleasure... Yet when I surveyed all that my hands had done and what I had toiled to achieve, everything was meaningless, a chasing after the wind; nothing was gained under the sun.

Solomon had everything. Whatever desire crossed his mind in the morning, he possessed the wealth and power to satisfy it. He built whatever he wanted. He took whatever beautiful woman caught his eye. He purchased every treasure and artifact his heart craved. 

And after scratching every possible itch, his conclusion was devastating: *It was all meaningless.*

Why? Because Solomon realized that death makes a mockery of every earthly acquisition. He could take nothing with him. Money, houses, reputation—they all blow away like wind. These things the world urges us to chase are *temporary*. They have no meaning in eternity.

### A Life Without Direction

I once bumped into an old friend at the metro. When I asked about her future, she told me she'd received a job offer at a law firm. Her plan? Climb to partnership, become a name partner, then retire early.

"What will you do when you retire?" I asked.

She paused. "I dunno. Take a trip around the world, I guess. And then... that's it."

I felt genuinely sad hearing those words. *Is this really how you want your life to count?*

Imagine standing before God after you die. He asks you to give an account of your life—how did you spend the time I gave you? And your answer is: *I retired early and took some vacations.*

What a waste.

Yet this is the fantasy our world peddles to us daily. Through social media, through influencers, through the voices we surround ourselves with—we're constantly being sold a lie about what makes life worth living.

### A Critical Question for You

So I ask you honestly: **Have you bought into the lies of our world? Are you living for your own selfish desires?**

In our social media-saturated age, we must be vigilant about what we consume. When you scroll through Instagram reels and TikTok clips, ask yourself: *What are these trying to sell me?* Most of what we consume is designed to sell an idealized lifestyle where people "win at life" by chasing their own desires. If you spend hours consuming this content, what effect will it have on your thinking, your priorities, your plans for the future?

And what about the real people in your life? Do you only listen to those who tell you to "live your best life now," who scratch your itching ears? Or do you have older, wiser Christians around you—not just peers, but mentors—who will tell you the hard truth?

**Don't buy into the selfish life. You can't live for a lie.**

## Point 2: Living for the Truth

So what *is* worth pursuing if not your own desires? What's the alternative to this empty chase?

Paul's answer: **Live for the truth.**

> **2 Timothy 4:1-2** In the presence of God and of Christ Jesus, who will judge the living and the dead, and in view of his appearing and his kingdom, I give you this charge: Preach the word.

Calling God Himself as witness, Paul charges Timothy to build his entire life around the truth of God's Word—specifically, the gospel message of Jesus Christ.

This gospel tells us that Jesus came to earth and died for our sins, removing the separation between us and God that our selfishness created. It tells us He rose from the dead to guarantee eternal life for those who believe. And it tells us He will return to judge both the living and the dead.

**Friends, this is the only truth with eternal consequences. This is the only thing that will never fade away.**

Have you believed this? Have you turned away from everything that won't satisfy you and placed your trust in the only message that overcomes death itself?

Paul believed this truth about Jesus, and it transformed his entire life. Now he wants Timothy—and us—to do the same. To shape our entire existence around the message of the gospel.

### Being a Soldier for Christ

While others around him chase myths and selfish desires, Timothy receives explicit orders:

> **2 Timothy 4:5** Keep your head in all situations, endure hardship, do the work of an evangelist, discharge all the duties of your ministry.

Like a general issuing marching orders to the next generation of soldiers, Paul commands Timothy: *No matter what everyone else does, you are to preach the truth of Jesus Christ.* This is his mission. It's the same mission we carry today.

Earlier in 2 Timothy 2, Paul develops this military metaphor:

> **2 Timothy 2:3-4** Join with me in suffering, like a good soldier of Christ Jesus. No one serving as a soldier gets entangled in civilian affairs, but rather tries to please his commanding officer.

A soldier can't simply do whatever he feels like. He's committed to a mission. He must live for a purpose higher than his own comfort—and sometimes that means making sacrifices.

### The Power of a Life Lived Differently

**Illustration: Oskar Schindler**

During the Holocaust, a wealthy German factory owner named Oskar Schindler made an extraordinary choice. Over five years, he spent his entire fortune helping Jewish people escape the Nazi regime. He purchased supplies and food for those hiding in his factory. He paid bribes to keep Nazi officials from discovering what he was doing. He supported the underground Jewish resistance.

By the end, he was penniless. He willingly gave up everything for a greater purpose.

That's the mentality we need as Christians. To surrender our own comfort and live for the greater purpose of sharing Jesus with the world. To be a light in the darkness for those still trapped in selfishness.

**Are you living for the truth?**

### How Truth Changes Everything

Your witness is more powerful than you realize. A study conducted years ago asked what the primary reason was for people converting to Christianity. It wasn't compelling arguments. It wasn't mystical experiences. **It was seeing other Christians live radically differently because of the gospel.**

People notice when you're living for the truth.

They notice when you're at a party and you're the only one who stays sober.

They notice when you sacrifice your weekend to serve at youth group.

They notice when you love people selflessly, expecting nothing in return.

These things stand out. They start conversations. They make people wonder if there's something real, something worth following, in the Christian message.

A friend from university only approached me about Jesus when he noticed I didn't swear. That simple difference sparked a conversation that changed his eternity.

**The time is now. You are on active duty.** If you believe in the truth of the gospel, you are a soldier for Christ. In every aspect of your life—your lifestyle, your language, your choices—you must live in a way that demonstrates your faith in Jesus.

## Point 3: Win the Crown

It sounds like hard work. I know. And if you live this way, you will make sacrifices your non-Christian friends won't make. It won't look like you're winning on the outside.

But in the end, it will be worth it. God guarantees it.

> **2 Timothy 4:6-8** For I am already being poured out like a drink offering, and the time for my departure is near. I have fought the good fight, I have finished the race, I have kept the faith. Now there is in store for me the crown of righteousness, which the Lord, the righteous Judge, will award to me on that day.

Paul offers his entire life as a sacrifice for the message of Jesus. If you've read Acts, you know the cost: whipping, imprisonment, stoning, expulsion from cities, starvation, beatings, shipwreck, false trials. Everything.

Yet Paul sounds triumphant, victorious! Why? Because he knows what awaits him: **the crown of righteousness.**

In ancient Rome and Greece, athletic competitions were sacred events. When the Olympics crowned a victor, they placed a wreath of flowers on the winner's head—the ultimate recognition of excellence, proof that you had run the race well.

Paul uses this imagery to describe what awaits him in heaven. An eternal crown, not of perishable flowers, but awarded by God Himself on heaven's podium. An imperishable reward for faithfulness.

This promise isn't just for Paul. Verse 8 extends it to all who have longed for Jesus's return—to every Christian throughout history who has trusted in Him, run their race well, and kept their faith to the end.

**We will not lose. There is delayed gratification coming.**

### The Long Game

**Illustration: Black Friday**

Imagine you own an old, broken-down laptop. It makes weird noises, it's covered in coffee stains, it's barely holding on. Meanwhile, all your friends have beautiful new MacBooks. It's frustrating.

But you wait. Weeks pass. Months pass. Then finally, Black Friday arrives, and you snag that laptop at 50% off. In that moment, all the waiting, all the frustration—it's instantly worth it.

The Christian life works the same way. We have our orders. We'll fight our whole lives to stay faithful to Christ, to preach the gospel. Sometimes it will feel like it's not worth it.

But when we finish our race, all will be made right.

Imagine this: You kneel before the God of heaven and earth. All the angels, all believers throughout history, bear witness. And the Almighty, in all His majesty, looks at you and says: *"Well done. Well done, good and faithful servant."*

There are no words in the English language to capture the joy of that moment.

Every hardship, every sacrifice you made in Jesus's name will become a distant memory as you step into eternity and reign with Christ forever.

**It will all have been worth it.**

---

## Conclusion

If Sophia Park could dedicate her life so completely to something as temporary as becoming a lawyer, how much more should we Christians strive for an eternal reward?

Life is incredibly short. It passes in a blink of an eye. Eight years ago, I was in your position. Time moves faster than you can imagine.

So please—don't waste your life chasing the selfish lifestyle the world sells you.

**Live for Jesus. Preach His message. Be faithful to God's Word to the very end. And win the crown.**
