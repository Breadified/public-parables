# Mark 9:33-41; 10:45

**Title:** Coming First in God's Eyes Means Sacrificial Service  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew  
**Date:** October 24, 2003

## The Question: Who Is Coming First?

We all know what "coming first" means in the everyday world. It's the question asked in classrooms about grades, on soccer fields about speed, in playgrounds about strength, among girls about popularity. It's the universal human desire to know: who ranks first? Who's at the top of the pecking order?

The disciples wrestling with this same question—and they were so ashamed of it that when Jesus asked what they'd been arguing about on the road, they kept silent. Yet their silence revealed the truth: they had been debating who among them was the greatest. Was it Peter? Judas? Did they count alphabetically? Each disciple had his own hope of standing first.

But Jesus' answer would completely redefine what "first" means.

## Jesus Redefines Greatness: Coming First = Sacrificial Service

> **Mark 9:35** "Sitting down, Jesus called the Twelve and said, 'If anyone wants to be first, he must be the very last, and the servant of all.'"

Jesus doesn't reject the desire to be first—He transforms it. The disciples imagined greatness in terms of power, reputation, and recognition. But Jesus teaches that coming first in God's eyes means coming last in this world. Not third from last. Not second from last. But *the very last*.

It means being at the absolute bottom of the ladder. Not merely a servant—because sometimes servants have servants too—but *the servant of all*. At the very bottom of the pecking order, serving everyone.

To illustrate this radical reversal, Jesus brought a little child to stand among them:

> **Mark 9:36-37** "He took a little child and had him stand among them. Taking him in his arms, he said to them, 'Whoever welcomes one of these little children in my name welcomes me; and whoever welcomes me does not welcome me but the one who sent me.'"

In that culture, in those times, children—especially small children—held the lowest position in the family. They brought no income, performed no important work, and occupied little status. Yet Jesus holds up the child as the model of what it means to welcome the kingdom of God.

### The Hard Truth About Greatness

To be first in God's eyes, you must be willing to be last in the world's eyes. And here's the impossible part: **you cannot be first in God's eyes without sacrificial service.** There is no shortcut, no alternative path to true greatness before Him.

We will be judged not by our yearly rankings or worldly achievements, but by our sacrificial service. This is what taking up your cross and following Jesus means. It's difficult, yes. But it's the way of the kingdom.

## Jesus Proves His Point: Jesus Sacrificially Serves

Jesus doesn't merely teach this principle—He embodies it completely.

> **Mark 10:45** "For even the Son of Man did not come to be served, but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many."

Think about it: Jesus came to earth as King of the universe. He is the highest in the order of all things. Yet no one served Him a feast in Parliament House. No one rolled out the red carpet. Instead of being served, He served others. And He didn't merely serve—He gave His life as a ransom for all of us.

Consider the contrast with earthly power: When President Bush visited Australia not long ago, he arrived with 250 guards to serve him, a special plane, pilots at his command, invitations to Parliament, and important people eager to wait on him. The Prime Minister even hosted a barbecue in his honor.

Jesus, though infinitely greater than any earthly ruler, humbled Himself. He asked nothing for Himself. He served. He gave His life. He went to the cross. And it was in that act of ultimate sacrificial service that God the Father raised Him from the dead, gave Him the highest name, and exalted Him to the place of honor in heaven.

This is what makes Jesus truly great. This is what winning looks like in God's kingdom.

## Application: Follow Jesus in Sacrificial Service

The world will tell you that winning means being served, being admired, being first. You might lose by the world's standards if you choose the way of sacrificial service. But Jesus has already gone before us. He's proven that this is not a hollow ideal or an impossible gimmick. Sacrificial service is the ultimate way of living. It is the way to come first in God's eyes.

Consider where you might serve sacrificially:

- **At home:** Are you willing to serve your family through obedience, even when it costs you? Do you help your siblings selflessly?
- **At school:** Do you reach out to the weak and friendless? Do you serve your classmates with generosity?
- **In your community:** How can you be last so that others might be lifted up?

## Conclusion

Friends, greatness in God's eyes has nothing to do with being the prettiest, the strongest, or the smartest. True greatness—coming first in God's kingdom—is sacrificial service. This is the standard Jesus set, and this is the way He walked.

I encourage you to follow Jesus and to serve sacrificially, knowing that He has already walked this road and proven its worth.
