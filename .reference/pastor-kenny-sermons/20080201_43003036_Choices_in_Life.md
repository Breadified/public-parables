# John 3:36

**Title:** Choices in Life
**Date:** 8 February 2008
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## A Fresh Start

Today marks the Lunar New Year—a celebration observed across many Asian countries. For the Chinese, we welcome the Year of the Rat, and the festivities have already begun with dinners and red packets exchanged. In Australia and many other places, this gives us something wonderful: two fresh starts in one year. We get to renew our New Year's resolutions in February, after we've already broken them in January! It's a gift—a chance to recommit and begin again.

## Choices Ahead

As we enter this new year, many of you will face significant choices. For Year 7 students, there are new schools, new people, new subjects, and decisions about buses and friendships. Year 10 students must choose sports, subjects for next year, and perhaps romantic interests. Year 12 students face university course decisions. Beyond academics, there are countless daily choices: which clubs to join, where to spend time at school, whether to push hard in your studies.

But among all these choices—some trivial, some important—there is one choice that matters most. It's the choice we want to present to you today through this lunchtime group, Soul Purpose. It's a choice for everyone here, whether you're young or old, junior or senior.

This choice is found in one verse in the Bible: **John 3:36**.

> Whoever believes in the Son has eternal life, but whoever rejects the Son will not see life, for God's wrath remains on him.

Notice the structure: two choices, marked by those two "whoevers."
- Whoever believes in the Son
- Whoever rejects the Son

## Understanding the Choice

What does this belief involve? It has two parts:

**First: Recognizing the Problem**

We must believe that we have all sinned and fallen short. We've done wrong—in God's eyes and often in the eyes of those around us. Because of this wrong, the wrath of God (the penalty) remains on us. This isn't comfortable to acknowledge, but it's the starting point.

**Second: Receiving the Solution**

The only way out is through rescue by Jesus, the Son of God. He paid the penalty we deserve by dying on our behalf. Through His sacrifice, we can be forgiven by God and restored to Him.

So the choice is stark: Will you believe in Jesus, who died to save you? Or will you reject Him?

## Why This Choice Matters

Some choices in life are trivial—Snickers versus M&Ms, for example. Who cares, as long as you get chocolate? But this choice carries eternal consequences.

**Eternal Life vs. God's Wrath**

If you believe in the Son, you gain eternal life. Think about what makes life worth living right now: the freshness of the air after rain, family holidays, games with friends, a movie night, acceptance into your dream school, Saturday at McDonald's, the joy of red packets. Imagine experiencing all of that goodness—and more—forever in eternity. Now imagine the opposite: having none of it. That is the weight of this choice.

## A Word to Those Listening

Some of you have already made the choice to believe in Jesus. If so, I encourage you: continue to work out your faith. Keep deciding, day by day, to follow Jesus.

Others haven't made this choice yet. I want to challenge you: make it your resolution this year. Come to a decision about Jesus. Will you believe in the One who died to save you, or will you reject Him?

Either way, Soul Purpose is here to help you through our talks and Bible studies.

## The Year Ahead

We live in a season the world calls "the Year of the Rat." But viewed through Jesus' eyes, this is something different. This is the year of the choice—to believe or to reject. That's what really defines our time.
