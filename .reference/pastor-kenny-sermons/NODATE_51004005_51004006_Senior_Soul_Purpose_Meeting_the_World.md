# Colossians 4:5-6

**Title:** Senior Soul Purpose - Meeting the World  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Scripture

> Colossians 4:2-6  
> Devote yourselves to prayer, being watchful and thankful. And pray for us, too, that God may open a door for our message, so that we may proclaim the mystery of Christ, for which I am in chains. Pray that I may proclaim it clearly, as I should. Be wise in the way you act toward outsiders; make the most of every opportunity. Let your conversation be always full of grace, seasoned with salt, so that you may know how to answer everyone.

## Foundation: Saved and Called to Good Works

Last week we established two foundational truths for the Christian life:

**First, we are saved from God's future wrath.** As Paul writes in Ephesians, "Because of his great love for us, God, who is rich in mercy, made us alive with Christ even when we were dead in transgressions—it is by grace you have been saved."

**Second, we are called to be people who act rightly and do good works.** The grace of God that brings salvation teaches us to say "No" to ungodliness and worldly passions, and to live self-controlled, upright and godly lives in this present age, while we wait for the blessed hope—the glorious appearing of our great God and Savior, Jesus Christ, who gave himself for us to redeem us from all wickedness and to purify for himself a people that are his very own, eager to do what is good.

**But how do we know what is righteous and good?** Scripture itself is our guide. As Paul tells Timothy, "All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness, so that the man of God may be thoroughly equipped for every good work."

## Meeting the World: How Should We Act?

As we live out righteousness and good works, we inevitably meet the world. Paul gives us four clear directives for how we should engage:

- **Be wise in action** (v. 5) — Our behavior toward outsiders must reflect discernment and prudence
- **Use every opportunity** (v. 5) — We are to make the most of every occasion to live out our faith
- **Be gracious in words** (v. 6) — Our conversation should always be full of grace
- **Speak clearly** (v. 4) — When we proclaim the gospel, we must speak with clarity

## The Bridge: From Good Works to Good Words

This raises an important question: **Are our good works leading to good words? Why or why not?**

The connection between our conduct and our communication is vital. Our righteous living opens doors for our message. People notice when our actions align with our words. Yet sometimes our good works can remain silent—a witness without explanation, helpfulness without gospel. The challenge is ensuring that the integrity of our lives naturally flows into conversations that give reason for the hope we possess.
