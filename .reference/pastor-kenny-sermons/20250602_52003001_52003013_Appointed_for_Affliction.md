# 1 Thessalonians 3:1-13

**Title:** Appointed for Affliction  
**Date:** June 2, 2025  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## The Paradox of Joy in Suffering

At first glance, this passage seems contradictory. Paul speaks of affliction and suffering, yet the underlying message is one of deep joy. How can we be happy when facing trials? The answer lies in understanding what comes after perseverance through suffering—true, unshakeable joy.

## Paul's Concern for the Thessalonians

> So when we could stand it no longer, we thought it best to be left by ourselves in Athens. We sent Timothy, who is our brother and co-worker in God's service in spreading the gospel of Christ, to strengthen and encourage you in your faith. (1 Thessalonians 3:1-2)

Paul had left the Thessalonian church amid persecution. His deep love for them compelled him to send Timothy—his trusted companion—to strengthen their faith during these trials. This wasn't a casual visit; it was a pastoral response to real suffering.

## The Nature of Christian Suffering

> So that no one would be unsettled by these trials. For you know quite well that we are destined for them. In fact, when we were with you, we kept telling you that we would be persecuted. And it turned out that way, as you well know. For this reason, when I could stand it no longer, I sent to find out about your faith. I was afraid that in some way the tempter had tempted you and that our labors might have been in vain. (1 Thessalonians 3:3-5)

### Why Do Christians Suffer?

**Persecution from the World:** There are those who resist the Gospel's central claim—that we are sinners in need of saving grace. This message threatens their worldview, so they attack and oppose those who proclaim it.

As Paul writes in 2 Timothy 3:12: *"In fact, everyone who wants to live a godly life in Christ Jesus will be persecuted."*

**Suffering as a Teacher:** Beyond external persecution, God uses affliction to teach us obedience. Even Jesus learned through suffering: *"Son though he was, he learned obedience from what he suffered"* (Hebrews 5:8).

If our Lord was not exempted from suffering's refining work, neither are we. Obedience itself is often painful—doing homework, completing chores, submitting to authority, listening to God. The pain of obedience is ultimately a teacher, not a punishment.

**The Choice Before Us:** Life contains pain regardless of our choices. We can experience pain as a consequence of disobedience, or pain as a vehicle for growth through obedience. When we choose obedience, our suffering becomes purposeful—a pathway to maturity and deeper faith.

## Joy After Standing Firm

> But Timothy has just now come to us from you and has brought good news about your faith and love. He has told us that you always have pleasant memories of us and that you long to see us, just as we also long to see you. Therefore, brothers and sisters, in all our distress and persecution we were encouraged about you because of your faith. For now we really live, since you are standing firm in the Lord. How can we thank God enough for you in return for all the joy we have in the presence of our God because of you? Night and day we pray most earnestly that we may see you again and supply what is lacking in your faith. (1 Thessalonians 3:6-10)

Timothy returned with transformative news: the Thessalonians were standing firm. Their faith had not crumbled. Their love remained active. And in hearing this, Paul's own joy was complete.

Notice the progression: *"For now we really live, since you are standing firm in the Lord."* Paul's deepest gladness came not from comfort or ease, but from witnessing persevering faith. This is the key insight—joy comes *after* suffering, *after* standing firm.

### Why Joy Requires Suffering

We cannot fully appreciate what we haven't lacked. Without suffering, we cannot comprehend joy in its fullest sense. This is why the wealthiest nations often struggle with the highest rates of depression and mental illness—without contrast, without comparison to real hardship, people lose the ability to recognize their blessings.

Those who have faced genuine hardship and come through it understand joy in a way that comfort alone cannot teach. They know what they've survived. They know what could have been lost. This awareness births genuine, profound gratitude.

### Joy Comes After...

True, lasting joy follows several crucial developments:

- **Suffering endured** with faith and perseverance
- **Standing firm** in conviction and commitment
- **Love demonstrated** for one another and for all
- **Unity found** despite our differences, recognizing we have far more in common than what divides us
- **Understanding God's love** poured out for us
- **Receiving Christ's sacrifice** on our behalf

## Don't Take Each Other for Granted

When Paul returns to the church and sees them still standing, his exhortation is clear: recognize the miracle of your continued faith. Appreciate one another. Don't assume your brothers and sisters will always be there—they are standing through real opposition, real temptation, real cost.

## The Hope That Sustains Us

Our happiness is not built on temporary things—video games, school grades, material possessions. These fade and disappoint. Instead, our joy is anchored in what is eternally true and what is yet to come.

We persevere because we know that after this present suffering comes the fulfillment of our hope. We look forward to heaven, where there will be no more tears, no more sorrow—only unlimited joy in God's presence.

## Closing Prayer

> Now may our God and Father himself and our Lord Jesus clear the way for us to come to you. May the Lord make your love increase and overflow for each other and for everyone else, just as ours does for you. May he strengthen your hearts so that you will be blameless and holy in the presence of our God and Father when our Lord Jesus comes with all his holy ones. (1 Thessalonians 3:11-13)

Paul's final words are a prayer for perseverance to the end—that their love would grow, their hearts would be strengthened, and they would stand blameless when Christ returns. This is the Christian's true joy: standing firm until the end, together with all God's holy ones.
