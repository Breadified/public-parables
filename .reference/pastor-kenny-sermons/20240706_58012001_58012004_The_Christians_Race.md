### Unusual Races Around the World

The Olympics features many unusual competitions beyond the marathon. In Gloucester, England, Cooper's Hill hosts an annual Cheese Roll—a massive cylindrical wheel of cheese is released down a steep slope, reaching speeds of 100 km/h while people chase it. The reward is the cheese itself, though the costs are high: broken bones, sprains, concussions, and lost teeth.

Another peculiar event is the Man vs. Horse Marathon that began in Wales in 1980, born from a pub debate about whether humans or horses could better endure cross-country running. After 25 years, one man finally defeated a horse at the 22-mile mark. Only one other human has since accomplished this feat.

In North Carolina, there's the Krispy Kreme Challenge: runners start at NC State's bell tower, run 2.5 miles to the Krispy Kreme store, eat a dozen donuts, and return to the tower—all in under an hour. It's a student-organized charity race benefiting the North Carolina Children's Hospital.

As we come to today's Scripture, we encounter another race—the Christian's race.

## 1. The Christian's Race

Scripture uses many metaphors to describe the Christian journey: the soil types from the Parable of the Sower, a building under construction, a battle, a growing tree. Each points to a specific aspect of faith. But when it comes to perseverance in Christ, perhaps "race" is the most fitting metaphor.

> **Hebrews 12:1** Therefore, since we are surrounded by such a great cloud of witnesses, let us throw off everything that hinders and the sin that so easily entangles. And let us run with perseverance the race marked out for us.

### The Race Has Boundaries

Like any race, there's a marked route. Whether flagged in a marathon or lined on a track, there are clear boundaries. And there's a finish line.

> **1 Corinthians 9:24-27** Do you not know that in a race all the runners run, but only one gets the prize? Run in such a way as to get the prize. Everyone who competes in the games goes into strict training. They do it to get a crown that will not last, but we do it to get a crown that will last forever. Therefore I do not run like someone running aimlessly; I do not fight like a boxer beating the air.

> **2 Timothy 4:7** I have fought the good fight, I have finished the race, I have kept the faith.

> **Philippians 3:14** I press on toward the goal to win the prize for which God has called me heavenward in Christ Jesus.

There is a goal. An end. So don't just jog casually or run without direction. There's a marked path, and we must follow the rules.

> **2 Timothy 2:5** Similarly, anyone who competes as an athlete does not receive the victor's crown except by competing according to the rules.

You cannot cheat. There are no shortcuts. But often, races are won at the start.

### A Good Beginning

Beginning well requires two things:

**First, we must know the truth.** We learn the Gospel—that Jesus died and rose for us. We understand there are two ways to live. We trust in what Christ accomplished on the cross. We trust Scripture as God's final word to us.

## 2. Don't Get Tangled (Verse 1)

> **Hebrews 12:1** Let us throw off everything that hinders and the sin that so easily entangles.

### Shed Your Burden

In a race, you need light equipment. Marathon runners wear minimal clothing—just a shirt and shorts. Running shoes become lighter each year. Runners obsess over reducing even milligrams of weight, conserving energy for distance rather than carrying load. (Historically, ancient marathon runners competed naked.)

Think of a distance runner at the finish line versus someone dressed in heavy, restrictive clothing. The lean runner finishes strong; the heavily dressed cannot compete effectively.

Sin acts like those heavy garments—dragging us down and preventing us from running well.

### What Entangles Us

Sin includes:
- **Harmful habits** that distort our thinking, waste our energy, and steal our joy
- **Unhealthy relationships** that pull us away from commitment to Christ
- **Guilt over past sin** that chains us to yesterday

But here's the truth from Philippians 3:12-14: *"Not that I have already obtained all this, or have already arrived at my goal, but I press on to take hold of that for which Christ Jesus took hold of me... Forgetting what is behind and straining toward what is ahead, I press on toward the goal to win the prize for which God has called me heavenward in Christ Jesus."*

Put the past behind you. Don't carry yesterday's failures into today's race.

### Anything That Hinders

"Everything that hinders" isn't limited to sin alone. Many things aren't sinful in themselves but still weigh us down and distract us from our mission.

The Tortoise and the Hare teaches us this: the hare didn't lose because he was slow. He lost because he became distracted. He fell asleep.

We face many distractions in modern life:
- **Excess possessions.** In 1930, the average woman owned 36 clothing items. Today, we own 120 pieces, yet 80% go unworn. Experts say we need only 30 carefully chosen pieces. Our closets, like our garages, overflow with clutter we forget we own.

- **Cultural burdens.** We pursue overseas vacations because "everyone" does, collecting Instagram photos at the same landmarks in the same exotic locations. Yet we live in a land of natural beauty—snow, mountains, sand dunes, beaches. Why do we feel obligated to travel far when beauty surrounds us? Iowa's cornfields hold quiet beauty, but no one visits because there's no social currency in it.

- **Career paths and majors chosen because others choose them.** We follow our neighbors into certain fields not from genuine calling but from cultural pressure. These burdens cost us—financially, spiritually, professionally. They keep us from running our own race.

### Identify What Slows You Down

As you commit to following Jesus seriously, ask yourself: What tangles around me? What weighs me down?

Is it sin?

Is it distraction?

What hinders your ability to persevere?

Let us cast aside everything that hinders.

## 3. Keep Your Eyes on the Prize (Verse 2)

> **Hebrews 12:2** Let us fix our eyes on Jesus, the pioneer and perfecter of our faith. For the joy set before him he endured the cross, scorning its shame, and sat down at the right hand of the throne of God.

### Jesus: Beginning and End

Jesus is the "pioneer" (the one who starts something) and the "perfecter" (the one who completes it). He is both the beginning and the end of our faith.

Notice how Jesus endured the cross. The Greek word "anti" (for) is key here. In Hebrews, it always means "instead of" or "in place of." Jesus didn't endure the cross *and then* experience joy. He endured the cross *in place of* the joy set before him—or, endured it *for the sake of* future joy.

He looked beyond the present suffering to the future reward. He despised the shame because he kept his eyes on what lay ahead.

### Our Example

We face the same challenges Jesus faced:
- **Endure the cross** (present suffering)
- **Despise the shame** (what others think)
- **Endure opposition** (resistance and hostility)
- **Don't grow weary or lose heart**

We do this for:
- **The joy set before us** (future reward)
- **Rest and communion with God** (eternal dwelling)

### Where Are You Looking?

Distance runners know the importance of training their gaze to look ahead, beyond their current position. On open terrain, they focus on the horizon, not on competing runners around them.

In the long distance race of life, where do we fix our eyes?

Are we looking at others, comparing ourselves, following the crowd? Are we pursuing the wrong prize?

Consider the legend of Pheidippides, the Greek messenger who ran from Marathon to Athens in 490 BC to announce a military victory. He ran 40 kilometers, shedding weapons and clothing to lighten his load, then collapsed and died after crying out "We won!"

He ran toward Athens.

We run toward the New Jerusalem—God's city. We shed everything that hinders us from obtaining that prize.

## 4. Perseverance Through Community (Verse 1)

> **Hebrews 12:1** We are surrounded by such a great cloud of witnesses.

### The Power of Witnesses

Perseverance grows when we see others who've finished well. Fear and anxiety dissolve when we know someone we respect has done it. We realize it's not impossible.

The witnesses Paul mentions throughout Hebrews 11—Abel, Enoch, Noah, Abraham, Sarah, Isaac, Jacob, Moses—all finished their races. Their examples prove it's possible.

This is why community matters. We surround ourselves with people of faith, with mentors and examples who finished well. We don't just have accounts and accountants around us; we have Christians who persevered. We have evidence of what's possible.

### Run Your Own Race

Yet we must run the race marked out for *us*, not someone else's race. We can be encouraged by witnesses, but we cannot simply imitate their path. Our calling is unique. Our obstacles are our own.

The question remains: Are you running toward Jesus, or are you still tangled in what holds you back?
