# 1 Peter 2:11-17

**Title:** Living as Aliens: Good Works and Submission to Authority

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## Introduction: Christians in Hostile Contexts

Throughout history, Christians have faced persecution when living under oppressive regimes. In 1949, the People's Republic of China was established under Communist atheistic ideology. Religion was discouraged by the state, missionaries were expelled as "spiritual aggressors," and churches were placed under government control. During the Cultural Revolution (1966-1976), religious expression was effectively banned even in official churches. Believers were arrested, imprisoned, tortured, Bibles were destroyed, and churches were looted.

As Western democracies continue shifting away from Christian values, Peter's letter speaks directly to our situation: How should Christians live when facing societal hostility to our faith?

## Part 1: Creating Opportunities Through Good Works (v. 12)

### The Call to Good Lives

> **1 Peter 2:12a** "Live such good lives (having a good conduct) among the pagans (gentiles) that..."

Peter calls us to live morally good lives—not the "Aussie Dream" of comfort and entertainment, nor the "Asian Dream" of professional success and material wealth. Good lives mean moral goodness that is **visible** to those around us.

> **1 Peter 2:11** "Dear friends, I urge you, as aliens and strangers in the world, to abstain from sinful desires, which war against your soul."

Our goodness is not private piety but public moral action. It's the decisions we make, the values we live by, the way we treat others—all of which can be observed by our neighbors.

### Expected Hostility

> **1 Peter 2:12b** "Though they accuse you of doing wrong..."

Peter anticipates slander. Just as the Pharisees called good evil and evil good in their treatment of Jesus, so the watching world will misunderstand and malign your Christian convictions.

> **1 Peter 4:4** "They think it strange that you do not plunge with them into the same flood of dissipation, and they heap abuse on you."

Don't let their non-recognition discourage you. Their accusations don't make your good works any less good.

### The Ultimate Vindication

> **1 Peter 2:12d** "...they may see your good deeds and glorify God on the day (of visitation/oversight) he visits us."

This vindication is not immediate. On the day of judgment, when God returns, those who rejected you will have to acknowledge the truth you lived out. Your good works will be vindicated before all creation.

### How Good Works Create Openings

**Silencing ignorant talk:**

> **1 Peter 2:15** "For it is God's will that by doing good you should silence the ignorant talk of foolish men."

When you live consistently with integrity, you render slander ineffective. Stop being hypocritical. Be humble. Let people know you're a Christian. Good deeds nullify false accusations.

**Opening doors for the gospel:**

> **1 Peter 3:15** "But in your hearts set apart Christ as Lord. Always be prepared to give an answer to everyone who asks you to give the reason for the hope that you have. But do this with gentleness and respect."

Here's Peter's strategy: When you live a morally distinctive life, your goodness first silences criticism, and then—crucially—people begin asking **why** you're different. This creates natural opportunities to speak about your faith.

The danger of letting only your good deeds speak is that people may think moral goodness is the path to salvation, not realizing you do good works **because** you're already saved, not in order to be saved. Eventually, you must speak the gospel.

### Be Radically Different

Christians must be deliberately contrarian in visible ways:

- **Family priorities:** Taking only a weekend trip to a wedding instead of a lengthy vacation reveals different priorities
- **Family size:** Having more children than typical prompts conversations about valuing life and God's design
- **Material choices:** Living modestly despite career success raises questions about worldly values
- **Celebration practices:** Inviting neighbors to church on Halloween or celebrating differently opens doors
- **Time commitments:** Church attendance, summer mission trips, campus Christian fellowships—all opportunities to be noticeably different

The problem is we've become too much like the pagan world. We pursue the same ambitions, study the same things, spend money on the same things, travel to the same places. What makes us visibly different? We are aliens in this world—our lives should reflect that reality.

**The challenge:** Look at the regular conversations in your life. What comes up? What you're studying, watching, buying? How can you reshape your choices to reflect God's values and create natural openings for conversations about faith?

## Part 2: Submission to Government Authority (v. 13-17)

### The Foundation: Government Authority Comes from God

> **1 Peter 2:13-14** "Submit yourselves for the Lord's sake to every human authority instituted among men: whether to the king, as the supreme authority, or to governors, who are sent by him to punish those who do wrong and to commend those who do right."

> **Romans 13:1-2** "Everyone must submit himself to the governing authorities, for there is no authority except that which God has established. The authorities that exist have been established by God. Consequently, he who rebels against the authority is rebelling against what God has instituted."

Government is one of God's institutions for maintaining order. Even imperfect governments are better than anarchy. We submit because the authority is from God, not because the leader is morally superior.

### Understanding Authority's Limits

> **1 Peter 2:17** "Show proper respect to everyone: love the brotherhood of believers, fear God, honor the king."

Notice the hierarchy: God is above all. You have multiple authorities—government, family, church—each with distinct purposes and powers. None is absolute; all answer ultimately to God.

**Different institutions have different purposes:**
- **Government:** Maintains order, punishes wrongdoing, protects citizens
- **Family:** Procreates, nurtures, teaches values, provides care
- **Church:** Gathers believers, teaches Scripture, provides community
- **Workplace:** Produces goods/services, employs people, generates wealth

Each operates within its proper sphere. Government cannot nurture like family. Family cannot enforce law like government. This is the doctrine of "sphere sovereignty"—each institution has God-given authority in its domain, not as a grant from the state but directly from God.

### When Submission Ends

The Bible provides clear examples of when Christians disobey governing authorities:

**The Hebrew midwives:**
> **Exodus 1:15-17** "The king of Egypt said to the Hebrew midwives...When you are helping the Hebrew women during childbirth...if you see that the baby is a boy, kill him...The midwives, however, feared God and did not do what the king of Egypt had told them to do; they let the boys live."

**Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego:**
> **Daniel 3:18-19** "But even if he does not, we want you to know, Your Majesty, that we will not serve your gods or worship the image of gold you have set up."

**Daniel's prayer:**
> **Daniel 6:10** "Now when Daniel learned that the decree had been published, he went home to his upstairs room where the windows opened toward Jerusalem. Three times a day he got down on his knees and prayed, giving thanks to his God, just as he had done before."

**Peter and John:**
> **Acts 4:18-21** "Then the Sanhedrin called them in again and commanded them not to speak or teach at all in the name of Jesus. But Peter and John replied, Which is right in God's eyes: to listen to you, or to him? You be the judges! As for us, we cannot help speaking about what we have seen and heard."

**The principle:** You cannot break God's moral laws. When government commands you to steal, murder, lie, or recant your faith—you say no and stand firm. **But everything else, you submit.**

Much of what governments require is morally neutral—speed limits, licensing, tax codes. You may disagree with these policies, but disobedience here is rebellion, not conscience.

> **1 Peter 2:16** "Live as free men, but do not use your freedom as a cover-up for evil; live as servants/slaves of God."

### Submission vs. the Modern Rebellion

For 200 years since the Enlightenment, Western culture has exalted personal freedom and rebelled against all authority. We've forgotten that submission—properly understood—is essential to every functioning institution.

- In the military, the chain of command keeps order; confusion of authority lines leads to disaster
- In sports, someone must be captain; no team functions without leadership
- In business, hierarchy enables coordination; anarchy produces chaos
- In families, parents guide children; children cannot vote on dinner

When the world rejects laws it dislikes, what does it do? Revolution. Protest. Civil disobedience. Destruction. This causes more damage than the original problem.

**In a democracy, you have tools:** Political parties, voting, the rule of law. Use these. Advocate for change through proper channels. But once laws are enacted—even ones you dislike—you submit. If you cannot in good conscience obey an unjust law, you may respectfully refuse and accept the legal consequences. But you don't burn cars, block streets, or enter offices to destroy property.

Example: Marriage redefinition. If you voted against it and lost, you don't keep protesting the results. Use the tools of democracy to advocate for your position. But the law is the law. You submit to the office and the law, even if you disagree with it. (This does not mean you change your beliefs or endorse the law—only that you obey it.) If religious conscience prevents you from participating in certain ceremonies, that's a conversation between you and your God, not a justification for rebellion.

### Submission in Severe Persecution

What if, like the Chinese church, religious freedom is prohibited? Peter's strategy still applies:

- Continue living good, moral lives even when unobserved
- Meet in alternative ways: one-on-one conversations, family gatherings, small networks
- Memorize Scripture so you carry God's Word even when Bibles are forbidden
- Demonstrate Christ through your conduct

The Chinese church, even under severe restriction, has grown to 39-41 million Protestant Christians. Many function as legally registered organizations. Their submission and good works have earned them respect—rumors suggest the government now consults churches to understand how Western democracies (founded on Christianity) operate.

**The comfort:** You are not the first to face this. Peter wrote during Nero's reign, when Christians were burned as garden lights. Peter himself was executed. Yet the gospel advanced. Trust God's vindication on the last day.

## Part 3: Submission in the Workplace (v. 18-25)

### Biblical Slavery vs. Modern Employment

> **1 Peter 2:18** "Slaves, submit yourselves to your masters with all respect."

We must understand biblical slavery in its historical context. It was not the 18th-19th century chattel slavery (kidnapping, racial bondage, families torn apart). Biblical slavery operated under strict guidelines:

**Legitimate forms of biblical servitude:**
- War captives (no prisons existed; lifelong service was more merciful than execution)
- Debt slaves (those who couldn't pay debts; bankruptcy protection didn't exist)
- Voluntary servants (people selling themselves to escape poverty)

**Biblical slave rights:**
- Could not be abused or beaten without consequence
- Could own property and conduct business on behalf of masters
- Had rest days and legal protections
- Hebrew slaves were freed at Jubilee (maximum 7 years servitude)

> **Exodus 21:20-21** "If a man beats his male or female slave with a rod and the slave dies as a direct result, he must be punished; but he is not to be punished if the slave gets up after a day or two, since the slave is his property."

> **Exodus 23:12** "Six days do your work, but on the seventh day do not work, so that your ox and your donkey may rest and the slave born in your household, and the alien as well, may be refreshed."

Peter's point applies to modern employment relationships where you experience various forms of bondage:

- Contract work (bonded for a year or more)
- Scholarships (obligating you to serve in a particular location)
- Mortgages and debt (binding you to employment)
- Student loans (requiring years of income to repay)
- Career paths with limited options

### The Teaching on Difficult Masters

> **1 Peter 2:18** "...not only to those who are good and considerate, but also to those who are harsh."

This is the hard part. You're called to submit even to difficult, unreasonable employers—not because they deserve it, but because of their office.

You don't submit to the person; you submit to the position. Just as we submit to elected leaders not because they're morally superior but because they hold the office of authority, so you submit to your boss because of his position, not his merit.

This stops us from playing judge over authority. If you only submit to leaders you like or agree with, submission becomes meaningless—anyone can do that. Peter wouldn't need to write about it.

### The Radical Call: Unjust Suffering

> **1 Peter 2:19-20** "For it is commendable if a man bears up under the pain of unjust suffering because he is conscious of God. But how is it to your credit if you receive a beating for doing wrong and endure it? But if you suffer for doing good and you endure it, this is commendable before God."

The world says: "Submit until they tell you something you don't like, then stop. Submit until they insult you, then stop. Submit until it hurts, then stop."

Peter says: Find a heart of submission, not exceptions to submission.

### The Example of Christ

> **1 Peter 2:21-25** "To this you were called, because Christ suffered for you, leaving you an example, that you should follow in his steps. He committed no sin, and no deceit was found in his mouth. When they hurled their insults at him, he did not retaliate; when he suffered, he made no threats. Instead, he entrusted himself to him who judges justly. He himself bore our sins in his body on the tree, so that we might die to sins and live for righteousness; by his wounds you have been healed. For you were like sheep going astray, but now you have returned to the Shepherd and Overseer of your souls."

Jesus did not retaliate when insulted. He made no threats when suffering. He entrusted himself to the Father's justice. He did not say, "But Pilate didn't review the evidence properly, so I'll call down my angels." He didn't retaliate against the chief priests for fabricated charges or curse the guards who spat on him.

Instead: "Father, forgive them, for they know not what they do."

He submitted as a slave to unjust suffering, knowing God would vindicate Him. We are called to follow His example.

### Practical Application in Modern Workplaces

How does this look in your job?

**First, don't sin against your employer:**
- Don't steal company property to make up for unpaid overtime
- Don't spend an hour of your workday on social media (studies show nearly 6% of workers do this)
- Don't gossip or undermine your boss
- Work with integrity even when unobserved

**Recognize your own bondage:** You may not be a slave, but you experience real constraints—debt, contracts, dependents, limited options. Within those constraints, do your job well.

**Show consideration:** Your boss also faces pressure. When you're the boss someday, you'll understand. Don't assume malice.

**Create opportunities for witness:** Your good work—honesty, integrity, diligence—speaks louder than complaints. When you're noticeably different, people ask why. That's your opening to share your faith.

**Know your limits:** If your boss demands you sin (steal, lie, compromise your conscience), you take it to higher authority through proper channels, or you resign. You don't rebel.

## Part 4: Submission in Marriage (3:1-6)

### The Purpose: Winning Through Goodness

> **1 Peter 3:1-2** "Wives, in the same way submit yourselves to your husbands so that, if any of them do not obey the word, they may be won over without words by the behavior of their wives, when they see the purity and reverence of your lives."

Peter addresses wives whose husbands are not Christians—a real situation in the early church. The strategy: submission combined with good conduct can witness to an unbelieving husband far more effectively than argument.

This requires a wife to demonstrate, through her life, the reality of her faith. Her purity, reverence, and moral character become her sermon.

### True Beauty: Inner Character

> **1 Peter 3:3-4** "Your beauty should not come from outward adornment, such as braided hair and the wearing of gold jewelry and fine clothes. Instead, it should be that of your inner self, the unfading beauty of a gentle and quiet spirit, which is of great worth in God's sight."

Peter challenges the cultural standard of beauty. Not elaborate hairstyles, gold jewelry, or fine clothes—these fade. True beauty is a gentle and quiet spirit, which has infinite worth before God.

A **pure life** doesn't harbor resentment, disdain, or complaints. A **reverent life** takes God's word seriously. A **gentle spirit** doesn't nag, manipulate, or argue. A **quiet spirit** accepts her husband's leadership without trying to usurp it.

### The Historical Pattern

> **1 Peter 3:5-6** "For this is the way the holy women of the past who put their hope in God used to make themselves beautiful. They were submissive to their own husbands, like Sarah, who obeyed Abraham and called him her master. You are her daughters if you do what is right and do not give way to fear."

**Sarah as the example:** Sarah was no passive pushover. She laughed at an angel (Genesis 18:10-12). She took decisive action when threatened. Yet she called Abraham "master."

This wasn't cultural coercion—Peter presents it as the example of a godly woman. Sarah's submission was freely chosen, rooted in her faith in God's promises.

### The Problem: Loss of Beauty Standards

Western culture has lost touch with what's truly beautiful because we've lost intergenerational community.

In the past, families spent time with extended relatives—aunts, grandparents, grandaunts. You saw people age. You witnessed the fading of physical beauty and the emergence of what truly lasts: character, gentleness, wisdom.

Now our relatives are overseas. We don't attend funerals. We surround ourselves with peers our own age, dressed from the same shops, reinforced by media showing only the youngest and skinniest. The result? Sisters compare themselves to each other, competing over who's skinnier or cuter. "That's so dumb!"

**God's perspective:** God cares about your true beauty—your inner character. Your worth isn't determined by appearance. It's okay to wear ill-fitting clothes, have boring hair, be plain-faced.

**The call to guys:** Stop looking for the wrong kind of girls. If you only pay attention to the physically hot, you make it harder for your sisters to be godly. Pay attention to the godly and modest. Model what you're looking for.

### Submission: From the Heart

Submission can't be reduced to a set of rules (be a doormat, never disagree, let your husband make all decisions). That misses the point.

**True submission is a matter of the heart:**
- A pure life that doesn't store up resentment
- A reverent life that honors God and His word
- A gentle spirit that doesn't nag or manipulate
- A quiet spirit that supports her husband's leadership

When this internal posture is present, external actions flow naturally.

### Submission is Unqualified

> **1 Peter 3:6** "You are her daughters if you do what is right and do not give way to fear."

"Do what is right"—submission to your husband is the right thing for wives. Not "right if he's a good Christian," not "right if he's treating you well," not "right if he's making good decisions." Simply: right.

**Do not give way to fear.** Fear of losing control. Fear of being taken advantage of. Fear of abuse. Peter calls wives to trust God and submit their wills to their husbands' leadership.

### The Objection: What About Domestic Abuse?

Domestic abuse is terrible. There is no excuse. It happens to both spouses—husbands with temper and fists, wives with stinging words and dripping nags that erode a man's confidence.

**The limit on submission:** Just as we submit to government but can call police when abused, so wives who are abused should:
- Seek help from authorities (police can arrest and prosecute)
- Withdraw from fellowship temporarily if needed
- Get counseling and support

**The deeper issue:** Don't throw out the authority structure because of abuse. The problem isn't authority; it's the abuse of authority. The solution is calling for correct use of authority, not dismantling the authority itself.

When police abuse their power, we don't replace them with the fire department. We call for better policing. Similarly, when husbands abuse their authority, we call for husbands to exercise authority rightly, not eliminate the authority.

Husbands bear real responsibility—to present their wives as spotless brides before Christ (Ephesians 5:27), to lead their children in faith, to love sacrificially. These are unqualified responsibilities. They can't be done without authority.

### The Objection: What About Mutual Submission?

Some cite Galatians 3:28 and Ephesians 5:21 to argue for mutual, symmetrical submission.

> **Galatians 3:28-29** "There is neither Jew nor Greek, slave nor free, male nor female, for you are all one in Christ Jesus. If you belong to Christ, then you are Abraham's seed, and heirs according to the promise."

**But:** This speaks to salvation equality, not the elimination of distinct roles. Race still exists. Gender still exists. Slavery still existed in Paul's day. The point is spiritual standing before God, not organizational structure.

> **Ephesians 5:21** "Submit to one another out of reverence for Christ."

**But:** This describes collective relationships, not symmetrical exchange. "Bear one another's burdens" (Galatians 6:2) doesn't mean everyone exchanges burdens equally—it means those more able help those less able. It's asymmetrical.

The context is clearly asymmetrical:
- Only wives are called to submit to husbands (not vice versa)
- Only children are called to obey parents (not vice versa)
- Only servants are called to obey masters (not vice versa)

Language of "mutuality" can describe asymmetrical relationships. Reality is asymmetrical. In any team—workplace, church, family, military—someone leads and others follow. Without clear authority, chaos results.

**Practical reality:** Democracy of two doesn't work in marriage. You can't vote on every decision. Someone must have the final say. Practically speaking, that someone is the husband.

### Submission in Mixed Marriages

> **1 Peter 3:1** "Wives, in the same way submit yourselves to your husbands so that, if any of them do not obey the word, they may be won over without words by the behavior of their wives..."

What if your husband isn't a Christian? Doesn't that change things?

No. The passage directly addresses this situation. Even a godless husband receives submission. The wife's hope is that her good conduct will eventually win him to Christ.

Of course, if he demands sin—stealing, lying, immorality—she refuses and doesn't comply. But day-to-day authority? Yes, she submits.

You submit to the office, not to the person. Just as you submit to a boss because he's the boss (not because he's better or morally superior), so you submit to your husband because he's your husband.

This is tough. It might mean being less involved in church than you'd like. Less time for ministry. An unequal division of household labor. Vacations you don't want to take. But a gentle and quiet spirit is of great worth before God.

### For Singles: Think About This Now

Most of you are not married, but this teaching is crucial for your future. These standards should guide your choice of a spouse.

**For women:** Before committing, ask: "Can I submit to this man's authority? Is he a Christian? Is he mature? Does he love God's word? Is he working on his godliness?" If the answer to any is no, don't risk it. Test the relationship—can you be in a group setting where you encourage him to lead? How does submission feel? If he's not a Christian, how can he lead you to Christ?

**For men:** Think about the woman you'll present to Christ. Will she listen to your leadership? Can you lead her? Are you mature enough to take on that responsibility? To present her as spotless before Christ requires spiritual maturity.

This isn't about rules but about attitude—an attitude that encourages your spouse to take the lead, that's happy for the spotlight to shine on them, that submits even when difficult.

## Part 5: Fatherhood and Authority (3:7)

### The Weight of Responsibility

> **1 Peter 3:7** "Husbands, in the same way, be considerate as you live with your wives and treat them with respect as the weaker partner and as heirs with you of the gracious gift of life, so that nothing will hinder your prayers."

> **Ephesians 5:25** "Husbands, love your wives, just as Christ loved the church and gave himself up for her."

Husbands bear real responsibility. You can't responsibly lead without the authority to do so. It's like being assigned a project worth half your grade but having your laptop and books confiscated—you have responsibility without power.

**The weaker partner:** Generally speaking:
- Men are 15-20% larger than women on average
- Men typically have greater physical strength due to testosterone
- Pregnancy and motherhood involve profound biological changes (bone brittleness from breastfeeding, hormonal shifts affecting joints)

Because mothers bear children, fathers must provide protection, provision, and care.

### The Call on Fathers

Husbands must:

**Never abandon the family.** Whether from boredom, ambition, or feeling inadequate—never leave. "To cut and run when the battle is at its height is not just an evasion of grave responsibility; in the end it is cowardice."

**Never abrogate your authority.** Use it. Don't stand by hoping things work out. Take responsibility even when you might fail.

**Don't act the hero; do your job.** You're not earning extra credit by providing for your family or raising your children. That's what you're supposed to do. The buck stops with you.

**Communicate, don't mind-read.** Talk about more than just problems. Show interest in your children's lives: "How was school? How did that situation go?" Be present.

**Be an all-rounder.** Provide materially, yes, but also physically (exercise with them), emotionally (listen, empathize), and spiritually (pray, teach God's word). Don't let screens substitute for presence.

**Guard their inputs.** Television is a poor substitute for a father's time. Be intentional about what influences your children.

### Multiple Authorities in God's Design

God has established various institutions with distinct authorities, all under His ultimate sovereignty:

- **Family:** Parents have authority over children (Ephesians 5:22-6:4)
- **Church:** Leaders have authority over members (Hebrews 13:17)
- **Government:** Officials have authority over citizens (Romans 13:1-7)
- **Workplace:** Employers have authority over employees (1 Peter 2:18)

Each authority has distinct tools and purposes:
- Government punishes criminals and protects order
- Family nurtures and teaches
- Church shepherds spiritually
- Employers organize productive work

No single authority should do everything. The state shouldn't raise children. Parents shouldn't enforce criminal law. Churches shouldn't collect taxes.

As Abraham Kuyper argued, each sphere has "sphere sovereignty"—derives its power directly from God, not as a grant from the state. The state's role is to protect these spheres and prevent any from overstepping.

## Conclusion: An Alien Strategy for a Hostile World

### The Problem of Our Culture

For 200 years, Western culture has exalted personal freedom and autonomy to the exclusion of authority. Submission has become a swear word. Obedience is seen as oppressive. We've lost understanding of how authority actually works.

This creates havoc:
- Teachers no longer have authority; they're guidance counselors
- Parents no longer have authority; they're friends with money
- Police no longer have authority; they're personal bodyguards
- Governments no longer have authority; they help you pursue your goals but can't tell you what to do

**But if we don't understand authority, we don't understand God.** If we teach our children to submit to no one but themselves, they will never submit to God. And we won't understand marriage, the Church, or any healthy institution.

### Peter's Integrated Strategy

Living as aliens in a pagan world requires an integrated approach:

1. **Live distinctly good lives** that create opportunities for witness
2. **Submit to authorities** placed over you (government, employer, spouse)
3. **Refuse to sin** when authority demands it
4. **Trust God's vindication** on the last day
5. **Maintain a heart of submission** to God above all

This strategy doesn't require a perfect government or perfect leaders. It works even in severe persecution, as the Chinese church demonstrates. It works when you're outnumbered and powerless, as Peter's own martyrdom under Nero shows.

### The Final Hope

Peter wrote this letter during Nero's reign—when Christians were tortured and executed, used as garden lights in the emperor's palace. The situation seemed hopeless. Yet Peter called his readers to submit, live well, and trust God's final judgment.

Peter himself was martyred—crucified upside-down, according to tradition, because he felt unworthy to die as his Master had.

He did not rebel. He did not organize resistance. He continued preaching the gospel, living out his faith, and entrusting himself to God's justice. And his faithfulness echoed through the centuries.

Today there are between 39-41 million Protestant Christians in China. Many worship legally. Their strength comes from those who, like Peter, submitted to authority while refusing to compromise their faith. The government now consults churches to understand how Western democracy—built on Christian foundations—actually works.

**Your calling:** Keep doing what is right and good. Abstain from sinful desires that war against your soul. Live as free people, but live as servants of God. Create opportunities to declare His praises through your visible goodness. Submit to the authorities He has placed over you, except when they demand you deny Him.

On the day He returns, He will vindicate every good work. Every sacrifice. Every act of faithful submission. Until then, live as aliens and strangers, pointing others toward the light through the brightness of your distinctive lives.
