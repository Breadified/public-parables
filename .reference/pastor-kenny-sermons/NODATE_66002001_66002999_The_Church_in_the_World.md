# Revelation 2 & 3
**Title:** The Church in the World
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Churches Facing Real Struggles

We're examining seven churches that faced genuine challenges—suffering, persecution, heresy, idolatry, and immorality. These aren't abstract theological problems; they're the lived experience of first-century believers. As we consider these struggles, ask yourself: which of these challenges do we see most acutely in our own churches and communities today?

## The Pattern of the Letters

Each of the seven letters follows a consistent, sixfold structure. As you read through Revelation 2 and 3, identify what these six elements are. This framework will help you understand how Jesus addresses each church with both diagnosis and hope.

## The Opening Descriptions

Notice that each letter begins with a description of Jesus drawn directly from Revelation 1. You can find these repeated references at:
- 2:1
- 2:8
- 2:12
- 2:18
- 3:1
- 3:7
- 3:14

These aren't random details—they're carefully chosen portraits of Christ, each one speaking directly to the particular situation of that church.

## The Promise to Overcomers

Throughout the letters, we see the refrain: "To him who overcomes..." Study this pattern in the following verses:
- 2:7
- 2:11
- 2:17
- 2:26
- 3:5
- 3:12
- 3:21

Then skim Revelation 20-22 to see where these promises find their ultimate fulfillment. Not all will be explicitly repeated, but you'll see echoes of these rewards throughout the final vision.

## The Open Door in Philadelphia

Philadelphia's letter contains a striking image: an open door that no one can shut. What does this door represent? Let Scripture help you understand:

**Authority and Access:**
- Isaiah 22:20-34 — What authority is God speaking of here?
- Isaiah 26:2
- Isaiah 56:4-5
- Isaiah 60:11
- Isaiah 60:14 (Compare with Revelation 3:9)

**The Contrast:**
- Matthew 23:13 — What do the Pharisees do with the door? How do they deny access?
- Matthew 16:19 — What role does Jesus give the Apostles regarding the keys and authority?

Notice how this language differs from the "door" mentioned in Revelation 4:1. That open door invites John into heaven itself, while the Philadelphia door speaks of spiritual authority and access to God's kingdom.

**Connection to Previous Teaching:**
How does the command in Revelation 3:10 echo themes from last week's study?

## Overcoming and Rewards

A crucial question arises: Do we who believe receive the rewards promised to those who overcome? Are these rewards earned through our good works?

For insight, consider:
- 1 John 5:1-5 — What does it mean to overcome?
- John 16:33 — How does Jesus frame victory in this world?

The tension between grace and effort, between God's sovereignty and our perseverance, runs through these promises.

## The Letters in Context

At first glance, the seven letters can feel disconnected from the rest of Revelation—almost like they belong to a different book. But they're integral to John's vision. How do these letters to the churches fit into the larger narrative arc of Revelation?

## Which Church Resembles Ours?

As you reflect on these seven churches—their struggles, faithfulness, and failures—which one most resembles your own church community? And why?
