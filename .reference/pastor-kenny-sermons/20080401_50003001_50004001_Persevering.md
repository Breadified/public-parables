# Philippians 3:1–4:1

**Title:** Persevering  
**Date:** 27 April 2008  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## Where Should You Put Your Confidence?

We spend our lives building confidence in things that ultimately cannot sustain us. We trust in our musical abilities, our athletic prowess, our wealth, our possessions, our fashion sense—whatever makes us feel good about ourselves. Some of us bank everything on our accomplishments: our certificates and credentials. Others build their identity around choosing a good spouse, or like the wealthy man in basketball, we measure ourselves by what we have accumulated. But Michael Jordan's final scene reminds us that all these things fade.

The apostle Paul addresses this directly in Philippians 3. He asks a profound question that cuts to the heart of human existence: *Where are you placing your ultimate confidence?*

---

## 1. Confidence Is Not Found in Being a Jew

> Finally, my brothers, rejoice in the Lord! It is no trouble for me to write the same things to you again, and it is a safeguard for you. Watch out for those dogs, those men who do evil, those mutilators of the flesh. For it is we who are the circumcision, we who worship by the Spirit of God, who glory in Christ Jesus, and who put no confidence in the flesh. (Philippians 3:1–3)

Paul is responding to false teachers—likely the circumcision party we see in Galatians—who insisted that confidence in God required confidence in Jewish heritage and the works of the law. Paul's credentials to speak on this matter are impeccable.

> Though I myself have reasons for such confidence. If anyone else thinks he has reasons to put confidence in the flesh, I have more: circumcised on the eighth day, of the people of Israel, of the tribe of Benjamin, a Hebrew of Hebrews; in regard to the law, a Pharisee; as for zeal, persecuting the church; as for legalistic righteousness, faultless. (Philippians 3:4–6)

Notice what Paul emphasizes—not merely a ritual circumcision, but a whole lineage, heritage, and impeccable religious pedigree. If anyone could boast in Jewish identity and religious achievement, it was Paul.

---

## 2. But He Considers Them Lost

> But whatever was to my profit I now consider loss for the sake of Christ. What is more, I consider everything a loss compared to the surpassing greatness of knowing Christ Jesus my Lord, for whose sake I have lost all things. I consider them rubbish, that I may gain Christ. (Philippians 3:7–8)

This is not hypocritical nostalgia. Paul genuinely gave up these things—not grudgingly, but gladly. He counted his entire Jewish heritage, his religious achievements, his standing in society, as fundamentally worthless when weighed against knowing Christ.

Think about this with us. Many of us place our hopes in a certain heritage—being Chinese, being American, being part of a distinguished family. The application cuts deep: *What are the things we actually put our hopes in?* When we examine our lives honestly, where is our real confidence?

---

## 3. Confidence Is in Christ Alone

> And be found in him, not having a righteousness of my own that comes from the law, but that which is through faith in Christ—the righteousness that comes from God and is by faith. I want to know Christ and the power of his resurrection and the fellowship of sharing in his sufferings, becoming like him in his death, and so, somehow, to attain to the resurrection from the dead. (Philippians 3:9–11)

Paul's confidence is singular: Christ. Not a denomination. Not a church building or institution. Not even your own intellect—I once thought my intellectual understanding would prevent me from falling away. It won't. Only Christ will.

This is what it means to *bank it on one man*—to place all your ultimate confidence in the person and work of Jesus Christ.

---

## 4. Continuing in That Confidence

> Not that I have already obtained all this, or have already been made perfect, but I press on to take hold of that for which Christ Jesus took hold of me. Brothers, I do not consider myself yet to have taken hold of it. But one thing I do: Forgetting what is behind and straining toward what is ahead, I press on toward the goal to win the prize for which God has called me heavenward in Christ Jesus. (Philippians 3:12–14)

Paul has already *been taken hold of* by Christ. The decisive work is done. Yet he continues pressing forward, forgetting what is behind and straining toward the goal. The Christian life is not about earning what you've already been given—it's about running the race you've already entered.

> All of us who are mature should take such a view of things. And if on some point you think differently, that too God will make clear to you. Only let us live up to what we have already attained. (Philippians 3:15–16)

If you've already been found in Christ, *stay on target*. Don't get distracted by this world, by heritage, by lineage, by the false confidence merchants who would draw you back. You've already received it all for heaven.

---

## 5. The Warning: Those Who Forget

> Join with others in following my example, brothers, and take note of those who live according to the pattern we gave you. For, as I have often told you before and now say again even with tears, many live as enemies of the cross of Christ. Their destiny is destruction, their god is their stomach, and their glory is in their shame. Their mind is on earthly things. (Philippians 3:17–19)

Paul weeps as he writes this. Some who claim to follow Christ have actually turned away, making their appetites and earthly things their ultimate confidence.

> But our citizenship is in heaven. And we eagerly await a Savior from there, the Lord Jesus Christ, who, by the power that enables him to bring everything under his control, will transform our lowly bodies so that they will be like his glorious body. (Philippians 3:20–21)

Your true home is not here. Your ultimate confidence is not in what this world offers. Christ will transform you completely—and He alone has the power to do it.
