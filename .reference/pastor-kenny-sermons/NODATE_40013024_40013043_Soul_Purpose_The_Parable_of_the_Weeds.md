# Matthew 13:24-43

**Title:** Soul Purpose - The Parable of the Weeds

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Understanding the Kingdom of Heaven

The kingdom of something—say Camelot, for those familiar with the history—was simply an area under the rule of a king. King Arthur ruled Camelot. Similarly, the kingdom of heaven is a place where God reigns. As Christians, we believe that Jesus is the king who rules his kingdom *now*. But the kingdom doesn't look very good at the moment, and today's passage explains why.

## The Parable of the Weeds

> In verse 24: "The kingdom of heaven is like a man who sowed good seed in his field. But while everyone was sleeping, his enemy came and sowed weeds among the wheat, and went away. When the wheat sprouted and formed heads, then the weeds also appeared."

The Son of Man—Jesus—sowed good seeds, yet somehow weeds are discovered later on. In some Bible versions, these weeds are called "tares."

Tares are a type of weed that closely resembles wheat, except that when eaten they can cause poisoning and even death in severe cases. In a field, you absolutely do not want these weeds.

### Why Are There Weeds?

The servants immediately question the owner: "Do you want us to go and pull them up?" Their reaction mirrors our own. We see the corruption and evil in this world—the murder, domestic violence, bullying, and racism we read about in the news—and we ask: *Jesus, if you are the king and this is your kingdom, why don't you clean up the mess right now?*

But notice the owner's reply:

> "No, because while you are pulling the weeds, you may root up the wheat with them. Let both grow together until the harvest. At that time I will tell the harvesters: First collect the weeds and tie them in bundles to be burned; then gather the wheat and bring it into my barn." (Matthew 13:29-30)

There's wisdom in this answer. If you tried to separate weeds from wheat while they're both growing, you'd risk destroying the good crop along with the bad. But more importantly for us as Christians, God is reassuring us in two ways:

1. **God ensures complete accuracy.** There will be no mistake in his judgment.
2. **God gives time for repentance.** He's giving everyone a chance—those who are not yet Christians—to accept his Word and put their faith in Him.

### Sleeping Spiritually

When does Satan sow weeds? "While everyone was sleeping." This could refer to physical sleep, but it also refers to spiritual sleep. When we are spiritually sleeping—when we're not vigilant, not grounded in God's Word—we become vulnerable to Satan's work.

The world is not as it should be. It's full of poisonous weeds of corruption and evil. This world appears to be a kingdom of weeds, not heaven. But God is patient. He will not act rashly or jump to wrong conclusions as we sometimes do. He takes his time, allowing his Word to grow within our hearts and giving all of us a chance to repent and believe in him before he judges.

## God's Patience and Our Responsibility

Because God does not judge immediately, we must make every effort to guard against Satan, who seeks to destroy the good seed that has been planted within us. Just as plants take time to grow and we must be patient before we see results, so too does God display patience—but not endless patience. He has set a time when he will finally judge.

This is the harvest time spoken of in verse 30: the weeds will be collected and tied in bundles to be burned, and the wheat will be gathered into the barn.

## The Parable of the Net

The parable of the net tells us that the kingdom of heaven doesn't discriminate. The net is let down to catch *all* fish, meaning everyone and anyone can be part of it. But just because you're exposed to the kingdom of heaven doesn't mean you automatically get into heaven. The bad fish will be separated from the good fish and cast away—not out of cruelty, but to prevent any defect in the bad fish from spreading to the good.

## A Question for Each of Us

The parable of the weeds asks us: Do you allow God's word to grow in your heart? Just like the question posed last week about the types of soil, which kind of soil are you? The answer should be the fourth type—the receptive soil that accepts God's word, withstands trials and opposition, and grows deeper in Jesus Christ.

It's time to evaluate your lives. Are you a Christian? Don't assume you are merely because your parents are. Don't think you are merely because you attend church every Sunday—what about the rest of the week? A true Christian is one who follows Jesus wholeheartedly as their king. Will you obey that king as befitting the subjects of a kingdom?

## The Harvest is Coming

If you have committed to following Jesus, that's terrific! Even though the world doesn't look much like a kingdom of heaven right now, there will come a time when the weeds will be taken away. Be patient as God is patient. You will be gathered and brought to the safety of the barn.

If you have not committed to following Jesus, be warned. There will be a harvest time. Jesus, the king and master, will return and pull out the weeds. Make sure you are not one of them when that time comes.
