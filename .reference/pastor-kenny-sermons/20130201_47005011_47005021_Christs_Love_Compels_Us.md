# 2 Corinthians 5:11-21

**Title:** Christ's Love Compels Us

**Date:** 15 February 2013

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## The Passage

> For Christ's love compels us, because we are convinced that one died for all, and therefore all died. And he died for all, that those who live should no longer live for themselves but for him who died for them and was raised again. (2 Corinthians 5:14-15)

> So from now on we regard no one from a worldly point of view. Though we once regarded Christ in this way, we do so no longer. Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation; the old has gone, the new has come! All this is from God, who reconciled us to himself through Christ and gave us the ministry of reconciliation: that God was reconciling the world to himself in Christ, not counting men's sins against them. And he has committed to us the message of reconciliation. We are therefore Christ's ambassadors, as though God were making his appeal through us. We implore you on Christ's behalf: Be reconciled to God. God made him who had no sin to be sin for us, so that in him we might become the righteousness of God. (2 Corinthians 5:16-21)

---

## Central Truth

**Since Christ died for us—therefore let us die for him and others.**

---

## Point One: Christ Died for All

Jesus loved us so deeply that he willingly died for everyone—not just some, but all. This wasn't a partial sacrifice or a limited offer. His death encompassed every person who has ever lived.

When we understand that Christ died for all, we grasp something transformative: we are saved not merely from the penalty of sin, but we are saved *for* a purpose. His death freed us from the bondage of living only for ourselves. Through his sacrifice, we are called to a radically different way of life—one centered not on personal gain but on love for the God who died for us.

This love of Christ is not a sentiment we can take lightly. It's a conviction that should reshape how we see ourselves, others, and our purpose on this earth.

---

## Point Two: Living for Others Through Love

But what does Christ's death actually mean for how we live? Scripture tells us clearly: *"he died for all, that those who live should no longer live for themselves but for him who died for them and was raised again."*

So how do we live for Jesus? Jesus himself answered this when asked about the greatest commandments: we are to love the Lord our God with all our heart, soul, and mind, and to love our neighbor as ourselves. **Living for Jesus means living to love others.**

Think of it like this: Imagine an empty cup sitting on a plate. The cup represents you; the plate represents the world around you. Now let water symbolize God's love. When you pour water into a cup, if you don't stop pouring, the water overflows onto the plate. In the same way, God has poured his infinite, never-ending love into us. Because his love is endless, it naturally overflows from our lives and flows out into the world around us.

This overflow of love takes practical shape in how we treat people. We forgive those who have wronged us. We care for others' needs without expecting anything in return. We see people through new eyes—not by worldly standards of status or appearance, but as those for whom Christ died.

---

## The Greatest Expression of Love

The deepest way we can show love to others is to share with them the message of reconciliation—to tell them about Jesus. We are Christ's ambassadors. God has entrusted us with the message that he is not counting our sins against us, but offering us reconciliation through Christ. 

This Valentine's season, let us remember that the greatest love we can show is not flowers or chocolates or acts of kindness alone—though these have their place. The greatest love we can show is to tell others about the One who died for all out of love for them.

---

## Conclusion

Are you convinced that Christ has died for all? If you truly believe this, then you know that all people need to hear about Jesus' message of reconciliation. Let your life overflow with his love, touching those around you through forgiveness, care, and most importantly, through sharing the hope of Christ.
