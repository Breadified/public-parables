# 1 Corinthians 1:18-31

**Title:** Wisdom and Humility: Living as God's Fools in a Bizarro World

**Date:** 9 June 2019

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## The Cross: God's Opposite Day

The message of the cross appears foolish to the world, yet it is the power of God. This is the heart of "opposite day"—what seems backwards to human wisdom is actually divine brilliance.

> For the message of the cross is foolishness to those who are perishing, but to us who are being saved it is the power of God. (1 Corinthians 1:18)

The cross symbolizes execution, death, and condemnation. From a worldly perspective, it makes no sense to build a faith around an instrument of torture. Why would anyone wear an electric chair or a noose around their neck as a symbol of hope?

Yet God's logic is completely inverted from ours. The cross was necessary because sin is devastatingly serious—so serious that its judgment is violent death. All of us deserve to be on that cross. Christ, our King, took our place voluntarily, not out of weakness but out of love. This satisfies both justice and mercy simultaneously. On the third day, He rose from the dead, transforming the cross from a symbol of defeat into a symbol of victory. The cross we wear is empty because Jesus has ascended.

To those without spiritual insight, however, the cross remains foolishness. They see only death, not resurrection; condemnation, not salvation.

## Two Worldviews Collide

### What the World Seeks

> Jews demand signs, and Greeks look for wisdom. (1 Corinthians 1:22)

The Jewish people sought miraculous power—visible demonstrations of divine authority. The Greek philosophers sought intellectual coherence—rational arguments and ideas. Neither could accept a message centered on "Christ crucified."

> But we preach Christ crucified: a stumbling block to Jews and foolishness to Gentiles, but to those whom God has called, both Jews and Greeks, Christ the power of God and the wisdom of God. (1 Corinthians 1:23-24)

God will destroy the wisdom of the wise and frustrate the intelligence of the intelligent:

> For it is written: "I will destroy the wisdom of the wise; the intelligence of the intelligent I will frustrate." (1 Corinthians 1:19)

Isaiah spoke these words when Judah faced the Assyrian threat. Instead of trusting God, their leaders sought an alliance with Egypt—a political solution to their peril. God promised that their worldly wisdom would become their destruction. Indeed, Judah was soon conquered, taken into exile, and their proud strategy collapsed.

But here is the truth: the foolishness of God is wiser than human wisdom, and the weakness of God is stronger than human strength.

## The World Always Chooses Wrong

### The Pattern of Sin

The nature of sin is picking the opposite of what God says. This is why the world will always choose wrongly—not just wrong, but as wrong as wrong can be.

> For although they knew God, they neither glorified him as God nor gave thanks to him, but their thinking became futile and their foolish hearts were darkened. Although they claimed to be wise, they became fools and exchanged the glory of the immortal God for images made to look like a mortal human being. (Romans 1:21-23)

Notice the inversions:
- **Immortal → Mortal**: They worshipped created things instead of the Creator
- **God → Images**: They traded the infinite for the finite
- **Truth → Lie**: They exchanged truth for deception
- **Creator → Created**: They served what was made instead of the Maker

This is opposite day in the darkest sense.

### The Cult of Indiscriminateness

In our postmodern age, there is a particular reason the world will always be wrong: the rejection of discrimination as such. Discrimination—the ability to distinguish between good and evil, right and wrong—is the essence of rational thought. Yet the modern world treats any judgment as a form of bigotry.

This began with legitimate concerns. We rightly abandoned racial and sexual discrimination in hiring, education, and public life. But this principle of non-discrimination has been extended to *everything*, even where distinctions are essential:

- All religions lead to the same place
- Equal pay for unequal work
- Men allowed in women's sports
- Atheists teaching in Christian schools
- Male declared as female

The logic? If discrimination led to Hitler, then the solution is no judgment at all. But this is a false equivalence. The answer to bad judgment is not the absence of judgment—it is *good* judgment.

### Calling Good Evil, Evil Good

In our society, you can act on almost anything, but the one thing forbidden is to say someone else is wrong or that your way is better. To make any distinction is to be labeled a racist, sexist, homophobe, or bigot.

Consider education. Good education teaches students to think critically and understand evidence. Yet increasingly, education means forgetting what you know in favor of fashionable ideology.

Consider healthcare. True healthcare preserves life. Yet euthanasia—the killing of vulnerable people—is now called healthcare. Abortion is rebranded as "medical care."

This inversion reaches its logical extreme: those who warn of genuine harm are accused of hatred. When you say, "This path leads to destruction," the world responds, "You want me to be destroyed!"

Yet Jesus Himself taught us to judge:

> Stop judging by mere appearances, but instead judge correctly. (John 7:24)

Good judgment is not optional—it is commanded.

## The Unforgivable Sin

There is a particular sin for which there is no forgiveness: calling good evil and evil good.

> And the teachers of the law who came down from Jerusalem said, "He is possessed by Beelzebul! By the prince of demons he is driving out demons." (Mark 3:22)

Jesus had just healed someone, casting out a demon. The Pharisees responded by calling His good work evil, attributing healing to Satan. This is the blasphemy against the Holy Spirit—calling what is pure and innocent evil, labeling the work of God as demonic.

When you see gospel preaching called evil, when you see the message of Christ crucified attacked as hatred, you are witnessing this sin. The world will increasingly call good evil and evil good, and this is a sign of spiritual blindness.

## God Chooses the Foolish

### Who We Were When Called

> Think of what you were when you were called. Not many of you were wise by human standards; not many were influential; not many were of noble birth. (1 Corinthians 1:26)

We came to God as fools—ignorant, weak, poor, young, or in trouble. We had nothing to boast about. Yet God used us anyway.

> God chose the foolish things of the world to shame the wise; God chose the weak things of the world to shame the strong. God chose the lowly things of this world and the despised things—and the things that are not—to nullify the things that are, so that no one may boast before him. (1 Corinthians 1:27-29)

This is the genius of God's plan. By choosing the weak and foolish, He ensures that no one can take credit. All glory belongs to Him alone.

### Christ Is Our Wisdom

> It is because of him that you are in Christ Jesus, who has become for us wisdom from God—that is, our righteousness, holiness and redemption. (1 Corinthians 1:30)

We have been given a secret, a great advantage in this world: access to God's wisdom. Through Christ, we understand the order of reality. We possess a lens to see the world clearly and a code to decipher its true meaning.

> Let the one who boasts boast in the Lord. (1 Corinthians 1:31)

## Stay Foolish for Christ

We are surrounded by a Bizarro world where language and values are inverted. So many people live by backwards logic that we may start to doubt ourselves. But you are not the Bizarro—let them think you are.

The world calls you outdated for believing in the reality of male and female. Stay foolish.

The world calls you naive for rejecting sexual experimentation. Stay foolish.

The world calls you an idiot for refusing to chase wealth, status, and careerism. Stay foolish.

> For since in the wisdom of God the world through its wisdom did not know him, God was pleased through the foolishness of what was preached to save those who believe. (1 Corinthians 1:21)

You can be the light in darkness. But this only works if you remain faithful to what God has revealed, even when the world calls it foolish.

---

## True Wisdom Defined

True wisdom is not knowledge that inflates the ego. Paul wrote:

> All of us possess knowledge. This knowledge puffs up, but love builds up. (1 Corinthians 8:1)

Real wisdom begins with reverence for God:

> The fear of the Lord is the beginning of knowledge. (Proverbs 1:7)

> The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom. (Psalm 111:10)

God grants wisdom, knowledge, and happiness to those who please Him. We have been given a secret—an enormous advantage in understanding reality. Don't lose your confidence. You have a chance to make a genuine difference in this Bizarro world.

Humility is not self-deprecation. It is simply not showing off what you do know. It is quiet confidence rooted in Christ, not in yourself.
