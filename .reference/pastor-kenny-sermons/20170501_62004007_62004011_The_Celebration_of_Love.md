# 1 John 4:7-11

**Title:** The Celebration of Love

**Date:** 27 May 2017

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## Understanding Love: Beyond the Surface

A wedding is a wonderful celebration—months of planning, festivities, and the joining of two families culminate in one central truth: we are celebrating love. But what kind of love deserves such celebration?

The world offers many definitions. Science describes love through evolutionary biology: physical attraction based on waist-to-hip ratios and broad shoulders. Chemistry offers another explanation, pointing to the cascade of neurochemicals that flood our systems—dopamine making us feel good, adrenaline quickening our hearts, serotonin sending us temporarily insane. Then come the attachment hormones, oxytocin and vasopressin, bonding us to our beloved.

These definitions explain the feelings we experience, but they miss something essential. The Scriptures reveal a deeper, theological definition of love—God's own definition.

## God Is Love

> 7 Dear friends, let us love one another, for love comes from God.

God is the source of all authentic love. Not merely a source, but something far more profound:

> 8 Whoever does not love does not know God, because God is love.

This is not suggesting that love is one attribute among many that God possesses—something He puts on like a mask. Love is not a fickle characteristic that comes and goes with His mood. Rather, love *is* His very nature. It permeates everything about Him. His desire, His motivation, His identity—all of it is love.

If you could take a chisel and break off a piece of God, it would be love. If you could peer into the deepest parts of His being, you would find love. If God were to sweat, it would be love. If you cut God, He would bleed love. This is who God fundamentally is.

## Jesus Reveals God's Love

> 9 This is how God showed his love among us: He sent his one and only Son into the world that we might live through him.

Two thousand years ago, God's love entered human history in Jesus Christ. What we see in Him is what we see in God: pure, selfless love.

> 10 This is love: not that we loved God, but that he loved us and sent his Son as an atoning sacrifice for our sins.

Jesus was crucified—a sacrificial death for crimes He did not commit. He took upon Himself the judgment we deserve. God literally bled. God was cut. All for our sake.

Unlike God, humanity is not fundamentally loving. While we are capable of love, it does not run through our core. Dig beneath the mask we wear, and what do we find? Selfishness. Hatred. Apathy. When pressure comes, we look out for ourselves first.

But Jesus showed us another way—other-centered, sacrificial love. He demonstrated God's patience and kindness. He revealed a God who is slow to anger and abounding in love. Those who have experienced this generosity of God through Jesus know what true love costs.

## We Are Called to Love One Another

> 11 Dear friends, since God so loved us, we also ought to love one another.

God's love flows through those who know His love and transforms how we treat one another. As one person's passions influence their spouse—their beloved takes up their interests, their way of seeing the world—so God's love flows through us to shape how we love others.

This is not the fleeting love of feelings. Marriage vows express this reality: "I will"—not merely "I do." We say "I do" about today, when both are attractive and appealing. But marriage is "I will"—a commitment extending to tomorrow and all the days beyond.

This is unconditional love. It persists when the chemistry fades, when physical beauty changes, when expectations aren't met. It continues when your spouse wrongs you—when he becomes difficult or she becomes demanding. When she's messy or he's aloof.

This love was first shown to us by God through Jesus Christ. It is a love that says: I choose you. I commit to you. Not because of what you offer, but because I love you.

Dear friends, since God so loved us, we also ought to love one another.
