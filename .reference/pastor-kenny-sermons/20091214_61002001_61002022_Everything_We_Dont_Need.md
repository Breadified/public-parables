# 2 Peter 2:1-22

**Title:** Everything We Don't Need  
**Date:** December 14, 2009  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Big Idea

1. False teachers exist among us
2. False teachers will be judged by God
3. Godly people will be saved

---

## Why We Should Care About Fakes

We instinctively hate fake things. Whether it's counterfeit clothing from a market that falls apart, a pocket knife that breaks immediately, or fake milk that harms children—we recognize that fakes are dangerous and often illegal.

We hate fake friends too—people who flatter us and tell us what we want to hear, only to use us for their own purposes. And we especially hate fake teachers. These are people who care only about earning money while pretending to know what they're teaching. We've all encountered them: the tone-deaf music teacher, the art teacher who can't draw, the volleyball coach in a long dress who didn't know how to play volleyball, or the elementary school teacher who corrected my daughter's spelling of "castle" to "karsol" and "giraffe" to "giraffe" with an extra "e."

Fake teachers are everywhere, and we despise them because we know they harm those who trust them.

---

## 1. False Teachers Exist (verses 1-3)

> But there were also false prophets among the people, just as there will be false teachers among you.

The problem is that false Christian teachers are not easy to spot. They don't wear dark hats and evil grins with a sign announcing themselves. Instead, they work in secret.

> They secretly introduce destructive heresy, denying the sovereign Lord who bought them, bringing swift destruction on themselves.

### How to Recognize False Teachers

**By Their Teaching**

False teachers teach things that don't hold up to scrutiny. They make things up without proof, relying only on their own assertions. They manipulate Scripture, taking it out of context to suit their own ends.

One simple test is logic. Kenneth Copeland claims he can control the weather—yet Hurricane Sandy still happened. Benny Hinn talks about miraculous healings, yet hospitals remain full of sick Christians. Notably, the "healings" he reports are almost always subjective things like backaches and headaches that come and go naturally, rather than verifiable, permanent miracles.

Scripture itself gives us clear tests:

> The poor you will always have with you, but you will not always have me. (Matthew 26:11)

> About that day or hour no one knows, not even the angels in heaven, nor the Son, but only the Father. (Matthew 24:36)

> A prophet who presumes to speak in my name anything I have not commanded is to be put to death. (Deuteronomy 18:20)

**Testing Against Sufficiency**

Another crucial test is whether a teacher undermines the sufficiency of Jesus or the sufficiency of Scripture.

As Christians, we believe that Jesus is completely sufficient for our salvation. His death on the cross is the unconditional act of love that saves us. Think of it this way: a bullet of divine judgment is heading toward our heart, and Jesus steps in front of it to protect us. He dies; we live. Therefore, Jesus is all we need to be right with God. That is everything.

False teachers inevitably appeal to our guilt and tell us:
- You need to do good works before God will accept you
- You need to give more money or God will forfeit your salvation
- You need to experience something miraculous or spiritual before you can be a real Christian
- You need an earthly priest or exorcist to protect you

In other words, they tell you that you don't have everything you need. If you truly believed you had everything in Jesus, you wouldn't buy what they're selling.

Similarly, as Christians, we believe the Bible is sufficient—that it contains everything we need to know God and understand Jesus. False teachers will say:
- You need another book to supplement this one
- You shouldn't read this book directly; just listen to my interpretation
- This book is too confusing for simple people like you; trust my reading instead

By doing this, false teachers deny the sovereign Lord and deny His chosen method of revealing Himself to us—through Scripture. Even though they operate in secret, we can recognize them by their false teachings.

**By Their Ungodliness**

> Many will follow their depraved conduct and will bring the way of truth into disrepute. In their greed these teachers will exploit you with fabricated stories.

> This is especially true of those who follow the corrupt desire of the flesh and despise authority. Bold and arrogant, they are not afraid to heap abuse on celestial beings; yet even angels, although stronger and more powerful, do not heap abuse on such beings. But these people blaspheme in matters they do not understand. They are like unreasoning animals, creatures of instinct, born only to be caught and destroyed, and like animals they too will perish.

> They will be paid back with harm for the harm they have done. Their idea of pleasure is to carouse in broad daylight. They are blots and blemishes, reveling in their pleasures while they feast with you. With eyes full of adultery, they never stop sinning; they seduce the unstable; they are experts in greed—an accursed brood! They have left the straight way and wandered off to follow the way of Balaam son of Bezer, who loved the wages of wickedness.

The agenda of false teachers is always marked by immorality or greed. Cult leaders often maintain multiple wives, dressing up sexual immorality as spiritual truth. This is why Paul insists in Titus and 1 & 2 Timothy that Christian leaders be "faithful to one wife"—if they'll cheat on their spouse, they'll cheat you.

They are greedy. Look at how they spend money. Look at the financial empires they build. Scientology exists to make money. Yet even if they hide their own ungodliness, they'll appeal to yours—promising health, prosperity, and self-help. They offer you more money and more health, but these appeals are dangerous because they distract from what truly matters.

Don't be fooled by the size of a ministry or by emotional, fervent prayer. This doesn't indicate godliness. Watch how these people act when no one is looking. Check their relationships—do they live morally with their spouses? Check their finances—where does the money go?

I once invited a famous American pastor to speak. Everyone loved him. But something was wrong. His interpretation of Scripture was unorthodox. He swore and used vulgar language. Worse, he claimed to have visions revealing which women in the congregation had been sexually abused—a "gift" from God that supposedly guided his pastoral care of these vulnerable people. I can tell you God would never work that way. A married man having visions about women in his care is not a mark of the Holy Spirit.

### Keep Your Leaders Accountable

I'm speaking to you, but you must test my interpretation of Scripture. Bring your Bible and check what's being preached each week. Observe your leaders' lives. Ask how they're doing. I had someone recently tell me off for speeding while driving—that kind of honest feedback is invaluable. Good leaders should show marks of genuine character: generosity with money, a modest lifestyle, loyalty to their spouse, faithfulness, and integrity.

This is why we must be especially careful with sermons consumed over the internet or at large conventions—we cannot observe the speaker's actual life. We miss crucial discernment.

## 2. False Teachers Will Be Judged (verses 4-8)

> For if God did not spare angels when they sinned, but sent them to hell, putting them in chains of darkness to be held for judgment; if he did not spare the ancient world when he brought the flood on its ungodly people, but protected Noah, a preacher of righteousness, and seven others; if he condemned the cities of Sodom and Gomorrah by burning them to ashes, and made them an example of what is going to happen to the ungodly...

God knows how to judge the ungodly. He set precedents throughout history.

When the world became corrupt, God did not spare the ancient world but brought the flood. He protected Noah—a righteous man—and seven others. When Sodom and Gomorrah descended into depravity, God did not spare those cities but burned them to ashes as an example.

God will not allow the ungodly to continue indefinitely. Their destruction is not sleeping; their condemnation has been hanging over them.

> They will be paid back with harm for the harm they have done.

Even when false teachers seem to flourish and face no consequences, God guarantees their judgment. The wrongness will eventually be made right. False teachers may blatantly disobey God and bring truth into disrepute, but in the end, justice will prevail.

Eventually, all the lies catch up. Eventually they are exposed.

## 3. Godly People Will Be Saved (verses 9-22)

> If this is so, then the Lord knows how to rescue the godly from trials and to hold the unrighteous for punishment on the day of judgment.

The flip side of God's judgment on false teachers is His salvation of the righteous. Just as He rescued Lot, a righteous man distressed by the depraved conduct around him—tormented daily by the lawless deeds he witnessed—God knows how to rescue His people from trials.

You may feel angry and frustrated by the injustice in the world. You may have been conned or abused. It feels terrible. But God will save. He knows how to rescue.

### A Warning for the Wayward

> If they have escaped the corruption of the world by knowing our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ and are again entangled in it and overcome, they are worse off at the end than they were at the beginning. It would have been better for them not to have known the way of righteousness, than to have known it and then to turn their backs on the sacred command that was passed on to them. Of them the proverbs are true: "A dog returns to its vomit," and, "A sow that is washed goes back to her wallowing in the mud."

False teachers were once like us. They knew the way but turned their backs on it. All the major cults began with people who had a Christian background—they fell away and caused great damage.

The false teachers Peter warns about were once Christian. They attended church. They knew Jesus. But they turned away.

Be careful. You know how good it is to be free from the slavery of sin. You know it is good to love Jesus. You know this because you've been bought by His blood. We belong to Jesus now, so don't jeopardize your relationship with Him by turning your back and listening to rubbish.

### Check Yourself

Check yourself regularly. Whether you aspire to Christian leadership or not, keep showing up to Christian fellowship to encourage one another and learn. If you find that primarily happens on Sunday and a weeknight, don't take jobs on those nights. Don't schedule classes then. I know the temptation—you think, "If I skip, I get two days off to play video games or go shopping." But that trade isn't worth it.

> Not many of you should presume to be teachers. (James 3:1)

Yet we live in an age where people think that googling a Bible passage on the internet is sufficient qualification for teaching. It's not. If you aspire to teach, keep yourself accountable. Read deeply. Track your own spiritual progress.

## The Real Thing

In the end, remember this: the Lord is not fake. He came into the world as a real person you could touch. He is real relationally, revealing our sins and calling us to truth. He is real in His teaching and His promises.

Put your eyes on the real thing. Put your eyes on Jesus.

> God knows how to rescue the godly from trials and to hold the unrighteous for punishment on the day of judgment.

We have everything we need in Him.
