# Galatians 2:11-21

**Title:** We Are Justified by Faith in Jesus Christ Alone
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction

In Galatians 2, Paul addresses a critical issue that threatened the very heart of the gospel message. False teachers had insisted that circumcision was necessary for salvation alongside faith in Christ. But Paul, alongside the Jerusalem apostles, made clear that this was false. The genuine gospel of Christ teaches that faith in Jesus alone is the only way to be saved.

This passage emphasizes four essential truths: **Faith Alone**, **Gospel Alone**, **Grace Alone**, and **Christ Alone**.

## The Problem: Peter's Hypocrisy (Verses 11-14)

Paul recounts how Peter, despite his agreement on the gospel in Jerusalem, suddenly isolated himself from Gentile Christians during mealtimes. He had begun observing Jewish dietary laws again, refusing to eat with those he now deemed unclean.

This was more than a personal inconsistency—it was a contradiction of the gospel itself. In Jerusalem, the false issue was circumcision. In Antioch, it became Jewish dietary laws. But the underlying problem was the same: adding human works to what Christ had already accomplished.

Paul confronts Peter directly, publicly calling out his hypocrisy. Peter's actions contradicted his own preaching. The cost of establishing a unified gospel between Paul and the Jerusalem apostles—the first ten verses of chapter 2 detail this struggle—made Peter's retreat all the more serious. The gospel is God-breathed (2 Timothy 3:16), carrying divine authority not just for the apostles, but for all Christians throughout history.

## Faith Alone (Verses 15-16)

Paul emphasizes the heart of the matter through powerful repetition. In just one verse, he uses the words "justified" and "faith" three times each:

> We who are Jews by birth and not 'Gentile sinners' know that a man is not justified by observing the law, but by faith in Jesus Christ. So we, too, have put our faith in Christ Jesus that we may be justified by faith in Christ and not by observing the law, because by observing the law no one will be justified.

This repetition is deliberate. It stresses one unmistakable point: **We are justified by faith in Jesus Christ, not by observing the law.**

To justify means to declare innocent, to pronounce someone righteous or guiltless. To be declared right with God does not require observing the Old Testament ceremonial laws, circumcision, or the separation of Jews and Gentiles. Righteousness comes through faith alone in Jesus Christ. As Romans 1:17 reminds us, the righteous will live by faith.

## Gospel Alone (Verses 11-14)

The gospel cannot be compromised by cultural practices or works-based religion, whether circumcision or dietary laws. When we require people to add anything to faith in Christ for salvation, we contradict the gospel itself.

The benefits of the gospel—the freedom, the righteousness, the eternal life—come only through living faith in the Son of God, not through the works of the law. Any attempt to supplement Christ's work insults both the gospel and the One who accomplished our salvation.

## Grace Alone & Christ Alone (Verses 17-21)

Grace is God's unmerited favor—a gift He gives to those who could never deserve it. John 3:16 captures this perfectly:

> For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life.

God gave His only Son to be crucified at Calvary, bearing the weight of all our sins so that through faith in Him, we may be saved and have eternal life.

Hebrews 9:28 makes clear: *"Christ was sacrificed once to take away the sins of many people."* Once. That single sacrifice was sufficient. Complete. To add anything else would be an insult to Christ, suggesting His sacrifice was inadequate.

Jesus was never a backup plan. He was not plan J after plans A through I failed. Christ is the trump card—the ultimate, decisive victory over sin and death. If righteousness could truly be gained through observing the law, then Christ's death would have been entirely unnecessary. As Paul writes:

> I do not set aside the grace of God, for if righteousness could be gained through the law, Christ died for nothing! (Verse 21)

Think about what this means. If most people could achieve righteousness through the law without needing Christ, then His death would be worthless. But Paul will not allow this conclusion. Salvation comes through faith in Christ alone, and no amount of good deeds we perform can contribute to our standing before God.

## Conclusion

Paul's final appeal captures the essence of the gospel:

> For it is by grace you have been saved, through faith—and this not from yourselves, it is the gift of God—not by works, so that no one can boast. (Ephesians 2:8-9)

Salvation is God's gracious gift, received through faith, accomplished solely through Christ's work. Our good deeds, our obedience, our works—they flow from this grace, but they do not earn it. Christ alone is sufficient.
