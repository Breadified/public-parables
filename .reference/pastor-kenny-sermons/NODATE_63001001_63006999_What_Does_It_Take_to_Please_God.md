# 2 John 1-6

**Title:** What Does It Take to Please God?

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## What Does It Take?

The fundamental question we must ask ourselves as believers is simple: What does it take to please God? This isn't merely an academic question—it strikes at the heart of our faith and our daily walk with the Lord. Understanding what pleases God should shape how we live, pray, and serve.

## Prayer Pleases the Personal God

God is not a distant, impersonal force. He is a personal God who draws near to those who call upon Him. When we come before Him in prayer, we engage in something deeply meaningful. Prayer is the channel through which we commune with our Father, and in doing so, we please Him.

God delights when His children bring their requests, their cares, and their hearts to Him in prayer. This isn't about using the right words or performing religious duty. It's about the genuine desire to connect with the God who knows us intimately and cares for us personally.

## People Being Saved Pleases God

There is nothing that brings greater joy to the heart of God than seeing lost people come to faith in Christ. Jesus Himself taught that the angels in heaven rejoice over one sinner who repents. When we participate in God's redemptive work—whether through sharing the gospel, interceding for the lost, or simply living out our faith before others—we align ourselves with God's deepest desires.

God's heart has always been turned toward the salvation of humanity. From the beginning of Scripture to its end, His redemptive work runs like a golden thread. To please God means to care about what He cares about: the souls of those who are far from Him.

## Jesus Pleases God

Throughout the Gospels, we hear God the Father affirm His Son: "This is my beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased." Jesus pleased God perfectly—not through external religious observance, but through His obedience, His sacrificial love, and His unwavering commitment to the Father's will.

Jesus is God's ultimate standard of what it means to please Him. In His life, death, and resurrection, Jesus demonstrated perfect alignment with the Father's heart and purpose.

## Pleasing God Through Jesus

As believers, we do not please God through our own efforts or righteousness. We please God through Jesus Christ. This is the central truth of the gospel. Jesus has done for us what we could never do for ourselves—He has perfectly pleased God on our behalf.

When we trust in Jesus, when we follow Him as Lord, when we align our lives with His teaching, we participate in His righteousness. God looks upon us through the lens of Christ's perfect obedience. This is the foundation of our acceptance before Him—not our performance, but Christ's.

## A Prayer That Pleases God

**Dear God, my Saviour and Lord,**

I am sorry that I have not pleased you in the past and see why you are angry with me. I thank you for sending your Son, Jesus, to save me by pleasing you on my behalf. Please help me now to please you by following Jesus as my Lord.

Amen.
