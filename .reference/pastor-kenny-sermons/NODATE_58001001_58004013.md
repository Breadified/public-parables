# Hebrews 1:1-4:13

**Series:** Jesus: The Better Messenger  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## Hebrews 1:1-2:4 — Jesus: The Better Messenger #1

### Superior Message (1:1-3)

The message God now brings through Jesus is better than the old because of four decisive reasons:

1. **Better Representation (1:1-3)** — Jesus is the radiance of God's glory and the exact representation of his being, sustaining all things by his powerful word.

2. **Better Name (1:5-6)** — Jesus inherits a name far superior to the angels, being called Son while they remain servants.

3. **Better Permanence (1:7-12)** — Unlike the created heavens that will wear out like a garment, Jesus remains unchanged forever.

4. **Better Authority (1:12-14)** — Jesus sits at the right hand of God while all enemies are made his footstool, whereas angels are merely ministering spirits.

### The Call to Listen

> "Therefore, listen!" (Hebrews 2:1)

This superior messenger demands our attention and submission. The implication is grave: if we neglect such a great salvation announced by the Son himself, how can we escape judgment?

## Hebrews 2:5-3:1 — Jesus: The Better Messenger #2 (for Humanity)

### Jesus: The Real Man

The opening question cuts to the heart: *What is real humanity?*

The culture offers false models—the action hero, the intellectual titan, the business magnate, the athlete, the celebrity, the influencer. But none of these represent true humanity.

> "What is man that you are mindful of him, the son of man that you care for him? You made him a little lower than the angels; you crowned him with glory and honor and put everything under his feet." (Hebrews 2:6-8)

Humanity was created in God's image with authority over all creation. Yet we don't see that authority realized in our broken world. But we see it perfectly in Jesus:

> "We see Jesus, who was made a little lower than the angels, now crowned with glory and honor because he suffered death, so that by the grace of God he might taste death for everyone." (Hebrews 2:9)

**Jesus is real humanity.** He alone embodies what we were meant to be.

### True Brotherhood Through Shared Humanity

Because Jesus took on full humanity—suffering, temptation, even death—he accomplished something miraculous:

> "In bringing many sons to glory, it was fitting that God, for whom and through whom everything exists, should make the author of their salvation perfect through suffering. Both the one who makes men holy and those who are made holy are of the same family. So Jesus is not ashamed to call them brothers." (Hebrews 2:10-11)

Jesus is not distant from us. He is our brother.

He shared our flesh and blood to destroy the one who holds power over death—the devil—and to free us from the slavery of death-fear that has bound us our entire lives. He became our merciful and faithful high priest, able to make atonement for our sins because he himself experienced temptation and suffering (Hebrews 2:14-18).

### The Paradox of Real Humanity

Through Jesus, we become real people—truly human in the eschatological sense. This doesn't mean those outside Christ lack humanity, but through faith in him, we discover authentic existence: real love, real brotherhood, real unity.

A true friend is one who dies for you. True unity doesn't rest on human-made structures or institutions but in our shared relationship with Jesus, the one sent from God and our true mediator.

### Therefore: Fix Your Thoughts on Jesus

> "Therefore, holy brothers, who share in the heavenly calling, fix your thoughts on Jesus, the apostle and high priest whom we confess." (Hebrews 3:1)

Jesus was faithful to the one who appointed him, just as Moses was faithful to God's house. Yet Jesus is worthy of greater honor than Moses himself, as the builder of a house possesses greater honor than the house. Moses served faithfully as a servant, testifying to what would come. But Christ is faithful as a son over God's house—and we are his house, if we hold fast to our confidence and hope (Hebrews 3:2-6).

## Hebrews 3:2-4:13 — Jesus: The Better Prophet

### The Danger of Hardened Hearts

> "Today, if you hear his voice, do not harden your hearts as you did in the rebellion, during the time of testing in the desert, where your fathers tested and tried me and for forty years saw what I did." (Hebrews 3:7-9)

The Holy Spirit speaks a warning through Psalm 95, recalling Israel's wilderness wandering. Despite witnessing God's power firsthand, that generation hardened their hearts in unbelief and never entered God's rest. Their bodies fell in the desert (Hebrews 3:16-19).

The warning is for us: *See to it that none of you has a sinful, unbelieving heart that turns away from the living God. But encourage one another daily, as long as it is called Today, so that none of you may be hardened by sin's deceitfulness* (Hebrews 3:12-13).

Faith must be held firmly to the end. The moment we stop believing is the moment we begin to drift.

### The Rest That Remains

> "Therefore, since the promise of entering his rest still stands, let us be careful that none of you be found to have fallen short of it." (Hebrews 4:1)

The gospel was preached to Israel in the wilderness, but the message held no benefit for them because they didn't combine it with faith (Hebrews 4:2). We who have believed do enter that rest—the rest God entered on the seventh day after creation, a Sabbath-rest for the people of God (Hebrews 4:3-10).

This isn't merely physical rest or an earthly promise. It's the eternal rest of knowing God, trusting in his finished work, and ceasing from our own fruitless striving.

### The Living Word and Ultimate Accountability

> "For the word of God is living and active. Sharper than any double-edged sword, it penetrates even to dividing soul and spirit, joints and marrow; it judges the thoughts and attitudes of the heart. Nothing in all creation is hidden from God's sight. Everything is uncovered and laid bare before the eyes of him to whom we must give account." (Hebrews 4:12-13)

God's word isn't static or ancient. It is living, active, and penetrating—it judges not just our outward actions but the deepest attitudes of our hearts. We stand completely exposed before the God to whom we must ultimately answer.

### The Compassionate High Priest

Yet this isn't meant to drive us to despair. Rather:

> "Therefore, since we have a great high priest who has gone through the heavens, Jesus the Son of God, let us hold firmly to the faith we profess. For we do not have a high priest who is unable to sympathize with our weaknesses, but we have one who has been tempted in every way, just as we are—yet was without sin. Let us then approach the throne of grace with confidence, so that we may receive mercy and find grace to help us in our time of need." (Hebrews 4:14-16)

Jesus understands our weakness intimately. He has walked this earth, faced every form of temptation, yet remained sinless. Therefore, we approach God not with fear but with confident boldness, knowing we will find mercy and grace precisely when we need it most.
