# Hebrews 1:1–2:3

**Title:** Jesus Is a Better Messenger  
**Date:** December 28, 2008  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction: The Message and the Messenger

We live in an age of overwhelming communication. Approximately 144.8 billion emails are sent every day, and workers spend 28% of their time managing their inboxes. Add to this the flood of text messages, social media notifications, advertisements, and junk mail—many of which conflict with one another. How do we know what is true?

We evaluate messages in two fundamental ways:

1. **By content:** Does it sound plausible? Is it true? How meaningful is it?
2. **By messenger:** Where does it come from? Is it from someone we trust? Is it reliable?

The author of Hebrews makes a compelling argument: we should place our confidence in both the message of Jesus *and* in Jesus himself as the messenger. This is the heart of chapters 1 and 2.

## Part 1: A Better Message (1:1–3)

### The Contrast: Past Revelation vs. Final Revelation

> **Hebrews 1:1–3:** In the past God spoke to our forefathers through the prophets at many times and in various ways, but in these last days he has spoken to us by his Son, whom he appointed heir of all things, and through whom he made the universe. The Son is the radiance of God's glory and the exact representation of his being, sustaining all things by his powerful word. After he had provided purification for sins, he sat down at the right hand of the Majesty in heaven.

The author presents a clear contrast:

| Past | These Last Days |
|------|-----------------|
| Many times and various ways | By His Son |
| Through prophets | Direct revelation |

### God's Historical Methods of Revelation

Throughout the Old Testament, God revealed himself through diverse means:

- **Burning bushes** (Moses)
- **Urim and Thummim** (Aaron)
- **Dreams** (Joseph, Pharaoh)
- **Visions** (Ezekiel)
- **Writing on the wall** (Daniel)
- **Even a donkey** (Balaam)

These were necessary because God is transcendent—beyond our world, beyond our time, the Creator. We cannot meditate our way to God; God must reveal himself to us through our senses: sound, sight, language.

### "The Last Days" Has Come

When the Old Testament prophets spoke of "the last days," they referred to God's ultimate intervention in history:

- **Isaiah and Micah** predicted a time when the temple would be truly established and both Jews and Gentiles would approach God, with His law going out to all nations.
- **Hosea** predicted Israel's return to their land and their true King.
- **Jeremiah** prophesied that in the last days, God's own Spirit would be poured out, and everyone who called on the Lord would be saved.

At Pentecost, Peter proclaimed that the last days had arrived (Acts 2:17). The final chapter of God's revelation was opening.

### Jesus: The Final and Perfect Revelation

The importance of the Son cannot be overstated. The remainder of verses 2 and 3 heap descriptor upon descriptor:

- **Appointed heir of all things**
- **Through whom he made the universe**
- **The radiance of God's glory**
- **The exact representation of his being**
- **Sustaining all things by his powerful word**
- **Provider of purification for sins**
- **Seated at the right hand of the Majesty in heaven**

The critical truth is this: **The Son is the exact representation of God's being.** If you saw Jesus, you saw God. If you heard Jesus, you heard God. In first-century Palestine, you could have witnessed God in the flesh—touched Him, smelled Him, heard Him speak Aramaic. The very words of God were heard in history from the one who perfectly embodies God's nature.

Though the Son is now seated at the right hand of God, His revelation has not ceased. The record of God's revelation in Jesus is preserved for us in Scripture. The Bible is the complete, written testimony of God's revelation—from the old prophets through to Jesus, the final and supreme revelation.

### The Finality of Revelation

If we think of Scripture as a letter, there is indeed a difference between reading "I love you" on a page and hearing it face-to-face. Yet the substance—the truth conveyed—is unchanged. The medium differs, but the message is complete.

**Because salvation and revelation are finalized in Christ, we must stop looking for further revelation.** We should not:

- Seek other ways of knowing God's will beyond Scripture
- Pursue fortune tellers or self-appointed prophets claiming new messages from God
- Ask God for dreams and visions as our primary means of guidance (the Old Testament consistently warns against divination and astrology)
- Chase so-called words of knowledge or mystical supernatural guidance

Some might object: "Couldn't God still speak to us through dreams and visions?" Technically, yes. But this misses the point. God has already spoken. He has revealed Himself completely. To demand additional, personal revelations is to act as if what He has already said isn't enough.

Consider the absurdity: If every human demanded that God appear at their personal convenience, and God obliged, He would cease to be God. He would become a novelty, a magic trick to be summoned at will. We would become gods instead.

God has chosen His time of revelation and accomplished it. It is recorded faithfully in Scripture. We are to read it and trust it.

Paul's warning is severe:

> **Galatians 1:8:** But even if we or an angel from heaven should preach a gospel other than the one we preached to you, let him be eternally condemned!

Alternate revelations can do one of two things: at best, they merely repeat what Scripture already tells us; at worst, they lead us to trust something other than the Christ of history. Either way, they are unnecessary and dangerous.

We must humble ourselves. We are not God. God has spoken. His revelation is complete. That is enough.

## Part 2: A Better Messenger (1:4–14)

Jesus is not merely a better final message. He Himself, as a messenger, is far superior to any previous messenger—whether angelic, prophetic, or human.

### Better Name (1:4–6)

> **Hebrews 1:4–6:** So he became as much superior to the angels as the name he has inherited is superior to theirs. For to which of the angels did God ever say, "You are my Son; today I have become your Father"? Or again, "I will be his Father, and he will be my Son"? And again, when God brings his firstborn into the world, he says, "Let all God's angels worship him."

The author's argument is straightforward: **Jesus has been given a name and title superior to all others.** He alone is called God's Son. He alone is God's Firstborn. No angel has ever been addressed in such terms.

It's true that Israel and certain kings were sometimes called "sons of God," but never in this sense. Jesus is God's Son in a unique, father-son relationship. The angels, by contrast, are servants—they bow before Him.

**Why do titles matter?** Consider this: if you're in financial trouble, you want to speak with someone who has "CPA" or "CA" in their credentials. If you're in legal trouble, you want an attorney admitted to the Supreme Court. If you're ill, you want someone with "Doctor" in front of their name. Titles represent qualification and authority. They command attention because they signal expertise.

Jesus has the ultimate title: Son of God. He is the exact representation of God. He comes from the Father and therefore knows more than any other messenger. When He speaks, we must listen.

### Better Permanence (1:7–12)

> **Hebrews 1:7–12:** In speaking of the angels he says, "He makes his angels winds, his servants flames of fire." But about the Son he says, "Your throne, O God, will last for ever and ever, and righteousness will be the scepter of your kingdom. You have loved righteousness and hated wickedness; therefore God, your God, has set you above your companions by anointing you with the oil of joy." He also says, "In the beginning, O Lord, you laid the foundations of the earth, and the heavens are the work of your hands; they will perish, but you remain; they will all wear out like a garment. You will roll them up like a robe; like a garment they will be changed. But you remain the same, and your years will never end."

The contrast here is striking. Angels are compared to wind and fire—powerful natural forces used by God to inspire awe and obedience. But wind and fire are temporary. They pass. Even bushfires and tornadoes eventually burn out.

The Son, by contrast, is eternally permanent. His throne will last forever and ever. He was present at the beginning of creation—He laid the foundations of the earth. He will be there at the end. The earth and heavens, made by His hands, will wear out like old clothing. They will perish and fade. But **He remains the same, and His years will never end.**

What He speaks is therefore more trustworthy than what any temporal messenger says. His revelation is not subject to change. It is not a message for this generation that might be revised in the next. It is eternal, unchanging truth.

Think of it this way: Would you buy a house from a 100-year-old established company or a 10-year-old startup? The established company carries greater assurance. Similarly, would you trust a message written on a cardboard garage-sale sign—temporary, scribbled, gone with the next rain—or one from a permanent, established business with a professional sign? When making an important purchase, you trust the one with permanence and credentials.

Jesus is the permanently established messenger. His revelation is complete and everlasting. It can be trusted.

### Better Authority (1:13–14)

> **Hebrews 1:13–14:** To which of the angels did God ever say, "Sit at my right hand until I make your enemies a footstool for your feet"? Are not all angels ministering spirits sent to serve those who will inherit salvation?

The comparison concludes decisively. Angels are sent to *serve*. The Son is seated to *rule*. While the angels run about their tasks, the Son sits in a position of ultimate authority—at the right hand of God, with all His enemies made a footstool beneath His feet.

This is not to say that Jesus is not a servant. He came to serve by giving His life as a ransom for many. But His service is finished. He has completed His work and now sits in a place of authority and rule, exalted above all heavenly beings.

What Jesus has spoken therefore carries final authority. His word is law. His revelation is supreme.

## Part 3: Therefore, Listen! (2:1–4)

> **Hebrews 2:1–4:** We must pay more careful attention, therefore, to what we have heard, so that we do not drift away. For if the message spoken by angels was binding, and every violation and disobedience received its just punishment, how much more shall we escape if we ignore such a great salvation? This salvation, which was first announced by the Lord, was confirmed to us by those who heard him. God also testified to it by signs, wonders and various miracles, and gifts of the Holy Spirit distributed according to his will.

The conclusion is inescapable. Given that Jesus is:

- **A better messenger** with a superior name
- **A permanent, unchanging messenger** whose throne endures forever
- **An authoritative messenger** seated at the right hand of God

—then we must **pay more careful attention to what we have heard** and **not drift away** from His message.

The logic is simple: If the messages delivered by angels were binding and violations brought just punishment, how much more serious are the consequences of ignoring the message delivered by the Son Himself—the Creator, the radiance of God's glory, the one seated at God's right hand?

This is **great salvation**. It was announced by the Lord Himself. It was confirmed by those who heard Him directly. God testified to it with signs, wonders, miracles, and the distribution of the Holy Spirit.

### A Final Appeal

Some of you may be hesitating, waiting for further revelation or additional confirmation. Let me be clear: **There is none coming.** This is as good as it gets. The Bible, for all its alleged faults, is the only way to know Jesus. It is the only way to hear His message. It is the only way to know the Messenger Himself.

If you have been waiting to hear the word of salvation, the answer is here. Jesus is not merely one option among many. There is no other Messiah coming. There is no other way to salvation. The plot is not still developing. There are no hidden chapters. The silence of the last 2,000 years is not a gap waiting to be filled. Salvation has been fully revealed. All is complete.

Listen to Jesus. Trust His message. Follow His authority. Pay careful attention. Do not drift away.
