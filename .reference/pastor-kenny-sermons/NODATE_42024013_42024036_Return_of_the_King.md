# Luke 24:13-36

**Title:** Return of the King
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Returning Home

When we return to a place we've left, we instinctively tick off a mental checklist—favorite foods, family visits, old haunts, obligatory photos. We do what matters most to us, what makes the journey worthwhile.

On the day of His resurrection, Jesus returns. But what does He tick off? What matters most in His return?

## 1. A Question on a Road Trip (13-24)

On that same Sunday, two disciples were traveling to Emmaus, a village seven miles from Jerusalem. As they walked, they discussed everything that had happened—the crucifixion, the empty tomb, the women's testimony, the men who had found it empty.

Yet for all their discussion and debate, they seemed unmoved by the resurrection itself. It was as though One Direction had arrived in Sydney and they simply hadn't noticed. Their faces were downcast, their hearts heavy with grief and confusion.

Jesus drew near and walked with them, but their eyes were held from recognizing Him. They saw only a stranger who seemed ignorant of the very events that consumed their thoughts.

**The Facts They Knew:**
- Jesus was a prophet, powerful in word and deed (v. 19)
- The chief priests and rulers handed Him over to be crucified (v. 20)
- It was the third day since all this took place (v. 21)
- The women reported an empty tomb and a vision of angels (v. 22-23)
- Men from their group confirmed the tomb was empty, but they did not see Jesus (v. 24)

They had the evidence. They had the testimony. Yet they had missed the meaning entirely.

## 2. The Key to the Answer (25-27)

Jesus rebuked them: "How foolish you are, and how slow to believe all that the prophets have spoken! Did not the Messiah have to suffer these things and then enter his glory?"

The problem was not that they lacked information. They were stuck in their grief. They were spiritually blind to who Jesus really was. They had rejected the suffering Christ—the one who would come not as a conquering king, but as a sacrificial redeemer.

**Jesus's Solution:**

Beginning with Moses and all the Prophets, He explained to them what was said in all the Scriptures concerning Himself. He interpreted the entire Old Testament—the Law, the Prophets, the Writings—as a unified testimony to His person and work.

The disciples saw Jesus as a side figure in the biblical story, perhaps a great prophet or redeemer, no different in kind from others. But Jesus is not a figure in Scripture; **He is the key to Scripture**. He is the climax and fulfillment of all that has been written. The prophets must be reinterpreted through Him. He is the suffering One, the ultimate Prophet, the final Word.

This is what scholars call Biblical Theology: the entire Bible tells one story about one Person—Jesus Christ.

## 3. A New Understanding (28-35)

As they approached Emmaus, Jesus acted as though He would continue on. But the disciples urged Him strongly to stay. When He sat at table with them, took bread, gave thanks, broke it, and gave it to them, their eyes were opened. They recognized Him—and He vanished from their sight.

Then understanding flooded their hearts: "Were not our hearts burning within us while He talked with us on the road and opened the Scriptures to us?"

The revelation came not through magic but through the opening of Scripture. As Jesus explained the Word, their hearts burned. When they finally beheld Him in the breaking of bread, the pieces fell into place.

## 4. The Pattern Repeated (36-45)

The disciples returned to Jerusalem and shared their testimony with the Eleven. While they were still speaking, Jesus Himself stood among them and said, "Peace be with you."

They were startled, thinking they saw a ghost. But Jesus showed them His hands and feet and ate fish before them. He was fully physical, fully risen, fully present.

Yet even with all this, they still struggled to believe. So Jesus opened their minds to understand the Scriptures: "This is what I told you while I was still with you: Everything must be fulfilled that is written about me in the Law of Moses, the Prophets and the Psalms."

## Application: Know the Whole Story

**The Bible is not primarily:**
- A moral textbook (though it speaks to morality)
- A decision manual (though it guides decisions)
- A crystal ball for the future (though it speaks of what's to come)
- A motivational device (though it's inspiring)
- About you (though it affects you deeply)

**The Bible is about Jesus.** The whole Bible tells His story.

When you read Genesis, you're reading about Jesus. When you read Leviticus, Psalms, and Malachi—Jesus is woven throughout. Learn to see Him:

- **Direct mention:** Jesus is explicitly named
- **Typology:** The lamb, the temple, the sacrifice
- **Prophecy:** Direct predictions of His coming
- **Preparatory:** The law as tutor, the sacrificial system
- **Reflective:** Grace and forgiveness as echoes of His work
- **Resultant:** Our ability to approach God in prayer through His work

This week, invest more than five minutes in Scripture. Set aside another five minutes—or thirty-five minutes a week—to dig deeper. Ask yourself: How does this passage point to Jesus? Read narratives in context. Let the Word transform your understanding.

## The Resurrection as Fulfillment

The power of the resurrection lies not merely in its miraculous nature. Many ancient myths speak of immortality. People are medically resurrected regularly. 

**The true significance is this: the resurrection fulfills Old Testament prophecy.**

In Luke 16, Abraham tells the rich man begging for a sign from the dead: "If they do not listen to Moses and the Prophets, they will not be convinced even if someone rises from the dead." 

You don't need to be a personal eyewitness to believe. If you trust Scripture, that is enough. You don't need to see a resurrected person—you have the Word of God.

## What's Next? (46-53)

Jesus told them: "This is what is written: The Messiah would suffer and rise from the dead on the third day, and repentance for forgiveness of sins would be proclaimed in His name to all the nations, beginning at Jerusalem."

The resurrection means death is dealt with. Forgiveness of sins is now available to all.

"You are witnesses of these things. And look, I am sending you what My Father promised. Stay in the city until you are empowered from on high."

The disciples would receive the Holy Spirit—not left hanging, but equipped with power to proclaim this gospel to all people. They would become witnesses, writers of Scripture, proclaimers of the message of Jesus to the world.

Jesus led them to Bethany, blessed them, and was carried up into heaven. They worshiped Him and returned to Jerusalem with great joy, continually praising God in the temple.

## The King Has Come

He came. He returned. He conquered death. He ascended to heaven. 

And He promises to come again.

Will you be ready? Will you know His Word? Will you point others to Him?
