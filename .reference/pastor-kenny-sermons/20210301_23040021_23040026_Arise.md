## Observing God Through His Handiwork

Isaiah speaks to a foundational question that echoes through the ages: How do we know God exists? He appeals not to philosophical arguments alone, but to evidence we can observe—to the handiwork of creation itself.

> Do you not know? Have you not heard? Has it not been told you from the beginning? Have you not understood since the earth was founded?

Isaiah directs our attention to the beginning of the universe itself. For most of human history, scientists believed in a steady-state universe—eternal, with no beginning and no end. But then came the discovery of the Doppler effect and measurements of the universe's rate of expansion. When you have a rate of expansion, you can work backwards to discover when everything began. Scientists discovered that our universe began with one enormous explosion of energy and light—what we now call the Big Bang. This singular event marked the beginning of everything that exists: the start of space, matter, and even time itself.

Here's what's remarkable: the Bible spoke of creation from the beginning, long before modern astronomy confirmed it. As the Psalmist wrote:

> The heavens declare the glory of God; the skies proclaim the work of his hands.

When we gaze up at the stars, we're drawn to ask the deepest questions. We see design, order, and complexity on a scale that demands explanation.

> He sits enthroned above the circle of the earth, and its people are like grasshoppers. He stretches out the heavens like a canopy, and spreads them out like a tent to live in.

Imagine the earth as a giant circus, with the heavens spread overhead like a tent. We are the grasshoppers inside. The natural question arises: Who is outside the tent? Who created this vast structure? At minimum, we should be curious.

### The Finely Tuned Universe

Contemporary astronomers and physicists have begun recognizing something remarkable about our universe: it appears impossibly fine-tuned for life. The fundamental forces, the location of Earth relative to Jupiter, our distance from the sun, the position of the Milky Way—countless parameters must fall within incredibly narrow ranges for complex life to exist.

John Polkinghorne, a physicist at Cambridge University who later became an Anglican priest, observed:

> When you realize that the laws of nature must be incredibly finely tuned to produce the universe we see, that conspires to plant the idea that the universe did not just happen, but that there must be a purpose behind it.

Allan Sandage, one of the most important astronomers of our time, calculated the Hubble constant and refined our understanding of the universe's age. Yet despite his scientific achievements, he was haunted by a fundamental question: Why is there something rather than nothing? As a young man, he considered himself almost an atheist. But through his scientific work, Sandage came to a profound realization:

> It was my science that drove me to the conclusion that the world is much more complicated than can be explained by science. It is only through the supernatural that I can understand the mystery of existence.

Astronomer George Smoot, reporting the findings of the Cosmic Background Explorer satellite, exclaimed:

> If you're religious, it's like looking at the face of God.

Why? Because something must have caused the Big Bang. Who else but God could accomplish such a thing? As astronomer Hugh Ross wrote:

> If the universe arose out of a big bang, it must have had a beginning. If it had a beginning, it must have a beginner.

The odds of a universe emerging by pure chance are septrillions to one. The evidence points not to random chance, but to intelligent design.

Charles Townes, who won the Nobel Prize in Physics for discovering the principles of the laser, observed:

> Many have a feeling that somehow intelligence must have been involved in the laws of the universe.

Since Isaac Newton, science has revealed that the world follows mathematical rules—rules humans can discover and understand. Yet this very rationality, this very order, points toward a mind behind creation.

### The Real Problem: Not Creation, But the Observer

Yet many refuse to see what the evidence displays. The problem is not God's absence from nature. The problem is us.

> He brings princes to naught and reduces the rulers of this world to nothing. No sooner are they planted, no sooner are they sown, no sooner do they take root in the ground, than he blows on them and they wither, and a whirlwind sweeps them away like chaff.

We resist acknowledging God because it would mean God has a claim over our lives. It would demand obedience. It would suggest accountability.

This is why many atheists, despite the evidence, work backwards from a desired conclusion. They begin with the assumption: "God does not exist." Then they attempt to construct arguments to justify that conclusion. But when you examine their reasoning carefully, you discover they engage in the same philosophical speculation they accuse Christians of practicing.

Atheists argue that Christians cannot know what happened before the universe existed because they weren't there. Yet the same criticism applies to them: How do they know something came from nothing? How do they know the universe wasn't created? Physicist George Ellis has noted:

> How can you test what existed before the universe existed? You can't.

Some atheists claim that true knowledge can only be empirically proven—a position called logical positivism. Yet the claim that "true knowledge can only be empirically found" is itself not empirically proven. It's assumed without justification. Why should we accept an unproven assumption?

When you examine contemporary atheist philosophy carefully, you discover endless speculation: that God is a concept outside existence and therefore nothing; that the universe is eternal with no beginning; that our entire existence is an illusion; that we're simulations created by alien beings. They've abandoned science entirely and entered the realm of pure philosophy—yet they criticize Christians for the same thing.

Those who live in glasshouses should not throw stones.

### The Consequence of Accountability

The real issue has nothing to do with science. It's about consequences. If God exists, we are accountable. That's what troubles people. They're happy to imagine aliens as our creators—aliens don't demand obedience, don't judge us, make no moral claims upon us.

But God? God brings princes to nothing. God calls us to account.

When people reject accountability to God, science itself suffers. Much of what passes for mainstream science today is not science at all—it's consensus, surveys, opinions dressed up with PhDs and credentials. True science demands testable, repeatable observations. Modern consensus claiming agreement proves nothing. Numbers and titles don't make speculation scientific.

## Jesus Appeared

But Christianity has a decisive advantage in this conversation. The universe remains silent about what caused the Big Bang. God, however, did not remain silent. God entered into time.

> In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God. He was with God in the beginning. Through him all things were made; without him nothing was made that has been made... The Word became flesh and made his dwelling among us.

Two thousand years ago, God became human. Jesus walked among us, and the human race conducted empirical observations:

- They tested his miraculous powers
- They listened to his wisdom
- They watched him bleed on the cross
- They witnessed his empirical resurrection and ascension

If you had lived in the first century, you could have taken a blood test, measured his height and weight, tested his reflexes, examined his miracles. These were observable facts, recorded by eyewitnesses.

Jesus is no longer physically present on earth—he rose from the dead and ascended to heaven. So we cannot conduct experiments on him today. But we have something equally valid: the historical testimony of eyewitnesses. Past scientific observation is called history. We rely on those who were there, who saw him, who documented what they witnessed.

### Comfort in the Storm

> To whom will you compare me? Or who is my equal? says the Holy One.

> Lift up your eyes and look to the heavens: Who created all these? He who brings out the starry host one by one and calls forth each of them by name. Because of his great power and mighty strength, not one of them is missing.

God knows each star by name. Not one is lost. Not one is forgotten.

> He gives strength to the weary and increases the power of the weak. Even youths grow tired and weary, and young men stumble and fall; but those who hope in the Lord will renew their strength. They will soar on wings like eagles; they will run and not grow weary, they will walk and not be faint.

Does God exist? The evidence from creation suggests yes. Did Jesus exist? History confirms it. He claimed to be God. If his words are true, then God exists.
