# 1 Timothy 2:1-7

**Title:** What Does It Take to Please God?

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Foundation: Prayer Pleases God

> First of all, then, I urge that supplications, prayers, intercessions, and thanksgivings be made for all people, for kings and all who are in high positions, that we may lead a peaceful and quiet life, godly and dignified in every way. This is good, and it is pleasing in the sight of God our Savior. (1 Timothy 2:1-3)

Paul begins by calling us to prayer—but not merely personal prayer. He calls for supplications, prayers, intercessions, and thanksgivings made *for all people*. This is the first pathway to pleasing God: lifting others before Him, including those in authority. When we pray this way, we align ourselves with God's character and demonstrate the honor He deserves.

## The Core of God's Pleasure: People Being Saved

> Who desires all people to be saved and to come to the knowledge of the truth. (1 Timothy 2:4)

At the heart of what pleases God lies His deep desire for salvation. God's pleasure is not found in abstract things but in the concrete reality of human beings coming to know Him through Christ. This reveals God's heart: He is a Savior who longs for all people to be saved and to come to knowledge of truth.

## The Mediator Who Pleases God: Jesus

> For there is one God, and there is one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus, who gave himself as a ransom for all, which is the testimony given at the proper time. (1 Timothy 2:5-6)

Jesus is the mediator—the bridge between God and humanity. He achieved this by giving Himself as a ransom for all. This is not something Jesus reluctantly did; His self-sacrifice was the ultimate act of pleasing the Father and fulfilling God's redemptive plan.

## How We Please God: Through Jesus as Our Ruler

Paul concludes by pointing to his own calling:

> For this I was appointed a preacher and an apostle (I am telling the truth, I am not lying), a teacher of the Gentiles in faith and truth. (1 Timothy 2:7)

We please God by following Jesus as our Ruler—just as Paul devoted his life to spreading this message. When we accept Jesus' mediatorial work, acknowledge God's desire for salvation, and live under His lordship, we participate in what pleases the Father.

## A Prayer That Reflects This Passage

Dear God, my Savior and Ruler,

Thank you for our government and for the peace and dignified lives they provide. I ask that they continue to govern wisely.

I confess that I have not pleased you in the past and acknowledge why you are angry with my sin.

I thank you for sending your Son, Jesus, to save me by pleasing you on my behalf through His ransom.

Help me now to please you by following Jesus as my Ruler and allowing His mediatorial work to transform my life.

Amen.
