# 1 Peter 1:1-2:3

**Title:** Alien Born  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Living Into Our Birthright

### Christians Are Born Into Living Hope

When we are born again as Christians, we enter into an extraordinary inheritance—a living hope that defines our identity as God's children. This is not merely wishful thinking, but a robust, living reality rooted in Christ's resurrection.

This hope carries with it an inheritance that belongs to us. Throughout Scripture, we see God's people receiving inheritances—Abraham received the land of Canaan, the tribes of Israel received their tribal territories, and the Levites received the Lord Himself as their inheritance. As Christians, we inherit something far greater: eternal life with our Father.

Our inheritance is protected. God guards it with a power that transcends our circumstances. Even when we face trials and doubts—and Peter acknowledges that genuine believers do experience doubt—our inheritance remains secure. Our hope is not diminished by present suffering but proves genuine through it. This hope points forward to a coming redemption, one that will be fully realized when we see Jesus face to face.

### Christians Are Born From the Living Word

Our new life does not rest on human authority or tradition, but on the living Word of God—a Word that was active in the past, is active in the present, and will remain active into the future.

The prophets of old spoke by the Spirit, searching into the mysteries of Christ's coming salvation. They testified beforehand of the sufferings and glories that would come. That living Word sustained God's people through centuries of waiting.

Today, that same living Word continues to sustain us. The gospel that was preached to us through the Holy Spirit is no less alive and powerful than it was in the days of the prophets. It challenges us, comforts us, and calls us forward.

And this Word will endure forever. While earthly powers crumble and human institutions fail, God's Word stands eternal. We must not allow cultural pressure to erode our faith or tempt us to assimilate into patterns that contradict Scripture. Instead, we must press forward in our allegiance to God's truth, knowing that this Word is our foundation.

## Living as Aliens on Earth

### Put Aside the Patterns of Your Old Life

As we transition into our new identity as God's people, we must consciously lay aside the behaviors and values that once defined us—malice, deceit, hypocrisy, envy, and slander. These patterns belong to our old self, not to our reborn identity.

### Grow in Genuine Longing for God

Like newborn infants who crave milk, we must develop an insatiable hunger for God's Word. This desire for spiritual nourishment is not a sign of immaturity but of genuine faith. As we taste the goodness of the Lord, our appetite for Him should only deepen.

This transformation happens as we remember who we truly are: aliens and strangers in this world, citizens of another kingdom entirely.

# 1 Peter 2:4-11

**Title:** Alien Nation  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Foundation of God's People

### Christ Is the Living Stone

Jesus Christ is the precious cornerstone upon which God's people are built. Though rejected by the world—stumbling over Him as a stone of judgment—He stands chosen and honored in God's sight. This echoes Isaiah's prophecy that God would lay in Zion a stone, tested and precious, on which the one who trusts will never be put to shame.

### We Are Built Into a Living Temple

The temple is no longer a building made of stone. In the Old Testament, God's presence dwelt in the tabernacle and temple—guiding His people in the wilderness, filling Solomon's temple with glory, departing in judgment through Ezekiel. But now, through Christ, that presence dwells in us. We are the temple of the Holy Spirit, the dwelling place of God's presence.

This truth demands that we treat one another with profound reverence. When we gather as God's people, we are meeting in the very sanctuary of God. Every interaction carries sacred weight because the Holy Spirit indwells both you and your brother or sister in Christ.

## Our Identity and Function

### We Are a Royal Priesthood

In the old covenant, only the tribe of Levi served as priests, offering sacrifices and interceding for the people. But Peter announces something revolutionary: all believers now function as priests. We have direct access to God, and we have been given the privilege of declaring His praises.

We declare these praises to one another, building up the body of Christ through our witness and encouragement. We also declare them to the watching world. Our lives become a living testimony that authenticates our faith and invites others into the hope we possess.

# 1 Peter 2:12-25

**Title:** Alien Strategy  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Living Distinctly in a Hostile World

### Conduct Yourself With Integrity

When the world observures your behavior, let it see something beautiful and true. Live in such a way that when others slander you, they cannot find fault in your actual conduct. Your integrity creates an opening for the gospel. When people witness your genuine goodness, some will be led to glorify God, while others will harden themselves against Him. But you will have created an opportunity for the truth to be heard.

### Submit to Authority Without Losing Your Freedom

Submit to every human authority established for the common good—whether to the king as supreme authority or to governors as his representatives. This obedience is not surrender of your conscience but a conscious choice to honor social order.

Yet understand that true freedom in Christ is paradoxical. You are free from sin, free from the fear of death, and free in your relationship with God. At the same time, you are a servant of all, subject not through coercion but through love. As Luther observed, a Christian is "a perfectly free lord of all, subject to none" and simultaneously "a perfectly dutiful servant of all, subject to all."

This is not hypocrisy but the highest freedom—choosing to serve because love constrains you, not because law compels you.

### Bear Suffering as Christ Did

For those enslaved to harsh masters, Peter offers something radical: they can glorify God through their suffering by following Christ's example. Christ suffered unjustly, without retaliation, entrusting His cause to the righteous Judge. When we endure wrongdoing with faith intact, we participate in the redemptive character of Christ.

This teaching does not sanctify slavery or abuse. Scripture distinguishes between slavery and slave trading, and prescribes protections and rights for enslaved people. Rather, Peter calls us to maintain our integrity and hope even in unjust circumstances—not to accept injustice as God's will, but to refuse to let it destroy our faith or character.

# 1 Peter 3:1-7

**Title:** Alien Relations  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Living Together in the Household

### Submission That Flows From the Heart

When Peter speaks of submission in marriage, he is calling for a willing orientation of the heart toward your husband. This submission is not coerced obedience but a conscious choice rooted in love and respect. When chosen freely, this submission becomes a powerful witness.

True beauty, Peter teaches, does not consist in elaborate outward adornment—clothing, jewelry, hairstyles—but in the hidden person of the heart. This beauty is imperishable and infinitely precious to God. The wife whose character reflects gentleness, quietness, and faith radiates a beauty that will not fade. Your husband may be won over not by arguments but by observing your genuine faith lived out in daily faithfulness.

### Honoring the Covenant

Wives honor their husbands. But this honoring is not absolute submission to every demand. God's Word is the highest authority. If a husband asks his wife to violate Scripture or her conscience, her loyalty to Christ takes precedence.

The question of what happens when a husband is not yet a believer deserves careful handling. Peter does not offer easy answers, only the call to remain faithful to your vows while remaining faithful to Christ. Your godly witness may be the very means through which God works in your husband's heart.

### The Gravity of Covenant Love

Husbands, understand that you bear responsibility for the spiritual and relational health of your family. Live with your wife in an understanding way, recognizing her worth as an equal heir of God's grace. To fail in this is to hinder your prayers and break the covenant bond. This is not incidental teaching—it strikes at the heart of what it means to be a father and a husband in God's household.
