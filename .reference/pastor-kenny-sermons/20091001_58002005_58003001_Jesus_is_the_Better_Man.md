# Hebrews 2:5-3:1

**Title:** Jesus is the Better Man

**Date:** 6 October 2009

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## What Is Man?

Throughout history, humanity has offered many answers to this fundamental question. Aristotle called us rational animals. Keynes saw us as economic consumers. Others have reduced us to machines, mere animals driven by instinct and hormone, or simply chemicals worth a few dollars. The writer of Hebrews asks this same question but arrives at a radically different conclusion—one centered entirely on Jesus.

## The Paradox: Low Yet Destined for Greatness

The author of Hebrews presents man with a stunning paradox: we are low in the chain of being, yet God has destined us for greatness.

### Man Is Lower Than the Angels

> "What is man that you are mindful of him, the son of man that you care for him? You made him a little lower than the angels; you crowned him with glory and honor and put everything under his feet." (Hebrews 2:6-8)

The angels are remarkable beings. Scripture shows us their power: they are stronger and more powerful than humans (2 Peter 2:11), capable of rolling away massive stones (Matthew 28:2), opening prison doors (Acts 5:19), and wielding divine authority. The cherubim in Solomon's temple stood fifteen feet tall with fifteen-foot wingspans. Angels have destroyed entire cities—Jerusalem nearly fell to an angel's judgment (1 Chronicles 21:15), Sodom and Gomorrah were utterly destroyed (Genesis 19), and the plagues of Egypt were carried out by destroying angels (Psalm 78:49). One angel alone struck down 185,000 Assyrian soldiers in a single night (Isaiah 37:36).

By contrast, man is lower than these magnificent beings. We are mortal and fragile. We possess no superhuman abilities—not the eyesight of an eagle, the strength of an ant, or the web-spinning skill of a spider. We are simply human.

### Yet God Has a Purpose for Man

Despite our frailty, God crowns man with glory and honor. He places everything under man's feet and leaves nothing that is not subject to him. This is the divine design: man is made to rule.

Consider the opening chapters of Genesis. The climax of creation is not the heavens or the earth—it is mankind. The animals are made for Adam to name. He is commanded to multiply, populate the earth, and have dominion over it. The entire world is a habitat created specifically for humanity.

And we have fulfilled this purpose remarkably well. Seven billion of us populate this earth today. We have built everything from the Great Wall of China to microprocessors. We have increased food production, diverted rivers, harnessed electricity, developed medicines, walked on the moon, and created Google. The world has been shaped by human ingenuity and rule.

## The Disappointment: We Do Not See Man in Charge

Yet the author of Hebrews draws a sobering conclusion:

> "Yet at present we do not see everything subject to him." (Hebrews 2:8)

The reality is starkly different from the ideal. Man as king is not true.

### The World Hits Back

The world is cursed because of man. We have consumed the earth's resources, polluted its rivers, and ruined its environment. The world does not submit to human rule—it strikes back. Bushfires, tsunamis, and snowstorms remind us that nature is not friendly to humanity. Death and disease are everywhere.

### Man Cannot Even Rule Himself

More painfully, man cannot rule himself. We fail at self-control. Our desires and passions overcome us. We fail to love one another. We cannot control our bodies from sickness. We may wear crowns over empires, but we have never conquered death and sorrow. We are destined to die—a reality we spend our lives trying to forget through youth culture, materialism, and endless distraction.

### A Dark Solution

In light of man's failure, some have concluded that the solution is to eliminate man altogether. The Matrix presents this as a provocative fiction: "You humans are a disease, a cancer of this planet." Some lobby groups like "No Kidding International" argue we should choose earth over birth, calculating that each child produces 1,600 metric tonnes of CO2 in their lifetime. Others advocate population control, abortion, eugenics, and euthanasia.

Yet this solution is like using a sledgehammer to kill a fly at dinner—you destroy the very house that gave the meal meaning. Yes, man is part of the problem, and we must consume less. But to remove man is to lose the reason the earth exists in the first place. The world is for people. It is good that the population grows. There are no useless people. Love humankind—welcome asylum seekers, embrace children, appreciate the world. We do not yet understand how many people can fit on this earth with wisdom and care.

Destroying man is not the solution.

## The Better Solution: Jesus in Charge

> "But we see Jesus, who was made a little lower than the angels, now crowned with glory and honor because he suffered death, so that by the grace of God he might taste death for everyone." (Hebrews 2:9)

Here is the stunning answer to man's failure: Jesus. God incarnate became human. The one who is higher than the angels became lower than the angels—mortal and vulnerable—and tasted death on behalf of all mankind.

### Jesus as the True Human

Jesus is the man. He is the completion of humanity. While man was made a prototype, Jesus embodies true humanity. He is:

- The one who does not sin, yet is fully tempted like us
- The one who rules perfectly, governing elements, calming storms, healing the sick, creating food from nothing
- The one who is completely other-centered, putting the interests of others before his own
- The one willing to die for others
- The one perfectly obedient to his Father

### Jesus Can Free Us from Eternal Death

> "Since the children have flesh and blood, he too shared in their humanity so that by his death he might destroy him who holds the power of death—that is, the devil—and free those who all their lives were held in slavery by their fear of death." (Hebrews 2:14-15)

The early church father Irenaeus understood something profound: what is incarnated is what is redeemed. Jesus can only redeem that which he is. If Jesus were merely an angel, he could redeem only angels. If he were a superhuman robot incapable of real temptation, he could redeem only robots. But Jesus became fully human so that he could redeem humanity.

Like someone taking the HSC exam for you—it must be another person, not an angel. Jesus faced sin like the rest of us. In Gethsemane, he genuinely prayed, "Not my will, but yours." He could truly swap places with us because he was truly one of us. Therefore, he can truly save us—not just from physical death, but from the second death, from hell itself.

> "For this reason he had to be made like his brothers in every way, in order that he might become a merciful and faithful high priest in service to God, and that he might make atonement for the sins of the people." (Hebrews 2:17)

### Jesus Can Help Us Now

> "Because he himself suffered when he was tempted, he is able to help those who are being tempted." (Hebrews 2:18)

When you struggle, Jesus knows. He understands your temptations, your pain, your fear. He has experienced everything you experience, yet he never sinned. This makes him perfectly able to take the penalty of our sins upon himself and to forgive any sin, no matter how grave.

There is no sinner here too bad for Jesus. If you are not yet a Christian, today is the day to trust him.

## The True Ideal of Humanity

The world tells us the ideal human is the richest, the smartest, the toughest, the most athletic, the most beautiful. Asian culture constructs its own ideal: the child who is lawful, married with one boy and one girl, invested in property—the perfect resume for proud parents.

But the real human, the true ideal, is the one who is kindest and most obedient—Jesus. He is the mortal who does not sin, not through stupidity or hypocrisy, but through genuine obedience. He is the mortal who rules perfectly. He is other-centered and willing to sacrifice everything.

### Living as Truly Human

What makes us truly human is not superhuman strength or achievement. It is love and sacrifice.

> "My command is this: Love each other as I have loved you. Greater love has no one than this, that he lay down his life for his friends." (John 15:12-13)

We may not become superhumans in this life, but we can all love. We can sacrifice daily for one another. We can take up our cross and follow Christ. We can offer our bodies as spiritual sacrifices to God.

> "Therefore, holy brothers, who share in the heavenly calling, fix your thoughts on Jesus, the apostle and high priest whom we confess." (Hebrews 3:1)

---

## Summary

**What is man?** Jesus is the man. He is not merely the sum of our experiences, nor are we mere chemicals or economic consumers. We are not accidents of nature or soulless machines. We are beings made for a purpose—to love, to sacrifice, to reflect the character of the one who created us.

If you want to be truly human, look to Jesus and be like him.
