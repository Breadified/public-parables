# 1 John 2:18-29

**Title:** Stick with Christ
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Problem: Things Don't Stick

We live in a world where adhesives fail. I have a car with a hole in the roof—sixteen years old now—patched with sticky tape, electrical tape, duct tape, even state-of-the-art double-layered color-matching tape. Yet when the rain comes, it all leaks. The glue loses its strength and becomes useless. We want things to stick. We want them to do their job.

John's first letter addresses something similar: the danger of not sticking with Christ.

## Antichrists Among Us

> Dear children, this is the last hour; and as you have heard that the antichrist is coming, even now many antichrists have come. This is how we know it is the last hour. They went out from us, but they did not really belong to us. For if they had belonged to us, they would have remained with us; but their going showed that none of them belonged to us. (1 John 2:18-19)

The passage describes people who have departed from the faith. They walked among the believers, but they did not remain. John calls them "antichrists."

We often think of "the Antichrist" as a Hollywood figure—some cosmic villain wielding fire and brimstone. But here, John uses the term more broadly. An antichrist is simply anyone who denies Christ.

The prefix "anti" means "against"—as in anti-pollution (against pollution) or anti-corruption (against corruption). So anti-christ means against Christ. By definition, an antichrist is anyone opposed to Christ. In our modern jargon, we'd simply say: a non-Christian.

## The Test: Acknowledging Jesus

> Who is the liar? It is the man who denies that Jesus is the Christ. Such a man is the antichrist—he denies the Father and the Son. No one who denies the Son has the Father; whoever acknowledges the Son has the Father also. (1 John 2:22-23)

The fundamental issue is simple: Do you acknowledge that Jesus is the Christ?

This is not about intellectual agreement or cultural Christianity. It's about genuine acknowledgment—a confession that places Jesus at the center of your faith and allegiance. Those who deny the Son, John insists, have also denied the Father. You cannot separate them. To reject Jesus is to reject God Himself.

## Remaining in the Truth

> See that what you have heard from the beginning remains in you. If it does, you also will remain in the Son and in the Father. And this is what he promised us—even eternal life. (1 John 2:24-25)

John exhorts his readers to hold fast to the original message—the gospel as they first received it. This foundational truth, if it remains in us, keeps us anchored in Christ and in the Father. It's the pathway to eternal life.

## The Anointing Within You

> As for you, the anointing you received from him remains in you, and you do not need anyone to teach you. But as his anointing teaches you about all things and as that anointing is real, not counterfeit—just as it has taught you, remain in him. (1 John 2:27)

John shifts focus here to the believers' resources. You have received an anointing—the Holy Spirit. This anointing is real, not counterfeit. It teaches you truth. You are not left defenseless against deception; the Spirit Himself indwells you to guide you into all truth.

This doesn't mean you don't need teachers or community. Rather, it means you have an inner witness to truth. You can discern between genuine teaching and falsehood because the Spirit within you testifies to what is real.

## The Call to Remain

> And now, dear children, continue in him, so that when he appears we may be confident and unashamed before him at his coming. If you know that he is righteous, you know that everyone who does what is right has been born of him. (1 John 2:28-29)

John's final appeal is clear: remain in Christ. This is not casual Christianity. This is a deliberate, sustained commitment to stick with Jesus—to let His truth stick to you and you to Him.

The promise is both comforting and motivating. When He returns, we will stand before Him confident and unashamed, not because of our perfection, but because we have remained in Him. And those who practice righteousness prove they have been born of Him.
