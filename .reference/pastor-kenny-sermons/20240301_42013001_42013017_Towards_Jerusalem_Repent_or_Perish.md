# Luke 13:1-17, 31-35

**Title:** Towards Jerusalem: Repent or Perish
**Date:** 17 March 2024
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Only a Few Will Repent (13:18-28)

### The Kingdom in Parables

Jesus teaches about the kingdom of God through two striking images. He compares it to a mustard seed that grows into a tree where birds nest in its branches, and to yeast hidden in three measures of flour that eventually works through all the dough.

> Then Jesus asked, "What is the kingdom of God like? What shall I compare it to? It is like a mustard seed, which a man took and planted in his garden. It grew and became a tree, and the birds perched in its branches." (Luke 13:18-19)
>
> "What shall I compare the kingdom of God to? It is like yeast that a woman took and mixed into about sixty pounds of flour until it worked all through the dough." (Luke 13:20-21)

These parables are typically interpreted as showing that God's kingdom will become large and prominent. However, Jesus immediately complicates this expectation with a sobering question.

### The Narrow Door and Difficult Truth

> Someone asked him, "Lord, are only a few people going to be saved?" (Luke 13:23)

Jesus responds with urgent words about the narrow door:

> "Make every effort to enter through the narrow door, because many, I tell you, will try to enter and will not be able to. Once the owner of the house gets up and closes the door, you will stand outside knocking and pleading, 'Sir, open the door for us.' But he will answer, 'I don't know you or where you come from.'" (Luke 13:24-25)

The tragedy deepens as those excluded argue their familiarity with Jesus:

> "Then you will say, 'We ate and drank with you, and you taught in our streets.' But he will reply, 'I don't know you or where you come from. Away from me, all you evildoers!'" (Luke 13:26-27)

### Exclusion and Reversal

> "There will be weeping there, and gnashing of teeth, when you see Abraham, Isaac and Jacob and all the prophets in the kingdom of God, but you yourselves thrown out. People will come from east and west and north and south, and will take their places at the feast in the kingdom of God. Indeed there are those who are last who will be first, and first who will be last." (Luke 13:28-30)

This passage echoes Paul's teaching that "not all Israel is Israel"—mere ethnic or cultural connection to God's people does not secure salvation. Many who expect to be included will find themselves excluded, while unexpected people from every corner of the earth will take their places at God's table.

### Application: The Call to Repentance

The kingdom grows mysteriously and persistently like yeast through dough, yet few respond to the call to repent. When the gospel is shared—whether through flyering, conversations, or proclamation—most will dismiss it, ignoring the invitation to turn back to God. Some blame their resistance on social circumstances: privilege, injustice, racism, and fears. Yet the narrow door remains, and the call to genuine repentance stands unchanged.
