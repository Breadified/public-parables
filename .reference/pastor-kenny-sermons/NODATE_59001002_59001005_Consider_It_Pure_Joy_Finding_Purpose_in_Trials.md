# James 1:2-5

**Title:** Consider It Pure Joy: Finding Purpose in Trials

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Consider It Pure Joy

> Consider it pure joy, my brothers and sisters, whenever you face trials of many kinds, because you know that the testing of your faith produces perseverance. (James 1:2-3)

This command seems counterintuitive. When facing difficulties—especially something like the HSC—our natural response is anxiety, not joy. I remember coming last in Extension Maths and vowing never to do it again. It felt like a failure. Yet looking back, it wasn't a bad thing at all.

The word "trials" here means tests. And James calls us to "pure joy"—not grim determination or resigned acceptance, but genuine rejoicing. The world tells us we should feel defeated when exams go poorly, that happiness would make us look arrogant or invite parental suspicion. But Scripture invites us into something radically different.

## Because Tests Produce Perseverance

> Let perseverance finish its work so that you may be mature and complete, not lacking anything. (James 1:4)

Here's why we can rejoice: tests are not the end of the story. They're stepping stones toward something greater.

Consider the HSC. You're not actually doing the HSC—you're becoming a nurse, a lawyer, an electrician. The exams are the pathway to your goals, not obstacles separate from them. You move *through* the tests toward completion, not away from them.

This is true for the Christian too. When James speaks of "testing of your faith," he's describing the process that shapes us into mature disciples. Being "complete, not lacking anything" means reaching that goal of spiritual maturity—becoming people of wisdom, integrity, and character.

**The Application:** With each day of study, you're getting closer. Every perseverance builds capacity for the next challenge. If you can survive the HSC, you can survive almost anything. And beyond just academic success, this season trains you for life: to be a wise professional, a great parent, a loyal citizen, a voice of reason in your community, and a grateful servant of God.

## When You Need Help, Ask

> If any of you lacks wisdom, you should ask God, who gives generously to all without finding fault, and it will be given to you. (James 1:5)

You are not alone in this journey. God stands ready to give wisdom generously to anyone who asks—without judgment, without hesitation. He is both willing and able.

Practically speaking, this extends to your tutors and teachers. They're here to help. For academic struggles, they can guide you through difficult concepts. For spiritual struggles, they can point you toward resources and support. Ask for help when you need it.

## The Encouragement

As you face these next few weeks, remember this: when worry creeps in and motivation fades, smile. Consider it pure joy. You have two anchors:

1. **Every day moves you closer.** These trials are not meaningless—they're building perseverance and maturity in you. You're not just surviving; you're becoming.

2. **You have help.** God gives generously. Your tutors, teachers, and community are here. You don't carry this weight alone.
