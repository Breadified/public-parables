## Understanding the Metaphor

The image of a wolf in sheep's clothing is ancient wisdom. Aesop's fables tell of a hungry wolf who couldn't hunt because shepherds were too watchful. One night he found a discarded sheepskin, dressed in it, and walked among the flock. A young lamb followed him unsuspecting—straight to slaughter.

Jesus uses this same imagery to warn us about false prophets. But the metaphor cuts deeper than mere deception. A naked wolf would scatter the flock in fear. But dressed in wool, the wolf can take time, choose his victims, and consume the innocent from within.

---

## 1. False Truths Are Attractive

> **Matthew 7:15** "Watch out for false prophets. They come to you in sheep's clothing, but inwardly they are ferocious wolves."

False prophets are literally "pseudo-prophets"—they sound like the real thing. True prophets call people back to God's revelation, challenging the present age with timeless truth. False prophets also call for change, but they lead people to follow their own agenda, not God's.

Israel's history reveals what characterizes false prophets. In **Jeremiah 23:13-14**, God identifies two hallmarks:

> "Among the prophets of Samaria I saw this repulsive thing: They prophesied by Baal and led my people Israel astray. And among the prophets of Jerusalem I have seen something horrible: They commit adultery and live a lie."

**The characteristics are clear:**

- Men of immoral character who live in deception
- They offer false hope through visions, false words, and false teachings
- Perversion of doctrine leads to perversion of flesh

This danger wasn't surprising to Jesus. He repeatedly warned of false prophets rising to mislead many (Matthew 24:11, 24:24). The apostle Paul echoed this warning with urgency:

> **Acts 20:29-31** "I know that after I leave, savage wolves will come in among you and will not spare the flock. Even from your own number men will arise and distort the truth in order to draw away disciples after them. So be on your guard! Remember that for three years I never stopped warning each of you night and day with tears."

The word "ferocious" means ravenous—sharp, calculating, intelligent predators who disguise themselves smartly. They use what's familiar to you:

- Authority you're willing to follow
- Traditions you recognize
- Language you speak
- Beliefs you respect

They infiltrate by exploiting your familiarity. Consider how we respect institutions like the tax office—we feel obligated, even intimidated. False teachers use this dynamic, combining authority with our fears and insecurities.

### The Mormon Example

Take Mormonism—the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints. Jesus' name is everywhere. A nativity scene on Pennant Hills Road displays his birth. You hear his name spoken with reverence. As a newcomer, you're drawn in.

But once inside, you discover their teaching has little to do with Jesus. They teach about Joseph Smith, Elohim, cosmic marriages, and becoming a god of your own planet. Jesus is the sheep's clothing. The wolf beneath teaches something entirely different.

Why use Jesus so prominently? Because everybody loves Jesus. The nativity scene attracts you. You hear his name respected. Then they start teaching you angels, red Indians, and their true doctrine. If they'd opened with Moroni and Joseph Smith, you'd have run away. But they come with baby Jesus—and you stay.

This is also true of Islam and Jehovah's Witnesses. They use Jesus as the gateway, then gradually redefine who he is and what he taught.

### What Attracts Christians

For those already in the Christian community, different temptations present themselves:

- **Prosperity doctrine**: Health, wealth, and happiness—a perversion of "ask and you shall receive"
- **Superstition**: Get-rich schemes promising something for nothing
- **False promises**: Quick solutions, easy answers, guaranteed results

But consider what the gospel actually offers: Not free (Christ paid it all), not wealthy in this life, not quick (we're still waiting for resurrection—patience required), and not easy (we face persecution).

## 2. Real Truths Produce Good Fruit

> **Matthew 7:16-20** "By their fruit you will recognize them. Do people pick grapes from thornbushes, or figs from thistles? Likewise, every good tree bears good fruit, but a bad tree bears bad fruit. A good tree cannot bear bad fruit, and a bad tree cannot bear good fruit. Every tree that does not bear good fruit is cut down and thrown into the fire. Thus, by their fruit you will recognize them."

This is the test. Not their appearance. Not their flowery words or gentle bleating. **Look at the fruit of their lives.**

The natural world obeys this law. Thornbushes don't produce grapes. Thistles don't produce figs. Your garden weeds don't somehow yield strawberries. Neither can a bad tree produce good fruit, nor a good tree produce bad fruit. This is not a suggestion—it's an immutable principle.

### Evaluating the Founders

Examine the personal lives of false prophets' founders:

Joseph Smith, Mormon founder, lived a life of deception and immorality. Mormons believed his teachings about polygamy until 1890—a lawless man producing lawless fruit.

Muhammad, Islam's founder, was a bloodthirsty warrior. By contrast, Jesus was lawful, living under authority even when unjust, paying taxes to an oppressive regime. He shed no blood except his own, given voluntarily for others.

This difference matters. Consider Asia Bibi, a Pakistani Christian woman. When asked about the difference between Jesus and Muhammad, she said: "Jesus Christ died on the cross for me—what did Muhammad ever do for you?"

The fruits reveal the tree. Perverse doctrines lead to perverse lives. Good doctrines lead to godly lives.

Over time, corruption cannot hide itself forever. Eventually, false gospels fail because they cannot address the fundamental human condition. Only the gospel of Christ truly rehabilitates the human heart. While decent followers of false religions may exist, they often thrive despite their religion, not because of it. The mediating influence of Christian culture in societies often makes false religions function better than they otherwise would.

But Jesus addresses the founders—the wolves, not the sheep. The false prophets who claim to speak for God are what we must evaluate.

### The Real Test

You cannot recognize wolves by their words alone. Look at the fruit of their lives and teachings. Fruit means reproduction and multiplication—bearing fruit that multiplies thirty, sixty, even a hundredfold. Paul speaks of Onesimus as "begotten" and Timothy as his "son in the faith." True prophets produce disciples who follow Christ, not themselves.

## 3. They Will Be Judged

> **Matthew 7:21-23** "Not everyone who says to me, 'Lord, Lord,' will enter the kingdom of heaven, but only the one who does the will of my Father who is in heaven. Many will say to me on that day, 'Lord, Lord, did we not prophesy in your name and in your name drive out demons and in your name perform many miracles?' Then I will tell them plainly, 'I never knew you. Away from me, you evildoers!'"

This passage disturbs many believers. They read it and wonder: Am I saved? Do I only say "Lord, Lord" without truly following? But read the context carefully. This passage targets false prophets—wolves in sheep's clothing within the Christian community.

The deeds they boast about are not what God requires. Their prophesying wasn't from God. Their miracles prove nothing; even the devil can perform false miracles (as Pharaoh's magicians matched Moses). What matters is that they distorted the truth for their own agenda.

God's standard is different. In the **Beatitudes (Matthew 5:3-12)**, Jesus reveals what truly matters:

> "Blessed are the poor in spirit...those who mourn...the meek...those who hunger and thirst for righteousness...the merciful...the pure in heart...the peacemakers...those persecuted for righteousness' sake."

If you place your trust in Christ in your spiritual poverty, in your humility, with no fruits to boast of—**you are saved.** The judgment passage is not about you. It's about the false prophets who come claiming authority they don't possess.

## 4. Wolves in Our Own Community

We recognize obvious false religions. Islam is plainly different from Christianity. Mormonism explicitly teaches contrary doctrine. But Jesus warned that wolves would come in sheep's clothing—true wolves, not strawmen easily dismissed.

So what false prophet actually threatens the church today?

There is a slow-creeping atheism clothed in Christian language. It uses words like stewardship, compassion, love, and equality—the language of Scripture. But it offers something Christianity doesn't: something for nothing.

### Cultural Marxism and Social Justice

When revolutionary Marxism failed after the world wars, ideologues shifted tactics. Workers proved more loyal to family, church, and nation than to class identity. So the strategy changed: undermine the political institutions, the church, and the family. This gradualist approach—cultural Marxism—aims to overturn society slowly through institutions.

The Fabian Society (founded England 1884) explicitly sought "democratic socialism via gradualist and reformist effort." The name comes from Roman general Fabius Maximus, who defeated enemies through attrition rather than direct confrontation. The motto was "march through institutions."

Today this appears as social justice—dressed in sheep's clothing:

1. **Looks like sheep**: Language of love, stewardship, equality, compassion
2. **Offers free lunch**: Wealth without work, electricity without mining, family without sex, rights without responsibility, belonging without citizenship
3. **Reveals its nature**: In league with those who deny biological sex, who would imprison you for your opinions, who exploit children for political purposes
4. **Always fails**: Every civilization that embraced Marxist ideology has collapsed under its weight

### The Danger Within

Christians unknowingly support this through social justice causes. Young believers are drawn to environmentalism, equity, and compassion—all biblically rooted values. But the gateway leads to abortion, euthanasia, and hostility toward traditional Christianity. It's the slow seduction into atheism and amorality.

**Social justice may be this generation's wolf in sheep's clothing.**

Yet we remain unaware. The enemy has told us their intentions plainly. The original Fabian Society logo depicted a wolf in sheep's clothing—they announced their strategy openly. The wolves come through politics and government, redefining marriage, controlling education, dictating what we may teach and believe.

This is why pastoral concern for worldview matters—not political partisanship, but defending the flock against the wolf that approaches through culture and government institutions.

## Final Word

The shepherd has a knife. The Aesop's fable concludes:

> "That evening the Wolf entered the fold with the flock. But it happened that the Shepherd took a fancy for mutton broth that very evening, and, picking up a knife, went to the fold. There the first he laid hands on and killed was the Wolf."

God's protection is sure. He knows his sheep. He knows the wolves. And he knows what must be done.

Our task is vigilance—not paranoia or conspiracy thinking, but clear-eyed discernment. Watch for what contradicts Scripture. Examine the fruits. Measure teachings against God's Word. And remember: **By their fruit you will recognize them.**
