# Numbers 25-26

**Title:** Seduced by Arousal  
**Date:** 13 October 2013  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## An Aroused Society

We live in a perpetually stimulated culture. Whether it's the constant ping of notifications, the rush of trending apps, or the endless scroll of social media, we're caught in a cycle of arousal. And nothing captures our attention quite like sexuality.

Sex is everywhere. Billboards dominate public spaces with hyper-sexualized messaging. Buses display semi-naked women. Pole-dancing themes appear in shopfronts. Pornographic magazines sit beside candy at petrol station counters. We've woven sexual imagery into our clothes, toys, computer games, anime, and music. Birthday parties for seven-year-old girls feature makeup lessons. Every television episode, magazine, newspaper, and catalog functions as soft pornography, deliberately designed to arouse.

Music videos directed at teenagers are sexually explicit. Even record companies know this—as Annie Lennox observed, "Certain record companies are peddling highly styled pornography with musical accompaniment." Hens parties center on lingerie or pole dancing lessons. We seem caught in an inescapable web.

Centuries ago, Israel faced similar temptations in the wilderness at Moab: arrogance, anger, avarice, and arousal. Their story speaks directly to our situation.

## 1. Sexual Immorality is a Sin Against God

> While Israel was staying in Shittim, the men began to indulge in sexual immorality with Moabite women, who invited them to the sacrifices to their gods. The people ate and bowed down before these gods. So Israel joined in worshiping the Baal of Peor. (Numbers 25:1-3)

The town of Shittim (meaning "acacia trees" in Hebrew) becomes the site of Israel's moral catastrophe. What begins as sexual immorality escalates into idolatry. The women seduce the men not just into sin, but into worshiping false gods.

This pattern reveals something crucial: **sex sells idolatry**. Advertisers understand this principle well. Sex gives products what marketers call "purchase advantage." We see sex used to sell everything—cars, deodorants, even organ donation and sliced mushrooms.

The Moabites weaponized sex to sell a false god. But consider: if something requires sex to entice you to buy it, that something is fake. The very fact that sex must be used to sell reveals that the product has no real value. No one uses sex to sell toilet paper—everyone needs it. When sex is used to sell freedom and liberation, we know it's a lie. All it delivers is bondage.

In a promiscuous society, women and children become victims: human trafficking, prostitution, objectification, abortion. This bondage pulls us away from the true God.

> And the LORD's anger burned against them... The LORD said to Moses, "Take all the leaders of these people, kill them and expose them in broad daylight before the LORD, so that the LORD's fierce anger may turn away from Israel." (Numbers 25:3-4)

A plague sweeps through the camp. People die. God's response is severe because the leaders—who bore moral authority and responsibility for instruction—did nothing to stop the immorality.

Why such violence? We must understand that sexual immorality breaks covenant with God. As Paul writes:

> Do you not know that the wicked will not inherit the kingdom of God? Do not be deceived: Neither the sexually immoral nor idolaters nor adulterers nor male prostitutes nor homosexual offenders nor thieves nor the greedy nor drunkards nor slanderers nor swindlers will inherit the kingdom of God. (1 Corinthians 6:9-10)

> Flee from sexual immorality. All other sins a man commits are outside his body, but he who sins sexually sins against his own body. Do you not know that your body is a temple of the Holy Spirit, who is in you, whom you have received from God? You are not your own; you were bought at a price. Therefore honor God with your body. (1 Corinthians 6:18-20)

When you sin sexually, you sin against God himself. Your body is not yours to do with as you please. Sex is not a private affair between consenting people—God is involved.

Israel made a covenant with God, much like a marriage vow. The leaders broke that covenant. They committed adultery against God. What does the jilted spouse do? We cannot treat offending God like a parking fine that we simply pay and move on. Our very lives depend on taking this seriously—not just in this life, but in the judgment to come.

## 2. Sexual Immorality is a Sin Against Each Other

> Then an Israelite man brought to his family a Midianite woman right before the eyes of Moses and the whole assembly of Israel while they were weeping at the entrance to the Tent of Meeting. (Numbers 25:6)

This is a shocking scene. Not content with secret immorality in Moab, this man brazenly brings a Midianite woman into the camp, into his family, before the entire congregation. The audacity signals something darker: the leaders themselves are complicit.

> When Phinehas son of Eleazar, the son of Aaron, the priest, saw this, he left the assembly, took a spear in his hand and followed the Israelite into the tent. He drove the spear through both of them—through the Israelite and into the woman's body. Then the plague against the Israelites was stopped; but those who died in the plague numbered 24,000. (Numbers 25:7-9)

In the chaos and paralysis, one man acts. Phinehas, zealous for God's honor, executes judgment. His action stops the plague—but 24,000 have already died.

The identities of the slain reveal the conspiracy:

> The name of the Israelite who was killed with the Midianite woman was Zimri son of Salu, the leader of a Simeonite family. And the name of the Midianite woman who was put to death was Cozbi daughter of Zur, a tribal chief of a Midianite family. (Numbers 25:14-15)

Zimri was no ordinary man—he was a tribal leader. His name appears in the census comparison between Numbers 1 and Numbers 26. While most tribes remained relatively stable over forty years, the Simeonites experienced a catastrophic 62.6% population decline. This wasn't coincidence. Zimri's leadership had steered his entire tribe into rebellion.

Cozbi's name is equally significant. In Akkadian, it sounds like "beautiful," but in Hebrew, it echoes "liar" and "deceiver."

> The LORD said to Moses, "Treat the Midianites as enemies and kill them, because they treated you as enemies when they deceived you in the affair of Peor and their sister Cozbi, the daughter of a Midianite leader, the woman who was killed when the plague came as a result of Peor." (Numbers 25:16-18)

Sexual immorality was not simply a personal failing—it was a calculated conspiracy. An alliance between a corrupt leader and foreign deceivers designed to seduce an entire nation away from God.

Consider the parallel in our time. In the 1980s, the AIDS epidemic began with a handful of key individuals who created a tipping point for global catastrophe. One man, a French flight attendant named Gaëtan Dugas, claimed 2,500 sexual partners across North America over twelve years. He was directly linked to forty of the first 248 reported AIDS cases in the United States—twenty percent of the initial epidemic. Researchers called him "Patient Zero." Even after learning he could infect others, he defiantly continued his practices.

Today, 1.7 million Americans live with HIV. Over 619,000 have died, with 17,000 dying annually. In Africa, the numbers are staggering. All of this traces back to the actions of a few defiant individuals.

If we could have stopped one man then, would it not have been reasonable? Would imprisonment, isolation, or other extreme measures have been excessive? Back in Numbers, 24,000 fathers, mothers, children, brothers, and sisters lay dead from an epidemic caused by the sexual immorality of a few. Is it unreasonable that Phinehas stopped it? Is it unjust that those few paid the ultimate price?

**Sexual immorality harms everyone.** All sexual activity outside of marriage damages the community:

- **Incest, pedophilia, and rape** are obviously destructive.
- **Adultery** breaks up families, diverting a parent's affection and resources away from their spouse and children.
- **Homosexual sex** stokes twisted desires in both participants.
- **Fornication**—sex between the unmarried, whether casual hookups, cohabitation, or "friends with benefits"—undermines commitment and increases the likelihood of relationship dissolution.
- **Prostitution** unites a person with something Jesus himself prohibited, creating spiritual infection that spreads to spouses.
- **Pornography** is perhaps the most insidious because it masquerades as private.

Sex with pornography rewires the brain. Neurological research shows that internet pornography affects the human brain as potently as cocaine or heroin—yet there are 40 million regular pornography users in America compared to 1.9 million cocaine users and 2 million heroin users. The effect simulates both substances combined, and tolerance builds over time, requiring escalation.

Sex is designed to build bonding between spouses, not addiction to fantasy. When we condition ourselves to use sex for our own pleasure rather than mutual connection, we train ourselves to be sexually selfish. This directly contradicts God's design.

Pornography rewires us to believe lies:
- That women naturally look airbrushed and perfected
- That these sexual acts represent normal intimacy
- That this is how we find true satisfaction and pleasure

Like all lies, pornography seduces, uses, then destroys. It kills relationships, robs us of joy, makes us lonelier, causes us to doubt God's love, and ultimately destroys us.

Women face similar temptations, though often expressed differently. Rather than visual stimulation, the temptation manifests as fantasy—through romance novels like *Fifty Shades of Grey* or Korean dramas that create unrealistic expectations of life and men.

When we disregard God's laws, we only hurt ourselves and others.

## 3. The Priestly Rescuer

> The LORD said to Moses, "Phinehas son of Eleazar, the son of Aaron, the priest, has turned my anger away from the Israelites; for he was as zealous as I am for my honor among them, so that in my zeal I did not put an end to them. Therefore tell him I am making my covenant of peace with him. He and his descendants will have a covenant of a lasting priesthood, because he was zealous for the honor of his God and made atonement for the Israelites." (Numbers 25:10-13)

God's assessment of Phinehas's act:
- He turned God's anger away from Israel
- He was zealous for God's honor
- He made atonement for the people
- His action brought peace between Israel and God

For this, Phinehas receives an eternal priesthood. He represents God himself.

Fast forward a thousand years. Another priest comes to the land—one named Jesus, a priest in the order of Melchizedek. Like Phinehas, he is zealous for God's honor. He clears the temple, angry at the desecration of God's house. Like Phinehas, he turns God's anger away and makes atonement.

But there is a crucial difference: Jesus became the sacrifice himself.

> Unlike the other high priests, he does not need to offer sacrifices day after day, first for his own sins, and then for the sins of the people. He sacrificed for their sins once for all when he offered himself. (Hebrews 7:27)

Jesus was not the one wielding the spear. He was the peace-bringer. Yet he himself was pierced. On the cross, a Roman centurion drove a spear into his side, and water and blood rushed out for all to see. Our leader died under punishment—but not for his own sin. He was punished for ours. For our stupid sins, our foolish sins, our defiant sins, our sexual sins.

## The Bride of God

Like a bride who forgets the greatness of her groom, we are easily seduced by an interloper. When the bride realizes her mistake and returns to her first love, she feels regret, hopelessness, anger at herself, and embarrassment at how easily she fell.

But Christ's forgiveness towers above all of this. His love and acceptance say: Repent. Turn away from that idol. Come back.

Come back regardless of who you've slept with, who you've fantasized about, what you've watched, or what unholy sex you've engaged in. Come back whether you feel distant from God or angry at yourself. God was furious. But God himself, through Jesus, has dealt with that anger. Christ has paid that debt in full and more. He drank God's judgment to the last drop.

> Do you not know that the wicked will not inherit the kingdom of God? Do not be deceived: Neither the sexually immoral nor idolaters nor adulterers nor male prostitutes nor homosexual offenders nor thieves nor the greedy nor drunkards nor slanderers nor swindlers will inherit the kingdom of God. And that is what some of you were. But you were washed, you were sanctified, you were justified in the name of the Lord Jesus Christ and by the Spirit of our God. (1 Corinthians 6:9-11)

## Practical Wisdom for Today

How do we guard ourselves in an aroused culture?

- **Be intentional about media.** Watch films with family and friends, not alone. Don't accept the excuse that "the plot is really good" when you're being seduced by sexual content. The plot is the lure.
- **Make computers and screens public.** Remove televisions and computers from bedrooms. Darkness enables deception; light exposes it.
- **Confess your sins.** Wounds heal in sunlight; darkness compounds infection.
- **Build healthy tiredness.** Stay busy so that by day's end, you're too weary for temptation's whisper.
- **Uphold God's standards.** God's laws exist to protect us, not restrict us.

God's law regarding lifelong, monogamous marriage has protected societies for thousands of years. When we abandoned these standards in the sexual revolution of the 1960s, we didn't gain freedom—we opened the door to epidemic. The very laws that could have slowed the spread of AIDS were dismantled as "old-fashioned" and "restrictive."

We cannot turn back time. But we can think carefully about the laws we change and the standards we abandon. God's law is given in love, restraining our worst impulses in a fallen world. Let us persevere knowing that God has anticipated our failures and provided a remedy in Christ.
