# Ephesians 1:13-14

**Title:** You Were Included  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## The Question of Inclusion

We all know the feeling of wanting to belong. As children, we remember that first day of school—the anxiety of wondering if we'd fit in, if we'd be accepted by our peers, if we'd have friends. As parents, we carry similar concerns for our own children: Will they be included? Will they be treated well? Will they find their place?

The early Christians faced this same fundamental question, but on a spiritual level: *Am I included with God?* Many of them came from pagan backgrounds, having lived their entire lives separated from Him—worshipping idols, trapped in greed, anger, bitterness, and sin. Their very nature seemed to disqualify them from belonging to God.

## Christ Included Us

Yet something miraculous happened. Listen to Paul's words:

> And you also were included in Christ when you heard the message of truth, the gospel of your salvation. When you believed, you were marked in him with a seal, the promised Holy Spirit, who is a deposit guaranteeing our inheritance until the redemption of those who are God's possession—to the praise of his glory. (Ephesians 1:13-14)

This is nothing short of a miracle. People who were once dead in their sins—greedy, bitter, angry, lying idolaters—were suddenly accepted and included in Christ. How? Through the gospel of salvation. Jesus died to remove the separation between us and God, and now He forgives us and includes us with Himself.

Notice the promise embedded in these verses: the Holy Spirit serves as a seal and a deposit, guaranteeing our inheritance in heaven. This means our inclusion isn't temporary or uncertain—it's eternally secured. Even death cannot separate us from Christ.

## What It Means to Be Included

Being included in Christ happens through two simple acts:

**First, we hear the message of truth.** A Christian is someone who listens to God's truth and believes God at His word.

**Second, we believe.** A Christian acknowledges God's diagnosis of their condition—that we are sinful and separated from Him—and accepts the gospel of salvation.

This faith journey isn't always straightforward. We may wrestle with doubts along the way. But at the end of the day, genuine faith means choosing to believe God despite our questions.

## Each Person Gains the Benefits

When we include Christ in our lives, we receive all these benefits. The transformation is real. Consider someone who once said they "hated reading the Bible" but now reads Scripture each evening with their father before sleep. That's what inclusion in Christ produces—a radical reorientation of the heart toward God and His Word.

## Chris Included Others

But there's another dimension to this passage that becomes clear when we think about someone who truly understood inclusion. When Chris embraced Christ, he didn't keep that blessing to himself—he became a channel of inclusion for others.

**He brought people into the church.** He welcomed newcomers and made them feel at home. He invited friends to church gatherings and made space for them in his life.

**He genuinely cared about people.** One friend wrote: "You would bounce from group to group asking people how their week was. You would ask if I wanted a back massage, we would chat about Kenny's talk, and you would tell me how you 'almost fell asleep' during his talk." He wanted to be included in everyone's lives, and he wanted everyone to feel included in his.

**He loved with unconditional love.** Another friend reflected: "One day when I am better at the gift of love, I will say I learnt it from you." This is what genuine inclusion produces—a heart that naturally opens to others.

**He showed visible transformation.** After the pandemic lockdowns lifted, something shifted in Chris. He attended church faithfully every single week. He began doing things he'd never done before—like buying a drink for someone at the Asian supermarket and offering it with genuine care. These small acts revealed a heart increasingly shaped by Christ's inclusion of him.

## Why We Miss Him So Much

It's no wonder the church feels his absence so deeply. He wasn't just a member—he was a beloved presence. Someone said he was among her "top 3 favourite people at church." His weeks were full not because he was obligated, but because he genuinely wanted to be part of everyone's lives: church gatherings, dinners, basketball games, karaoke dates, movies, wedding receptions. He was so included and so beloved that he was invited to events where others weren't—not because of status, but because people wanted him there.

## The Assurance We Hold

To Chris's family—Uncle, Aunty, and Alex—we want you to know with certainty:

**Yes, Chris was included.** His friends loved him, cared for him, and prayed for him. He felt the warmth of genuine Christian community.

**Yes, Chris is included in Christ.** He believed the gospel. He was marked with the seal of the Holy Spirit. His inheritance in heaven is guaranteed. Even in death, he is not separated from his Savior.

## Prayer

Heavenly Father, we thank you for Chris's life on earth and for the joy and comfort he brought to others. We thank you for rescuing him and giving him eternal life. Help us now as we grieve—help us to grieve with hope and with purpose. May we see the example of Christ-likeness that Chris embodied, and may his life inspire us to love others more deeply and more freely. Amen.
