# Romans 1:18-32

**Title:** The Wrath of God
**Date:** 4 November 2012
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction: We Know Nothing?

There's a famous defense in legal proceedings—the "Sergeant Schultz defense" from the 1960s sitcom Hogan's Heroes. When confronted with inconvenient facts, the character simply says, "I see nothing, I know nothing." This tactic has been recycled through history under various names: the "dumb CEO defense," the "ostrich defense," and more recently, the "Sam Bankman-Fried defense."

When it comes to God and the world's condemnation, Paul asks a penetrating question: Do we know nothing, or do we know something? His answer will shake us.

## 1. We Know (Romans 1:18-21)

> The wrath of God is being revealed from heaven against all the godlessness and wickedness of people, who suppress the truth by their wickedness. Since what may be known about God is plain to them, because God has made it plain to them. For since the creation of the world God's invisible qualities—his eternal power and divine nature—have been clearly seen, being understood from what has been made, so that people are without excuse. For although they knew God, they neither glorified him as God nor gave thanks to him, but their thinking became futile and their foolish hearts were darkened.

Paul is clear: we know something. In fact, we know logically and empirically. The knowledge of God is accessible through both reason and observation.

### Natural Revelation

Natural or general revelation is the discovery of God's attributes through studying creation. This contrasts with written revelation—the Bible—where God communicates with us directly.

The logic seems simple enough. Creation bears the fingerprints of God's mind and character. When you observe a building's intricate design and careful construction, you can conclude three things without knowing the builder: there was a designer, the designer is skilled, and the designer values beauty and order. Similarly, from creation's complexity we can deduce God's existence through two classic arguments.

**The Cosmological Argument:** We know cause precedes effect. The universe—composed of space, time, and matter—must have a first cause. This first cause must be God, and we can describe God as:
- Independent of space (limitless)
- Independent of time (eternal)
- Independent of matter (immaterial)

These are God's invisible attributes, accessible to all creation.

**The Designer Argument:** Creation's intricacy indicates designed features. This implies a designer—God. Everyone has access to creation, so everyone knows God's attributes. Therefore, no excuses.

### The Problem with Natural Revelation Alone

However, there's a critical flaw in relying on natural revelation alone. Paul wrestles with this honestly.

When we look at a building, how do we know the designer exists rather than it being random generation? When we see a strange or flawed building, we might conclude there's no designer at all. If we observe corruption in the world, does that prove there's no designer—or perhaps a flawed one? And even if we conclude there's a designer, how do we know it's one God rather than multiple gods? These are subjective interpretations.

With the cosmological argument, we're really just guessing. We assume there's a first cause, but how do we know it isn't infinite regress? How do we know there wasn't multiple universes?

The fundamental problem: without already knowing God, we cannot reliably read Him from nature. We might look at a cow—so useful to humanity—and decide God looks like a cow. Without direct revelation, there's no reliable knowledge of the true God.

Medieval theology fell into this trap, trying to logically infer God's purposes rather than reading Scripture directly. The Reformation correctly addressed this error.

### Adam and Israel: The Real Knowledge

Paul's passage actually refers to two specific groups who knew God directly: **Adam and Israel**.

**Adam's Knowledge:**
- Adam lived in the garden with God, who walked among him and Eve
- God literally appeared so Adam could know Him directly
- Adam was present at creation itself, naming the animals
- Yet like Romans 1 describes, although Adam knew God, he failed to honor Him as God
- His thinking became futile; his heart darkened
- He desired to be like God, to know good and evil

Adam's fall reveals a reversal of creation:
- Man is made in God's image, but now God is made in man's image
- Mankind should govern animals, but now the serpent governs mankind

**Israel's Knowledge:**
The passage parallels another historical moment. Notice the language echoes Genesis 1:26 and the golden calf incident:

> At Horeb they made a calf and worshiped an idol cast from metal. They exchanged their glorious God for an image of a bull, which eats grass. They forgot the God who saved them, who had done great things in Egypt. (Psalm 106:19-21)

Israel received direct revelation at Mount Sinai. God spoke to Moses and the people. The Law explicitly warned against making idols:

> You saw no form of any kind the day the Lord spoke to you at Horeb out of the fire. Therefore watch yourselves very carefully, so that you do not become corrupt and make for yourselves an idol, an image of any shape, whether formed like a man or a woman, or like any animal on earth or any bird that flies in the air, or like any creature that moves along the ground or any fish in the waters below. (Deuteronomy 4:15-18)

Yet upon Moses's return, they built a golden calf. Israel was given all the knowledge of God but ignored it. There is no excuse.

### We Are in Adam

Paul's conclusion applies to all of us. Whether you're a Jew condemned as an Israelite, or a non-Jew condemned as descended from Adam—we all stand under the same judgment.

We might protest: "I am not Adam. I was never presented with direct revelation." But this misses something crucial about how humanity works. We inherit both the wisdom and the sins of our ancestors. We don't start with a clean slate. We benefit from 2,000 years of Confucian ethics, from centuries of national development, from our parents' decisions to migrate to prosperous lands, from their investment in our education.

Similarly, we inherit the consequences of Adam's sin. We accept all the good things about being human. We also accept all the bad things. And one of those bad things is that our ancestor Adam sinned, and in him, we are all now condemned.

The conclusion for Adam, for Israel, and for us is the same: **We know. We have no excuse.**

## 2. We Refuse to Know (Romans 1:18)

But here's the deeper problem:

> The wrath of God is being revealed from heaven against all the godlessness and wickedness of people, **who suppress the truth** by their wickedness.

It's not that we don't know. The problem is that **we refuse to know**.

Like Sergeant Schultz, we choose ignorance. We suppress the truth. And the consequences match the crime perfectly.

## 3. The Cascading Consequences (Romans 1:23-32)

### Corruption Begins with Idolatry

> They exchanged the glory of the immortal God for images made to look like a mortal human being and birds and animals and reptiles. (1:23)

When we suppress the truth, what knowledge of God can we obtain? We get it from our imagination and from nature. We see a cow—such a useful animal—and think perhaps God looks like a cow. We see a powerful person and imagine God must resemble them. We worship celebrities, spend money on magazines about them, imitate their clothing and lifestyle. Or we look to ourselves: "My needs are the most important. I come first. I think, therefore I am. iPhone. iPad. I."

This idolatry—worshiping created things instead of the Creator—sets everything else in motion.

### Consequence 1: Corruption of Our Bodies

> Therefore God gave them over in the sinful desires of their hearts to sexual impurity for the degrading of their bodies with one another. (1:24)

When we refuse to acknowledge God, our thinking becomes futile and our hearts darken. The consequence: our bodies become degraded. Like a disease moving from brain to heart to multiple organ failure, sin corrupts our physical bodies.

We dishonored God, so we dishonor ourselves.

### Consequence 2: Corruption of Relationships

> They exchanged the truth about God for a lie, and worshiped and served created things rather than the Creator. Because of this, God gave them over to shameful lusts. Even their women exchanged natural sexual relations for unnatural ones. In the same way the men also abandoned natural relations with women and were inflamed with lust for one another. Men committed shameful acts with other men, and received in themselves the due penalty for their error. (1:25-27)

Sexual immorality follows idolatry. They go hand in hand. Once you suppress the truth, let your imagination run wild about what your god should look like, and suddenly there's no morality left. Right is whatever you think is right. Wrong is whatever you dislike.

The argument from nature cuts both ways. Some claim a "gay gene" makes homosexuality natural. But Paul uses the very same argument: these relations are "contrary to nature." Which nature is right? This is precisely where natural revelation fails. **We need something outside this world to tell us what is right and wrong.**

### Consequence 3: Corruption of Morality

> Furthermore, just as they did not think it worthwhile to retain the knowledge of God, so God gave them over to a depraved mind, so that they do what ought not to be done. They have become filled with every kind of wickedness, evil, greed and depravity. They are full of envy, murder, strife, deceit and malice. They are gossips, slanderers, God-haters, insolent, arrogant and boastful; they invent ways of doing evil; they disobey their parents; they have no understanding, no fidelity, no love, no mercy. Although they know God's righteous decree that those who do such things deserve death, they not only continue to do these very things but also approve of those who practice them. (1:28-32)

Notice that final phrase: they "approve of those who practice them." This is crucial. Not only do they sin; they celebrate sin in others. They legislate it. They redefine it as virtue.

We see this cascade playing out:
- After the same-sex marriage debate began, we see approval of polygamy, of pederasty, of pedophilia
- Late-term abortions increasing, even after 28 weeks when babies could be safely delivered
- A society that knows God's righteous decree yet actively promotes its violation

Our bodies, relationships, and morality become like animals. The corruption becomes almost complete.

## 4. The Real Problem is Moral, Not Logical

Kenny emphasizes what many miss: **this is not a logical problem. It's a moral problem.** We don't want to know.

We know something is wrong, but we choose not to acknowledge it. Like Sergeant Schultz, we say, "I see nothing, I know nothing."

## 5. Why We Need Direct Revelation

Natural revelation cannot be enough. Just looking at creation, without God's direct Word, leads to idolatry and corruption.

We need something outside this world to tell us what is right and wrong. We need the Bible. We need Jesus.

This has profound implications for evangelism. Your friends will not work out Jesus by themselves. They cannot guess God. They cannot intuit the gospel through stargazing or study. **They need you.**

Don't suppress the truth. Tell your friends. Don't be ashamed. There is no other revelation available to them.

In a Christian society, the famous saying works: "Preach the gospel at all times, and when necessary, use words." But in a post-Christian world that has suppressed truth, your words are essential. When you speak truth—about marriage, about human dignity, about the sanctity of life—you'll be vilified. They'll call you hateful. But they've exchanged the truth for a lie. You're their only hope.

## Conclusion: The Wrath and the Gospel

> For the wrath of God is being revealed from heaven against all the godlessness and wickedness of people, who suppress the truth by their wickedness.

The wrath of God is real. Idolatry, immorality, and all manner of wickedness reveal it. Few of us make it through life without sin—and even those who try are complicit. We vote for wickedness out of self-interest. We buy cheap clothes made by exploited workers. We inherit both the benefits and the curse of Adam's fall.

But here's the transforming truth: if you are forgiven through Christ, you are not someone who suppresses the truth. You are someone who receives it, acknowledges it, and submits to it. That's the difference between us and those under God's wrath.

The gospel is revealed for this reason—to save us from the very judgment Romans 1 describes. **We need Jesus. The power of salvation. There is no other way.**
