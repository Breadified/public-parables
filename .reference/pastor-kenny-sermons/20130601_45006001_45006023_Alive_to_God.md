# Romans 6:1-23

**Title:** Alive to God  
**Date:** 16 June 2013 / 20 January 2025  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## The Logical Question

Over the last two weeks, we've traced the great arc of Scripture: Creation and Redemption. But now we face an urgent question: If Jesus has forgiven all our sins—past, present, and future—does that mean we can continue sinning without consequence?

The logic seems airtight. Christ's death was powerful enough to cover every sin. If God has accepted us at our worst and secured our salvation forever, then nothing we do in the future can jeopardize that acceptance. God has forgiven it all.

This is exactly the question Paul anticipates in Romans 6:

> What shall we say, then? Shall we go on sinning so that grace may increase? (6:1)

Paul is referring back to 5:20b, where he declares that grace abounds. If grace abounds to cover our sins, the natural follow-up question is: Can I abound in sin then?

## Paul's Answer: By No Means

Paul's response is emphatic and decisive: "By no means!" (6:2)

He gives us two profound reasons why we should no longer sin:
1. We have died to the power of sin
2. We are freed from slavery to sin

---

## Part One: We Have Died to the Power of Sin

### The Death That Happened to You

Paul's first argument rests on a reality already accomplished: we have already died to sin.

> We are those who have died to sin; how can we live in it any longer? (6:2b)

To make this argument concrete, Paul appeals to something every believer has experienced: baptism.

> All of us who were baptized into Christ Jesus were baptized into his death. (6:3)

Baptism is a water ritual, a symbol of repentance and conversion. When we were baptized, we declared before God and others: "I am turning away from my old life. I am following Jesus now." We joined ourselves to Jesus, and that joining meant being baptized into his death.

> We were therefore buried with him through baptism into death. (6:4)

Think about what this means. Just as Christ died, so we died. On the day you became a Christian—whether or not there was a baptism ceremony, whether or not you told anyone—something inside you died. Sin died. It no longer has power over you. It cannot kill you anymore.

The answer to the earlier question becomes simple: We do not sin anymore because we are dead to sin. Why would a dead person serve a dead master?

### A Picture: The Cancer

Imagine you discovered cancer in your body. You did what anyone would do—you found the best doctor you could afford. You made the appointments. You paid the fees. You faced surgery.

While waiting for that operation, you lived in fear. You thought about death. Maybe you wrote a will. There were tears and worry and pain. But then came the day of surgery. The cancer—that life-sucking abnormality—was cut out and removed. It died.

Now imagine if, after recovery, someone asked you: "Since you've been healed, would you like to put the cancer back? Let me frame a photograph of it. Display it in your lounge room. Play with it. Put it next to your pillow. Maybe in another part of your body? Let's see how that feels. Maybe it won't hurt this time."

What a dumb idea.

Sin is that cancerous thing that was inside us, killing us slowly. Jesus removed it. Jesus killed it. So why on earth would we want sin back again? Why hang out with death?

### Remember What Sin Cost You

Do you remember what sin took from you? The tears. The sadness. The brokenness. The fights. The hurt it caused others. The frustration, disappointment, hopelessness, and despair. The guilt that made you feel dead on the inside.

That's why you went to God. That's why you repented. You knew you had done wrong. You knew you couldn't fix it yourself. You knew you needed to be changed. So you went to Jesus. You died to that life.

Now sin offers you nothing worth having. Gossip. Greed. Stealing. Cheating. Anger. Pornography. Adultery. Idolatry. These things only promise more sadness, more despair, more guilt, more disappointment.

**Please do not go back to sin.** Jesus has given you a second chance at life. Struggle against temptation. Speak to someone about it, because sin hates to be exposed. Help each other keep sin dead.

Instead, remember that you are raised with Christ for life. Jesus forgave you. He loves you. He gave you hope, purpose, security, and peace. You have friendship, relationship, contentment, and true love. You are alive with him.

## Part Two: We Are Freed from Serving Sin

### The Two Masters

Paul now repeats his earlier question:

> What then? Are we to sin because we are not under law but under grace? (6:15)

And he gives the same answer: "By no means!"

This time, Paul uses the language of slavery to make his point clear. He speaks in terms everyone understands—the master-slave relationship:

> Do you not know that if you present yourselves to anyone as obedient slaves, you are slaves of the one whom you obey, either of sin, which leads to death, or of obedience, which leads to righteousness? (6:16)

Here's the reality: you are like a slave. There are two masters you can choose to serve. The first is Sin. The second is God. Which master do you want to follow?

### The Cruel Master: Sin

The first master is cruel. You know what slavery to this master looks like:

> Just as you used to offer yourselves as slaves to impurity and to ever-increasing wickedness, so now offer yourselves as slaves to righteousness leading to holiness. (6:19)

This echoes the image of the Israelites enslaved to Pharaoh in Egypt. Pharaoh kidnapped them. He tied them up and held them captive in his domain. He forced them to serve him. He put them in chains. He punished them. He abused them. They could not escape.

What did sin make you do? It wasn't righteousness. It made you do all sorts of things. Sin abused you. It ruined your life. It wasted away your days. It left you for dead. And when you recovered, it started the abuse again.

> What benefit did you reap at that time from the things you are now ashamed of? Those things result in death! (6:21)

Nothing. Only shame and death. Sin abused your body and left you to rot.

But then you cried out: "If only someone could save me from this agony!"

### The Gentle Master: God

And another Master arose—a different kind of Master altogether. This Master heard your cry. He is not like Sin. His yoke is light. His burden is not heavy. Jesus defeated Sin, and then He freed you.

> But now that you have been set free from sin and have become slaves of God, the benefit you reap leads to holiness, and the result is eternal life. (6:22)

> For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord. (6:23)

### The Question Makes No Sense

So to ask, "Shall I sin?" is like an Israelite who escaped Egypt asking, "Should I go back to slavery?" It doesn't make sense.

When I was a teenager, I worked in a grocery shop called Franklins. The conditions were poor. I had to lift dusty, dirty boxes. There was no barcode scanner, so everything was done by hand. I stood on my feet three to six hours at a time, all for $5 an hour. It was dirty, heavy, boring, poorly paid work.

If I had the choice, why would I ever go back to that work? Now I have an air-conditioned office. Why would I return to those conditions?

The same applies to sin. Why go back to sin when you have God as your Master? Yes, following God can be hard. Yes, you are slaves to God. But His rule is far better. He is gentle, kind, and loves you. He died for you. What other master would do that?

You are no longer under sin's control. You have been freed.

### Serving the Better Master

Instead, let us serve God wholeheartedly:

> Do not let sin reign in your mortal body so that you obey its evil desires. Do not offer any part of yourself to sin as an instrument of wickedness, but rather offer yourselves to God as those who have been brought from death to life; and offer every part of yourself to him as an instrument of righteousness. (6:12-13)

> For sin shall no longer be your master, because you are not under the law, but under grace. (6:14)

Use your body, your limbs, as instruments for righteousness. Let us serve Him wholeheartedly. Jesus is a kind Master who cares for us, who wants only the best for us, who showed His love on the cross.

Let us use our eyes to see the needs of others. Let us use our mouths to speak wholesome words. Let us use our hands to help the weak and serve one another. Let us use our feet to come to church. Let us use our faces to smile at one another, knowing that Jesus died for that brother and sister of yours.

---

## A Realistic Hope

This does not mean we will live a perfect life. I think we are foolish and forget. But hopefully, we will quickly repent and get back on track with God. And God will forgive you—not because you are sinless, but because His Son was sinless.

As we remember that the sinless Son died for us, I think logically we wouldn't want to sin anymore. We have died to sin. We have been freed from its power. Now let us live this new life, serving only God.
