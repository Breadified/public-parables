# Galatians 1:1-24

**Title:** The One and Only  
**Date:** 18 July 2021  
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## The Exclusivity of the Gospel

In our culture, tolerance has become the highest virtue. We celebrate multicultural societies, international cooperation, and the idea that all views are equally valid. These benefits are real—relative peace, economic flourishing, diverse experiences. Yet Paul's letter opens with a message that seems shockingly intolerant to modern ears.

> **Galatians 1:8-9** "But even if we or an angel from heaven should preach a gospel other than the one we preached to you, let them be under God's curse! As we have already said, so now I say again: If anybody is preaching to you a gospel other than what you accepted, let them be under God's curse!"

Paul's language is uncompromising. The Greek word *anathema* (cursed) means "dedicated to evil"—damned. Paul is not advocating a witch hunt or claiming personal judgment authority. Rather, he is stating plainly and factually that any other gospel leads to hell.

This exclusivity flows necessarily from the gospel itself:

- **The cross** means there was no other way. God, in His infinite wisdom, would not have required Christ's death if alternatives existed.
- **Grace** means we could do nothing to save ourselves; we had to rely entirely on Jesus.
- **Christ as Lord** is not one lord among many, but THE supreme Lord over all.

The gospel's message is clear: trust in Jesus and be rescued from damnation, or reject Him and remain cursed. There is no middle ground, no partial acceptance, no "close enough."

### Why This Matters

When we try to harmonize the gospel with everything else—to accept all "gospels" as valid—we pervert the good news into something else entirely. A gospel that works with every other belief system, that asks nothing difficult, that makes no exclusive claims, is no gospel at all. It's been twisted into something unrecognizable.

> **Galatians 1:7** "Which is really no gospel at all. Evidently some people are throwing you into confusion and are trying to pervert the gospel of Christ."

This perversion looks like taking Jesus' moral teachings while rejecting the cross as harsh or the resurrection as implausible. You may be "Christianized" in this way, which works fine for this life. But not the next.

### An Illustration: Mathematics, Not English

Getting the gospel right is more like mathematics than English. An essay has millions of variables; many answers can be acceptable. But mathematics has only one correct answer. If the answer is 252, it cannot be 251 or 253 or 251.9999 repeating.

In mathematics, it doesn't matter whether you arrived at the answer through understanding, memorization, or copying from a neighbor. Whether you're clever or know nothing of algebra. **You get full marks.**

Similarly, all other paths—whether deism, atheism, or other religions—may be closer or further from the truth, but they're still wrong. Only the gospel of grace gives the right answer. And once you have that answer, it's enough. Period.

## Paul's Authority: From God Alone

Paul begins by establishing his credentials not to boast, but to defend the gospel's purity.

> **Galatians 1:1-4** "Paul, an apostle—sent not from men nor by a man, but by Jesus Christ and God the Father, who raised him from the dead—and all the brothers and sisters with me, To the churches in Galatia: Grace and peace to you from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ, who gave himself for our sins to rescue us from the present evil age, according to the will of our God and Father."

"Apostle" means "sent." Paul was sent directly by Jesus Christ, not by any human institution. This claim matters because he's about to accuse the Galatians of abandoning the true gospel for a corrupted version.

### Paul's Personal Testimony

Paul's authority comes from his direct encounter with the risen Christ. On the road to Damascus, Jesus appeared to him—a former persecutor became an apostle.

> **Galatians 1:11-12** "I want you to know, brothers and sisters, that the gospel I preached is not of human origin. I did not receive it from any man, nor was I taught it; rather, I received it by revelation from Jesus Christ."

This is crucial. Paul didn't receive the gospel through proper theological channels or from the Jerusalem apostles. He received it directly from Jesus.

> **Galatians 1:13-14** "For you have heard of my previous way of life in Judaism, how intensely I persecuted the church of God and tried to destroy it. I was advancing in Judaism beyond many of my own age among my people and was extremely zealous for the traditions of my fathers."

Paul was committed to a different gospel—the works-based righteousness of Judaism. He had no reason to fabricate grace; this message cost him everything.

### Independence, Not Isolation

Paul's subsequent movements reveal his independence:

> **Galatians 1:15-17** "But when God, who set me apart from my mother's womb and called me by his grace, was pleased to reveal his Son in me so that I might preach him among the Gentiles, my immediate response was not to consult any human being. I did not go up to Jerusalem to see those who were apostles before I was, but I went into Arabia. Later I returned to Damascus."

After his conversion, Paul spent time in Arabia—likely processing this revelation. Only after three years did he go to Jerusalem:

> **Galatians 1:18-20** "Then after three years, I went up to Jerusalem to get acquainted with Cephas and stayed with him fifteen days only. I saw none of the other apostles—only James, the Lord's brother. I assure you before God that what I am writing you is no lie."

Fifteen days—the minimum needed to become acquainted. Paul deliberately limited his exposure to the Jerusalem leadership, ensuring his gospel remained untainted by human influence.

> **Galatians 1:21-24** "Then I went to Syria and Cilicia. I was personally unknown to the churches of Judea that are in Christ. They only heard the report: 'The man who formerly persecuted us is now preaching the faith he once tried to destroy.' And they praised God because of me."

Paul was unknown to the Judean churches. His message came from God through Jesus, not through institutional approval or consensus.

## Defending Against Compromise

Critics accused Paul of offering "easy grace" to win popularity. His response is devastating:

> **Galatians 1:10** "Am I now trying to win the approval of human beings, or of God? Or am I trying to please people?"

The irony is sharp. If Paul wanted to please people, he would preach that all ways lead to heaven, that all views are valid. Look at the celebrities and politicians—the culturally popular voices—they validate every perspective. **That** gains approval.

But to say "only one way"? That makes you intolerant, a bigot. Paul's exclusionary gospel proves he's not a people-pleaser.

> **Galatians 1:10** "If I were still trying to please people, I would not be a servant of Christ."

Paul serves Christ, not culture. He brings the original, uncompromising gospel of God's grace—not a product tested by focus groups or polled for political correctness.

## The Challenge of Our Age

Modern society claims to embrace tolerance, yet it increasingly enforces its own exclusivity. Try asserting that only males and females exist, or that marriage is for life. Suggest that certain policies are counterproductive, and you'll be "cursed" in the contemporary sense—canceled, condemned, silenced.

Society insists: **There is only one way.** The irony is that secular tolerance, which once positioned itself against religious exclusivity, has become every bit as demanding.

Among all the competing "gospels" before us, we must decide which one is true. Paul's answer remains unchanged: There is only one gospel—the gospel of grace through Christ's cross. No negotiation. No compromise. No middle ground.

That is the message before us today.
