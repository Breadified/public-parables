# Mark 13:1-37; 14:32-41, 50-72

**Title:** The End of the World
**Date:** 3 April 2022
**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

## Introduction: A Question About the Future

The disciples approached Jesus with a question that echoes through the ages: When will the end of the world come? As He sat on the Mount of Olives overlooking the temple, Peter, James, John, and Andrew asked Him privately, "Tell us, when will these things happen? And what will be the sign that they are all about to be fulfilled?"

Their question arose from Jesus' sobering announcement: not one stone of the magnificent temple would remain on another—all would be thrown down. This prompted them to seek understanding about the future, about judgment, about the end of all things.

## Part One: Watch Out for the Future (Signs of the End)

### The Command to Be Alert

Jesus begins His answer not with reassurance but with a warning. Where the disciples had marveled at the temple's grandeur, Jesus told them to **look out**—to **watch** and **be on guard** (Greek: *blepe*—to be vigilant, to pay attention).

Three categories of signs would mark the approach of the end:

### First Sign: Deceivers and Confusion of Identity

> 13:5 Jesus said to them: "Watch out that no one deceives you. 6 Many will come in my name, claiming, 'I am he,' and will deceive many."
>
> 13:21 At that time if anyone says to you, 'Look, here is the Messiah!' or, 'Look, there he is!' do not believe it. 22 For false messiahs and false prophets will appear and perform signs and wonders to deceive, if possible, even the elect.

One of the defining signs of the end would be **confusion about identity**. Who is the Messiah? Where is He? False claimants would emerge, performing impressive signs and miracles to lead people astray—even threatening to deceive the elect themselves.

**Historical Application:**
Throughout history, many have claimed to be the Messiah or a prophet sent by God:
- In ancient times, scholars like Hippolytus, Sextus Julius Africanus, and Irenaeus predicted Jesus would return by 500 AD
- Islam claims revelation through the prophet Muhammad
- Mormonism claims additional prophets and revelation
- Sun Myung Moon in Korea presented himself as another Messiah, with followers calling themselves "Moonies"

The pattern persists: each generation produces those who claim divine authority, sowing confusion about truth and identity.

### Second Sign: Wars and Rumors of Wars

> 13:7 When you hear of wars and rumors of wars, do not be alarmed. Such things must happen, but the end is still to come. 8 Nation will rise against nation, and kingdom against kingdom. There will be earthquakes in various places, and famines. These are the beginning of birth pains.

Jesus speaks of **rumors of wars**—disinformation, confusion about whether conflict is actually occurring. Wars and rumors of wars compound the confusion about identity. People hear of conflict but cannot discern truth from rumor.

**Historical Application:**
The 20th century was the most murderous in recorded history, with an estimated 187 million deaths from war—more than 10% of the world's 1913 population. What began in 1914 continued as nearly unbroken conflict with brief respites. After a century of war, we remain at war. The Ukraine-Russia conflict, America's longest war in Afghanistan with threats of escalation—these testify to the endless cycle of conflict Jesus described.

### Third Sign: Persecution and Family Division

> 13:9 "You must be on your guard. You will be handed over to the local councils and flogged in the synagogues. On account of me you will stand before governors and kings as witnesses to them."

The gospel's advance comes through suffering. Believers will face arrest, trial, and opposition. Yet there is comfort in this:

> 13:11 Whenever you are arrested and brought to trial, do not worry beforehand about what to say. Just say whatever is given you at the time, for it is not you speaking, but the Holy Spirit.

> 13:12 "Brother will betray brother to death, and a father his child. Children will rebel against their parents and have them put to death."

The deepest persecution comes through fractured families—the most intimate betrayals.

**Historical Application:**
German Chancellor Angela Merkel declared in 2013 that Christianity is "the most persecuted religion in the world." A comprehensive survey showed that approximately 200 million Christians (10% of global Christianity) face social disadvantage, harassment, or active oppression for their faith.

The Middle East tells a particularly tragic story. Between half and two-thirds of Christians have departed or been killed over the past century:
- In 1990, Iraq had over 1.2 million Christians
- By 2003, fewer than 500,000 remained
- By 2013, fewer than 200,000 remained
- In twenty years, 80% were gone

Prince Charles noted in December 2013 that the Middle East and North Africa—Christianity's ancient heartland—now have the lowest concentration of Christians in the world at just 4%, declining dramatically over recent decades. This is where Jesus was born, where His disciples lived, where the church has thrived for two millennia.

### Fourth Sign: Cosmological and Geological Distress

> 13:14 "When you see 'the abomination that causes desolation' standing where it does not belong—let the reader understand—then let those who are in Judea flee to the mountains."

A terrible calamity approaches with such sudden urgency that there is no time to prepare:

> 13:15 Let no one on the housetop go down or enter the house to take anything out.
>
> 13:16 Let no one in the field go back to get their cloak.

The imagery is of catastrophic urgency—people fleeing from rooftops, abandoning possessions, racing to higher ground. Recent history has shown such events: the 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami released energy equivalent to 23,000 Hiroshima-type atomic bombs.

> 13:17 How dreadful it will be in those days for pregnant women and nursing mothers!

Jesus focuses compassion on the most vulnerable—those whose condition makes flight nearly impossible.

> 13:18-19 Pray that this will not take place in winter, because those will be days of distress unequaled from the beginning, when God created the world, until now—and never to be equaled again.

The distress will be cosmic in scale, unparalleled since creation itself. Earthquakes in various places, famines—these are described as birth pains, the groaning of creation itself.

> 13:24-25 "But in those days, following that distress, the sun will be darkened, and the moon will not give its light; the stars will fall from the sky, and the heavenly bodies will be shaken."

## A Problem With These Signs

Yet questions emerge. Haven't such calamities been occurring for two thousand years? For thousands of years before that? What use is a sign that describes everyday life? How can we distinguish genuine signs from the perpetual chaos of history?

Moreover, scientifically, some elements seem fantastical. The sun, 4.6 billion years old and halfway through its existence, has another 5 billion years of hydrogen fuel. If the sun's darkening is a sign, it would require an incomprehensibly long wait.

And then there is verse 30:

> 13:30 Truly I tell you, this generation will certainly not pass away until all these things have happened.

This verse troubles many interpreters. Jesus seems to promise that His generation would witness all these events. Yet nearly two thousand years have passed. How do we reconcile this?

## Part Two: Watch Out for the Past (How Prophecy Was Fulfilled)

Perhaps the answer lies in recognizing that **the signs Jesus spoke of were already fulfilled in His own time**—in the events surrounding His death and resurrection. To understand this, we must read the subsequent chapters of Mark and observe how the predictions of Chapter 13 find their fulfillment in Chapter 14.

### Deceivers and Confusion About Identity

> 14:44 Now the betrayer had arranged a signal with them: "The one I kiss is the man; arrest him and lead him away under guard."

Why a kiss? The setting is Passover—crowded, chaotic. A kiss served to distinguish Jesus from the crowd, to resolve confusion. Yet the very need for such a sign demonstrates the confusion Jesus predicted: **Who is the Messiah? How do we identify Him?**

### Wars and Rumors of War

> 14:43 Just as he was speaking, Judas, one of the Twelve, appeared. With him was a crowd armed with swords and clubs, sent from the chief priests, the teachers of the law, and the elders.

An armed group mobilizes—swords and clubs. But Jesus asks:

> 14:48 "Am I leading a rebellion," said Jesus, "that you have come out with swords and clubs to capture me?"

War threatens but does not materialize. It is a rumor of war, a show of force that proves unnecessary. No actual rebellion occurs.

### Persecution and Betrayal

The prophecy of brothers betraying brothers finds its tragic enactment:

> 14:10 Then Judas Iscariot, one of the Twelve, went to the chief priests to betray Jesus to them.

One who ate with Him from the beginning—a friend, a disciple—betrays Him.

> 14:66-68 While Peter was below in the courtyard, one of the servant girls of the high priest came by. When she saw Peter warming himself, she looked closely at him. "You also were with that Nazarene, Jesus," she said. But he denied it. "I don't know or understand what you're talking about," he said.

Peter, the closest disciple, denies even knowing Jesus.

All the disciples abandon Him:

> 14:50-52 Then everyone deserted him and fled. A young man, wearing nothing but a linen garment, was following Jesus. When they seized him, he fled naked, leaving his garment behind.

Such panic ensues that disciples leave behind even their clothing—precisely mirroring Jesus' own prediction that there would be no time even to grab a cloak.

### Cosmological Distress

The ultimate cosmic sign comes at Jesus' crucifixion:

> 15:33-34 At the sixth hour darkness came over the whole land until the ninth hour. And at the ninth hour Jesus cried out in a loud voice, "Eloi, Eloi, lama sabachthani?"—which means, "My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?"

**Darkness at noon.** The sun darkened. God forsakes God.

This is not mere tragedy. This is the end of the world. The abomination that causes desolation: **God Himself, in Christ, dies.**

Judgment has begun—but not as arbitrary punishment from an angry deity. Judgment begins with God Himself. The horror of judgment is borne by Christ's body. It was His mother who lost her child, standing at a distance to watch her son's agony.

> 15:40-41 Some women were watching from a distance. Among them were Mary Magdalene, Mary the mother of James the younger and of Joses, and Salome. In Galilee these women had followed him and cared for his needs.

He endured this not out of cruelty or vengeance, but **out of love for humankind**—to spare us from judgment.

## Part Three: Watch Out for the Present (Living in the Echo)

The end of the world came in AD 33. Some have calculated that it occurred precisely during this Easter weekend—when Pontius Pilate was prefect of Judea (AD 26-36), when Herod Antipas had recently married Herodias, after John the Baptist's beheading. The only Passover Thursday night in that window was AD 33.

Nearly two thousand years have passed since then. Yet we remain in the echo of that end.

### The World in Echo

When the sun exhausts its hydrogen in five billion years, it will not disappear instantly. It will expand into a superheated supernova, then collapse into a white dwarf—taking an immense time to wind down. Yet despite all this tremendous activity, it is a dead sun.

Similarly, **make no mistake: the world is dead.** It takes time to fizzle out, but the end has come. We simply do not perceive it because echoes have been reverberating for millennia—for all of our lifetimes. We have become numbed to them. We mistake them for the normal state of things rather than recognizing them as death throes.

### Recognizing the Signs in Our Time

But reminders pierce through periodically. When we pause from distraction and think. When death strikes those close to us. For those who die, the end of the world is immediate and total. For those who remain, death awakens us to the question: What is wrong with this world? When is the end?

Each war echoes that final war at the cross. Each earthquake reminds us of the cosmic shaking. Each eclipse mirrors that darkness at noon.

### The Call to Watch

For believers: Stand firm. You will be protected on the final day.

For unbelievers: Can you see the sign that the end of the world is near? The protection that Jesus offers is no longer hidden—all signs have been given. All we face now are echoes.

Do not be surprised by depravity. Do not await evolution; expect degradation. The world has been sentenced. It only echoes its judgment endlessly.

Yet Jesus did this for you. Out of love. So that death will not be the end of us.

Cling to Him.
