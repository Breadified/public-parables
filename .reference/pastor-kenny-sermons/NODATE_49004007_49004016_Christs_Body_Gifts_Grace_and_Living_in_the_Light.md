# Ephesians 4:7-16; 5:8-21

**Title:** Christ's Body: Gifts, Grace, and Living in the Light

**Speaker:** Kenny Liew

---

## God's People and the Church

God's people are those united under one Lord, one Spirit, one faith, and one baptism. Scripture identifies them as the saints and faithful in Christ Jesus—those chosen in Christ before the foundation of the world, predestined for adoption as God's children. They are the redeemed, those who heard the gospel of salvation and believed, sealed with the Holy Spirit.

---

## Building Up the Church

### What Does "Built Up" Mean?

To build up the church means to mature God's people, to encourage them in faith and knowledge. When we speak the truth in love, people grow. The church builds itself up in love as members serve one another with their gifts and talents.

### The Essential Element: Love

Love is most necessary for building up God's people. Through truth spoken in love and mutual service in love, the body of Christ matures and becomes what God intends.

### Everyone's Role in the Church

By definition, being part of the church means you are already involved. The question is not whether you participate, but how. While the church has many tasks, the most important calling for every member is simple: look out for one another. Build each other up in love, maturity, and knowledge.

Here's a helpful reframing: think of "gifts" not as special talents (which sounds self-centered) but as opportunities. This shifts the question from "What special abilities do I have?" to "What needs can I meet, even with my ordinary abilities?" When you serve that need, you've exercised your gift.

### Speaking Truth in Love

Speaking truth to one another in love is not always easy. It's simple only if everyone loves and adores your every word—which is rarely the case. Most of us have experienced the difficulty of lovingly encouraging someone toward growth in love, maturity, and knowledge. Yet this work is essential to the church's health.

## Living as Light in a Dark World

### Our Relationship with Darkness

Scripture is clear: have nothing to do with the deeds of darkness. Instead, expose them. Our identity has changed—we are no longer called to live the old way. Paul emphasizes this through repeated warnings: avoid sexual immorality, don't be deceived by the worldly philosophies that surround us.

### Why Expose Darkness?

Exposing darkness brings it into the light, making it visible rather than hidden. When sin operates in secret, we can pretend it's not there. But when it's exposed, we can no longer hide from it. While exposure doesn't prevent all sin, it certainly makes it harder to continue living as though nothing is wrong.

### Making the Most of Our Time

Paul urges us to make the most of the opportunities God gives us. This means living wisely—not in foolishness, but in understanding God's will. It means remaining pure, avoiding greed, and actively exposing darkness. This is how we make our days count.

### Filled with the Holy Spirit

Many interpret "filled with the Spirit" as receiving some sort of extraordinary power to be extra spiritual. But Kenny's understanding aligns with the deeper meaning: to be filled with the Spirit means to be filled with Christian qualities and the fruits of the Spirit.

Consider the context. Paul isn't talking about mystical experiences; he's describing what it means to live as a Christian from chapter 3 onward—speaking truth in love, building each other up, serving one another. The phrase is used in contrast to being "filled with wine"—Paul is saying, be filled with good works and the fruits of the Spirit, not with alcohol. Be filled with godly songs, not the bawdy drinking songs of those who indulge in wine.

### Benefits of Living Filled with the Spirit

When we are filled with the Spirit—when we live out Christian qualities—we do the Lord's will. We expose darkness. We submit to one another in love. This mutual submission becomes the foundation for how we relate to one another in every relationship: with spouses, with children, with employers, with everyone.

---

## Evidence of the Spirit's Work

The fruits of a life filled with the Spirit are visible: believers loving one another, building each other up in knowledge and maturity, and progressively putting away their deeds of darkness. These evidences are never final proof and never grounds for boasting about our own righteousness. But they are signs that God is at work in a life, transforming it into the likeness of Christ. When you see these fruits in others, give thanks and rejoice—God is indeed working in His church.
