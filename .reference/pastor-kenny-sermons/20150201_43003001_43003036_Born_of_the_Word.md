## Introduction: Stories of Birth

We encounter many significant birth stories throughout history. On 31 October 1517, Martin Luther nailed his 95 theses to the church door in Wittenberg—an act that sparked the Protestant Reformation. That same date marks another anniversary: 31 October 2004, when my daughter was born, just days after I completed my thesis on Martin Luther and we moved houses.

Today we explore a different kind of birth story—one far more significant than either of these. Jesus speaks to us about being born again, about spiritual rebirth that brings us into God's kingdom.

---

## Part 1: Born of the Spirit

### The Question of New Birth

> Very truly I tell you, no one can see the kingdom of God unless they are born again (from above, *anothen*). (John 3:3)

Nicodemus, a respected Pharisee and member of the Jewish ruling council, comes to Jesus at night with an honest question. He recognizes something divine in Jesus—his signs and teachings reveal God's presence. Yet when Jesus speaks of being "born again," Nicodemus is perplexed.

> How can someone be born when they are old? Surely they cannot enter a second time into their mother's womb to be born! (John 3:4)

Nicodemus takes Jesus literally, imagining a physical return to the womb. This reflects a genuine confusion: *How can this rebirth actually happen?* It's the question all of us are asking.

### Water and Spirit: A Promise from Scripture

Jesus clarifies:

> Very truly I tell you, no one can enter the kingdom of God unless they are born of water and the Spirit. Flesh gives birth to flesh, but the Spirit gives birth to spirit. (John 3:5-6)

This phrase has been interpreted many ways. Some see physical birth (the amniotic waters), others see baptism. But there's a deeper layer. In the Old Testament, water and wind together symbolize God's blessing and renewal.

Isaiah prophesied:

> For I will pour water on the thirsty land, and streams on the dry ground; I will pour out my Spirit on your offspring, and my blessing on your descendants. (Isaiah 44:3)

In an agricultural economy, this language speaks volumes. Water brings life to crops; the Spirit (breath/wind) brings life to people. Together, they represent divine restoration. This same imagery appears in Ezekiel's vision of dry bones coming to life—death transformed into breath and vitality.

### The Metaphor of Wind

Jesus uses the wind to illuminate this mystery:

> The wind blows wherever it pleases. You hear its sound, but you cannot tell where it comes from or where it is going. So it is with everyone born of the Spirit. (John 3:8)

Why this metaphor? Because the Spirit works with the same mysterious power as the wind. We cannot control it, predict it, or fully comprehend it. We only see its effects. The Spirit gives us a "second breath"—not transformation of what already exists, but resurrection of what was dead.

### The Picture of the Serpent

Jesus then makes a striking connection:

> Just as Moses lifted up the snake in the wilderness, so the Son of Man must be lifted up, that everyone who believes may have eternal life in him. (John 3:14-15)

During Israel's wilderness wandering, the people complained against God. In judgment, venomous snakes were sent among them, and many died. But God offered a way of salvation: Moses lifted a bronze serpent on a pole, and anyone who looked at it lived (Numbers 21).

It seems paradoxical—the very instrument of death becomes the means of healing. Similarly, the Son of Man, lifted on the cross, becomes our salvation. To "look at him" means to believe, to accept the provision of God through Jesus.

### The Promise of Eternal Life

> For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life. (John 3:16)

The verdict is clear: belief in Jesus is belief that God is truthful. Rejection of Him is already condemnation, because we have rejected truth itself.

### The Application: Our Spiritual Rebirth

We are all spiritually dead—"dead bones" in Ezekiel's vision. Putting makeup on a corpse changes nothing. We don't need transformation; we need resuscitation. We need a new breath.

No matter what point in life we've reached—whether at the deepest dungeon of sin—we can receive a second wind, a new beginning. This is what Jesus offers: not improved morality, but new life itself.

## Part 2: Born of the Word

### Where Is Jesus Today?

If we need to "look at" Jesus to live, the natural question arises: *Where do we encounter Him today?* We can't see an actual cross or a physical Jesus. Where is He present?

> The one whom God has sent speaks the words of God, for God gives the Spirit without limit. (John 3:34)

Jesus is present in the Word of God. His words are "full of the Spirit and life" (John 6:63). When Peter was asked if the disciples might leave Jesus, he replied:

> Lord, to whom shall we go? You have the words of eternal life. (John 6:68)

Peter understood a vital truth: the words of Jesus are words of eternal life. In them, the Spirit dwells without limit.

### The Power of Scripture

We often take the Bible for granted—an app on our phones, ink on creased pages. But we hold something extraordinary: the very words through which God does His work in us. This is how the Spirit works today.

Consider the Reformation. During the Dark Ages, people had abandoned Scripture. Then came the Renaissance and the printing press. In 1452—65 years before Luther's revolution—the first book produced by this new technology was a Bible. It was expensive; few copies circulated. But the chain reaction had begun. Technology improved. Paper improved. Ink improved. Costs decreased.

Eventually, Martin Luther had a Bible in his hands. He read it. He believed it. And his faith transformed history.

Today, that same Word rests in our hands. We must cherish it:

- **Pick up a copy of the Gospel of John**
- **Read it. Bring it to church. Mark it. Learn it. Make it your own**
- **Paper Bibles are superior to phones for study**—you can see context, turn to cross-references quickly, trace connections that a small screen obscures.

### Testimony and Truth

> He testifies to what he has seen and heard, but no one accepts his testimony. Whoever has accepted it has certified that God is truthful. (John 3:32-33)

To accept the Word is to certify that God is truthful. The Word stands as God's testimony to us. Our response determines everything.

---

## Conclusion: Your Birth Story

Most of us don't know the details of our physical birth. But we can know our spiritual birth story—the moment we were born again through faith in Jesus and His Word.

As you leave today, carry this truth: you need a restart. You need new life. And that life comes through believing in Jesus and His words of eternal life. Don't remain spiritually dead. Look to Him. Believe. And receive the new breath of the Spirit that makes you alive forever.
