# 100 Questions That Challenge the Bible

Provocative, hard-hitting questions that skeptics, doubters, and critics actually ask. Designed to force honest wrestling with Scripture—and to let the Bible answer for itself.

---

## Category 1: Does God Even Exist? (15 Questions)

1. If God exists, why is there no direct, undeniable proof?
2. Isn't "God" just a concept humans invented to explain what they didn't understand?
3. Why should I believe in your God and not Zeus, Thor, or any other deity?
4. If everything needs a creator, who created God?
5. Isn't the universe better explained by science than by an invisible deity?
6. Why would an all-powerful God need to be worshipped?
7. If God is real, why does He hide instead of making Himself obvious?
8. Isn't faith just believing something without evidence—and isn't that irrational?
9. How can you claim God is "necessary" when billions live without believing in Him?
10. Doesn't the vastness of the universe make humanity seem insignificant rather than specially created?
11. Why do believers from every religion claim the same certainty about their God?
12. If God speaks to people, why do they all hear different and contradictory things?
13. Isn't religious experience just a product of brain chemistry and psychology?
14. Why would God create a universe with so much empty, lifeless space if Earth was the point?
15. If God is love, why did He design a world where survival depends on death and predation?

---

## Category 2: The Problem of Evil—God's Biggest Failure? (12 Questions)

16. If God is all-powerful and all-loving, why do innocent children get cancer?
17. Why did God create a world He knew would be filled with suffering?
18. How can you worship a God who stood by during the Holocaust?
19. If God has a "plan," does that mean He planned for rape, murder, and torture?
20. Why does God answer prayers for parking spots but not for starving children?
21. Isn't it cruel to create people knowing most of them will end up in eternal hell?
22. If God is sovereign, isn't He ultimately responsible for all evil?
23. Why would a loving God create Satan if He knew what Satan would do?
24. How is eternal conscious torment a just punishment for a finite life of sin?
25. If free will explains evil, why didn't God create beings who freely choose good?
26. Why does God seem to intervene in trivial matters but ignore genocides?
27. Isn't "God works in mysterious ways" just an excuse when there's no good answer?

---

## Category 3: Is Jesus Who Christians Claim? (12 Questions)

28. Why should I believe Jesus existed when there's almost no contemporary evidence?
29. Isn't the Jesus story just a recycled myth from older dying-and-rising gods?
30. If Jesus was God, why did He seem ignorant of things (like the date of His return)?
31. Why do the Gospel accounts contradict each other on basic details?
32. Couldn't the disciples have simply made up the resurrection to keep their movement alive?
33. Why should I trust accounts written decades after Jesus supposedly lived?
34. If Jesus was sinless, why did He get baptized for repentance?
35. Why did Jesus curse a fig tree for not having fruit out of season? Isn't that petty?
36. If Jesus is the "Prince of Peace," why did He say He came to bring a sword?
37. Why did Jesus seem to ignore or insult non-Jews (like calling a woman a "dog")?
38. If Jesus knew He would rise again, was His sacrifice really that significant?
39. Why would God need a blood sacrifice to forgive? Couldn't He just forgive?

---

## Category 4: Is the Bible Trustworthy—Or a Mess of Errors? (18 Questions)

40. How can you claim the Bible is "inerrant" when it contains obvious contradictions?
41. Why does Genesis 1 and Genesis 2 give different orders of creation?
42. Why do the Gospels disagree about what was written on Jesus' cross?
43. Why do Matthew and Luke give completely different genealogies for Jesus?
44. How can the Bible be trusted when we don't have the original manuscripts?
45. Hasn't the Bible been copied and translated so many times that the original meaning is lost?
46. Why did the church get to decide which books made it into the Bible and which didn't?
47. Why should I trust books written by anonymous authors with theological agendas?
48. Why does the Bible contain scientific errors (like a flat earth, a solid sky dome, bats as birds)?
49. Why does the Bible endorse slavery instead of condemning it outright?
50. Why does God command genocide of entire people groups, including children?
51. Why does the Old Testament prescribe death for minor offenses like picking up sticks on the Sabbath?
52. Why does the Bible treat women as property?
53. Why does God seem jealous, angry, and petty in the Old Testament but loving in the New?
54. Did God change His mind, or is the Bible just inconsistent about His character?
55. Why does the Bible include books that contradict each other theologically (James vs. Paul on faith and works)?
56. If the Bible is God's Word, why is it so easily misunderstood and misused?
57. Why would God's "perfect revelation" require scholars and translators to understand it?

---

## Category 5: Science vs. Scripture—Can't Both Be True? (10 Questions)

58. Doesn't evolution completely disprove the Genesis creation account?
59. How can you believe the earth is 6,000 years old when all evidence points to billions?
60. Why does the Bible describe a global flood when there's no geological evidence for it?
61. If Adam and Eve were the first humans, where did Cain's wife come from?
62. How can you take the Bible seriously when it describes the sun standing still?
63. Why does the Bible treat mental illness and epilepsy as demon possession?
64. Doesn't the Bible's cosmology (firmament, waters above, pillars of the earth) reflect ancient ignorance?
65. If God created humans, why do we have vestigial organs and "design flaws"?
66. Why would God create a universe that looks exactly like it doesn't need Him to function?
67. If miracles break natural laws, doesn't that make them by definition the least likely explanation?

---

## Category 6: Christianity's Exclusive Claims—Arrogant or True? (10 Questions)

68. How can you claim Christianity is the only true religion when you were probably just born into it?
69. What makes your holy book more valid than the Quran, the Vedas, or the Book of Mormon?
70. Isn't it arrogant to claim billions of people in other religions are all wrong?
71. If Christianity is true, why are there so many denominations that can't agree on anything?
72. Why would a loving God send sincere, moral people to hell just for having the wrong beliefs?
73. How is it fair that someone's eternal destiny depends on where and when they were born?
74. If Christianity is self-evidently true, why does it require so much apologetics to defend?
75. Why do Christians interpret the same Bible in completely opposite ways?
76. If the Holy Spirit guides believers into truth, why do Spirit-filled Christians disagree on everything?
77. Isn't Christianity just a tool of colonialism and cultural imperialism?

---

## Category 7: The Character of God—Worthy of Worship? (10 Questions)

78. Why does God demand worship and threaten punishment for those who don't give it? Isn't that narcissism?
79. How is it "loving" to create a place of eternal torture for your own children?
80. Why does God test people if He already knows the outcome?
81. Why did God harden Pharaoh's heart and then punish him for having a hard heart?
82. Why did God kill Uzzah for steadying the Ark when he was trying to help?
83. Why did God send bears to maul 42 children for mocking a prophet's baldness?
84. Why does God accept human sacrifice in the case of Jephthah's daughter?
85. If God is unchanging, why does He "repent" or "regret" His decisions in Scripture?
86. Why would a just God punish children for the sins of their parents to the third and fourth generation?
87. Why does God play favorites (loving Jacob, hating Esau before they were born)?

---

## Category 8: The Bible's Morality—Outdated and Dangerous? (8 Questions)

88. Why should ancient Middle Eastern morality apply to modern life?
89. Why does the Bible regulate slavery instead of abolishing it?
90. Why does the Bible prescribe marrying a rape victim to her rapist?
91. Why does Paul tell slaves to obey their masters and women to be silent?
92. Why does the Bible condemn homosexuality but permit polygamy?
93. How can you use the Bible for morality when it's been used to justify atrocities?
94. If morality comes from God, does that mean genocide is okay when God commands it?
95. Why do Christians cherry-pick which Old Testament laws to follow?

---

## Category 9: Faith, Doubt, and Honesty (10 Questions)

96. If Christianity requires faith, isn't that an admission that there's not enough evidence?
97. Why does God punish honest doubt but reward blind belief?
98. If I sincerely seek God and don't find Him, is that my fault or His?
99. Why do so many intelligent, educated people reject Christianity after studying it?
100. If I have to suppress my reason to believe, is that really what God wants?

---

## Bonus: The Questions Beneath the Questions (5)

101. Am I rejecting Christianity because of evidence—or because I don't want it to be true?
102. What would I have to see or experience to change my mind?
103. Have I actually read the Bible, or am I rejecting what others say it teaches?
104. Am I holding the Bible to a standard I don't apply to anything else I believe?
105. If Christianity were true, would I be willing to follow it even if it cost me everything?

---

## How to Use These Questions

These questions are not meant to destroy faith—they are meant to **forge it**. 

The goal is to sit with the discomfort, search the Scriptures, and discover whether the Bible can answer its critics. A faith that has never been tested is a faith that cannot be trusted.

> "The heart of the discerning acquires knowledge, for the ears of the wise seek it out." — Proverbs 18:15
